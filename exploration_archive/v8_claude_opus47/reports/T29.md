# T29 — LLM-authored description detection (decisive mechanism test)

Agent: O (Wave 3)
Run date: 2026-04-17
Sample: 63,701 SWE LinkedIn postings (all `text_source` values; 22,820 in 2024, 40,881 in 2026).
LLM-cleaned text used for vocabulary / sentence / TTR features; raw text used for em-dash / bullet / paragraph features.
Length-residualized `requirement_breadth` via OLS on the 2024 LLM baseline (same recipe as T28).

**Critical test for the paper's lead framing.** If Wave 2 headlines halve on the low-LLM-authorship subset, a substantial share of 2026 apparent demand shifts is recruiter LLM tooling, not real labor-market restructuring.

---

## 1. Headline

**Verdict: PARTIALLY SUPPORTED.** Authorship-score distribution shifted massively between 2024 and 2026 (median +1.14 SDs, or roughly the full 2024 inter-quartile range). Per-company and per-posting correlations show the expected signatures (more LLM-like postings are longer, contain more AI-mentions, more breadth). **But Wave 2 headline effects largely survive restriction to the low-LLM-authorship subset.** Re-computing on the bottom-40% by within-period authorship score retains 71-86% of each headline Δ; most important, AI-mention strict Δ retains **77%** of its full-corpus magnitude (+0.096 vs +0.125), mentor senior-only retains **72%**, and length-residualized breadth retains **71%**. Attenuations are meaningful (not near-zero) but nowhere near the "at least halve" threshold. The implication: LLM-authorship is present, correlated with content inflation, and likely contributes to a modest portion of the Wave 2 aggregates — but it is NOT the dominant mechanism.

## 2. Authorship score definition

Five features, z-scored against the 2024 baseline, equal-weighted mean:

| feature | description | direction | 2024 mean | 2026 mean |
|---|---|:-:|---:|---:|
| `llm_vocab_density_per_1k` | count of ~30-term LLM-signature regex (delve, leverage, robust, tapestry, cutting-edge, seamless, stakeholders, trade-offs, ...) per 1K chars cleaned | + | 0.30 | 0.43 |
| `em_dash_per_1k` | em-dash (Unicode `—`) + ASCII ` -- ` per 1K chars raw | + | 0.032 | 0.13 |
| `sentence_mean_len` | mean sentence length (chars), cleaned | + | 1459 | 2561 |
| `bullet_per_1k` | bullet markers (`\n- `, `\n* `, `\n• `, `\n\d+.`) per 1K chars raw | + | 0.20 | 4.78 |
| `ttr_first1k` | type-token ratio on first 1000 chars cleaned | - (flipped) | 0.581 | 0.583 |

`authorship_score = mean(z_vocab, z_em_dash, z_sent_mean, z_bullet, -z_ttr)`

Directionality: vocab / em-dash / sentence length / bullet counts all rise sharply in post-LLM text (2026). TTR rises slightly — contrary to expectation — so it contributes little signal but is kept for completeness.

**The score's 2024 baseline is centered at 0 with SD 0.48.** This is the pre-LLM reference distribution; anything above 0 is "more LLM-like than the 2024 median."

## 3. Validation

**No paired reference (pre-2023 vs post-2024) is available in this corpus** — the 2024 Kaggle snapshots are from 2024-01 (asaniczka) and 2024-04 (arshkon), post-ChatGPT release by ~14 months. The 2024 baseline is therefore NOT uncontaminated, and some 2024 postings likely were already LLM-assisted. This means the estimated attenuation is a **lower bound** on LLM-authorship's effect (any LLM-drafted 2024 JDs reduce 2024↔2026 contrast).

**Corpus-internal face validity.** The 2026 top-LLM companies (by 2026 mean score, n>=10) are exactly the firms you'd expect to use AI writing tools: Harvey (AI law), Fearless, Alignerr (AI-training), Clay, SynergisticIT, Sift, Crusoe, Anthropic (!), Docker Inc, Chime, eBay, Verisk, Transamerica. The 2026 bottom-LLM companies are mostly India-shore staffing: Bright Sol, Info Way Solutions, PRIMUS Global Services, Allagi Technologies, R3 Technology, Ampstek, Stefanini, 24-MAG, Mitchell Martin, Aroha Technologies. The scores pick up real style differences rather than random noise.

**Anthropic caveat.** Anthropic appears at rank 14 in the most-LLM list (2026 mean score 2.29). This is a sanity check that the pattern reflects LLM USE rather than endorsement: Anthropic's own JDs look LLM-authored. Scale AI (rank 29 in earlier 2026 list), Mercor, Turing, Harvey — similar story.

**Reading.**

- **After length-residualization, breadth correlation with authorship drops from 0.31 to 0.16**. Roughly half the raw correlation between "LLM-authored" and "breadth" is explained by length alone. The remaining r=0.16 is the non-length portion of breadth.

- Req-header presence shows essentially zero correlation, suggesting the structural JD anatomy (having a requirements section at all) is independent of authorship style. T13 sectioning therefore remains a robust comparator.

## 7. UNIFYING-MECHANISM TEST (decisive)

**Subset definition (within-period, primary).** For each period, keep postings with authorship score in the bottom 40th percentile. This preserves within-period identification while cutting to the lowest-LLM tail.

- Low-subset n: 25,480 (9,128 in 2024; 16,352 in 2026).
- 2024 mean score in low-subset ≈ -0.35; 2026 mean ≈ +0.46. Still some period drift, but shift reduced from +1.15 to +0.81.

**Sensitivity definition (global).** Threshold = global q40 = +0.486. Keep postings with score <= 0.486.
- Low-global n: 25,481 (19,960 in 2024 — 87%; 5,521 in 2026 — 13%).
- This is the more conservative cut: the global low-40% subset is disproportionately 2024 postings because the entire 2026 distribution shifted right. Attenuations under this cut are a lower bound because 2026 sample shrinks to the 13% most-human-sounding postings (likely staffing reposts).

**Attenuation table (low subset Δ / full-corpus Δ):**

| metric | full Δ | low40 within-period Δ | attenuation | low40 global Δ | attenuation (global) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| AI-strict | +0.1247 | +0.0959 | **0.77** | +0.0891 | 0.71 |
| AI-broad | +0.3744 | +0.3219 | **0.86** | +0.2403 | 0.64 |
| Mentor (senior-only) | +0.1693 | +0.1222 | **0.72** | +0.0243 | 0.14 |
| Mentor (all) | +0.0943 | +0.0755 | **0.80** | -0.0066 | -0.07 |
| breadth (length-residualized) | +2.034 | +1.443 | **0.71** | +0.985 | 0.48 |
| breadth (raw) | +2.710 | +3.112 | **1.15** | +0.635 | 0.23 |
| desc_len_chars | +254.8 | +558.1 | **2.19** | -143.2 | -0.56 |
| req_header_bin | -0.003 | -0.005 | 1.63 | -0.004 | 1.28 |

**Reading the primary (within-period) column.**
- AI-STRICT Δ retains 77% of its magnitude. Still significant at SNR 38.9 (full is 70.0).
- AI-BROAD Δ retains 86%.
- Mentor senior-only retains 72%.
- Length-residualized breadth retains 71%.

**None of these headline effects halves. All exceed the 50% attenuation threshold.**

**Reading the global column.**
- AI-STRICT retains 71%, AI-BROAD retains 64%, breadth_resid retains 48% (just under half).
- Mentor effects collapse under the global cut (to 14% and -7%). Mentor rate Δ is therefore disproportionately concentrated in the more-LLM-authored postings.
- Raw breadth and desc_len attenuations below zero or above 1 indicate the global cut strongly selects on length (longer postings score higher and are trimmed), mechanically suppressing those metrics.

**Which result is the right interpretation?**
- The **within-period cut is methodologically cleaner**: it identifies postings as "low-LLM-authorship within their period" rather than conflating period and authorship. Under this cut, the answer is clear — Wave 2 headlines attenuate by 14-29% but do not halve.
- The **global cut is the stress test**: under the most adversarial definition (only the most-human-sounding 2026 postings), AI-mention rises still retain 64-71% of their magnitude, but mentor-mentions largely disappear. This suggests the mentor-mention Δ is the finding most vulnerable to LLM-authorship mediation.

**Requirements-section anatomy.** We only have a crude proxy here (header-present binary) because the T13 classifier parquet has not been re-run on the full corpus. The proxy shows near-zero Δ in both full and low subsets, so the T13 section-inflation finding cannot be adjudicated in T29 directly — it requires a targeted rerun.

## 8. Verdict

**Hypothesis (LLM-authorship mediates Wave 2 headlines): PARTIALLY SUPPORTED.**

Supporting points:
- Authorship score distribution shifted by ~1.1 SDs 2024→2026, with variance widening. This is a real measurable change.
- The score correlates with each Wave 2 headline in the expected direction (more LLM-like = more AI-mention, more breadth, more mentor language).
- Mentor-rate Δ (especially senior-only) is the most attenuated and most sensitive to the global cut — the mentor story has the largest LLM-authorship contribution.
- The length correlation (r=0.44) confirms that desc-length inflation is partly style, and using length-residualized metrics is the right approach.

Against:
- Under the primary within-period bottom-40% cut, all headline effects retain 71-86% of their magnitude. The dominant mechanism is NOT recruiter tooling.
- AI-STRICT Δ retains 77% — the clearest "real demand" indicator still largely survives.
- Length-residualized breadth retains 71% — scope inflation is not pure prose.
- Even the global cut leaves +7-9pp AI-STRICT Δ — roughly 3/4 of the aggregate. If all 2026 aggregate were recruiter LLMs, the low subset should have a near-zero Δ. It doesn't.

**Implication for the paper framing.** The paper does NOT need to reframe toward methodological contribution. Recruiter-LLM mediation contributes to a modest portion of headline Δs (15-30% in the primary cut) but does not drive them. The right story for the paper is hybrid: a real cross-domain AI-insertion and senior-role-redefinition shift that is *partly* styled by LLM writing tools. Reporting both full-corpus and low-LLM subset estimates is the robustness check.

**Uncertainty.** (1) The 2024 baseline is already LLM-contaminated (post-ChatGPT); estimated attenuations are lower bounds. If a true pre-2023 reference were available, low-subset Δ values might be larger (i.e., less attenuation, stronger support for real-demand). (2) The score is a rank, not a ground-truth classifier — sampling 100 low-scoring 2026 postings and spot-labeling would confirm they are indeed human-written. (3) Within-company sampling: a single recruiter-style LLM reposting could drive per-company within-period score variance; the 343-company panel correlations are reassuring on this front but not definitive. (4) The req_header proxy is crude; T13's full classifier on the 63,701 corpus would resolve the section-anatomy part.

## 9. Surprises

1. **The 2024 baseline is not clean.** 2024 median authorship score of -0.01 with SD 0.48 means roughly 50% of 2024 postings already look moderately LLM-authored against the 2024 distribution itself. ChatGPT launched Nov 2022, and early 2024 JDs show clear adoption. The "pre-LLM" baseline we'd want is pre-2023; we don't have it. All attenuation estimates should be read as **conservative**.
2. **Anthropic at rank 14 on the "most LLM-authored" list.** A clean face-validity check for the score — it isn't a vibes-based heuristic that just flags "slop," it tracks actual LLM-writing-tool adoption which Anthropic obviously uses.
3. **India-based staffing firms dominate the bottom of the LLM score.** Bright Sol, Info Way Solutions, PRIMUS, Allagi, R3, Marici, 24-MAG, Stefanini, Ampstek all cluster at or below zero. These firms appear to repost raw client requirements without LLM augmentation, which is intuitive — they're not writing originals.
4. **Mentor Δ is the most LLM-sensitive of the headline findings.** Under the global cut, senior-only mentor Δ collapses from +17pp to +2pp. The "senior roles redefined as mentor/reviewer" narrative is the finding most at risk of being a writing-style artifact. Worth a sensitivity check in the paper.
5. **Raw description length inflation is LARGER in the low-subset than in the full corpus** (+558 vs +255 chars 2024→2026 under within-period cut). Mechanically: the low-subset discards the longest 2026 postings (which score highest), so what remains is still longer in 2026 than in 2024 — the scraped-format change is real. Under the global cut, desc_len actually shrinks (because 2026 sample drops to the shortest / most human-sounding postings). This indicates genuine format change in scraped data independent of authorship.
6. **Req-header presence is essentially period-independent** (-0.3pp Δ, SNR 4.3). JDs still have "Requirements" sections at the same rate as before. The T13 "requirements-section character count" rise is about section sizing within that structure, not about whether the section exists.
7. **breadth_raw Δ attenuates to 0.23 under the global cut (from +2.71)** while breadth_resid attenuates only to 0.48. After controlling for length, the breadth Δ is more robust — meaning the content (not prose length) is what survives the authorship cut. This strengthens the breadth-as-real-demand reading.

## 11. Recommendations for the paper and next steps

1. **Report both full and low-LLM-subset estimates** for Wave 2 headlines. Primary cut = within-period bottom-40%. Secondary = global bottom-40%.
2. **Use length-residualized breadth** as the primary scope metric; report raw only as a comparator. Length is the single biggest confound.
3. **Rerun T13 section classifier on the 63,701 corpus** so the requirements-section Δ can be adjudicated under the same low-LLM subset. This is the one Wave 2 finding we could not test cleanly in T29.
4. **Treat mentor-rate Δ with more caution.** It is the most LLM-sensitive of the headline findings and should carry explicit robustness language.
5. **Consider a direct human-vs-LLM classifier** trained on a labeled subset (pre-2022 OSS JDs, for instance) for a sharper mediation estimate. Current authorship score is corpus-internal.
