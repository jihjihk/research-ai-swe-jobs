## Headline findings

1. **AI-mention rise is 102% within-company (STRICT primary).** On the T16 overlap panel (240 companies, arshkon≥3 & scraped≥3), the Oaxaca decomposition of binary AI-mention (STRICT pattern) gives aggregate +12.1pp, within-company +12.4pp, between-company −0.3pp. The within component EXCEEDS the aggregate because composition shift (weight reallocation across these 240 cos) is slightly AI-dilutive. Robust 4-of-5 across sensitivities (all-cos, no-specialist, no-aggregator, cap-50, pooled-2024). Under the BROAD pattern (strict + `\bai\b|\bml\b|machine learning|llm|...`), within = 27.1pp of aggregate +39.1pp = **69% within** — still majority within-company but with real composition contribution. **This RAISES T06's earlier "91% within-company" finding on AI-mention: on the T16 overlap panel with the V1-refined strict vocabulary, within-company is the near-entirety of the signal.**

2. **Length-residualized requirement_breadth rises +1.4 within-company** on the primary panel (aggregate +1.40, within +1.43, between −0.03). The within-company share is 102% — i.e., **the same companies are writing broader requirements in 2026 as in 2024**, even after removing the length-proxy component (residualizing on cleaned description length). 76% of panel companies have positive residualized breadth Δ; 62% have Δ > 1.0 (large positive). This is the cleanest within-company test of scope inflation that survives V1's length-residualization critique.

3. **Five clusters emerge from k-means on the change vector, silhouette best at k=5 (0.188).** The content-based names (derived from mean Δ on each dimension) are: (0) **"Entry-contracting retreat"** (n=15, −32pp entry-J2, modest +11pp AI, desc shrinks); (1) **"AI-forward tech-stacking"** (n=25, +41pp AI-strict, +3.4 tech, +3.9 breadth_resid); (2) **"Mass-hiring tech-stackers"** (n=84, modest +7pp AI, +1.6 tech, +1.6 breadth_resid, huge n_sc — includes JPMorgan, Amazon, Capital One); (3) **"Broad-reqs tech-stacking"** (n=54, +12pp AI, +4.8 tech, +4.8 breadth_resid — includes Booz Allen, TCS, Adobe); (4) **"Consolidating giants"** (n=62, +10pp AI, slight entry ramp, −0.5 tech, −1.2 breadth_resid — Google, Microsoft, AWS, Wells Fargo). Clusters are distinguishable on ALL dimensions simultaneously, not collapsed to a single axis.

4. **New entrants look MORE like generalists than returning specialists, not less.** 5,916 new-entrant cos in scraped (74.5% of scraped company set; consistent with T06). New-vs-returning profile: AI-strict 16.1% vs 13.0% (new HIGHER), mean breadth_resid 0.44 vs 0.58 (new LOWER), mean tech_count 6.98 vs 6.94 (≈ same), J2 3.4% vs 3.1% (≈ same), aggregator rate 20.0% vs 13.9% (new more aggregator-heavy), specialist rate 6.0% vs 12.6% (new LESS specialist-dense). **Finding: new entrants are AI-ambient and aggregator-leaning, but they are NOT driving the scope-inflation signal — that's a within-company phenomenon among the 240-co overlap panel.**

5. **Aggregators do not have meaningfully different trajectories from direct employers.** Mean change vectors for aggregator-heavy (agg_rate_sc ≥ 0.5, n=36) vs direct employer (n=204): d_ai_strict +0.141 vs +0.124 (nearly equal), d_entry_j2 +0.011 vs −0.016 (aggregators ramped entry modestly; directs dropped it modestly), d_mean_breadth_resid +1.78 vs +1.79 (essentially identical), d_mean_tech_count +1.77 vs +1.88 (close). Aggregators did NOT drive scope inflation differentially.

6. **Labeled-subset sensitivity: within-AI finding holds; within-J2 finding FLIPS (as V1 predicted).** On the 196-company labeled-only overlap panel: ai_strict aggregate +11.4pp, within +12.0pp (cleanly within-company again). But entry_share_j2 aggregate flips from −0.5pp (full panel) to +2.0pp (labeled-only), with within +0.6pp. V1's "LLM-frame J2 flip" is corroborated at the company-level. **Any J2-headline within-company claim must be reported for both the full panel AND the labeled-only subset.**

## Methodology

**Panels.** 240 cos with ≥3 arshkon LinkedIn SWE + ≥3 scraped LinkedIn SWE (primary); 589 cos with ≥3 pooled-2024 LinkedIn SWE + ≥3 scraped LinkedIn SWE (secondary pooled); 196-co labeled-only subset (sensitivity on LLM-frame).

**Metrics per company-period.** (a) Entry shares: J1 (`seniority_final='entry'`), J2 (`IN ('entry','associate')`), J3 (`yoe≤2`). (b) AI-mention binary under STRICT pattern (V1-refined: `copilot|cursor|claude|chatgpt|openai api|gpt-?\d+|gemini|codex|llamaindex|langchain|prompt engineering|fine-tuning|rag|vector database|pinecone|huggingface`; `agent_bare` and `mcp` dropped per V1) and BROAD pattern (STRICT ∪ `\bai\b|\bml\b|machine learning|llm|generative ai|genai|anthropic`). (c) Mean raw description length, mean LLM-cleaned description length. (d) Mean tech_count (from shared tech matrix). (e) Org-scope count per 1k cleaned chars. (f) Composite `breadth_raw` = tech_count + org_scope_count + cred_count + soft_count (cleaned text). (g) `breadth_resid` = `breadth_raw − (6.498 + 0.00182 × cleaned_length)` (OLS length-residualization, global fit).

**Decomposition.** Symmetric Oaxaca: aggregate Δ = Σw_sc·y_sc/Σw_sc − Σw_ar·y_ar/Σw_ar; within = Σ s_avg·(y_sc − y_ar); between = Σ (s_sc − s_ar)·y_avg; where s = w/Σw and s_avg = (s_ar + s_sc)/2, y_avg = (y_ar + y_sc)/2. This splits the aggregate into (same-company change weighted by avg share) + (weight-reallocation term).

**Clustering.** Standardized 6-dim change vector: (Δ-J2, Δ-ai_strict, Δ-cleaned_len, Δ-tech, Δ-org_scope, Δ-breadth_resid). k-means k∈{3,4,5,6}, n_init=20/50, random_state=42. Silhouette scores: k=3 0.155, k=4 0.169, **k=5 0.188**, k=6 0.184. Selected k=5.

**Sensitivities.** (a) full 240-co panel; (b) no-specialist (221 cos; drop 19 of the 240-co specialists); (c) no-aggregator (213 cos; drop row-level aggregators then re-aggregate); (d) pooled-2024 baseline (589 cos); (e) cap-per-company at 50 postings (240 cos but sampled); (f) labeled-only subset (196 cos).

## Implications for RQ1a / RQ1b

**RQ1a (junior share / employer restructuring of who gets hired).** The within-company entry-share Δ is tiny in magnitude and direction-dependent on J definition (J1/J2 down ~0.5pp full panel; J2 flips to +2pp under labeled-only; J3 +5.9pp overwhelmingly between-company). **The "same companies changed who they hired" hypothesis is weak on the T16 panel:** 96% of the J3 rise is driven by weight reallocation across companies (new-entrant pipelines, tech-giant intern ramps), not by established posters changing their intake mix. The one cluster that DID contract entry (Cluster 0, n=15: Boeing, Intel, Docusign, Dexian, INSPYR) is a small concentrated segment, not a pervasive pattern. This corroborates the Wave-2 narrative: **aggregate entry change is mostly composition; the period-rewriting story is what's real, not a hiring-mix story.**

**RQ1b (scope inflation within the same jobs).** **Length-residualized requirement_breadth is 102% within-company on the primary panel (+1.43 of +1.40 aggregate), and the within-share is 94–147% across all five sensitivities. 76% of same-company pairs have positive Δ; 62% have Δ > 1.0.** This is the cleanest within-company test of scope inflation we've generated, and the result is affirmative: **the same companies ARE writing broader requirements in 2026 (as measured by tech + soft + cred + org-scope counts) even after controlling for length.** The mean Δ of 1.43 composite units is roughly a 20% rise relative to arshkon baseline (8.09 → 9.52 raw; length-residualized adds clarity). Mean tech_count also rises +1.76 within-company (from 5.52 to 7.28 raw tech items). Org_scope per 1k FALLS within-company (−0.126), which is interesting: companies are adding MORE diverse techs + credentials + soft skills, but the cross-functional/stakeholder/ownership language is actually getting DENSER-packed (total count up, but per-1k-char rate down because overall cleaned length went down). V1's interpretation ("breadth is 71% content / 29% length") holds at the within-company level as well.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **Cleaned description length SHRANK −848 chars within-company on the primary panel, even as raw length grew +935 chars within-company.** This is the boilerplate-growth story: raw length includes EEO / benefits / recruiter-boilerplate which grew a lot, while LLM-cleaned (core posting) SHRANK. Within-company cleaned length is −67% of aggregate: companies are writing leaner core requirements and wrapping them in more boilerplate. Consistent with T13's finding that requirements SHRANK while benefits + role_summary + about_company grew. This contradicts the naive "longer postings = more scope" reading; scope inflation is happening in the core, not in the wrapping.

2. **Google is a "consolidating giant" (cluster 4), not an "AI-forward" company by change-vector.** Google's d_ai_strict is only +9.4pp — below average for the panel. Google's d_tech is +1.37, d_breadth_resid is −3.2 (!), d_cleaned_len is −850. Google is writing LEANER, NARROWER postings in 2026 while ramping its new-grad funnel (TikTok-clone d_J2 +0.9pp; modest). The "tech giants are the AI vanguard" intuition is wrong on this data: **consulting firms (Deloitte, ICE, ConsultNet, VDart, Pinterest) are cluster-1 "AI-forward" and are the ones adding the most AI vocabulary per posting.**

3. **Aggregator-heavy cos changed their ai_strict rate by +14.1pp vs direct employers +12.4pp — nearly identical.** I expected aggregators to have structurally different trajectories (less context, more boilerplate, more recruiter-posted). They don't. Both subsets converged on similar AI-mention and similar breadth growth. This means the "aggregator = different market" framing is less central to T16 than we hypothesized pre-dispatch.

4. **V1's flipped J2 reproduces cleanly at the company level.** I confirmed that J2 direction is SIGN-DEPENDENT on whether you use full panel (−0.53pp aggregate) or labeled-only (+1.99pp). **Not just row-level — it flips at the company-panel level too.** This is more disturbing than V1 suggested: within-company J2 is not a stable headline under any reasonable presentation.

5. **New entrants' J3 share is LOWER than returning, not higher.** Returning cos have J3 = 13.5%; new entrants have J3 = 9.3%. The "new entrants are ramping juniors" hypothesis that would reconcile T06's between-company J3 story is WRONG at the cohort level: new-entrant cohorts post OLDER (mean yoe 5.16) jobs than returning cohorts. The J3 rise is coming from returning-but-reweighted cos (Google/Microsoft/Walmart-type intern pipelines becoming more visible), not from a surge of young companies hiring juniors.

6. **Breadth_resid median per co is +1.88 — larger than the mean (+1.79).** Distribution is actually LEFT-skewed (a handful of companies SHRANK breadth dramatically, e.g., Intel −11.2, General Motors −1.6, Viasat −4.2; these are cluster-0 "Entry-contracting retreat"). For the typical (median) company, the scope inflation is MORE pronounced than the average shows. **Scope inflation is the modal company's story, not just a fat-tailed few.**

7. **The ai_ml_engineering archetype's +47pp within-company AI-strict growth happens in only 9 company-archetype pairs**, but it's massive. This is a "specialist fast-growers" signature: cos that already posted ML roles in 2024 dramatically intensified their AI vocabulary by 2026. If we're using AI-mention as a labor-market diagnostic, the ML-archetype contribution is heavily disproportionate.

## Data quality caveats

- **Overlap panel is scraped-heavy (6× more scraped rows than arshkon).** Cap-50 sensitivity (decomp_arsh_scraped_cap50.csv) confirms the findings hold under equal-weight sampling, so the heavy-weighting of the scraped side is not driving the results.
- **Pooled-2024 baseline introduces asaniczka-only cos.** 589 vs 240 co difference; these are lower-precision-on-entry-labels (asaniczka missing native entry labels per T02). Reported as sensitivity only.
- **`agg_heavy` definition uses agg_rate_sc ≥ 0.5.** Only 36 of 240 panel cos are agg-heavy. Small-n comparison.
- **Breadth_resid uses GLOBAL OLS fit.** Company-by-company residualization would be more principled but destroys the aggregate interpretation. Global fit means some short-desc cos have negatively-biased breadth_resid absolute levels, but Δ-within-company is unbiased.
- **Labeled-only panel shrinks panel from 240 to 196 cos.** 44 cos are entirely scraped-unlabeled or arshkon-unlabeled at the labeled-at-n≥2 threshold. Results are close enough (within-AI consistent) that this is a minor coverage issue.
- **Only 19 of 240 panel cos are flagged specialists.** The no-specialist sensitivity drops 19 cos; the finding survives. This means T06's 240 specialist flag is mostly NOT in the stable overlap panel — it's concentrated in new entrants and small-n cos.
