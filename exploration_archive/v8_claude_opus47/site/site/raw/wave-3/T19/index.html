    T19 — Temporal patterns¶

    Agent K, Wave 3. 2026-04-17. Input: data/unified.parquet.

    Headline (<200 words)¶

    Cross-period changes are NOT a linear continuation of within-2024 trends. AI-mention (strict) cross-period annualized rate (≈0.063/yr) is ~3.8× the within-2024 annualized rate (0.017/yr); AI-mention (broad) is ~2.7×. Description length, by contrast, DROPS within-2024 (arshkon is shorter than asaniczka) but RISES cross-period — sign flip, not acceleration. Tech-count median and org-scope median are stable within-2024 (no signal) but rise cross-period. Junior J2 share has the opposite pattern: within-2024 it appears to rise (asaniczka 0.013 → arshkon 0.041) at an implausible 0.11/yr annualized, then falls cross-period (arshkon 0.041 → scraped 0.027), giving a negative acceleration ratio. This reflects arshkon's LLM-frame seniority labeling artifact (V1-confirmed) rather than real junior flows. Within the scraped window, AI-mention is stable (CV 0.14), tech-count and breadth are stable (CV 0.04–0.06), and Sunday postings have elevated broad AI-mention (0.58 vs ~0.49 weekday). Scraper first day is a 1.6× backlog spike (2,448 vs 1,537 mean). posting_age_days is degenerate (all 1) — age distribution is not informative. Three snapshots bracket key model releases: Asaniczka 2024-01 pre-Claude-3, arshkon 2024-04 post-Claude-3 pre-GPT-4o, scraped 2026-03+04 post-Claude-4 and Gemini-2.5-Pro.

    Scope¶

    SWE-only temporal analysis (primary), with cross-group seniority views carried from T18.

    • SWE sample: 63,701 rows.
    • Periods: 2024-01 (asaniczka, N=18,129), 2024-04 (arshkon, N=4,691), 2026-03 (scraped, N=19,777), 2026-04 (scraped, N=21,104).
    • Scripts: exploration/scripts/T19_01_rate_of_change.py through T19_04_timeline_plot.py.
    • Tables: exploration/tables/T19/.
    • Figures: exploration/figures/T19/.

    Top 3 cross-period acceleration ratios¶

    1. ai_strict: 3.81× — AI-mention growth is ~4× faster cross-period than within-2024 would predict.
    2. ai_broad: 2.75× — broad AI framing accelerates but less dramatically.
    3. No other metric has a positive acceleration ratio > 1; the others are either sign-flipping (length) or flat within-2024.

    Interpretation¶

    The AI-specific language expansion is discontinuous relative to the within-2024 baseline — it is fundamentally a post-2024 phenomenon, not an extrapolation of a smooth 2024 trajectory. Description length has the opposite pattern: within-2024 it contracts (asaniczka → arshkon) and cross-period it expands; this tells us the within-2024 snapshots are not representative of a "posting verbosity" trajectory, just differences between two sources. Junior J2 share is contaminated by arshkon's labeling regime.

    Step 3 — Scraper yield (T19_scraper_yield_daily.csv)¶

    • First scrape day (2026-03-20): 2,448 SWE postings.
    • Mean of subsequent days: 1,537.
    • Ratio: 1.59×.

    First day is elevated but not pathologically so. Consistent with a backlog flush from initial LinkedIn scrape indexing — real effect, moderate magnitude. Down-weight first day when computing trends within-scraped.

    Also: lowest daily SWE counts fall on Mondays (2,179 weekly total, only 474 and 310 on 2026-04-06 and 2026-04-13 respectively) — pattern of Mondays being low-yield scrape days that needs investigation (possibly LinkedIn posting volume, possibly scraper timing).

    Step 4 — Posting age (T19_posting_age_distribution.csv)¶

    posting_age_days coverage: - Non-null n = 329 (out of 40,881 scraped SWE) - All values = 1 day - Distribution: degenerate (std = 0)

    posting_age_days is not a usable lifecycle signal at this coverage and cardinality. Action item: either the scraper is only retaining fresh postings (so age is always 1) or the field is not being populated for most rows. Downstream tasks should not use posting_age_days for ghost / lifecycle analysis without source validation.

    Step 6 — Timeline contextualization (T19_timeline.png, T19_timeline_daily_ai.csv)¶

    Snapshots relative to model-release chronology:

    • Asaniczka 2024-01 — pre-Claude-3 (March 2024), ~10 months post-GPT-4.
    • Arshkon 2024-04 — post-Claude-3 (March 2024), pre-GPT-4o (May 2024), pre-Claude-3.5-Sonnet (June 2024). Coding-agent wave not yet mainstream in postings.
    • Scraped 2026-03 + 2026-04 — post-o1 (Sep 2024), post-Claude-4 (Sep 2025), post-Gemini-2.5-Pro (Mar 2026). Cursor, Copilot, Claude-Code, Codex tools mature.

    The acceleration ratio >> 1 for AI-mention aligns temporally with the post-Claude-3.5-Sonnet / post-Cursor-adoption period. Not formal causal identification — just temporal coincidence worth flagging for the paper narrative.

    Sensitivity checks¶

    (e) Date-window effects: within-arshkon CV for ai_strict is 1.44 — high. Reporting arshkon as "a 2-week sample mid-range 0.014" rather than a point is advised. Within-asaniczka (Jan 12–17) is not separated here but the 5-day window is short enough that heterogeneity is likely smaller.

    (f) Seasonality / day-of-week: 7-day scraped window averaging absorbs day-of-week; within-2024 sources are single-week windows, so day-of-week is a nuisance variable. Monday+Sunday bias toward senior postings is flagged as a day-of-week sampling effect.

    Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. Acceleration ratio 3.8× for AI-strict. The AI-tool-vocabulary growth from 2024 → 2026 is NOT a linear continuation of within-2024 trajectory; it is a discrete post-2024 phenomenon. This is arguably the strongest piece of direct evidence in the project that something "stepped" after the within-2024 window ended.
    2. Description length sign flip: within-2024 length DROPS (asaniczka 3733 → arshkon 2974), cross-period length RISES. Any paper claim that "posting length grew 2024→2026" is actually measuring a cross-source artifact at the within-2024 level. Be careful when computing trend slopes.
    3. J2 share goes up then down: asaniczka 0.013 → arshkon 0.041 → scraped 0.027. Arshkon is the outlier. Consistent with V1's LLM-frame J2 flip finding — arshkon's labeled seniority is systematically different from other sources.
    4. Tech-count median is flat within-2024 (4→4) and then rises (5). The median is less sensitive than the mean; discrete unit changes tell you that the "typical" SWE posting now has one more tech token. This is consistent with the mean-level DiD in T18.
    5. Sunday broad AI-mention is 0.58 vs weekday 0.48–0.52. Weekend postings are AI-heavier. Possibly because AI-forward startups post on weekends more than traditional employers do, or because Friday/Saturday scheduling pushes high-priority postings into weekend scrape runs.
    6. posting_age_days is degenerate. 329/40,881 non-null, all value 1. A scheduled task should populate this field properly if it is to drive any lifecycle analysis.
    7. Within-scraped stability is solid for content metrics but poor for J2. CV 0.04–0.14 for AI-mention/tech/breadth means we can treat "scraped" as a coherent window; CV 0.54 for J2 means seniority cells are still underpowered.
    8. Low scraper yield on Mondays (474, 310 on sample Mondays). Needs infra investigation; is this LinkedIn volume reality, or scraper cadence?

    Data-quality caveats¶

    • posting_age_days unusable — flag for pipeline.
    • arshkon within-window heterogeneity — treat as 2-week sample.
    • Within-2024 calibration uses asaniczka Jan and arshkon April, ~3-month gap. All within-2024 annualizations assume linearity over a very short window — they are not reliable trend extrapolations, they are calibration checks.
    • Cross-period annualization uses a single 2-year midpoint diff; with only two periods we cannot distinguish a step at a specific time from a smooth 2-year trend. The acceleration ratio is a comparison of slopes, not a test of step timing.

    Action items (downstream)¶

    • Wave 4 synthesis: quote the 3.8× / 2.75× acceleration ratios as the cleanest temporal evidence that the AI-mention shift is post-2024.
    • T30 / seniority work: continue to flag arshkon J1/J2 as LLM-frame-affected; use scraped J2 (0.03) as the more reliable 2026 value.
    • Scraper owner: investigate posting_age_days (all-1 degeneracy) and Monday-volume asymmetry; re-populate age if needed.
    • Paper narrative: timeline figure shows the three snapshots against model releases — usable for the introduction/setup figure.
