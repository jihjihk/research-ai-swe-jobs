    T21 — Senior role evolution 2024 → 2026¶

    Agent: L (Wave 3) Date: 2026-04-17 Primary slice: SWE, LinkedIn, text_source='llm', known-seniority. Senior = mid-senior + director. Junior = entry + associate. Primary senior baseline per Wave 3 steering: arshkon-only 2024 (n=2,066 senior) vs scraped 2026 (n=6,164 senior). Secondary: pooled 2024 (arshkon + asaniczka = 12,823 senior). Sample sizes: 2024 mid-senior 12,724, director 99 (arshkon-only mid-senior 2,053, director 13). 2026 mid-senior 6,052, director 112. Patterns (post precision check, see §1): mgmt-strict, orch-strict, orch-broad (adds agent), strat-strict, strat-broad (adds stakeholder). Scripts: exploration/scripts/T21_precision_check.py, T21_build_densities.py, T21_deep_analysis.py, T21_figures.py. Tables: exploration/tables/T21/ (9 CSVs). Figures: exploration/figures/T21/ (4 PNG).

    Headline findings¶

    1. The management-orchestration-strategic shift happened at SENIOR but largely rode a broader corpus-wide mentor-language expansion that reached every level. Mentor-binary share at mid-senior rose 0.188 → 0.324 (+13.6pp, ratio 1.73×); at entry only 0.075 → 0.080 (+0.5pp, ratio 1.07×); at associate fell 0.128 → 0.078 (−5.0pp). Mentor rose disproportionately at mid-senior and director, minimally at entry. The "senior archetype shift toward mentoring" claim from Gate 2 SURVIVES the cross-seniority check: 2026 mid-senior absorbed 13× more mentor-rate growth than entry did. Cross-seniority rate ratios: entry 1.07×, associate 0.61×, mid-senior 1.73×, director 1.30×.
    2. Strategic-scope density nearly doubled at director (0.42 → 0.61, +19pp), rose substantially at mid-senior (0.22 → 0.34, +12pp). Orchestration-strict binary share at senior: mid-senior 0.30 → 0.51 (+21pp), director 0.28 → 0.56 (+28pp). Director-vs-mid-senior strat-broad ratio was 1.94× in 2024, 1.78× in 2026 — director remains the strat-heavy role but the gap closed as mid-seniors absorbed strat language.
    3. Precision check drops: agent 44% strict, stakeholder 42% strict, team_building 10% — all excluded from strict patterns. pipeline 92% — retained. Full strict orchestration pattern excludes agent; broad includes it for sensitivity. Full strict strategic pattern excludes stakeholder; broad includes it. 2026 binary-share deltas are large enough that the strict-vs-broad choice does not change the verdict.
    4. Four sub-archetypes emerge by k-means (k=4) on senior-density features. The emergent "mgmt+orch+strat+AI" cluster is 97% 2026, n=860, and has mean AI-mention share 1.00. Cluster composition: baseline_ic (n=4,795, 70% 2026, AI 0%); mgmt_orch (n=1,523, 78% 2026, mentor 96%, AI 0%); strat_ai (n=1,052, 76% 2026); mgmt_orch_strat_ai (n=860, 97% 2026, AI 100%, mentor 39%). The 4th cluster barely existed in 2024 (n=30) and is now a substantial senior sub-segment (n=830 in 2026).
    5. AI-mentioning senior postings have HIGHER mgmt-binary (0.41 vs 0.32) and HIGHER orch (0.86 vs 0.47 density units) than non-AI senior postings in 2026. In 2024 the pattern was reversed-ish (AI-senior had similar mgmt, less mgmt density). AI mention in 2026 is correlated with managerial/orchestration language, not replacing it. 2026 senior AI share: 13.6% (vs 0.9% in 2024, 15.2× rise).
    6. Staff-titled 2026 senior postings have +16pp higher mgmt-binary (0.48 vs 0.32), +4pp higher orch (0.55 vs 0.51), +11pp higher strat-strict (0.23 vs 0.12), +16pp higher mentor (0.47 vs 0.30), and +4.6pp higher AI (0.18 vs 0.13) than non-staff seniors. Staff-title postings in 2026 concentrate all four signals. The staff title doubled 2.6% → 6.3% (T10). Staff is an emergent senior archetype that bundles mentorship + orchestration + strategy + AI.
    7. ML/AI's senior density shift dominates every metric. Δ AI-mention 2026−2024 by archetype: ml_ai +29.9pp, frontend +12.9pp, data_eng +9.5pp, generic +3.3pp. Δ orch-strict: ml_ai +39.3pp, data_eng +31.1pp, frontend +28.1pp. Δ mgmt-binary: frontend +22.9pp, ml_ai +18.0pp, java +14.6pp. Shift is NOT uniform — concentrated in ML/AI for AI and orchestration, more even for management.

    1. Profile densities per 1K chars cleaned text (Step 1)¶

    1a. Pattern precision check (strict V1-style)¶

    Sampled 50 hits per sub-term (25 from 2024, 25 from 2026), senior-posting subset, auto-classified via rule-based TP/FP/AMBIG with keyword windows. Protocol in T21_precision_check.py. Full samples in exploration/artifacts/T21/T21_precision_samples.csv.

    Sub-term TP FP AMBIG precision_strict precision_relaxed Verdict
    \bagent(s)?\b 22 3 25 44% 88% DROP (strict)
    \bpipeline(s)?\b 46 0 4 92% 100% KEEP
    \bstakeholders?\b 21 0 29 42% 100% DROP (strict)
    \bteam[- ]?building\b 3 0 27 10% 100% DROP

    Notes: strict precision treats AMBIG as non-TP (conservative); relaxed = TP/(TP+FP) ignores AMBIG. agent AMBIG are mostly bare mentions without enough context in a ±90-char window to confirm AI-agent vs user-agent/insurance-agent; relaxed precision 88% would pass if we counted confidently-not-FP as TP, but we apply the 80% strict floor. stakeholder AMBIG means no role-scope keyword in window — stakeholder is used as a generic noun too often to serve as a strategic scope signal without more context. pipeline is robust: only 4 AMBIG, 0 FP in senior context (CI/data/ML contexts dominate).

    1b. Final strict patterns¶

    people_management_strict   = \b(mentor|coach|hire|headcount|performance[- ]?review)\w*
technical_orchestration_strict = \b(architecture review|code review|system design|technical direction|
                                    ai orchestration|workflow|pipeline|automation|evaluate|validate|
                                    quality gate|guardrails|prompt engineering|tool selection)\w*
technical_orchestration_broad  = orchestration_strict | \bagent(s)?\b   (sensitivity only)
strategic_scope_strict     = \b(business impact|revenue|product strategy|roadmap|prioritization|
                                resource allocation|budgeting|cross[- ]functional alignment)\w*
strategic_scope_broad      = strat_strict | \bstakeholders?\b  (sensitivity only)

    Management retains the V1-refined pattern (mentor|coach|hire|headcount|performance_review). The precision-verified mentor carries 13.9% → 20.8% of the senior sample (see §7) and dominates the management signal as Gate 2 flagged.

    4. AI interaction: senior + AI-mentioning vs non-AI (Step 4)¶

    From T21_ai_interaction.csv. AI-mention share at senior level rose 0.9% (2024) → 13.6% (2026), a 15.2× rise.

    Period AI n mgmt-bin orch-strict-dens strat-broad-dens
    2024 +AI 115 0.130 0.428 0.156
    2024 −AI 12,708 0.199 0.253 0.179
    2026 +AI 838 0.414 0.857 0.306
    2026 −AI 5,326 0.325 0.470 0.265

    In 2024 AI-mentioning senior postings had lower management binary than non-AI (0.130 vs 0.199). In 2026 the pattern reverses: AI-mentioning seniors have +9pp more management and 1.8× higher orchestration density. AI adoption in 2026 is not "AI replacing managerial work" but "AI showing up alongside more managerial language," consistent with the emergent sub-archetype in §3.

    Orchestration density in AI-mentioning 2026 seniors (0.857) is 1.8× the non-AI 2026 senior mean (0.470) and 3.4× the 2024 AI-mentioning value (0.428). AI postings carry specifically the orchestration signature (workflow/pipeline/automation/guardrails).

    9. Surprises¶

    1. Associate mentor-binary FELL from 0.128 (2024) to 0.078 (2026). In every other seniority level mentor rose; in associate it dropped. The associate cell is small (39/51) so treat as directional, but the pattern is consistent with T10 finding of "associate label shrinking/retreating" — 2026 associate-labeled postings are becoming a narrower, more-IC-ish niche rather than a junior-mentorship role.
    2. Director mentor-binary rose less (+6.5pp) than mid-senior mentor-binary (+13.6pp). In 2024 director mentor > mid-senior mentor; in 2026 director < mid-senior. Mentorship language has migrated from director-coded to mid-senior-coded. Mid-senior is now the primary owner of "mentor junior engineers" language.
    3. AI-mentioning senior postings in 2024 had lower mgmt-binary (0.130 vs 0.199 non-AI). In 2024 AI postings skewed toward research/IC roles. The flip in 2026 (AI-senior 0.414 mgmt vs non-AI 0.325 mgmt) represents the emergence of a new archetype — AI deployed with management scope rather than instead of management scope.
    4. The emergent "mgmt_orch_strat_ai" sub-archetype (cluster 2) barely existed in 2024 (n=30) and now has 860 members, 97% of whom are 2026. This is a cleanly emergent senior sub-archetype — if I had to name one "new senior role" that the Wave 2 data supports, this is it. AI + orchestration + moderate management + modest strategy. Staff-title is the job-title handle for approximately this content profile.
    5. Precision of stakeholder as a strategic-scope term is only 42%. Using it in strat-broad inflates strategic-scope-binary by ~10pp relative to strat-strict across all 2026 seniority cells — but the direction of the shift is identical in both patterns, so the choice doesn't change conclusions. stakeholder is an excellent sensitivity sub-term but a bad primary.
    6. Systems-engineering is the one domain that did not participate in the senior archetype shift. AI delta 0, mgmt delta +0.7pp. The "AI restructuring" narrative does not apply uniformly — systems/embedded senior roles look essentially the same 2024 → 2026. This is a natural control domain for RQ4 interviews (ask a systems senior "did AI change your role?" and expect "no"; ask an ML/AI senior and expect "yes").
    7. Staff-title profile is more distinctively "senior-archetype-heavy" than the director title itself is. 2026 staff mgmt-binary 0.478 > director 0.304; staff orch 0.550 ~ director 0.563; staff strat-strict 0.229 < director 0.268; staff AI 0.176 < director 0.232. Staff is not a "director-lite" label — staff title bundles the senior archetypes in a different mix than director does, leaning more on management and mentorship. Staff is an engineering-track senior archetype; director is a management-track senior archetype. Both grew 2024→2026, but staff grew faster (2.6% → 6.3% share per T10) and carries the "new senior IC" content profile.
