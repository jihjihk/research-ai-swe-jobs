    T28 — Domain-stratified scope changes (archetype-projected)¶

    Agent: O (Wave 3) Run date: 2026-04-17 Sample: 34,102 SWE LinkedIn postings with text_source='llm' (the only slice with reliable content features). Archetype labels from T09's 22-class taxonomy, projected onto the 34k LLM corpus via nearest-centroid over 384-dim MiniLM embeddings. Primary junior slice: J2 (seniority_final ∈ {entry, associate}). Primary senior slice: S1 (seniority_final ∈ {mid-senior, director}). Patterns: V1-refined AI-strict / AI-broad / mentor (per Gate 2 LOCK). requirement_breadth length-residualized via OLS on the 2024 LLM baseline (beta0=-15.00, beta1=3.29, R^2=0.16).

    1. Headline¶

    The +12.7pp AI-STRICT rise is broadly distributed across domains but amplified ~2x in the AI/ML archetype, not concentrated there. All 20 archetypes with n>=100 in both periods show a positive strict AI-mention Δ, 18 of 20 exceed +5pp, and 15 of 20 exceed +10pp. The AI/ML archetype has the largest absolute share (45% in 2026) and the largest absolute Δ (+26.4pp), but removing it from the aggregate would drop the corpus Δ from +12.5pp to roughly +10pp, not halve it. The "cross-domain AI insertion" framing from Gate 1 is supported.

    The 2024→2026 AI/ML archetype explosion (2.6%→16.2% share, +13.6pp) is driven by NEW employers, not by existing employers pivoting. 935 companies appear in AI/ML only in 2026 (n=1,634 postings), versus 83 present-both employers contributing 380 postings in 2026. Existing-both contributed just 23% of the 2026 AI/ML volume; new entrants contributed 81% (Google, Anthropic, Uber, JPMC, Microsoft AI, Alignerr, General Motors, Scale AI, Mercor are all zero-2024 here under LinkedIn SWE-filter).

    Within-archetype entry-share rise continues to dominate. Updated J2 decomposition on the full LLM corpus: +2.80pp within-archetype + 0.24pp between-archetype + 0.28pp interaction = +3.32pp aggregate; within-share = 84.3% — essentially identical to T09's 84.7% on the 8k sample. The within-domain result is not a sampling artifact.

    3. Entry-share decomposition (shift-share)¶

    Definitions: - p_k = archetype share; π_k = within-archetype entry share (J2 or J3). - within_k = p_k(2024) * (π_k(2026) - π_k(2024)) - between_k = π_k(2024) * (p_k(2026) - p_k(2024)) - interaction_k = (p_k(2026) - p_k(2024)) * (π_k(2026) - π_k(2024))

    Top within-archetype contributors to J2 rise: generic_software_engineer (+0.72pp), frontend_react (+0.27pp), dotnet_web_sql (+0.27pp, despite the archetype shrinking), systems_engineering (+0.25pp).

    J3 (yoe_numeric <= 2, yoe-known subset, n=23,429):

    component value share of Δ

    J3 within-archetype rise is even stronger than J2; generic_software_engineer alone contributes +2.40pp. Negative interaction reflects that growing archetypes (AI/ML, backend_platform) happen to have moderate J3 shares that didn't grow as fast within, while shrinking archetypes had large within-archetype rises.

    Confirmation: The 84.7% within-archetype figure from T09 on the 8k sample survives replication on the full 34k LLM corpus (84.3% J2; 102% J3). Junior-share rise is not composition.

    Verdict on mechanism. The rise is NOT concentrated in AI/ML — AI-STRICT growth is broadly distributed. AI/ML contributes disproportionately (it's ~2x larger than the average archetype's delta), but the corpus aggregate would still be ~+10pp without it. The cross-domain AI-insertion framing is supported.

    5. Domain-stratified scope (breadth, tech_count, org_scope)¶

    Length-residualized requirement_breadth Δ (positive = more content per log-length):

    • AI/ML engineering: +2.80 (SNR 11.0) — largest
    • java_spring_backend: +1.94 (SNR 8.1)
    • qa_test_automation: +2.15 (SNR 5.7)
    • cloud_devops: +1.39 (SNR 8.1)
    • azure_dotnet_web: +2.82 (SNR 6.5)
    • angular_frontend: +2.77 (SNR 5.5)
    • recommendation_ecommerce: +0.52 (weak)
    • systems_engineering: +0.70 (smallest of top-10)

    Tech-count Δ (mean technologies cited): +0.5 to +3.0 per archetype; AI/ML +2.17 (SNR 11.1), azure_dotnet_web +2.42 (SNR 6.2), angular_frontend +3.03 (SNR 6.5).

    org_scope_count Δ: +0.16 to +0.64 across archetypes — universally positive. Largest: technology_leadership (+0.64), backend_platform (+0.56), angular_frontend (+0.48), AI/ML (+0.44). This is the "how many boxes the JD talks about" metric and it grew in every archetype.

    credential_stack_depth Δ: +0.13 to +1.08 per archetype; golang_cloud (+1.08), azure_dotnet_web (+0.99), recommendation_ecommerce (+0.95), dotnet_web_sql (+0.81). AI/ML modest (+0.37).

    desc_len_chars Δ: +85 to +593 chars. AI/ML +442, recommendation_ecommerce +594, azure_dotnet_web +364, customer_project_engineer +241. Systems_engineering tiny (+85). Description-length inflation is real and universal but bounded. (Note: scraped 2026 raw descriptions are much longer at ~3.0K median chars vs. ~1.5K LLM-cleaned; see T29 authorship for that story.)

    Interpretation. Scope inflation under length-residualization is universal and sizeable across archetypes. It is not purely a "JDs got longer" artifact. T11's aggregate breadth rise (+1.0 resid) is conservative; every major archetype shows a rise of +0.7 to +2.8 in length-residualized breadth.

    6. Junior vs senior gap by archetype¶

    J2 cells are small in many archetypes in 2024, so gap-change estimates have high variance. Reading the larger-cell archetypes where both J2 and S1 have n>=50 in both periods:

    • ai_ml_engineering: J2 breadth 11.8→13.4 (+1.5), S1 11.2→15.0 (+3.8). Gap flipped from J2-lean (-0.66) to S1-lean (+1.60); gap widened by +2.26. Senior AI/ML roles got much more content-heavy, juniors less so.
    • generic_software_engineer: J2 breadth 7.5→8.7, S1 7.96→9.25. Gap stable (+0.51 → +0.54).
    • backend_platform: J2 9.4→11.1, S1 10.2→12.0. Gap flat (+0.80 → +0.85).
    • cloud_devops: J2 14.8→12.5 (n 16→32), S1 12.1→14.2. Gap flipped from -2.7 to +1.7 — but J2 2024 n=16 is small.
    • data_engineering: J2 9.1→9.4 (n 36→45), S1 10.5→11.9. Gap grew from +1.45 to +2.52.

    Mentor rate (J2 vs S1): J2 mentor mentions went from 6.4% to 16.2% (+9.8pp), S1 from 19.1% to 36.0% (+16.9pp). Mentor expansion is larger in senior roles, but juniors also gained significant mentor language.

    Summary. The junior-senior content gap is closing in a few archetypes (AI/ML senior grew fastest, but J2 growth there was also non-trivial; qa_test_automation gap closed), but in most the gap is stable or widening in favor of seniors. AI/ML is the clearest "senior role redefinition" case in the archetype dataset.

    9. Surprises¶

    1. Systems engineering's AI-STRICT stayed at zero (0.08%→0.24%, Δ=+0.16pp, SNR 0.6). Of all 20 primary archetypes, only systems_engineering failed to show a meaningful AI-named-tool rise. Hardware/firmware roles appear genuinely unaffected by LLM tooling in the named-tool sense. This is a clean natural-experiment "control" and is important for the paper.

    2. AI/ML volume explosion is 81% new entrants, not existing-firm pivoting. The T09 insight ("AI/ML expansion is a supply-side response") lands strongly: Google, Anthropic, Microsoft AI, Uber, JPMC, General Motors, Scale AI, Mercor, Alignerr are all zero-in-2024 for this archetype under our LinkedIn SWE filter. The "pivot" story is much smaller than the "new entrant" story.

    3. AWS cloud services mentor-rate fell (-2.8pp) — against the corpus tide. Combined with AWS cloud services' -2.2pp share drop, this suggests the archetype itself is fragmenting: some postings are being reclassified as cloud_devops or backend_platform where mentor-rate is rising.

    4. Python backend developer AI-STRICT +14.4pp — higher than AI/ML from its lower base. Python backend is becoming an AI-adjacent archetype and might deserve its own analysis cut in the paper.

    5. Description length inflation is uniform: every archetype grew +85 to +594 chars in desc_len, with the smallest growth in systems_engineering. This is the "JDs got longer" story that must be handled via the length-residualization (done in Section 5) before making content claims.

    6. Tech leadership mentor Δ +4.4pp is unusually small given that tech_leadership archetype JDs are by construction about leading others. 2024 baseline was already 38% vs corpus 14%; there's simply less headroom, and 2026 tech_leadership mentor is 43% — still the highest in the corpus.

    7. Aggregators confound AI/ML top-employer ranks. "Jobs via Dice" 67, "myGwork" 11, "Jobs for Humanity" 12 (in 2024 only), "hackajob" 10 — these are not the employer, and they spiked in 2026. Same for staffing "CyberCoders" (12 2024, 7 2026). This suggests aggregator weight rose in AI/ML specifically, and any employer-level analysis of AI/ML must remove them.

    10. Methodology notes¶

    Archetype projection. T09 labeled 8,000 postings into 22 archetypes via BERTopic + domain seed. We projected those labels to the full 34,102 LLM corpus via nearest-centroid in the 384-dim MiniLM embedding space. Sanity check: projection accuracy on the 8k labeled rows (which are members of the 34k) is 99.6% (script log). This is high because the 8k are themselves used to compute centroids, but the 26k out-of-sample rows use the same centroids, and the resulting archetype distribution is stable and interpretable.

    Length residualization. requirement_breadth_resid = breadth - (β0 + β1 * log(desc_len)) with β0, β1 fit on the 2024 LLM baseline. β1=3.29 means breadth grows roughly as the 3.29th-power of log-length (linear in log). R^2=0.16 — length explains 16% of breadth variance; the rest is content.

    LLM rows only. This report uses text_source='llm' rows (34,102). The 29,599 text_source='raw' rows (mainly the 2026 raw-scraped corpus) are excluded because their unprocessed text confounds sentence / breadth / tech_count metrics. This is the same choice made in T09/T11/V1.

    What was NOT done in T28. Archetype-by-archetype cross-posting-uniformity (whether copies of the same JD cluster within an archetype) is left for follow-up. Interaction between AI-STRICT and company panel is done in T29 Section 6 (correlations), not here.
