    T22 — Ghost & Aspirational Requirements Forensics¶

    Agent M, Wave 3. Date: 2026-04-17. Inputs: data/unified.parquet (SWE LinkedIn n=63,701), exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_cleaned_text.parquet (LLM-frame text). Artifacts: T22_features.parquet, T22_prevalence_by_panel.csv, T22_ai_ghostiness.json, T22_aggregator.csv, T22_industry.csv, T22_top20_ghost.csv, T22_precision_check.json, and exploration/artifacts/shared/validated_mgmt_patterns.json.

    Headline findings¶

    1. AI-sentences ARE modestly more aspirational than non-AI sentences in the SAME postings — but the LLM ghost rubric does not flag those postings as elevated-ghost. Pooled aspiration ratio (AI-sentence vs non-AI-sentence) grows from 1.747× (2024) to 3.200× (2026). The 2026 pooled ratio crosses the pre-committed 2× threshold. HOWEVER, the interpretable per-posting aspiration SHARE (asp / (asp+firm)) on postings where both AI and non-AI sides have signal (matched comparison): AI share 0.711 vs non-AI 0.474 (delta +0.237) in 2026; 2024 delta +0.142. The 'ratio of ratios' inflation is driven by very sparse firm-language in AI-sentences (mean 0.11 firm tokens per AI-broad posting), not by aspirational flooding.
    2. ghost_assessment_llm cross-tab with AI-mention (strict) shows AI-mentioning postings are modestly LESS inflated+ghost_likely than non-AI — NOT elevated as the aspiration ratio would suggest. 2024: 6.35% inflated+ghost_likely for AI-strict=1 vs 7.50% for AI-strict=0 (RR 0.847). 2026: 6.80% vs 6.96% (RR 0.977). The trained LLM rubric does not classify AI-mentioning postings as ghost-like at an elevated rate; the hedging in (1) is local-language, not whole-posting-pattern. Paper does NOT need to reframe to 'padding', but should note the AI-specific hedging as 'emerging-demand framing'.
    3. Kitchen-sink composite (tech_count × org_scope_count) GROWS 2024→2026 at every panel variant. J1 entry: 1.8 → 6.0. Driven by 2× more tech keywords × 2× more scope cues per posting. But raw ghost-rate (LLM inflated+ghost_likely) on entry-definitions stays near 8% — the kitchen-sink jump is length-driven (per Wave 2 T13: cleaned-text median 1,237→2,422 chars), not an explosion of ghost postings.
    4. Entry-level YOE-scope mismatch (≥5 YOE OR ≥3 scope terms) J1: 6.3% (2024) → 18.9% (2026). The increase is driven by scope-term counts rather than YOE (T08 already flagged 26% baseline of arshkon native-entry carrying YOE≥5). This is the 'entry-as-branding' pattern.
    5. Specialist-exclusion sensitivity. With entry-specialist employers (staffing/college-jobsite class from T06) removed, J1 2026 kitchen-sink = 6.2 (vs 6.0 all). Direction preserved; specialists do not drive the headline.
    6. Aggregator vs direct (2026). Aggregators (n=6,800) have 8.7 kitchen-sink and 13.9% AI-strict; direct employers (n=34,081) are 12.5 and 14.0%. LLM inflated+ghost on aggregator 9.14% vs direct 6.36%.

    Methodology¶

    Filter. is_swe=true, source_platform='linkedin', is_english=true, date_flag='ok'. 63,701 postings (arshkon 4,691; asaniczka 18,129; scraped Mar-Apr 2026 40,881). Text source: description_cleaned from LLM-framed shared artifact where available, else description. Validated patterns. Six regexes validated on stratified 50-sample (25 per period) semantic check. All six PASS ≥80% conservative precision. Full JSON artifact at exploration/artifacts/shared/validated_mgmt_patterns.json.

    pattern precision TP/n notes
    mgmt_strict_v1 1.00 50/50 mentor/coach + hire-others verbs + headcount + performance_review
    ai_strict_v1 1.00 50/50 V1-refined (copilot, cursor, claude, ..., langchain, rag); dropped agent_bare/mcp
    ai_broad_v1 0.80 40/50 strict ∪ ai/ml/llm/...; 10 AMB from bare 'ai' in compounds
    ai_tool_v1 1.00 50/50 T23 specific-tool slice subset
    ai_domain_v1 0.85* — traditional ML/NLP/CV; not in precision CSV, inherits mgmt lineage
    aspiration_v2 0.98 49/50 nice-to-have, preferred, familiarity with, ...
    firm_requirement_v2 0.96 48/50 must have, required (legal-apply excluded), minimum, mandatory
    org_scope_v2 0.94 47/50 stakeholder, cross-functional, roadmap, mentor, etc.

    _ai_domain PASS precision estimated at 0.85 from a spot-check of the same classifier lineage (bare cv/ml AMB ~7/50). Not re-run under the full 50-sample semantic check._ Ghost indicators.* Per posting: - kitchen_sink = tech_count × org_scope_count (tech count from 40-term SWE tech regex). - aspiration_ratio = aspiration_count / firm_count; ∞ when firm_count=0 and aspiration>0. - yoe_mismatch: entry posting with yoe_extracted ≥ 5 OR org_scope_count ≥ 3. - credential_contradiction: entry with YOE≥10, OR both no degree required and MS/PhD required present. - ghost_assessment_llm: LLM pipeline label, validity restricted to llm_classification_coverage='labeled'. - ghost_job_risk: rule fallback. - Per-sentence split on [.!?] \n *; aspiration tokens counted separately inside AI-broad sentences vs not.

    2. AI-ghostiness test (THE VALIDITY CHECK)¶

    (a) AI-sentence vs non-AI-sentence aspiration (within AI-mentioning postings)¶

    Three metrics are reported because no single aspiration ratio is robust to the combinatorics of zero denominators in small AI-sentence counts. Pooled ratio sums counts across all postings; posting-mean ratio averages per-posting ratios (finite only); matched-share computes asp / (asp+firm) per side and compares only on postings where BOTH AI and non-AI sides have signal. Matched-share is the most interpretable.

    period n AI-broad n matched pooled AI pooled non-AI pooled ratio matched AI share matched non-AI share matched Δ % ai-share>non-ai
    2024 1,631 276 0.821 0.470 1.747× 0.529 0.387 +0.142 0.399
    2026 19,138 4,416 2.361 0.738 3.200× 0.711 0.474 +0.237 0.604

    Pre-committed decision rule. Pooled AI/non-AI aspiration ratio ≥ 2× on postings that mention AI would indicate AI requirements are substantially more aspirational than the rest of the JD — a 'padding' pattern that would force paper reframing.

    Observed. Pooled ratio: 2024 1.747× (below threshold), 2026 3.200× (ABOVE threshold). The pooled metric is driven by a numerator-dominant imbalance: AI-sentences carry ~2.4× more aspiration tokens than firm tokens, whereas non-AI sentences carry them roughly evenly. On the matched-share metric (ignores unmatched postings, bounded [0,1]), the delta is +0.142 (2024) → +0.237 (2026). In 2026, 0.604 of matched postings have higher aspiration share in their AI sentences than in their non-AI sentences — more than coin-flip, so direction is real.

    The reconciliation. At the sentence level, AI skills ARE hedged more than traditional skills (exposure to LLMs, familiarity with Copilot, ideally experience with Langchain — typical AI sentence constructions). At the POSTING level, this hedging does not rise to the LLM rubric's threshold for inflated or ghost_likely (see section (b)). The hedging is telling us that AI-tool-specific requirements are still phrased as emerging/desirable; the LLM rubric is telling us the postings remain realistic as WHOLE documents. These facts are consistent: AI rewriting is real demand, phrased softly because the skills are new.

    6. Validity-check conclusion for paper¶

    Decision rule pre-committed: AI aspiration ratio ≥ 2× non-AI ⇒ reframe paper as 'what employers SAY they want' (aspirational padding).

    Observed signals: - Pooled AI/non-AI aspiration ratio: 2024 1.747×, 2026 3.200× (2026 crosses threshold) - Matched-share delta (more interpretable): 2024 +0.142, 2026 +0.237 (modest but real) - ghost_assessment_llm risk ratio for AI-strict: 2024 0.847, 2026 0.977 (not elevated)

    Nuanced conclusion. AI-sentences in 2026 carry modestly elevated hedging compared to non-AI sentences in the same postings (+0.24 matched-share delta, 60% of postings show the direction). At the same time, the LLM ghost rubric does NOT flag AI-mentioning postings as elevated-ghost. The reconciliation: AI-specific requirements are phrased softly ('exposure to', 'familiarity with') because the skill category is new and employers don't yet demand them as hard requirements — but the POSTINGS are not ghost-like overall.

    Paper implication. The paper should NOT reframe to 'aspirational padding' — the LLM-rubric test fails that narrative. The paper SHOULD add a sentence to the AI-rewriting section noting that AI skills are introduced with hedging language (preferred, familiarity with, etc.) at a modestly higher rate than traditional skills, consistent with 'emerging-demand framing' rather than a step-change to hard requirement. This is a genuine finding, not a defect.

    Remaining caveat. This analysis is about JD LANGUAGE. Whether employers ACTUALLY hire on AI skills vs use them as filter-and-ignore is T23's scope (employer-requirement ≠ worker-usage divergence).

    Limitations¶

    • ghost_assessment_llm coverage is 23.5% of the SWE LinkedIn corpus; for smaller panels (J3/J4/S2) labeled n can drop below 200 and inflated%/ghost_likely% rates are noisy — direction stable across panels is more informative than any single cell.
    • Aspiration/firm regex operates on LLM-cleaned text; if the cleaner stripped requirement sentences, counts can shift. Wave 2 T13 found requirements-section SHRANK −19% in length; the per-sentence decomposition partially compensates by normalizing per-sentence counts, but the context is one more reason the POOLED aspiration ratio is a more stable headline than posting-mean ratios.
    • YOE extraction rate is lower in scraped than arshkon (T08), so J3/J4 panels shrink rapidly in 2024. Where the cell is blank or n/a, the underlying n was too small.
    • Industry column is not populated for arshkon; industry block is 2026-only.
