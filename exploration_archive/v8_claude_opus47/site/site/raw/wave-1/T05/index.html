    T05 — Cross-dataset comparability¶

    Agent: C (Wave 1) Date: 2026-04-17 Scope: SWE, LinkedIn, English, date_flag='ok' unless stated. Three sources compared: kaggle_arshkon, kaggle_asaniczka, scraped (LinkedIn). Indeed sensitivity added for step 9. Input: data/unified.parquet. All queries via DuckDB. Script: exploration/scripts/T05_cross_dataset.py (+ T05_T06_figures.py).

    Headline finding¶

    The three sources are not measuring the same posting population. Every cross-source comparison rejects the null of identical distributions at p ≈ 0, and the within-2024 calibration (arshkon vs asaniczka) shows that the between-instrument effect is typically larger than any cross-period effect we observe. The J1 entry-share gap between arshkon and asaniczka (2024 only, h = 0.18) is 5× the cross-period arshkon-vs-scraped J1 gap (h = 0.03) — so the "entry share rose in 2026" or "entry share fell in 2026" claim cannot be defended from these numbers alone.

    Platform labeling stability is weak. For the 20 SWE titles that appear both in arshkon and in scraped-LinkedIn, seniority_native = 'entry' fell in 14/20 and rose in 6/20 even when the same title was compared, while mean extracted YOE rose in 12/20 and fell in 8/20. The joint pattern — native entry rate down, YOE up — points at LinkedIn relabeling more than at a uniform market content shift.

    Indeed cross-validation partially rescues LinkedIn. Indeed scraped (rule-based seniority only, high unknown rate) shows J1 = 3.05% and J3 (YOE ≤ 2) = 18.1%, both within noise of LinkedIn scraped (J1 = 3.12%, J3 = 17.2%). The LinkedIn-specific artifact hypothesis is NOT supported for entry-share levels, but the within-2024 noise remains the dominant robustness concern.

    Most-trustworthy cross-source comparisons: company identity (Jaccard stable and interpretable), state-share direction (top metros reorder predictably), mean YOE (small 2024-to-2026 movement, mostly within noise). Least trustworthy: native-seniority-based entry shares and rates of any text-derived keyword density (description length grew ~1.5× between 2024 and 2026).

    Pairwise Kolmogorov-Smirnov on 20k-row samples:

    a b KS stat p
    arshkon asaniczka 0.255 4.1e-3
    arshkon scraped 0.319 4.8e-5

    All pairwise differences reject the null. Median grew 64% from arshkon (2,974 chars) to scraped (4,862 chars). See figures/T05/01_length_hist.png.

    Artifact risk: Instrument-driven. Kaggle arshkon/asaniczka were HTML-stripped and dedup was more aggressive; scraped preserves markdown formatting including explicit section headers, bullet points, and benefits blocks. Text-density metrics (mentions per 1K characters) built from the 2024-to-2026 contrast will automatically under-count 2026 density relative to 2024 unless normalized against description_core_llm.

    7. Artifact diagnostic + within-2024 calibration¶

    This is the key table. Cohen's h for three share-based metrics, under each pairwise comparison:

    Metric within-2024 h (arsh vs asa) arsh vs scraped h pool-2024 vs scraped h SNR (cross / within)
    J1 — seniority_final = entry 0.176 0.034 −0.097 0.19
    J2 — entry ∪ associate 0.181 0.043 −0.091 0.24
    J3 — yoe_extracted ≤ 2 0.139 −0.060 −0.172 0.43

    Signal-to-noise ratio is below 2 for all three — well below the materiality threshold. Arshkon J1 (3.73%) is higher than both asaniczka J1 (1.13%) and scraped J1 (3.12%); the pooled 2024-vs-scraped comparison actually runs negative (2024 had higher J1 entry share than 2026) when asaniczka is weighted in.

    Direction under different operationalizations:

    arshkon asaniczka scraped
    J1 (entry %) 3.73% 1.13% 3.12%
    J2 (entry∪assoc %) 4.05% 1.26% 3.25%
    J3 (YOE ≤ 2, of YOE-known) 14.98% 10.36% 17.19%

    J3 alone shows a positive 2024-to-2026 shift (+2pp arshkon-vs-scraped; +7pp pooled-vs-scraped), and that's also the metric least dependent on native-seniority labeling. Two plausible readings: 1. The JYP shift is real — scraped postings shifted their YOE floor downward. 2. The JYP shift is artifact — the scraped text preserves more boilerplate including junior-ish phrasing that the rule-based YOE parser picks up.

    Sensitivity-disagreement drill-in¶

    The dominant sensitivity failure in T05 is the within-2024 calibration passing a noise floor that the cross-period effect does not exceed. This is the "SNR<2" row in §7. The mechanism is straightforward: 1. Arshkon uses seniority_native = 'entry' as a rich signal for Stage 5 and Stage 10 routing; asaniczka has ZERO native entry labels, so all asaniczka entry comes from the Stage 10 LLM pass. 2. Arshkon's seniority_final_source='llm' rate is different from asaniczka's by construction; asaniczka entry count reflects only what the LLM could detect from description text alone. 3. Scraped is structurally similar to asaniczka on this dimension (native label is LinkedIn-current, not 2024-snapshot), but its description text carries more boilerplate length.

    The 2024 pool choice (arshkon only vs pooled 2024) alone flips the J1 direction of change: arshkon-alone-vs-scraped is a tiny rise; pooled-2024-vs-scraped is a clear fall. Downstream agents must choose ONE 2024 baseline and justify it; the orchestrator should pick pooled 2024 if volume is the priority, arshkon-only if native-label fidelity is the priority.

    Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. Within-2024 cross-source noise dwarfs cross-period effects. J1 SNR 0.19 is the headline. No one should make an "entry share rose 0.5pp" claim without weighing it against 2.6pp arshkon-vs-asaniczka noise on the same metric.

    2. Developer (bare title) lost 61 percentage points of native entry share between 2024 and 2026. This is specific to one title bucket — not a uniform drift — and suggests a LinkedIn taxonomy change for generic "developer" roles rather than a content change.

    3. Indeed scraped matches LinkedIn scraped on entry share (3.05% vs 3.12%). The within-instrument LinkedIn artifact story does not extend to end-of-pipeline entry share; the artifact, if any, sits in native-label drift upstream of seniority_final.

    4. The scraped corpus is 64% longer at the median than arshkon. For any text-density metric (mentions per 1K chars), expect naïve 2024-to-2026 rates to appear lower in 2026 simply because more boilerplate is preserved. T11/T12/T14 must use description_core_llm and normalize.

    5. Asaniczka is 35% unknown-seniority; arshkon and scraped are both 52-53%. Asaniczka has a materially richer seniority_native signal (all rows have 'mid-senior' or 'associate'), so its seniority_final gets filled more aggressively by Stage 10 LLM routing — that's the source of the calibration gap, not a different posting population.

    6. Industry labels drifted from "IT Services and IT Consulting" (arshkon 33%, scraped 18%) to "Software Development" (arshkon 17%, scraped 37%). This is a LinkedIn industry-taxonomy change, not a real sectoral shift. Treat as metadata.

    Data quality caveats¶

    • asaniczka has zero company_industry and zero native entry labels. All asaniczka-driven conclusions about entry-share, industry, or native-seniority drift are invalid by construction.
    • Indeed has zero native seniority; Stage 10 LLM is not routed on Indeed. Its seniority labels come from Stage 5 strong title rules only, with 54% unknown. Use only as a robustness probe, never as primary.
    • Scraped industry column includes ~2.6% of compound labels absent in arshkon. Before any industry-stratified comparison, collapse compounds to a primary token using a deterministic rule (take the first comma-separated piece).
    • Multi-location representative (is_multi_location=true) rows drop naturally from per-metro rollups. Scraped has 3,090 such reps (7.6% of its SWE-LinkedIn). Agents computing per-metro metrics must quote this excluded count.

    Action items for downstream tasks¶

    • T08 distribution profiling: for every share metric you report 2024-to-2026, compute SNR = |arsh-vs-scraped h| / |arsh-vs-asa h|. Flag anything < 2 as "not above within-2024 noise floor."
    • T10 title evolution: use §5's titles-unique-to-scraped as the candidate list for emergent AI-engineering roles. Cross-check against T10's own vocabulary diff.
    • T11 requirements complexity + T12 corpus comparison: length normalization is essential — scraped is 64% longer at median. Density metrics MUST use description_core_llm filtered to llm_extraction_coverage = 'labeled'.
    • T16 company strategies: company Jaccard is the most trustworthy cross-source metric. Use the 125-company arshkon∩scraped overlap panel (computed in T06 §5) as the decomposition unit.
    • T20/T21 seniority: primary seniority definition must be J2 or J3 (not J1) given J1's low power and high within-2024 noise. T30 will produce the canonical recommendation.
    • T18 cross-occupation DiD: the platform-labeling drift on shared titles (native entry ↓, YOE ↑) is a plausible alternative explanation for any SWE-specific "junior compression" signal. Control occupations should be subjected to the same shared-title stability test before declaring SWE-specific effects.
