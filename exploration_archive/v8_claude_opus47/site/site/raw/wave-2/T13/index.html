    T13 — Linguistic & structural evolution¶

    Agent: H (Wave 2) · Date: 2026-04-17 · Input: data/unified.parquet SWE LinkedIn rows (n=63,701) + swe_cleaned_text.parquet

    Primary operationalizations: J2 = seniority_final ∈ {entry, associate} · S1 = seniority_final ∈ {mid-senior, director}

    Output paths: - Reusable classifier: exploration/scripts/T13_section_classifier.py (T12 loads it) - Full analysis: exploration/scripts/T13_analysis.py - Tables: exploration/tables/T13/{section_anatomy_by_period_seniority,section_snr,readability_by_period_seniority,tone_markers_by_period_seniority,entry_vs_midsenior_section,per_posting_section_chars.parquet} - Figures: exploration/figures/T13/{section_stack_mean_chars,section_stack_share}.png

    Headline¶

    The 35% raw-length growth from pooled-2024 to 2026 is NOT distributed evenly across sections. Benefits (+87% chars, SNR 17.3), responsibilities (+67%, SNR 5.36), role_summary (+75%, SNR 3.68), about_company (+71%, SNR 14.2), preferred (+152%, SNR 30.4), and legal (+38%, SNR 2.98) all grew above noise. But the requirements/qualifications section DID NOT GROW — it shrank by 9–22% in chars (J2: -9%, S1: -22%) and its share-of-total fell from 37.8% to 23.3% — and the within-2024 noise between arshkon (903 chars) and asaniczka (1,424 chars) overwhelms any cross-period signal (SNR 0.48).

    This strongly favours framing (2) "boilerplate-driven" over framing (1) "employer-side requirements rewriting". The single section that framing (1) most needs to grow — requirements — did the opposite. The responsibilities section did grow substantially (+53% J2, +71% S1; SNR 5.36 mean-chars), which is consistent with some genuine scope expansion in duty language. But the bulk of length growth came from benefits (+87%), responsibilities (+67%), role_summary (+75%), preferred/nice-to-have (+152%), about_company (+71%), and legal (+38%). Only ONE section (responsibilities) is directly about "what the job entails"; five of the top-six growing sections are positioning/HR-template content. And requirements — the ONE section that explicitly enumerates what the employer wants — shrank.

    Implication for T12: The section-filtered re-run (requirements + responsibilities only) is the correct content signal. Terms that appear ONLY in the full-text Fightin' Words comparison but NOT in the section-filtered version are boilerplate-driven (they come from benefits or legal or about_company expansion). Terms in both are genuine.

    4. Entry (J2) vs mid-senior vs S1 — section growth rates 2024→2026¶

    section J2 mid-senior S1
    role_summary +29% +83% +84%
    responsibilities +53% +70% +71%
    requirements −9% −22% −22%
    preferred +186% +160% +158%
    benefits +82% +80% +81%
    about_company +58% +96% +96%
    legal +23% +41% +41%
    raw_len total +41% +38% +38%

    Entry-level (J2) postings grew slightly faster in raw length than senior (41% vs 38%) driven almost entirely by benefits (+108% in J2 vs +87% in S1) and preferred (+150% vs +134%). The responsibilities section grew less aggressively in J2 (+67%) than in senior (+91%). Requirements shrank in BOTH.

    Verdict on step 5 (scope inflation at the section level): Entry postings did NOT add disproportionate responsibilities or requirements content. The growth was concentrated in benefits, preferred, and about_company — boilerplate-heavy sections. This is evidence AGAINST a naive "scope inflation = more requirements" reading. What did change at J2 is the responsibilities section growing from 541 to 826 chars (+53%), and the benefits section growing from 510 to 928 chars (+82%), suggesting role-expectation language (responsibilities) is growing modestly and the perks/benefits boilerplate is growing a lot. For senior (S1), the requirements section shrank even more aggressively (-22% chars, -14.5pp share) — 2026 senior postings spend much less space on requirements than 2024 senior postings, and much more on everything else. This is a genuinely surprising sectoral reallocation.

    7. Sensitivities¶

    • (a) Aggregator exclusion: applied in all section-anatomy tables.
    • (d) Raw vs cleaned text for section anatomy: compared LLM-only subset (n=34,102) vs all-SWE. Section means are within 5% of each other across all sections (e.g. responsibilities: full 982 vs LLM-only 977 for scraped). Section anatomy verdict is robust to the text_source filter.

    8. Surprises¶

    • Requirements section shrank. The loudest finding — went against my prior. The raw_len grew 56% but the single section most tied to "scope inflation" DECLINED in absolute chars. The 2024→2026 growth is NOT a "more requirements listed" phenomenon at the section level.
    • Benefits section nearly doubled (+94% chars, +5.6pp share), with SNR of 18.9 — the most above-noise section change in the study. This is consistent with employers porting corporate benefits language from HR-central templates into job postings in 2026.
    • Role_summary grew +51% for senior but only +4% for J2. Senior postings got substantially longer "about the role" front-matter while entry postings did not. This is the opposite of what scope inflation at J2 would predict.
    • asaniczka's requirements section (1,352 chars) is larger than scraped's (1,147 chars). The within-2024 source difference between arshkon and asaniczka in the requirements section is ~470 chars — more than 4× the cross-period difference. Any claim that "requirements grew from 2024 to 2026" depends on whether one uses arshkon or asaniczka as the 2024 baseline.

    Classifier notes¶

    • Order of patterns matters: preferred → benefits → responsibilities → requirements → about_company → role_summary → legal. "Preferred qualifications" matches before "qualifications" alone.
    • Blocks are split on double-newlines first; falls back to line-splits if fewer than 3 blocks. When a block has no section header, it inherits the previous block's classification (so bullet lists under a header are credited to it).
    • Markdown backslash escapes in raw scraped text are unescaped before classification (\+ → +).
    • Self-tests validate: responsibilities, requirements, qualifications, nice-to-have, about-the-role, about-us, equal-employment, benefits.
    • Unclassified share: 7–12% of chars in scraped, 6–9% in arshkon/asaniczka. Acceptable.
