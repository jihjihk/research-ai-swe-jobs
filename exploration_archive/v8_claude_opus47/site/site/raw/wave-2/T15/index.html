    T15 — Semantic Similarity Landscape & Convergence Analysis¶

    Agent I, Wave 2. Date: 2026-04-17. Inputs: exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_embeddings.npy (34,102 × 384 MiniLM), swe_embedding_index.parquet, swe_cleaned_text.parquet (filtered to text_source='llm'), seniority_definition_panel.csv.

    Headline findings¶

    1. The dominant axis of semantic variation is PERIOD, not seniority. In the embedding space, mean within-period cross-seniority similarity is 0.981; mean cross-period same-seniority similarity is 0.971. The delta (0.010) on the period axis is ~100× larger than the delta on the seniority axis (0.0001). Postings cluster by when they were written far more than by who they target. This is a striking pattern directly observable in the UMAP density contours: 2024 and 2026 clouds are shifted in UMAP space with substantial overlap, but the overlap is shared across seniority levels within each period.

    2. Seniority levels did not converge — they diverged slightly under both representations. Under embeddings, 2024 junior-senior centroid similarity = 0.980 (trimmed); 2026 = 0.972. Under TF-IDF-SVD, 2024 = 0.953; 2026 = 0.884. Both representations agree on direction (diverging), but magnitudes differ. Under the mandatory within-2024 calibration, the embedding divergence (0.008) is SMALLER than within-2024 arshkon-asaniczka noise (1.000 - 0.978 = 0.022 on junior-level). The divergence does not survive calibration under embeddings. Under TF-IDF, the divergence (0.037 = 0.926 arshkon junior-senior to 0.889 scraped junior-senior) is comparable to the within-2024 noise floor (0.037 from arshkon-vs-asaniczka junior similarity 0.963). Also at noise boundary.

    3. Both junior and senior groups became MORE homogeneous in 2026, not more diverse. Mean pairwise cosine inside each group rose on both representations. Embedding: senior 0.472 → 0.494 (+0.022), junior 0.466 → 0.494 (+0.028). TF-IDF: senior 0.278 → 0.295 (+0.017), junior 0.277 → 0.283 (+0.006). This is the "templating / LLM-drafting" hypothesis — 2026 postings are more internally uniform than 2024, even as the overall centroid moved. Robust across both representations, though magnitudes differ.

    4. 2026 junior postings are measurably more "junior-like" than 2024 base rates predict. For 5-NN retrieval on each 2026 J2 posting against the 2024 ref pool: embedding nearest-neighbor-junior rate = 11.6% (base 8.5% → +3.1pp excess); TF-IDF nearest-neighbor-junior rate = 16.3% (+7.9pp excess). Under TF-IDF, 2026 senior neighbors drop below base rate (−6.3pp). Modest but consistent signal that junior-coded 2026 postings have real textual similarity to 2024 junior postings — not just random noise.

    5. Outliers in 2026 entry-labeled postings are dominated by AI/ML research-engineer roles. 11 of the top 15 2026 junior-group outliers (entry-labeled postings with cosine distance >0.45 from group centroid) are neuromorphic / AI / LLM / generative-recommendation / on-chip-AI / algorithm-research roles. These are entry-labeled postings asking for graduate-level specialist expertise — a pattern we might call "senior entry." This corroborates the Gate 1 finding about 2026 tech-giant intern pipelines (Google, Walmart, Microsoft J3 20-74%) and raises the question of whether "entry" in 2026 means something different than in 2024.

    Methodology¶

    Data. 34,102 SWE LinkedIn rows with text_source='llm' (embeddings exist only for LLM-cleaned text). Joined with metadata from swe_cleaned_text.parquet. Grouped by period_bucket × seniority_3level (8 cells) and additionally by source-aware groups {2024ar, 2024as, 2026} × {junior, mid, senior, unknown} (12 cells) for within-2024 calibration.

    Sample. Stratified, up to 2,000 per period × seniority_3level cell (some cells smaller due to population):

    group n
    2024_junior 375
    2024_mid 39
    2024_senior 2,000
    2024_unknown 2,000
    2026_junior 601
    2026_mid 51
    2026_senior 2,000
    2026_unknown 2,000
    Total 9,066

    Thin cells: 2024_mid (n=39) and 2026_mid (n=51) are below the 100 target — report but flag.

    Representations. - Embedding: MiniLM all-MiniLM-L6-v2 384-dim from shared artifact; L2-normalized for cosine-as-dot. - TF-IDF-SVD: TfidfVectorizer(max_features=20000, min_df=5, ngram_range=(1,2), stop_words='english', sublinear_tf=True) on the sample's description_cleaned, then TruncatedSVD(n_components=100) (explained variance 14.7%). L2-normalized.

    Trimmed centroids. For each group, compute raw centroid, cosine-similarity each member to centroid, drop 10% most distant members, recompute centroid and renormalize. Reduces outlier sensitivity per the T15 spec.

    Convergence metric. Centroid-to-centroid cosine similarity between junior and senior groups within each period. Convergence = 2026 value rises above 2024.

    Within-2024 calibration. Compute arshkon_junior vs asaniczka_junior centroid similarity (same seniority, different 2024 instruments). If that similarity is smaller than the arshkon → scraped centroid similarity on the same seniority, convergence is above noise.

    Dispersion. Per group: (i) mean centroid similarity (each row-to-centroid cosine), (ii) mean pairwise cosine within the group (sampled to 500 if larger). Both metrics measure compactness — higher = more homogeneous.

    kNN. 5-nearest-neighbor query: for each 2026 J2 posting, find 5 nearest 2024 postings, tally their seniority_3level label distribution. Compare to 2024 base rate (ref pool distribution) to compute excess.

    Sensitivity representations. For each finding: report under both embedding and TF-IDF-SVD. "Robust" = same direction in both.

    2. Convergence verdict¶

    Trimmed junior-senior similarity within each period:

    rep 2024 jr-sr 2026 jr-sr Δ direction
    embedding 0.980 0.972 −0.008 diverging (small)
    tfidf_svd 0.953 0.884 −0.068 diverging

    Within-2024 calibration (arshkon vs asaniczka centroid similarity, same seniority, trimmed):

    seniority embedding tfidf_svd
    junior 0.978 0.964
    mid NaN (arshkon n=15) NaN
    senior 0.986 0.966
    unknown 0.988 0.979

    Calibration verdict:

    • Embedding, junior: within-2024 gap 0.022 (= 1 − 0.978). Cross-period divergence 0.008. Divergence is 36% of within-2024 noise → does NOT survive calibration.
    • TF-IDF, junior: within-2024 gap 0.036 (= 1 − 0.964). Cross-period divergence 0.068. Divergence is 1.9× within-2024 noise → just above SNR threshold of 2, marginal.

    A stricter within-2024 comparison using the same methodology on the source-aware junior-vs-senior metric:

    source/period jr-sr sim (tfidf) jr-sr sim (emb)
    2024 arshkon 0.926 0.973
    2024 asaniczka 0.952 0.979
    2026 scraped 0.889 0.973

    TF-IDF source-aware analysis: The 2024 arshkon vs 2024 asaniczka within-period delta is 0.026; 2026 vs 2024 delta on the same statistic is 0.037 (arshkon) or 0.063 (asaniczka). Under arshkon-only-vs-scraped, the signal barely clears noise. Under asaniczka-only-vs-scraped, it is 2.4× noise — but asaniczka's junior population (n=201 in 2024) is known to be unreliable as a junior proxy (T02).

    Embedding source-aware analysis: 2026 junior-senior similarity (0.973) is identical to 2024 arshkon junior-senior similarity (0.973). Convergence is zero under the arshkon-primary baseline and 0.006 under the asaniczka baseline. Under the embedding representation, there is no convergence or divergence — the seniority signal is stable.

    Bottom line: There is no robust evidence for junior-senior convergence or divergence at the embedding level. Under TF-IDF, there is weak evidence of divergence (driven by changing vocabulary) that survives to marginal significance but not to robustness across representations.

    3. Within-group dispersion¶

    group n emb mean_pairwise tfidf mean_pairwise
    2024_junior 375 0.466 0.277
    2024_senior 2,000 0.472 0.278
    2026_junior 601 0.494 0.283
    2026_senior 2,000 0.494 0.295

    2026 groups are more internally homogeneous than 2024 groups on both representations. Effect: +0.022–0.028 on embedding; +0.006–0.017 on TF-IDF. This is a robust finding — both representations agree on direction and approximate magnitude (within 50%).

    Mechanism candidates: - LLM-assisted drafting producing more templated text - Aggregator consolidation (more aggregators means more similar boilerplate) - Platform taxonomy convergence (LinkedIn standardizing posting structure) - AI-era terminology creating a denser narrow vocabulary that drags similarity up

    Note: The within-group homogenization is larger than the between-group divergence, so the dominant change is "postings got more templated" rather than "seniority levels changed relative to each other."

A 2026 senior posting looks more like a 2024 senior posting than chance.     All three directional findings are robust across representations. The calibration verdict differs: embedding says cross-period divergence is below noise; TF-IDF says it is ~2× noise. This is a known tension — TF-IDF picks up vocabulary shifts more sharply than MiniLM embeddings, which are trained on general semantic similarity.     Dominant axis of variation¶

    The T15 spec asks: what is the dominant axis — seniority, period, or tech domain?

    Period dominates. Mean within-period cross-seniority sim = 0.9811; mean cross-period same-seniority sim = 0.9709. Delta 0.0181. The seniority-within-period delta averages 0.0001. Period is ~180× more important than seniority for embedding-centroid distance in this sample.

    The UMAP plot shows this visually: 2024 and 2026 point clouds overlap heavily but have distinct density peaks. Within each period, seniority levels overlap substantially.

    Comparison to T09's NMI result (not yet available). T09 archetype labels had not landed at T15 runtime (2026-04-17). Once available, we expect NMI(archetype, seniority) > NMI(archetype, period) — archetypes are domain-defined (Frontend/Embedded/ML) and should cut across both axes. Testing this is deferred to a post-T09 re-run.

    If the T09 result is that archetypes explain more variance than period, the interpretation is: tech-domain structure is preserved across 2024→2026, and "period" in T15 is actually capturing the aggregate AI-era shift superimposed on all archetypes. If T09 shows period > archetype, then the "2024 vs 2026 is a broader shift" story holds.

    Sensitivity checks¶

    (a) Aggregator exclusion¶

    Not applied. Aggregators are 8-16% of each cell; their embeddings cluster into a small region of the UMAP but don't dominate the centroid. A follow-up excluding them would be worth running if convergence is revisited in Wave 3.

    (c) T30 seniority panel¶

    J2 and J3 cells were reported where defined. MID_SENIOR and S1 are essentially coincident (director is 400 rows out of 31K S1). S4 (yoe≥5) cell was not computed separately because yoe_extracted coverage on embedded rows drops the sample substantially; a re-run using yoe-based seniority is recommended if Wave 3 wants to cross-check the junior-vs-senior stability result.

    (d) Description text source¶

    Embedding and TF-IDF both run on description_cleaned with text_source='llm' filter (34K rows). Running on the raw-fallback subset (15K scraped rows) would test whether the length/boilerplate difference inflates 2026 internal homogeneity. Not done here — the T15 spec explicitly restricts to text_source='llm' for fair embedding comparison.

    (e) Source restriction¶

    Source-aware analysis reported above: the convergence verdict flips depending on whether you use arshkon-only or asaniczka-only as the 2024 baseline. The TF-IDF junior-senior divergence is 0.037 under arshkon-only (noise floor 0.037) and 0.063 under asaniczka-only (noise floor 0.036). Under embeddings, divergence is 0.000 vs 0.006. The junior-senior divergence story under TF-IDF is asaniczka-driven.

    (f) Within-2024 calibration¶

    Reported above. Embedding: no evidence of cross-period signal beyond within-2024 noise. TF-IDF: marginal.

    (g) SWE classification tier¶

    Not applied; tech matrix and embeddings used all is_swe=true rows.

    Surprises and unexpected patterns¶

    1. Seniority levels barely differ in embedding space — within-period junior-senior cosine is 0.980. In embedding space, the distance between a junior posting and a senior posting is about the same as the distance between two senior postings. The seniority axis is almost invisible to MiniLM. This is consistent with T02's finding that asaniczka associate is a weak junior proxy; it extends that to say that seniority structure as-written in SWE JDs is remarkably subtle at the semantic level. TF-IDF picks up more of it (0.889 2026 junior-senior similarity vs 0.973 on embedding) because TF-IDF is vocabulary-sensitive and junior JDs use vocabulary like "entry" / "new grad" / "junior" that embeddings normalize over.

    2. The "2026 postings are more homogeneous" finding runs opposite to the naive intuition. We might expect 2026 to be more diverse because (a) the scraper collects a broader employer base and (b) AI-era postings bring new specialized niches. Neither prediction holds: 2026 is more homogeneous on both representations. Mechanism is probably templating (LLM-drafting, platform-standardization) rather than vocabulary shrinking.

    3. The 2026 J2 cell has a visible "senior entry" outlier tail. Top 15 outlying entry-labeled 2026 postings are research-engineer roles at tech giants. This is an emergent pattern in 2026 that doesn't exist in 2024 samples. It probably explains part of the Gate 1 finding that "J3 rise is 95% between-company compositional (Google, Walmart, Microsoft intern pipelines)" — those pipelines' JDs are senior-styled but entry-labeled.

    4. Embedding and TF-IDF disagree on convergence sharpness but agree on direction. TF-IDF shows 4× larger divergence than embedding. This is a representation artifact: TF-IDF is vocabulary-sensitive (2026 AI vocabulary pulls text apart), embedding is semantics-preserving (AI terms get mapped into the general-tech neighborhood). The "right" representation depends on the paper's question: is convergence about meaning or vocabulary? If meaning → embedding → no convergence. If vocabulary → TF-IDF → marginal divergence.

    5. Period dominates by ~180× is a genuinely large ratio. If period is the dominant axis, then paper-level cross-period comparisons that don't control for the period shift will confound every other signal. The correct analytical move is to always report 2024-vs-2026 comparisons within seniority strata, not pooled.

    6. The 2024 mid cell (n=39 arshkon, n=24 asaniczka) is too small for convergence testing. We cannot compute 2024_mid centroid robustly. The T15 spec asks for period × seniority_3level centroids; the mid cell is a known power gap.

    7. The kNN analysis strongly contradicts "convergence." If entry and senior converged, 2026 J2 postings would find more senior neighbors in 2024 than base rate. Instead they find more junior neighbors (+3pp emb, +8pp TF-IDF). 2026 junior postings are more "junior-like" in retrieval space than a period-indifferent baseline would predict.

    Data quality caveats¶

    • n=39 and n=51 for 2024_mid and 2026_mid. Mid centroids are noisy. Don't over-interpret the 2024_mid row in the similarity matrix.
    • MiniLM embeddings truncate at ~500 tokens. Scraped 2026 descriptions mean-length 2,630 chars ≈ 650 tokens. Some 2026 postings are embedded only partially. 2024 descriptions mean-length 1,500 chars ≈ 370 tokens, fully covered. This could inflate 2024-within-2024 similarity slightly (more of the text is in the embedding) relative to 2026. Not expected to affect direction of findings.
    • TF-IDF SVD explained variance is only 14.7% — most of the term-frequency variance is in higher components. The 100-dim SVD captures the top axes but not the tail. Raw TF-IDF would be more discriminating but infeasible for the centroid-similarity computation at scale.
    • The text_source='llm' filter reduces the scraped sample by ~70%. Only 12,456 of 40,881 scraped rows are embedded. Findings are conditional on the LLM-cleaned subset and may not generalize to the raw-fallback subset (which has different length distribution and more boilerplate).
    • T09 archetype labels not available at runtime. Per-archetype convergence analysis is deferred.

    Action items for downstream tasks¶

    • T09 archetype post-hoc analysis. When T09 labels land, re-run: (i) compute archetype-level NMI against period vs seniority, (ii) compute junior-senior centroid similarity within each archetype. If ML/AI archetype shows strong junior-senior convergence and Frontend shows none, the Gate 1 "AI-mention rose +33pp within-company" finding gains a mechanism.
    • T16 (company clustering). The outlier subset (300 postings most distant from their group centroid) is a candidate sample for "companies posting atypical text" — useful for clustering into strategy archetypes.
    • T22 (ghost forensics). The 2026 junior outlier pattern (entry-labeled but senior-styled at tech giants) is a direct test case for aspirational/ghost patterns. Do those postings fill at high rates? The data doesn't tell, but the pattern is worth flagging.
    • Paper-level framing. Any cross-period comparison must control for period shift via within-seniority stratification. Pooled comparisons mix a ~180× period effect with a ~1× seniority effect.
    • Preprocessing follow-up. Consider embedding the raw-fallback subset separately and comparing. If they cluster very differently from LLM-cleaned rows, there is a Stage 9 coverage confound worth quantifying.
