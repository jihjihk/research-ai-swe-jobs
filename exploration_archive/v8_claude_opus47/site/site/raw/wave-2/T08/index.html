    2.3 YOE distribution by bucket¶

    From anomaly_yoe_spikes.csv (Figure figures/T08/fig2_yoe_distribution.png):

    • Sharp modal spikes at 5, 10, 15, and 20 across all three buckets. The YOE parser resolves phrases like "5+ years" to an integer floor, so this is an expected instrument artifact, not a real skill distribution. Any YOE-based cutoff (J3/J4/S4) is sensitive to exact cut location relative to these spikes.
    • Median is 5 in all three buckets (calibration table marks this metric as yoe_extracted_median: undefined — correct — no meaningful cross-period shift in median YOE).

    2.4 Metro and industry composition¶

    From industry_top15_by_bucket.csv and a cross-period diff:

    • Industry drift arshkon → scraped (SWE-LinkedIn):
    • ↑ Software Development: 13.1% → 26.0% (+12.8 pp)
    • ↑ Technology / Internet: 2.3% → 4.9% (+2.6 pp)
    • ↓ IT Services and IT Consulting: 25.8% → 12.5% (−13.3 pp)
    • ↓ Staffing and Recruiting: 5.8% → 2.6% (−3.2 pp)
    • ↓ Information Services: 3.2% → 0.4% (−2.8 pp)

    This is a large composition shift. Product/engineering companies moved up; consulting and staffing moved down. This partially explains why per-posting AI mentions rose — tech companies talk about AI more than staffing agencies do. §7 decomposes J2 share into within-domain and between-domain components; the between component is small for J2 share but industry composition shift is a plausible driver of the corpus-wide +21 pp org_scope rise.

    • Metro composition: scraped concentrates heavily in search-target metros by construction; cross-period comparison of metro_area distributions is confounded by the scraper's metro targeting and not interpretable as a true geographic shift. See T17 (Wave 3) for geographic analysis.

    3. Arshkon native-entry-label YOE diagnostic (anomaly)¶

    Table: exploration/tables/T08/arshkon_native_yoe_diagnostic.csv.

    seniority_native n_all n_yoe YOE mean YOE median share ≥ 5 share ≤ 2
    intern 20 1 2.0 2.0 0.0% 5.0%
    entry 738 458 4.18 3.18 26.4% 17.8%
    associate 263 149 3.89 3.00 18.3% 16.7%
    mid-senior 2,231 1,477 5.61 5.00 45.5% 5.4%
    director 20 14 7.71 7.50 60.0% 5.0%
    executive 11 8 8.00 6.50 63.6% 0.0%

    Anomaly: arshkon rows labeled seniority_native = 'entry' have a YOE mean of 4.18 and a ≥ 5 YOE share of 26.4% — i.e., one in four "entry" rows demand ≥ 5 years of experience in the description text. Only 17.8% of entry-native rows actually have YOE ≤ 2. Compare to: Stage 5 strong-rule entry rows (i.e., seniority_final='entry' filtered to YOE-known) have mean YOE=2.05 (arshkon) / 2.58 (asaniczka) / 1.92 (scraped) — much more coherent.

    Implication: LinkedIn's own native entry label is a YOE-divergent label. This is consistent with the "inflated entry-level" narrative in the market (entry postings demanding experienced candidates), and it is a data-quality feature of the arshkon snapshot that should be flagged to Wave 3 (T22 ghost forensics). It also supports the Gate 1 decision to down-weight seniority_native as a baseline and use seniority_final (Stage-5+10 strong rule + LLM) as primary — native entry labels should not be trusted as a pure junior slice.

    Asaniczka note: per preprocessing-schema.md, asaniczka has zero native entry labels, so we cannot run the same diagnostic there. However, asaniczka's 204 seniority_final='entry' rows are all Stage-5/10 assigned — their YOE mean is 2.58 (well below mid-senior's 6.18), so the label-vs-YOE coherence is preserved in seniority_final.

    6. Ranked changes — a second look through effect size¶

    The calibration_table.csv metric set plus the six extra binary metrics from this task yields 28 metrics. After rank-scoring (see §1 rule), the top tier (rank score > 5) is:

    • 5 tech/content metrics (tech_count_mean, 4 length metrics — raw and cleaned, mean and median)
    • 3 AI metrics (ai_mention_binary, ai_tool_binary, ai_mention_density)
    • 2 scope metrics (org_scope_binary, soft_skill_binary)

    The middle tier (rank score 0.5–5) is dominated by management_strict, senior_3level, J3, desc_length Cohen's d, and a few column-availability metrics. The bottom tier (rank score < 0.5) is where J1, J2, yoe_known, aggregator, ghost, and remote sit — either because the within-2024 noise swamps the signal, or because the cross-period effect is small.

    Interpretive note: the rank score is |effect| × SNR with SNR clipped at 200. If we used |effect| alone (ignoring SNR), the ranking would shift — ai_mention_binary would lead by a mile (+36 pp), followed by org_scope (+21 pp), desc_cleaned_length (+1039 chars scaled), etc. Adding the SNR filter is what moves ai_tool_binary to the

    1 rank despite its smaller absolute effect (+13.7 pp): its within-2024 noise is tiny¶

    (0.4 pp), so the cross-period effect is exceptionally clean.

    7. Domain × seniority decomposition (H1 check)¶

    9. Anomalies worth investigating¶

    9.1 Scraped seniority_final_source = 'unknown' pool is gigantic¶

    From seniority_source_breakdown.csv:

    source bucket final_source count share
    arshkon llm 2,917 62.2%
    arshkon title_keyword/manager 1,771 37.8%
    arshkon unknown 3 0.06%
    asaniczka llm 5,190 28.6%
    asaniczka title_keyword/manager 10,654 58.8%
    asaniczka unknown 2,285 12.6%
    scraped llm 6,918 16.9%
    scraped title_keyword/manager 17,798 43.5%
    scraped unknown 16,165 39.5%

    Scraped has 16,165 rows where the seniority labeler never even ran (Stage 9 core frame did not include them). On arshkon+asaniczka, the LLM reached ~85–99% of rows; on scraped only 17% were LLM-routed. This means that the apparent rise in unknown seniority on scraped (34% arshkon unknown → 53% scraped unknown) is not a market signal — it is a Stage 9/10 selected-frame artifact. Within LLM-labeled subset only, scraped unknown is 82% (6918 labeled → 5676 still unknown by LLM) vs arshkon 83% (2917 → 2421), basically flat, and J2 share within labeled-only rises arshkon 3.36% → scraped 4.70% (+1.34 pp). Wave 3 agents should use llm_classification_coverage='labeled' as a sensitivity when interpreting scraped seniority shifts.

    9.2 Native entry YOE divergence (see §3)¶

    seniority_native='entry' rows in arshkon have 26.4% with ≥ 5 YOE. Flag for T22.

    9.3 Description length quintupling/doubling on scraped¶

    Scraped raw desc_length mean = 4,912 chars vs arshkon 3,306 (+48.6%). Scraped cleaned desc_length mean = 2,630 vs arshkon 1,329 (+97.9%). The cleaned-length rise is ~2× the raw-length rise — i.e., even after boilerplate removal, scraped postings hold twice as much substantive text. This is a real employer-behavior shift, not an artifact — mid-seniors alone went +47% on length within S1 (§4), and cleaned text was LLM-extracted so it removes boilerplate.

    9.4 YOE spikes at 5/10/15/20 (§2.3)¶

    Not a bug, but material. Any YOE cutoff at 5 includes the ~3,530 scraped rows whose YOE is resolved as exactly 5 via "5+ years" phrasing. Sensitivity analyses that vary cutoffs by ±1 year around 5 will move a lot of rows.

    9.5 Scraped date_posted effectively missing¶

    Schema claims 2.8%; actual is 0.80% on SWE-LinkedIn scraped (328/40,881). posting_age_days has the same coverage. This rules out any scraped-native date-based analysis — all temporal work must use period bucket or scrape_week (scrape_date coverage is 100% on scraped).

    9.6 remote_share is degenerate¶

    is_remote_inferred is false for every row in all three buckets of the default filter. Either the inference stage is broken, or every remote-flagged row fails another filter (non-English, date out of range, etc.). Worth checking with Preprocessing team. In the meantime, remote-work analyses need to go through location text matching rather than the is_remote_inferred column.

    9.7 metro_assigned_share rose from 59% → 75%¶

    Scraped rows have metro_area assigned at 75% vs arshkon 59% / asaniczka 71%. This is a scraper-by-construction effect (scrape queries target specific metros), not a market signal — but it means geographic analysis on scraped will include a strongly non-random subset.

    11. Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. ml_ai junior share FELL from arshkon to scraped (8.9% → 5.5%). If a narrative is "AI coding is automating junior roles," ml_ai/ai-engineering postings would shed juniors fastest; the data (on small n=146 arshkon cell) is directionally consistent, but every tech domain except data, devops, and qa shows a J2 decrease. The J2 drop is broad-based and within-domain (§7 decomposition), not concentrated in AI-specialist roles.

    2. Arshkon native-entry rows are YOE-incoherent. 26.4% of LinkedIn-labeled "entry" arshkon postings require ≥ 5 years. This pre-dates 2026's AI narrative — it's a 2024 labeling problem baked into LinkedIn-native labels. Worth cross-referencing with T22 ghost forensics; "inflated entry" was already a real phenomenon in 2024.

    3. S1 mean YOE fell 0.35 years (arshkon 6.06 → scraped 5.83), SNR 2.4. Senior postings are asking for less experience, not more, when measured on yoe_extracted. Combined with the +40 pp AI-mention rise on S1 slice (§4), the picture is: "senior roles: less YOE, more AI mentions, more org-scope language, much longer descriptions." This points to senior role re-definition rather than credential inflation — a notable asymmetry with the "AI raises the experience bar" story.

    4. Industry composition shift is enormous (§2.4). Software Development share doubled (+12.8 pp) while IT Services dropped by nearly the same amount (−13.3 pp). The 2026 scrape is sampling a different employer mix than arshkon's 2024 Kaggle snapshot. For domain-stratified findings in Wave 3, the industry composition shift should be a pre-registered confounder.

    5. Within LLM-labeled subset only, scraped J2 = 4.70% vs arshkon 3.36% (vs overall scraped 3.25%). When we restrict to rows the LLM actually saw, the J2 direction flips back to UP, even on arshkon-only baseline. The Stage 9 selection frame is mechanically depressing scraped J2. Wave 3 should run every junior-share claim under both "all rows" and "llm_classification_coverage='labeled' only" and flag disagreement as a data-pipeline issue, not a market signal.

    6. is_remote_inferred is identically false on every SWE-LinkedIn row after the default filter (§9.6). This rules out any remote/hybrid mix analysis via that column. The Preprocessing team may want to investigate.

    7. The smallest employers (size Q1, ≤ 92 employees) have the highest AI-mention share in arshkon (7.6%) — higher than the largest employers (Q4, 6.8%). AI language was not a big-tech-only phenomenon in the 2024 snapshot. Note: can't replicate this cross-period because scraped has no company_size.

    8. Only 3.7% of arshkon's raw "+5/+10/+15/+20" YOE lumps show up as YOE-known via the rule-based parser (§2.3) — a very material chunk of the YOE distribution sits at exactly the 5-year cutoff. The J3/J4 and S4 definitions are sensitive to ±1 year shifts of the cut.

    9. Senior share is essentially flat on arshkon-only baseline (−0.95 pp S1, within MDE ~2.1 pp). The headline "scope inflation + senior redefinition" narrative (RQ1) cannot rest on senior share shifting compositionally; it has to rest on the content shift inside the senior slice (AI + scope + length), which IS above noise. Wave 2 agents working on RQ1 should lead with the content shift, not a share-of-postings claim.
