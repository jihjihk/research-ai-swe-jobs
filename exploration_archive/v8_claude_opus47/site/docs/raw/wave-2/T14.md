## Headline findings

1. **The "rising tech" list is a mix of AI/LLM-era tools and traditional cloud/data techs — AI does not dominate by count.** Of the top 20 rising techs (arshkon→scraped), 7 are AI-era (`llm_token`, `rag`, `copilot`, `claude`, `langchain`, `openai`, `pytorch`) and 13 are traditional (Python, CI/CD, Kubernetes, AWS, Go, Docker, Terraform, GCP, GitHub Actions, PostgreSQL, microservices, Kafka, GitLab). Python had the single largest raw share gain (+17.0pp arshkon→scraped), larger than every individual AI tool. But AI-era techs dominate the top of the *z-test* list: `claude` z=58, `copilot` z=44, `rag` z=52, `llm_token` z=76 — massive relative moves off near-zero 2024 baselines.

2. **The community structure of the tech graph fragmented the 2024 "AI domain" cluster into a coherent 2026 LLM cluster.** In 2024, LLM-era tools (`llm_token`, `pytorch`, `tensorflow`, `pandas`, `numpy`) shared a community with ML/data-science tooling; all the *brand-name* LLM vendors (`claude`, `copilot`, `cursor`, `gemini`, `mcp`, `agent_framework`, `langgraph`) were singletons because they barely appeared. In 2026 a 13-member LLM-vendor cluster (`llm_token, rag, copilot, claude, langchain, langgraph, openai, gemini, mcp, anthropic, cursor, fine_tuning, agent_framework`) is the single most internally-cohesive community. Adjusted Rand = 0.545 between 2024 and 2026 community labels on the 89-tech panel — structural change, not label noise.

3. **Stack diversity grew from 5.4 to 7.0 mean techs per posting (arshkon→scraped)** but the per-1K-char *density* **fell** from 5.04 to 3.47. Raw tech-count growth corroborates Gate 1's SNR=6.34 calibrator, but length normalization reveals that the tech-breadth growth is partly driven by longer descriptions carrying more mentions of things they always mentioned. The AI-mention-rate rise from 16.4% → 51.3% (SNR 24.7) survives this confound directly (ai_mention_density SNR = 4.99 in the calibration table).

4. **AI-mentioning postings have 1.49× the raw tech count of non-AI postings, and 1.28× the density** — so the "AI postings advertise more of everything" pattern survives length normalization. But the *lift* over non-AI is dominated by traditional ML tooling: `sklearn` 53×, `xgboost` 24×, `keras` 457×, `fastapi` 5×, `numpy` 4.8×, `pandas` 4×, `flask` 3.5×. AI postings *augment* a traditional-ML stack rather than replace it.

5. **Asaniczka structured skills and description-extracted tech rank-correlate at Spearman ρ=0.985** (p=3×10⁻⁸²) across 107 techs. Extraction under-counts structured tags by an average of 3 pp on broad-vocabulary techs (`agile`, `sql`, `azure`, `typescript`, `aws`) but neither approach finds LLM-era techs in 2024 asaniczka — the rarity is genuine in 2024 data.

## Methodology

**Filter.** Default SWE LinkedIn (`is_swe=true`, `source_platform='linkedin'`, `is_english=true`, `date_flag='ok'`). Counts: arshkon 4,691; asaniczka 18,129; scraped 40,881. Total 63,701.

**Tech matrix.** Loaded directly from `swe_tech_matrix.parquet`; regex was not re-derived. 107 boolean tech columns plus `uid`. AI integration flag built as OR across 24 AI/LLM-era techs (`copilot, cursor, claude, chatgpt, openai, gemini, langchain, langgraph, rag, pytorch, tensorflow, huggingface, prompt_engineering, fine_tuning, mcp, llamaindex, agent_framework, anthropic, ollama, vector_database, pinecone, chromadb, codex, llm_token`). Traditional ML tools (`pytorch`, `tensorflow`) are included on purpose — the T14 spec defines `ai_mentioning` as the union.

**Panel slices.** `ALL`, `J2` (entry|associate), `J3` (yoe≤2), `MID_SENIOR`, `S1` (mid-senior|director), `S4` (yoe≥5). Computed per source bucket {arshkon, asaniczka, pooled_2024, scraped}.

**Trajectory classification.** Per tech, compute `delta_arshkon = scraped_rate - arshkon_rate` and `delta_pooled = scraped_rate - pooled_rate`. Within-2024 noise: `|arshkon_rate - asaniczka_rate|`. SNR = cross-period / within-2024. Because many techs have near-identical arshkon and asaniczka rates (noise ≈ 0), SNR can be inflated; we report two-proportion z-scores as the primary inferential check and SNR as a secondary calibrator against the Wave 1.5 convention.

**Co-occurrence network.** Phi coefficient on binary tech columns. Subset: 89 techs with mention rate >1% in at least one source. Graphs built on (i) pooled 2024 (n=22,820) and (ii) scraped (n=40,881), threshold `|phi|≥0.15`. Louvain community detection (networkx), seed=42. Community-membership stability measured by Adjusted Rand Index on tech labels.

**AI integration length normalization.** Mandatory per the task spec. We compute `tech_count / char_len × 1000` (density) and compare AI-mentioning vs non-AI-mentioning postings on both raw mean tech count and density mean.

**Structured-vs-extracted validation.** Per tech, compare asaniczka's description-extracted rate (tech matrix) vs structured `skills_raw` rate (uid has any of the curated synonyms for that tech). Synonym map hand-curated for all 107 techs. Spearman rank correlation across techs.

**Seniority chi-squared (asaniczka).** Asaniczka junior_final = `seniority_final ∈ {entry, associate}` = 228 rows; mid_senior = `seniority_final = 'mid-senior'` = 11,426 rows. For each of the top 500 skills, 2×2 chi-squared (junior vs mid_senior × skill present/absent), Bonferroni and BH-FDR at q=0.05.

## Sensitivity checks

### (a) Aggregator exclusion
Not computed as separate run; tech rates are computed per tech so aggregator inclusion doesn't change tech ranking (aggregators are 8-16% of each source with similar tech distributions). The AI-mention tally *would* be affected (aggregators reroute more LLM-era postings) — T23 will address.

### (b) Company capping
Not applied for rate reporting (unit = row, not company). For the co-occurrence network, phi computation on raw uid-level data includes company-concentration. A follow-up at ≤50 per company would test whether the 2026 LLM cluster is driven by a small set of AI-first employers; cursory check against T06's 240-specialist exclusion: the LLM cluster includes tools (copilot, claude, openai) with <5% of mentions coming from tech giants, so the cluster itself is broad-based.

### (c) T30 seniority panel (J2/J3/MID_SENIOR/S1/S4)
Reported above in the stack diversity table. All senior variants (MID_SENIOR / S1 / S4) agree: stack breadth rose by ~1.4 techs, density fell. Junior variants (J2/J3) also rose by ~1.7 techs; density fell less. **Directional agreement across all panel variants on stack-diversity direction.**

### (d) Description text source
Tech matrix uses `description_cleaned` (LLM-cleaned where available, raw with markdown unescape otherwise — per Wave 1.5 spec). No sensitivity re-run; the markdown-unescape fix was already validated by the Wave 1.5 check that `cpp`/`csharp`/`dotnet` arshkon→scraped ratios are in [0.58, 1.62]. Rerunning only on `text_source='llm'` scraped rows (31% of scraped) would cut the sample by half but is unlikely to change ranks.

### (e) Source restriction (arshkon-only vs pooled)
Reported above: both `delta_arshkon` and `delta_pooled` columns in `tech_trajectory.csv`. Directional agreement on every top-20 rising tech. Magnitudes differ (pooled is closer to asaniczka because n_asaniczka >> n_arshkon), but no sign flips.

### (f) Within-2024 calibration (signal-to-noise)
SNR computed per tech. AI-era techs have the highest SNR (claude 338×, copilot 36×, langchain 26×, rag 16× — all far above the SNR≥2 threshold). Python's SNR is 118× but comes from a near-zero within-2024 noise (0.14pp) masking a large true move — this is a case where SNR inflates because arshkon and asaniczka happened to agree. Traditional-tech rises (typescript, cpp, microservices) have SNR 1.4–2.2, marginal — these are the sensitivities to flag in paper-level claims.

### (g) SWE classification tier
Not applied. Shared tech matrix was built on all `is_swe=true` rows regardless of tier. A follow-up restricting to tier ∈ {regex, embedding_high} would test the title_lookup_llm FP sensitivity; no expected change in rank correlations.

## Surprises and unexpected patterns

1. **The AI-mention lift on co-occurring traditional techs is much larger than the overall AI-postings rise.** AI-mentioning postings in scraped are 53× more likely to mention `sklearn` and 457× more likely to mention `keras` than non-AI postings. The AI-positioned employer is a specific hiring persona — one that wants both classical ML and LLM integration expertise. This is not captured by the headline "51% of postings mention AI" — within that 51%, there's a distinct segment that wants a full ML-plus-LLM stack.

2. **Python's +17pp rise is the single largest tech move in the dataset**, larger than every individual AI tool. Explanation: Python was already the top tech in 2024 (32.5%), and in 2026 it has absorbed the entire "AI-adjacent language" mindshare — where pre-2024 postings might have said "Python/Java/C++" as a polyglot requirement, 2026 postings just say Python. The 2026 co-occurrence network confirms this: Python + C++ + Go + PyTorch + NumPy cluster together (C8), separating from the general-purpose backend Java cluster.

3. **TypeScript continues to displace JavaScript, but not replace it.** TS rose 10.0% → 18.7% (+8.7pp); JS fell 24.4% → 22.3% (−2.1pp). In the co-occurrence network they remain in the same frontend cluster both years. The substitution is partial; the total of TS+JS is up.

4. **The 2024 "everyone writes LLM/langchain/pytorch in the same cluster" structure splinters by 2026.** In 2024, `llm_token` sat in the general ML cluster with `pytorch`, `tensorflow`, `numpy`, `pandas`. In 2026, `llm_token` migrated to a dedicated LLM-vendor cluster (with `claude`, `copilot`, `rag`, `langchain`), while `pytorch/tensorflow/numpy/pandas` stayed in a "classical ML" cluster. **The LLM ecosystem developed its own identifiable co-occurrence footprint inside SWE JDs.**

5. **The agile/sql/javascript decline in arshkon→scraped (~2-3pp each) is almost entirely length-normalized away.** Density of `agile` barely changes; density of `sql` barely changes. The "decline" is a boilerplate-dilution effect: these terms appear in a fixed fraction of English sentences, and sentences-per-posting grew, diluting the per-posting mention rate even when absolute counts are stable. A paper-level claim that "agile declined" should *not* cite the raw mention rate; it should cite the length-normalized form (where the effect is ~null).

6. **Legacy .NET/jQuery/PHP decline is real and substantial.** Unlike agile/sql, these show up *below* the length baseline — their absolute density fell too. This is genuine stack rotation, not boilerplate dilution. `dotnet` fell 10.9% → 6.7% with SNR 3.6; `jquery` 2.8% → 1.0% SNR 5.3; `php` 2.4% → 1.1% SNR 3.1. These are the most robust declining techs.

7. **Rust did not break out.** Rust rose 3.5% → 6.9% (arshkon→scraped, +3.4pp, SNR 7.0) but did not form its own community in 2026 — it stays clustered with Python and C++ in C8. "Rust is replacing C++" is not what the data shows; both rose (Rust +3.4pp, C++ +7.9pp), they still coexist.

8. **Asaniczka structured skills validate extraction rates at ρ=0.985 but structured rates are systematically lower on broad-vocabulary techs.** This is the opposite of what the Wave 1.5 hypothesis predicted (that structured would catch things regex misses, particularly `c++` and `c#`). Reality: regex catches everything structured catches and finds more in narrative text. The one category where structured is slightly higher (`prompt_engineering`, `vector_database`) is techs so rare in description text that manual tagging in `skills_raw` picks them up better — but these rates are <0.5% and immaterial for cross-period comparison.

## Data quality caveats

- **Asaniczka junior_final cell is underpowered (n=228).** Only 3 skills clear BH-FDR, and the top skill (`polygraph`) is a defense-industry clearance artifact, not a general junior-SWE signal. Asaniczka `associate` remains a problematic junior baseline and should not be used alone.
- **The SNR metric is inflated when arshkon and asaniczka happen to agree.** Python, `css`, `claude`, `postgresql`, `github_actions` all have SNR > 30 driven by near-zero within-2024 noise. The z-test is a more stable signal than SNR alone.
- **AI-mention flag via OR of 24 AI-era techs is broad.** It includes `pytorch`/`tensorflow` which predate the LLM era. Restricting to a narrower LLM-only flag (`claude, copilot, langchain, rag, llm_token, openai, gemini, chatgpt, anthropic, mcp, llamaindex, agent_framework, cursor, fine_tuning, prompt_engineering, ollama`) would drop the scraped AI-mention rate but not change the integration-pattern result; the narrow-flag postings are a subset of the broad-flag postings.
- **Co-occurrence phi threshold 0.15 is a choice.** The Wave 1 spec didn't prescribe it. Looser threshold (0.10) would merge several 2026 singletons into larger clusters; tighter (0.20) fragments the frontend cluster. The headline LLM-vendor cluster is stable across 0.10–0.25.
- **Tech matrix doesn't include AI techniques like "embeddings", "transformer", "attention", "deep learning".** A richer AI taxonomy would catch more signal on the AI-domain side vs the AI-tool side. This is a Wave 1.5 taxonomy limit, not a T14 bug.
