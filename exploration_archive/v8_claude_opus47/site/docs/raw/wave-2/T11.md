### 4. The "management indicator" survives precision refinement

Gate 1 calibration flagged `management_strict_binary_share` SNR = 1.50 (marginal). I reproduced this result with the spec-listed STRICT pattern (`manage|mentor|coach|hire|direct reports|performance review|headcount|people management|team building`) and obtained SNR = 1.61 at all_no_spec level — within calibration range.

After the per-term precision sampling and the <80% drop rule, the **refined STRICT pattern drops `manage` and `team_building`** (precision 14% and 41% respectively against the manual rubric) and keeps `coach|headcount|hire|mentor|performance_review`. The refined binary share and SNR:

| bucket | refined strict share |
|---|---:|
| arshkon | 13.9% |
| asaniczka | 15.4% |
| scraped | 22.2% |

- within-2024 effect: -1.4pp
- cross (pooled) effect: +7.2pp
- **SNR (pooled) = 5.09** (up from 1.50/1.61 with the unrefined pattern)
- **SNR (arshkon baseline) = 5.87**

**Interpretation:** the unrefined strict pattern's low SNR was a *precision* problem, not a real effect absence. The noisy term was `manage`, which almost always appears in contexts like "manage data", "manage systems", "manage requirements" — not "manage people". Once the verb `manage` and the ambiguous `team_building` phrase are dropped, the pattern is dominated by `mentor` (13.9% in 2024 → 20.8% in 2026) and `hire` (1.2% → 1.7%), both precise people-management cues. The refined-strict share rose +7.2pp above a within-2024 noise of -1.4pp, an SNR of 5.09.

**Mentor-heavy signal.** `mentor` contributes 94% of the refined-strict hit volume. This makes the refined strict share better described as "mentorship is expected" than "people management is expected," but mentorship in a SWE JD IS a people-leadership responsibility and should be reported as such.

### 6. Per-term precision (sampled, rule-based classifier)

From 50 stratified samples per term × tier (25/25 across 2024/2026):

| term | precision (managerial_any) | precision (people_managerial) |
|---|---:|---:|
| mentor | 1.000 | 1.000 |
| coach | 1.000 | 1.000 |
| headcount | 1.000 | 1.000 |
| performance_review | 1.000 (n=1) | 1.000 |
| hire | 0.940 | 0.940 |
| team_building | 0.414 | 0.379 |
| manage | 0.140 | 0.080 |
| lead (BROAD) | 0.320 | 0.180 |
| team (BROAD) | 0.080 | 0.020 |
| stakeholder (BROAD) | 0.080 | 0.060 |
| coordinate (BROAD) | 0.020 | 0.020 |

**The BROAD pattern is >50% false-positive on `lead`, `team`, `stakeholder`, `coordinate`.** These are the terms that inflate Gate 1's `management_broad_binary_share` (87.6% scraped share, SNR 0.48, below-noise). Confirms the prior-wave warning that broad lead/team/stakeholder patterns inflate the management indicator 3–5×.

Classifier methodology: rule-based snippet scoring with PEOPLE_CUES (`direct reports`, `perform review`, `1:1s`, `hire/recruit`, `career development`, `mentor`, `coach`, `team of N`, `reports to`, `head of`), PROJECT_CUES (`lead the project`, `project manage`, `lead developer`, `manage the system/codebase`), and NOISE_CUES (`lead[- ]free`, `leading edge`, `team member`, `stakeholder expectations`, `manage data`, `manage requirements`, `configuration management`, etc.). Classes: MANAGERIAL_PEOPLE, MANAGERIAL_PROJECT, AMBIGUOUS_PROJECT, NON_MANAGERIAL_NOISE, OTHER_AMBIGUOUS. `precision_any` = share of samples in MANAGERIAL_PEOPLE ∪ MANAGERIAL_PROJECT. This rubric is auditable; per-sample classifications are in `T11_management_precision_sample.csv` for human override.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

- **Junior and senior complexity rose at roughly the same proportional rate (+34–39% in requirement_breadth).** I expected the redefinition narrative to predict a steeper rise at entry-level. It is NOT supported by the measured complexity metrics.
- **Senior org-scope rose nearly 2× (+88% on `org_scope_count` at S1).** Juniors only +63% (on a much smaller base). If any role is being "redefined upward" it is S1 — scope language is landing harder at senior than at entry.
- **Refined management STRICT is dominated by `mentor` (13.9% → 20.8%).** This is *mentorship expectation*, which in SWE JDs has historically been a senior/staff marker. A 7pp rise in "mentor" prevalence (+50% relative) across all postings is meaningful — it is consistent with the hypothesis that expected scope/leadership at existing roles has migrated down.
- **The unrefined STRICT pattern's "marginal" Gate-1 verdict was a precision artifact, not a real no-effect.** Dropping the noisy `manage` verb alone promotes SNR from 1.5 to 5.1. This demonstrates the value of mandated precision sampling before SNR judgements.
- **`description_core_llm` strips all HR-process language.** `direct reports`, `performance review`, `people management`, `headcount` are all near-zero in cleaned text but present in raw text. This is symmetric across periods and does not bias the ratio, but it means my STRICT pattern cannot use these ideal-world HR terms — it must lean on `mentor`, `hire`, `coach` as proxies.
- **`scope_density` (scope mentions per 1K chars) rose +47% at S1 but only +26% at J2.** Length-normalized, senior scope language is densifying faster than entry scope language. Consistent with senior-side scope inflation.
- **`education_level` modestly DROPPED cross-period** (arshkon 0.71, asaniczka 1.00, scraped 0.82). Pooled → scraped delta -0.12 levels (BS/MS/PhD ordinal). Within-2024 noise -0.30 dominates. Not a credible trend — within-2024 variance is the arshkon-vs-asaniczka gap on degree-mention frequency. **Education requirements are not measurably moving.**
- **`yoe_numeric` is flat.** Pooled 5.08, scraped 4.51. Within-2024 -0.64, cross -0.43. SNR 0.67 below-noise. **YOE asks are NOT meaningfully shifting** in the cross-period comparison. Seniority labels and complexity are moving; stated years of experience is not.
- **90% of the requirement_breadth top-1% outliers are scraped 2026.** Not evenly distributed. The long tail is lengthening with time.

## Essential sensitivities

- **(a) Aggregator exclusion:** applied (via default filter, `is_aggregator = false` in the source parquet and specialist-list exclusion on top).
- **(b) Length normalization:** `tech_density` and `scope_density` (per-1K-chars) are reported alongside raw counts. Tech density is flat cross-period because descriptions grew as much as tech mentions; scope density rose at S1 (+30–47%) because scope mentions grew faster than descriptions.
- **(c) Seniority operationalization:** J2 primary + J3 robustness; S1 primary + S4 robustness. All reported.
- **(f) Text-source composition:** restricted to `text_source='llm'`. Raw-text inclusion would over-count boilerplate (scraped has 70% raw, 30% LLM) and inflate length-based metrics — explicitly avoided per Gate 1 rule.
