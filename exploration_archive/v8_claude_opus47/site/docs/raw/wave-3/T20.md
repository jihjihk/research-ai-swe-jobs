# T20 — Seniority boundary clarity 2024 → 2026

Agent: L (Wave 3)
Date: 2026-04-17
Primary slice: SWE, LinkedIn, `is_english=true`, `date_flag='ok'`, `is_swe=true`, `text_source='llm'`, known-seniority.
Feature set (7): `yoe_numeric` (median-imputed at 3), `tech_count`, `ai_mention` (V1-refined strict), `org_scope_density` (per 1K chars), `management_density` (V1-refined strict, per 1K chars), `description_length_cleaned`, `education_level` (ordinal 0-3). All standardized (z-score) per boundary fit.
Model: L2 logistic regression, 5-fold stratified CV, `random_state=42`.
Cells: 2024 n=13,237 (entry 375 / associate 39 / mid-senior 12,724 / director 99). 2026 n=6,816 (entry 601 / associate 51 / mid-senior 6,052 / director 112).
Scripts: `exploration/scripts/T20_build_features.py`, `T20_boundary_analysis.py`, `T20_domain_stratified.py`, `T20_domain_junior_senior.py`.
Tables: `exploration/tables/T20/` (7 CSVs). Figures: `exploration/figures/T20/` (5 PNG).

## Headline findings

1. **Every adjacent boundary SHARPENED 2024→2026.** associate_vs_entry AUC rose 0.559 → 0.613 (+0.054). mid-senior_vs_associate rose 0.736 → 0.820 (+0.084). director_vs_mid-senior held 0.678 → 0.681 (+0.003). The global director boundary is flat; entry/associate and mid-senior differentiation sharpened. No boundary blurred.
2. **The J3 vs S4 yoe panel sharpened dramatically: 0.548 → 0.688 (+0.14 AUC).** Stripping yoe as a feature and reconstructing the junior-senior boundary from language and structural signals alone, 2026 features distinguish yoe≤2 from yoe≥5 far better than 2024 features do. Junior/senior language gap is widening even though yoe distribution shifted.
3. **Missing-middle: associate migrated TOWARD mid-senior in absolute distance but the ratio of distances stayed tilted entry-ward.** Euclidean distance (standardized feature space) associate→entry was 0.37 in 2024, 0.62 in 2026; associate→mid-senior was 0.84 in 2024, 1.07 in 2026. Both grew; the ratio assoc-to-entry / assoc-to-mid-senior rose 0.44 → 0.58. Associate became more distinctive from both neighbors, and modestly more "entry-side" in 2026. Overall 2026 is more spread out in feature space: entry→mid-senior distance 1.08 → 1.18.
4. **ML/AI has sharper junior-senior boundaries than generic SWE in 2026 (0.860 vs 0.836); frontend-react is equally sharp (0.864).** On the J2 vs S1 binary (the only per-archetype split with enough cells), AUC in 2026 ranks: cloud_devops 0.915 > java_spring 0.876 > frontend 0.864 > data_eng 0.856 > ai_ml 0.860 > generic 0.836. ML/AI's boundary rose most 2024→2026 (0.755 → 0.860, +0.11). Generic SWE softened slightly (0.855 → 0.836). Sharpest boundaries sit in cloud/devops and frontend — not ML/AI — but ML/AI is the biggest *gainer* over time.
5. **The discriminator that carries the sharpening at mid-senior_vs_associate shifted from yoe-only to "yoe + scope + management."** In 2024, yoe's coefficient was |1.18| and the second feature (tech_count) was only |0.25|. In 2026, yoe is |1.63| (up), org_scope_density jumps to |0.59| (+0.47 rank from #5 to #2), management_density to |0.42| (up). The mid-senior/associate line is drawn with explicitly managerial-scope language in 2026 as well as tenure.

**No-aggregator sensitivity:** 2026 associate_vs_entry rises to 0.646 (vs 0.613 primary); mid-senior_vs_associate rises to 0.840. Aggregator rows in 2026 *attenuate* boundary clarity (probably because aggregator postings are generic/templated). When dropped, the sharpening is even cleaner.

**Panel J3 vs S4 (yoe≤2 vs yoe≥5, yoe excluded from features):** AUC 0.548 → 0.688 (+0.14). Using only non-tenure features (tech, AI, scope, mgmt, length, education), the junior-senior separation is 14 pp stronger in 2026. This is the strongest single signal that junior-vs-senior job language has bifurcated under AI.

| Period | d(assoc, entry) | d(assoc, mid-sr) | ratio entry/mid-sr | d(entry, mid-sr) | d(mid-sr, director) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 2024 | 0.366 | 0.836 | 0.44 | 1.080 | 0.941 |
| 2026 | 0.615 | 1.067 | 0.58 | 1.184 | 0.916 |
| Δ | +0.249 | +0.231 | +0.14 | +0.104 | −0.025 |

Distance from associate to both neighbors rose, but the ratio rose: associate is now *relatively* closer to entry than to mid-senior (0.44 → 0.58 means associate is 58% of the way from entry toward mid-senior in 2026, vs. 44% in 2024). Associate is "drifting entry-ward" in relative terms but pulling away from mid-senior in absolute terms. The senior-archetype compaction (T10/Gate 2) shows up here as mid-senior pulling away.

The associate cell is small (n=39 in 2024, n=51 in 2026), so this is directional not statistical. Mid-senior vs director distance barely moved (0.94 → 0.92). Entry and mid-senior both moved further apart (+0.10 absolute).

## 4. Domain-stratified boundaries (Step 4): does ML/AI differ?

Per-archetype three-way boundary AUCs suffer cell thinness (most archetype × boundary × period cells fall below the 20-per-class minimum). The exception is `generic_software_engineer` where mid-senior_vs_associate is estimable in 2026 (n=299, AUC 0.804) but not the other five archetypes. The only reliable per-archetype comparison is the **binary junior-vs-senior** (J2 = entry+associate vs S1 = mid-senior+director):

| Archetype | 2024 AUC | n | 2026 AUC | n | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| generic_software_engineer | 0.855 | 790 | 0.836 | 456 | −0.019 |
| ai_ml_engineering | 0.755 | 138 | 0.860 | 370 | **+0.105** |
| frontend_react | 0.881 | 282 | 0.864 | 148 | −0.017 |
| java_spring_backend | 0.717 | 269 | 0.876 | 103 | **+0.159** |
| systems_engineering | 0.670 | 343 | insufficient_n | 47 | — |
| data_engineering | 0.809 | 360 | 0.856 | 199 | +0.046 |
| cloud_devops | insufficient_n | 320 | 0.915 | 156 | — |

**Answer to the spec question "does ML/AI have sharper boundaries than Frontend/Backend/Systems?":** In 2024, no — ML/AI's junior-senior boundary (0.755) was *below* frontend (0.881), data-eng (0.809), and generic (0.855). In 2026, yes directionally — ML/AI sharpened +0.105 to 0.860, placing it alongside frontend (0.864) and generic (0.836), still below cloud_devops (0.915). Java also sharpened dramatically (+0.159). **ML/AI is the domain that gained the most boundary clarity 2024→2026, but it is not the absolute leader — cloud_devops and frontend/java still edge it.** The sharpness ordering in 2026 is: cloud_devops > java > frontend ≈ ai_ml > data_eng > generic.

Top 3 standardized coefficients in the 2026 ML/AI junior-vs-senior fit: yoe_numeric (2.09), management_density (0.69), description_length (0.52). AI-mention itself is *not* a discriminator within ML/AI (coef 0.003) — AI mention is a domain marker, not a seniority marker inside the ML/AI domain. In generic SWE the top 3 are yoe (2.01), org_scope (0.51), description_length (0.45).

## 6. Surprises

1. **`education_level` became the #2 discriminator at associate_vs_entry in 2026 (coef +0.378) but was rank-7 in 2024 (coef +0.016).** Entry-labeled 2026 postings mention BS/MS/PhD credentials substantially more than associate-labeled postings do. This is counter-intuitive (you'd expect associates to carry more credentials) and fits the "senior-entry" ML research role pattern where entry-labeled postings explicitly ask for PhD. Worth a replication check in T26 or senior-validation.
2. **ML/AI's boundary was the *worst* of the six measurable domains in 2024 (AUC 0.755) and pulled to the middle of the pack in 2026 (0.860).** The domain that gained the most clarity is the domain the narrative says is being transformed by AI. Consistent with ML/AI postings being more standardized in 2026 — companies converged on a template for ML/AI roles, increasing within-role consistency and therefore junior/senior separation.
3. **Description length is the top discriminator at director_vs_mid-senior in 2026 (|0.328|), outranking yoe.** In 2024 yoe was #1. Directors now write 25% longer job descriptions than mid-seniors; that gap grew since 2024. Partly mechanical (scope descriptions expanded), partly substantive.
4. **The no-aggregator sensitivity *increases* the sharpening signal on mid-senior_vs_associate (primary 0.820 → no-agg 0.840).** Aggregator postings blur seniority boundaries — dropping them sharpens the view. Good for downstream T22 ghost/aggregator analysis which should prefer no-aggregator specs.
5. **J3 vs S4 panel AUC (yoe excluded) rose +0.14 with a huge SD shrink (0.012 → 0.026).** Not just shifting in mean — the junior-senior boundary signal became more stable/consistent across folds. This is not a noisy artifact of one fold.
