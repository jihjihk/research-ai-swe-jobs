# Prior art: the "AI is clustered in tech hubs" narrative

The dominant 2024-2026 narrative in prominent outlets is that AI hiring
remains geographically **concentrated** in a small set of coastal hubs — the
Bay Area above all, then Seattle and New York. A secondary, softer narrative
("the rest is catching up") exists but is always framed as modest catch-up
from a tiny base, never as *outward diffusion of the dominant archetype*.

## Source 1 — Brookings, "Mapping the AI Economy" (July 2025)
- **Authors:** Mark Muro, Shriya Methkupally, Yang You (Brookings Metro)
- **URL:** https://www.brookings.edu/articles/mapping-the-ai-economy-which-regions-are-ready-for-the-next-technology-leap/
- **Claim:** San Francisco and San Jose are "unmatched" AI "superstars"; 28 star-hub metros come close but remain below them.
- **Quant:** Superstars + star hubs together capture **67%** of all US AI job postings. Bay Area alone = **13%**; Seattle = **7%**. Only "modest diffusion beyond primary AI centers."

## Source 2 — Brookings, "Without intervention, genAI jobs will continue to cluster" (2024)
- **Author:** Mark Muro et al.
- **URL:** https://www.brookings.edu/articles/new-data-shows-that-without-intervention-generative-ai-jobs-will-continue-to-cluster-in-the-same-big-tech-hubs/
- **Claim:** Absent deliberate policy intervention, generative-AI jobs will keep clustering in the same incumbent tech hubs.
- **Quant:** Top metros' share of genAI postings is **rising**, not falling; framed as a self-reinforcing concentration.

## Source 3 — PitchBook, "Q4 2025 Analyst Note: AI, Megadeals, and a Concentrated Venture Market"
- **URL:** https://pitchbook.com/news/reports/q4-2025-pitchbook-analyst-note-ai-megadeals-and-the-making-of-a-concentrated-venture-market
- **Claim:** AI capital is collapsing into a handful of metros; "rise of the rest" has reversed.
- **Quant:** SF Bay Area = **52.4%** of all US AI/ML VC in 2025 (up from ~40% pre-2023); ~**70%** of all US VC goes to Bay Area + NYC + LA + Boston. SF metro alone raised **$152.5B** in 2025.

## Source 4 — CBRE, "San Francisco Bay Area Sees Surge in Tech Talent with AI Skills" (2025)
- **URL:** https://www.cbre.com/press-releases/san-francisco-bay-area-sees-surge-in-tech-talent-with-ai-skills-fueling-next-tech-growth-cycle
- **Claim:** AI-skilled talent growth concentrating in existing majors.
- **Quant:** AI-skilled tech workforce **+50% YoY** to ~517k, growth "concentrating in existing major markets — particularly SF, NYC, Toronto." SF AI-skilled talent **+24%** YoY; AI roles in San Jose at **6x** national average.

## Flag — novelty check

I found **no prominent source** predicting that the ML/LLM archetype itself
would diffuse outward such that non-hub metros would post **higher** AI-mention
gains than Seattle or SF. The closest hedges:

- Brookings (2025) notes "modest diffusion" to Texas Triangle and emerging
  metros — but frames them as *catching up from a small base*, with superstars
  still pulling ahead in absolute terms.
- allwork.space (Mar 2026) headline "Silicon Valley Still Dominates AI Hiring,
  But These New U.S. Tech Hubs Are Emerging" names Dallas, Huntsville,
  Northwest Arkansas — again as emerging, not leading.

**None** claim Atlanta / Tampa / Miami / Salt Lake City are out-gaining SF or
Seattle in AI-mention growth, and **none** argue the tech-hub premium is under
2 pp. The piece's finding is genuinely novel against this prior art.
