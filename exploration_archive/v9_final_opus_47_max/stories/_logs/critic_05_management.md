# Critic notes — 05_management_never_there.md

**Verdict:** Publishable with targeted fixes; the arithmetic slip and the implicit "these papers are wrong" framing are the two must-fix items.

## Required revisions

- **Arithmetic: "one in three" is wrong.** 28% precision = 1 in 3.57, not 1 in 3. Write "roughly one in 3.5" or "fewer than one in three." As written, the piece rounds *toward* charity to its own finding, which is the opposite of what an adversarial methods piece should do.

- **All three precision numbers (0.28, 0.55, 0.98) are in the evidence block — good.** But the "worst sub-patterns" stats (hire 7%, performance review 25%) are cited as facts; evidence block confirms them (row 2). OK. No other unsourced quantitatives.

- **Framing of Deming & Kahn / Acemoglu et al. / Lightcast.** The draft currently says their patterns are "not validated," which is correct, but the surrounding rhetoric ("decade of academic output," "unfalsifiable results," the whole "artifact" framing of the lead case) invites the reader to infer that *their* findings also collapse. That is not established. Add one sentence, explicitly: "We do not claim these findings are artifacts; we claim the reader cannot tell, because the test was not run." Without this, the piece is unfair.

- **"Decade of academic output."** Four sources — three unvalidated, one counter-example — does not license a decade-wide claim. Either soften to "a visible strand of the literature" / "leading examples in the genre," or add a footnote acknowledging this is an illustrative not exhaustive audit.

- **Hansen et al. citation.** Fair in direction but the piece uses it to imply "if remote needed this, everything does." That is a rhetorical leap — remote is a known-hard case precisely because the same token has two senses. The generality claim ("a dozen others almost certainly do too") should be hedged to "plausibly" or supported.

- **Missing CI caveat on the 28%.** The evidence block itself notes ±5pp uncertainty on repeated sampling. The main text states 28% as a point estimate with no uncertainty. Add half a sentence: "measured on a 50-posting sample; repeat sampling would move this a few points either way, but not near 0.80."

- **"Table stakes" recommendation.** Well-calibrated for gray literature (Lightcast). Slightly strong for peer-reviewed work already in print; reframe as prospective norm, not retroactive indictment.

- **Rhetorical overreach:** "unfalsifiable results" (para 6) — too strong, change to "results that cannot be independently checked." "Invitation for" reads as imputing motive.
