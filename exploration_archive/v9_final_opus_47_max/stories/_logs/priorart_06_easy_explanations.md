# Prior-art brief: the five convenient stories already in public circulation

Each explanation we rejected has a loud public champion. Below, ranked by prior-art volume, are the stories the piece is pushing against.

## Ranking (loudest first)

1. **LLM-authorship mediation** — ubiquitous; covered by Fortune, SHRM, Josh Bersin.
2. **Hiring-bar lowering** — strong but contested; HR Dive and LinkedIn Talent push "skills-based" / degree-drop narrative.
3. **Legacy-to-AI substitution** — loud in developer press (GitHub Blog, Stack Overflow, Medium).
4. **Hiring-selectivity (JOLTS trough)** — loud in macro press (Indeed Hiring Lab, KPMG, CNN) but rarely linked to JD content.
5. **Sampling-frame artifact** — quietest in popular press; lives in methods footnotes (Lightcast/BGT representativeness literature).

## Sources by claim

### 1. LLM-authorship mediation — "ChatGPT is writing the JDs"

- Chloe Berger, **Fortune**, "Recruiters caution against using AI to write job postings because it's been trained on 'crappy' descriptions," 2025-09-30 — AI drafts are "pulling in all the badly-written job descriptions that exist in the world"; recruiters warn of generic, bloated copy. https://fortune.com/2025/09/30/recruiters-caution-against-using-ai-write-job-postings-been-trained-on-crappy-descriptions/
- Josh Bersin, **joshbersin.com**, "Job Redesign Around AI," 2025-03 — AI now "compresses weeks of tedious [JD] work into an intelligent automated workflow in minutes." https://joshbersin.com/2025/03/job-redesign-around-ai-work-intelligence-tools-arrive/
- SHRM Talent Trends 2025 (cited widely) — 66% of AI-using recruiting organizations apply ChatGPT to job descriptions.

### 2. Hiring-bar lowering — "slack market, easier asks"

- Ginger Christ, **HR Dive**, "1 in 4 employers say they'll eliminate degree requirements by year's end," 2025 — 25% dropping BA requirements; 38% swapping in skills assessments. https://www.hrdive.com/news/employer-eliminate-degree-requirements-2025/748998/
- Cory Stahle, **Indeed Hiring Lab**, "Experience Requirements Have Tightened Amid the Tech Hiring Freeze," 2025-07-30 — counter-evidence, but the framing that tech companies *could* loosen is everywhere. https://www.hiringlab.org/2025/07/30/experience-requirements-have-tightened-amid-the-tech-hiring-freeze/
- **LinkedIn Future of Recruiting 2025** — companies urged to refine JDs to "attract broader talent pools." https://business.linkedin.com/talent-solutions/resources/future-of-recruiting

### 3. Hiring-selectivity / JOLTS trough — "tight budgets, picky firms"

- **Indeed Hiring Lab**, "December 2025 JOLTS Report: Balance or Breaking Point?" 2026-02-05 — hiring rate at 3.1%, lowest since April 2020. https://www.hiringlab.org/2026/02/05/december-2025-jolts-report-balance-or-breaking-point/
- **McKinsey State of AI 2025** (via FinalRoundAI recap, 2025-11) — 30% of firms planning AI-driven cuts; workforce-reducers outnumber adders 2:1. https://www.finalroundai.com/blog/mckinsey-report-layoffs-due-to-ai
- Bryan Mena, **CNN Business**, "US job openings hit lowest level in more than a year," 2026-01-07 — openings slump to 7.15M. https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/07/economy/us-jolts-job-openings-layoffs-november

### 4. Sampling-frame artifact — "different firm mixes, not a real shift"

- Hyeri Choi & Ioana Marinescu, **SSRN / Data in Brief**, "Data for labor market concentration using Lightcast," 2024 — canonical representativeness caveat: online postings over-represent professional roles vs JOLTS. https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4695576
- Washington ESD, "Representativeness Analysis of the Burning Glass Job Posting Data" — industry/geography tilt acknowledged. https://esd.wa.gov/media/pdf/2046/market20representation20new20uspdf/download?inline=

### 5. Legacy-to-AI substitution — "Java/COBOL architects replaced by AI engineers"

- **GitHub Blog**, "How GitHub Copilot and AI agents are saving legacy systems," 2025 — AI reshaping legacy-modernization roles. https://github.blog/ai-and-ml/github-copilot/how-github-copilot-and-ai-agents-are-saving-legacy-systems/
- **Stack Overflow Blog**, "AI vs Gen Z: How AI has changed the career pathway for junior developers," 2025-12-26 — 20% decline in 22-25 developer employment; junior ladder substituted for AI-oversight roles. https://stackoverflow.blog/2025/12/26/ai-vs-gen-z/
- Brynjolfsson, Chandar & Chen, **Stanford Digital Economy Lab**, "Canaries in the Coal Mine?" 2025-08 — 16% employment decline for early-career workers in AI-exposed occupations; substitution narrative at its most academically credentialed. https://digitaleconomy.stanford.edu/publication/canaries-in-the-coal-mine-six-facts-about-the-recent-employment-effects-of-artificial-intelligence/
