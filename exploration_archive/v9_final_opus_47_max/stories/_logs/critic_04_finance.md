# Critic — 04_finance_densest

## Revisions required

- **Internal contradiction on the headline stat.** Prose says the two lines "are indistinguishable on the Wilson confidence interval." Evidence block says "significant at 95% (non-overlapping Wilson CI)." They cannot both be true. Recompute: FinSvcs 15.74% (14.13–17.51) vs SoftDev 15.25% at n=6,870 has a Wilson CI of roughly 14.42–16.10. These overlap. Evidence block is wrong; prose is closer to right — but then the piece's climax ("they are ahead," "setting the pace") becomes unearned. Pick one story: indistinguishable (the honest read) or ahead. It cannot be both within one column.

- **The 0.5pp gap is not material.** A 0.49pp difference on a 15% base, with overlapping CIs, is noise. Headline metaphor ("writing as if they were competing with Anthropic") overclaims. Downgrade to "parity," which is still a strong story against the Deloitte/McKinsey narrative.

- **Unsourced "fifteen-fold" growth.** Paragraph 8 claims the applied-AI archetype "grew fifteen-fold in the dataset between 2024 and 2026." Not in the evidence block. Worse, the sensitivity verdict explicitly forbids 2024–2026 cross-period claims ("industry drift"). Either cite it or strike it. Recommend strike.

- **"Twenty distinct AI-tool references" for Citigroup.** Evidence says "20 hits, 5.91/1K chars" — hits, not distinct tools. The prose lists eight. Rephrase as "twenty AI-tool mentions" or cut the number.

- **Health care buried.** 20.87% exceeds every financial cell and the overall tech share. It gets one adverb ("conspicuously") and no follow-up. This may be a better piece. At minimum, the finance column should own the fact that finance is not the densest — health care is — and explain why the column is about finance anyway (the opponent narrative). Currently reads like lede-burying.

- **Layer mismatch partly acknowledged, partly elided.** Paragraph 7 concedes the consultancy metrics measure production and the posting data measures hiring language. Good. But paragraph 2 still treats the Deloitte/McKinsey/Gartner claims as directly refuted ("say something different"). Soften the frame: the narrative is incomplete, not wrong.

- **"Compliance slows the model approval. It does not slow the hiring."** Rhetorical, and not in evidence. The data shows hiring-language density; it says nothing about whether compliance is or isn't slowing hiring. Cut or reframe as open question.

- **Small-n protagonists.** BlackRock 44%, Wells Fargo 36%, SoFi 33% at n≥10 is thin. Add the n or flag the filter in-prose (one phrase: "among firms with at least ten postings").

- **Taxonomy-drift caveat invisible.** The 2026-only constraint is load-bearing and appears nowhere in prose. Add one clause.

- **Length.** At ~600 words with contradictions and unsourced claims, the piece has not earned it. Tighten to 450 after fixes.

## Verdict

Provocative frame, but the headline comparison is not statistically what the piece claims, and an unsourced 2024–2026 growth number violates the project's own drift rule — rewrite before filing.
