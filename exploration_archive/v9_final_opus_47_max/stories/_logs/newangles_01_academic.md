# New candidate angles from recent academic labor-economics

Ten specific, quantitative claims from 2024-2026 NBER / SSRN / digital-lab work, beyond the 8 drafted pieces. All testable on the 68k LinkedIn SWE-postings panel.

## 1. Wage compression inside exposed roles (HIGH, contrarian)

- **Source:** Klein Teeselink, SSRN 5516798 (2025). Data-led diff-in-diff.
- **Claim:** UK exposed occupations lost 23.4% of postings and GBP 2,951 (6.3%) of advertised salary; high-salary occupations dropped 34.2% vs. near-zero for low-salary.
- **Test:** archetype x salary-band x period; does Applied-AI (piece 7) show pay stagnation despite higher YOE?
- **vs. 8 pieces:** ORTHOGONAL - tests wage side.
- **Headline:** "Denser AI postings, thinner paychecks: UK compression on the US side."

## 2. U-shape across education tiers (HIGH)

- **Source:** Hosseini Maasoum & Lichtinger, SSRN 5425555 (2025). 62M workers, 285k firms.
- **Claim:** Junior declines at adopting firms are largest for mid-tier graduates; elite and low-tier less affected.
- **Test:** company-prestige tier (Fortune 500 vs. mid-market vs. small) x seniority x period.
- **vs. 8 pieces:** REINFORCES piece 1, adds new axis.
- **Headline:** "Not all juniors: the missing middle in AI hiring."

## 3. Task-dispersion offsets task-exposure (background)

- **Source:** Hampole, Papanikolaou, Schmidt, Seegmiller, NBER 33509 (Feb 2025). Data-led.
- **Claim:** Mean task exposure depresses within-firm labor demand; concentration in few tasks offsets losses via reallocation.
- **Test:** AI-pattern coverage across JD sections as dispersion proxy.
- **vs. 8 pieces:** ORTHOGONAL, lens for piece 6. Analytical background.

## 4. AI adopters hire MORE juniors (HIGH, contrarian)

- **Source:** Babina, Fedyk, He, Hodson, JFE 151 (2024). Data-led, firm-level AI investment.
- **Claim:** AI-investing firms flatten hierarchy with a significant rise in junior-worker share; growth concentrates in larger firms.
- **Test:** firm-AI-intensity quartile x period x seniority-mix within firm.
- **vs. 8 pieces:** AGREES with piece 1 but supplies complementarity mechanism, not displacement.
- **Headline:** "AI firms hire more juniors, not fewer: Babina replicated on SWE postings."

## 5. LLM earnings effects are near zero (HIGH)

- **Source:** Humlum & Vestergaard, NBER 33777 / BFI 2025-56. Danish linked employer-employee.
- **Claim:** Chatbots save 3% of time; diff-in-diff CIs rule out earnings effects >1%; new tasks for only 8.4% of workers; cross-sectional premia vanish under selection controls.
- **Test:** AI-pattern density x advertised-comp trajectory, same firm-title over time.
- **vs. 8 pieces:** AGREES with piece 2, extends puzzle to pay.
- **Headline:** "Copilot in every IDE, not in any paycheck."

## 6. AI lengthens the workday (HIGH, contrarian)

- **Source:** "AI and the Extended Workday," NBER 33536 (2025). Data-led.
- **Claim:** Higher AI exposure is associated with longer hours and reduced leisure, strongest in competitive labor markets.
- **Test:** phrases "fast-paced", "on-call", "extended hours" x AI-pattern density x industry concentration.
- **vs. 8 pieces:** ORTHOGONAL.
- **Headline:** "More AI, longer day: the productivity paradox inside JDs."

## 7. Junior declines are slower hiring, not layoffs (background)

- **Source:** Hosseini & Lichtinger (2025); Fed Note "AI Adoption and Firms' Job-Posting Behavior" (Mar 2026).
- **Claim:** Junior decline driven by slower hiring; high-AI-exposure postings fall -0.14% at 2y and -0.30% at 5y, concentrated in non-IT firms.
- **Test:** posting-flow vs. stock, junior-to-senior ratio trajectory, tech vs. non-tech firms.
- **vs. 8 pieces:** MECHANISM for piece 1.

## 8. Copilot raises output 5.9% and language exploration 22% (HIGH)

- **Source:** Hoffmann, Boysel, Nagle, Peng, Xu, HBS WP 25-021 (Apr 2025). OSS natural experiment.
- **Claim:** Copilot raises project-level contributions 5.9%, individual 2.1%, and new-language exposure 22%.
- **Test:** breadth of languages/frameworks in JDs x AI-pattern density x period.
- **vs. 8 pieces:** AGREES with piece 7, adds skill-surface dimension.
- **Headline:** "AI postings demand polyglots: the complementarity fingerprint."

## 9. Tech experience-requirement inflation (HIGH)

- **Source:** Indeed Hiring Lab, "Experience Requirements Have Tightened" (Jul 2025).
- **Claim:** Q2-2022 to Q2-2025: tech postings requiring 5+ years rose 37% -> 42%; senior titles down 19%, junior/standard down 34%.
- **Test:** YOE-requirement distribution over time, same company and title stubs; is inflation orthogonal to AI density or driven by it?
- **vs. 8 pieces:** REINFORCES piece 1, extends piece 7.
- **Headline:** "The 5-year floor: how entry got redefined."

## 10. AI-skills wage premium keeps rising (HIGH)

- **Source:** Lightcast Global AI Skills Outlook (Jul 2025); PwC Global AI Jobs Barometer (2025).
- **Claim:** AI-skills wage premium rose from ~25% (2023) to 56% (PwC 2024) / 28% (Lightcast 2025); 51% of AI-skill postings sit outside IT/CS.
- **Test:** advertised comp where present, AI-pattern flag x year x industry; does premium differ in regulated finance (piece 4)?
- **vs. 8 pieces:** TENSION with claim 1.
- **Headline:** "Does the AI premium survive in SWE? Finance says yes; rest says maybe."
