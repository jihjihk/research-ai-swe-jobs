# Prior art: "Regulated finance is slow on AI"

## Conventional-wisdom sources (sector trails tech, compliance is the brake)

1. **Deloitte — 2026 Banking & Capital Markets Outlook** (Deloitte Center for Financial Services, late 2025). Claim: bank AI implementation is "throttled by brittle and fragmented data foundations, mounting compliance demands, outdated legacy systems, and internal resistance to change," with initiatives "stuck in isolated proofs of concept." Quant: 36% of respondents prefer "incremental adoption… risk mitigation ahead of scaling."
   URL: https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/what-we-do/capabilities/applied-artificial-intelligence/blogs/pulse-check-series-latest-ai-developments/ai-adoption-challenges-ai-trends.html

2. **McKinsey — "Banking's AI angst"** (McKinsey Financial Services, 2025). Claim: banks are stuck in "pilot purgatory"; slow adopters risk "fighting over diminished profits." Quant: >90% of bank data users say data is unavailable/slow; 81% cite data quality as a top blocker.
   URL: https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/week-in-charts/bankings-ai-angst

3. **Gartner via Argano / mobileLIVE** (2025 AI Maturity Curve, cited Q4 2025). Claim: "73% of banking AI initiatives never make it past the pilot stage"; only 11% of financial firms report measurable ROI. Quant: 84% of financial-services agentic-AI pilots have not moved to production (just 16% in production).
   URL: https://www.mobilelive.ai/blog/2026-trends-guiding-financial-services-out-of-ai-pilot-purgatory

4. **Deskpro Research — "AI Adoption: 92% in Tech vs. 58% in Regulated Industries"** (2025). Claim: a 34-point adoption gap between tech and regulated sectors, attributed to pre-deployment compliance validation (HIPAA, PCI DSS, FedRAMP). Quant: 92% vs. 58%.
   URL: https://www.deskpro.com/blog/ai-adoption-report-support-operations-regulated-industries

5. **American Banker — "AI adoption at banks is triggering a compliance nightmare"** (opinion, 2025). Claim: banks "slow their own adoption" because each institution "improvises" governance from scratch; 63% of surveyed banks have no AI governance policy.
   URL: https://www.americanbanker.com/opinion/ai-adoption-at-banks-is-triggering-a-compliance-nightmare

## Counterfactual — banks ARE hiring hard (supports our 57.4% top-cell finding)

6. **CNBC — "JPMorgan Chase's blueprint to become the world's first fully AI-powered megabank"** (Sept 30, 2025). Claim: JPM's **LLM Suite** went from zero to 200,000 onboarded users in 8 months; ~250,000 employees now have access; 450 gen-AI use cases in production. Tech budget: **$19.8B in 2026 (+10% YoY), largest in industry**. American Banker named LLM Suite 2025 "Innovation of the Year."
   URL: https://www.cnbc.com/2025/09/30/jpmorgan-chase-fully-ai-connected-megabank.html

7. **Citi Careers + eFinancialCareers** (2025–2026). Claim: Citi is posting Lead/Senior AI Engineer (VP/SVP) roles across NY, Jersey City, London, Mississauga, Tampa — building "agentic AI frameworks" integrating OpenAI, Anthropic, Google LLMs. Quant: $300K salaries for new NYC Generative AI team in investment banking.
   URLs: https://jobs.citi.com/job/jersey-city/lead-ai-engineer-for-banking-technology/287/94029763344 ; https://www.efinancialcareers.com/news/citi-generative-ai-in-investment-banking

## Take for the piece

Conventional wisdom (sources 1–5, from the top consultancies and trade press) is unambiguous: regulated finance lags tech on AI because of compliance. The counterfactual (6–7) is about spending and internal tools — **not** about who is doing the densest AI hiring at the posting level. That is where our 57.4% top-cell finding becomes a genuine surprise rather than old news: it is not just "JPM has a chatbot," it is that the densest AI-requirement postings in the US SWE market have tilted from Big Tech toward the regulated balance sheets.
