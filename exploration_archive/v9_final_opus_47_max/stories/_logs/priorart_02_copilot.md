# Copilot paradox — prior-art sources

## "Table stakes" narrative (employer-side commentary)

6. **McKinsey, "Unleashing developer productivity with generative AI" (2024, updated 2025).**
   - Claim: "More than 90% of software teams surveyed use AI for core engineering tasks"; teams report 20–30% efficiency gains.
   - URL: https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/tech-and-ai/our-insights/unleashing-developer-productivity-with-generative-ai
   - Quant: **>90% of teams use AI for core engineering**; 6 hrs/week saved.

## Headline-anchor contrast

The widest reputable gap between worker-side use and employer codification:

- **Stack Overflow 2024: 82% of professional developers use AI tools to write code.**
- **McKinsey 2024: >90% of software teams use AI for core engineering.**
- **GitHub/MSFT: Copilot in 90% of Fortune 100.**
- **This study: GitHub Copilot named in 0.10% of 2026 SWE JDs.**

Sharpest single-line contrast for the lede: **"90% of Fortune 100 companies use Copilot; 0.1% of their job postings say so."** Or the worker-side version: **"82% of developers code with AI; 1 in 1,000 job ads ask them to."**
