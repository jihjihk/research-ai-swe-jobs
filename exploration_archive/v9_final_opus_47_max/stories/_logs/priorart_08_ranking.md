# Prior-art brief — "Employers rank right, they just can't count"

The finding: Spearman rho = +0.92 between worker-side AI usage and employer-side AI requirements across 16 occupation subgroups. Employer codification is 10–30% of worker adoption (accountants 72×, SWE ~7×, nurses ∞). The market has the **pattern** right; only the **level** is off. Below, seven recent sources working adjacent terrain — none draws the rank-vs-level distinction.

## Sources

1. **McKinsey, "Superagency in the Workplace" (Jan 2025)** — Singla, Sukharevsky, Yee.
   URL: https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/tech-and-ai/our-insights/superagency-in-the-workplace-empowering-people-to-unlock-ais-full-potential-at-work
   Claim: "C-suite executives estimate only 4% of employees use gen AI for 30%+ of daily work; the true figure is ~13%." **Level disagreement only.** No occupation-level ranking comparison.

2. **BCG, "AI at Work 2025: Momentum Builds, but Gaps Remain"** — Kotlyar et al., Oct 2025.
   URL: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/ai-at-work-momentum-builds-but-gaps-remain
   Claim: ~75% of employees use AI regularly; only 36% are satisfied with employer training; frontline workers hit a "silicon ceiling." **Level (employer formalization << worker usage).** Rank not discussed.

3. **MIT / Fortune, "Shadow AI Economy"** (Aug 2025).
   URL: https://fortune.com/2025/08/19/shadow-ai-economy-mit-study-genai-divide-llm-chatbots/
   Claim: Only 40% of companies buy official LLM subscriptions; 90%+ of firms have employees using personal AI tools for work; 78% use unapproved tools. **Level / formalization gap.** Cross-occupational rank not attempted.

4. **World Economic Forum, "The AI perception gap" (Jan 2026)**.
   URL: https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/01/ai-perception-gap/
   Claim: Workers acknowledge AI's importance but "underestimate its impact on their own roles"; mid-level roles face greater pressure than entry-level. **Both level and role-mix, framed psychologically — not as market codification vs usage.**

5. **Federal Reserve FEDS Note, "AI Adoption and Firms' Job-Posting Behavior"** (Mar 2026).
   URL: https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/notes/feds-notes/ai-adoption-and-firms-job-posting-behavior-20260327.html
   Claim: AI-skill demand in postings has spread from computer/math to ~a quarter of all occupations since 2015; 5–40% of firms have adopted AI. **Employer-side only** — no cross-check against worker usage. Closest to our data but does not do the comparison.

6. **Anthropic Economic Index (March 2026 "Learning curves" report)** — Handa, Tamkin et al.
   URL: https://www.anthropic.com/research/economic-index-march-2026-report
   Claim: Computer programmers show 74.5% AI exposure; customer service 70.1%; medical record specialists 66.7% — usage concentrated in mid-to-high wage technical occupations. **Worker-side ranking only.** No employer-posting cross-walk published.

7. **Lightcast, "Beyond the Buzz" (Jul 2025)**.
   URL: https://lightcast.io/resources/blog/beyond-the-buzz-press-release-2025-07-23
   Claim: 51% of AI-skill postings are now outside IT; HR postings grew 66%, Education 200% (from a low base). **Employer-side rank by sector**, acknowledges "low baseline" — implicitly a level story, but does not benchmark against worker usage surveys.

## Assessment

Four distinct prior-art camps: (a) McKinsey/BCG/WEF document the **level gap** (workers ahead of leaders); (b) MIT/SAP frames it as **shadow AI**; (c) Fed/Lightcast map **employer postings** cross-occupationally; (d) Anthropic Economic Index maps **worker usage** cross-occupationally. **Nobody joins the two maps.** The +0.92 rank correlation is the novel finding — it reconciles the "employers are out of step" narrative (true on level) with an unreported fact: employers rank occupations correctly. The story's angle: the market is not panicking or hallucinating; it is slow-coding a real signal.
