# Prior art: the prevailing narrative the piece contests

The "AI is eating entry-level software engineering jobs" narrative is now the consensus view in mainstream business and tech press. Four sources — two data-led, two forecast/anecdote-led — anchor it.

## 1. Brynjolfsson, Chandar & Chen — Stanford Digital Economy Lab (DATA)

- **Source:** Stanford Digital Economy Lab working paper (covered Fortune, TIME, Axios, CBS)
- **Title:** "Canaries in the Coal Mine? Six Facts about the Recent Employment Effects of Artificial Intelligence"
- **Authors:** Erik Brynjolfsson, Bharat Chandar, Ruyu Chen
- **Date:** August 2025
- **URL:** https://digitaleconomy.stanford.edu/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/Canaries_BrynjolfssonChandarChen.pdf
- **Claim:** Using ADP payroll microdata, early-career workers (ages 22-25) in the most AI-exposed occupations — with software developers leading — saw a 13% relative decline in employment since late 2022, while older workers in the same occupations held steady or grew; entry-level SWE employment specifically fell ~20%.

## 2. Amodei interview — Axios (FORECAST/ANECDOTE)

- **Source:** Axios ("Behind the Curtain")
- **Title:** "Behind the Curtain: A white-collar bloodbath"
- **Authors:** Jim VandeHei & Mike Allen
- **Date:** May 28, 2025
- **URL:** https://www.axios.com/2025/05/28/ai-jobs-white-collar-unemployment-anthropic
- **Claim:** Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei warns AI "could wipe out half of all entry-level white-collar jobs — and spike unemployment to 10-20% — in the next one to five years," singling out tech/coding among the first to go.

## 3. SignalFire State of Talent Report (DATA)

- **Source:** SignalFire (VC firm) — covered by TechCrunch, SF Standard, Business Insider
- **Title:** "The SignalFire State of Tech Talent Report — 2025"
- **Author:** SignalFire research team (Asher Bantock et al.)
- **Date:** May 2025
- **URL:** https://www.signalfire.com/blog/signalfire-state-of-talent-report-2025
- **Claim:** Big Tech hired 25% fewer new graduates in 2024 vs. 2023; new-grad share at the Magnificent Seven has dropped more than half since 2022, with AI cited as the primary driver — "entry-level roles are vanishing."

## 4. Derek Thompson — Substack (ANECDOTE/COMMENTARY)

- **Source:** Derek Thompson Substack (ex-Atlantic staff writer, high-reach commentator)
- **Title:** "Young People Face a Hiring Crisis. AI Is Making It Worse."
- **Author:** Derek Thompson
- **Date:** June 2025 (with follow-up "The Evidence That AI Is Destroying Jobs For Young People Just Got Stronger," Aug 2025)
- **URL:** https://www.derekthompson.org/p/young-people-face-a-hiring-crisis
- **Claim:** The recent-grad job market "hasn't looked this bad in at least 30 or 40 years" relative to the overall labor force, and AI-exposed roles — software developers foremost — are where the damage is concentrated.

## Evidence posture

- **Data-led:** Stanford/Brynjolfsson (payroll microdata), SignalFire (hiring counts at named firms).
- **Forecast/anecdote-led:** Amodei/Axios (CEO prediction, no data release), Thompson (synthesis plus expert quotes from career-services directors).

All four treat postings, payroll, or hiring *counts* — none examine the *seniority composition of live requisitions* in the way a 68,000-posting longitudinal dataset can, which is the contrast this piece will exploit.
