# Prior art: the prevailing narrative the piece inverts

The dominant narrative since 2023 is that LLMs flatten the engineering hierarchy — natural-language prompting replaces syntax, juniors ship senior-grade code, the "10x engineer" is commoditised. Four prominent sources anchor it.

## 1. Andrej Karpathy — X / Twitter (FORECAST/ANECDOTE)

- **Source:** X (then Twitter), later amplified in The New Yorker
- **Title:** "The hottest new programming language is English"
- **Author:** Andrej Karpathy (ex-OpenAI, ex-Tesla)
- **Date:** January 24, 2023 (extended by the "vibe coding" post, Feb 2025)
- **URL:** https://x.com/karpathy/status/1617979122625712128
- **Claim:** LLMs let humans command computers in natural language, so formal programming languages — and by extension the specialist skill tier that commands them — are on the way out; his Feb 2025 follow-up coined "vibe coding" ("fully give in to the vibes... forget that the code even exists"), later named Collins Dictionary Word of the Year 2025.
- **Posture:** Speculative/cultural, not data-backed. ~4M views; the industry's defining slogan for the democratisation thesis.

## 2. Jensen Huang — World Government Summit keynote (FORECAST)

- **Source:** NVIDIA CEO remarks, covered in Tom's Hardware, TechRadar
- **Title:** "Don't learn to code — leave it up to AI"
- **Author:** Jensen Huang
- **Date:** February 2024
- **URL:** https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/jensen-huang-advises-against-learning-to-code-leave-it-up-to-ai
- **Claim:** "It is our job to create computing technology such that nobody has to program. And that the programming language is human." Domain expertise replaces coding as the scarce input.
- **Posture:** Forecast, not data-backed. The bluntest "bar is lowered" statement from a first-tier CEO.

## 3. Sam Altman — multiple interviews / OpenAI DevDay (FORECAST)

- **Source:** Aggregated Altman remarks, e.g. NextBigFuture, YourStory
- **Title:** "Coding will go away; anyone can build apps"
- **Author:** Sam Altman
- **Date:** March 2024, extended in Dec 2024 remarks
- **URL:** https://www.nextbigfuture.com/2024/03/sam-altman-openai-qstar-is-still-secret-and-sam-says-coding-will-go-away.html
- **Claim:** By end of 2025 AI will out-code any human; the programmer's role collapses to describing intent. Software development will be democratised — "anyone can build powerful apps, regardless of coding skills."
- **Posture:** Forecast with self-interest. The canonical "skill premium collapses" claim.

## 4. Matt Welsh — Communications of the ACM (ESSAY/FORECAST)

- **Source:** CACM / Medium / CS50 tech talk
- **Title:** "The End of Programming"
- **Author:** Matt Welsh (ex-Harvard CS, ex-Google engineering lead, founder Fixie.ai)
- **Date:** December 2022 (still widely cited through 2025)
- **URL:** https://cacm.acm.org/magazines/2023/1/267976-the-end-of-programming/fulltext
- **Claim:** Conventional programming is headed for extinction in ~3 years; most software will be trained, not written; the surviving human roles shrink to "teacher," PM, or code reviewer.
- **Posture:** Forecast, no data. The most cited long-form statement of the thesis inside the CS establishment.

## Strongest counter-evidence to name precisely

The **Karpathy–Huang–Altman consensus** — that LLMs make English the programming language and thereby lower the experience bar — is what the 15.6× Applied-AI archetype inverts. It is forecast-driven and culturally dominant; no major voice has paired it with requisition-level evidence.

## Who has already argued the opposite (novelty check)

- **Fastly / DORA-adjacent data (2025):** senior devs ship 2.5× more AI-assisted code than juniors (productivity, not hiring).
- **Harvard 62M-resume study (2025):** GenAI-adopting firms cut junior hiring while senior roles held or grew (hiring counts, not seniority *within* a role).
- **Gergely Orosz, "The Seniority Rollercoaster":** AI amplifies seniors; bifurcated market for AI + senior engineers (commentary, no posting-level data).

**Novelty gap this piece fills:** no prior source shows that *within the frontier AI-building role itself* — the very job that is supposed to embody democratised coding — firms are now demanding *more* YOE and skewing *more* toward director titles than other senior SWE tracks. The Applied-AI archetype is where the democratisation story should be strongest, and it's where the bar is rising fastest.
