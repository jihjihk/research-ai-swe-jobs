# Critic — 06_easy_explanations

## Revisions (ordered by severity)

- **T29 "rejected" overstates the result.** Evidence block says content preserved 80-130% on low-LLM quartile, and length growth was only 52% preserved — i.e., length IS ~half LLM-mediated. The piece frames LLM authorship as a rejected mechanism wholesale. It should be narrowed: "LLM authorship is rejected as the *dominant* mediator of content change; it partially mediates length." The body actually says this (length grew half as much) but the thesis, deck, and closer ("authorship contamination — had their turn, none held") flatten the distinction. Add one qualifying clause in both deck and final paragraph.

- **T37: "13 of 15" vs evidence "13-14 of 15" vs V2 "14 of 15."** Internal inconsistency between body (13) and evidence block (13-14). Resolve to 14 (the larger-denominator, more honest version); if 13 is the correct on-sensitivity count, disclose the sensitivity explicitly. As written, the piece picks the smaller number without saying why.

- **T36 needs explicit hedging.** n=11 disappearing titles, only 6 of 11 matched to neighbours — the piece reports neither denominator. "Eleven 2024 titles... were mapped to their closest 2026 titles" implies all eleven mapped; six did. The 3.6% vs 14.4% gap is strong, but the reader deserves the n=6 and a one-clause hedge ("on a small sample of eleven titles"). Currently the paragraph reads with false confidence.

- **Closing sentence assumes the positive.** "Employers have decided to write more AI-requirement language" is a causal-intentional claim not established by rejecting five alternatives. Five rejections leaves a residual, not a proven agent. Suggest: "…because the AI-requirement rise is a property of what employers are writing, not of who or how it is being written." Preserves the point without over-reaching into intent.

- **Quantitative claim not in evidence: Cohen's d = 0.22.** The d statistic for LLM-stylometric rise is not in the evidence block (which gives only effect preservation on the low-quartile subset). Either add it to the evidence block or delete it from the body.

- **Quantitative claim not in evidence: r = +0.20, p = 0.0015.** Evidence block gives "desc-length r = +0.20" but no p-value. Drop the p or source it.

- **Strawman check — hiring-bar paragraph.** HR-press doesn't uniformly claim "firms lowered the bar therefore requirements shrank." The piece conflates degree-drop evidence with requirements-section-length evidence; the sources cited argue the former, not the latter. Tighten: the tested claim is length-shrink-as-bar-lowering, which is the piece's own construction, not HR Dive's.

- **Flow — paragraph 1 (LLM authorship) is ~40% longer than the others and carries two statistics the others don't.** Either trim to match or promote it to the lead rejection with a transitional sentence acknowledging it does the most work.

## Verdict

Strong argument; T29 is overstated and two numerics are uncited — fix before publication.
