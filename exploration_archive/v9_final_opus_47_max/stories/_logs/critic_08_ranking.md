# Critic notes — 08_ranking_right.md

**Verdict:** Publishable after targeted fixes; headline defensible, but prose outruns evidence in four places.

## Required revisions

- **Missing CI on the headline.** Spearman +0.92 with n=16 has a Fisher-z 95% CI of roughly **[+0.79, +0.97]** (p ~3e-7). Still a strong correlation, but a piece whose argument is "the ranking is tight, not the level" must put a number on "tight." Add: "n=16 gives a 95% CI of roughly 0.79 to 0.97." Do not leave it as bare 0.92.

- **The 72× gap is benchmark-dependent; the piece buries this.** The evidence block concedes the accountant benchmark (Thomson Reuters "ever tried", 50%) is upward-biased and that SWE compresses to ~5× under daily-use. Main text leads twice with "seventy-fold" as pattern-level fact. Add one clause: "on the most generous worker benchmark; under stricter daily-use definitions the gaps compress but do not disappear." As written, the lead number is the softest in the piece.

- **"Nurse 0.00%" needs one word of honesty.** Literal zero on 6,801 is arithmetically correct; Wilson upper 95% CI is ~0.06%. Write "0.00% (upper 95% CI 0.06%)" or at minimum "essentially zero." A literal-zero claim without an upper bound invites a lazy rebuttal.

- **"Employers are not panicking, they're late" is under-defended.** Data show rank-coherence and level-lag. They do not rule out "panicking AND late." Soften to: "the data are inconsistent with 'employers misread which jobs AI changed'; they remain consistent with 'employers are both alarmed and slow.'" Current framing treats two readings as mutually exclusive; they are not.

- **"Management-consulting industry selling perception-problem framing since 2023" is editorial and unsourced.** Either cite (McKinsey Jan 2025, BCG Oct 2025) or replace with "the perception-problem framing that dominates current coverage." As written, reads as aside-for-effect.

- **Closer is rhetorical, not predictive.** "Next two years spent writing down what the previous two already did" — if flourish, fine. If forecast, specify what falsifies (e.g., SWE employer rate <25% by 2028) and note the piece has no model of codification lag. Recommend softening with "likely" or "the pattern suggests."

- **Level/rank distinction is the spine but "rank" appears only twice.** Para 4 pivots on "pattern/ordering"; para 6 on "level/direction/ordering." Introduce LEVEL vs RANK explicitly in para 3 as "two orthogonal quantities," then hold the terms. Reader currently reconstructs the distinction unaided.

- **No other unsourced quantitatives.** 63–90%, 10.6%, 1.0%, 0.7%, 0.00%, 6,801, sixteen, eighteen, +0.92 all trace to the evidence block. Clean on that axis.
