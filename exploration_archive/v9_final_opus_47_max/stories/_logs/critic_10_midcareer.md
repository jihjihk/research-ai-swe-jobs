# Critic notes — 10_where_middle_narrowed

**Verdict:** Publishable claim, but the piece over-sells the arshkon corroboration and under-discloses sampling caveats. Revise before publication.

## Revisions needed

- **Arshkon SNR is weak.** D falls 1.00 pp on arshkon vs 3.32 pp on pooled — a 3.3x gap. The piece treats arshkon as "conservative corroboration," but 1.00 pp on a 17% base is ~6% relative; without a standard error or bootstrap CI the claim that D "is the only bucket negative on both" is descriptively true but statistically weak. Add a one-clause caveat: "the arshkon magnitude is small and sits near the noise floor; the robust finding is directional, not magnitude-equal."

- **Baseline caveat buried.** The asaniczka-heaviness of the pooled baseline is mentioned in one subordinate clause ("this study's T30 methodology note flags the asymmetry explicitly"). For a piece whose headline claim rests on which baseline you believe, that disclosure needs its own sentence. Readers who skim will anchor on the 3.32 pp figure.

- **5-7 asymmetry handled unfairly.** The piece writes "rises on the cleaner baseline and is flat on the weaker one." But 5-7 *falls* 1.88 pp on pooled — that's not "flat," and calling pooled the "weaker" baseline after using it in the lede is convenient. Either commit to arshkon as primary or acknowledge the pooled result cuts against Mechanism 2.

- **Mechanism 1 and 2 are speculation, not evidence.** Scope inflation is cross-referenced to another piece but not shown here. Mechanism 2 (the "2019-2020 graduate sweet spot" story) is pure narrative extrapolation — no data in the evidence block supports cohort-level AI fluency. Label both as interpretations, not findings. The current prose reads as causal.

- **Willison/Hosseini conflation.** Willison's "Seniority Rollercoaster" is a broader labour-market argument; Hosseini/Lichtinger use education tiers on resumes, not YOE on postings. The piece rebuts both as if they made identical YOE-bucket predictions. Unfair. At minimum, note that Hosseini's unit of analysis differs and the rebuttal is weaker against him.

- **Absolute counts absent.** 8-10's 3.32 pp pooled drop on 25,815 labelled 2026 postings is ~858 absolute postings below its 2024 share-equivalent — not the 3,625 the prompt suggests (that number reflects something else). Either way, give readers one absolute number so they can gauge scale. RATES alone obscure whether this is 200 jobs or 2,000.

- **Posting-vs-payroll elision.** The closing "layoff discourse has been looking at the wrong part of the distribution" conflates postings (demand signal) with layoffs (separations). The collection emphasises this distinction elsewhere; this piece drops it. Soften to "hiring-demand distribution" or flag the asymmetry.

- **Quantitative claims check:** All pp figures trace to the evidence block. "Relative rise of more than half" for 0-2 (3.97/7.31 = 54%) is supported. No orphan numbers found.
