Wave 2, Agent H. Run: 2026-04-20. Inputs: `data/unified.parquet` (SWE LinkedIn default filter), plus `exploration/artifacts/shared/seniority_definition_panel.csv`.

Sample: 68,137 SWE LinkedIn rows with `description` length ≥ 50 chars (arshkon 4,691 + asaniczka 18,129 + scraped 45,317). Primary analyses use RAW `description` (per spec dim d; the boilerplate-vs-content question requires raw text). ## Headline verdict — boilerplate, not content

**Description length grew 2024 → 2026 by +1,130 chars (+30%, all-SWE pooled 2024 baseline → scraped). The growth is NOT concentrated in requirements.** The key decomposition:

| Section | 2024 mean chars | 2026 mean chars | Δ chars | Δ % | 2024 share | 2026 share | Δ share pp |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Summary / About role | 464 | 628 | +164 | +35% | 12.3% | 12.8% | +0.5 |
| Responsibilities | **723** | **1,077** | **+355** | **+49%** | 19.2% | 22.0% | **+2.8** |
| **Requirements** | **887** | **984** | **+98** | **+11%** | **23.6%** | **20.1%** | **−3.5** |
| Preferred qualifications | 365 | 531 | +165 | +45% | 9.7% | 10.8% | +1.1 |
| **Benefits / Perks / Pay** | **322** | **609** | **+287** | **+89%** | 8.6% | 12.4% | **+3.9** |
| About company | 154 | 247 | +93 | +60% | 4.1% | 5.0% | +1.0 |
| **Legal / EEO** | **109** | **196** | **+87** | **+80%** | 2.9% | 4.0% | **+1.1** |
| Unclassified | 740 | 622 | −118 | −16% | 19.7% | 12.7% | −7.0 |
| **Total raw length** | **3,763** | **4,893** | **+1,130** | **+30%** | 100% | 100% | — |

The three findings that determine the scope-inflation verdict are:

1. **Requirements share SHRANK by 3.5 pp** (23.6% → 20.1%). Requirements absolute chars rose only +11%, the smallest non-noise growth of any section. Under the within-2024 calibration (see below), the +98 char change is *below the within-2024 noise floor* (SNR 0.45).
2. **Responsibilities grew most in absolute terms** (+355 chars, +49%). This is real signal (SNR 3.74) and is consistent with "postings describe more of what the role does" — not with "postings demand more of candidates."
3. **Boilerplate (benefits + about + legal) nearly doubled**: 587 → 1,051 chars (+464, +79%). This trio accounts for 41% of the total length growth. SNRs: benefits 4.87, about 24.15, legal 7.39 — all robustly real.

**T13 verdict on Gate 1's "is the +1,750-char growth real content or boilerplate?" question: BOILERPLATE-LED, with a real responsibilities expansion and essentially no requirements expansion.** The "scope inflation" narrative built on description-length growth does not survive T13 unless it is narrowed to "responsibilities are being described more fully" — and even that reframe must sit alongside the near-doubling of boilerplate which is plausibly an LLM-authorship artifact (T29 will test).

## 1. Readability metrics

Readability is stable across periods — grammar-based metrics barely move. Token diversity (TTR, lexicon) scales with length. Raw description, pooled 2024 vs scraped 2026, all-SWE:

| Metric | 2024 (n=22,820) | 2026 (n=45,317) | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Flesch-Kincaid grade | 17.89 | 18.04 | +0.15 |
| Flesch reading ease | 11.45 | 10.97 | −0.48 |
| Gunning-Fog | 21.33 | 21.56 | +0.24 |
| Avg sentence length | 31.7 | 30.3 | −1.4 |
| Type-token ratio (first 1K chars) | 0.690 | 0.704 | +0.014 |
| Syllable count | 1,052 | 1,307 | +255 |
| Lexicon count | 525 | 654 | +129 |

All readability moves are within-2024 noise (SNRs: FK 0.66, FRE 0.13, Fog 1.77, sentence-length 0.09). The only two readability metrics with SNR > 1 are TTR (1.54) and syllable/lexicon count (which track length). **JDs are not getting easier or harder to read.**

Entry-level sensitivities (J3 vs J1):
- J3 (YOE ≤ 2): 2024 n=1,667, 2026 n=2,911. Length 4,166 → 5,317 (+28%).
- J1 (entry label): 2024 n=415, 2026 n=1,650. Length 3,413 → 4,951 (+45%). Sample imbalance; J1 2024 noisy.

Core-LLM supplement (see `text_source_sensitivity.csv`) — mean lengths drop to 2,303 (2024) → 2,618 (scraped) because LLM-cleaned text already stripped benefits/boilerplate. Readability direction is unchanged.

## 3. Tone markers

| Metric (per 1K chars) | 2024 all | 2026 all | Δ | SNR |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Imperative density ("you will", "must", "should") | 0.751 | 0.607 | **−0.144** | **37.4** |
| Inclusive density ("we", "our team", "join us") | 0.470 | 0.539 | +0.068 | 7.7 |
| Marketing density ("exciting", "cutting-edge", "world-class") | 0.187 | 0.174 | −0.013 | 2.7 |
| Passive voice density | 0.579 | 0.533 | −0.046 | 0.33 |

**Imperative density dropped sharply (−19%). Inclusive density rose (+14%).** Postings shifted from a directive "here is what you must do" voice toward a relational "this is us; come join us" voice. This is consistent with LLM-era recruiter drafting style.

Marketing density is essentially flat — "innovative / cutting-edge" per 1K chars didn't spike despite AI-adjacent framing rising across the board.

Entry-level (J3): imperative 0.749 → 0.663 (−12%), inclusive 0.501 → 0.574 (+15%), marketing 0.190 → 0.190 (flat). J3 follows the aggregate pattern.

## 4. Entry-level deep dive

For J3 (YOE ≤ 2) specifically:
- Raw length: 4,166 → 5,317 (+28%).
- Requirements chars: 1,057 → 1,001 (**−5%** in absolute). Share: 25.4% → 18.8% (−6.6 pp).
- Responsibilities chars: 858 → 1,150 (+34%). Share: 20.6% → 21.6% (+1.0 pp).
- Benefits chars: 367 → 704 (+92%). Share: 8.8% → 13.2% (+4.4 pp).
- Preferred chars: 396 → 639 (+61%). Share: 9.5% → 12.0% (+2.5 pp).

The pattern: **junior postings in 2026 are longer, but the extra length is in responsibilities, benefits, and "preferred / nice-to-have" — not in required qualifications.** If you define scope-inflation as "the minimum bar rose," the J3 raw-text evidence says no. If you define it as "the nice-to-have bar rose," preferred is up a bit, and "responsibilities described in more detail" is up a lot.

J1 (entry label, n=415 in 2024, 1650 in 2026) shows the same directional pattern but with more noise; the small 2024 J1 sample amplifies every metric.

## 6. Surprises and unexpected patterns

1. **Requirements share DROPS universally.** Every seniority bucket shows −2.6 to −6.6 pp requirements-share decline. Gate 1's "scope inflation" hypothesis predicted the opposite.
2. **Benefits section nearly doubled in absolute terms (+89%) and grew to 12.4% of all postings.** 2026 scraped postings spend a much larger share of their text on perks / comp disclosures / pay-transparency language. California pay-transparency laws (2023+) and similar legislation are a plausible driver; T29's LLM-authorship check will separate "legal compliance" from "LLM-generated fluff."
3. **Legal section nearly doubled in share.** EEO / pay-transparency / disclosure language is a material fraction of 2026 postings (4.0%) but was 2.9% in 2024. This is almost certainly regulatory.
4. **Imperative density dropped 19%.** JDs sound less like orders ("you will", "must") and more like invitations ("we" / "our team" / "join us"). This could be LLM-style recruiter drafting (T29) or a conscious industry shift in tone.
5. **Unclassified share dropped 7 pp** (19.7% → 12.7%). 2026 postings are more structured — they have more header-labeled sections. This is consistent with the markdown-formatting hypothesis (scraped preserves markdown; Kaggle text is HTML-stripped). But it also means my classifier loses less text to "unclassified" in 2026 than in 2024, which slightly inflates 2026 per-section counts. I chose a high-recall classifier; see caveat.
6. **J3 (junior YOE ≤ 2) requirements section SHRANK in absolute chars** (−57, −5%). No one predicted this going in.
7. **Responsibilities grew fastest for seniors (+53%) and slowest for J3 juniors (+34%).** Combined with #6 and the J1 label sensitivity, the "most length growth is happening to senior postings' responsibilities" story is clean.

## 7. What this means for Wave 2+ downstream

- **Scope-inflation claims based on description-length growth must be rewritten.** The pooled +1,130 char delta is boilerplate + responsibilities, not requirements. Requirements growth is at or below the within-2024 noise floor.
- **For scope-inflation to survive, it needs to be recast as responsibilities-breadth growth (not requirements stacking).** T11's credential-stacking work should check whether credential density moves independently of raw chars.
- **T12 section-filtered Fightin' Words (requirements + responsibilities only) is the right follow-up.** If 2024 → 2026 reqresp-filtered terms diverge from full-text, the difference is all in the boilerplate.
- **T29 (LLM-authorship detection) should test whether the legal + benefits + about_company growth correlates with recruiter-LLM drafting.** If yes, the tone shift + boilerplate expansion are both instances of recruiter-LLM authorship mediation.
- **The section classifier is available at** `exploration/scripts/T13_section_classifier.py` **and is consumed by T12 (next) and by Wave 3 T18 / Wave 3.5 T33.** The per-posting readability + tone + section parquet lives at `exploration/artifacts/shared/T13_readability_metrics.parquet` (68,137 × ~30 cols) and is consumed by T29.

## Caveats

1. **2026 scraped text preserves markdown section headers; 2024 Kaggle text does not.** This means 2026 postings are easier to classify into sections (unclassified share drops 7 pp). My classifier handles both formats (see `T13_section_classifier.py` tests), but some of the "content moved into classified sections" in 2026 is format availability, not real content growth. The responsibilities / benefits / legal growth survives this concern because within-2024 calibration shows arshkon-vs-asaniczka differences on those sections are small (<120 chars each) while 2024→2026 differences are 300–400 chars.
2. **The classifier is high-recall, not high-precision.** Compound headers like "Role Overview And Core Responsibilities" are attributed to responsibilities (correctly, given the content below). A regex-based section classifier will miss postings with non-standard headers, and will over-attribute to fallback labels. Unclassified share of 12–19% is a reasonable error bar on section-level precision.
3. **Entry-level J1-label baseline is small** (2024 n=415). All J1 entry-label findings should be read as directional signals, not point estimates.
4. **Cross-period means pool two 2024 sources (arshkon + asaniczka) against scraped 2026.** Within-2024 calibration is the check; it shows arshkon and asaniczka differ on some metrics (sentence length, reading ease) and agree on others. Use the calibration SNR column before believing any specific magnitude.
