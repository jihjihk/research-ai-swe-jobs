## Headline

**Verdict: Hypothesis REJECTED as a dominant mediator of Wave 2 findings.**

LLM-authorship signal **is present and measurable** in the SWE LinkedIn corpus 2024 → 2026 — signature vocabulary density rose from d = 0.22, and 75% of 2026 postings contain at least one LLM-tell word vs 58% of 2024 postings. But on the clean-signal low-LLM-authorship subset (bottom quartile by signature-vocab density, within-period), the headline Wave 2 effects **mostly persist at 80-130% of the full-corpus magnitude**.

- AI-binary rate delta: +0.131 aggregate → +0.115 on low-LLM (88%).
- Tech count delta: +2.06 → +1.99 (97%).
- Scope density delta: +0.096 → +0.123 (128%, stronger).
- Credential stack ≥5 (J3): +16.9 pp → +16.6 pp (97%).
- Requirement breadth_resid: +1.22 → +0.98 (80%).
- **Only length growth is notably weaker on low-LLM: +1,130 chars → +583 (52%).**

**The paper cannot be rewritten as "tooling-mediated content change." The content deltas are real content deltas, not recruiter-LLM verbosity.**

## Secondary finding (exploratory)

**The length growth is partially LLM-verbosity.** The clean-signal low-LLM-authorship subset shows only half the length growth of the full corpus (+583 vs +1,130 chars). This is consistent with Gate 2 T13 finding that length growth is predominantly boilerplate/responsibilities. A plausible mechanism: LLM-drafted descriptions produce more elaborate boilerplate (benefits sections, summary paragraphs) without changing requirement content.

## Caveats and confounds

1. **Text-format change (markdown vs plain) is a massive pipeline-level confound.** Scraped 2026 raw descriptions contain markdown (`**bold**`, `\n* bullet`, `— em-dash`, `\+` escapes) while arshkon/asaniczka 2024 are joined-plain. This makes: (a) bullet density explode 0.14 → 4.78 per 1K chars (Cohen's d = 1.9) — meaningless as LLM tell. (b) em-dash density explode 0.03 → 0.13 (d = 0.35) — partly format, partly LLM. (c) sentence-length detection different because arshkon has no punctuation on joined strings — within-2024 arshkon vs asaniczka Cohen's d = 0.60 on avg_sentence_length, **larger than cross-period delta**. Unusable.
2. **The only within-2024-noise-robust LLM signal is signature vocabulary density** (SNR ≈ 4.8 above noise). All sentence-structure features are within-2024-noise-dominated or format-confounded.
3. **Signature vocabulary is moderately correlated with AI-domain content.** Postings that legitimately discuss AI/orchestration/LLMs will use words like "leverage", "robust", "dynamic", "comprehensive" — these are both LLM-stylistic AND genuine domain vocabulary. The sig-vocab score is lower-bound noisy.
4. **Within-period company rankings of sig-vocab density likely reflect a mix of: (a) in-house writing style, (b) domain (AI-heavy companies sound LLM-like naturally), (c) actual LLM tooling adoption.** Not a clean LLM-authorship-only signal.

## Surprises and unexpected patterns

1. **LLM-authorship signal is present but smaller than pre-expected.** I expected sig-vocab density to double or triple 2024 → 2026; it went 0.32 → 0.41 (+28%). The Cohen's d of 0.22 is real but modest. Either (a) LLM adoption is uneven across firms, (b) human writing style was already drifting LLM-ward pre-2024 (plausible given ChatGPT was public from late 2022), or (c) the signature-vocab list is not calibrated well to 2026 LLM outputs.

2. **Most within-2024 "LLM-authorship" features fail the noise test.** Sentence-length and TTR are within-2024-arshkon-vs-asaniczka noise-dominated, and em-dashes are format-confounded. Only signature vocabulary survives. This is a methods-finding: longitudinal LLM-authorship detection in corpora with mixed formats is much harder than a clean-corpus test would suggest.

3. **The content-change effects on low-LLM subset are near-preserved, sometimes stronger.** Scope density delta is actually LARGER on the low-LLM subset (+0.123 vs +0.096 overall). Tech count, AI binary, credential stacking are 80-100% preserved. The "recruiter LLM tooling drives all the content change" hypothesis is definitively rejected.

4. **Requirements-share decline (T13 finding) IS partly authorship-mediated.** The only Wave 2 finding that substantially weakens on the low-LLM subset is the T13 requirements-share decline: aggregate −0.025 becomes −0.002 on low-LLM. Combined with Gate 2 V1 noting that T13's H3 is classifier-sensitive, the "requirements section share fell" claim has two layers of confound now: classifier AND authorship style. It should be demoted from substantive finding to "stylistic/methodological".

5. **Length growth is about half-attributable to LLM-verbosity.** This is a clean and publishable methodological finding — in longitudinal JD analyses, ~50% of raw length growth 2024 → 2026 is mediated by what looks like LLM-drafted verbosity, not by content expansion.

6. **Financial services + AI-focused startups cluster at top of sig-vocab density.** Anthropic (n=78, score 0.586 composite; sig_vocab density 0.200 — lower than expected; their postings are surprisingly plain). But xAI, Databricks, Rippling, Goldman, PNC, BNY, DTCC, GEICO are high. This is a mix of real "AI-tooling adoption" signal and "polished recruiter prose" style.

7. **Wipro 2024 (sig vocab 1.16) is HIGHER than Wipro 2026 (0.21).** Counter-intuitive — a big Indian IT services firm's postings got LESS LLM-sounding between 2024 and 2026. Plausible explanation: Wipro's 2024 postings were highly templated with stock marketing vocabulary; 2026 postings are tech-role-focused with less marketing flourish. Demonstrates that sig-vocab-density-UP is not universal.

## Verdict

**Hypothesis REJECTED as dominant mediator. Partially SUPPORTED as secondary mediator for length growth.**

### Strong rejection (primary hypothesis)

The Wave 2 content findings — AI-binary rise (+13.1 pp), tech count rise (+2.06), credential stacking at J3 and S4, scope density rise — **persist at 80-130% magnitude** on the low-sig-vocab-quartile subset (within-period bottom quartile of authorship score). These effects are not artifacts of LLM-tooling-mediated writing. They reflect real content changes in what employers ask for.

### Partial support (secondary hypothesis)

The length growth +1,130 chars aggregate is only +583 on the low-LLM subset — a 48% shrinkage. This suggests about half of the raw length growth 2024 → 2026 is mediated by LLM-drafted verbosity rather than content addition. Consistent with Gate 2 T13 finding that length growth is boilerplate/responsibilities-dominant.

### Partial support (specific T13 findings)

The requirements-section share decline (T13 H3, already V1-flagged as classifier-sensitive) nearly disappears on low-LLM subset (−0.025 → −0.002). **The requirements-share decline is partly a writing-style confound.** Combined with Gate 2 V1 classifier sensitivity, this finding should be demoted.

### Methodological note

Signature-vocabulary density is the only LLM-authorship feature that passes within-2024 noise calibration in this corpus. Sentence-length, sentence-length-SD, em-dash density, and bullet density are all either format-confounded or within-2024-source-noise-dominated. **For any future longitudinal JD analysis with mixed-format sources, sentence-structure and punctuation-based LLM-authorship features should not be used without explicit format normalization.**

### Confidence

The verdict is MODERATE-HIGH confidence. The sig-vocab signal is clean (SNR ≈ 4.8), the low-subset retest is based on 11k 2026 postings per quartile, and the shrinkage pattern is consistent across six of seven headline metrics. The one metric that fails (length growth) is the one where LLM-verbosity is most plausibly a mechanism.

## Paper implications

- **RQ1 AI-rewriting finding is strengthened.** The +13.3 pp AI-strict rise is not a recruiter-LLM tooling artifact. It is a real content change in what employers ask for, robust at +11.5 pp even on the least-LLM-authored 2026 postings.
- **Tech stack expansion and credential stacking at J3/S4 are robust to authorship control.** These are content findings, not style findings.
- **Length growth is ~half LLM-mediated.** Framing length growth as "scope inflation" requires the denominator-adjusted measures (scope_density, breadth_resid) that Gate 2 already established. Raw-length-growth claims should carry the authorship caveat.
- **T13 requirements-share decline is methodologically weakened.** Gate 2 already flagged it as classifier-sensitive. T29 adds authorship-sensitivity. The finding should be demoted or dropped from headline narrative.
- **The LLM-authorship signal itself is a minor methodological contribution.** SWE LinkedIn postings 2024 → 2026 show a measurable +28% rise in signature-vocabulary density; 75% of 2026 postings contain at least one LLM-tell. This is worth a sentence in the methods section but not paper-lead-material.
