# T10. Title taxonomy evolution

Agent G, Wave 2. Generated 2026-04-20.

**Question.** How has the SWE title landscape evolved 2024 → 2026? What titles emerged, disappeared, or changed meaning, and does the title space look structurally different?

**TL;DR.**
- **The title vocabulary fragmented only modestly.** Unique titles per 1K postings is essentially flat pooled-2024 (454) vs scraped-2026 (488). The top-10 and top-50 cumulative shares barely move (12.7%/26.6% vs 13.7%/25.3%), so the head of the distribution is stable. The headline impression of "the title space is exploding" is mostly an artifact of the scraped corpus being 2× the size of pooled 2024; per-1K richness is stable.
- **But the HEAD of the title distribution has rotated.** 175 titles appear ≥ 10× in scraped 2026 with < 3 appearances in arshkon 2024 — these are the "truly new" titles, dominated by AI/ML compounds and new-formatted descriptors. Only 2 titles qualify as "arshkon-disappearing" under the task's strict threshold (arshkon ≥ 10 / scraped < 3): `senior dotnet developer` (n=19) and `java architect` (n=15). The pooled-2024 strict-threshold list is longer (58 titles) because asaniczka carries a wide tail of aggregator-and-clearance titles that scraper queries did not surface.
- **AI/ML compound titles are the biggest structural shift.** Any-AI-term share in titles rose from 9.6% (pooled 2024) to 23.5% (scraped 2026) — a +13.9 pp jump. The rise is driven by a new `ai engineer`-family (`ai engineer`, `ai/machine learning engineer`, `senior ai/machine learning engineer`, `applied ai engineer`, `principal ai engineer`, `ai platform engineer`, `ai agent engineer`, `agentic ai engineer`, `founding ai engineer`, etc.). These titles almost universally have n=0 in arshkon; the `ai engineer` canonical title alone went from n=2 to n=240 (120×).
- **Seniority-marker share in titles is stable or slightly reshuffled.** "Senior" share drops from 39.3% pooled 2024 → 27.0% scraped 2026, while "staff" rose (2.7% → 5.5%) and "director/manager" markers rose modestly. "Junior/entry" title-marker share barely changes (1.2% → 3.0%). The 12 pp drop in "senior" titles is notable — counter to the inflation intuition, employers in 2026 are not putting "senior" in the title as often, despite the YOE-derived senior share only falling ~7 pp (per T30 S4). **Surprise.**
- **Same-title content drift is large but within the within-2024 noise floor.** TF-IDF cosine between pooled-2024 and scraped-2026 descriptions for the 10 most common shared titles is 0.76-0.87. Within-2024 cosine (arshkon vs asaniczka) for those same titles is 0.68-0.89 — the cross-period gap is only slightly wider than the within-period gap. Same title does NOT mean dramatically different content on average (the drift is in specific content dimensions — AI mentions, scope terms — that T11 and T12 / T13 measure directly).
- **Emerging category shares.** `ai_engineering` title-category share went from 0.6% pooled 2024 → 4.7% scraped 2026 (+4.1 pp) — the single largest category-level shift. `ml_infrastructure` +0.17 pp, `data_engineering` +2.3 pp, `platform_engineering` +0.5 pp, `mobile` +0.5 pp. `qa_testing` DROPS (5.1% → 3.5%, -1.6 pp). This is the paper's concrete "new role categories" finding.
- **Disappearing-title list (Wave 3.5 T36 input).** Strict arshkon-only threshold yields only 2 titles; pooled-2024 ≥ 10 / scraped < 3 yields 58. The pooled-2024 list is cluttered with clearance- and aggregator-heavy strings that reflect scraper sampling-frame differences, not real role disappearance. **A cleaner interpretation is that arshkon-specific declines are .NET/Java architect/senior PHP/Drupal/Salesforce legacy-stack patterns** — these reappear under the ≥5 threshold (see `disappearing_titles_arshkon_ge5.csv`).
- **Sensitivities are boring.** Aggregator exclusion, cap=20, cap=50 all move the per-1K and per-category numbers by < 2 pp. The findings are robust.

Scripts: `exploration/scripts/T10_title_taxonomy.py`, `exploration/scripts/T10_figures.py`. Tables in `exploration/tables/T10/`, figures in `exploration/figures/T10/`.

## Step 4. Title-to-content alignment (TF-IDF cosine)

For the 10 most common shared-both-periods titles, fit TF-IDF on all posting-level descriptions across arshkon + asaniczka + scraped for that title, compute mean-vector per group, take cosine between group means.

| title | n_arshkon | n_scraped | cos arshkon vs scraped | cos pooled-2024 vs scraped | within-2024 cos (null floor) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| software engineer | 149 | 1,545 | 0.787 | 0.856 | 0.886 |
| senior software engineer | 171 | 1,444 | 0.767 | 0.857 | 0.859 |
| data engineer | 86 | 764 | 0.799 | 0.839 | 0.883 |
| senior data engineer | 47 | 476 | 0.743 | 0.823 | 0.849 |
| devops engineer | 57 | 464 | 0.752 | 0.820 | 0.839 |
| full stack engineer | 58 | 382 | 0.767 | 0.806 | 0.865 |
| embedded software engineer | 32 | 200 | **0.585** | 0.777 | 0.679 |
| cloud engineer | 30 | 198 | 0.695 | 0.754 | 0.773 |
| java developer | 41 | 179 | 0.660 | 0.781 | 0.770 |
| system engineer | 42 | 118 | 0.764 | 0.840 | 0.812 |

**Interpretation.**
- Pooled-2024 vs scraped cosine is 0.75 - 0.86; within-2024 null floor is 0.68 - 0.89.
- Cross-period drift is only slightly above the within-period noise floor. On SOFTWARE ENGINEER, cos-pooled-vs-scraped (0.856) essentially equals the within-2024 floor (0.886) — same title, same content on average.
- The most drifted title is `embedded software engineer` where arshkon-vs-scraped cos is 0.585 (vs a within-2024 floor of 0.679). This indicates embedded descriptions have genuinely changed content composition; likely adding more modern-tooling content that did not exist in 2024.
- The `java developer` cross-period cos (0.66) also lies modestly below its within-2024 floor (0.77), suggesting some drift on that title.

**Implication.** Most shared titles still read like the same role on average. Content-level change is not uniform across titles; the SWE title taxonomy IS adding content (see T11 breadth/AI-mention results below), but the ADDITIONS average out against the stable core of each title. T12 and T13 will find the dimension-specific changes that TF-IDF flattens.

## Step 5. Title inflation / deflation signals

Share of SWE titles containing each seniority marker (priority: principal > staff > director > manager > senior > lead > mid > junior > none):

| group | junior | senior | lead | principal | staff | director | manager | none |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| arshkon 2024 | 2.5% | 26.2% | 5.5% | 2.8% | 2.5% | 0.2% | 0.7% | 59.1% |
| asaniczka 2024 | 0.9% | 42.7% | 8.5% | 5.9% | 2.7% | 0.2% | 1.4% | 37.5% |
| pooled 2024 | 1.2% | 39.3% | 7.9% | 5.2% | 2.7% | 0.2% | 1.3% | 41.9% |
| scraped 2026 | **3.0%** | **27.0%** | 5.0% | 3.7% | **5.5%** | 0.6% | 0.6% | **54.1%** |

**Key observations:**
- **"Senior" title-marker share drops 12 pp** pooled-2024 → scraped-2026 (39% → 27%). This is a substantial change and surprising. It is also driven in part by the arshkon-vs-asaniczka asymmetry in 2024 (asaniczka 43% has more "senior" titles than arshkon 26%); the scraped number 27% is actually close to arshkon.
- **"Staff" title share DOUBLES** (2.7% → 5.5%). Staff engineers are a growing labeled tier in 2026.
- **"Junior" title-marker share rises** from 1.2% → 3.0% (+1.8 pp).
- **"None" share (no marker in title)** rose from 42% → 54%. More titles are role-descriptor only (no seniority prefix).

**Caveat for the "senior drop".** The 2024 anchor is confounded by the asaniczka-vs-arshkon asymmetry (T05 documented asaniczka's "senior" share is higher than arshkon). Arshkon-only "senior"-title share is 26%; scraped 27% matches. This could be read as "no real senior-title decline, asaniczka was outlier". OR that the scraped corpus behaves like arshkon (not asaniczka) in this dimension. Either way, the arshkon-only sensitivity weakens the headline "senior drop."

Non-aggregator and cap-20/cap-50 sensitivities move these numbers by ≤ 2 pp.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **Title-space fragmentation is essentially flat per 1K postings.** Unique-per-1K is 454 pooled-2024 vs 488 scraped-2026 — a modest ~8% rise. The impression of "title taxonomy is fragmenting" is largely an artifact of corpus size, not per-capita richness. This weakens the "title space is exploding" framing.

2. **"Senior" title-marker share DROPPED 12 pp pooled-2024 → scraped-2026** (39% → 27%). Even against arshkon-only (26%) the scraped 27% is essentially flat, so there's no arshkon-vs-scraped "senior rose" story in title markers. But comparing to the full pooled-2024 baseline, "senior" title prevalence fell substantially. This is interpretable with the T05 asaniczka-asymmetry caveat: asaniczka in 2024 over-weighted "senior"-prefixed titles, and the scraped corpus behaves closer to arshkon on this dimension.

3. **"Staff" title share DOUBLED** (2.7% → 5.5%). This is consistent with anecdotal reports of the staff-engineer tier becoming more common; quantitatively confirmed at the title level.

4. **"None" (no seniority marker in title) rose 12 pp** (42% → 54%). The rise is mostly compensating the senior-drop. More 2026 titles are pure role descriptors (e.g., `full stack engineer`, `ai engineer`, `platform engineer`) without seniority prefixes. This implies either: (a) employers are pushing seniority to the description rather than the title, or (b) role-type titles are in expanding share while seniority-prefix titles are stable-share.

5. **Content drift for shared titles is only slightly above within-2024 noise floor** (cosine ~0.80-0.86 pooled-2024-vs-scraped vs ~0.77-0.89 within-2024). TF-IDF at this aggregation level captures the stable core of each role but averages out dimension-specific changes (AI language, scope language — which T11 shows clearly rose). The aggregate "same-title = same-content" impression is partly a flattening artifact.

6. **QA/testing titles are shrinking as a share of SWE titles** (5.1% → 3.5% for qa_testing category). Combined with `embedded_firmware` (2.5% → 1.7%), these two legacy-SWE categories are visibly losing share. This is one of the cleaner "role displacement" signals in the T10 data.

7. **"Founding" and "forward deployed" as 2026-only title patterns.** 23 `founding software engineer` and 15 `founding ai engineer` and 14 `forward deployed software engineer` postings in 2026 scraped with arshkon counts of 0. These reflect startup/enterprise-GPU-company emergence in the 2026 hiring mix — a composition-shift signal consistent with Gate 1's between-company story.
