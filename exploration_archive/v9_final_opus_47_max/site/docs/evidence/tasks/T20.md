# T20 — Seniority boundary clarity (supervised-feature discriminability)

**Orientation:** DISCOVERY complement to T15. Tests Gate 2 prior that boundaries **sharpened** 2024→2026 using a second method (logistic-regression AUC on structured features) rather than unsupervised text cosine. Also unpacks which features drive separation, attributes gap movement to senior vs junior side, and fits a continuous YOE × period interaction.

**Filters:** SWE LinkedIn (`source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english=true AND date_flag='ok' AND is_swe=true`). For AUC models, `seniority_final != 'unknown'`. For gap evolution, primary panel is J3/S4 (YOE-based label-independent), with J1/S1 and J2/S2 as T30 panel sensitivities.

**Features (9):** `yoe_min_years_llm` (fallback `yoe_extracted`), `tech_count`, `ai_binary`, `scope_density`, `mgmt_strong_density`, `description_cleaned_length`, `education_level`, `requirement_breadth`, `credential_stack_depth`. Source: `T11_posting_features.parquet`. Median-imputed per fold before standardizing.

**Model:** Logistic regression (L2, C=1.0, lbfgs), 5-fold stratified CV AUC (random_state=20202020). Full-data standardized coefficients used for feature importance ranking.

**Sample sizes (`seniority_final`, default filter):**

| Period | entry | associate | mid-senior | director |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 415 | 54 | 13,574 | 91 |
| 2026 | 1,650 | 121 | 19,703 | 325 |

Associate cells are sparse (54 and 121). Entry↔associate and associate↔mid-senior AUCs should be read with that caveat.

---

## Headline

**Boundaries sharpened, especially at the middle of the ladder.** Supervised AUC on structured features rose at 2 of 3 adjacent boundaries and at the aggregate `junior↔senior` 3-level boundary. The junior-side of the ladder sharpened more than the senior-side; the `mid-senior↔director` boundary actually **softened** (AUC fell −0.022), meaning directors look more like mid-seniors in 2026 than in 2024 on structured features. Continuous YOE × period interaction is significant and positive for 5 of 6 outcomes (p < 10⁻⁶), confirming that YOE sorts roles more strongly in 2026. Both findings corroborate T15's TF-IDF cosine divergence — two independent methods agree.

## 1) Boundary AUC, 2024 vs 2026

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| entry ↔ associate | 2024 | 415 | 54 | **0.708** | |
| entry ↔ associate | 2026 | 1,650 | 121 | **0.801** | **+0.093** ↑ |
| associate ↔ mid-senior | 2024 | 54 | 13,574 | **0.743** | |
| associate ↔ mid-senior | 2026 | 121 | 19,703 | **0.893** | **+0.150** ↑ |
| mid-senior ↔ director | 2024 | 13,574 | 91 | **0.723** | |
| mid-senior ↔ director | 2026 | 19,703 | 325 | **0.701** | −0.022 ↓ |
| junior ↔ senior (`seniority_3level`) | 2024 | 415 | 13,665 | **0.825** | |
| junior ↔ senior (`seniority_3level`) | 2026 | 1,650 | 20,028 | **0.836** | +0.010 ↑ |

*Interpretation.* At the junior/associate end of the ladder, structured features became noticeably better at separating adjacent levels. The biggest gain is `associate ↔ mid-senior` (+0.15 AUC). The `mid-senior ↔ director` boundary slightly softened — directors in 2026 look more like mid-seniors than 2024 directors did.

**AUC CV fold stability.** Per-fold AUCs are within ±0.05 of the mean for all boundaries except `entry↔associate 2024` (ranging 0.39-0.73 across sensitivities) where the 54-row associate cell dominates fold variance. The associate↔mid-senior 2024 AUC is more variable across runs (0.743–0.747 across two consecutive runs is within fold noise). Treat the precise AUC values as indicative; the direction (sharpen vs soften) is robust.

**Cross-method agreement with T15.** T15's TF-IDF junior↔senior cosine went 0.946 → 0.863 (diverging, Δ = −0.083). T20's supervised `junior↔senior` AUC went 0.825 → 0.836 (diverging, Δ = +0.010). Both methods agree direction. Magnitudes are not directly comparable (cosine is in [0,1] scale, AUC in [0.5,1.0]), but the small AUC change is consistent with the small unsupervised cosine change at the 3-level aggregate — the structural-feature signal is compressed relative to open text.

## 4) Continuous YOE × period interaction

File: `yoe_period_interaction.csv`. Model: `M ~ YOE + period + YOE × period + log(description_length)`, OLS with HC1 robust SE, on LLM-frame rows (n=38,730).

| Outcome | YOE × period coef | 95% CI | p | R² |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **requirement_breadth** | **+0.273** | [+0.234, +0.311] | 4.98e-44 | 0.219 |
| **tech_count** | **+0.177** | [+0.142, +0.211] | 5.49e-24 | 0.067 |
| credential_stack_depth | +0.017 | [+0.011, +0.024] | 3.09e-07 | 0.309 |
| scope_density | +0.0089 | [+0.0061, +0.0118] | 1.01e-09 | 0.037 |
| mgmt_strong_density | +0.0088 | [+0.0073, +0.0103] | 2.84e-31 | 0.016 |
| ai_binary | +0.00036 | [−0.0012, +0.0019] | 0.655 | 0.065 |

**Reads.**
- 5 of 6 outcomes have a strictly positive, significant YOE × period_2026 interaction — **YOE sorts roles more strongly in 2026** on structured features. The continuous analogue of boundary sharpening is affirmed.
- The biggest interaction is `requirement_breadth` (+0.27 items per YOE-year extra in 2026 vs 2024). At YOE = 5 this adds 1.4 items of requirement_breadth beyond the 2024 pattern; at YOE = 10, 2.7 items.
- AI-binary is the exception: the interaction is essentially zero (p = 0.66). AI mentions rose uniformly with `period_2026` (+0.12 across all YOE levels); the coefficient on `YOE × period` is null. **AI mentions became more universal in 2026; they are not more concentrated at senior levels than before.** This is the cleanest corroboration of Gate 2's "AI is at junior level at least as much as senior" observation.
- `mgmt_strong_density` + YOE × period is small but significant (+0.0088 per YOE year per period-2026). Given 0.55 precision it is diagnostic-only.

## 5) Missing middle — is associate disappearing, entry-ward, or mid-ward?

File: `missing_middle.csv`. Euclidean distance on z-standardized features from the associate centroid to each neighbor centroid.

| Period | associate → entry | associate → mid-senior | associate → director |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 0.71 | 1.21 | 2.08 |
| 2026 | 0.82 | 1.44 | 2.22 |

**Findings.**
- `associate → entry` distance grew +0.11 (from 0.71 to 0.82).
- `associate → mid-senior` distance grew +0.23 (from 1.21 to 1.44). **Largest growth.**
- `associate → director` grew +0.14.
- All three neighbor distances GREW. Associate is becoming MORE distinct from all neighbors on structured features, not merging into entry or mid-senior.
- **Cosine-based read** (from `centroid_similarity_matrix.csv`): within-2026, associate↔mid-senior cosine = **0.036** (near-orthogonal), while within-2024 it was **0.550**. That is a very large divergence. Associate↔entry cosine held roughly flat (0.89 in 2026 vs 0.97 in 2024).
- Interpretation: associate as a level is NOT disappearing into entry or mid-senior; it has carved out its own feature profile. But with n=121 in 2026 (vs 19,703 mid-senior), the absolute share of associate postings is tiny (0.6% of known-seniority rows in 2026). The level is distinct but rare.

**Caveat.** Associate sample sizes are small (54 / 121). The centroid distance direction is robust but the absolute magnitudes are fragile.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **The `mid-senior↔director` boundary SOFTENED while the junior-side sharpened.** AUC fell from 0.723 (2024) to 0.701 (2026). Centroid cosine for mid-senior↔director jumped from +0.50 to +0.91. Directors look more like mid-seniors on structured features in 2026 than they did in 2024. This is the inverse of the headline finding. The asymmetric sharpening pattern (junior-side YES, senior-apex NO) is new and should inform Wave 3.5 T34's emergent-senior-archetype question.

2. **`credential_stack_depth` became the dominant entry↔associate discriminator in 2026 (std coef +1.08), flipping from YOE-and-length being dominant in 2024.** Associates now differ from entries primarily by stacking more credential categories. In 2026, associates also stack MORE credentials than mid-seniors (credential_stack_depth coef went NEGATIVE for associate↔mid-senior in 2026: −0.78). The ranking has inverted — this is a genuine re-wiring of the associate tier's feature signature.

3. **AI-binary has no significant YOE × period interaction** (p = 0.66). Contrary to a "AI was added to senior-only postings" prior, the continuous model shows AI rose at ALL YOE levels between 2024 and 2026 by the same amount. AI is now a floor-level expectation, not a senior-specific addition. This aligns with Gate 2's `copilot rate: J3 4.6% > S4 4.1%` observation.

4. **"Missing middle" is a misnomer.** Associate did not disappear into entry or mid-senior; it became MORE distinct from both neighbors (distance +0.11 from entry, +0.23 from mid-senior). Associate is the tier that most *carved out* an independent identity in 2026, even though its volume share is only ~0.6% of known-seniority rows.

5. **Boundary sharpening survives YOE removal.** Even when YOE is dropped from the feature set entirely, the sharpening Δ is larger at the junior-side boundaries (+0.19, +0.15) than the full model. The non-YOE features (requirement_breadth, tech_count, scope_density, credential_stack_depth, description length) by themselves sort levels better in 2026.

6. **Requirement_breadth YOE×period interaction is 4.98e-44 significant.** Every additional year of YOE in 2026 adds +0.27 items of requirement_breadth beyond what 2024 would predict. At YOE=10 this is 2.7 extra items — more than the entire 2024 gap between J3 and S4 (which was 1.09 items). Requirement_breadth is becoming a VERY strong YOE-sorting feature in 2026.

7. **Within-2024 calibration noise eats several Δgap claims.** Tech_count, description_length, education_level, and credential_stack_depth gap deltas are below the within-2024 arshkon/asaniczka noise floor. Only requirement_breadth (SNR 3.3) and ai_binary (SNR 3.8) have clean signal on the Δgap dimension. This means the "feature-level gap widening" narrative is cleanest for requirement_breadth and ai_binary; the tech_count and description_length stories are supplemental, not leads.

8. **Aggregator exclusion TIGHTENS the junior-side sharpening and FLIPS the 3level aggregate slightly negative** (0.853 → 0.841, Δ −0.012). The headline `junior↔senior` sharpening at the 3-level aggregate is partly aggregator-driven. The adjacent-pair boundary sharpening is NOT aggregator-driven — it holds strongly with aggregators excluded.
