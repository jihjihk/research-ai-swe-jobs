**Read this carefully.** Among companies with ≥5 SWE postings that have ≥1 LLM-labeled row, **58–63% have ZERO entry-labeled postings under J3 (yoe_llm ≤ 2)**. Under J1 (seniority_final='entry'), 77–87% have zero.

**Entry posting is a specialty activity.** The story of "does entry share rise or fall" is a story about a minority subset of employers that do entry hiring. This has several implications downstream:

1. Aggregate entry-share changes are driven by changes in that minority's behavior, not by a market-wide redistribution of hiring activity to more junior levels.
2. Entry-share changes driven by composition of the subset (different firms in the flagged set) may look very different from changes driven by the same firms changing behavior. **See the decomposition results in Step 5.**
3. A 1pp shift in aggregate entry share can represent a substantial change in entry-posting density among the minority of firms that post entry roles.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **Entry-level posting is a minority employer activity, not a market-wide one.** 58–63% of ≥5-post companies have ZERO entry-labeled postings under J3. This reframes every aggregate "entry share X" claim as really a claim about a specific minority subset. The "entry share" metric in aggregate is a weighted average where most firms contribute 0 — it has high leverage from a small number of employers.

2. **Within-company entry-share change was roughly zero (2024→2026) in the overlap panel under J3.** The +5pp aggregate rise is almost entirely between-company. This is genuinely important for Wave 2/3 — "the entry-share gap" is compositional, not behavioral for the same firms.

3. **J4 (yoe_llm ≤ 3) flags Amazon, AWS, Google, Microsoft, PNC, Discover Financial as "entry specialists"** with 60–80% shares. The reason is that big-tech uses quantized YOE bands — Google scraped has 187 roles at yoe_llm=2, 139 at 5, 111 at 8 (bimodal); Microsoft scraped has 83 at yoe=2, 83 at yoe=4. A "3-year" cutoff lands right in the middle of the lower band. **Do not use J4 as a primary entry criterion in downstream work.** The task spec was wise to designate J3 as primary.

4. **AI mention rate 2024→2026 is mostly within-company (+31pp out of +39pp total).** This is one of the cleanest "same employer, new behavior" patterns in the panel. If the AI-hiring-wave claim is going to hold up, this is the clean version.

5. **Duplicate-template audit is essentially empty after Stage-4 multi-location dedup.** Only 6 companies in scraped have any residual dup-ratio ≥ 2 with n ≥ 5. This is the strongest evidence that the multi-location collapse in Stage 4 is working as intended. Multi-location flag carries 7.4% of scraped SWE rows and concentrates in Meta, Deloitte, CoreWeave, Turing — all plausible cases of legitimate cross-metro postings.

6. **Asaniczka HHI triples when aggregators are included (19 non-agg → 54 all).** Jobs-for-Humanity→Capital One aggregator resolution adds 571 more postings to an already-large Capital One cohort. Asaniczka is more aggregator-dependent than the other sources.

7. **The returning-companies cohort is a tighter analytical frame.** 2,109 companies account for 55% of scraped SWE postings. Restricting cross-period comparisons to this cohort mechanically controls for the "new-entrant dilution" and "departed-company selection" effects. This is exactly why T37 exists, and this artifact is ready to consume.

8. **"Jobs via Dice" alone (1,934 postings, 4.3% of scraped SWE) is the single biggest concentration lever in any corpus-level scraped analysis.** Excluding aggregators removes it; the is_aggregator flag does this correctly in the unified artifact.

9. **New entrants in 2026 (6,368 companies) have broader company representation but shorter postings (4,615 vs 5,121 chars) and lower J3 share (12.8% vs 15.3%) than returning companies.** A "new entrants are drifting toward non-entry roles" pattern is visible but weak.

10. **Salesforce (not listed in top-20) and other SaaS vendors are under-represented relative to expectation.** Observed in the top-20 scan but not in the top-20 itself for any source. Worth noting for Wave 2 tech-ecosystem analyses — if Salesforce ecosystem titles matter, they will come from the long tail.

## Recommended default for Wave 2/3 aggregates

For any corpus-wide aggregate claim 2024→2026, the default should be **to present at least two of**:
- Raw aggregate (what you naively get).
- Per-company weight-capped (n_per_co ≤ 50) or weighted by 1/sqrt(n_per_co).
- Restricted to the returning-company cohort (n=2,109 companies).
- With and without `is_aggregator` exclusion.
- Under both J1 and J3 for any entry-related metric.

If two variants agree, the finding is robust. If they disagree, report both and explain the driver (composition vs within-firm).
