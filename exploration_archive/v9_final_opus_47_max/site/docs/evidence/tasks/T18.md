# T18 — Cross-occupation boundary analysis

**Owner:** Agent K
**Wave:** 3
**Filter:** `source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english AND date_flag='ok' AND (is_swe OR is_swe_adjacent OR is_control)`.
**Design:** 3 occupation groups (SWE, SWE_ADJACENT, CONTROL) × 2 periods (2024 = pooled arshkon+asaniczka; 2026 = scraped). All pattern-based metrics use the V1-validated regexes from `exploration/artifacts/shared/validated_mgmt_patterns.json` (AI-strict core for 2024 to drop fine-tuning noise; AI-strict full for 2026; scope_v1_rebuilt for scope).

Tables: `exploration/tables/T18/`. Script: `exploration/scripts/T18_cross_occupation.py`.

---

## Headline

The AI-mention rise is **SWE-specific, at ~13 pp magnitude above control**. Over the same two-year window:

| Group | n (2024) | n (2026) | AI-strict 2024 | AI-strict 2026 | Δ (pp) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| SWE | 22,820 | 45,317 | 0.74% | 14.93% | **+14.19** |
| SWE-adjacent | 11,816 | 12,890 | 0.58% | 11.43% | +10.84 |
| Control | 113,955 | 46,572 | 0.17% | 0.34% | +0.17 |

**DiD (SWE − CONTROL) for `ai_strict_prev` = +14.02 pp, 95% CI [+13.67, +14.37] pp.** The control drift is +0.17 pp (essentially zero). The SWE AI rise is not a general labor-market phenomenon — **it is a SWE (and secondarily SWE-adjacent) phenomenon**, almost entirely preserved under formal DiD. RQ3's cross-occupation-universality concern is resolved in favor of **SWE-specificity** for AI-mention.

Other metrics (length, scope, tech-count, seniority shares) also show SWE-distinct deltas under DiD. The one metric that inverts is **requirements-section share (T13 classifier)**, where SWE Δ is **negative** (−2.7 pp) while control Δ is **positive** (+4.8 pp), giving DiD = **−7.6 pp** — SWE is anti-trending against control. Under a simpler-regex alternative classifier the sign flips: SWE +11.5 pp, DiD +5.0 pp. Classifier-sensitive, consistent with Gate 2 demotion.

## Methodology (reconstructed)

1. **Parallel trends** (`parallel_trends.csv`, `_wide.csv`): for each (occupation × period) cell, compute raw-description prevalence/length/tech counts plus T13-classifier section shares (subsampled to 3,000 per cell for speed).
2. **DiD** (`did_table.csv`): `swe_delta = swe_2026 − swe_2024`; `control_delta` similarly; DiD = swe_delta − control_delta (and swe_delta − adj_delta). 95% CI computed for proportion-valued metrics via pooled-variance SE for delta-of-deltas.
3. **Boundary similarity** (`boundary_similarity.csv`, `migrating_terms.csv`): 200 LLM-labeled SWE + 200 LLM-labeled SWE-adjacent per period; TF-IDF (min_df=3, 1-2 ngrams, stop_words=english, max_features=10k); cosine between centroids.
4. **Adjacent role deep dive** (`adjacent_role_dynamics.csv`, `_raw.csv`, `adjacent_similarity_to_swe.csv`): 10 title-regex families (ML engineer, DevOps/SRE, data engineer, etc.) × 2 periods, metric deltas + cosine-to-SWE centroid.
5. **AI gradient** (`ai_gradient.csv`): per-period AI-strict prevalence ordered by occupation.
6. **Classifier sensitivity** (`requirements_classifier_sensitivity.csv`, `_flip_flags.json`): T13 structured classifier vs simpler header-regex on requirements share; flip flags booleans.
7. **Sensitivities**: aggregator-exclusion and SWE-tier (drop `title_lookup_llm`) recompute loops were coded but CSV outputs are not in this run's `tables/T18/` — step7 either was not executed to disk or its writes did not persist; **action item**: re-run `step7_sensitivities` in isolation to ship `aggregator_sensitivity.csv` / `swe_tier_sensitivity.csv` before Gate 3. The main DiD headline does not depend on them.

## Core findings

### 1. AI-mention is SWE-specific (primary paper-novelty result)

| Metric | SWE Δ | Adj Δ | Ctl Δ | DiD (SWE−Ctl) | 95% CI | Direction flip under DiD? | DiD as % of SWE Δ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `ai_strict_prev` | +0.1419 | +0.1084 | +0.0017 | **+0.1402** | [+0.1367, +0.1437] | no | **98.8%** |

The SWE AI delta is 98.8% survived by DiD — i.e. control contributes essentially nothing. **The AI mention rise is not a universal labor-market phenomenon; it is concentrated in SWE and SWE-adjacent roles.** SWE − adjacent DiD is +3.34 pp — smaller, indicating substantial AI diffusion into adjacent tech roles, but still with SWE leading.

Adjacent-role decomposition (`adjacent_role_dynamics.csv`) confirms the within-adjacent heterogeneity:
- ML engineer AI-strict 12.0% → 54.9% (+42.8 pp, by far the largest)
- Data scientist 3.6% → 19.4% (+15.8 pp)
- Solutions architect 0.7% → 16.3% (+15.6 pp)
- Data engineer 0.4% → 9.5% (+9.1 pp)
- Security engineer 0.2% → 6.1% (+5.9 pp)
- DevOps/SRE 0.3% → 5.3% (+5.0 pp)
- Data analyst 0.2% → 2.0% (+1.9 pp)
- QA/quality engineer 0.0% → 2.5% (+2.5 pp)
- Network engineer 0.0% → 1.2% (+1.2 pp)
- Systems administrator 0.0% → 1.0% (+1.0 pp)

ML engineer is where AI-mention has become majority-prevalence. The AI rise crosses a 50% threshold for ML-engineer postings between 2024 and 2026 — that is effectively *redefining* the role around AI tooling.

### 2. Description length and tech count rise in SWE beyond control (still SWE-specific, but less sharply)

| Metric | SWE Δ | Ctl Δ | DiD | DiD as % of SWE Δ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `length_median` (chars) | +1,248 | +772 | +476 | 38.2% |
| `length_mean` | +1,130 | +702 | +428 | 37.9% |
| `tech_count_mean` | +1.00 | +0.06 | +0.94 | 94.1% |
| `tech_count_median` | +1 | 0 | +1 | 100% |

Length rises universally but SWE rises more. 62% of SWE length Δ is shared with control, so "postings got longer" is partly market-wide — but tech-count Δ is almost entirely SWE-specific. The Gate 2 "boilerplate-led length growth" story is corroborated: cross-occupation controls show the same direction.

### 3. Scope-language rises universally; SWE not specifically distinctive

| Metric | SWE Δ | Adj Δ | Ctl Δ | DiD (SWE−Ctl) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `scope_prev` (V1 scope_v1_rebuilt) | +0.207 | +0.239 | +0.172 | +0.035 |

SWE scope Δ is **only 17% SWE-specific** by DiD. Scope-language rise is broadly cross-occupation; SWE_ADJACENT actually rose more than SWE. The Wave 2 "universal scope broadening" finding survives cross-occupation — scope inflation is a **general trend in posting language**, not an SWE-specific phenomenon. This *weakens* the RQ1 scope-inflation paper-novelty argument relative to the AI-mention argument.

### 4. Requirements-section share: classifier-sensitive DiD with direction flip

| Classifier | SWE Δ | Adj Δ | Ctl Δ | DiD (SWE−Ctl) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| T13 structured | **−0.027** | +0.00002 | +0.048 | **−0.076** |
| simple regex | +0.115 | +0.022 | +0.065 | +0.050 |

Direction flips at both the SWE-level and DiD level (`requirements_classifier_flip_flags.json`: both flags true). Gate 2 already demoted this claim. **For T33 (Wave 3.5 hiring-bar regression), the choice of classifier materially determines whether SWE requirements-section share is contracting vs expanding against control.**

### 5. Entry-share Δ has small but directionally SWE-specific DiD

| Metric | SWE Δ | Ctl Δ | DiD | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `sen_entry_share` (seniority_final) | +0.0182 | +0.0099 | +0.0083 | [+0.0051, +0.0115] |

SWE entry share rose 1.8 pp; control rose 1.0 pp; DiD is +0.83 pp (~45% of the SWE Δ). Small but non-zero — SWE entry share is rising modestly faster than control. Gate 1 + T08 pooled-within-company "+5 pp" J3 rise likely over-estimated SWE-specificity; much of it is shared with the broader labor market.

### 6. Mid-senior share dropped in SWE but rose in control — DiD is strongly negative

| Metric | SWE Δ | Ctl Δ | DiD | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `sen_mid-senior_share` | −0.1600 | +0.0622 | **−0.2222** | [−0.2314, −0.2131] |

This is the largest and most robust SWE-specific result in the table. **SWE is seeing a 22 pp larger mid-senior-share shrinkage than control.** Combined with the rising "unknown" share (SWE: 38.1% → 51.9%) and flat entry share, the SWE seniority distribution in 2026 is being *reclassified into "unknown"* (native labels going missing on scraped data, an artifact noted in T03/T05 rather than a true composition change). Worth noting this DiD magnitude is driven partly by `seniority_final` measurement artifacts, not only by underlying SWE restructuring.

### 7. Boundary similarity: SWE ↔ SWE-adjacent CONVERGED, not diverged

| Pair | 2024 cosine | 2026 cosine | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| SWE vs SWE_ADJACENT | 0.785 | 0.826 | **+0.041** |
| SWE self (2024 vs 2026) | — | — | 0.864 |
| SWE_ADJACENT self (2024 vs 2026) | — | — | 0.827 |

**SWE and SWE-adjacent content grew MORE similar, not less, between 2024 and 2026.** Interpretation: both corpora picked up the same AI-tooling vocabulary, narrowing the content gap. Adjacent self-drift (0.827) is ~the same magnitude as SWE-to-adjacent cross-period, meaning adjacent roles changed about as much as they became SWE-like. The SWE↔adjacent boundary is **blurring at the content level**, even though seniority boundaries within SWE sharpened (T15/T20).

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **SWE ↔ SWE-adjacent content cosine *rose* 0.785 → 0.826.** The occupational boundary between SWE and closely-adjacent tech roles is blurring on content, even as the seniority boundary within SWE sharpened (T15, T20). Two different boundary effects in the same corpus, in opposite directions.
2. **ML engineer AI-mention crossed 50% (12% → 55%).** Majority prevalence. The role's definition effectively inverted between 2024 and 2026.
3. **SWE mid-senior DiD is −22 pp,** the largest DiD magnitude in the entire table, but driven primarily by `seniority_final='unknown'` rising 38% → 52% in SWE 2026 — a measurement artifact (native labels missing on scraped) rather than substantive seniority composition shift. The artifact is SWE-specific because the SWE scraper filter has specific coverage gaps that control roles don't.
4. **Scope DiD is only 3.5 pp / 17% of SWE Δ.** The "universal scope broadening" finding (T11 + V1) is weaker when cross-occupation controls are brought in — scope-language rise is a cross-industry writing-style shift, not SWE-distinctive. This suggests the "scope inflation" paper narrative should either (a) become a minor supporting finding instead of a headline, or (b) be reframed as "SWE scope broadening in-line with general labor-market scope-language escalation".
5. **Systems administrator nearly disappeared in scraped** (n 1,340 → 96). Either the scraper's SWE-adjacent query stratification de-emphasized systems administration, or the role is declining in LinkedIn posting volume — not distinguishable from T18 alone. Worth following up.
6. **Control entry-share rose 1.0 pp 2024→2026 on its own.** The Wave 2 "junior rung tension" is partly a labor-market-wide phenomenon at the reduced DiD of +0.83 pp rather than the raw SWE Δ of +1.82 pp. This weakens RQ1's employer-side junior-rewriting story by ~45%.
7. **SWE_ADJACENT scope Δ (+0.24) > SWE scope Δ (+0.21).** Adjacent roles are adopting scope language *more* than SWE is. Consistent with the boundary-blurring result: adjacent roles are Moody toward the SWE style faster than vice-versa.

## Data-quality caveats

- **`seniority_final='unknown'` on 2026 scraped SWE is 51.9%.** Native LinkedIn seniority is missing on the 2026 scrape for much of the SWE cohort. T03/T05 already noted this; the T18 mid-senior DiD inherits the artifact. For paper claims about seniority-share Δ, rely on `yoe_min_years_llm`-based J3/S4 (T30 primary) rather than `seniority_final` shares.
- **Aggregator-sensitivity tables were not materialized in the current run.** `step7_sensitivities` is coded but `aggregator_sensitivity.csv` and `swe_tier_sensitivity.csv` are not present in `tables/T18/`. Re-run before Gate 3 to confirm aggregator exclusion doesn't shift headline DiD. Action item.
- **The AI-strict pattern uses CORE (without fine-tuning) for 2024 and FULL (with fine-tuning) for 2026.** This is per Gate 2 pre-commit #9 (V1-validated precision: fine-tuning in 2024 is 0.47). It makes the AI Δ slightly conservative on the 2024 end. Consistent across all three occupation groups.
- **SWE-adjacent n is small in 2026** (n=12,890) relative to SWE (n=45,317) and control (n=46,572). The adjacent-group DiD uncertainty is higher than SWE vs control.
- **Requirements-section classifier is unresolved** across all downstream uses. T13 classifier gives SWE DiD −7.6 pp; simpler regex gives +5.0 pp. Either use T13 primary with T33 sensitivity loop, or cite the range.
- **Control group composition differs across periods.** Control n dropped 114k → 47k. Scrape filter changed. We assume the control composition shift is orthogonal to the effects being measured; plausible given the ~0 pp AI Δ in control.

## Answering the paper-novelty question

**Does the SWE-specific effect survive DiD against control?**

- **AI-mention: YES, robustly.** DiD +14.0 pp, 95% CI [+13.7, +14.4] pp, 98.8% of the SWE Δ. The AI rise is **SWE-specific**, not a general labor-market phenomenon. This is the **strongest single novelty signal in the Wave 3 evidence body** so far.
- **Tech-count: YES.** DiD +0.94 / 94% of SWE Δ. SWE tech stacks widened well beyond control drift.
- **Description length: PARTIALLY.** DiD +428 chars, 38% of SWE Δ. Length growth is SWE-specific only at the margin; most of the length rise is shared with control.
- **Scope-language: NO.** DiD +3.5 pp, only 17% of SWE Δ. Scope broadening is largely a cross-occupation trend.
- **Requirements-section share: CLASSIFIER-SENSITIVE.** Either −7.6 pp (T13) or +5.0 pp (simple regex). Cannot be cited as a clean direction.
- **Entry-share: WEAKLY.** DiD +0.83 pp, 45% of SWE Δ. Some SWE-specificity, but smaller than Gate 1 implied.
- **Mid-senior share: ARTIFACT-HEAVY.** Large DiD but driven by measurement hole, not substantive composition change.

**RQ3 paper-novelty verdict:** The AI rise is SWE-specific and cross-occupation-contrasted. This is the finding to lead on. The scope-inflation finding should be demoted or reframed. Boundary-blurring (SWE ↔ adjacent) is an additional SWE-relevant finding worth a paragraph. The within-SWE seniority-boundary-sharpening finding (T15/T20) operates on a different boundary and should be presented separately.

## Open questions

- Why did systems administrator volume collapse in scraped (1,340 → 96)? Scraper query stratification or real decline?
- Is the SWE-adjacent scope Δ (+0.239) larger than SWE Δ (+0.207) reflecting genuine adjacent-role scope inflation, or a composition shift in which adjacent roles in 2026 were sampled disproportionately from scope-heavy sub-families (ML engineer, solutions architect)?
- Does the boundary-convergence (SWE ↔ adjacent cosine +0.04) persist after controlling for AI/ML-family share? If the convergence is primarily driven by the ML-engineer sub-family, it's a within-family effect, not a cross-boundary effect. Worth T32 or Wave 4 follow-up.
