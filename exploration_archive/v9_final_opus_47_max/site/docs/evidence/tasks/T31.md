Patterns: V1-validated `ai_strict_v1_rebuilt` (0.96 precision) and `mgmt_strict_v1_rebuilt` (0.98) from `validated_mgmt_patterns.json`; `ai_broad_no_mcp` drops MCP sub-token per Gate 2 pre-commit.

## Headline findings

1. **Pair-level AI-strict drift +13.4 pp** on the cleanest-overlap arshkon_min3 panel (23 qualifying pairs) and **+13.4 pp** on pooled_min5 panel (33 pairs) — **LARGER than T16 company-level +7.7–8.3 pp** on the same companies. The same-title restriction does NOT shrink the AI-rewriting signal; it amplifies it. The dominant within-co AI rise IS the within-pair rewriting, not title-composition shifts. `ai_broad_no_mcp` drift: +24.8 pp arshkon-min3, +31.6 pp pooled-min5.

2. **Pair-level breadth_resid drift is NEAR ZERO and NEGATIVE** on the primary n≥3 panel (arshkon_min3 −0.02; pooled_min5 +0.08). T16 company-level breadth_resid delta was +0.40 pooled and +0.69 arshkon — so **breadth_resid rise DOES shrink 90%+ when restricted to same-title pairs.** Where a company broadens scope, it is doing so via adding new titles / rewriting job levels, NOT by fattening same-title requirements. The "senior-scope-inflation" signal is primarily cross-title within a company, not within-title at a posting level. **Pair-level < T16 company-level for breadth.**

3. **Pair-level mgmt_strict drift +12.2 pp (arshkon) / +16.0 pp (pooled)** — positive and meaningful on the V1-rebuilt pattern. Under the cleaner V1-validated management pattern, management responsibilities DID rise at same-title pairs (contradicting the original T11 "mgmt fell" on noisy mgmt_broad). Supports T21 "mgmt is flat, not falling — and at pair level, slightly rising".

4. **Requirements-section character share fell ~1.8-1.9 pp at pair level.** Direction matches T13 aggregate (−3.5 pp) but magnitude is halved under pair restriction. Consistent with T13's classifier-sensitive H3 and T29's LLM-authorship mediation — the within-pair requirements-share decline is smaller but still present.

5. **Description length rose +293-305 chars at pair level** (median ≈ +321) — half of T13's aggregate +1,130-char growth. Pair restriction removes 60-75% of length inflation: most length growth happened via new titles (new role templates, AI/ML archetypes) rather than within-pair re-templating.

6. **YOE dropped at pair level: rule-based YOE −0.60 (arshkon) / −1.01 (pooled); LLM YOE −0.31 / −0.16.** Same-title posts are asking for LESS experience in 2026 than 2024 — modest but direction-consistent across both measures on the pool panel. Entry-ification of roles is observable at pair level.

7. **Top-20 AI exemplars: Wells Fargo, Microsoft, Capital One, Walmart, Amazon dominate.** Wells Fargo "lead software engineer" Δ=+52.9 pp (0% 2024 → 52.9% 2026, n=4 → 17). Microsoft "software engineer ii" Δ=+40 pp (n=6 → 35). The Microsoft representative 2026 postings explicitly cite Copilot, Generative AI, AI Systems — Microsoft re-templated its "Software Engineer II" role to be AI-forward.

8. **Archetype stratification: 9-13 pairs sit in `software/systems/requirements` (T09 archetype 6)** — the generic-SWE hub — with AI Δ=+12 pp but breadth_resid Δ=−0.37. The largest-n archetype shows the core pattern. `systems/agent/workflows` (archetype 25; pooled n=3) shows the LARGEST breadth_resid Δ (+2.17) and positive AI (+21.8 pp) — as expected, the emerging AI-agent archetype is where scope AND AI rise together. `models/systems/llm` (archetype 1) shows positive AI drift but NEGATIVE breadth_resid drift — the ML/LLM archetype's breadth is stable while AI language densifies.

9. **Consistency vs T16: pair-level AI drift EXCEEDS T16 company-level** (pooled +16.3 pp pair vs +9.2 pp T16 on matched 18 companies). **Pair-level breadth drift UNDERSHOOTS T16** (pair −0.17 vs T16 +0.40 on same 18 companies). Spearman rank-correlation AI pair-vs-T16 = 0.35 (positive, weak); breadth pair-vs-T16 = −0.29 (negative). The AI signal agrees in rank AND amplifies in magnitude; breadth signal disagrees.

## Surprises

1. **AI drift at pair level EXCEEDS company level.** Expected that restricting to same-title would SHRINK the signal (title composition was supposed to be doing the work). Opposite result: pair-level +13.4 pp > T16-company-level +7.7-8.3 pp. **Within-firm AI rewriting happens at the same-title level, not via title recomposition.** Wells Fargo writes 17 "lead software engineer" postings in 2026, and more than half mention AI; in 2024 none did. That's pure same-title rewriting.

2. **Breadth drift at pair level SHRINKS 90%+.** T16's company-level breadth rise was +0.40 pooled. Pair-level is +0.08 pooled and −0.02 arshkon. **The scope-inflation story is cross-title within-company, not same-title.** Companies inflate scope by retitling / adding senior titles / adding new archetypes, not by making existing same-title roles broader. This is a refinement of T28's "scope inflation is within-domain" — within-domain, but across titles. A paper-level refinement.

3. **Same-title mgmt rises +12-16 pp** on the V1-validated pattern. Contradicts T11's "mgmt fell" and corroborates T21's rebuilt-pattern finding that management is at worst flat. At pair level, management signals (mentor, direct reports, headcount) actually RISE. The T11 "mgmt density fell" was a precision-0.28 pattern artifact.

4. **YOE at same-title pairs DROPPED.** Mean yoe_rule −1.0 yr (pooled), yoe_llm −0.16 yr. Not huge, but direction-clean. Companies are asking for LESS experience for the SAME role in 2026 vs 2024. Aligned with T08's aggregate J3 share rise — entry-ification is visible at pair level.

5. **`systems/agent/workflows` archetype (T09 cluster 25) shows breadth, mgmt, AND AI all climbing**, whereas `models/systems/llm` (cluster 1) shows AI climbing but breadth shrinking. Within AI-family postings, different flavors behave differently — the AI-agent / MLOps archetype is expanding scope, while the ML/LLM archetype is narrowing to focus.

6. **Aggregators absorb LESS AI language than direct employers** (pair-level). Removing aggregators RAISES AI-drift from +13.4 to +16.5 pp. Direct employers lead the same-title rewrites; aggregators preserve old templates. This is the same pattern T22 found for ghost-likeness (aggregators cleaner than direct on scope inflation) but OPPOSITE pattern for AI (direct cleaner than aggregators on AI infusion). Two different axes.

7. **Microsoft "software engineer ii" is the single most-templated AI rewrite** (n=35 postings in 2026 with 40% AI-strict). Representative read shows Copilot / GenAI / AI Systems / Cloud & AI org labels. Microsoft re-scripted its mid-level SWE into an AI role at scale.

## Headline claims for SYNTHESIS

1. **Same-company × same-title AI drift (+13.4 pp) EXCEEDS company-level AI drift (+7.7-8.3 pp)** — evidence: `tables/T31/drift_distribution.csv` + `tables/T31/consistency_vs_t16.csv`; sensitivity verdict: **robust** (consistent across arshkon_min3 and pooled_min5 panels, aggregator-exclusion tightens to +16.5 pp, cap10 moves ≤0.01). **Within-firm AI rewriting is a same-title phenomenon, not a title-recomposition phenomenon.**

2. **Same-company × same-title breadth_resid drift SHRINKS 90%+ vs T16 company-level** — evidence: `tables/T31/drift_distribution.csv` (pair +0.08 pooled) + `tables/T31/consistency_vs_t16.csv` (T16 co-level +0.40 on same 18 cos); sensitivity verdict: **robust** (direction consistent across panels; aggregator exclusion −0.03; cap10 +0.03). **Scope inflation is cross-title within-company, NOT within-title.** This refines Gate 2's "scope inflation universal, senior > junior" into: scope inflation operates via re-titling / adding senior titles / adding new archetypes — not by making same-title roles broader.

3. **V1-validated management language rises +12-16 pp at pair level** — evidence: `tables/T31/drift_distribution.csv` (mgmt_strict_v1 Δ +0.122 arshkon / +0.160 pooled); sensitivity verdict: **partial** — n=23-33 small; robust to aggregator exclusion (larger +0.23 without agg); direction-consistent with T21 pair-level management rebuild. **Corroborates T21: the V1-validated management pattern, applied at pair level, gives a MILD RISE — definitively rejecting T11's precision-0.28 "management fell" finding.**
