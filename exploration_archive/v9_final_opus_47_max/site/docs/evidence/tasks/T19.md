## Headline

The 2024→2026 **acceleration ratio is clearly above 1 only for AI-mention (2.80×) and scope prevalence (1.54×)**. Every seniority-share metric and description length have acceleration ratios **below 1** — within-2024 source-to-source variation exceeds the cross-period trend when both are annualized. This is consistent with Gate 1/2 findings: the AI-content signal is robustly cross-period; seniority-share direction is robust but magnitude sits near the within-2024 noise floor under annualized comparison.

**Data window facts:**
- arshkon: 2024-04-05 to 2024-04-20 (15 days of activity; midpoint 2024-04-12, n=4,691)
- asaniczka: 2024-01-12 to 2024-01-17 (~6 days; midpoint 2024-01-14, n=18,129)
- scraped 2026-03: 2026-03-20 to 2026-03-31 (n=19,740)
- scraped 2026-04: 2026-04-01 to 2026-04-18 (n=25,577)

arshkon and asaniczka midpoints are ~89 days apart; the scraped window is ~29 days wide; arshkon→scraped-pooled midpoint distance is ~791 days (2.17 years).

**Interpretation:**

- **Acceleration ratio > 2 for AI-mention** is the strong rate-of-change signal. Under an honest annualized framing, the AI content rise is the only cross-period movement that clearly accelerated above within-2024 variability. This is consistent with T08 / T12 / T23 where AI-strict carries the strongest SNR across the exploration.
- **Scope at 1.54×** is borderline-above-noise — Gate 2's universal scope-broadening is confirmed as a cross-period trend, though with modest acceleration.
- **Seniority shares and length** show acceleration ratios < 1 because the within-2024 arshkon-vs-asaniczka gap is *very* large on annualized comparison: the two snapshots are only ~89 days apart, so any small raw difference between them yields a big annualized rate. The cross-period raw deltas (J3 +4.96 pp, S4 −7.49 pp) remain above the within-2024 *raw* delta on the same metric (J3 +4.75 pp, S4 −7.09 pp) — but the annualized framing collapses once the within-2024 window is only 89 days wide. **Practical upshot:** rate-of-change as an analytic framing is not the right lens for seniority claims — the within-2024 gap reflects cross-*instrument* variability, not a real within-period trend. Use raw 2024→2026 deltas (T08, T11, T15, T30 panel) as primary; do not cite annualized seniority rates.

## Timeline context

Our three analytical points:

- **asaniczka (2024-01-14 midpoint).** AI tool releases immediately prior: GPT-4 (Mar 2023), GPT-4 Turbo (Nov 2023). Claude 2 was current; Claude 3 had not released. Copilot was available but GA less than 18 months.

- **scraped pooled midpoint (2026-04-05).** Post-Claude 4 Opus (Sep 2025), post-Gemini 2.5 Pro (Mar 2026). ~24 months of major frontier-model releases between the arshkon midpoint and the scraped midpoint.

We cannot attribute observed posting changes to any single release; the window encompasses the entire "agent-era" arrival and broad enterprise adoption of frontier assistants. ## Surprises

1. **Scraped first-day content profile is nearly identical to subsequent days**, despite 1.66× volume. The "first day is an accumulated backlog" concern does not translate into content distortion. Reassuring for using the first-day data in analyses.

2. **Acceleration ratios < 1 for seniority metrics do NOT mean seniority is unchanged 2024→2026.** They mean the within-2024 arshkon-vs-asaniczka gap is large relative to the cross-period trend when both are annualized. This is a methodological artifact of the ~89-day within-2024 window being very short. The raw cross-period deltas remain the primary reference (T30 panel); this report flags annualized rates as an inappropriate frame for seniority.

3. **Within-arshkon daily stability is reassuring.** Some prior exploration runs have worried that arshkon might carry internal drift across its 15-day window. The tercile and daily tables show ai_strict varying 1.5-2.7% across days — well within sampling noise. arshkon is a defensible single-point 2024 baseline.

4. **No day-of-week effect on the scraped window.** Thursday is slightly higher on ai_strict (16.6%) and length (4,912) but not enough to worry about weekday-skew in the 30-day window.

5. **Posting-age data is effectively missing** (0.9% coverage, all at 1 day). The original hope of characterizing posting lifecycle from our scrape is not feasible with current coverage. Consider it out of scope for the current paper.

## Data-quality caveats

- **Cross-period annualized rates are not comparable to within-2024 annualized rates in magnitude** when the within-2024 window is ~3 months. Use raw pp-change, not annualized.
- **Asaniczka is 6 days wide**; arshkon 15 days. Neither offers a meaningful intra-source temporal window for 2024 stability beyond the daily variation they already show.
- **`date_posted` on scraped LinkedIn is 2.8% populated** (per T01); we rely on `scrape_date` for temporal framing of 2026 data. Indeed has `date_posted` but Indeed is out of primary scope.
- **The "~791 days" cross-period distance** is measured midpoint-to-midpoint. This is the right denominator for rate-of-change, not start-to-end.
