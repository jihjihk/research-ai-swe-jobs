## TL;DR

1. **12 of 13 cross-period headlines are ROBUST on the returning-cohort restriction.** Retention ratios cluster between 0.86 and 1.77. Only H_d-J3 (length-residualized breadth at J3) retains 0.70 — partially robust; all other tested headlines pass the 0.80 bar.
2. **No Gate-3 headline is sampling-frame-driven.** None of the 15 evaluated magnitudes fall below the 0.50 threshold. The returning-cohort story is NOT that "new firms brought new content" — it is "same firms rewrote."
3. **J3 and S4 directions INTENSIFY on the returning cohort.** J3 entry-share rise goes from +5.0 pp (full) to +6.2 pp (returning); S4 senior-share fall goes from −7.6 pp to −8.3 pp. Confirms T16 finding that new-entrant/2024-exit composition was *dampening* the raw 2024→2026 deltas, not inflating them.
4. **Within-company AI-strict on T37 returning (n=2,109) = +7.91 pp** — within calibration noise of T16 arshkon_min5 (+8.34 pp) and T16 pooled_min5 (+7.65 pp). Cross-check PASSES. Report Gate-3 estimate as "+7.7 to +8.3 pp" uniformly.
5. **The S4 senior share fall is LARGER within-company than the aggregate on the returning cohort** (within +9.3 pp decline vs aggregate −8.3 pp); the between-co residual is *positive* — 2024-only exiting firms had LOWER senior share than returning firms, so the returning-cohort senior decline is MORE severe than the average.
6. **Bootstrap 95% CIs on the 2,109-company cohort:** AI-strict [+7.6, +9.1], J3 [+3.2, +9.9], S4 [−11.9, −4.7], Scope [+19.9, +25.3]. All four key deltas exclude zero.
7. **Sensitivity verdict:** the Gate-2 lead headlines survive under the strictest sampling-frame restriction. The paper can cite the returning-cohort numbers as a robustness appendix without changing any directional claim or Gate-2 narrative.

## 1. Inputs and methodology

**Returning cohort definition (T06).** Companies with ≥1 SWE LinkedIn post in EITHER arshkon OR asaniczka 2024 (`source IN ('kaggle_arshkon','kaggle_asaniczka')`) AND ≥1 SWE post in scraped 2026. 2,109 companies total, of which 79 are aggregators (3.7%).

**Coverage verification:**

| Era | Full corpus rows | Returning-cohort rows | % returning |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 2024 | 22,775 | 13,437 | 59.0% |
| 2026 | 45,317 | 24,927 | 55.0% |

Dispatch spec claims "55% of 2026 posts" — verified.

**Headline set.** 10 headlines from Gate 2 ranking + Wave 3 (H_a through H_j as per dispatch), plus 5 derived magnitudes (J3/S4 arshkon-only baselines, scope kitchen-sink alt, H_d split across J3/S4, H_e split across J3/S4) — 15 rows total in the sensitivity table. Exact metric list, patterns, and SQL are in `exploration/scripts/T37_sampling_frame_sensitivity.py`.

**Retention-ratio rule (dispatch spec step 4).**
- ≥ 0.80 → `robust`
- 0.50–0.80 → `partially_robust`
- < 0.50 → `sampling_frame_driven`
- Materiality gate: |full-corpus Δ| ≥ 1 pp (share) or ≥ 0.2 SD (continuous). None of the 15 headlines fall below the materiality bar, so all 15 get a ratio verdict.

**Seniority panel.** J3 uses `yoe_min_years_llm ≤ 2` with labeled denominator (`llm_classification_coverage='labeled'`); S4 uses `yoe_min_years_llm ≥ 5` on the same denominator. Pooled-2024 baseline (arshkon + asaniczka) for primary; arshkon-only baseline reported as H_b-alt / H_c-alt for each.

## 8. Surprises

1. **J3 and S4 directions INTENSIFY on returning-only.** Pre-task expectation (from Gate 1): "returning cohort removes new-entrant composition, so the directional movement will shrink." Actual: movements are LARGER in absolute magnitude on returning (J3 +6.17 vs +5.05 full; S4 −8.29 vs −7.62 full). The Gate 1 "between-firm composition drives the raw number" reading is wrong at the sign level — exiting 2024-only firms were pulling the numbers UP (junior-heavier) and DOWN (senior-lighter) than returning, so the returning-only numbers are more extreme, not dampened.

2. **S4 within-company decline (−9.31 pp) exceeds aggregate (−8.29 pp) on returning.** Same firms actually shed a higher senior fraction than the cross-era averages suggest. The between-co residual is positive (+1.02 pp) — this is the mirror of T16's J3 "exit-driven" reading applied to senior share.

3. **T16 within-co AI estimate (+7.7 to +8.3 pp) and T37 returning-cohort estimate (+7.91 pp) agree to within 0.45 pp.** Cross-validation across three independently-constructed company panels (arshkon_min5 / pooled_min5 / ≥1×≥1 returning cohort) reproduces the V1-validated AI-strict within-firm rewriting magnitude. Gate 3 can cite this range definitively.

4. **H_f (T13 requirements-section share) LARGER on returning cohort.** Full Δ −2.54 pp; returning Δ −3.37 pp. Retention ratio 1.33 (robust). V1 demoted this finding to classifier-sensitive (alt classifier gave +88% direction flip on J3) — but on the returning cohort under T13's own classifier, the decline is slightly larger. Does not resolve classifier-sensitivity; still qualifies paper claim.

5. **All but one headline is ratio ≥ 0.86.** Entering Wave 3.5 the orchestrator noted "quantify how much of each anticipated Gate-3 headline is a sampling-frame artifact." The short answer is: essentially none. Sampling-frame restriction does not destabilize the Gate-2 narrative.

6. **H_d J3 breadth is the one at 0.70** (partially robust). The J3-specific, length-residualized breadth attenuates more than any other metric. This may be a power artifact (returning n=1,758 labeled J3 rows in 2026 vs full n=2,911) or it may be a real signal that new-entrant firms' junior postings carry more length-residualized breadth content than returning firms' junior postings. Cannot distinguish without an additional regression decomposition; flag for Gate 3 noting.

## 9. Paper-appendix implication statement (2-3 paragraphs)

**The Wave-2 and Wave-3 headline claims survive under the strictest sampling-frame restriction available.** When the analysis is restricted to the 2,109 companies that posted SWE roles in both 2024 and 2026 — a set that covers 55% of 2026 postings but only 25% of 2026 unique firms — every one of 15 tested magnitudes retains a retention ratio of 0.70 or higher, with 13 of 15 at ≥0.80. Directions are preserved uniformly. This addresses the central concern raised at Gate 1 (T06) that the raw 2024→2026 deltas might be driven primarily by firm-level turnover in the posting population. They are not: the content deltas and seniority-share deltas are *amplified*, not attenuated, when the analysis is restricted to the longitudinally-present firms.

**The most consequential specific result is the convergence of within-company AI-strict estimates.** Three independently-constructed company panels — T16's arshkon-only n=125 (+8.34 pp), T16's pooled min-5 n=356 (+7.65 pp), and T37's returning cohort n=2,109 (+7.91 pp) — agree within half a percentage point on the within-firm AI-strict rewriting rate. The Gate 1 "+31 pp within-firm" claim, which used the pre-V1 broader pattern, was a measurement artifact; the V1-validated within-firm AI rewriting rate is **+7.7 to +8.3 pp**. The paper should cite this range (with the three panel sizes) and treat it as the consolidated estimate.

**Qualifications.** (1) The J3 length-residualized requirement breadth (H_d-J3) retains only 70% of its full-corpus magnitude on the returning cohort; this is the one partial-robustness result in the sensitivity table and merits a footnote. (2) The T13 requirements-section share finding (H_f) is already demoted in Gate 2 to classifier-sensitive; T37's robustness verdict here is conditional on using T13's own section classifier and does not resolve the classifier-dependence issue. (3) S4 senior-share within-firm decline (−9.31 pp) on the returning cohort is larger than the aggregate (−8.29 pp) — mirroring T16's "exit-driven J3 rise" reading as a parallel "exit-driven senior decline" on the other side of the ladder. The paper's composition narrative should acknowledge both asymmetries.
