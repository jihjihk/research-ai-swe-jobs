## Headline findings

1. **AI-family crystallizes into a cohesive 2026 ecosystem.** 60 of 135 techs are classified rising under within-2024 calibration (SNR ≥ 2); the AI-family (17 LLM-era techs) shows the steepest rises and the largest phi-coefficient growth 2024 → 2026. `llm` 1.3 % → 13.7 %, `ai_agent` 0.07 % → 10.2 %, `rag` 0.1 % → 5.4 %, `copilot` 0.09 % → 4.1 %. Every AI tech except `langchain↔llamaindex` and `tensorflow↔pytorch` had effectively-zero pairwise phi in 2024.
2. **Ecosystem is *adding* on top, not *substituting*.** AI-strict 2026 postings mention **11.4 mean techs** vs 6.8 for non-AI 2026 (raw count). Length-normalized density 2.79 vs 1.88 techs/1K chars — density gap (48 %) is **smaller** than raw gap (67 %) but still substantial. AI-rich postings are not just longer; they genuinely pack more tech.
3. **Cloud-native + Python is the structural bed the AI stack is attaching to.** Among 2026 AI-strict postings: Python 68 % (vs 46 % non-AI), AWS 44 % (vs 34 %), Kubernetes 27 % (vs 21 %), Docker 25 % (vs 18 %). C++, bash, and Agile are **under-represented** in AI postings (AI postings skew away from low-level systems and ceremony language).
4. **Stack diversity is rising uniformly.** Median techs/posting: all-SWE 4 (2024) → 7 (2026). J3 entry: 4 → 6. S4 senior: 5 → 7. The % of postings mentioning ≥ 10 techs went from 19 % (2024) → 32 % (2026). Scope is not broadening in *one* seniority; it is broadening across the board.
5. **SQL, C#/.NET, Agile, jQuery, SQL Server, PHP are all declining at SNR ≥ 2.** Classic MS/enterprise-web stack is shrinking in the LLM era. This is a crystallizing decline, not calibration noise (their within-2024 arshkon↔asaniczka gap is small).
6. **Structured-vs-extracted validation passes decisively.** Spearman r = 0.967 (n = 53 tech-skill-aligned pairs, p < 1e-30) between asaniczka `skills_raw` and the regex-extracted tech matrix. Structured rates are systematically lower (regex picks up extra mentions in surrounding prose — 8-20 pp higher for generic terms like `agile`, `ci/cd`, `c`, `sql`); ranking is preserved. Neither signal has a recall-only gap large enough to worry about.
7. **Structured seniority gap (asaniczka 2024): aws/cloud/devops skills skew senior; `c++`, `troubleshooting`, `computer science`, `mechanical/electrical/computer engineering` skew entry.** Mentoring +2.9 pp senior-vs-entry (p_fdr < 1e-4). This is a structured-skill baseline for the Wave 3 seniority-boundary tasks (T20, T33).

## 8. Structured vs extracted validation

Aligned on 53 tech-skill pairs with explicit alias mapping (e.g., `aws` → {"aws", "amazon web services"}).

- **Spearman r = 0.967, p < 1e-30.** Rank correlation is extremely tight.
- Regex-extracted rates are systematically higher than structured rates on generic terms: `agile` 33.8 % vs 13.2 %, `ci_cd` 17.8 % vs 6.4 %, `sql` 28.0 % vs 20.2 %, `c` 11.3 % vs 3.2 %, `aws` 26.3 % vs 19.4 %. Regex picks up prose mentions like "agile methodology" or "SQL databases" that the structured `skills_raw` field does not enumerate.
- Direction is preserved — regex over-counts uniformly in *prose-like* categories. It does not introduce ordering errors. The rank signal is preserved for both hypothesis testing and for cross-tech comparisons.

**Conclusion.** Extracted tech matrix is defensible for mention-rate signals. For *counts of distinct core skills*, structured `skills_raw` is a tighter measure on asaniczka; for coverage across all three sources, regex is the only option (asaniczka is the only source with `skills_raw`). `tables/T14/structured_vs_extracted.csv` + `tables/T14/structured_vs_extracted_meta.json`.

AI-family rises are **stable across all three sensitivities** — within ± 1 pp of the baseline — meaning the rise is a broad-based employer-side phenomenon, not concentrated in a few high-volume firms. This is consistent with the T06 Gate-1 finding that AI-mention rise is within-company.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **AI arrives at J3 and S4 simultaneously.** Prior was that LLM-era tools would appear first in senior roles then migrate down. Empirically, `copilot` is actually 0.5 pp *more* present in J3 (4.6 %) than S4 (4.1 %); `ai_agent` is approximately equal at both levels (+8.8 pp J3 vs +10.0 pp S4). This is inconsistent with an RQ2 strict "requirements migrate downward" mechanism for AI-tool language. AI language is appearing as a *vocabulary shift* across the entire SWE frame, not as a migrating requirement.
2. **CI/CD is the single largest S4 riser, +20.6 pp.** Bigger than any AI-family term for senior roles. This is a crystallizing expectation that senior engineers *own* pipeline / deployment, not just write code. It corroborates the structured-skill finding that seniors skew toward orchestration and running systems (§9).
3. **SQL and C# decline is flat across 2024 sources (within-2024 gap < 0.2 pp) but dramatic cross-period.** Most tech rises have within-2024 noise of 1-5 pp; SQL and C# have effectively zero within-2024 noise. Their decline is thus a very high-confidence cross-period signal; they are genuinely disappearing relative to the rest of the stack.
4. **pinecone↔weaviate phi was ZERO in 2024 (-0.0002), 0.71 in 2026.** In 2024 both were rare enough and uncorrelated; in 2026 they are a cohesive "vector DB" cluster. This is the sharpest crystallization signal in the whole matrix and a clean setup for T35's full network analysis.
5. **AI-strict postings are 48 % denser in tech mentions even after length normalization.** The naive reading "AI postings are just longer" is refuted — they genuinely pack more tech. They are *technically-maximal* postings, a different posting archetype.
6. **Structured `skills_raw` under-counts generic terms by 20-30 % relative to regex prose-extraction.** This is the opposite of what we feared (that regex would be loose and over-capture). Ranking correlation 0.97 means neither signal is seriously broken; but for *absolute* "share of postings mentioning X", regex gives the upper bound and structured gives the lower bound. The truth for asaniczka is probably in between.
7. **Senior postings emphasize "mentoring" (+2.9 pp over entry) in asaniczka structured skills, FDR-significant.** The structured-skill signal confirms what all the text-based analyses assume: there is a *people-management* vocabulary at senior that is absent at entry. This is T21 territory.
8. **laravel decline is sharper than expected** — 0.6 % arshkon vs 0.2 % scraped. PHP ecosystem has not just stopped growing, it is disappearing from the labor-market text corpus.
