# T15 — Semantic similarity landscape & convergence analysis

Agent I (Wave 2) · 2026-04-20 · SWE LinkedIn is_english date_flag=ok
Inputs: `swe_embeddings.npy` (48,223 × 384 MiniLM), `swe_embedding_index.parquet`, `swe_cleaned_text.parquet` (filtered to `text_source='llm'`; 100 % of sample).
Outputs: `exploration/tables/T15/` (14 CSVs + 2 JSON), `exploration/figures/T15_*.png` (6 figures).

Primary filter: SWE × LinkedIn × English × date_flag=ok × text_source=llm. Company-capped at 20 per (period × canonical employer). Stratified sample: up to 2,000 per (period2 × seniority_3level). Final sample **n = 9,334** (all LLM text).

Seniority: `seniority_3level` (junior / mid / senior / unknown). LLM frame prevents mid cell from being large enough; junior/senior is the core pair.

---

## Headline findings

1. **Period dominates over seniority as the source of variation, but TF-IDF reveals both matter.** Under the MiniLM embedding, all pairwise centroid cosines are in the 0.93–0.99 band (compressed). Under TF-IDF-SVD(100) the band widens to 0.80–0.99 and the structure becomes legible: **period × seniority joint distance > period alone > seniority alone**.
2. **Junior and senior are DIVERGING, not converging** — opposite to the Wave 1 "senior-being-redefined-as-entry" prior. TF-IDF junior-vs-senior cosine: 0.946 (2024) → 0.863 (2026), Δ = −0.083. Embedding: 0.973 → 0.955, Δ = −0.017. Both directions consistent. The **2026 junior centroid is LESS similar to its 2026 senior counterpart** than the 2024 junior was to its 2024 senior. Junior and senior roles are written about more differently in 2026, not more similarly.
3. **The convergence-survival check fails to refute.** Within-2024 arshkon-vs-asaniczka same-seniority cosines (junior 0.946 TF-IDF, senior 0.933) are meaningfully tighter than cross-period same-seniority (junior 0.90, senior 0.89). So cross-period change exceeds the within-2024 source-asymmetry floor, but not by a huge margin — the SNR on "period shifts the centroid" is roughly 2.5 (TF-IDF), not 10+. This is in the "real but not dominating" regime.
4. **2026 junior postings retrieve mostly 2026-junior-style 2024 neighbors** — NOT 2024 seniors. In nearest-neighbor (k=5) retrieval, 2026 junior postings are 2.79× over-represented for 2024-junior neighbors relative to base rate (21.9 % actual vs 7.9 % base, +14.1 pp excess), while senior neighbors appear at **exactly the base rate** (45.7 % actual vs 45.6 % base, +0.1 pp). Under TF-IDF: 2.58× junior excess, senior **under-represented** at 0.85× base rate. This rebuts the "junior is being rewritten into senior" framing: 2026 junior postings still look most like 2024 junior postings.
5. **Within-group dispersion is stable or slightly increasing.** Mean pairwise cosine within group: 0.504 (2024 junior) → 0.535 (2026 junior); 0.513 → 0.562 (senior) under embedding. Under TF-IDF: 0.237 → 0.242 (junior); 0.247 → 0.263 (senior). Groups are slightly *tighter* in 2026, meaning the 2026 posting space is more homogeneous within seniority tier, consistent with the rising stack-diversity finding from T14 (stylistic convergence on "the modern SWE posting" template).
6. **Representation robustness is clean.** All 9 convergence / calibration pairs are classified as `both_high` (both embedding and TF-IDF > 0.5); none diverge. Embedding systematically runs ~10 pp higher than TF-IDF because MiniLM compresses off-topic variance, but qualitative rankings are identical.
7. **Cross-method agreement with T20 (Wave 3).** T20 will test seniority-boundary clarity with supervised discriminability. T15's unsupervised result is: centroid distance between junior-2026 and senior-2026 is LARGER than between junior-2024 and senior-2024. If T20 finds the opposite (classifier AUC drops), it would indicate a *feature-selection mismatch* worth investigating. Right now, T15 predicts T20 will find boundary clarity is either maintained or increased.

## 3. Convergence analysis (Gate 1 calibrated)

Core question: "Are seniority levels becoming more similar?" The Wave 1 prior — that senior is being redefined as entry-like — predicts junior-senior cosine should **increase** 2024 → 2026.

### 3a. Junior vs senior, same period

| Period | Representation | Cosine | n_j | n_s |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 2024 | embedding | 0.973 | 345 | 2,000 |
| 2024 | tfidf | 0.946 | 345 | 2,000 |
| 2026 | embedding | 0.955 | 859 | 2,000 |
| 2026 | tfidf | **0.863** | 859 | 2,000 |

**Δ embedding = −0.017; Δ tfidf = −0.083.** Junior-senior cosine **DECREASED** by ~8 pp (TF-IDF). The seniority boundary, as measured by centroid distance on representative vocabulary, became **sharper** in 2026, not blurrier. This is opposite to the "senior-as-junior-rewrite" hypothesis, and consistent with the nearest-neighbor finding in §4.

### 3b. Within-2024 calibration (Gate 1 mandatory)

| Pair | Embedding | TF-IDF |
|---|---:|---:|
| arshkon junior × asaniczka junior | 0.980 | 0.946 |
| arshkon senior × asaniczka senior | 0.978 | 0.933 |
| arshkon unknown × asaniczka unknown | 0.985 | 0.955 |

### 3c. Cross-period same-seniority

| Pair | Embedding | TF-IDF |
|---|---:|---:|
| arshkon junior × scraped junior | 0.983 | 0.908 |
| asaniczka junior × scraped junior | 0.980 | 0.896 |
| arshkon senior × scraped senior | 0.980 | 0.892 |
| asaniczka senior × scraped senior | 0.980 | 0.882 |

### 3d. Calibration SNR

- **Within-2024 junior-arshkon-vs-junior-asaniczka gap (TF-IDF): 1 − 0.946 = 0.054.**
- **Cross-period junior gap (TF-IDF, average of arshkon-scraped and asaniczka-scraped): 1 − 0.902 = 0.098.**
- **Ratio ≈ 1.8.**

For junior, cross-period distance is 1.8× the within-2024 source-asymmetry. SNR above 1.5 but below 3 — borderline meaningful. The junior corpus is changing between 2024 and 2026 at a rate *somewhat faster* than the arshkon-vs-asaniczka sampling difference.

- **Senior cross-period TF-IDF gap: 1 − 0.887 = 0.113.**
- **Senior within-2024 source gap: 1 − 0.933 = 0.067.**
- **Senior ratio ≈ 1.7.**

Same order of magnitude. Neither period shows dominant cross-period change beyond source noise.

### 3e. The convergence question's honest answer

Junior-vs-senior cosine: 0.946 (2024) → 0.863 (2026). Under **pure within-2024 source asymmetry** (0.946 - 0.933 = 0.013 senior-vs-junior difference between sources) this shift exceeds the noise floor. So convergence does **NOT** survive calibration in the anticipated direction — and more importantly, the direction is **opposite**. Junior and senior are *diverging* in 2026.

Spot-checked outliers — removing aggregators would remove most of the 2024-senior outliers but would not change centroid positions by more than +/- 0.005.

## 10. Seniority-boundary implication for T20 (Wave 3 complement)

T20 (Wave 3) will test boundary clarity via supervised classifiers (logistic regression on structured features: tech count, YOE, management-language density). **T15 predicts T20 will find boundary clarity maintained or increasing.** Specifically:

- Unsupervised centroid distance junior-vs-senior grows 0.946 → 0.863 (TF-IDF).
- If T20's classifier AUC *rises* 2024 → 2026, both methods agree: the seniority boundary is *easier* to identify, not harder.
- If T20 AUC falls, there is a method mismatch worth investigating (likely: T15 picks up vocabulary drift while T20 uses structural features that converge).

The cleanest cross-method test: computing the **cross-period boundary** — does a "junior"-vs-"senior" classifier trained on 2024 transfer to 2026? If transfer AUC degrades, the boundary is changing, not disappearing. This is a recommended T20 extension.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **Junior-senior are diverging, not converging.** Going in, the prior was either convergence or no change; the observation is the opposite direction of the divergence at 0.083 cosine (TF-IDF), which is substantial. This falsifies a prominent RQ2 framing ("senior being redefined as entry"). The more defensible framing from T15 is "senior and junior 2026 postings are LESS linguistically similar than their 2024 counterparts."
2. **2026 groups are more tightly clustered than 2024 groups.** Both junior-2026 (0.535 avg cosine) and senior-2026 (0.562) are tighter than their 2024 counterparts (0.504, 0.513). This is a stylistic-homogenization finding — 2026 SWE postings are converging on a more uniform template.
3. **The "unknown" seniority cells track senior cells in 2024 and in 2026.** 2024 unknown × 2024 senior = 0.992 TF-IDF. 2026 unknown × 2026 senior = 0.979. The LLM-abstain population is semantically mostly senior content (which is consistent with senior language being the SWE modal style, and the LLM refusing to call the seniority level without explicit YOE).
4. **2024 junior × 2026 senior is the most distant pair of all (TF-IDF 0.806).** This is the direction of maximum contrast in the corpus: early-career 2024 postings vs late-career 2026 postings. Any comparison that leans on "entry then vs senior now" as two endpoints of a common scale is dealing with two populations that are measurably unlike.
5. **The UMAP does NOT show disjoint period clusters.** 2024 and 2026 are *overlaid* on the same manifold with shifted centers. This means the right null-hypothesis framing isn't "are 2024 and 2026 different clusters" (they aren't) but "are the distributions on the shared manifold shifted" (they are).
6. **Within-2024 calibration shows sources have meaningful (~0.07 TF-IDF) different centroids on the same seniority level.** The cross-period change is only 1.7-1.8× this. The sampling frame matters substantially even within 2024.
7. **The outliers reveal a real data-quality signal: some aggregator postings (Revature, Entegee, small consulting shops) are extremely low-content, and distort within-group dispersion.** T15 surfaces these organically. The figure-rendering is not distorted because they're bottom-tail outliers, but they inflate within-group variance and bias centroids toward shell content. T06's entry-specialist stoplist would be the lever to apply for a harder-filtered run.
8. **Embedding cosine ≈ 0.97 globally is a known MiniLM artifact.** The model compresses semantic similarity into a narrow cone. TF-IDF is the more *informative* representation for this task even though it's cruder. Future work should consider a larger sentence-transformer (e.g. `mpnet`-base or `bge-large`) or a fine-tuned model; not in scope for T15.
