## Headline

**Tech co-occurrence networks crystallized 2024 → 2026: modularity +0.029 (0.632 → 0.662), edges +24% (317 → 393), communities 9 → 10, all on the same 135-tech taxonomy.** The shift is not dramatic — 5 of 10 2026 communities are "stable_partial" with Jaccard ≥ 0.57 against their 2024 counterparts — but it is directionally real, seed-robust (modularity SD < 0.002 both periods), and survives aggregator exclusion (+0.022 Δ) and cap-20 (+0.029 Δ).

**The biggest structural change is on the data side, not the AI side.** T14's headline phi-pair crystallizations (pinecone×weaviate 0→0.71, rag×llm 0.20→0.51) produced the coalescence of an LLM/AI cluster in 2024 *already* (pooled arshkon+asaniczka had enough adjacency at phi > 0.15 to form a 16-tech cluster). What crystallized in 2026 was:

1. **AI coding-tools split off** from the LLM-engineering cluster into their own tight 5-tech community (copilot, cursor_tool, chatgpt, claude_tool, codex) — genuine new-in-2026 clustering that didn't exist in 2024 (4 of 5 members had no above-threshold co-occurrence in 2024).
2. **Legacy data-eng fragmented** into a modern data-eng stack (Snowflake, dbt, Airflow, Databricks, BigQuery, Spark, Flink) and a web-backend/PHP cluster (PHP, Laravel, Django, MongoDB, Redis) — a clean two-way split of 2024's composite comm 2.
3. **Python ML stack separated** from Hugging Face into a traditional-ML cluster (pandas, numpy, sklearn, pytorch, tensorflow, keras, jupyter, mlflow, xgboost) — hugging_face migrated to the LLM cluster.

**Key correction vs T14 narrative.** T14 showed specific phi pairs crystallizing (pinecone×weaviate 0→0.71). At the *cluster* level, the pooled 2024 baseline (arshkon+asaniczka) already had a 16-tech LLM/AI cluster with phi > 0.15 connectivity — it did not "form" in 2026. What happened in 2026:

## Surprises

1. **LLM cluster already existed in 2024 pooled** (comm 3, n=16). The narrative that "AI ecosystem formed between 2024 and 2026" is overstated at the cluster level — what formed was the *edge-weighted adjacency* (phi rose from near-zero to 0.5-0.7 on specific pairs), but the tech set was largely already co-occurring above the 0.15 threshold in 2024 because arshkon+asaniczka pooled has ~19K rows. The 2026 change is an absorption of 4 new members (mcp, gemini, chroma, claude_api) plus a *split* into LLM-engineering vs AI dev-tools sub-clusters.

2. **AI dev-tools split off into their own community in 2026.** This is Louvain's finding that copilot+cursor+chatgpt+claude_tool+codex co-occur more tightly with each other than with langchain/rag/pinecone in 2026. Interpretation: "AI tools at the desk" (editors, chatbots) now live in postings separately from "LLM engineering" (vector DBs, retrieval, prompt engineering, fine-tuning). The developer-productivity use case is semantically distinct from the LLM-systems-engineering use case.

3. **Observability disappeared as a separate cluster in 2026** — Datadog/New Relic/PagerDuty/Grafana/Prometheus/Splunk all joined the devops_platform_stack. Interpretation: observability tools are now mentioned in the same postings as K8s/Terraform/Docker/AWS — platform work has consolidated. This is consistent with T14's CI/CD Δ +20.6pp as the largest S4 riser.

4. **Data-eng cluster fragmented** (Snowflake/dbt/Airflow/Databricks separated from Kafka/MongoDB/PostgreSQL). 2026 postings distinguish "warehouse + analytics stack" from "event + web-backend DB stack" more sharply. 2024 postings mentioned both more interchangeably.

5. **Python ML stack is "stable_partial" not growing**. The pandas/numpy/sklearn/pytorch/tensorflow constellation already existed in 2024 as a clean cluster; it maintained its coherence (Jaccard 0.64) but did NOT coalesce further. The real AI growth lives in the LLM cluster.

6. **Louvain extreme stability (SD < 0.002)** — expected this to be noisier. The underlying phi matrix is dense enough that Louvain's randomness doesn't matter.

7. **Only 2 true "new" or "weakly_mapped" communities** — the fragmentation and merger events (devops+observability, data-eng→2 pieces, ruby separation) dominate over genuine new cluster formation. Most 2026 communities descend from 2024 communities with modification.

## Limitations

1. **Company cap is applied within period_era — not within-source within-period.** A high-posting employer active in both 2024 arshkon and 2026 scraped can contribute up to 50 postings per period. This is the design: we want per-period structure, not per-source.

2. **Phi threshold 0.15 is arbitrary but T14-consistent.** Lowering to 0.10 would add more edges and reduce modularity (more blurred); raising to 0.20 would produce smaller clusters and higher modularity. Direction of Δ (rising modularity 2024→2026) should survive threshold variation because both periods use the same threshold, but magnitude would shift.

3. **AI dev-tools n=5 cluster is tight but small.** Its "40% new-arrivals" classification is sensitive to the threshold rules. Functionally it is a new-in-2026 cluster; formally under the dispatch rules it is "weakly_mapped." Report as "quasi-new."

4. **Modularity Δ of +0.03 is small in absolute terms.** Louvain modularity on realistic networks commonly sits in 0.5-0.8; a 0.03 absolute rise is ~4.6% relative. Directionally clear; substantively moderate. Do not over-state "dramatic crystallization."

5. **T14's specific-pair phi crystallizations** (pinecone×weaviate 0→0.71) are real and V1-verified. The cluster-level finding here adds that these pairs sit inside an already-coherent cluster that has been stable-plus-accretionary rather than newly-formed.
