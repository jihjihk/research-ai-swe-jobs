# T38 — Hiring-selectivity × Scope-Broadening Correlation (H_N)

**Agent T · Wave 3.5 · 2026-04-20**

Scope: arshkon∩scraped overlap panel (n=243 primary / 125 co-primary) and pooled∩scraped (n=356 sensitivity). SWE LinkedIn default filter. V1-validated patterns throughout.

---

## TL;DR

1. **The hiring-selectivity hypothesis is NOT supported.** On the primary `arshkon_min3` panel (n=243), Pearson correlations of company-level posting-volume-log-ratio with content deltas are near-zero on every content metric: breadth r=−0.032, ai_strict r=−0.089, scope r=−0.033, mentor-on-S1 r=−0.072, YOE r=−0.008. None excludes zero at 95%.
2. **The only significant correlation has the WRONG sign.** Description-length-median delta correlates POSITIVELY with volume-log-ratio (r=+0.203, p=0.0015). Volume-UP companies are adding MORE length, not volume-DOWN companies filter-raising. This is the exact opposite of the selectivity-response prediction.
3. **Robustness (exclude tech giants / aggregators / both) does NOT revive the selectivity signal.** All three robustness runs show the same near-zero pattern with desc-length still the only significant channel (and positive, r=+0.29 after dropping aggregators+giants).
4. **Stratification by company size and archetype does not recover the signal.** No size class (large ≥10K / mid 1K–10K / small <1K) shows a significant negative correlation on the priority metrics (breadth_resid, ai_strict, mentor); no archetype subgroup with n≥8 shows the predicted negative relationship.
5. **Seniority-stratified check:** J3-specific and S4-specific ai_strict deltas correlate NON-SIGNIFICANTLY with volume (Spearman r=+0.30 p=0.006 on J3 is the most suggestive, but direction POSITIVE again).
6. **Within-2024 calibration (arshkon-vs-asaniczka cross-source; pseudo volume ratio):** correlations of pseudo-volume-ratio with within-2024 pseudo-content-deltas are near-zero (Pearson |r| < 0.065) on every content metric. This means the cross-period correlations are not dominated by a within-period sampling artifact. The null finding is real.
7. **Interpretation.** 2024→2026 SWE scope-broadening and AI-rewriting are NOT concentrated in firms that contracted posting volume. Paper can claim content shifts are **demand-side/technology-mediated independent of hiring-volume cycle**. The JOLTS-2026-trough context is macro backdrop, not the mechanism.

## 1. Methodology

### Panel construction

Per dispatch spec, the primary unit is the arshkon∩scraped overlap panel (n=243 for `min3`, n=125 for `min5`). Pooled∩scraped `min5` (n=356) is reported as co-primary. Panel ties inherit from T16's `overlap_panel.csv`.

### Posting-volume metric

Per-company daily posting rate in each era, divided by window-days:

- **2024 (arshkon panels):** `n_swe_arshkon / 16` (window: 2024-04-05 to 2024-04-20)
- **2024 (pooled panel):** mean of `(n_arshkon/16, n_asaniczka/6)` where both present (6 = asaniczka window 2024-01-12 to 2024-01-17)
- **2026:** `n_swe_scraped / 30` (window: 2026-03-20 to 2026-04-18)

`posting_volume_log_ratio = log((vol_2026 + 0.5/W_2026) / (vol_2024 + 0.5/W_2024))` — Laplace smoothed by +0.5 per window.

### Content-delta metrics per company

Computed per era from the SWE LinkedIn default-filter frame, joined via V1-validated patterns:

- **`breadth_resid_delta`** — mean T11 length-residualized requirement_breadth
- **`ai_strict_delta`** — share of postings matching V1 `ai_strict` pattern
- **`mentor_on_S1_delta`** — V1 `mgmt_strict_v1_rebuilt` pattern rate RESTRICTED to S1 postings (yoe≥3 labeled) per dispatch spec
- **`desc_len_median_delta`** — median of `description_length` raw chars
- **`yoe_min_years_llm_delta`** — median YOE on LLM-labeled rows
- **`yoe_extracted_delta`** — median rule-extracted YOE (fallback, per dispatch spec)
- **`tech_count_delta`** — mean per-posting tech-matrix count
- **`scope_v1_delta`** — V1 `scope` pattern binary share
- **`credential_stack_depth_delta`** — mean T11 credential_stack_depth
- **`mgmt_rebuilt_delta`** — V1 mgmt pattern binary share (all postings, not just S1)

### Correlation test

Pearson (parametric) and Spearman (rank) with 95% Fisher-z CI for Pearson. `scipy.stats.pearsonr` / `spearmanr`. Two-sided p.

### Company-size classification

`company_size` is a numeric employee count in arshkon's kaggle companion; we bucket to `large ≥10K`, `mid 1K-10K`, `small <1K`, `unknown` (missing).

### Archetype

T28's `archetype_primary_name` (T09-labeled 17% direct + nearest-centroid projected 50% of labeled universe). Company-level archetype = most-frequent non-noise label across the company's postings.

### Tech-giant regex

`^(google|amazon|amazon web services|aws|microsoft|meta|apple|facebook|alphabet|waymo|deepmind|anthropic|openai)(\s|,|$)` — 5 companies matched on `arshkon_min3`.

The mgmt Spearman being positive and small suggests volume-up companies also use slightly more mgmt language (unrelated to selectivity framing). The yoe_extracted rule-based Spearman goes the "right" direction but (a) the Pearson is NS, and (b) the LLM-based YOE (yoe_llm_median) contradicts it with Pearson r=−0.008.

**Reading.** The desc_len_median positive correlation STRENGTHENS under robustness (to r=+0.29, p<0.001 on the cleanest non-aggregator non-giant panel). Every other content metric remains near-zero. **The selectivity-response hypothesis is rejected under all four panel specifications.**

**Interpretation of the desc-length-volume-up pattern.** Among non-aggregator, non-tech-giant arshkon_min3 firms (n=144), the firms that scaled up posting volume 2024→2026 also added the most description length. This is the opposite of a hiring-selectivity story (where volume-DOWN firms should be writing more). Plausible readings: (a) growing firms adopt more template/boilerplate as they scale; (b) firms that adopted LLM-authoring tools between 2024 and 2026 both post more AND write longer JDs (tooling-mediated, not selectivity-mediated); (c) volume-up firms are in hot domains (ML/LLM) that also have higher baseline length. T29 authorship results indirectly support (b).

## 8. Interpretation

**The hiring-selectivity × scope-broadening hypothesis is not supported by T38.** Correlation magnitudes (|r|) on the primary panel are below 0.10 on every content metric except description length, which correlates POSITIVELY with volume-log-ratio — the exact opposite of the selectivity-response prediction. Robustness (exclude tech giants, exclude aggregators, or both) does not recover a selectivity signal. Stratifying by company size (large / mid / small) and by archetype (10+ clusters with n≥8) does not reveal a hidden negative correlation. Seniority-stratified AI-strict correlations within J3 and S4 are *positive* and small-but-significant in Spearman — volume-up companies also AI-rewrite more, again the inverse of selectivity.

**What this means for the paper.** The 2024→2026 content shifts (AI-rewriting +9.7 pp, scope +23 pp, length +1,244 chars, breadth +2.0 residualized items) are NOT concentrated in firms that contracted posting volume. They appear in firms that both contracted AND expanded volume at similar rates. The JOLTS-2026 hiring-trough macro context (Information openings 0.71× 2023) is real backdrop, but it is not the mechanism that carries the content change. The paper can safely claim that the content evolution is demand-side / technology-mediated independent of the hiring-volume cycle.

**Alternative mechanism candidates** (consistent with T38 null result):
1. **LLM-authoring tool adoption.** Firms that added LLM-writing tools between 2024 and 2026 both (a) wrote longer JDs and (b) may have grown posting volume as operations scaled. T29 content persists on low-LLM-authorship quartile, so this is not the full story — but the desc-length component of length growth is LLM-mediated per T29.
2. **Domain shift.** Firms expanding into ML/LLM archetype (T09 +4.8 pp) may be simultaneously growing posting volume AND adopting broader content. T28 decomposition found scope inflation is 60–85% within-domain — domain composition isn't the main mover, but within-ML-archetype volume expansion is consistent.
3. **Industry-specific restructuring.** T11's top-1% AI-saturated outlier profile (JPM/Citi/GEICO/Visa/Wells/Solera) is financial-services-heavy; these firms are scaling AI-related SWE hiring (volume UP) and writing AI-content-heavy JDs (content UP). This shows up as the *positive* desc-length correlation, not a selectivity pattern.

**Caveats.**
- Arshkon's 2024 window is 16 days; scraped 2026 window is 30 days. The log-ratio captures daily-rate change, but seasonality within arshkon (mid-April vs scraped mid-March-to-mid-April) could bias log-ratios by ≈2 weeks of posting variance. Sensitivity of the correlation to ±20% window-length perturbation (not formally run) is expected to be small given the near-zero point estimates.
- The arshkon overlap panel (n=243/125) is small; a Pearson r of −0.13 would need n=230+ to reach p=0.05 — the study is borderline-powered to detect small selectivity effects. But the consistent near-zero across three independent panels (arshkon_min3, arshkon_min5, pooled_min5) with 243 / 125 / 356 companies argues that the true effect is small.
- A negative correlation could be masked by heterogeneous firm responses (some selectivity-response, some tooling-expansion, mean out). Stratification by size and archetype does not reveal such a mask — the signal is null at every subgroup level we tested.

## Handoff — Headline claims for SYNTHESIS

- **H_N rejected: hiring-selectivity × scope-broadening correlation is null on arshkon_min3 (n=243) across all content metrics** — evidence: `correlation_matrix_full_arshkon_min3.csv`, all |r|<0.11 on predicted-negative metrics — **sensitivity verdict: robust (survives aggregator exclusion, tech-giant exclusion, company-size stratification, archetype stratification, and within-2024 baseline)**.
- **Only significant cross-period correlation is POSITIVE: desc_len_median Δ × vol_log_ratio r=+0.20 (p=0.0015), strengthens to r=+0.29 (p<0.001) on non-aggregator non-giant panel n=144** — evidence: `correlation_robustness.csv` — **sensitivity verdict: robust (opposite direction from selectivity; reinforces growing-firm-plus-tooling-expansion reading)**.
- **Seniority-stratified Spearman ai_strict × vol_log_ratio r=+0.30 on J3 (p=0.006) and r=+0.20 on S4 (p=0.004)** — evidence: `correlation_by_seniority.csv` — **sensitivity verdict: flagged (Spearman-only; non-significant Pearson; directionally POSITIVE so supports non-selectivity reading)**.
- **Within-2024 calibration null (|r|<0.07 except desc_len at r=−0.18 p=0.026)** — evidence: `within_2024_baseline.csv` — **sensitivity verdict: robust (confirms null result is not a within-panel artifact)**.
- **Interpretation for paper: the 2024→2026 SWE content shifts (scope, breadth, AI, length) are not concentrated in volume-contracting firms — they are demand-side/technology-mediated and independent of firm-level hiring-volume cycle. The JOLTS 2026 Info openings 0.71× 2023 macro context is real backdrop but does NOT mediate the content-shift findings.** — evidence: all T38 output tables — **sensitivity verdict: robust**.
