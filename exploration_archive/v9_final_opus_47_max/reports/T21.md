# T21 — Senior role evolution deep dive

**Orientation:** DISCOVERY. How are senior SWE roles (`seniority_final IN ('mid-senior', 'director')`) evolving 2024→2026? Deep look at management, technical orchestration, and strategic language densities; 5-cluster language-profile solution; director-specific profile; AI × senior interaction; cross-seniority management comparison.

**Filters:** SWE LinkedIn (`source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english=true AND date_flag='ok' AND is_swe=true`), `seniority_final IN ('mid-senior','director')`. Text: `swe_cleaned_text.parquet` (68,137 rows, LLM-cleaned where available, raw otherwise).

**Cohort sizes:**

| Period | mid-senior | director | total |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 13,574 | 91 | 13,665 |
| 2026 | 19,703 | 325 | 20,028 |

Director cells are thin but 3.6× larger in 2026 than 2024.

**Patterns.** V1-validated patterns loaded from `validated_mgmt_patterns.json`. This task uses:
- `mgmt_strict_v1_rebuilt`: `\b(?:mentor(?:s|ed|ing)?\s+(?:junior|engineers?|team(?:s)?|others|the team|engineering|peers|sd(?:e|es))|coach(?:es|ed|ing)?\s+(?:team|engineers?|junior|peers)|direct reports?|headcount|hiring manager|hiring decisions?)\b`
- `mgmt_strict` (T11 original, V1 precision 0.55).
- `orch` (T21 spec): architecture/code review, system design, technical direction, AI orchestration, agentic/multi-agent, workflow, pipeline, automation, evaluate, validate, quality gate, guardrails, prompt engineering, tool selection.
- `strat` (T21 spec): stakeholder, business impact, revenue, product strategy, roadmap, prioritization, resource allocation, budget, cross-functional alignment.
- `ai_strict` (V1 precision 0.86).
- `mentor_binary`: mentor or coach requiring an object (junior/engineers/team/peers/etc).

## Headline

**Senior SWE roles are shifting from low-content generic postings to technical-orchestration and AI-oriented profiles. Management language is NOT the primary driver of the shift — orchestration is.** The T11 claim "management density FELL" does not survive: using V1's validated rebuilt management pattern, mid-senior mgmt density is flat (0.039 → 0.038, Δ≈0). What rose is orchestration density (+0.67/1K chars for mid-senior, +0.44 for director) and AI-strict binary (+10.7 pp for mid-senior, +13.7 pp for director). The strategic-language pattern fails semantic precision (0.32 overall on the 50-row sample) because "stakeholders" is a collaboration term, not a strategic responsibility — so strategic-language claims are **provisional/flagged**.

### Leading cluster-level finding

5-cluster k-means on log-density features:

| Cluster | 2024 share | 2026 share | Δ pp |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low-profile generic (all densities ~0) | 62.0% | 38.4% | **−23.6** |
| Tech-orchestration (non-AI) | 14.9% | 26.2% | **+11.4** |
| AI-oriented | 1.1% | 11.2% | **+10.2** |
| Strategic-language | 18.7% | 18.8% | +0.1 (flat) |
| People-management | 3.4% | 5.3% | +1.9 |

**The "low-profile generic" senior posting — short, low on all content signals — shrank by 23.6 pp.** Its volume was redistributed to orchestration-heavy clusters (tech-orch +11.4, AI-oriented +10.2). People-management grew only +1.9 pp. Strategic-language (which fails precision) was flat.

## 1) Pattern validation on fresh 50-row stratified sample

File: `exploration/tables/T21/pattern_validation.csv` + `sample_contexts_{pattern}.csv`.

Method: 50 matches per pattern, 25 from 2024 + 25 from 2026, stratified sampling (RNG seed 21212121). Programmatic adjudication implemented per pattern with FP-catcher rules; manual spot-check of 35 out of 50 mgmt_rebuilt rows confirmed all 35 as genuine TPs.

| Pattern | Precision 2024 | Precision 2026 | Overall | Gate 2 target | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **mgmt_strict_v1_rebuilt** | **1.00** | **1.00** | **1.00** | 0.85 | **PASS** (above V1 target) |
| orch | 0.64 | 0.76 | 0.70 | 0.80 | **FAIL** (borderline; FP dominated by generic "evaluate" and "pipelines" in ambiguous contexts) |
| strat | 0.28 | 0.36 | 0.32 | 0.80 | **FAIL** (FP dominated by "stakeholders" used as a collaboration word — same failure mode V1 identified for mgmt_broad's "stakeholder" subterm at 0.18) |

*V1 prior on mgmt_strict (original): 0.55 overall (hire 0.07, performance_review 0.25 sub-pattern failures). The V1-rebuilt pattern drops `hire` and `performance_review`, and narrows `mentor` to require a junior/engineer/team/peer OBJECT — that narrowing eliminated the "mentorship program" perk-framing false positive. Confirmed on this sample at 1.00 precision.*

**Interpretation for T21 claims.**
- Management density claims using `mgmt_strict_v1_rebuilt` are USABLE as primary.
- Orchestration density claims are USABLE as secondary/diagnostic (0.70 pooled). The `evaluate`+`validate` subterms dominate the FP; the `workflow`, `pipeline`, `agentic`, `prompt engineering`, `system design` subterms are strong. Orchestration claims should be reported with a note that 20-30% of matches may be generic.
- **Strategic-language claims are demoted to DIAGNOSTIC ONLY.** The pattern is dominated by "stakeholders" (32/50 matches) which is overwhelmingly a collaboration word, not a strategic responsibility. Wave 3.5 T22 should consider rebuilding strat with stricter forms (e.g., "engage/manage/align/influence X stakeholder" or drop stakeholder entirely and use "roadmap" + "prioritization" + "business impact").

FP examples dominating low precision:
- strat FP: "communication with stakeholders", "feedback from stakeholders" (stakeholder as audience)
- strat FP: "error budgets" (SRE engineering term, not strategic budget)
- orch FP: "evaluate functionality" in QA context; "evaluate alternatives" in vendor-selection context.

**Pre-commit 10 compliance:** Reporting both V1-rebuilt (1.00) and original T11 mgmt_strict (0.55 per V1) densities in §3. The 0.80 threshold is met only by mgmt_strict_v1_rebuilt.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **The V1-rebuilt management pattern validated at 1.00 precision** on my 50-row stratified sample. That substantially exceeds V1's 0.85 target. The "mentor X" object-requirement construction eliminated the `mentorship program` perk false positive; the `coach X` + `direct reports` + `headcount` + `hiring manager/decisions` subterms are clean. This validates the Gate 2 pre-commit to use mgmt_strict_v1_rebuilt as primary.

2. **Under the validated pattern, the "management density fell" claim from T11 does NOT reproduce — it is flat.** Mid-senior mgmt_rebuilt density: 0.039 (2024) → 0.038 (2026), Δ ≈ 0, within-2024 SNR 0.1. This is a meaningful downgrade of a T11 lead claim. The T11 finding captured `code review` and `performance review`-related noise, not actual management-responsibility signal.

3. **Strategic-language pattern fails precision at 0.32** — stakeholders (32/50 matches in the sample) is almost entirely a collaboration word ("communicate with stakeholders", "stakeholder feedback"), not strategic responsibility. This replicates V1's `stakeholder` sub-term failure in mgmt_broad (0.18). **Wave 3.5 T22 should rebuild strat with stricter object requirements** (e.g., "engage/manage/align X stakeholders") or drop `stakeholders` entirely.

4. **Directors became LESS strategic-language-weighted** (40.7% → 33.2%, −7.5 pp), not more. This is contrary to a prior that would have expected directors to become more strategic-heavy in 2026. Much of the 2024 "strategic" language is driven by "stakeholders" which is collaboration, not strategy — so the apparent decline may partly reflect directors getting MORE specific content (orchestration, AI) pushing out vague stakeholder language.

5. **The "low-profile generic" cluster, which was 62% of senior postings in 2024, dropped to 38% in 2026.** This is the biggest single cluster shift. "Generic" senior postings have been replaced with content-rich (orchestration- and AI-oriented) postings. This aligns with T13's finding that description length grew by 50%+.

6. **AI-oriented senior postings are LESS management-heavy than non-AI senior postings** (mgmt_rebuilt 0.030 vs 0.039 in 2026; mentor_binary 5.2% vs 5.3%). AI-adoption at senior level DOES NOT pull senior roles toward people-management framing. The opposite: AI-senior roles are the most technical-orchestration-heavy subset (orch_density 1.91 vs 0.98).

7. **Mid-senior AI-strict rose 10.7 pp; director AI-strict rose 13.7 pp.** Under the V1-validated AI-strict pattern (0.86 precision), AI-mention rose slightly faster at the director level than the mid-senior level — directors are adopting AI language faster than mid-seniors in absolute terms, though from a lower base.

8. **Cross-seniority management comparison supports the "management is not expanding anywhere" reading.** Under the rebuilt pattern, mgmt_rebuilt_density is flat at senior, flat at mid (associate), and only a trivial +0.004 rise at junior. This means the "field-wide template shift toward management language" hypothesis is NOT supported. The field-wide shift is toward ORCHESTRATION language (junior +0.67, senior +0.67, unknown +0.72 per 1K chars).

9. **Tech-orchestration (non-AI) cluster nearly DOUBLED** (14.9% → 26.2%). Even excluding AI-mentioning postings, senior roles became markedly more orchestration-language-dense. This is a content change beyond AI adoption — CI/CD, pipelines, workflows, system design as an intrinsic shift in what senior work looks like. Aligns with Gate 2's "CI/CD as senior riser +20.6 pp" finding.

10. **Cluster silhouette (0.477) is not perfect but indicates real separation.** The clustering is legible: 4 of 5 clusters have a dominant dimension (orch-heavy non-AI, orch-heavy AI, management-heavy, strategic-heavy). Only "low-profile generic" is a "rest" cluster. This is well-behaved for a 5-cluster solution in a high-dim language space.
