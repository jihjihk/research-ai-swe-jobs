## Executive Summary

Comprehensive distribution profiling across all key variables reveals three headline findings:

1. **The YOE paradox is RESOLVED: it reflects genuine slot elimination, not a measurement artifact.** Entry-level median YOE dropped from 3.0 (arshkon 2024) to 2.0 (scraped 2026), driven by the near-elimination of high-YOE "entry-level" postings (5+ YOE entry share fell from 22.8% to 2.4%). This holds within titles, within seniority provenance, after company capping, and with aggregators excluded. The interpretation: companies that formerly labeled 3-5+ YOE roles as "entry-level" have either eliminated those slots or reclassified them upward, leaving only genuinely junior (0-2 YOE) positions in the entry tier.

2. **AI/ML technology adoption shows massive, calibration-validated increases.** Agent frameworks (0.1% to 10.8%), LLMs (1.8% to 14.4%), and Claude/Copilot mentions all exploded. These changes dwarf within-2024 instrument noise by 5-17x, confirming genuine market shifts.

3. **Description length grew 57% (core: 52%) for mid-senior roles**, the single largest structural effect (Cohen's d = 0.77). This length inflation means ALL text-derived metrics must be length-normalized.

## 3. YOE Paradox Deep Dive (HIGHEST PRIORITY)

### 3.1 The Paradox Stated

Wave 1 found that entry-level median YOE **decreased** from 3.0 (arshkon 2024) to 2.0 (scraped 2026). This appears to contradict the "scope inflation" hypothesis -- if employers are asking more of junior roles, why would YOE requirements go DOWN?

### 3.2 Resolution: Slot Elimination + Genuine Entry Residual

**Finding: The YOE decrease is real and reflects the selective elimination of high-YOE "entry-level" slots.**

Evidence from five independent decompositions:

#### A. Binned YOE distribution (slot elimination)

| YOE bin | 2024-04 share | 2026-03 share | Change |
|---------|-------------:|-------------:|-------:|
| Missing | 42.7% | 67.3% | +24.6pp |
| 1-2 | 17.7% | 27.2% | +9.5pp |
| 3-4 | **16.9%** | **3.2%** | **-13.7pp** |
| 5+ | **22.8%** | **2.4%** | **-20.4pp** |

The 5+ YOE category collapsed from 22.8% to 2.4% of entry-level postings. The 3-4 YOE category collapsed similarly. What remains is the 1-2 YOE tier, which actually grew in share.

**Ghost job risk confirms this:** In arshkon, 22.8% of entry-level posts were flagged as "high" ghost risk (entry label + 5+ YOE) vs only 2.4% in scraped. The pipeline's ghost detector essentially identifies the same phenomenon.

#### B. Within-title analysis

Of 14 matched titles (>=3 observations in both periods), **11 show YOE decrease or stability, 2 are unchanged, and 1 shows increase**:

| Title | Med YOE 2024 | Med YOE 2026 | Change |
|-------|-------------:|-------------:|-------:|
| full stack developer | 6.0 | 2.0 | **-4.0** |
| servicenow developer | 6.0 | 2.0 | **-4.0** |
| java developer | 5.0 | 2.0 | **-3.0** |
| quality assurance engineer | 3.0 | 1.0 | **-2.0** |
| software developer | 3.0 | 2.0 | -1.0 |
| data engineer | 3.0 | 2.0 | -1.0 |
| cloud engineer | 3.0 | 2.0 | -1.0 |
| systems engineer | 3.0 | 2.0 | -1.0 |
| software engineer | 2.0 | 2.0 | 0.0 |
| product engineer | 2.0 | 2.0 | 0.0 |

The decrease is within-title, not compositional. Titles that formerly asked for 5-6 YOE at "entry-level" now ask for 2 YOE.

#### C. Seniority provenance decomposition

Even restricting to the same provenance pathway:

| Provenance | Med YOE 2024 | Med YOE 2026 | Change |
|------------|-------------:|-------------:|-------:|
| native_backfill | 3.0 | 2.0 | -1.0 |
| title_keyword | 2.0 | 2.0 | 0.0 |

The native_backfill pathway (platform-assigned "entry" labels) shows the same decrease. Title_keyword (imputed from title text) was already at 2.0 in both periods.

#### D. Aggregator decomposition

| Segment | Med YOE 2024 | Med YOE 2026 | Change |
|---------|-------------:|-------------:|-------:|
| Direct employers | 3.0 | 2.0 | -1.0 |
| Aggregators | 5.0 | 2.0 | **-3.0** |

Aggregators show the largest YOE drop, but direct employers show it too. This is not an aggregator-specific artifact.

#### E. Company capping sensitivity

| Company cap | Med YOE 2024 | Med YOE 2026 |
|------------:|-------------:|-------------:|
| 3 per company | 3.0 | 2.0 |
| 5 per company | 3.0 | 2.0 |
| 10 per company | 3.0 | 2.0 |
| 50 per company | 3.0 | 2.0 |
| No cap | 3.0 | 2.0 |

The YOE decrease is completely invariant to company capping.

#### F. Top employers composition

Arshkon 2024-04 top entry-level employers were staffing agencies posting inflated requirements:
- Tata Consultancy Services (n=30, med YOE=8.0)
- Open Systems Technologies (n=26, med YOE=5.0)
- TEKsystems (n=22, med YOE=5.0)

Scraped 2026-03 top entry-level employers are direct employers with genuine entry requirements:
- Jobs via Dice (n=213, med YOE=2.0)
- Epic (n=163, med YOE=NaN -- no YOE listed)
- TikTok (n=93, med YOE=2.0)
- Cisco (n=51, med YOE=1.0)
- SpaceX (n=37, med YOE=2.0)

### 3.3 Statistical Test

Mann-Whitney U test for entry-level YOE, 2024-04 vs 2026-03:
- U = 399,360, p = 2.94e-81
- Cohen's d = 0.94 (large effect)
- Mean: 4.04 -> 2.17
- Median: 3.0 -> 2.0

### 3.4 YOE Paradox: Interpretation

The YOE paradox is NOT a contradiction of scope inflation. It is evidence of a complementary phenomenon: **slot purification**. The story is:

1. In 2024, "entry-level" was a broad label encompassing both genuinely junior roles (0-2 YOE) AND experienced roles mislabeled as entry (3-8 YOE). Many of the latter were from staffing agencies.
2. By 2026, the high-YOE "entry-level" slots were eliminated -- either reclassified to appropriate seniority levels, or the roles themselves were cut.
3. What remains in the entry tier are genuinely junior positions with YOE requirements matching their seniority label.

This is **consistent** with scope inflation if we distinguish between:
- **Vertical inflation** (junior roles demanding more): not supported by YOE data
- **Horizontal elimination** (junior slots disappearing entirely): supported by junior share decline (-5.9pp)
- **Label purification** (entry tier becoming more accurately labeled): supported by YOE data

The 3+ YOE "entry-level" roles that disappeared were likely never genuinely junior. Their elimination from the entry tier reduces noise and makes the remaining junior-senior comparison cleaner.

### 3.5 YOE Coverage Caveat

YOE extraction coverage is lower for entry-level in 2026 (32.7%) than 2024 (57.3%). This is partially because more 2026 entry posts omit explicit YOE requirements entirely (especially from companies like Epic where 163 entry posts have no extracted YOE). This is consistent with genuinely entry-level roles not bothering to state a YOE floor.

## 7. Surprises & Unexpected Patterns

### S1: The YOE paradox is a FEATURE, not a bug
The most surprising finding is that the YOE decrease is not an artifact but a genuine market signal. The 2024 "entry-level" tier was contaminated with experienced-hire roles mislabeled as entry. By 2026, these have been purified out. This has a major methodological implication: the 2024 entry-level YOE distribution was bimodal (genuine juniors + mislabeled seniors), making any 2024 entry-level text analysis contaminated by senior-level content.

### S2: Aggregator share at mid-level quadrupled
Mid-level aggregator share went from 3.1% to 12.7% (Cohen's h = 0.38). This is the 7th largest effect size. Staffing agencies appear to be shifting their SWE postings from entry-level to mid-level roles.

### S3: Python became a majority requirement
Python prevalence in mid-senior SWE postings went from 33.9% to 49.6%. It is now mentioned in essentially half of all senior SWE job postings, up from one-third just two years ago. This is the largest single general-purpose technology shift.

### S4: The new AI technology stack appeared from near-zero
Agent frameworks (0.1% to 10.8%), RAG (0.5% to 5.3%), LangChain (0.2% to 3.4%), and prompt engineering (0.3% to 3.7%) all emerged from essentially zero prevalence. These represent genuinely new requirements that did not exist in the 2024 labor market.

### S5: Agile practice mentions declined
Agile/Scrum mentions went from 33.0% to 28.4%. This is unexpected -- agile methodologies are if anything more prevalent in industry. This may reflect a shift in what employers emphasize in job postings rather than a decline in practice.

### S6: C/C++ nearly disappeared from mid-senior SWE
C/C++ went from 1.2% to 0.08%, a near-complete elimination. This is surprising even if it was already rare -- the remaining roles presumably represent embedded/systems work that shifted to Rust (which grew from 1.2% to 4.2%).

### S7: YOE extraction coverage is an informative signal
The lower YOE extraction rate for entry-level in 2026 (32.7% vs 57.3%) is not missing data -- it likely reflects that genuinely entry-level roles do not specify YOE requirements, while the inflated "entry" roles of 2024 did specify (5+ YOE). The missingness itself is evidence of slot purification.

## 8. Methodological Implications for Downstream Tasks

1. **Length normalization is mandatory.** The 55-77% increase in description length (d=0.77) means all text-derived metrics (keyword counts, topic prevalence, etc.) must be normalized per 1K characters at minimum.

2. **The 2024 entry tier is contaminated.** Approximately 23% of arshkon entry-level postings had 5+ YOE requirements. Any analysis of "entry-level requirements" in 2024 should either (a) filter to YOE <= 2 or (b) treat the bimodal distribution as a known confounder.

3. **Aggregator sensitivity is critical for entry-level.** The entry-level junior share decline shrinks from -5.9pp to -3.1pp when aggregators are excluded. Report both.

4. **Within-2024 calibration identifies genuine signals.** Technologies with ratio > 3 (python, AI/ML, agent frameworks, terraform, kubernetes) are genuine changes. Technologies with ratio < 2 (dotnet, CICD) are within the noise floor.

5. **YOE paradox is consistent with, not contradictory to, RQ1.** The correct framing: employers reduced the number of junior slots (share decline) AND the remaining junior slots have more accurate labels (YOE decrease). This is structural reorganization of the entry tier, not scope inflation within it.
