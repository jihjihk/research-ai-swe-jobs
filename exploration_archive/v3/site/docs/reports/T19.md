# T19: Temporal Patterns & Rate-of-Change Estimation

**Task:** Characterize temporal structure across three discrete snapshots and estimate rates of change, including acceleration.

**Snapshots:** Asaniczka (Jan 2024), Arshkon (Apr 2024), Scraped (Mar 2026).

**Bottom line: GenAI prevalence accelerated dramatically (8x), description length grew at 2.5x the within-2024 rate, while entry-level share dynamics are dominated by cross-source measurement differences. Within-March metrics are stable (CV < 10% for most measures), supporting the scraped data as a reliable snapshot. Scraper yield shows moderate first-day backlog (1.6x) with clear weekend dips.**

---

## 1. Rate-of-Change Estimation (SWE)

### 1.1 Snapshot Values

| Metric | Jan 2024 | Apr 2024 | Mar 2026 |
|--------|----------|----------|----------|
| Entry share (known seniority) | 0.56% | 20.4% | 14.5% |
| AI prevalence (broad) | 14.8% | 12.3% | 39.1% |
| GenAI prevalence | 2.5% | 2.8% | 23.2% |
| Description length (median core) | 2,419 | 2,504 | 4,171 |
| Tech mention count (mean) | 4.62 | 4.68 | 5.50 |
| Scope density (collab/stakeholder) | 27.3% | 29.1% | 45.1% |

### 1.2 Annualized Rates and Acceleration

| Metric | Within-2024 (annualized) | Cross-period (annualized) | Acceleration ratio |
|--------|:------------------------:|:-------------------------:|:------------------:|
| Entry share | +79.4pp/yr* | -3.0pp/yr | -0.04 |
| AI prevalence | -9.7pp/yr | +13.4pp/yr | -1.37** |
| GenAI prevalence | +1.2pp/yr | +10.2pp/yr | **8.25** |
| Description length | +340 chars/yr | +834 chars/yr | **2.45** |
| Tech mention count | +0.27/yr | +0.41/yr | **1.53** |
| Scope density | +7.5pp/yr | +8.0pp/yr | 1.06 |

\* Entry share within-2024 rate is meaningless -- the Jan 2024 value (0.56%) reflects asaniczka's lack of native entry-level labels, not actual entry-level prevalence.

\** AI prevalence shows a negative within-2024 rate because asaniczka (14.8%) > arshkon (12.3%). This is likely a source composition artifact rather than a real decrease.

### 1.3 Interpretation

**Genuine acceleration signals:**
1. **GenAI prevalence: 8.25x acceleration.** Within-2024, GenAI was growing at +1.2pp/year. Cross-period, it grew at +10.2pp/year. This is the single strongest acceleration signal and aligns with the release of GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, o1, and subsequent models in the intervening period.

2. **Description length: 2.45x acceleration.** Descriptions grew faster in the 2024-2026 period than within 2024. This could reflect genuine content expansion or evolving platform norms.

3. **Tech mention count: 1.53x acceleration.** Moderate acceleration in the number of technologies mentioned per posting.

**Stable rate (no acceleration):**
- **Scope density: 1.06x.** Cross-functional/stakeholder language grew at essentially the same rate within 2024 and cross-period. This suggests scope inflation was already underway in early 2024.

**Unreliable rates (cross-source artifacts):**
- **Entry share** -- confounded by asaniczka's missing entry-level labels.
- **Broad AI prevalence** -- the within-2024 dip is likely a source artifact.

| Metric | CV |

The within-2024 period (Jan to Apr) spans before the major 2024 release wave (GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, o1). The low within-2024 GenAI growth rate (+1.2pp/yr) vs the high cross-period rate (+10.2pp/yr) is temporally consistent with the model release timeline.

## 6. Key Caveats

1. **Three-point estimation is fragile.** With only three snapshots, within-2024 rates are estimated from a single 3-month difference (Jan-Apr 2024). Small source composition differences can dominate.

2. **Cross-source confounds for entry share.** Asaniczka (Jan 2024) has zero native entry-level labels. Entry share comparisons between asaniczka and arshkon are meaningless. Only arshkon-to-scraped is valid for entry share trends.

3. **Within-2024 AI prevalence dip.** Asaniczka shows higher AI prevalence (14.8%) than arshkon (12.3%). This is likely a source composition effect rather than a genuine 3-month decline. Different search queries, sample frames, and LinkedIn API access could explain this.

4. **Acceleration ratios with small denominators.** When the within-2024 rate is near zero or negative, the acceleration ratio is undefined or misleading. Interpret with caution.

5. **First-day scraper backlog.** The first scrape day (Mar 20) has 1.6x normal volume. This is mild and unlikely to bias metrics, but worth noting for volume-based analyses.
