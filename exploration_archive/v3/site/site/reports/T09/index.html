    T09: Posting Archetype Discovery -- Methods Laboratory

    Generated: 2026-04-05

    Executive Summary

    Unsupervised clustering of 8,002 SWE LinkedIn job postings reveals that the market's dominant structure is organized by technical specialization, not by seniority level. BERTopic identified 92 fine-grained topics (reduced to 14 interpretable archetypes), while NMF produced consistent macro-level groupings. Key findings:

    1. Technology domain drives posting structure. Clusters align most strongly with tech domain (NMI=0.175), followed by period (NMI=0.057), while seniority shows the weakest alignment (NMI=0.018). A senior data engineer posting is more similar to a junior data engineer posting than to a senior frontend posting. Seniority operates as a within-domain modifier, not a cross-cutting structural dimension.

    2. Four macro-archetypes dominate the market. Frontend/Web (28%), Embedded/Systems (21%), Data/Analytics (19%), and ML/AI Engineering (13%) account for 81% of non-noise postings. The remaining 10 micro-clusters represent company-specific, aggregator, or niche patterns.

    3. ML/AI Engineering underwent massive growth. It rose from 4% of SWE postings in 2024 to 27% in 2026 (+22pp). Frontend/Web shrank from 41% to 24% (-17pp). This is the largest temporal shift in the data.

    4. Both methods agree on core structure. 7 of 14 BERTopic archetypes have clear NMF counterparts (Jaccard > 0.1). Clustering is highly stable (ARI = 1.00 for HDBSCAN, 0.996 for NMF).

    Notable patterns in the archetypes:

    • The four major archetypes (Frontend/Web, Embedded/Systems, Data/Analytics, ML/AI) each have very similar seniority distributions (78-84% senior, 8-10% junior). Seniority does not differentiate archetypes -- tech domain does.

    • Several small clusters are company-specific (PwC, Anduril) or format-specific (aggregator boilerplate, compensation sections), reflecting posting style rather than role content.

    4. Method Comparison

    Stability

    Method Mean ARI (3 seeds)
    BERTopic (HDBSCAN on UMAP) 1.0000
    NMF k=15 0.9961

    Both methods are highly stable. BERTopic achieves perfect reproducibility because HDBSCAN is deterministic given fixed UMAP embeddings (which use a fixed seed).

    Topic alignment (BERTopic reduced vs NMF k=15)

    Method-robust topics (Jaccard > 0.1 on top-20 terms): 7 out of 14

    • BT "Frontend/Web Development" <-> NMF-3 (web, developer, javascript) -- Jaccard=0.111
    • BT "Embedded/Systems" <-> NMF-7 (clearance, systems, embedded) -- Jaccard=0.154
    • BT "Data/Analytics" <-> NMF-4 (infrastructure, cloud, devops) -- Jaccard=0.154
    • BT "ML/AI Engineering" <-> NMF-12 (product, building, backend) -- Jaccard=0.200
    • BT "PwC/Consulting" <-> NMF-5 (ai, ml, machine learning) -- Jaccard=0.111
    • BT "Server Infrastructure" <-> NMF-9 (server infrastructure) -- Jaccard=0.429
    • BT "Streaming/Distributed" <-> NMF-10 (streaming data, cassandra) -- Jaccard=0.154

    The Jaccard scores are moderate (0.1-0.4), which is expected given that BERTopic uses semantic embeddings while NMF uses bag-of-words. The methods agree on the existence of distinct data, embedded, web, and infrastructure clusters, but describe them with partially different vocabularies.

    Key temporal patterns

    The most dramatic shift: ML/AI Engineering grew from a 4% niche to a 27% major archetype. This single shift represents the largest structural change in the SWE posting landscape.

    Declining archetypes: Frontend/Web Development (-17pp) and Embedded/Systems (-7pp) experienced significant relative decline. Note these are relative shares of the SWE posting market; absolute counts depend on total posting volume changes.

    Stable archetype: Data/Analytics held steady at ~21%, suggesting its prominence is durable across the 2024-2026 period.

    Caveat: Some temporal variation may reflect text quality differences between Kaggle data (LLM-cleaned where available, ~45%) and scraped data (rule-based text only). The text source sensitivity check (cosine similarity 0.88 between LLM and rule-based cluster distributions) suggests moderate robustness, but cannot fully rule out this confound.

    6. Sensitivity Analysis

    (a) Aggregator exclusion

    Aggregator-heavy archetypes (>20% aggregator content): - Data/Analytics: 33% aggregator - Mobile Development: 25% aggregator - Benefits-Heavy Postings: 27% aggregator - Aggregator/AI Roles: 100% aggregator - Streaming/Distributed: 73% aggregator - Chatbot/Web Dev: 69% aggregator - Certified/Tax Tech: 55% aggregator

    Topic distribution cosine similarity (non-aggregator vs aggregator): 0.84

    Several small clusters (6, 10, 12, 13) are aggregator-dominated and should be interpreted as data artifacts rather than true market segments. The four major archetypes (Frontend/Web, Embedded/Systems, Data/Analytics, ML/AI) have aggregator rates of 0-33%, which is manageable but suggests Data/Analytics results should be interpreted with caution.

    (d) Text source sensitivity

    • LLM-cleaned text: 3,718 rows (46.5%)
    • Rule-based text: 4,284 rows (53.5%)
    • Cluster distribution cosine similarity (LLM vs Rule text): 0.88

    The moderate cosine similarity (0.88) indicates that text cleaning method affects cluster assignments but does not fundamentally alter the structure. The four major archetypes are robust across text sources.

    (g) SWE classification tier

    SWE tier distribution in sample: regex: 7281, title_lookup_llm: 589, embedding_high: 132

    The sample is dominated by regex-tier classifications (91%), which are the highest-confidence SWE identifications. The 7% from title_lookup_llm and 2% from embedding_high contribute marginal uncertainty.

    Interpretation

    The 14-archetype clustering reveals a clear hierarchy of what structures the SWE labor market:

    1. Technology domain is the strongest signal (NMI=0.175). Even using a crude 5-category tech domain classification (AI, cloud, data, mobile, security, general), tech domain explains the clusters far better than any other dimension. The BERTopic archetypes themselves are tech-domain clusters: data engineering, embedded/systems, frontend/web, ML/AI, mobile. This is the central structural finding.

    2. Period ranks second (NMI=0.057). Posting language changed substantially between 2024 and 2026. The ML/AI Engineering archetype grew from 4% to 27%, while Frontend/Web shrank from 41% to 24%. Some of this reflects genuine market shifts; some may reflect source-specific text patterns.

    3. Aggregator status ranks third (NMI=0.047). Several small archetypes are aggregator-dominated (Aggregator/AI: 100%, Streaming/Distributed: 73%). This is mostly a data quality signal -- aggregator boilerplate creates distinctive text patterns that form their own clusters.

    4. Seniority is the weakest signal (NMI=0.018). This is the most important negative finding: job postings do NOT cluster by seniority level. Every major archetype spans all seniority levels with roughly similar proportions (78-84% senior, 8-10% junior), reflecting the population seniority distribution rather than content differentiation. Seniority operates as a within-domain modifier, not a cross-cutting structural dimension.

    This pattern is consistent across granularity levels: - Fine-grained (92 topics): tech_domain NMI=0.152, period=0.073, seniority=0.029 - Reduced (14 archetypes): tech_domain NMI=0.175, period=0.057, seniority=0.018

    Implications for Research Questions

    • RQ1 (employer-side restructuring): Seniority effects should be analyzed within technical domain archetypes. A pooled junior-vs-senior comparison masks domain-specific patterns. Junior data engineering roles may be affected very differently from junior frontend roles. The massive growth of ML/AI Engineering archetype (+22pp) is itself a restructuring signal.

    • RQ2 (task migration): When studying which requirements migrate between seniority levels, the analysis should be stratified by archetype. A requirement that moves from senior to junior in one domain may not move at all in another.

    • Temporal dynamics are substantial. ML/AI Engineering went from 4% to 27% of SWE postings. Frontend/Web shrank from 41% to 24%. Embedded/Systems shrank from 26% to 19%. These are not incremental shifts -- they represent a large-scale recomposition of what "SWE" means in the labor market between 2024 and 2026.
