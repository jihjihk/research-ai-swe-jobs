    T01: Column Coverage Audit

    Data source: data/unified.parquet (Stage 11 final output, 99 columns, 1,395,790 rows) Generated: 2026-04-05 Filters: SWE subset = source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english=true AND date_flag='ok' AND is_swe=true

    Summary

    Of the 99 columns in data/unified.parquet, 28 columns are >50% null in at least one SWE source. Most are expected source-specific metadata gaps, but analysis-critical gaps include company_industry (0% for asaniczka), company_size (0% for asaniczka and scraped), skills_raw (0% for scraped), and description_core_llm (0% for scraped). All Stage 10 LLM classification columns are entirely null across all sources (no budget allocated). Core rule-based analysis columns have adequate coverage for cross-period work, but text analyses must use description_core (rule-based, ~44% accuracy) rather than the higher-quality description_core_llm for any analysis involving scraped data.

    5. Semantic Differences Across Sources

    5a. company_industry: Compound labels in scraped

    Source % with comma-separated labels Example
    arshkon 5.7% (282/4,981) "Technology, Information and Internet" (genuine multi-word name)
    scraped 21.2% (5,114/24,090) "Accounting, IT Services and IT Consulting, and Business Consulting and Services" (multi-industry composite)

    Scraped LinkedIn profiles often list multiple industries per company (e.g., "Software Development, Financial Services, and Technology, Information and Internet"). Arshkon's comma-containing labels are mostly single industries with commas in their names. Cross-source industry comparison requires splitting or first-industry extraction for scraped data.

    5b. seniority_native: Different label vocabularies

    Source Labels available Entry-level?
    arshkon entry, associate, mid-senior, director, intern, executive + 31.4% null Yes (769 SWE)
    asaniczka associate, mid-senior only No
    scraped entry, associate, mid-senior, director, intern, executive + 17.4% null Yes (2,789 SWE)

    Asaniczka provides only 2 seniority levels. This is the binding constraint for entry-level historical analysis — see T02 for the associate-as-junior-proxy assessment.

    5e. Description length: Systematic growth

    Source Period Mean raw length Median raw length Mean core_length Median core_length
    arshkon 2024-04 3,316 2,954 2,738 2,504
    asaniczka 2024-01 4,052 3,825 2,696 2,419
    scraped 2026-03 5,203 5,158 4,314 4,171

    Raw descriptions grew ~57% (median) and core descriptions grew ~67% (median) from 2024 to 2026. The growth in core_length is larger than in raw length, suggesting boilerplate removal is less effective on the longer scraped descriptions. All cross-period text analyses must length-normalize.

    8. Key Findings

    1. 28 columns have >50% null in at least one SWE source. 6 are analysis-critical, 6 are all-null LLM columns, and 16 are expected source-specific metadata gaps.

    2. description_core_llm = 0% for scraped is the most consequential gap. All cross-period text analyses are constrained to description_core (rule-based, ~44% boilerplate removal accuracy) or raw description.

    3. All Stage 10 LLM columns are null. No LLM classification budget was allocated. Rule-based columns are the sole analysis variables.

    4. company_industry has different semantics: 21.2% compound labels in scraped vs 5.7% in arshkon. Requires normalization.

    5. Remote work flags are a cross-period data artifact (0% in 2024, 22.6% in scraped). Do not interpret.

    6. Description length grew ~57-67%. All text analyses must control for length.

    7. Core analysis columns have adequate coverage. seniority_final, yoe_extracted, description_core, company_name_canonical, and ghost_job_risk support cross-period analysis, though seniority loses 7-19% to unknown labels and metro_area drops 23-39%.
