# T13: Company-level patterns
## Finding
Among LinkedIn SWE postings with canonical company names, 391 companies appear in both arshkon and scraped. Those overlap companies cover 36.1% of arshkon SWE postings and 40.6% of scraped SWE postings, so most of the cross-period shift is composition outside the overlap set.
Within the overlap set, entry share falls by 9.1 pp, with -7.2 pp coming from within-company change and -1.9 pp from composition; AI prevalence rises by 11.1 pp, almost entirely within-company (+10.4 pp); and mean description length rises by 1,629 chars, split between +989 within-company and +640 composition.
The new 2026 companies table is dominated by staffing/aggregator names such as Jobs via Dice and Turing, while the disappeared 2024 table is heavier on DataAnnotation, Dice, Collabera, and other large-volume intermediaries.
## Implication for analysis
Use the overlap-company decomposition when arguing for within-firm change, and use the new/disappeared company tables when discussing market-entry or churn. This is the right separation for RQ1 and RQ3 because it prevents composition shifts from being mistaken for within-employer restructuring.
## Data quality note
Company size is observed almost entirely in arshkon at the posting level, so the size-band split inherits arshkon-observed size for matched companies and leaves non-matched scraped companies unbanded. That makes the split useful for overlap-company context, but not a symmetric cross-period size comparison.
