# T18: Description anatomy
## Finding
Across 29,883 known-seniority SWE postings, the median section signal is sparse: requirements is the only consistently nonzero median section (14 chars in 2024-01, 74 in 2024-04, 62 in 2026-03). The growth signal is clearer in the means, where requirements length rises from 417 to 489 to 653 chars, benefits rises from 123 to 131 to 490, legal/EEO rises from 53 to 67 to 198, and responsibilities rises from 51 to 91 to 107. Entry-level postings follow the same pattern: the requirements median falls from 90 chars in 2024-04 to 37 in 2026-03, while benefits and legal/EEO become much more common and longer.
## Implication for analysis
For RQ1, this points to two mechanisms at once: some of the length increase is substantive job-content expansion, and some is residual boilerplate/administrative text. The decomposition suggests the 2026 length growth is not just more requirements, but also more benefits and legal/EEO material, especially in the entry-level postings.
## Data quality note
The section classifier is regex-based and conservative, so paragraphs that do not look like a JD section heading are tagged as `unclassified`. Because Stage 8 already includes rule-based boilerplate removal, the benefits/legal/about shares here are likely understated relative to raw postings, and medians are often zero because many postings only expose one or two recognizable sections. `description_core_llm` is not available in Stage 8.
