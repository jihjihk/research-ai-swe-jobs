# T02: Seniority label comparison
## Finding
`seniority_final` is the stable seniority variable. On SWE rows with native labels, `seniority_final` agrees with `seniority_native` 96.4% of the time (Cohen's kappa 0.83), while the raw rule-based `seniority_imputed` only reaches 61.3% agreement with native labels (kappa 0.13) and 66.9% agreement with `seniority_final` (kappa 0.27). The class-level gap is concentrated in sparse labels: `entry` and `mid-senior` are near-perfect under `seniority_final`, while `associate` and `director` remain noisier. The native-grounded accuracy is essentially the same in arshkon and scraped data after backfill: `seniority_final` is 95.7% accurate in arshkon and 94.7% in scraped rows.

RQ1 direction is unchanged across seniority variants. Using `entry` as the junior rung, the share falls from 20.4% to 13.8% between 2024-04 and 2026-03 under `seniority_final` (-6.6 pp), from 22.4% to 13.6% under native labels only (-8.8 pp), and from 21.5% to 14.2% under the high-confidence subset (-7.3 pp). The 2024-01 native-only baseline is structurally unusable for entry-level trend analysis because asaniczka has zero native entry labels.

## Implication for analysis
Use `seniority_final` for the main RQ1 series and reserve `seniority_native` for validation and sensitivity checks. The junior-share decline is robust to the seniority definition, so the paper can treat the direction of change as stable and focus on the magnitude under different label rules. The source of the label matters mainly for precision, not for the sign of the trend.

## Data quality note
The native-vs-final comparison is strong overall, but sparse classes are still unstable. In the native-grounded audit, `entry` and `mid-senior` are close to deterministic under `seniority_final`, while `associate` and `director` have materially lower accuracy because the sample sizes are small and the boundary is fuzzier. The historical 2024-01 asaniczka slice should not be used for entry-level trend claims based on native labels.

## Action items
Keep `seniority_final` as the default RQ1 seniority field. Exclude 2024-01 asaniczka from any entry-level native-label trend graph. If an appendix sensitivity is needed, show the small 2024-04 to 2026-03 spread across `seniority_final`, `seniority_native`, and the high-confidence subset to demonstrate robustness.
