# T16: Company Hiring Strategy Typology

## Headline Finding

Among companies with at least 3 SWE LinkedIn postings in both arshkon 2024 and scraped 2026, the AI/tool and skill-breadth expansion is mostly a **within-company** change, not just a reshuffling toward different employers. The same common firms moved from 3.74% to 23.23% broad-AI mention prevalence and from 2.23% to 19.80% AI-tool-specific prevalence. In the midpoint decomposition, 91-92% of the absolute AI/tool component is within company.

The junior story is the opposite: explicit entry labels and YOE proxies diverge. In the arshkon-to-scraped common-company panel, J1 entry share fell from 3.46% to 3.02%, while J3 low-YOE share rose from 14.42% to 21.05%. The low-YOE increase is mostly between-company reweighting, not uniform within-company relabeling.

## Methodology

Default row filter: `source_platform='linkedin'`, `is_english=true`, `date_flag='ok'`, `is_swe=true`.

Company panel:

- Primary overlap: companies with at least 3 SWE postings in both `kaggle_arshkon` 2024 and scraped LinkedIn 2026. This produced 240 companies.
- Source sensitivity: companies with at least 3 postings in pooled 2024 (`kaggle_arshkon` + `kaggle_asaniczka`) and scraped 2026. This produced 589 companies.
- Company grouping uses `company_name_canonical`; aggregators are retained in the primary panel and excluded in a sensitivity.

Metric definitions and denominators:

- Broad AI prevalence: any shared tech-matrix broad AI column used by V1/T08, including `mcp`; denominator is all eligible postings in the relevant company/period.
- AI-tool-specific prevalence: shared taxonomy `category='ai_tool'`, excluding `mcp`; denominator is all eligible postings in the relevant company/period.
- Tech breadth: mean count of all shared tech-matrix indicators; denominator is all eligible postings.
- Platform tech breadth: mean count of shared taxonomy `cloud`, `devops`, `ops`, `architecture`, and `tooling` columns; denominator is all eligible postings.
- Requirement breadth and org-scope counts: T11 regex-derived features from `posting_complexity_features.parquet`, restricted to `text_source='llm'`; denominator is LLM-labeled text rows only.
- J1/J2 use `seniority_final`; J3/J4 use `yoe_extracted`; YOE-share denominators are postings with non-null extracted YOE.

No new keyword regex was introduced. AI/tool indicators, org-scope indicators, and requirement-breadth components reuse Wave 2/V1-validated artifacts. T22 has not run yet, so org/scope and management-like terms should be read as posting-language signals, not screened-requirement proof.

### Trajectory Clusters

K-means on standardized company-level deltas produced 5 descriptive trajectories:

| Trajectory | Companies | Main pattern |
|---|---:|---|
| Stack-breadth expanders | 97 | Small J1 change, modest J3 growth, +10.0 pp AI-tool strict, +3.94 tech count, +4.17 requirement breadth |
| Low-YOE growth, tool-light shift | 66 | J1 and J3 up, but tech count and LLM requirement breadth down |
| AI-heavy scope expanders | 51 | J1/J3 down or flat, but +37.8 pp AI-tool strict and +3.01 LLM requirement breadth |

| Label/YOE divergence spike | 12 | J1 down sharply, J3 up sharply, AI/tool and breadth also up |

The clusters should be treated as exploratory typology, not causal classes. Cleaned-text deltas are imputed for clustering when a company lacks LLM rows in one period; the raw feature table preserves the missingness.

### New 2026 Entrants

Against any 2024 company appearance, scraped 2026 has 5,916 new companies contributing 18,317 postings, or 44.8% of scraped LinkedIn SWE postings.

New entrants differ from returning firms in ways that complicate a simple "new AI employers explain everything" story:

- Broad AI prevalence is higher for new entrants: 25.44% vs 22.28%.
- AI-tool strict prevalence is higher: 21.79% vs 18.90%.
- Mean tech count is nearly the same: 7.28 vs 7.24.
- LLM-text requirement breadth is nearly the same: 9.39 vs 9.38.
- J3 low-YOE share is lower for new entrants: 14.59% vs 19.10%.
- Aggregator share is higher: 19.97% vs 13.93%.

Top new entrants by posting volume include `Jobs via Dice` (1,711 postings, all aggregator), `Haystack` (232), `PayPal` (137), `Akkodis Group Nordics` (134), and `TalentAlly` (126). AI-native firms show up but do not dominate volume; for example `Anthropic` has 77 postings with 79.2% broad-AI and 75.3% AI-tool strict prevalence.

### Aggregators Versus Direct Employers

In scraped 2026, aggregators account for 6,800 of 40,881 eligible postings. They do not create the AI expansion:

- Direct employers: 24.27% broad AI, 20.63% AI-tool strict.
- Aggregators: 20.82% broad AI, 18.00% AI-tool strict.
- Aggregators have lower low-YOE share: 13.19% vs 17.85%.
- Aggregators have slightly higher mean tech count: 7.44 vs 7.23.

Aggregator exclusion leaves the main AI/tool and requirement-breadth directions intact.

### Archetype Dimension

For overlap-company rows with T09 labels and LLM text, broad AI increased in every archetype. AI-tool strict increases were largest in `AI LLM Platforms` (+29.8 pp), then `Large Employer Data Platforms` (+17.4 pp), `Product Backend Engineering` (+12.2 pp), and `Java Dotnet Web Services` (+8.3 pp).

Requirement breadth rose in 7 of 8 archetypes; the exception is `Large Employer Data Platforms` (-1.71 signals), despite its AI/tool growth. This is a useful contradiction: not every AI/domain shift is a generic breadth increase.

## Sensitivity Checks

- Aggregator exclusion: AI/tool, tech breadth, platform breadth, and requirement breadth remain directionally stable. In arshkon-only common companies, broad AI changes +19.84 pp after exclusion; AI-tool strict changes +17.79 pp.
- Source restriction: material for seniority. Pooled 2024 gives J1 up, while arshkon-only gives J1 down. Do not cite a single company-level junior-share direction without specifying the 2024 source.
- LLM coverage: scraped has 12,534 LLM-text rows out of 40,881 eligible rows. Requirement breadth, org scope, and cleaned length claims are LLM-labeled subset claims.
- SWE-tier exclusion: overall pooled headline shifts are directionally stable when excluding `title_lookup_llm` rows.

## Surprises

- The strongest within-company movement is not junior reduction; it is AI/tool and stack breadth growth.
- New entrants are somewhat more AI-heavy, but their requirement breadth is almost identical to returning firms.
- Low-YOE expansion in the common-company panel is mostly between-company reweighting, suggesting a changing mix of overlap employers rather than uniform relabeling inside each firm.
- Domain labels matter: one large-employer data-platform archetype shows AI growth with lower requirement breadth.

## Evidence Strength

Strong for company-level AI/tool and tech-breadth expansion: full default SWE frame, 240 arshkon-scraped overlap companies, stable aggregator sensitivity, and validated shared tech indicators.

Moderate for requirement breadth: strong direction but restricted to LLM-cleaned rows, with scraped LLM coverage around 31%.

Weak-to-mixed for company-level junior-share direction: J1/J2 and J3/J4 disagree, and source restriction changes the label-based result.

## Data Quality Caveats

- Company canonicalization can merge or split firms imperfectly, especially aggregators and subsidiaries.
- `company_industry` is null for asaniczka and therefore less useful in pooled 2024.
- T11/T22 scope caveat: org-scope and requirement-breadth metrics identify posting language, not whether employers actually screen those requirements.
- Broad AI and AI-tool strict are separate constructs; broad AI includes `mcp`, while AI-tool strict excludes it.

## Action Items

1. Treat company-level AI/tool and tech-breadth expansion as a strong mechanism result for the paper.
2. Do not lead with "common companies reduced junior hiring"; present the label-vs-YOE divergence instead.
3. Use T22 to test whether within-company breadth growth is screened, preferred, aspirational, or template language.
4. In analysis-phase models, include company fixed effects or company reweighting for AI/tool and breadth outcomes; for junior outcomes, report J1-J4 separately.
