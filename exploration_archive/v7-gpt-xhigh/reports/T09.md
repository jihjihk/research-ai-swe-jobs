# T09. Posting Archetype Discovery / Methods Laboratory

## Headline Finding

Natural SWE posting structure is mostly domain/technology structure, not seniority structure. In the chosen NMF-8 taxonomy, Normalized Mutual Information is highest for `tech_domain` (0.205), then top-company grouping (0.098), source (0.047), period (0.043), and year (0.041). Seniority alignment is near-zero (0.0069). The market-level archetype shifts are therefore more consistent with domain/source/company recomposition than with postings falling into naturally separate junior/mid/senior language clusters.

The strongest temporal movement in the LLM-cleaned labeled subset is AI/LLM expansion: `AI LLM Platforms` rises from 3.1% of labeled 2024 rows to 17.4% of labeled 2026 rows. `Product Backend Engineering` and `Cloud DevOps Infrastructure` also grow, while `Enterprise Application Support`, `Java Dotnet Web Services`, and `Defense Clearance Systems` shrink as corpus shares.

Downstream labels were written to `exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet` for 33,741 eligible `text_source='llm'` rows with at least 100 cleaned characters.

## Methodology

Primary sample: 8,000 LinkedIn SWE postings from `exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_cleaned_text.parquet`, joined to `swe_embedding_index.parquet` and `swe_embeddings.npy`. All sampled rows use `text_source='llm'`; raw fallback rows were excluded from modeling. Rows with fewer than 100 cleaned characters were excluded. Company cap was 50 postings per `company_name_canonical` before sample allocation.

Sample allocation was year-balanced and source-period controlled:

| Source-period | Rows |
|---|---:|
| kaggle_arshkon, 2024-04 | 2,400 |
| kaggle_asaniczka, 2024-01 | 1,600 |
| scraped, 2026-03 | 2,000 |
| scraped, 2026-04 | 2,000 |

This gives 4,000 rows from 2024 and 4,000 from 2026 while preferring arshkon inside the historical allocation. Seniority was stratified within each source-period according to available capped counts. Final sample seniority mix: 308 junior, 29 mid, 4,002 senior, and 3,661 unknown. Mid-level is thin because it is thin in the shared artifact, not because of a modeling choice.

Company concentration in the modeling sample was bounded: the largest companies each contribute at most 50 rows (0.6% of the sample), and the top five contribute 250 rows (3.1%). Aggregators are 956 of 8,000 rows: 10.5% in 2024 and 13.4% in 2026.

All DuckDB joins used `PRAGMA memory_limit='4GB'; PRAGMA threads=1;`. The run used peak RSS about 1.8GB. No preprocessing stages were rerun and no new LLM calls were made.

## Method Comparison

BERTopic completed on the same 8,000-row sample using shared embeddings, UMAP, HDBSCAN, and `min_topic_size=30`. The primary seed produced 3 topics, 0.19% outliers, and rough NPMI coherence 0.122. Top terms were a large general requirements topic plus Android and iOS subtopics. This was not stable: across three seeds, ARI averaged 0.341 with a minimum of 0.019, and one seed produced 26 topics with 41.8% outliers. The `min_topic_size=20` and `50` sensitivity runs both returned 3 topics in the primary seed.

NMF was more stable and more useful for downstream stratification. On TF-IDF cleaned text:

| Method | Variant | Topics | Noise | Rough coherence | Stability ARI |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| BERTopic | min_topic_size=30 primary seed | 3 | 0.2% | 0.122 | 0.341 mean |
| NMF | k=5 | 5 | 0.0% | 0.169 | 0.9998 |
| NMF | k=8 | 8 | 0.0% | 0.211 | 0.9993 |
| NMF | k=12 | 12 | 0.0% | 0.367 | 0.9961 |
| NMF | k=15 | 15 | 0.0% | 0.397 | 0.7225 |

I chose NMF k=8 for the shared label artifact. k=12 had higher coherence but split the taxonomy more finely than downstream Wave 2/3 tasks need; k=15 became materially less stable. BERTopic is useful as a diagnostic because it surfaced mobile subtopics, but it is too seed-sensitive here to serve as the primary archetype layer.

Method alignment was limited. BERTopic's general requirements topic overlaps most with NMF's `Enterprise Application Support`; its Android/iOS subtopics overlap weakly with broad NMF components. This is a real methods finding: density-based clustering can discover small platform-specific pockets, while NMF gives a more stable market-wide domain taxonomy.

|---|---:|---:|---:|

| Cloud DevOps Infrastructure | 10.0% | 15.9% | +6.0 pp |
| Embedded Firmware Systems | 7.5% | 7.8% | +0.3 pp |
| Large Employer Data Platforms | 5.4% | 4.2% | -1.3 pp |
| Defense Clearance Systems | 13.4% | 7.4% | -6.0 pp |
| Java Dotnet Web Services | 19.8% | 13.7% | -6.1 pp |

The AI/LLM expansion survives the essential sensitivities. Excluding aggregators gives +14.5 pp; excluding `swe_classification_tier='title_lookup_llm'` gives +14.5 pp. `Large Employer Data Platforms` is the fragile case: it falls by -1.3 pp in all rows but rises by +1.9 pp after excluding aggregators, so downstream agents should not treat that small trend as robust.

## Dominant Structure Test

NMI between NMF archetype labels and candidate explanatory structures:

| Factor | NMI |
|---|---:|
| Tech/domain signal | 0.205 |
| Top-30 company group | 0.098 |
| Source | 0.047 |
| Period | 0.043 |
| Year | 0.041 |
| SWE classification tier | 0.016 |
| Seniority 3-level | 0.0069 |
| Aggregator flag | 0.0058 |

Interpretation: the archetypes are not seniority archetypes. They are mostly domain/technology archetypes, with a secondary company/source component. This supports using T09 labels for domain stratification in T11, T15, T16, T20, and T28, but not as a substitute for seniority definitions.

## Sensitivity Checks

Aggregator exclusion: the main growth/shrinkage story is stable for AI/LLM, product backend, cloud/devops, enterprise support, defense, and Java/.NET services. Aggregator exclusion changes magnitudes and flips only the small `Large Employer Data Platforms` trend.

Description text source: raw-description NMF on the same 8,000 rows is not a valid substitute for LLM-cleaned text. Clean-vs-raw assignment NMI is only 0.199. Raw top terms are dominated by boilerplate and company/source artifacts: EEO language, `Capital One`, `TikTok`, `JPMorgan Chase`, `Google`, salary/benefits language, and location strings. This is the strongest validation of the Gate 1.5 instruction to use `text_source='llm'` for text-dependent modeling.

SWE classification tier: excluding `title_lookup_llm` preserves the main AI/LLM, product backend, cloud/devops, enterprise-support, defense, and Java/.NET directions. It does not resolve the `Large Employer Data Platforms` fragility.

## Coverage And Caveats

Label artifact coverage is limited to LLM-cleaned rows with at least 100 cleaned characters:

| Source-period | Labels |
|---|---:|
| kaggle_asaniczka 2024-01 | 16,786 |
| kaggle_arshkon 2024-04 | 4,575 |
| scraped 2026-03 | 6,243 |
| scraped 2026-04 | 6,137 |

Raw fallback rows are intentionally unlabeled: 1,092 asaniczka rows, 4 arshkon rows, 13,452 scraped March rows, and 14,895 scraped April rows. Therefore scraped 2026 archetype distributions describe the LLM-cleaned subset, not the full scraped SWE corpus.

NMF component names are descriptive labels, not hand-validated occupational categories. `Large Employer Data Platforms` still contains some employer-template residue, visible in terms such as `millions americans`; downstream uses should keep company capping and raw-text boilerplate risks in view.

NMF convergence warnings appeared at 350 iterations for some fits. Stability remained high for k=8 (mean ARI 0.999), so this is a numerical caveat rather than a failed run.

## Surprises & Unexpected Patterns

- Seniority is almost absent from the unsupervised structure. This weakens any expectation that junior and senior postings naturally form separate semantic clusters.
- AI/LLM is the clearest growing archetype, but it is not the least junior-heavy archetype in the LLM-labeled subset.
- BERTopic mostly found one general topic plus mobile subtopics under the primary seed, then changed substantially under another seed. The small mobile pockets are real enough to notice but not stable enough for the shared taxonomy.
- Raw descriptions recreate company and compliance boilerplate more than job-content archetypes. This is more severe than a mild noise issue.
- Defense/clearance is a distinct archetype and shrinks as a labeled-row share, but its within-archetype YOE profile remains senior-heavy.

## Downstream Use

Use `exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet` by joining on `uid`. The artifact has exactly the requested columns: `uid`, `archetype`, `archetype_name`.

Recommended use: domain stratification for text, complexity, seniority-boundary, company, and geography analyses. Do not use these labels as causal treatment categories, and do not infer full-corpus scraped proportions without reporting LLM-cleaned coverage.
