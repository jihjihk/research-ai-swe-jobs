# T11. Requirements Complexity And Credential Stacking

## Headline Finding

Requirements became materially broader in the LLM-cleaned SWE subset. Mean `requirement_breadth` rises from 7.23 in pooled 2024 to 9.39 in scraped 2026 (+2.16 distinct requirement signals). Mean `credential_stack_depth` rises from 2.65 to 3.13 (+0.49 of 7 categories), and the share of postings with stack depth at least 4 rises from about 24% in each 2024 source to 38.9% in scraped 2026.

This is not just an aggregate seniority-composition result. Requirement breadth rises under every junior panel definition and every senior panel definition. For junior definitions: J1 +1.74, J2 +1.84, J3 +1.75, J4 +2.08. For senior definitions: S1 +2.40, S2 +6.16, S3 +2.32, S4 +2.61.

The strongest components are technology breadth, AI-specific requirements, and organizational-scope language. Tech density per 1,000 characters barely changes in all-SWE (+0.11 and below within-2024 calibration), so technology count growth should be read as broader stacks in longer/richer descriptions, not necessarily higher density.

## Methodology

Primary sample: shared `swe_cleaned_text.parquet` rows with `text_source='llm'`, joined to `swe_tech_matrix.parquet` for technology counts. This gives 34,258 LLM-cleaned LinkedIn SWE rows: 4,687 arshkon, 17,037 asaniczka, and 12,534 scraped rows. Scraped LLM-cleaned coverage remains limited: 6,325 rows in 2026-03 and 6,209 rows in 2026-04, versus 28,347 scraped raw-fallback rows excluded from the primary text-sensitive results.

Metrics:

- `tech_count`: distinct shared tech-matrix indicators.
- `ai_requirement_count`: AI/domain/tool subset of the tech matrix, excluding generic `numpy`, `pandas`, `scipy`, and ambiguous `mcp`.
- `requirement_breadth`: non-AI tech count + AI count + soft-skill count + org-scope count + strong-management count + education-any + YOE-any.
- `credential_stack_depth`: number of non-empty categories among tech, education, YOE, soft skills, org scope, strong management, and AI.
- `tech_density`: `tech_count` per 1,000 cleaned-text characters.
- `scope_density`: org-scope count per 1,000 cleaned-text characters.

T30 definitions are reported for all seniority-stratified claims: J1-J4 for junior and S1-S4 for senior. Row flags follow the published T30 definitions; the aggregate T30 panel CSV is copied into the task outputs for traceability.

Custom regex families were semantically checked on 50 sampled surrounding-context matches per major family. Manual precision after tightening:

| Family | Precision | Note |
|---|---:|---|
| Soft skill | 94% | Requirement-style communication/collaboration/problem-solving/teamwork. |
| Org scope | 92% | Ownership, end-to-end, cross-functional, stakeholder, autonomy. |
| Management strong | 92% | Bare `hire` was removed after a contract-to-hire false positive. |
| Management broad extra | 86% | Broad coordination context, not people-management evidence. |
| AI requirement | 96% | Generic scientific libraries and ambiguous `mcp` excluded. |

## Main Results

### Complexity And Stacking

All-SWE LLM-cleaned means:

| Metric | pooled 2024 | scraped 2026 | Change | Within-2024 diff |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Requirement breadth | 7.23 | 9.39 | +2.16 | -0.16 |
| Credential stack depth | 2.65 | 3.13 | +0.49 | +0.04 |
| Tech count | 5.18 | 6.94 | +1.75 | -0.16 |
| AI requirement count | 0.08 | 0.53 | +0.45 | -0.03 |
| Org-scope count | 0.57 | 0.86 | +0.30 | -0.01 |
| Scope density | 0.58 | 0.80 | +0.22 | -0.04 |
| Tech density | 7.49 | 7.59 | +0.11 | -0.22 |

Requirement breadth and stack depth are clearly above within-2024 instrument variation. Tech density is not; scope density is.

### Entry-Level Complexity

Every junior definition shows higher requirement breadth in scraped 2026:

| Definition | pooled 2024 | scraped 2026 | Change |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| J1: `seniority_final='entry'` | 6.26 | 8.00 | +1.74 |
| J2: entry/associate | 6.27 | 8.12 | +1.84 |
| J3: YOE <= 2 | 7.46 | 9.21 | +1.75 |
| J4: YOE <= 3 | 7.44 | 9.51 | +2.08 |

The strongest junior components are tech count and AI count. J1 AI requirement count rises from 0.08 to 0.60; J3 rises from 0.09 to 0.50.

Scope inflation is more definition-sensitive. J1/J2 org-scope counts rise, but J1/J2 scope density is weak relative to within-2024 variation. J3/J4 scope density rises more robustly: J3 +0.17 and J4 +0.19 per 1,000 characters.

### Senior Complexity And Management

Senior complexity rises under all senior definitions:

| Definition | Requirement breadth change | Stack-depth change | Strong-management change |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| S1: mid-senior/director | +2.40 | +0.59 | +0.075 |
| S2: director | +6.16 | +0.51 | +0.260 |
| S3: raw senior title regex | +2.32 | +0.62 | +0.080 |
| S4: YOE >= 5 | +2.61 | +0.50 | +0.062 |

Management language does not support a simple "senior roles moved away from management" claim in this metric. Strong management rises for S1/S3/S4 and rises sharply for the sparse director-only S2 group. At the all-SWE level, strong management only rises modestly (+0.025) and is close to within-2024 noise, so the senior-specific result is the more relevant one.

The top strong-management triggers are still mentorship-heavy, not direct-report-heavy. In pooled 2024, `mentor` appears in 10.3% of LLM-text postings and `coach` in 2.2%; in scraped 2026, `mentor` is 12.7% and `coach` is 2.2%. Direct reports, performance reviews, hiring, and headcount are all rare.

The broad tier rises more visibly, but it should be interpreted as coordination/leadership context: `team` rises from 43.9% to 59.9%, and `stakeholder` from 15.8% to 23.0%.

### Domain-Stratified Scope

T09 archetypes show that complexity growth is not only an AI-domain story. Among all LLM-labeled rows, requirement breadth rises in 7 of 8 archetypes. Large increases appear in `Java Dotnet Web Services` (+2.92), `AI LLM Platforms` (+2.11), `Cloud DevOps Infrastructure` (+2.06), and `Defense Clearance Systems` (+1.94).

For J3 low-YOE rows, requirement breadth also rises in 7 of 8 archetypes. `Large Employer Data Platforms` is the exception on breadth (-1.52), but its scope density rises (+0.48), suggesting a changing template/domain mix rather than simple deflation.

### Outliers

The top 1% threshold is `requirement_breadth >= 23` in the LLM-cleaned sample. Outliers are not pure template bloat. The heuristic split found mostly `likely_real_complex_role` and `mixed_needs_review`, with no large standalone template-bloat class. Scraped outliers are still company/aggregator-concentrated: the likely-real subset is 21.6% aggregators and includes `Jobs via Dice`, `JPMorgan Chase & Co.`, `GEICO`, `Rivago Infotech Inc`, and `Equifax` among top companies.

## Surprises And Unexpected Patterns

- AI requirements rose inside junior definitions, not only in senior roles.
- J1 label-based entry postings show breadth growth but weak scope-density growth; low-YOE J3/J4 show stronger scope-density evidence.
- Senior management language rises when measured with strong patterns, but the rise is mostly mentorship/coordination rather than direct-report management.
- Technology count rises strongly, while tech density does not. This prevents a clean claim that postings became more tech-dense independent of description length.
- Complexity growth appears across most T09 domains, so domain recomposition alone is unlikely to explain the full change.

## Sensitivity Checks

Aggregator exclusion preserves the main result. Requirement breadth changes: all-SWE +2.29, J1 +1.63, J3 +1.82, S1 +2.56, S4 +2.59.

Company cap 50 preserves and slightly strengthens the main result. Requirement breadth changes: all-SWE +2.30, J1 +1.74, J3 +2.07, S1 +2.58, S4 +2.68.

Excluding entry-specialist employers preserves the main result. Requirement breadth changes: all-SWE +2.30, J1 +2.29, J3 +2.13, S1 +2.55, S4 +2.74.

Text-source sensitivity does not flip direction. Including raw fallback increases the magnitude: all-SWE requirement breadth change is +2.64 versus +2.16 in LLM-cleaned text. This likely reflects boilerplate/template contamination in raw scraped descriptions, so the LLM-cleaned estimate should remain primary.

Within-2024 calibration supports requirement breadth, stack depth, AI count, org-scope count, and scope density. It does not support strong claims about tech density, education level, or all-SWE strong-management change.

## Caveats

The primary text-dependent findings describe the LLM-cleaned subset, not the full scraped corpus. Scraped cleaned-text coverage is about 31% of scraped LinkedIn SWE rows.

Regex features are interpretable indicators, not parsed requirements. The semantic validation removes obvious low-precision patterns, but it cannot distinguish all aspirational requirements from screened requirements.

`management_broad_count` is intentionally broad. Use it for coordination language, not people-management claims.

S2 director-only cells are small. Treat S2 as top-ladder descriptive evidence, not a powered senior estimate.
