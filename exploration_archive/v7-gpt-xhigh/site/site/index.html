    The strongest story is skill-surface expansion across software-producing technical work.¶

    The exploration weakened the initial junior-collapse framing. The evidence now points to employer-side posting-language restructuring: AI/tool/platform/workflow language expanded across returning companies, metros, SWE, and adjacent technical roles, while seniority labels and YOE floors became unreliable anchors.

    This package uses existing exploration reports, gate memos, and figures only. It does not add new analysis, inspect data artifacts, rerun preprocessing, or make LLM calls.

    What changed¶

    The original frame asked whether AI coding agents were collapsing junior SWE demand and pushing senior work downward. The completed synthesis says that story is not robust. Junior measures are construct-dependent, and several aggregate panels show junior definitions rising rather than falling.

    The stronger narrative is narrower and more defensible: postings broadened around AI, tools, platforms, workflows, and requirement breadth across software-producing technical work. That is a posting-content claim, not an employment claim.

    Lead findings¶

    Finding Evidence Boundary
    Returning companies sharply expanded AI/tool language. T16: broad AI 3.74% -> 23.23%; AI-tool 2.23% -> 19.80%; 91-92% of AI/tool change decomposed as within-company. Company canonicalization and binary mention measures remain caveats; V2 is missing.
    Expansion is SWE-amplified but adjacent-parallel. T18: SWE and adjacent roles rose much more than controls on AI/tool, tech count, and requirement breadth. Occupation boundaries and adjacent-role heterogeneity need formal modeling.
    AI/tool diffusion is geographically broad. T17: all 26 eligible metros increased broad-AI and AI-tool prevalence. Metro-known frame, source composition, and multi-location exclusions shape denominators.
    Requirement and technology breadth rose across seniority. T11, T14, V1: breadth rises under J1-J4 and S1/S4. Text-sensitive claims are LLM-cleaned subset claims; density and force are separate questions.
    Seniority labels and YOE floors diverge. T08, T16, T30, T15: entry labels and low-YOE rows identify different populations. T20 boundary modeling failed, so this remains a measurement result needing targeted follow-up.
    Domain and technology structure dominate seniority structure. T09, T15, V1: archetype/domain explains far more representation structure than seniority. Archetype and semantic findings are LLM-cleaned subset evidence.

    Evidence boundary¶

    The site treats these as explicit gaps rather than inferred results:

    • T19 missing: no temporal-rate stability, scraped-window consistency, posting-age, backlog, or flow evidence.
    • T20/T21 missing: no seniority-boundary classifier, associate-distance model, or senior-role deep dive.
    • T22/T23 missing: no ghost/aspirational requirement-force analysis and no employer-worker usage divergence evidence.
    • V2 missing: Wave 3 T16/T17/T18 are synthesized by Gate 3 but lack independent verification comparable to V1.
