    T18 Cross-Occupation Boundary Analysis¶

    Headline Finding¶

    Wave 2's AI/platform/workflow expansion is SWE-amplified, not SWE-specific. The same direction appears in SWE-adjacent roles, often at nearly the same magnitude, while controls move only modestly. This weakens a paper frame based on uniquely SWE restructuring, but supports a stronger frame: software-adjacent technical work is absorbing AI/tool/platform requirements much faster than the broader control labor market.

    Key result, default LinkedIn / English / date-ok frame:

    Metric SWE 2024 -> 2026 Adjacent 2024 -> 2026 Control 2024 -> 2026 SWE-control DiD Verdict
    Broad AI, raw binary 12.5% -> 39.7% (+27.2 pp) 11.8% -> 38.8% (+27.0 pp) 0.5% -> 3.1% (+2.6 pp) +24.5 pp SWE-amplified, adjacent-parallel
    AI-tool, raw binary 2.2% -> 24.0% (+21.8 pp) 2.1% -> 21.2% (+19.1 pp) 0.01% -> 0.8% (+0.8 pp) +21.0 pp SWE-amplified
    AI-domain, raw binary 11.9% -> 29.0% (+17.1 pp) 11.6% -> 32.8% (+21.2 pp) 0.5% -> 2.6% (+2.1 pp) +14.9 pp adjacent even stronger
    Bounded tech count, raw mean 5.00 -> 7.36 (+2.36) 3.06 -> 5.37 (+2.31) 0.22 -> 0.35 (+0.14) +2.22 SWE-amplified
    Requirement breadth, LLM-labeled 8.15 -> 10.71 (+2.56) 6.39 -> 9.31 (+2.93) 2.21 -> 2.92 (+0.70) +1.86 SWE-amplified, adjacent stronger
    Org-scope prevalence, LLM-labeled 46.5% -> 61.9% (+15.5 pp) 49.6% -> 67.5% (+17.8 pp) 20.4% -> 31.8% (+11.4 pp) +4.1 pp partly field-wide

    Interpretation: the Wave 2 story should not be written as "SWE alone changed." It is more defensible as an AI-era expansion in software-producing and software-adjacent technical roles, with controls showing a smaller background rise.

    Methodology¶

    Frame: source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english=true AND date_flag='ok', grouped as SWE (is_swe), SWE-adjacent (is_swe_adjacent), and controls (is_control). Eligible rows: 242,854 total in the feature artifact.

    Text and metric definitions:

    • Broad AI: raw-description binary mention of AI-tool or AI-domain families, excluding mcp from the primary definition.
    • AI-tool: LLMs, generative AI, coding assistants, model APIs, agents, prompt engineering, model fine-tuning, evals, RAG, LLM frameworks, and vector databases. After validation, the script tightened false-prone subpatterns for bare Claude, legal LLM, and generic non-model "fine-tuning."
    • AI-domain: machine learning, deep learning, NLP, computer vision, MLOps, PyTorch/TensorFlow, Hugging Face, and AI/ML/artificial-intelligence domain language; generic NumPy/Pandas/SciPy/scikit-learn are not included.
    • Tech count: a bounded high-frequency cross-occupation taxonomy, not the full T14 148-indicator SWE matrix.
    • Org-scope and requirement breadth: LLM-labeled cleaned text only. Denominator is llm_extraction_coverage='labeled'.
    • TF-IDF boundary sample: 200 SWE and 200 SWE-adjacent LLM-cleaned descriptions per period, 800 rows total.

    LLM coverage is the main denominator caveat: adjacent text coverage is nearly complete, while scraped SWE and control text-sensitive metrics are subset evidence.

    Boundary Shift¶

    The generic SWE-adjacent boundary does not clearly converge toward SWE in TF-IDF space.

    Similarity 2024 2026 Change
    SWE-adjacent centroid cosine 0.838 0.817 -0.021
    Mean pairwise cosine 0.066 0.074 +0.007

    The centroid result moves apart, while the pairwise mean rises slightly. I treat this as mixed/no generic convergence, consistent with T15's rejection of broad semantic convergence.

    Terms moving into adjacent descriptions include python, cloud, automation, test, familiarity, cross functional, apis, tooling, exposure, sdlc, and github. These look like workflow/platform and engineering-practice migration, not wholesale occupation collapse.

    Adjacent Roles¶

    Some adjacent families are nearly SWE-like on the measured requirement surface:

    2026 adjacent family n Broad AI AI-tool AI-domain Tech count gap vs SWE Breadth gap vs SWE
    Data scientist / ML 2,558 83.2% 40.5% 79.8% -0.22 +0.75
    Data engineer 138 43.5% 24.6% 31.9% -0.66 +0.48
    Product/solutions/support 161 28.6% 21.1% 18.0% -1.79 -0.41

    Data scientist/ML and data engineer are the closest to SWE by these metrics, but they are not "indistinguishable" in title/domain terms. The better claim is boundary thickening: specific adjacent families now share AI/platform requirements with SWE.

    Seniority Panel¶

    SWE junior definitions all rise, matching Wave 2's label/YOE ambiguity rather than junior elimination.

    Variant Definition SWE change Verdict
    J1 seniority_final='entry' +1.46 pp Up
    J2 entry/associate +1.41 pp Up
    J3 yoe_extracted<=2, YOE-known denom +5.98 pp Up
    J4 yoe_extracted<=3, YOE-known denom +8.12 pp Up

    SWE senior definitions are mixed: S1 mid-senior/director -16.25 pp, S2 director +0.26 pp, S3 senior-title regex -14.42 pp, S4 YOE>=5 -7.50 pp. Agreement verdict: junior unanimous up; senior mixed because director-only rises while broader senior definitions fall.

    Controls behave differently: label-based junior rises slightly, but YOE-based low-experience shares fall sharply. This is another sign that occupation-specific seniority labels and YOE floors are not interchangeable.

    Sensitivity Checks¶

    The core SWE-control DiD survives required sensitivities.

    Metric Base DiD Exclude aggregators Exclude SWE title_lookup_llm Company cap 50
    Broad AI +24.5 pp +26.4 pp +24.8 pp +22.4 pp
    AI-tool +21.0 pp +21.4 pp +21.4 pp +20.9 pp
    Tech count +2.22 +2.40 +2.14 +2.41
    Requirement breadth +1.86 +2.09 +1.76 +2.16
    Org-scope prevalence +4.1 pp +4.9 pp +4.1 pp +3.4 pp

    Within-2024 calibration strengthens the AI/tech interpretation: arshkon-to-asaniczka differences are tiny or opposite-signed for AI and tech count, while cross-period DiD is large. Org-scope is less clean because controls also rise.

    Evidence Strength¶

    Strong:

    • AI-tool and broad-AI growth is sharply larger in SWE and adjacent than controls.
    • Bounded tech count and LLM-labeled requirement breadth rise in SWE and adjacent, with sensitivity robustness.
    • Adjacent roles, especially data scientist/ML and data engineering, share much of the 2026 AI/platform surface.

    Moderate:

    • Boundary migration terms from TF-IDF are discovery evidence only, based on capped samples.
    • Org-scope language is real but partly field-wide.

    Weak/rejected:

    • Generic SWE-adjacent semantic convergence. Centroid similarity falls, and pairwise similarity rises only slightly.

    Data Quality Caveats¶

    • The T18 tech count is a bounded cross-occupation taxonomy, not T14's full SWE tech matrix.
    • Requirement breadth and org-scope metrics are LLM-labeled subset evidence.
    • Raw binary AI prevalence can include company/background AI mentions, not only hard requirements. Manual review of 100 saved AI contexts found semantic AI-topic matches after tightening patterns, but requirement force is not parsed.
    • mcp is tracked separately and excluded from primary AI due ambiguity.
    • The full T18 script should not be rerun casually; the final feature artifact is readable and nonzero, but the advisor requested no further full reruns.

    Action Items¶

    1. Frame the paper story as SWE-amplified technical-role restructuring, not uniquely SWE-only restructuring.
    2. Treat controls as a background trend check: they move on length and org-scope, but not much on AI/tool/tech/breadth.
    3. Follow up on data scientist/ML and data engineer boundaries in T20/T28 if boundary mechanisms become central.
    4. For paper-grade AI-tool rates, keep the tightened patterns and continue excluding mcp from primary AI.
