# T13. Linguistic & Structural Evolution

## Headline

In LLM-cleaned text, 2026 SWE postings are longer and harder to read, but the growth is not mostly benefits/legal/about-company boilerplate. Pooled-2024 to scraped-2026 mean cleaned length rises from 946 to 1,094 characters (+148). The growth decomposes into role-summary (+45.5 chars), responsibilities (+27.0), requirements (+15.1), and still-unclassified cleaned content (+47.3); benefits/about/legal contribute only about +1.0 char in the cleaned-text primary.

The raw-description sensitivity tells a different story: raw scraped descriptions are about 5,000 chars on the same LLM-covered rows and roughly 27% of raw characters fall in benefits/about/legal sections. This is why `description_core_llm` remains the right primary text source for content claims.

## Methodology

Script: `exploration/scripts/T13_linguistic_structural_evolution.py`.

Primary frame: shared LinkedIn SWE text artifact, `text_source='llm'`, nonempty cleaned text. Primary section/tone rows: 4,653 arshkon 2024, 16,992 asaniczka 2024, and 12,456 scraped 2026. Coverage including raw fallback is reported in `exploration/tables/T13/text_source_coverage.csv`.

Section classifier: regex heading parser for role summary, responsibilities, requirements, preferred, benefits, about company, legal/EEO, and unclassified. Because LLM-cleaned text often removes punctuation but leaves flattened heading tokens, the classifier includes a conservative flattened-heading fallback with plausibility cues. T12 uses `requirements_responsibilities_text`, which includes responsibilities, requirements, and preferred sections.

Readability: bounded deterministic sample, at most 500 rows per source-period-seniority cell. The sample has 2,402 pooled-2024 rows and 2,652 scraped-2026 rows. Absolute readability scores are inflated because LLM-cleaned text often lacks sentence punctuation; use them as relative metrics only.

## Main Findings

1. **Cleaned-text length growth is real but modest relative to raw growth.** Mean LLM-cleaned length rises from 946 chars in pooled 2024 to 1,094 in scraped 2026 (+15.7%). Arshkon-only to scraped is larger (+192 chars), with requirements and role-summary each accounting for about 42% of that arshkon-baseline growth.

2. **Requirements/responsibilities did not expand as a share of cleaned text.** Core req/resp/preferred share is nearly flat: pooled 2024 42.0% to scraped 2026 41.5%. In levels, requirements/responsibilities grow, but the whole cleaned posting grows too.

3. **Raw boilerplate is large, cleaned boilerplate is not.** On the same LLM-covered rows, raw scraped descriptions average 4,999 chars. Raw scraped section shares are about 18.3% responsibilities, 21.0% requirements, 7.3% preferred, and 26.7% benefits/about/legal. In cleaned scraped text, benefits/about/legal are only about 0.13% of characters.

4. **Readability worsens directionally.** In the bounded sample, FK grade rises from 38.7 to 45.9, average sentence length from 56.9 to 78.1 words, and type-token ratio falls from 0.848 to 0.829. Senior rows remain the hardest to read, but all seniority groups move in the same direction.

5. **Tone becomes less direct but more marketed.** Pooled imperative markers fall from 0.024 to 0.011 per 1K cleaned chars, and inclusive/direct language also falls. Marketing markers rise from 0.252 to 0.319 per 1K chars; share with any marketing marker rises from 18.9% to 26.4%. Aggregator exclusion does not flip this.

6. **Entry structure changes are not boilerplate-led.** Under J1-J4, junior core-section share rises or stays high: J1 37.6% to 44.3%, J2 38.3% to 43.9%, J3 45.4% to 47.8%, J4 45.3% to 46.5%. The S1 senior comparator falls from 43.5% to 41.3%.

## Surprises & Unexpected Patterns

- LLM-cleaned text often preserves section words but not heading punctuation. A line-heading-only parser would have classified most cleaned text as unsectioned.
- The strongest raw-text expansion is not a safe content signal; raw descriptions preserve benefits/legal/company sections that the cleaned-text column removes.
- Direct-address/imperative language declines while marketing language rises, so 2026 postings are not simply more conversational.
- J1 entry-labeled postings become more core-section-heavy relative to S1 senior postings, which complicates a pure "entry postings are inflated by boilerplate" interpretation.

## Sensitivity Checks

Aggregator exclusion: tone directions hold. Marketing density is 0.232 to 0.328 per 1K chars with aggregators excluded, versus 0.252 to 0.319 in all rows.

Description text source: raw same-row descriptions produce much higher length and boilerplate shares. This is a material text-source sensitivity and supports using LLM-cleaned text as the primary for T12.

Source restriction: pooled-2024 dampens requirement-growth relative to arshkon-only. Arshkon-only to scraped shows requirements +81.5 chars and role summary +82.4; pooled-2024 to scraped shows requirements +15.1 and role summary +45.5.

## Caveats

- Section labels are heuristic parser outputs, not hand-validated gold labels.
- Readability scores are relative only because cleaned text strips or normalizes punctuation.
- `preferred` is included in the reusable T12 core text because it is requirement-like, but reports distinguish it from required qualifications.
- Tone marker context samples are saved in `tone_marker_context_samples.csv`; no high-precision semantic claim is made from those regexes alone.
