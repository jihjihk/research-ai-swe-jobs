# T17: Geographic Market Structure

## Headline Finding

The AI/tool shift is geographically broad rather than confined to a few tech hubs. Across the 26 metros with at least 50 SWE LinkedIn postings in both pooled 2024 and scraped 2026, broad-AI prevalence increased in all 26 metros, with a mean change of +17.6 pp. AI-tool-specific prevalence also increased in all 26 metros, with a mean change of +16.1 pp.

Tech hubs have somewhat larger AI/tool increases, but the difference is not the main story: heuristic tech hubs average +19.8 pp broad AI versus +16.7 pp in other metros. AI change is essentially uncorrelated with 2024 metro posting volume and with entry/low-YOE share changes.

## Methodology

Default row filter: `source_platform='linkedin'`, `is_english=true`, `date_flag='ok'`, `is_swe=true`.

Metro rollup rules:

- Use `metro_area`, not scraper search metro metadata.
- Exclude `is_multi_location=true`; do not expand multi-location rows.
- Primary comparison uses pooled 2024 (`kaggle_arshkon` + `kaggle_asaniczka`) versus scraped 2026.
- Eligible metro threshold: at least 50 SWE postings in both periods. This produced 26 metros.

Metric definitions and denominators:

- Broad AI prevalence: any V1/T08 broad AI tech-matrix column, including `mcp`; denominator is all eligible metro-period postings.
- AI-tool-specific prevalence: shared taxonomy `category='ai_tool'`, excluding `mcp`; denominator is all eligible metro-period postings.
- J1/J2 use `seniority_final`; denominator is all eligible metro-period postings.
- J3/J4 use `yoe_extracted`; denominator is postings with non-null extracted YOE.
- Requirement breadth and org-scope counts use T11 features restricted to `text_source='llm'`; denominator is LLM-text rows.
- Raw length is median `description_length`; cleaned length is median T11 `char_len` among LLM-text rows.
- Tech diversity is the count of shared tech indicators with at least one mention in the metro-period.

## Concentration And Correlation Tests

Heuristic tech hubs: San Francisco Bay Area, Seattle, New York City, Boston, Austin, Raleigh-Durham-Chapel Hill, San Diego, Los Angeles.

| Group | Broad AI mean change | AI-tool strict mean change | Requirement breadth mean change |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Tech-hub heuristic metros | +19.8 pp | +18.1 pp | +2.01 |
| Other metros | +16.7 pp | +15.2 pp | +2.20 |
| Top 2024 volume quartile | +18.5 pp | +16.8 pp | +2.28 |
| Lower-volume metros | +17.4 pp | +15.8 pp | +2.10 |

Metro-level correlations:

- Broad-AI change vs J1 entry-share change: Pearson r = -0.19, p = 0.34.
- Broad-AI change vs J3 low-YOE share change: r = 0.04, p = 0.86.
- AI-tool strict change vs J3 low-YOE share change: r = -0.02, p = 0.92.
- Broad-AI change vs requirement-breadth change: r = 0.44, p = 0.026.
- Broad-AI change vs 2024 posting volume: r = -0.02, p = 0.93.

Interpretation: AI/tool growth travels with requirement-breadth growth, not with a clear metro-level junior/low-YOE decline.

This reinforces the Gate 2 warning: geography does not rescue a simple junior-share story.

## Archetype / Domain Composition

T09 archetype labels exist only for LLM-text rows. Within the 26 eligible metros, archetype-labeled rows total 12,586 in 2024 and 9,078 in 2026.

A simple archetype-mix adjustment suggests most metro AI variation is domain-composition aligned rather than a metro-specific shock. Among labeled rows:

- Mean actual broad-AI delta: +16.87 pp.
- Mean predicted delta from metro archetype mix and national archetype-period AI rates: +16.84 pp.
- Mean residual delta: +0.02 pp.
- Median absolute residual: 1.85 pp.

This does not prove causality; it says that once the LLM-labeled archetype mix is considered, little systematic metro residual remains.

## Remote Work

Remote work is not usable in this artifact. Scraped 2026 has 40,881 eligible LinkedIn SWE postings, but `is_remote_inferred` is zero for all rows, including the 30,560 metro-rollup rows. Since 2024 remote flags are known artifact zeros and 2026 is also zero here, this field needs a scraper/location-stage audit before any remote-work claim.

## Sensitivity Checks

Aggregator exclusion:

- AI findings are stable: broad-AI mean delta +17.5 pp versus +17.6 pp primary; AI-tool strict +15.9 pp versus +16.1 pp primary.
- Seniority metrics are sensitive at the metro level: 9 of 26 metros have material J1 or J3 changes under aggregator exclusion.
- Requirement breadth is mostly stable, though 4 of 26 metros cross the >30% material-change threshold.

Company cap at 20 postings per company per metro-period:

- AI findings are stable: broad-AI mean delta +17.8 pp; AI-tool strict +16.2 pp.
- Requirement breadth and tech count are stable in almost all metros.
- J3 low-YOE share is more sensitive: 6 of 26 metros are material under the cap.

Arshkon-only 2024 source restriction:

- AI remains positive in all 18 eligible metros.
- Label-based junior shares turn mostly negative.
- Low-YOE shares become mixed.

## Surprises

- New York City has only 70 pooled-2024 eligible postings but more than 2,000 scraped-2026 postings, so its large AI increase is partly a coverage/composition warning.
- Tampa Bay has the largest broad-AI increase despite not being in the tech-hub heuristic group.
- Remote inference is all zero in scraped 2026, not just 2024.
- AI growth is more correlated with requirement-breadth growth than with tech-count growth or seniority shifts.

## Evidence Strength

Strong for broad geographic AI/tool expansion: all 26 eligible metros move upward, and aggregator/company-cap sensitivities are stable.

Moderate for requirement-breadth geography: all metros move upward, but the result is restricted to LLM-text rows and scraped LLM coverage is limited.

Weak-to-mixed for seniority geography: pooled 2024 shows mostly upward junior/low-YOE shares, but arshkon-only source restriction reverses or weakens label-based patterns.

Not usable for remote work until the all-zero 2026 field is audited.

## Data Quality Caveats

- Metro assignment excludes multi-location postings and unresolved locations; this is correct for rollups but changes denominators.
- `search_metro_name` is not used; only `metro_area` is used.
- Asaniczka and arshkon have different geographic and company coverage, which affects pooled-2024 metro baselines.
- Archetype-composition checks are LLM-labeled subset analyses.
- The tech-hub grouping is a heuristic, not an official labor-market classification.

## Action Items

1. Use geography as supporting evidence for broad AI/tool diffusion, not as a lead mechanism.
2. Keep seniority geography secondary and always report arshkon-only sensitivity.
3. Audit `is_remote_inferred` before any remote-work analysis.
4. In formal models, include metro fixed effects or metro-level controls for domain mix, but expect the AI/tool signal to remain broadly positive.
