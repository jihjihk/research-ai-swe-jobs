# T07. External Benchmarks & Power Analysis

## Headline Finding

The main feasibility result is uneven by seniority definition. All-SWE comparisons and broad senior definitions are well powered. Junior analyses are power-bound if they use T30's label-quality primary J1 (`seniority_final = entry`) on arshkon alone: the arshkon-vs-scraped J1 MDE is 11.3 pp for binary outcomes and 0.226 SD for continuous outcomes. Power improves materially with YOE-based junior definitions: J3 is adequate arshkon-only and well powered when 2024 is pooled; J4 is well powered in both source specifications.

Power-only input for Gate 1: J4 is the strongest junior definition and J3 is the better powered stricter low-YOE validator. This does **not** overrule T30's label-quality recommendation of J1 as the clean employer-posted junior label. It means Wave 2/3 junior content analyses should treat J1 as a thin, high-label-quality estimand and pre-plan J3/J4 as powered checks. On the senior side, power and T30's label-quality recommendation align: S1 should be primary, paired with S4. S2 is not usable for inferential director-only analysis.

MDEs are two-sample, two-sided alpha 0.05, 80% power. `MDE_binary` is a conservative absolute percentage-point MDE using worst-case variance at p = 0.5. `MDE_continuous` is a standardized mean difference.

Verdicts:

| verdict | rule |
|---|---|
| well-powered | binary MDE <= 5 pp and continuous MDE <= 0.10 SD |
| adequate | binary MDE <= 8 pp and continuous MDE <= 0.20 SD |
| thin | binary MDE <= 14 pp and continuous MDE <= 0.35 SD |
| underpowered | worse than thin, or a minimum cell below 50 |

## Gate 1 Verdicts

All-SWE analyses can proceed. They are well powered under both arshkon-only and pooled-2024 historical baselines.

Junior analyses need a split decision. J1 is too thin for small effects in arshkon-only comparisons; it can detect only large binary differences around 11 pp or continuous differences around 0.23 SD. J2 adds little power over J1. J3 is the minimum practical powered junior definition for arshkon-only work, and J4 is the power-favorable junior definition. Because J3/J4 are YOE-based rather than employer-posted labels, use them as powered validators rather than silently redefining the junior construct.

Senior analyses should use S1 as primary from both power and label-quality perspectives. S4 is also well powered and should remain the YOE-based validator. S2 director-only is descriptive only; there are 13 arshkon director rows. S3 is also too thin and T30 already flagged it as a title-normalization artifact.

## External Benchmarks

### BLS OEWS State Employment

State benchmark succeeded using official BLS OEWS time-series metadata and the BLS public API:

- metadata: `https://downloadt.bls.gov/pub/time.series/oe/`
- API: `https://api.bls.gov/publicAPI/v2/timeseries/data/`
- OEWS table reference: `https://www.bls.gov/oes/tables.htm`

The requested SOC 15-1256 is not present in the current OEWS occupation metadata. Current metadata includes 15-1252 Software Developers and 15-1253 Software Quality Assurance Analysts and Testers, so I used 15-1253 as the documented substitute for the requested QA/testing companion code.

Pearson correlations between OEWS state employment and our state-level SWE posting counts are high:

|---|---|---:|---:|
| 15-1252 employment | all LinkedIn SWE | 51 | 0.981 |
| 15-1252 employment | scraped 2026 SWE | 51 | 0.975 |
| 15-1252 employment | arshkon SWE | 51 | 0.973 |
| 15-1252 + 15-1253 employment | all LinkedIn SWE | 51 | 0.982 |
| 15-1252 + 15-1253 employment | scraped 2026 SWE | 51 | 0.974 |
| 15-1252 + 15-1253 employment | arshkon SWE | 51 | 0.974 |

This clears the target r > 0.80 geographic sanity check. It should not be read as proof that the sample is representative in levels: OEWS is an employment stock, while our data are LinkedIn postings filtered through historical Kaggle snapshots and a 26-metro scrape.

## What The Sample Represents

The sample represents English, date-valid LinkedIn SWE postings from two 2024 LinkedIn Kaggle snapshots and the current 2026 scraped LinkedIn window. It is strongest for posting-content and posting-composition comparisons within LinkedIn, especially all-SWE, broad senior, and powered low-YOE junior definitions.

It cannot support claims about total SWE employment, unemployment, wages, all job boards, non-US markets, or complete vacancy flow. It also cannot cleanly separate true market change from source-instrument differences without the required arshkon-vs-asaniczka calibration checks and sensitivity analyses.

## Surprises & Unexpected Patterns

J2 barely improves power over J1. Combining `entry` and `associate` sounds like it should solve thin junior cells, but arshkon moves only from 175 to 190 rows and scraped from 1,275 to 1,327.

The director-only senior definition is directionally interesting but inferentially unusable. T30 found S2 moves up, but T07 shows arshkon has only 13 S2 rows.

The geographic correlation with OEWS is extremely high despite the scraped source being a 26-metro design. This supports geographic face validity, but it may mainly reflect the dominance of large SWE states such as CA, TX, NY, WA, VA, and MA.

Null metro exclusions are larger than expected: 31.4% of 2024 rows and 25.2% of 2026 rows are outside the metro-known frame. This matters for any metro heterogeneity claim.

The FRED context is less benign than a simple "AI-era growth" story: information-sector job openings are lower in the latest available observation than in January 2024, while layoffs/discharges are much higher.

## Downstream Actions

Use `feasibility_summary.csv` as the Gate 1 power table. For junior Wave 2/3 analyses, keep T30's J1 as the high-label-quality estimand but do not rely on it alone for modest effects; pair it with J3/J4 and state when a J1 null is power-limited. For senior analyses, use S1 primary and S4 validation; treat S2 and S3 as descriptive/sensitivity only.

Use 25 metros for stricter >=100-per-period metro analysis. Include New York City only in a >=50 threshold specification or a separately flagged sensitivity.

For company-panel work, the 240-company overlap is enough to proceed, but use company weighting/capping or company fixed effects because the panel is heavily skewed toward large 2026 employers.
