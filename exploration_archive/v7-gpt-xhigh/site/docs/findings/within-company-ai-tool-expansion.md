# Returning companies sharply expanded AI and tool language.

<p class="lead">The lead mechanism result is not that new AI-native firms entered the sample. In the overlap panel, the same returning companies changed their posting language.</p>

## Evidence

| Metric in common-company arshkon-to-scraped panel | 2024 | 2026 | Change | Decomposition |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Broad AI prevalence | 3.74% | 23.23% | +19.49 pp | +17.73 pp within, +1.76 pp between |
| AI-tool strict prevalence | 2.23% | 19.80% | +17.57 pp | +16.16 pp within, +1.41 pp between |
| Tech count | 5.45 | 7.19 | +1.74 | +2.06 within, -0.32 between |
| Requirement breadth, LLM text | 7.34 | 9.25 | +1.91 | +2.23 within, -0.33 between |

Source: [T16](../raw/reports/T16.md). T16 uses 240 arshkon-to-scraped overlap companies for AI/tool and tech metrics, and 223 companies with LLM-text rows for requirement breadth.

## Why this matters

This finding is the best evidence that the 2026 language shift is not only market entry by AI-heavy employers. Scraped 2026 new entrants are somewhat more AI-heavy, but returning firms also moved sharply. New entrants and returning firms have nearly identical LLM-text requirement breadth in T16.

## Sensitivities

- Aggregator exclusion leaves the main AI/tool and requirement-breadth directions intact.
- Pooled-2024 versus arshkon-only source choice matters for junior label shares, but not for the AI/tool expansion.
- V1 verified the broad-AI prevalence jump from the shared tech matrix; V2 did not run for T16.

## Boundaries

T16 measures posting-language mentions, not requirement force. T22 failed, so this page does not claim the new AI/tool language is screened, required, or tied to hiring outcomes.
