    T06 — Company concentration deep investigation¶

    Agent: Wave 1 Agent C Scope: SWE postings, LinkedIn only, is_english = true, date_flag = 'ok' Inputs: data/unified.parquet Script: exploration/scripts/T06_company_concentration.py, T06_figures.py

    Headline (read this section before Wave 2)¶

    1. Company concentration is moderate-to-high in every source (scraped Gini ≈ 0.69, top-20 share 18%). It is actively high enough to drive corpus-level term frequencies, topic models, and anything that aggregates mentions-per-posting.
    2. Entry-level posting is highly concentrated on a small subset of companies. 79% of arshkon companies, 89% of asaniczka companies, and 79% of scraped companies with ≥5 SWE postings post ZERO entry-labeled roles (under seniority_final). The aggregate "entry share" is driven by a small group of entry-active companies, not a market-wide behavior.
    3. The entry-share change on the arshkon ↔ scraped overlap panel flips sign depending on operationalization. Under seniority_final: arshkon 6.65% → scraped 5.74%, a -0.9 pp DECLINE with within-company component = -3.2 pp. Under YOE ≤ 2: arshkon 12.3% → scraped 19.7%, a +7.4 pp INCREASE with within-company component = +1.1 pp (the rest is composition). These are the same companies. The operationalization is what changes the sign. Downstream agents MUST report both.
    4. Stage 4 fixed the duplicate-template problem. No within-source company has a dup_ratio that violates the ≈1.0 target after multi-location collapse. The prior "6 companies with 4-25 identical descriptions" issue is gone. Residual dup_ratios in scraped (Turing 3.0, DataAnnotation 2.7, Mercor 1.8) are small and are real repostings over the scrape window, not group artifacts.
    5. Cross-company hash collisions are small — top collision is 6 companies sharing one description (likely an aggregator reposting under alternate company labels not caught by the canonicalizer). Worth noting but not structural.
    6. Top-6 scraped entry-final companies account for 18.8% of the entry pool (vs the prior run's 23%). TikTok + ByteDance alone are 10.4% of the entry pool. The prior run's finding was substantially driven by description duplication; the current finding is driven by genuine entry-poster concentration plus one ByteDance-group spike. Treat the scraped entry pool as a small-number problem even after the fix.

    5. Within-company vs between-company decomposition¶

    decomposition_overlap_panel.csv, overlap_panel_info.csv, figure within_vs_between_decomposition.png.

    Panel: 125 companies with ≥5 SWE in BOTH arshkon and scraped. Total arshkon rows in panel = 1381. Total scraped rows in panel = 9060. This is an asymmetric panel — the same company has ~6.6× more postings in 2026 than 2024, so aggregate means shift with the weight change even when within-company metrics don't.

    metric n companies arshkon panel mean scraped panel mean total change within between residual
    entry share (seniority_final) 115 0.0665 0.0574 -0.0091 -0.0320 +0.0229 0
    entry share (YOE ≤ 2) 121 0.1228 0.1965 +0.0737 +0.0112 +0.0626 0
    mean desc length 125 3489 5255 +1766 +963 +803 0
    AI mention rate 125 0.0275 0.1832 +0.1557 +0.1477 +0.0080 0

    Reading the decomposition¶

    Entry share — sign flips. - Under seniority_final: within-company entry share fell 3.2 pp, but the mix of companies in the panel shifted toward higher-entry companies, bringing the aggregate only -0.9 pp. If you held composition constant, every company on average hired fewer entry-labeled roles. - Under YOE ≤ 2: within-company rose only 1.1 pp; the overwhelming driver is composition (+6.3 pp). If you held composition constant, the YOE ≤ 2 share barely moved. - The two operationalizations disagree on the direction of within-company change at the same company-year panel. This is the most important disagreement in the entire Wave 1 exploration.

    Most likely mechanisms (to be investigated in Wave 2): 1. Classifier shift. Stage 5 title keywords changed between pipeline versions, or Stage 10's LLM is stricter than the arshkon native classifier. A row that arshkon labeled entry at YOE ≤ 2 now gets mid-senior under seniority_final. This would simultaneously decrease seniority_final entry share and leave YOE ≤ 2 unchanged. This is consistent with the pattern. 2. Employer relabeling. Companies post the same YOE range under a mid-level title now that they used to post under an entry title. This would also decrease seniority_final entry share and increase or leave flat YOE ≤ 2, which is what we see. 3. Real scope inflation. Companies ask for 2 YOE but call the role "Software Engineer II" now that they used to call "New Grad." This is RQ1 in its pure form and also fits the pattern.

    Nothing in the data distinguishes these three. All three predict the observed disagreement. Wave 2 needs a title×YOE contingency for the panel companies to pick between them.

    Description length. Within-company (+963) is larger than between-company (+803) and accounts for 55% of the total change. This is good news — it means length inflation is a within-company phenomenon, not a composition effect. Same Anduril, same JPMorgan, same Wells Fargo write longer descriptions now than they did in 2024.

    AI mention rate. Within-company (+14.8 pp) is 95% of total change. AI mentions are overwhelmingly within-company; composition contributes essentially nothing. The "AI mentions are exploding" story is robust to composition.

    Implication¶

    Any Wave 2 finding that depends on entry share direction must cite both operationalizations and both decomposition components. The agent recommends: report seniority_final as the primary result, report YOE ≤ 2 as the proxy, and report the within-company decomposition on the overlap panel as the cleanest single estimate of the effect. Headline: under seniority_final, within-company entry share dropped 3.2 pp at the same 115 companies that appear in both panels; under YOE ≤ 2, it rose 1.1 pp at 121 companies. The direction flip is itself the finding.

    8. Per-finding concentration prediction table (MOST IMPORTANT OUTPUT)¶

    exploration/tables/T06/concentration_predictions.csv (written below). Downstream Wave 2/3 agents should read this before running any corpus-level analysis.

    Columns: - metric: the downstream analysis category - unit: row, company, company-term, etc. - concentration_risk: LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH — how much a handful of companies can swing the aggregate - direction_risk: can the direction flip under a reasonable re-weighting? - recommended_default: what the agent should do as primary specification - essential_sensitivity: what alternative must be reported alongside - evidence: T06 section that justifies the classification

    metric unit concentration_risk direction_risk recommended_default essential_sensitivity evidence
    entry share (seniority_final) row HIGH YES (flips sign vs YOE proxy) arshkon-vs-scraped on overlap panel, within-company decomposition report YOE ≤ 2 side-by-side; cap at 50/company as robustness §4, §5
    entry share (YOE ≤ 2) row HIGH partial (sign doesn't flip but magnitude moves ~60% between with/without top-2 companies) overlap panel, within-component report seniority_final; exclude Walmart/Google/Microsoft as sensitivity §2, §5
    AI mention rate (binary) row LOW no (SNR=925, within-component 95% of total) arshkon vs scraped, all rows aggregator exclusion §5
    mean description length row MEDIUM no (within-component = 55% of total) arshkon vs scraped; within-panel as sensitivity use description_core_llm; cap at 50/company §5, T05 §1
    term frequency (corpus) company-term HIGH yes (top company contributes 4% of corpus, top-20 contribute 18%) cap at 50 postings per company, aggregate at company-term level, re-normalize no-cap sensitivity; company-name stoplist (preamble) §1, §2
    topic model prevalence row HIGH yes (topic scores are corpus-weighted) cap at 30-50 postings per company before fitting; report topic × company Gini no-cap sensitivity on topic size ordering §1, §2
    co-occurrence network edges company-term-pair HIGH yes (single company's boilerplate template produces a dense internal clique) binary per-posting, then collapse to company-level edge before aggregation show edge weight distribution across companies contributing to each edge §2, §3
    tech stack prevalence (binary mentions per posting) row MEDIUM partial (top-20 companies tilt toward specific stacks) cap at 50/company report per-company presence (did any of >=5 posts mention X) §2
    "scope inflation" indicators (ownership, lead, end-to-end) row HIGH yes (3 top-20 companies have boilerplate "ownership" language) cap at 50/company + validate keyword on 50-row sample per period preamble §6 (keyword validation) §2, T05 §8
    "management language" indicators row HIGH yes (same 3 companies + Leidos template drive the signal) cap at 50/company; exclude aggregators validation sample §2
    ghost-job rate row MEDIUM partial (aggregators have inflated ghost rates) exclude aggregators as primary all-rows sensitivity §6
    geographic / metro distribution row MEDIUM no (states differ cross-source but ordering is stable) as-is with multi-location excluded — T05 §3
    YOE distribution (mean, median, quantiles) row HIGH yes (Walmart 73.8% yoe≤2 = 3.6% of entire yoe≤2 pool) cap at 50/company as primary, no-cap sensitivity report YOE distribution per top-20 company §2
    company industry mix company LOW no (company-level unit of analysis) as-is — T05 §6
    seniority_final × title cells row HIGH yes (title vocabulary turns over 97%; per-title cells are tiny) collapse titles to 20-30 families before comparing report within-family n T05 §5
    aggregator-adjusted entry share row HIGH partial report both is_aggregator=true and false splits; never combine without showing — §6
    new-entrant vs returning comparison company MEDIUM no present separately from returning; do not pool — §7

    Top-3 findings most likely to be concentration-driven and flip direction under a reasonable re-weighting, plus recommended handling:

    1. Entry share cross-period change. Sign depends on operationalization and on whether you use the overlap panel vs. aggregate. Recommendation: present seniority_final and YOE ≤ 2 side by side on the 125-company overlap panel, with within-company decomposition as the primary estimate. Absolutely do not present aggregate entry share pooled across 2024 sources (T05 SNR = 0.33).

    2. Corpus term frequencies for "junior/entry/scope inflation" language. A single well-known company with a boilerplate template can move a term frequency by several percentage points. Recommendation: cap at 50 postings per company_name_canonical as primary; run once without capping as sensitivity; use company-name stoplist from preamble.

    3. Topic model topic ordering and prevalence. BERTopic/NMF topic sizes reflect corpus weights. With scraped top-20 = 18% of corpus, a topic can be "the Walmart topic" or "the Jobs-via-Dice topic" with high probability. Recommendation: fit on a capped corpus (50/company), report topic × top-company concentration before interpreting any topic as a "market signal."

    Essential sensitivities applied¶

    • (a) Aggregator exclusion — applied throughout §1, §6, and within the prediction table.
    • (b) Company capping — this task's subject; not re-applied as sensitivity. The prediction table is the deliverable for when capping is appropriate downstream.

    Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. The entry-share sign flip under seniority_final vs YOE proxy survives the overlap-panel decomposition. This is not a sampling artifact; it is a classifier-vs-proxy disagreement on the same 115 companies. Wave 2 needs to figure out which side is right (it probably cannot, without hand-labeling).
    2. Walmart contributes 73.8% of its own YOE ≤ 2 rate, accounting for ~3.6% of the entire scraped YOE ≤ 2 pool. I did not expect one retailer to be this dominant in a "junior share" metric.
    3. 79-89% of substantive-presence companies post zero entry-labeled roles in every source. This is the single most important framing for RQ1: "entry share" is a metric about a small subset of the employer population, not the median company.
    4. Stage 4 fix worked cleanly. Dup_ratio = 1.0 for all scraped entry-posting companies. The prior-run's "6 companies with identical descriptions" is no longer visible, which means the concentration pattern we see now (top-6 = 18.8% of the entry pool) is the genuine concentration pattern.
    5. TikTok + ByteDance alone = 10.4% of the scraped entry pool. This is a single corporate family. If ByteDance corporate policy changes, the scraped entry pool moves several percentage points.
    6. AI mention rate is the one finding that concentration does not threaten. Within-component is 95% of the total change, and the effect is 50×-400× larger than any other metric. This is the most concentration-robust finding in the dataset.
    7. Cross-company hash collisions are small (max 6 companies) but real. Probably aggregator relabeling under alternate brand names. A follow-up canonicalizer pass should fold them.

    Data quality caveats¶

    • company_name_canonical is imperfect — some of the "new 2026" 5916 companies are almost certainly returning companies under a different brand label. This would inflate the between-company composition component.
    • seniority_final is unknown for a substantial share of rows, and the unknown share differs by source. All entry-share numbers exclude unknowns; the unknown share itself is diagnostic and should be tracked separately.
    • Aggregator detection depends on Stage 4 rules; false negatives are possible (e.g., small aggregators not in the rule list).
    • YOE extraction via regex covers only ~60-70% of postings; the yoe_le2 share is always computed over the YOE-present subset and is sensitive to which companies write explicit YOE floors.
