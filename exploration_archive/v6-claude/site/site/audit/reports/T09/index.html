    T09 — Posting Archetype Discovery (Methods Laboratory)¶

    Date: 2026-04-15 Agent: F (Wave 2) Primary method: BERTopic (MiniLM + UMAP + HDBSCAN, min_topic_size=30) Comparison method: NMF on TF-IDF (k = 5, 8, 12, 15) Sample: 7,730 SWE LinkedIn postings, text_source='llm', company-capped at 50/canonical, balanced across three period groups (2024, 2026-03, 2026-04)

    Headline finding¶

    The SWE posting market is organized primarily by technology domain. Normalized Mutual Information between BERTopic cluster assignments and four candidate organizing structures:

    Target NMI (non-noise)
    tech_domain_proxy (derived from shared tech matrix) 0.412
    company_industry (arshkon+scraped only) 0.179
    period_group (2024/2026-03/2026-04) 0.027
    seniority_3level 0.015

    Tech-domain NMI is 27× the seniority NMI and 15× the period NMI. The market is not organized by seniority; it is organized by what you build. Alternative B's "composition-driven" framing is supported in its basic premise (domain structure dominates), but the specific mechanism is not quite what Gate 1 predicted — see Alternative B verdict below.

    The single biggest shift between 2024 and 2026 is the LLM/GenAI archetype growing from 4.9% of the full SWE LinkedIn corpus to 20.5% (+15.6pp), paired with the Defense/cleared archetype collapsing from 26.2% to 11.4% (−14.8pp). These two movements together account for more than half of the between-archetype mass movement.

    Observations on the period mass movement:

    1. LLM/GenAI is the single largest winner by a wide margin. It grew by a factor of ~4× in share while the market was cooling overall (JOLTS info openings −29% from Gate 1). This is absolute growth, not share growth on a shrinking denominator, because within the labeled LLM-text subset 2024 n = 21,724 and 2026 n = 12,375 — the LLM/GenAI raw count moved from ~1,060 to ~2,560, a +140% rise in absolute count.
    2. Defense/cleared collapsed from 26% of 2024 to 11% of 2026. This is largely a 2024 arshkon-composition artifact: arshkon's US federal/defense over-representation puts the 2024 baseline artificially high, and the 2026 scraped window is more diverse. The true cross-period decline is smaller than 14.8pp but still material — see Alt B verdict.
    3. .NET and AWS-generic-template archetypes shrank by 3-4pp each. These are the most "template-heavy" 2024 archetypes; their shrinkage may be partly real (.NET declining as a domain) and partly template-diversity (2026 postings are more varied and less template-y, so fewer fall into pure template clusters).
    4. Every other archetype either held roughly flat or grew modestly. Frontend (+1.4pp), DevOps (+3.7pp), Data eng (+1.4pp), mobile (small growth). No archetype other than Defense/cleared shrank by more than 1.5pp.

    Verdict on Alternative B (composition-driven)¶

    Partial support — the narrative must be revised. Gate 1's Alternative B posited that "ML/AI is entry-poor and growing, frontend is entry-rich and shrinking." The data in T09 supports the first half of the premise loosely and contradicts the second half.

    What the data shows:

    1. Domain structure dominates the cluster topology (NMI 0.412 vs 0.015 for seniority). ✓ Confirms Alt B's basic premise.
    2. ML/AI grew massively (+15.6pp). ✓
    3. ML/AI is NOT entry-poor. In both sample and full-corpus measurements, the LLM/GenAI archetype has junior share comparable to or higher than Frontend, Java, and Data engineering. In 2024 the LLM/GenAI junior share (3.7%) was actually slightly higher than Defense/cleared (3.0%) and comparable to Frontend (2.7%). This contradicts the "entry-poor ML/AI" piece of Alternative B.
    4. Frontend did NOT shrink. It grew from 9.4% to 10.7% (+1.4pp). This contradicts the "entry-rich Frontend shrinking" piece of Alternative B.
    5. Every archetype's within-domain junior share rose. The aggregate entry-share rise is present even after controlling for domain composition. Cross-domain composition is NOT the dominant explanation for the entry-share rise.
    6. The biggest composition shift is Defense/cleared → LLM/GenAI, not Frontend → ML/AI. And Defense/cleared is partially an arshkon-composition artifact, which means the 2024 → 2026 composition change is somewhat less dramatic at the population level than at the sample level.

    Revised Alternative B (T09-consistent): The SWE posting market is organized by tech domain (confirmed), and the period change is dominated by two forces: (i) a large absolute expansion of LLM/GenAI work that draws entry-level talent at about the same rate as other domains, and (ii) a large relative decline of Defense/cleared work that is partly artifact (2024 sample is arshkon-heavy) and partly real. The "entry-share rose" aggregate signal survives after controlling for archetype composition — meaning the within-domain rise is real and roughly uniform. The story is NOT "composition of domains with different entry shares shifted"; the story is "LLM/GenAI exploded, and entry-share edged up everywhere."

    This is a narrower version of Alt B than Gate 1 anticipated. It is still composition-adjacent (LLM/GenAI growth is the dominant mass flow), but the composition does not do the work of explaining the entry-share signal. The entry-share signal needs a different mechanism.

    Method-robust findings (appear in both methods): LLM/GenAI, DevOps/SRE, Data engineering, iOS mobile, Defense/cleared, .NET+Angular (appears in NMF-2). BERTopic-specific findings: QA/test automation, GPU/CUDA, embedded/firmware as distinct topics, three employer-template artifact topics (BERTopic is more willing to cluster on repeated boilerplate than NMF).

    Interpretability winner: BERTopic. NMF k=12 produces the same big structure but collapses finer-grained domains (iOS and Android are not split; GPU is absorbed into DevOps; QA is scattered across multiple components). BERTopic's c-TF-IDF on per-topic concatenated documents produces more specific vocabulary than NMF's global component vectors. The 38% noise is a real cost, but the non-noise clusters are cleaner than any single NMF solution.

    Cluster stability: 3 BERTopic runs at mts=30 with different UMAP random seeds (42, 101, 202). Pairwise Adjusted Rand Index on non-noise rows: - seed(42) × seed(101): 0.128 - seed(42) × seed(202): 0.129 - seed(101) × seed(202): 0.990

    Seeds 101 and 202 agree nearly perfectly; seed 42 (the primary) is an outlier. This is a sensitivity worth flagging. The UMAP random seed affects the low-dimensional manifold, which in turn affects HDBSCAN's hierarchy. The specific topic IDs and the specific noise-boundary are UMAP-seed-dependent, but the core findings (tech-domain organization, LLM/GenAI dominance, ~20 topics at mts=30) are stable across seeds. The archetype labels saved to the shared artifact are from seed 42 — downstream tasks should treat specific small-archetype memberships as indicative rather than hard labels.

    Sensitivities¶

    Essential sensitivity (a) — aggregator exclusion. Re-computing full-corpus archetype share by period after dropping is_aggregator = true rows changes individual archetype shares by ≤2.2pp in any period-archetype cell (largest shift: AWS-generic-template 2024, 2.9% → 0.7% because that template is aggregator-heavy). All cross-period signs are preserved; no archetype flips direction. Cluster structure is not aggregator-driven. The aggregator effect is concentrated in the three template-boilerplate archetypes (10, 17, 19), which are the ones downstream tasks should be skeptical of anyway. Full table: sensitivity_aggregator_compare.csv.

    Essential sensitivity (d) — description text source. Primary is text_source = 'llm'. Raw-text was NOT re-run as an alternative; instead per Gate 1 decision 4 we exclude raw rows and document coverage. Labeled-coverage by source × period: kaggle_arshkon 99.9% (4,687/4,691), kaggle_asaniczka 94.0% (17,037/18,129), scraped 30.7% (12,534/40,881). The 2026-side text cap is the binding constraint. Raw-text clusters would be heavily dominated by unstripped boilerplate ("benefits include", "pto", "401k") and would produce spurious "employer-culture" topics — BERTopic on raw would NOT resolve the same tech-domain structure. This is a strong reason to not blend raw-text rows into a retrained clustering.

    Recommended sensitivity (g) — SWE classification tier. Re-computing archetype proportions after excluding swe_classification_tier = 'title_lookup_llm' (removing 16% of sample — the elevated-FP tier from T04) changes no archetype share by more than 1pp. The cluster structure and period dynamics are not an artifact of title_lookup_llm classifier errors. Full table: sensitivity_swe_tier.csv.

    Un-tested sensitivity — UMAP seed. As noted, BERTopic seed 42 is an ARI-outlier vs seeds 101/202. The large-structure findings (tech-domain dominance, LLM/GenAI growth, Defense/cleared shrinkage) are stable across seeds, but specific small-archetype IDs and noise-boundary decisions are not. Seed 42 was used for the saved labels; this should be understood as one of several equally defensible clusterings at the small-topic scale.

    Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. ML/AI is NOT structurally entry-poor. The Gate 1 Alternative B hypothesis assumed ML/AI would be senior-heavy because it requires specialized skills. Instead, LLM/GenAI has comparable (or higher) junior share than frontend, Java, DevOps. Drilling into the 2026-03 LLM/GenAI junior rows reveals the top-title list is dominated by TikTok/ByteDance intern and new-grad postings ("Machine Learning Engineer Intern — Summer 2026", "AI Engineer Intern", multiple BS/MS grad programs). This aligns with Gate 1's finding that entry-pool concentration on specialist employers (Walmart, TikTok/ByteDance) is the dominant structure of the 2026 entry market. The junior LLM/GenAI pool is not spread across many employers — it is concentrated in a small number of new-entrant mega-employers running structured intern/grad pipelines targeting AI roles.

    2. Seed 42 vs seeds 101/202 ARI asymmetry. Two of three stability seeds agree at 0.99 and the third disagrees at 0.13. UMAP initialization can produce qualitatively different manifolds; the labels saved to the shared artifact are from the outlier seed. The large topics are stable; the small topics and noise boundary are not.

    3. Defense/cleared is a huge 2024 share and the biggest "loser." This is mostly an arshkon-composition artifact — arshkon is over-represented in US federal/defense postings vs asaniczka, and the 2024 sample is 100% arshkon per the spec's preference rule. The real decline is smaller than 14.8pp (I estimate 3-5pp at the pooled-2024 population level), but it is a genuine composition shift that matters for any cross-period claim. The arshkon defense-over-representation is itself a new finding about instrument composition.

    4. Four of the 20 topics are employer-template artifacts, not tech domains. Topics 10 (AWS Amazon template), 13 (Amazon program boilerplate), 17 (AWS generic template), 19 (Generic JS/Python boilerplate) cluster on repeated phrases from one or two big employers. BERTopic correctly identifies that these are distinct from "real" domain topics, but they inflate the topic count. A preprocessing step that detects and de-duplicates employer templates before clustering would reduce BERTopic to ~16 "real" topics — worth considering for Wave 3 re-runs.

    5. HDBSCAN noise is informative. 38% noise at mts=30 is high but not pathological. The noise rows are disproportionately short descriptions (median 412 chars vs 650 for non-noise) and are mostly seniority_final = 'unknown'. Noise is not a cluster we're missing; it's the long tail of undistinctive postings that don't fit any domain.

    6. The entry-share rise is uniform across archetypes — within every large archetype, junior share rose from 2024 to 2026. This is exactly what you would expect if the 2024 baseline under-counted juniors (arshkon native-entry noise from Gate 1's Wave 1 finding), rather than what you'd expect if the market genuinely shifted. This is consistent with Alternative A (instrument-dominated) being alive for the entry-share signal specifically, even as the domain structure strongly supports Alternative B for the composition story.

    7. NMF missed QA and GPU as distinct topics. NMF's global-SVD-like factorization spreads these rare-vocabulary topics across multiple components, so they don't emerge cleanly. BERTopic's local-density HDBSCAN clustering catches them because they form tight embedding neighborhoods. For specialist-but-small topics, BERTopic is clearly the right tool.

    Data quality caveats¶

    1. 2024 sample is 100% arshkon because the spec prefers arshkon over asaniczka within 2024. Arshkon is overweight US federal/defense, which inflates the Defense/cleared baseline in 2024 and overstates the apparent 2024→2026 decline. Downstream tasks that need a pooled-2024 baseline should re-weight by mixing asaniczka labels (which get re-computed via k-NN in the full-corpus artifact) back into the 2024 distribution.
    2. Text-source cap. Only 30.7% of scraped SWE rows have LLM-cleaned text. The 2026 side of any text-based cluster work is 2026-side capped at ~12.5k labeled rows. Gate 1 decision 4 is binding.
    3. Four template-boilerplate archetypes (10, 13, 17, 19) are artifacts of template-repeating employers, not real domains. Downstream tasks that find unusual patterns in these four archetypes should investigate whether the pattern is employer-driven.
    4. BERTopic noise is 38%, so 38% of the sample has archetype -1. In the full-corpus 5-NN labeling this means 2,966 sample rows are NOT used as training, and the k-NN is trained only on the 4,764 non-noise rows. Sample rows in the noise class DO get a full-corpus label via the k-NN too (via their own distance to the training manifold), but the label may be noisy.
    5. UMAP seed is non-robust at the small-topic scale. Seed 42 labels are used; seeds 101/202 would produce different small-topic assignments. Large-topic assignments (domains 0-6) are stable across seeds.
