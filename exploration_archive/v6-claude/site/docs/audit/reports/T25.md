This report is the meta-document for T25. The five artifacts themselves live in `exploration/artifacts/interview/`; each has a caveats-applied interpretive note in that directory's README. This report explains (a) what the artifacts are meant to do, (b) how I selected them from the full body of exploration findings, (c) the per-artifact data provenance, and (d) the planned interview-phase use.

## Purpose

These artifacts are NOT finished analysis figures. They are **data-prompted interview stimuli** for the RQ4 qualitative phase. The distinction matters: each artifact is chosen because it encodes a finding that is non-obvious, can be shown in a single glance, and that an interviewee can plausibly be asked to explain, confirm, or contest. They are calibration material for a qualitative researcher entering conversations with hiring managers, recruiters, and senior SWE ICs.

Selection criteria (in priority order):
1. The artifact encodes a core finding from Gate 3 that survived V2 verification.
2. The stimulus is visually compact and a non-expert can parse the main point in under 30 seconds.
3. The finding is surprising or counterintuitive enough to generate reflection in the interviewee.
4. The interviewee has plausible access to the mechanism — they are in a position to explain WHY the pattern exists, not just whether it exists.
5. The probe is specific and testable qualitatively — not a vague "tell us about AI in hiring."

## Artifact selection rationale

The four core findings identified in Gate 3 / V2 / SYNTHESIS are:
1. RQ3 inverted (posting language LAGS worker AI adoption)
2. Senior technical orchestration specialization (+98% / +156%)
3. Tool-stack restructuring (field-wide, 92% within-company, bifurcated)
4. Aggregate junior-narrowing null; within-archetype credential convergence surviving

These four findings generate a natural set of five artifacts:

1. **RQ3 inverted** → `inverted_rq3_divergence.png` + per-tool panel (Finding 1 directly visualized).
2. **Senior orchestration** → `senior_orchestration_shift.png` with AI × senior interaction (Finding 2 directly visualized).
3. **Tool-stack restructuring at the company level** → two complementary artifacts:
   - `archetype_pivot.png` shows the 74.6% pivot rate at the corpus level with illustrative company examples.
   - `paired_company_jds.csv` shows the same companies at the text level.
4. **Authorship style shift** → `authorship_style_shift.png` (the mechanism candidate unifying T13/T15/T29).

Finding 4 (junior null + credential convergence) was NOT selected for a dedicated artifact. Rationale: it is a methodological contribution best communicated through a specification-dependence table or a sensitivity bar, neither of which generates a strong qualitative probe. The T25 brief calls for interview elicitation, and non-findings do not elicit well in interview settings. The credential-stack convergence result is best presented in the paper's tables, not in a qualitative stimulus.

## Artifact 1 — `inverted_rq3_divergence.png`

**Finding.** RQ3 inverted. Worker AI any-use is 80.8% (Stack Overflow 2025 professional developers). SWE posting broad AI rate is 28.6%, narrow AI 34.6%, AI-as-tool 6.9%, and hard AI requirement (AI mentioned in the requirements section specifically) 6.0%. The paper's lead empirical claim. Direction holds across the 50-85% worker-rate sensitivity range.

**Data source.**
- Posting rates: `exploration/tables/T23/posting_ai_rates_by_period.csv` (T23 primary) and `divergence_headline.csv`.
- Per-tool: `exploration/tables/T23/per_tool_divergence.csv`.
- Worker rate: SO 2025 AI survey via T23 benchmarks.json.
- Sensitivity band: `exploration/tables/T23/benchmark_sensitivity.csv`.
- Hard-requirement rate: T22 section-classifier output (6.0%).

**Gate 3 narrowings applied.**
- Broad AI gap (−52.2 pp, ratio 0.35) is presented as the lead number. Per-tool ratios are shown but labeled "approximate" because the SO 2025 per-tool figures are extrapolations from the "AI agent user" subset multiplied by 80.8% any-use. The 108× ChatGPT ratio is NOT displayed (denominator near zero, reviewer-vulnerable).
- Sensitivity band (50-85%) is shown as a shaded region so the interviewee can see that the direction holds even at the lowest plausible worker rate.

**Interview probe.** "Your company uses AI tools daily but the job posting barely names them. Why do you think that is?"

**Target interviewees.** Hiring managers and recruiters at tool-stack adopter companies (Adobe, AT&T, Deloitte, American Express, Aditi Consulting); any pro-dev who has applied to an SWE posting in 2025/2026.

**Expected responses to elicit.** Template lag; intentional tool-neutrality ("we want people who pick the best tool"); HR/legal concerns about naming specific vendors; "we expect developers to figure out AI on their own"; recruiter unfamiliarity with tool names.

## Artifact 2 — `senior_orchestration_shift.png`

**Finding.** Senior SWE roles specialized toward hands-on technical orchestration. Mid-senior tech-orch density +98% (0.168 → 0.332 per 1K chars), director tech-orch +156% (0.118 → 0.302). Director people-management fell 21%; mid-senior people-management rose 25%. The AI × senior interaction is ENTIRELY in the orchestration profile: in 2026, AI-mentioning senior postings have +76% orchestration density over non-AI senior postings, with IDENTICAL people-management density.

**Data source.**
- Density table: `exploration/tables/T21/density_by_profile_period_seniority.csv`.
- AI × senior interaction: `exploration/tables/T21/ai_senior_interaction.csv`.
- Validated pattern bundle: `exploration/artifacts/shared/validated_mgmt_patterns.json`.

**Gate 3 narrowings applied.**
- Per V1 correction 2 re-corrected by T21's rebuild: narrow object-noun-phrase people-management patterns DO pass ≥80% precision. The +25% mid-senior rise is reported with the caveat that the `mentor` sub-pattern is style-correlated (T29) — some fraction is template, some is real.
- The orchestration rise is NOT style-correlated and is presented as the clean signal.
- V2 verified the +98% / +156% numbers to 4 decimal places with 100% precision on a 100-row audit.
- V2 Alt 2 confirmed +120% director orchestration even after excluding the top 10 AI-heavy director companies.

**Interview probe.** "Did your director-level role actually become more hands-on technical since 2024, or is that just how you write the posting now?"

**Target interviewees.** Senior individual contributors (staff, principal, distinguished) and directors who moved between people-management and IC tracks between 2024 and 2026; their recruiters.

**Expected responses to elicit.** (a) Real role change driven by AI adoption — directors are expected to drive architecture reviews, prompt engineering, agentic workflow design. (b) Template-level rewriting — the posting changed but the daily work didn't. (c) Flattening of the senior-to-director distinction. (d) Staff-ladder adoption as a response to the distinction flattening.

## Artifact 3 — `archetype_pivot.png`

**Finding.** 74.6% of overlap-panel companies pivoted their dominant T09 archetype between 2024 and 2026 (179 of 240). Holds at 73.2% under ≥5-per-period threshold. Median per-company total-variation distance is 0.629 — half the companies redistribute more than 63% of their archetype mass. Companies are not just growing their posting count; they are reconfiguring *what kind* of SWE they hire.

**Data source.**
- Pivot table: `exploration/tables/T16/archetype_dominant_pivot.csv`.
- Per-company TVD: `exploration/tables/T16/archetype_tvd_per_company.csv`.
- Cluster typology: `exploration/tables/T16/company_typology_k4.csv`.

**Illustrative companies shown:** AT&T (Java enterprise → LLM/GenAI), Adobe, American Express, Deloitte, Aditi Consulting. All are cluster-3 tool-stack adopter members from T16.

**Gate 3 narrowings applied.**
- V2 re-derived at 71.7% on a slightly different denominator (223 panel companies with both-year labels, dropping the no-text bucket). Within 3 pp of the 74.6% headline; pivot rate framing holds.
- 30.5% scraped-side archetype label coverage is a known caveat. Pivot rate is presented as directional evidence of within-company portfolio restructuring, not as a precise point estimate.

**Interview probe.** "Your company changed what kind of SWE you post for between 2024 and 2026 — why?"

**Target interviewees.** Hiring managers at cluster-3 companies (Deloitte, AT&T, Amex, Aditi) and at the LLM/GenAI new-entrant companies (Anthropic, Microsoft AI, Alignerr, Harvey).

**Expected responses to elicit.** (a) Client demand shift (consulting explanation). (b) AI skill signaling — the company wants to appear AI-forward. (c) Real portfolio reconfiguration — fewer JS/Java roles, more LLM/agentic roles. (d) M&A driven reorganization. (e) The pivot is an artifact of how/which postings happen to be live at scrape time.

## Artifact 4 — `authorship_style_shift.png`

**Finding.** T29 authorship score shifted +0.33 std between 2024 and 2026. 88.7% of 2026 postings score above the 2024 median. 3.9% fall below 2024 p25. Distribution translated upward without compression (variance barely changed: std 0.331 → 0.339). Top 2026 LLM-score companies include Alignerr, Walmart, LinkedIn, Intuit, Intel, Ford, Rippling, Microsoft AI, Cognizant, Harvey, Anthropic, Microsoft. Walmart was already high-score in 2024 and rose further. AI explosion survives style matching at 0-7% attenuation — the authorship shift does NOT undermine the AI finding.

**Data source.**
- Period stats: `exploration/tables/T29/period_stats.csv`.
- Per-posting scores: `exploration/tables/T29/authorship_scores.csv`.
- Top companies: `exploration/tables/T29/top_llm_companies_2024.csv`, `top_llm_companies_2026.csv`.

**Gate 3 narrowings applied.**
- Per V2 narrowing 1, I do NOT present the style-matched char_len "flip to −411 chars" claim. The sign-flip is feature-set dependent. The figure presents only the raw distribution shift (+0.33 std) which is robust.
- Raw-text sensitivity (T29 section 9) cuts the median shift roughly in half to ~+0.07. The figure uses the cleaned-text distribution (which is what T29's primary run used) with a caption note that ~50% of the shift may be instrument-driven (Kaggle unformatted vs scraped markdown bullet density).
- The interpretation shifts from "length flips under matching" (dropped) to "length growth is mostly style migration, authorship drift is a first-class longitudinal confound" (retained).

**Interview probe.** "Are you using LLM drafting tools (ChatGPT, Claude, a recruiter tool like Gem/Beamery/Paradox) to write postings? When did you start?"

**Target interviewees.** Top-LLM-score 2026 company recruiters (Alignerr, Intuit, LinkedIn, Intel, Harvey, Microsoft AI). Also low-LLM-score companies for contrast — who is still writing postings by hand and why.

**Expected responses to elicit.** (a) Yes, adopted recruiter-LLM tools in 2024-2025. (b) Company policy disallows. (c) Used for first drafts but heavily edited. (d) Unaware of the tools. (e) Specific tool names — useful for the analysis phase's company-level authorship classifier (H8 in T24).

## Artifact 5 — `paired_company_jds.csv` + `paired_company_jds.md`

**Finding.** Same-company paired job descriptions from 5 tool-stack adopter cluster companies (Adobe, AT&T, American Express, Aditi Consulting, Deloitte), showing the 2024 → 2026 text-level shift directly.

**Data source.** `data/unified.parquet`, `description_core_llm` column. For each company × period, the longest available posting that passes the default SWE LinkedIn filter was selected. Both CSV and human-readable Markdown formats are produced (the Markdown is formatted for printing in interview sessions as stimulus material).

**Gate 3 narrowings applied.**
- 2024 sources are kaggle_arshkon / kaggle_asaniczka (HTML-stripped); 2026 is scraped (preserves markdown). Length difference is partly instrument. The interviewer should be prepared for the interviewee to comment on format changes (bullets, headers) as well as content changes.
- The paired postings are not guaranteed to be the same role — we selected the longest per period within the same company. For interview use, this is a feature: the interviewee can see WHICH kinds of roles their company posts in each period.

**Interview probe.** "Between 2024 and 2026 your posting added AI language — what actually changed in the work? Point to the part that is meaningfully new vs the part that is just phrasing."

**Target interviewees.** Hiring managers or recruiters at the specific 5 companies (Adobe, AT&T, Amex, Aditi, Deloitte) who recognize their own company's posting templates.

**Expected responses to elicit.** (a) Specific technologies added; (b) responsibility-level changes (architectural, orchestration); (c) template vs content distinction; (d) "I don't write those postings — our vendor does"; (e) "That's the template from 2023 — we've updated again since"; (f) AI skill-signaling to clients.

## Interview methodology notes (for the RQ4 phase agent)

Each artifact has ONE primary probe. Do not chain multiple probes off a single artifact; the interviewee should have exactly one question to answer per stimulus, and the answer should take 2-5 minutes of conversation.

Order of presentation (suggested):
1. Start with `archetype_pivot.png` — low-stakes framing question about corporate change.
2. Then `inverted_rq3_divergence.png` — the headline finding, contextualized by the earlier archetype discussion.
3. Then `senior_orchestration_shift.png` — focused on role definition, assumes prior framing is established.
4. Then `paired_company_jds.md` — text-level stimulus, grounds the earlier abstract findings in concrete language.
5. Close with `authorship_style_shift.png` — the most meta-level probe, only if time permits and the interviewee is engaged.

If the interviewee is NOT a hiring manager (e.g., a senior IC reflecting on their own experience), drop artifact 5 (too recruiter-specific) and use the time on artifact 3 instead.

**What the artifacts are NOT designed to do.** They are not ready for slide-deck presentation; they are presentation-quality but lack title cards, section slides, or interactive elements. For T27 (Wave 5 findings package), the site agent should rebuild or adapt them into proper presentation figures.
