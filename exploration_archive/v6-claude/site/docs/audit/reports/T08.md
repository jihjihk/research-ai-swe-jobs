## Headline findings

1. **The aggregate `seniority_final` entry-share rise (2.70% → 6.70% of known) is almost entirely a choice of 2024 baseline, a choice of company pool, or both.** Under **arshkon-only vs scraped** (the source-restriction primary per sensitivity dimension (e)), the entry share of known **falls** 7.72% → 6.70%. Under **scraped with TikTok/ByteDance excluded** (which contribute 10.4% of the 1,275-row scraped entry pool), the rate drops to 6.00% — so (arshkon vs scraped-minus-top-specialists) is 7.72% → 6.00%, a ~1.7 pp decline. Under **scraped with seven high-specialist entry companies excluded** (TikTok, ByteDance, SynergisticIT, WayUp, Jobs via Dice, Leidos, IBM), the rate is 5.67% — a ~2.1 pp decline vs arshkon. **The aggregate rise reported in Gate 1 is an artifact of (a) pooling the low-coverage asaniczka baseline, which has no native entry labels, and (b) a small number of entry-specialist employers in the scraped window.** T08 finds this the strongest piece of evidence for Alternative A (instrument-dominated) to date.

2. **The only cross-period metrics that both (i) have large effect sizes AND (ii) survive within-2024 calibration (SNR ≥ 2) are AI/requirements-structure metrics.** The top-5 effect-size ranking with their SNR status: (1) description_length 0.69 SNR 2.51 survives marginal; (2) ai_keyword_any 0.22 SNR 18.8 survives; (3) org_scope_term_rate 0.19 SNR 27.0 survives (but expected inflated by length/boilerplate — see caveat below); (4) ai_tool_specific 0.17 SNR 31.6 survives; (5) tech_python 0.17 SNR 28.3 survives. **No entry-share, junior-share, or YOE-based metric survives SNR ≥ 2.** `seniority_final_entry_share_of_known` SNR 0.17; `yoe_le2_share` SNR 0.48; `yoe_extracted_mean` SNR 0.34. The ranked change list (tables/T08/ranked_changes_by_effect.csv) is the primary output for this step.

3. **The three-way junior-share comparison shows `seniority_final` and YOE ≤ 2 agree on direction but disagree on magnitude by a factor of ~2**, and the choice of 2024 baseline (pooled vs arshkon-only) flips the direction under `seniority_final`. YOE ≤ 2 always says "scraped is higher," but the rise is well inside the within-2024 noise floor (SNR 0.48). T08 supports the Gate 1 decision that entry-share is a specification-dependent quantity that cannot anchor a headline in its current form.

4. **Company-size stratification in arshkon shows entry share and AI prevalence BOTH increase monotonically with company size quartile** — Q1 smallest firms 2.77% entry / 4.2% AI; Q4 largest 4.64% entry / 5.2% AI; yoe ≤ 2 share rises 11.6% (Q1) → 19.9% (Q4). Large firms are the entry-level and AI-mention posters in arshkon. This is context for interpreting the 2026 pool: the scraped window is dominated by large firms (Walmart, Google, TikTok/ByteDance, IBM, Amazon), so the aggregate entry rate may partly reflect a company-size composition shift rather than a temporal shift.

## Step 5 — Junior share trends (three-way comparison)

Full table in `tables/T08/junior_share_operationalizations.csv`. Headline cells:

| scraped_2026_strict_swe | 38,722 | 3.17% | 6.68% | 11.58% | 17.14% | 33.65% |

**Key readings:**

- **Under pooled-2024 vs scraped-2026 (Gate 1's primary):** `seniority_final` says +4.0 pp of known (2.70% → 6.70%); YOE ≤ 2 says +6.0 pp of known (11.22% → 17.19%); YOE ≤ 3 says +8.1 pp of known (25.63% → 33.74%). All three agree on direction, but `seniority_final` magnitude is ~2/3 of YOE ≤ 2 and ~1/2 of YOE ≤ 3.

- **Under arshkon-only vs scraped-2026 (source restriction primary per sensitivity (e)):** `seniority_final` says **−1.02 pp of known** (7.72% → 6.70%); YOE ≤ 2 says +2.21 pp of known (14.98% → 17.19%); YOE ≤ 3 says +2.58 pp of known (31.16% → 33.74%). **`seniority_final` flips sign.** The YOE proxies barely move and are well inside the 8.2 pp MDE for pooled-2024 entry (T07) and the 0.48 SNR floor (calibration table).

- **Under arshkon-only vs scraped-2026 excluding TikTok/ByteDance+5 specialists:** `seniority_final` drops to 5.67% of known, a −2.05 pp decline vs arshkon (7.72% → 5.67%).

**Interpretation.** The `seniority_final` entry share flips direction based on whether we include asaniczka in the 2024 pool and whether we include ~7 entry-specialist companies in the 2026 pool. The YOE ≤ 2 proxy shows a small consistent rise across all slices, but it is below the within-2024 SNR floor (0.48) — meaning the arshkon vs asaniczka difference on YOE ≤ 2 is *larger* than the cross-period difference, so we cannot distinguish this rise from instrument noise.

**Why `seniority_final` and YOE ≤ 2 disagree so much.** They are measuring overlapping but not identical populations. The `seniority_final` column uses LLM-inferred titles/responsibilities; the YOE ≤ 2 proxy uses extracted minimum years. A posting can be YOE ≤ 2 but not `seniority_final = 'entry'` if it mentions "1+ years" in a senior-titled role (common) or if the LLM refused to commit to entry. Conversely, `seniority_final = 'entry'` with no YOE mention (common when the description doesn't cite years). These are different slices, and in arshkon, 50% of `seniority_final = 'entry'` rows have YOE ≤ 2 while 9% of YOE ≤ 2 rows are `seniority_final = 'entry'`. The disagreement is structural, not noise.

**Alternative A vs Alternative B evaluation (T08-only verdict).**

- **Alternative A (instrument-dominated):** The evidence for A is strong. The aggregate `seniority_final` rise is entirely explained by pooling the low-coverage asaniczka baseline (2024 arshkon-only entry_known = 7.72% is higher than 2026 scraped 6.70%). The native entry label quality in arshkon is even worse than Gate 1 reported (42.6% YOE ≥ 5, not 26%). The seniority_known denominator dropped from 61% → 47% between periods, a denominator-drift confound. The within-2024 SNR for `seniority_final_entry_share_of_known` is 0.17, near zero.
- **Alternative B (composition-dominated):** The evidence for B is moderate. Company-size stratification in arshkon shows large firms post more entry. Company concentration (TikTok/ByteDance contribute 10.4% of the scraped entry_final pool) moves the direction when those firms are excluded. However, T08 cannot evaluate the domain-composition piece of Alternative B because T09 archetype labels aren't available.
- **Original RQ1 (junior narrowing):** The aggregate `seniority_final` entry share on arshkon-vs-scraped (the cleanest 2024 baseline after Gate 1) is **7.72% → 6.70% (falling)**, so the original RQ1 "junior share falling" hypothesis has weak corroboration under this specification — but the direction is within the 8.2-pp MDE, so this is not statistically defensible as an effect.

**T08 verdict: Alternative A is the best-supported framing of the three based on T08 alone.** The aggregate "entry rose" signal is an artifact of baseline choice, denominator composition, and a small number of entry-specialist employers. The real signal (the only cross-period direction that survives SNR ≥ 2) is the AI mention and tech-stack rise.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns (not already in Gate 1)

1. **Arshkon native='entry' YOE ≥ 5 share is 42.6%, not 26% as stated in Gate 1.** The noise is ~60% larger than Gate 1 reported. T03 should be spot-checked for this number. The stronger version of the finding: **only ~28% of arshkon native='entry' rows have YOE ≤ 2**, so the native classifier is majority-wrong on this core diagnostic sample. The Stage-10 LLM refuses 83.7% of these rows (correctly).

2. **`yoe_extracted_mean` and `yoe_extracted` distribution SHIFTED LEFT between periods (5.69 → 5.05).** Median is stable at 5, but the share with YOE ≥ 10 dropped from 13.3% → 9.1%. This is the opposite direction of what a scope-inflation story would predict at the upper tail — senior postings are asking for FEWER years, not more. The effect is below SNR (0.34) and direction is probably noise, but it is worth noting that NO YOE-based metric points toward "jobs want more experience."

3. **The scraped 2026 top-entry companies list is dominated by INTERMEDIARIES (aggregators, staffing firms, college jobsites), not employers hiring juniors.** SynergisticIT (100% entry, n=17), WayUp (100% entry, n=16), Jobs via Dice (100% entry, n=41), Lensa (100% entry, n=13), Leidos (100% entry, n=28), Emonics LLC (100% entry, n=9) — at least 5 of the top-15 "entry-posting" companies are staffing/aggregator/college-jobsite platforms. These are structurally different from "Amazon hired 16 entry SWE". **This is a company-concentration finding that Gate 1's "TikTok+ByteDance 10.4%" number understates** — TikTok/ByteDance are real employers, but the tail below them is heavily intermediary-driven. The aggregator exclusion sensitivity (exclude `is_aggregator = true`) does NOT catch all of these: SynergisticIT and WayUp are not marked as aggregators in the `is_aggregator` flag, because they are "direct employers" by the scraper's attribution logic even though their business model is intermediation.

4. **Seniority-known denominator dropped from 61% → 47% between 2024 and 2026** — mostly driven by the Stage-10 LLM budget cap. This means the "of known" entry share has a moving denominator, which is a denominator-drift confound any time the comparison is "of known". Gate 1 flagged this at 53% unknown; T08's 46.6% is comparable, with the difference driven by the arshkon+asaniczka vs scraped ratio.

5. **Extreme-length companies are dominated by finance/insurance/consulting** (Thomson Reuters, Transamerica, S&P Global, Ernst & Young, GEICO, American Express) — industries where compliance/benefits boilerplate is expected. This is T13's territory but strongly suggests that a meaningful share of the 56% length growth is in non-requirements sections.

6. **YOE ≤ 3 share (expanded junior proxy) moves similarly to YOE ≤ 2 and is below SNR.** Gate 1 didn't report a YOE ≤ 3 sensitivity; T08 adds it and it does not change the picture: pooled-2024 25.63% → scraped 33.74%, arshkon-only 31.16% → scraped 33.74% (barely changes under arshkon-only). ## Data quality caveats

- **The seniority_known denominator is not stable across periods.** Any "of known" comparison is partially a denominator-composition comparison. Reporting "of all" alongside is the correct mitigation, but the two denominators give different directions under some sensitivity combinations.
- **The `is_aggregator` flag does not catch all intermediaries.** SynergisticIT, WayUp, and several staffing firms are structurally intermediaries but not marked as aggregators. A corrected "intermediary" flag may be needed before any entry-share finding is considered robust.
- **`company_size` is unavailable for scraped**, blocking cross-period decomposition of the firm-size composition effect. The 2024-side Q1-Q4 stratification suggests this could matter materially (entry share rises ~1.9× from Q1 to Q4).
- **description_length growth is confounded with the Kaggle HTML-strip vs scraped markdown instrument difference** (T05's SNR 2.29 for length is marginal). This metric should not be used as a headline without T13's section-anatomy breakdown.
- **`org_scope_term_rate` SNR 27.0 and `management_indicator_rate_broad` SNR 57.5 are upper bounds** pending keyword validation. Gate 1 flagged the 3-5× inflation risk for management_broad; no validation for org_scope has been scheduled — this is an action item for T21/T22.
