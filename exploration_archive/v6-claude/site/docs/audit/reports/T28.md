# T28 — Domain-stratified scope changes by archetype

Date: 2026-04-15
Agent: Wave 3 O
Input: `exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet`, `swe_cleaned_text.parquet`, `swe_tech_matrix.parquet`, `data/unified.parquet`

## Headline

**Aggregate scope and content changes are almost entirely within-domain, not between-domain composition shifts.** The Kitagawa decomposition of the 2024→2026 entry-share change puts 85% (`seniority_final`) to 107% (`yoe_le2_of_known`) of the total change inside the within-domain component; between-domain composition contributes 2-10%. The T09 "uniform within-archetype junior-share rise" result survives replacement of `seniority_final` with the label-independent YOE ≤ 2 proxy — 15 of 16 large archetypes rise under YOE ≤ 2 (of all), 13 of 13 rise under YOE ≤ 2 (of known). **Alt A's "instrument-dominated apparent rise" reading WEAKENS** because the rise survives under a proxy that does not depend on any seniority classifier.

Within-archetype junior/senior content comparison reveals convergence that the T15 corpus-level null missed: **credential_stack_depth gap closes (and often flips) in ALL 10 large archetypes**; `requirement_breadth` gap closes in 5/10. **LLM/GenAI is the exception** — there the junior/senior gap in requirement_breadth and tech_count WIDENS (+1.4 and +2.0 respectively), i.e., LLM/GenAI senior postings are pulling away from LLM/GenAI junior postings on content breadth.

**LLM/GenAI deep dive**: 68% of 2026 volume comes from companies NOT present in the 2024 sample. Only 138 of 1,174 2026 LLM/GenAI companies overlap with 2024. Entry share rose under both operationalizations (`seniority_final` 2.2% → 7.4%, YOE≤2 8.8% → 11.6%). Description length +30% (2054 → 2664 chars). The LLM/GenAI explosion is mostly NEW entrants, not existing employers pivoting.

## Methodology

- Archetype coverage: 34,099 of 63,701 SWE LinkedIn rows are labeled (53.5%). **Coverage is 94.8% in 2024 but only 30.5% in 2026**, because scraped text goes through a smaller Stage-9 LLM window (text_source='llm' rows only). Every 2026 archetype-level figure should be read with this coverage constraint in mind.
- Used a conservative subset of the Gate-2 / T21-validated clean pattern set: scope is `end-to-end` + `cross-functional` (no bare `ownership`), mentoring is `\bmentor(?:ing|ship|s|ed)?\b` only. The T21 validated bundle at `exploration/artifacts/shared/validated_mgmt_patterns.json` contains 13 people-mgmt patterns (all precision=1.0), 14 tech-orch patterns, 9 strategic patterns — T28 uses only `mentor` from the people-mgmt set to preserve apples-to-apples with Gate 2's narrow mentor finding. A fuller pattern set would strengthen the magnitudes but the qualitative direction of the mentor rise is already clear here. Follow-up with T21's full pattern bundle recommended.
- AI mention is reported narrow (`\bai\b|\bartificial intelligence\b`) and broad (union of 17 tech-matrix AI columns). Both pass SNR ≥ 2.
- Density metrics (`scope_density`, `credential_stack_depth`, `requirement_breadth`) computed on cleaned LLM text only (`llm_extraction_coverage='labeled'`). Binary metrics allow raw-text fallback for recall.
- Kitagawa-style decomposition: `agg_2026 - agg_2024 = Σ w₂₀₂₄Δr + Σ r₂₀₂₄Δw + Σ Δw·Δr`. Restricted to labeled archetypes (excludes archetype=-2 "no text"). See `exploration/scripts/T28_archetype_decomposition.py`.

## 2. Domain × seniority decomposition of the entry-share change

### Aggregate decomposition (labeled archetypes only)

| Metric | 2024 | 2026 | Δ | Within (pp) | Between (pp) | Interaction | Within % | Between % |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `seniority_final` entry | 1.72% | 4.83% | +3.10 pp | +2.64 | +0.11 | +0.36 | **85.0%** | 3.4% |

**Interpretation.** Between-domain market-share shifts contribute at most 9.5% of the entry-share change. The overwhelming share of the apparent change is happening inside archetypes, not as a reallocation across them. Alt B ("ML/AI eats frontend") is decisively ruled out — as Gate 2 already flagged, ML/AI is not entry-poor, and the ML/AI growth shifts the between-domain component only marginally.

### Uniform-rise check under YOE ≤ 2

Does the T09 "within-archetype entry share rose uniformly in 9/10 large archetypes" result hold under the label-independent YOE proxy?

| Spec | large archetypes rising | flat/down |
|---|---|---|
| `seniority_final` | 16/16 large archetypes | 0 |
| `yoe_le2_of_all` | 15/16 | 1 (Azure data platform, tiny: −0.58 pp) |
| `yoe_le2_of_known` | 13/13 large archetypes (with n_known ≥ 100 in both periods) | 0 |

**Conclusion — Alt A weakens.** The uniform within-archetype rise is robust to replacing `seniority_final` with YOE ≤ 2, a metric that does not depend on any seniority classifier. Both operationalizations produce the same qualitative pattern: within-archetype entry share rose broadly. Under `seniority_final` the biggest risers are Agile/Scrum generalist (+5.9 pp), LLM/GenAI (+5.3 pp), .NET (+4.3 pp), JS frontend (+4.0 pp). Under YOE ≤ 2 of known the biggest risers are Defense/cleared (+10.5 pp) and Embedded (+8.5 pp).

Under `seniority_final`, GPU/CUDA (the Gate-2-corrected exception) has n < 100 in both periods and drops out of the "large archetype" set — so we do not replicate V1's exact "9/10 with GPU/CUDA falling" claim here; we see 16/16 rising in the n ≥ 100 set under `seniority_final`, and 15/16 rising under YOE ≤ 2 of all. This is a stricter version of the Gate 2 corrected statement.

**The "mostly instrument artifact" reading is not sufficient.** If Stage 10 LLM labeling drift were the main driver, we would expect the uniform rise to *weaken or disappear* under YOE ≤ 2, which does not depend on the LLM. It doesn't weaken. This does not rule out Alt A entirely — the uniform pattern is still more consistent with a "category-wide shift" than with a specific-archetype restructuring — but the mechanism is no longer about LLM label quality.

## 4. Junior vs senior content within each archetype

For each (archetype, metric, period), computed the mid_senior − entry mean gap, then Δgap = gap_2026 − gap_2024. Negative Δgap ⇒ convergence.

### Credential stack depth gap — CONVERGING in all 10 large archetypes

| Archetype | gap_2024 | gap_2026 | Δ gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Data engineering | +0.56 | **−0.31** | −0.88 |
| Java enterprise | +0.42 | **−0.32** | −0.74 |
| AWS cloud SDE | +0.08 | **−0.61** | −0.68 |
| JS frontend | +0.33 | **−0.35** | −0.68 |
| Defense / cleared | +0.70 | +0.08 | −0.62 |
| LLM/GenAI | +0.53 | **−0.07** | −0.60 |
| .NET / ASP.NET | +0.19 | **−0.40** | −0.59 |
| Agile/Scrum generalist | +0.80 | +0.22 | −0.58 |
| Embedded / firmware | +0.66 | +0.30 | −0.35 |
| DevOps / SRE | +0.06 | **−0.24** | −0.30 |

**In 7 of 10 large archetypes, the credential stack gap FLIPS SIGN** — entry postings in 2026 stack MORE credential categories than mid-senior postings in the same archetype. This is a within-domain convergence finding that the T15 corpus-level null completely hides. The mechanism is consistent with entry postings accumulating years-experience + degree + AI/tool requirements together, while senior postings drop overt credential language.

### Requirement breadth gap

5 of 10 large archetypes converged (Defense, AWS cloud SDE, Embedded, Agile generalist, JS frontend); 4 diverged (DevOps, Data, Java, LLM/GenAI). **LLM/GenAI DIVERGED the most (+1.4)** — LLM/GenAI senior postings are asking for 1.6 more requirement categories than LLM/GenAI entry postings in 2026 vs only 0.2 more in 2024. This is the clearest case where the mid_senior/entry gap widened.

### tech_count gap

Similar pattern. LLM/GenAI's gap widened +2.0, Data engineering's +1.1, DevOps +1.1. AWS cloud SDE's gap collapsed from +2.15 to −1.51 (junior now has more tech mentions than senior — artifact of very few junior rows in that archetype).

### Mentor gap — DIVERGING in every archetype

All 10 large archetypes show a WIDER mentor gap in 2026 than 2024 (senior postings adopted mentor language faster than entry postings). This is consistent with a "senior redefinition" reading — except that T29's authorship analysis shows `mentor` is among the style tokens that correlate with LLM authorship. Read with T29.

See `exploration/tables/T28/step4_junior_senior_gap.csv` and `exploration/figures/T28/fig4_junior_senior_gap_change.png`.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **Credential stack depth gap closes in 10/10 large archetypes and flips in 7/10.** The T15 corpus-level null on convergence masks a clean within-domain convergence on this specific metric. Entry postings in 2026 are stacking more credentials than mid-senior postings in the same archetype. This is a genuine, novel RQ2-adjacent finding.

2. **AI vocabulary has spread into traditionally non-AI archetypes.** JS frontend +14.7 pp broad AI, .NET +13.6, Java +11.6. This is not an ML/AI-archetype story; this is broad AI-layer-stacking across every SWE domain. T14's network analysis found it structurally; T28 locates it within archetypes.

3. **LLM/GenAI growth is 68% from brand-new employers.** The cluster is not formed by existing companies pivoting into AI — it's formed by a wave of new employers entering the posting market. Only 138 companies appear in both 2024 and 2026 LLM/GenAI clusters.

4. **Mentor rise is broad across ALL large archetypes (+10 to +24 pp), not concentrated in LLM/GenAI.** Combined with T29's correlation evidence, this points to mentoring language being downstream of LLM drafting style, not senior-role change.

5. **Requirement breadth diverges (junior vs senior) in LLM/GenAI but converges in older archetypes.** The structural "senior pulling away" story holds specifically in LLM/GenAI, not as a general corpus pattern. If the paper wants to say "ML/AI seniors are becoming harder to match against juniors," this is the evidence.

6. **Credential stack depth fell aggregate in this rebuild.** My pattern set is narrower than T11's and may miss pattern types; Gate 2's "credential_stack rose" headline is *not* reproduced under this rebuild. **This is a pattern-dependence flag that T22 (scope pattern validation) should resolve.**

7. **The `seniority_final` vs `yoe_le2` uniform-rise disagreement is minimal**. Both produce the same qualitative "every archetype rises" story. Gate 2's Alt A reading depended on the rise being an artifact of LLM label drift. Since the rise holds under YOE (which doesn't use LLM labels for its entry definition), Alt A has to propose a mechanism that affects both classifiers — e.g., LinkedIn-side posting behavior or employer-mix shift inside archetypes. The label-drift story alone is no longer sufficient.

## Data quality caveats

- Scraped 2026 archetype coverage is only 30.5% — the 2026 side of every archetype cross-tab is non-random (biased toward the kind of 2026 postings that passed the Stage-9 LLM text cleaning filter).
- The pattern sets here are mine, not T11's. Pattern drift may explain small magnitude differences with Gate 2 headlines.
- Archetype = −2 ("no text / raw-only") is 69.9% of the 2026 sample and 1.7% of the 2024 sample. My step 2 decomposition excludes these rows. An alternative that assigns them to their own bucket would give a slightly different decomposition but would not flip the within-vs-between conclusion because the unlabeled rows pass through both the 2024 and 2026 denominators.
- Mentor pattern is `mentor|mentoring|mentorship` — simpler than the T11 strict management pattern. It is consistent with Gate 2 Correction 2 ("only `mentor` is clean").
