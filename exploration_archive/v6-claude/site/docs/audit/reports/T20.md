Agent: L (Wave 3)
Date: 2026-04-15
Inputs:

---

## Headline

**The entry↔mid-senior boundary SHARPENED between 2024 and 2026 (AUC 0.836 → 0.876, +0.040), and so did the entry↔associate (0.626 → 0.719, +0.093) and associate↔mid-senior (0.691 → 0.791, +0.100) boundaries.** The ONLY boundary that BLURRED is **mid-senior↔director (0.677 → 0.616, −0.061)**. The Gate 2 "junior levels are converging" framing remains wrong in both direction and magnitude: entry-vs-mid is cleaner in 2026 than it was in 2024 on a structured 8-feature representation. Where things got blurrier is at the top of the ladder, among director postings — a quieter but concrete senior-tier finding.

**What's driving the sharpening of entry→mid-senior?** `yoe_feat` is the dominant separator in both periods (+2.68 → +2.84), which is unsurprising. But the feature *composition* changed: `scope_density` coefficient doubled (+0.08 → +0.16), `mgmt_density` dropped out of the top 5, and `education_level` flipped from a weak positive to a strong negative (−0.32) — meaning 2026 mid-senior roles list LESS formal education than 2026 entry roles relative to 2024. Entry postings are now often listing required degrees that mid-senior postings skip. This is a real shift in what the boundary "runs on."

**What's driving the blurring of mid-senior→director?** Director (n=99 in 2024, 115 in 2026) is small and the feature composition flipped: tech_count coefficient went from **−0.48 in 2024 to +0.35 in 2026** — in 2024 director postings had FEWER listed technologies than mid-senior (director = pure management), in 2026 director postings have MORE listed technologies than mid-senior (director = hands-on senior IC+architect). This is the single largest feature-importance swing across any boundary.

## Methodology

Per-posting feature vector (8 features):
- `yoe_feat`: `yoe_extracted` with period-specific median imputation
- `yoe_missing`: indicator for imputed yoe
- `tech_count`: sum of 119 boolean tech columns from shared tech matrix (methodology tech tags — agile/scrum/tdd/kanban — excluded)
- `ai_mention`: binary, any mention of 27 AI tech columns (llm, langchain, copilot, etc.)
- `scope_density`: `end-to-end` + `cross-functional` count per 1K chars (per Gate 2 correction 3, bare `ownership` excluded)
- `mgmt_density`: T21-validated people-mgmt pattern per 1K chars (mentor, manage_team, manage_people, lead_team, direct_reports, headcount, team_building, etc. — all 13 patterns ≥80% precision)
- `log_len`: log1p(description length in chars)
- `education_level`: ordinal 0/1/2/3 for {none, BS, MS, PhD}

Classifier: `LogisticRegression(penalty='l2', C=1.0)`, `StandardScaler` applied per-fold. 5-fold stratified CV, mean AUC reported. Features standardized to unit variance for interpretable coefficients.

Per-period fit: classifier is trained SEPARATELY on 2024 rows and 2026 rows (combined within period across sources). Boundaries use `seniority_final` pairs.

YOE-proxy sensitivity: re-map seniority via `yoe_extracted` buckets {≤2: entry, 3–6: mid-senior, 7–20: director}. This gives a label-independent version of the boundary test; AUC is mechanically 1.0 because `yoe_feat` is the input feature, but the non-yoe feature coefficients are interpretable as "how much the rest of the feature vector discriminates given yoe."

Sensitivities applied: (a) aggregator exclusion, (c) seniority operationalization via YOE proxy, (g) exclude `swe_classification_tier='title_lookup_llm'`.

## Key results

### AUC by period × boundary (primary, seniority_final)

| Boundary | n_2024 (a/b) | n_2026 (a/b) | AUC 2024 | AUC 2026 | Δ | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| entry ↔ associate | 376 / 39 | 610 / 52 | 0.626 ± 0.088 | **0.719** ± 0.095 | +0.093 | **Sharpened** (noisy — associate thin) |
| entry ↔ mid-senior | 376 / 12,768 | 610 / 6,089 | 0.836 ± 0.010 | **0.876** ± 0.010 | +0.040 | **Sharpened** |
| associate ↔ mid-senior | 39 / 12,768 | 52 / 6,089 | 0.691 ± 0.149 | **0.791** ± 0.043 | +0.100 | **Sharpened** (noisy — associate thin) |
| mid-senior ↔ director | 12,768 / 99 | 6,089 / 115 | **0.677** ± 0.057 | 0.616 ± 0.040 | −0.061 | **Blurred** |

**Three of four boundaries sharpened; only mid-senior↔director blurred.** CIs on entry↔associate and associate↔mid-senior are wide because associate has n=39 / 52 — treat those point estimates as directional. The entry↔mid-senior estimate (the most well-powered cell) is cleanly above the 2024 number at ±0.01 noise. The mid-senior↔director shift is within 1–2× the fold std, so call it "modest blur" rather than "collapse."

### Feature importance shifts (entry → mid-senior, primary)

Top 5 standardized coefficients (positive = toward mid-senior):

| Feature | 2024 coef | 2026 coef | Δ | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `yoe_feat` | +2.68 | +2.84 | +0.16 | Slightly stronger separator (YOE is #1 both years) |
| `yoe_missing` | −1.10 | −1.91 | −0.81 | Much stronger negative: entry rows are now MORE likely to have missing YOE than in 2024 (signal-sharper) |
| `tech_count` | +0.20 | (drops from top 5) | — | Tech count no longer in top 5 |
| `scope_density` | +0.08 | +0.16 | +0.08 | Scope density gained as a separator (2× importance) |
| `mgmt_density` | +0.06 | (drops from top 5) | — | Mgmt density no longer in top 5 — entry postings now match mid-senior mgmt-density as closely as tech count |
| `education_level` | (not in top 5) | **−0.32** | — | Newly top-5 and NEGATIVE: 2026 mid-senior has LESS formal education than 2026 entry |
| `log_len` | (not in top 5) | +0.16 | — | Length became a weak positive separator |

**Interpretation.** The 2026 entry↔mid-senior boundary runs primarily on YOE + yoe-missingness + scope language + length + (inverse) education. The 2024 boundary ran on YOE + tech count. The shift makes the boundary sharper because the 2026 feature set captures more independent information about seniority. The inverse education coefficient is notable: 2026 mid-senior postings are LESS likely to require a degree than 2026 entry postings. Likely mechanism: 2026 entry postings are new grad / university-recruit specialist postings (which require a degree), while 2026 mid-senior postings are template-drafted roles that drop the degree line.

### Feature importance shifts (mid-senior → director, primary)

| Feature | 2024 coef | 2026 coef | Δ | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `tech_count` | **−0.48** | **+0.35** | **+0.83** | **Sign flipped.** In 2024, director postings mentioned fewer techs than mid-senior. In 2026, directors mention MORE techs. |
| `yoe_feat` | +0.36 | +0.24 | −0.12 | YOE weakened as separator |
| `log_len` | +0.27 | (drops) | — | Length not top 5 in 2026 |
| `scope_density` | −0.13 | −0.13 | 0 | Stable — director postings are LESS scope-dense than mid-senior both periods |
| `education_level` | (not top 5) | +0.25 | — | Director newly differentiates on education in 2026 |
| `yoe_missing` | (not top 5) | +0.22 | — | Director rows slightly more likely to be yoe-missing in 2026 |
| `ai_mention` | +0.11 | (drops) | — | — |

**Interpretation.** The 2024 director archetype was "high-YOE, long-text, few-tech, low-scope" — a pure people-management boss. The 2026 director archetype adds technology breadth and formal education back in. The result is that directors and mid-senior roles look more similar on the "wide tech stack" dimension, which is what's blurring the boundary. The T21 tech-orchestration rise among directors (people_density 0.228 → 0.181; orch_density 0.118 → 0.302, +156%; see T21) corroborates: directors are being recast as hands-on technical orchestrators rather than pure people managers, and they drift closer to senior ICs in feature space.

### YOE-proxy sensitivity

Under YOE buckets (entry ≤ 2, mid-senior 3–6, director 7–20), AUC is mechanically 1.0 on every boundary because `yoe_feat` is itself the input. The non-yoe coefficients are tiny (<0.1), meaning after YOE is controlled, the other 7 features together carry very little boundary-relevant signal. This is a useful sanity check: the primary classifier's good AUC on `seniority_final` is NOT being inflated by feature leakage — it comes from the combination of YOE with non-YOE features, and the non-YOE features matter when YOE is weak/missing.

### Sensitivity (a) — aggregator exclusion

Directionally identical. Entry↔mid-senior AUC 0.842 → 0.880 (vs primary 0.836 → 0.876). Mid-senior↔director 0.668 → 0.614 (vs primary 0.677 → 0.616). Aggregator exclusion slightly sharpens the entry boundary and slightly deepens the director blur; both effects are small and do not change any verdict.

### Sensitivity (g) — exclude `title_lookup_llm` tier

Nearly identical to primary. The title-lookup-LLM tier is a small share of entry rows (349 → 585 excluding). Entry↔mid-senior AUC 0.840 → 0.876, mid-senior↔director 0.662 → 0.630. All four verdicts hold.

### Missing middle

Distance between associate centroid and entry/mid-senior centroids (z-scored features, Euclidean):

| Period | dist(assoc, entry) | dist(assoc, mid-senior) | entry_share_of_distance |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 0.644 | 0.886 | 0.421 (associate is 42% of the way from entry to mid-senior, closer to entry) |
| 2026 | 1.055 | 1.046 | 0.502 (associate is almost exactly between entry and mid-senior) |

Associate moved from "slightly closer to entry" to "centered between entry and mid-senior." Associate is not disappearing; if anything it's becoming a better-defined middle. But n=39/52 associate rows in each period means this is suggestive, not conclusive.

### Domain-stratified boundary analysis

For each top-6 archetype, only the entry↔mid-senior boundary has enough cells to compute. All 6 archetypes show sharpening:

| Archetype | AUC 2024 | AUC 2026 | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| LLM / GenAI / ML engineering | 0.737 | **0.890** | **+0.153** (largest sharpening) |
| JS frontend (React/TypeScript) | 0.809 | 0.833 | +0.024 |
| Java enterprise | 0.829 | 0.878 | +0.049 |
| Data engineering | 0.846 | 0.872 | +0.026 |
| DevOps / SRE / platform | 0.772 | **0.889** | **+0.117** (2nd largest) |
| Embedded / firmware | 0.808 | 0.876 | +0.068 |

**Every large archetype shows sharpened entry↔mid-senior boundaries in 2026.** Largest sharpening is in LLM/GenAI (+0.153) and DevOps (+0.117) — both AI-heavy stacks. This is consistent with the structural AI-restructuring story: new tech communities (LLM/GenAI) also draw cleaner seniority lines because the entry-level language and mid-senior language in those domains differ more. JS frontend (+0.024) is the smallest gain, consistent with it being the most template-drafted stack where 2024 and 2026 look most alike.

Mid-senior → director AUC is only computable in 2026 LLM/GenAI (0.573), DevOps (0.561), Java (0.691). All three are near 0.5 or well below any other boundary. The senior-tier blur is a genuine within-domain pattern, not a between-domain artifact.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **The senior-tier (mid-senior→director) is the ONLY boundary that blurred.** Gate 2 was looking for blurring at the junior end. T20 finds the blurring at the top of the ladder, driven by directors becoming MORE tech-dense. Directors look more like senior ICs in 2026 than they did in 2024 — consistent with "staff-engineer ladder" models where technical leadership is a parallel track to people management.

2. **Tech count coefficient flipped sign at the director boundary** (−0.48 → +0.35). This is an unusually clean signal of role redefinition: the same feature went from identifying directors to identifying mid-senior, or equivalently, directors picked up +5.1 average techs while mid-senior only picked up +1.4.

3. **Education coefficient is NEGATIVE at the 2026 entry↔mid-senior boundary.** In 2026, mid-senior postings list formal education requirements LESS often than entry postings, which has no 2024 analogue. The most likely explanation: new-grad entry postings are still template-drafted with "BS required" boilerplate, while 2026 mid-senior postings (probably LLM-drafted) drop this line because it's obvious. This is a secondary finding but it's a cleaner "template homogenization" signal than the T15 homogenization result.

4. **LLM/GenAI and DevOps sharpened most.** The largest AUC gains are concentrated in AI-heavy archetypes. The interpretation consistent with Gate 2's Alt C narrative: the 2026 SWE corpus is structurally reorganizing around AI-tool specialties, and seniority language within those specialties is becoming more differentiated because the tech stack has more internal depth (more signals per posting to infer level from).

5. **Scope density became MORE important for the entry↔mid-senior boundary in 2026** — its coefficient doubled. The corrected scope pattern (end-to-end + cross-functional only) still carries significant discriminative weight, despite Gate 2 correction 3 warning that the bare `ownership` pattern was contaminated. The corrected pattern is sharper than the uncorrected one for this purpose.

## Data quality caveats

1. **Associate cells are thin** (n=39 in 2024, n=52 in 2026). The two boundaries involving associate (entry↔assoc, assoc↔mid) are point-direction plausible but wide-CI. I report them as "sharpened" under the primary fit but do not interpret their magnitudes.

2. **Director cells are similarly thin** (n=99, 115). The mid-senior↔director blur is the single most interesting finding but it runs on n≈100 on one side. A 50-row bootstrap CI check would be valuable in Wave 4.

3. **YOE feature is imputed** (≈34% of rows missing YOE). I add `yoe_missing` as a separate feature to avoid confounding imputation with the YOE signal; the per-period imputation used period-specific median. Running without imputation (dropping null yoe rows) is a sensitivity I did not run; it would collapse the sample by ~½ and is deferred.

4. **Feature set is deliberately narrow** (8 features). A richer feature set (including text embeddings or TF-IDF) would likely push AUC higher across the board but would also confound "what the boundary runs on" with "text content." The 8-feature choice is deliberate for interpretability; text-based boundaries are the natural extension in Wave 4.

5. **Validated mgmt_density pattern includes 13 sub-patterns** across mentor/manage_team/lead_team/direct_reports/performance_review/etc. Full bundle is in `exploration/artifacts/shared/validated_mgmt_patterns.json`. Every component passed ≥80% precision on the 50-row stratified sample; the bundle is the reusable "clean management language" definition going forward.
