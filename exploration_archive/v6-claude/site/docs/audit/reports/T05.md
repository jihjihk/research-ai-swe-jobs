# T05 — Cross-dataset comparability

**Agent:** Wave 1 Agent C
**Scope:** SWE postings, LinkedIn only, `is_english = true`, `date_flag = 'ok'`
**Inputs:** `data/unified.parquet`
**Script:** `exploration/scripts/T05_cross_dataset_comparability.py`

## Headline

- **Description length:** large, real shift (scraped ~4.9 K chars vs ~3.3 K arshkon vs ~3.9 K asaniczka). KS statistic arshkon↔scraped = 0.296 (p≈0). Signal-to-noise (cross-period / within-2024) = **2.29** — above but not far above instrument noise, driven partly by markdown preservation in the scraped text.
- **AI mention rate (binary):** 2.81% (arshkon) → 2.80% (asaniczka) → **18.8% (scraped)**. Within-2024 noise ≈ 0. **SNR = 925**. This is the cleanest cross-period change in the dataset.
- **Entry share under `seniority_final`:** arshkon 7.72%, asaniczka 1.74%, scraped 6.70%. Within-2024 gap (5.98 pp) is ~3× larger than the cross-period gap. **SNR = 0.33 — well below the instrument-noise threshold.** The scraped entry share is almost identical to the historical arshkon baseline. Any "entry-share declining" story cannot be told from `seniority_final` pooled over 2024 sources.
- **YOE ≤ 2 share:** arshkon 15.0%, asaniczka 10.4%, scraped 17.2%. Within-2024 noise (4.6 pp) ≈ cross-period effect (4.5 pp). **SNR = 0.98**. Also below the threshold.
- **Mean YOE:** arshkon 5.22, asaniczka 5.80, scraped 5.05. SNR = 0.80. Also indistinguishable from noise.
- **Geographic:** state distributions differ significantly in every pair (chi² p ≈ 0 for all three). The within-2024 arshkon-vs-asaniczka chi² is 373; arshkon-vs-scraped is 2596 and asaniczka-vs-scraped is 6212 — so the cross-period geographic shift is much larger than the within-2024 variation but it is still substantial within 2024, and two different platforms/collection methods can look very different even in the same year. Interpret geographic shifts with care.
- **Industry composition (arshkon vs scraped):** systematically different, but sample bias in arshkon (small, recruiting/consulting-heavy) is the likely dominant driver — see the aggregator profile in T06.
- **Company overlap:** Jaccard(arshkon, scraped) = 0.109; Jaccard(asaniczka, scraped) = 0.135. ~44% of arshkon companies appear in the scraped window; only 13% of scraped companies are in arshkon. This asymmetry matters for the T06 panel (125 companies with ≥5 SWE in both arshkon and scraped).
- **Title vocabulary:** Jaccard(arshkon, scraped) = 0.026 (very low). Most titles are unique to one window — title-level comparisons must collapse to families.

## 7. Artifact diagnostic — which comparisons are trustworthy?

| metric | direction survives pooling? | within-2024 SNR | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI mention rate | yes | 925 | **trustworthy** — real, enormous |
| mean desc length | yes | 2.29 | trustworthy but inflated by markdown reinsertion; use `description_core_llm` |
| geographic mix | yes | ~7 | real but reflects collection-method differences too |
| seniority_final entry share | **no** | 0.33 | **not above noise** — do not pool 2024 sources for this |
| YOE ≤ 2 share | marginal | 0.98 | **not above noise** |
| mean YOE | marginal | 0.80 | **not above noise** |
| title vocabulary | yes (divergence large) | ~2 | structural instrument difference — collapse to title families |
| company overlap | yes (divergence large) | similar | instrument-driven long tail |

**Primary takeaway:** the most-cited cross-period RQ1 outcomes (entry share, YOE, mean YOE) cannot be asserted naively by pooling the 2024 sources. The arshkon-only vs scraped comparison is the safer default; asaniczka is useful for power but not for entry-level directionality.

## 9. Platform labeling stability test

Top-20 SWE titles that appear in both arshkon and scraped (see `top20_title_seniority_final.csv`, `top20_title_yoe.csv`, `top20_title_seniority_native.csv`).

**Disagreement pattern for the top title "software engineer":**
- `seniority_final` entry share: 6.5% (arshkon) → 4.9% (scraped). **Decline.**
- `yoe_extracted <= 2` share: 18.2% (arshkon) → 20.2% (scraped). **Increase.**
- Mean YOE: 4.83 → 5.00 (stable).

**Same disagreement at most top titles:** "devops engineer" goes from 0% entry (final) to 4.6%, while YOE ≤ 2 goes from 2.1% to 11.5%. "machine learning engineer" entry share goes from 4.0% to 0.5% but YOE ≤ 2 stays ~15%. "ai engineer" has almost no arshkon baseline. The two operationalizations frequently disagree in sign.

Interpretation options (non-exclusive):
1. **LLM classifier conservatism.** Stage 10's LLM is stricter than arshkon's native label, so a posting that says "2-3 yrs" and arshkon tagged `entry` is now `mid-senior`. That would push `seniority_final` down while YOE stays put.
2. **Platform label drift.** LinkedIn may be tagging fewer postings as "Entry level" in 2026 than in 2024 (so `seniority_native` shifts), and Stage 10's LLM is not fully offsetting that.
3. **Real scope inflation at the same YOE level.** A company writes "entry level" but asks for 2+ years and calls it a mid role — consistent with RQ1.

The arshkon-only native comparison (`top20_title_seniority_native.csv`) is mostly sparse because the scraped source has very few `seniority_native = 'entry'` rows (asaniczka has zero by construction, and scraped's native labels are concentrated in `mid_senior`). This ambiguity is exactly why T06's within-vs-between decomposition needs both operationalizations — see T06.

**Indeed cross-validation (`indeed_vs_linkedin_entry.csv`):**

| platform | n | entry_share_of_known | yoe_le2_share |
|---|---|---|---|
| indeed | 11860 | 0.0661 | 0.1806 |
| linkedin (scraped) | 40881 | 0.0670 | 0.1719 |

Indeed and LinkedIn scraped give essentially identical entry shares and YOE distributions. The LinkedIn-platform-relabeling hypothesis weakens substantially: whatever is driving `seniority_final` at 6.7% in the 2026 scraped data is also driving it at 6.6% on an entirely different platform pulled through the same harmonizer. That is a strong signal that the Stage 5/10 pipeline is measuring the same thing across platforms, and that the within-2024 SNR shortfall is a real label-coverage problem (asaniczka missing entry labels) rather than a cross-platform artifact.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **Entry share survives Indeed cross-validation.** LinkedIn scraped and Indeed scraped agree on entry share to 0.1 pp. This rules out the "LinkedIn platform relabeling" worry for RQ1-related metrics as long as arshkon (not asaniczka) is the 2024 baseline.
2. **The SNR table is more punishing than I expected for the headline RQ metrics.** Entry share, YOE-≤2, mean YOE all fail the SNR = 2 cutoff once you pool 2024 sources. This has to be flagged before the formal analysis.
3. **Within top-20 titles, `seniority_final` and YOE proxy frequently disagree in sign, not just magnitude.** The direction of the "junior share" finding depends on which one you use. See T06 decomposition for the full story.
4. **Top-50 company overlap is only 21 companies between arshkon and scraped top-50.** The "big-employer" lists that dominate corpus-level analyses have a 58% replacement rate across sources.
5. **Cross-company description collisions are rare** — the top collision is 6 companies sharing a hash (Stage 4 aggregator-group collapse working as intended, see T06 §3).

## Data quality caveats

- KS-test p-values hit floating-point zero because of large N — do not interpret p-values, interpret the D statistic magnitude.
- `yoe_extracted` is a regex-derived proxy; it is available for ~60-70% of postings with large coverage differences across sources.
- All "entry share" numbers exclude `seniority_final = 'unknown'`. Unknown shares differ across sources (~1% arshkon, ~50% asaniczka pre-LLM, ~53% scraped pre-LLM), and `seniority_final_source` is worth profiling before any formal comparison.
- Indeed's `seniority_final` only has Stage 5 strong-rule labels (no LLM frame) — treat the Indeed 0.066 number as a floor, not a direct comparable.

## Action items for downstream tasks

- **For RQ1 (junior share):** default to arshkon vs scraped, do not pool asaniczka, and present both `seniority_final` and YOE ≤ 2. Disagreement in direction is itself a finding to investigate (see T06 §5). The within-2024 calibration should appear in every table that compares 2024 to 2026.
- **For T08/T09 (distribution profiling, topic models):** use `description_core_llm` with `llm_extraction_coverage = 'labeled'` for any length-sensitive metric. Raw-length findings are confounded with the Kaggle→scraped formatting shift.
- **For text-based analyses:** cap at 20-50 postings per `company_name_canonical` (see T06 §1) — the scraped top-20 take 18% of the corpus.
- **For the feasibility agent (T07):** the arshkon ↔ scraped company overlap panel is 125 companies at ≥5 SWE; at ≥3 SWE the panel is likely 300-400. Power on within-company effects is workable for length/AI but weaker for entry share.
