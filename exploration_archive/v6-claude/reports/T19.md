Agent K, Wave 3. Run date: 2026-04-15. Input: `data/unified.parquet`.

**One-sentence verdict:** The 2024→2026 AI vocabulary shift is genuinely faster than any within-2024 drift by a wide margin (acceleration ratio 3.1-3.2× on AI broad/narrow, 6.7× on copilot, 90× on claude_tool), the scraped window is internally stable enough that the JOLTS macro cooling cannot be driving the cross-period AI findings (cross-period / within-scraped-window ratio is 15-25× for AI metrics), and the one metric where the JOLTS confound IS present — entry share — has a cross-period effect size smaller than within-scraped-window noise (ratio 0.86), confirming Gate 2's "entry share rise is instrument-driven" reading.

## Methodology

### Rate-of-change estimation
For each key metric, computed:
- `rate_within_2024_per_year = (arshkon_value − asaniczka_value) / 0.244 years`
- `rate_cross_ars_to_scr_per_year = (scraped_value − arshkon_value) / 1.969 years`
- `acceleration_cross_over_within = rate_cross / rate_within_2024`

Both rates use observed midpoint date differences, not hardcoded month gaps.

**Key caveat:** The within-2024 rate is estimated on a 0.244-year window, so small differences become large per-year rates. An acceleration ratio > 1 does NOT automatically mean the cross-period rate is "large" in absolute terms — it means it is large *relative to* the noisy within-2024 baseline. We report both the raw rates and the ratio so the reader can judge.

### Within-source stability
arshkon has 12 distinct `date_posted` values across its 15-day window; we split them into two halves of 6 days each and computed the headline metric set on each half.

scraped has 26 distinct `scrape_date` values across the 26-day window; we computed headline metrics per-day then also in a first-half / second-half split for the JOLTS confound comparison.

### JOLTS confound ratio
The comparison is:
- Within-scraped delta = (last half mean) − (first half mean) — this estimates the maximum time-within-window effect the scraped snapshot can produce.
- Cross-period delta = scraped overall mean − arshkon overall mean.
- Ratio = |cross-period delta| / |within-scraped delta|.

**Interpretation:** Ratio ≥ 10 means the cross-period effect is an order of magnitude larger than the largest within-window drift — a very conservative threshold for "not confounded". Ratios 1–3 would mean "confounded, findings should be treated as upper bounds."

### Levene's test
`scipy.stats.levene(*per_date_groups, center='median')` on description_length, n=26 daily groups, one per scrape_date.

### Timeline figure
Three source windows as horizontal bars; vertical dashed lines for major AI releases between Mar 2023 and Mar 2026; no causal claims, illustrative only.

**Why description length has a negative within-2024 rate:** asaniczka mean length is higher than arshkon mean length (3881 vs 3306). This is a sampling / preprocessing artifact between the two kaggle sources, not a real 2024 trend. The sign flip between within-2024 and cross-period is exactly the signature of a cross-source instrument effect, not a temporal one. We report it honestly rather than pretend length moved monotonically.

**Why scope density has a below-1 acceleration ratio:** within-2024 spread is relatively large (0.210 → 0.239 between asaniczka and arshkon over 0.244 yr), producing a within-2024 rate of +0.117 pp/year. The cross-period rate (+0.077/year) is actually smaller in per-year terms, even though the absolute cross-period rise is +15.1 pp. This is a measurement-window effect: short within-window baselines inflate within-2024 per-year rates.

**Why entry share has a sign-flipped acceleration ratio:** within-2024, asaniczka→arshkon entry share rose strongly (0.011 → 0.037, +2.6 pp in 0.244 yr = +10.7 pp/year), but this is almost certainly a differential-labeling artifact — asaniczka has zero native entry labels, so its `seniority_final` entry share is only populated by Stage 10 LLM output. Cross-period entry share then FELL slightly (0.037 → 0.031). The "within-2024 rate is larger than the cross-period rate" signature is exactly the instrument-dominance pattern Gate 2 identified.

**Two distinct verdicts:**

- **AI metrics pass the JOLTS confound check cleanly.** Cross-period rates are 8-25× within-window drift — an order of magnitude above noise.
- **Entry-share fails the JOLTS confound check.** The cross-period entry-share delta (−0.006) is smaller in absolute value than the within-scraped-window drift (−0.007). **We cannot distinguish the cross-period entry-share effect from noise within a single window.** This is the cleanest statistical articulation of the Wave 2 / Gate 2 finding that the entry-share rise is instrument-driven rather than a real labor-market change.

The second verdict is the single most important T19 finding for the paper: it gives a concrete "ratio below 1" confound-check failure that can be cited against any entry-level narrowing claim.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **Entry-share cross-period effect is smaller than within-scraped-window drift.** This is the cleanest single confound-check failure in the entire exploration so far. Any entry-level claim must cite this — not just the Gate 2 qualitative "instrument-driven" framing, but the quantitative 0.86× ratio.

2. **Scope-metric acceleration ratio is below 1 (0.66×).** The within-2024 asaniczka→arshkon scope-density jump (+3 pp over 0.24 yr) is large per-year. The cross-period rise (+15 pp over 2 yr) is large absolute but smaller per-year. Interpretation: scope language may have been on a steady upward trend throughout 2024 that did not accelerate in 2026 — or more likely, this is an instrument difference between asaniczka and arshkon. We cannot distinguish with 3 snapshots. **Scope density is therefore a weaker headline than AI prevalence: its rise is real in absolute magnitude, but the rate-of-change test does not cleanly rule out that it was already accelerating in 2024.**

3. **Description length has negative within-2024 per-year rate, positive cross-period rate.** This sign flip is a dead giveaway that the within-2024 comparison is an instrument artifact (asaniczka has higher mean length than arshkon by ~575 chars with no natural temporal interpretation). Any length-growth claim should use the cross-period rate (+816 chars/year or +1606 absolute) and explicitly disclose that the within-2024 rate is instrument-contaminated.

4. **Sundays have 0.59 AI broad (vs Tuesday's 0.49).** Weekend employer mix is meaningfully different — likely tech employers with always-on scheduling vs traditional employers posting on weekdays. Any daily or weekly time-series modeling of scraped data needs day-of-week fixed effects.

5. **posting_age_days coverage is 0.8%, not the 1.4% T01 flagged.** Even worse than previously thought. The field is effectively unusable for stock-vs-flow analysis.

6. **arshkon's within-window AI broad trend is slightly NEGATIVE** (0.181 → 0.163 from early to late halves, a −1.7 pp drift over 8 days). This is consistent with day-of-week / employer-mix effects dominating a 15-day window, not a real declining AI trend in April 2024.

7. **Levene's test rejects variance homogeneity on description length (p < 0.0001)** but the variance differences are completely dwarfed by the cross-period effect, so it is a statistically-detectable but practically-negligible source of noise.

8. **Copilot acceleration 6.7× vs Claude tool acceleration 90.9×** — both clearly above 1, but the relative magnitudes reflect base rates. Claude tool had essentially zero in 2024 (0.02%), so its within-2024 denominator is tiny and any cross-period growth produces a giant ratio. Copilot had 0.15% in 2024 and its ratio is more conservative. Both are real effects; the magnitudes of the ratios are not directly comparable.

## Data quality caveats

1. **Source date-range asymmetry.** asaniczka spans 5 days, arshkon 15 days, scraped 26 days. Within-source analyses are most informative on scraped (most days) and least informative on asaniczka (very short window).

2. **Instrument vs temporal confounding.** The within-2024 rate estimates compare asaniczka (2024-01-15 midpoint) to arshkon (2024-04-12.5 midpoint) — different samplers, different platforms' internal pipelines, different search frames, different HTML-stripping behavior. We cannot disentangle instrument difference from real 0.24-year drift. The rate-of-change framework therefore treats within-2024 as an upper bound on instrument noise, and reports acceleration ratios as a conservative signal-to-noise statistic rather than a clean rate comparison.

3. **posting_age_days unusable** (0.8% coverage, all = 1). Cannot characterize the posting lifecycle.

4. **Scraped scrape_date is fully populated** (100%) but `date_posted` in scraped is only 0.8% — which is why posting_age_days is so thin. Any "when was this posting created" analysis in scraped must use scrape_date as a proxy.

5. **Controls for day-of-week are not applied in the main rate-of-change computation.** If the scraped window had a different day-of-week distribution than arshkon (arshkon is uniform across its 15 days; scraped over-represents weekends relative to weekdays when counting rows), this could produce a small bias. The bias cannot exceed ~5 pp on AI broad given the 10 pp day-of-week spread; our cross-period effect is 35 pp; the day-of-week effect is therefore negligible for the headline but might matter for ±1 pp claims.
