# T16 — Company hiring strategy typology

**Agent:** J (Wave 3) · **Date:** 2026-04-15
**Inputs:** `data/unified.parquet`, `exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_tech_matrix.parquet`, `exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet`
**Script:** `exploration/scripts/T16_company_typology.py`
**Panel:** companies with >=3 SWE postings in BOTH `kaggle_arshkon` (2024) and `scraped` (2026), LinkedIn-only. **n = 240 companies** (125 at >=5 per T07 feasibility).

---

## Headline

The clean "tool-stack adopter" cluster exists and is measurable. **46 of 240 overlap-panel companies (19%) form a cluster with ΔAI-any rate of +0.52, Δdesc-length +1,149 chars, and Δscope (end-to-end + cross-functional) +0.33, while their within-company entry-share moved essentially zero under both `seniority_final` (+0.001) and YOE ≤ 2 (−0.002).** This is the strongest direct corroboration of Alt C ("tool-stack restructuring is the real change") in the exploration so far: a quarter of the companies that appear in both periods pivoted hard on AI vocabulary without moving their seniority mix.

The within-company decomposition verdict confirms the Wave 2 finding that the two entry operationalizations disagree directionally on the same 240-company panel: **`seniority_final` within-company change is −0.032 (entry rate falls within-company), YOE ≤ 2 within-company change is +0.015 (entry rate rises within-company).** The 0.047 pp gap between these is the specification-dependence finding at the within-company level.

**Archetype pivots are pervasive.** 179 of 240 (74.6%) companies — or 60 of 82 (73.2%) among those with >=5 archetype-labeled rows per period — changed their dominant archetype between 2024 and 2026. Median per-company archetype total variation distance is 0.629. Companies are not just growing their postings; they are reconfiguring what *kind* of SWE they post.

**New market finding — the uniform within-archetype entry rise from T09 does NOT reproduce in the within-company decomposition stratified by archetype.** Under `seniority_final`, LLM/GenAI within-component is +0.148 but Java enterprise is −0.054, Defense −0.027, Agile-generalist −0.185. The T09 "every large archetype rose" pattern is a between-company composition artifact, not a within-company fact. This is material for the Gate 3 reading of T09/T08.

### 3a. Why the "tool-stack adopter" cluster is the clean test of Alt C

Cluster 3 is exactly the pattern Alt C predicts: employers adopting AI vocabulary (+0.52 pp AI rate, >10× the panel mean baseline) and writing longer, scope-heavier descriptions, while their seniority distribution moves by less than a rounding step under either operationalization. These 46 companies did not narrow their junior rung; they restructured their *tool stack vocabulary*.

The companies in cluster 3 skew toward system integrators, consulting, cloud/enterprise software, and staffing firms that rebrand roles quickly — exactly the kinds of employers that would rewrite descriptions to match industry vocabulary shifts. They do NOT skew tech-native (no FAANG here; Adobe is the most tech-native). Gate 2 speculated the tool-stack adopters would be FAANG-adjacent; the data says the opposite — it's the **downstream adopters** rewriting their templates to look modern.

**Verdict on within-company entry share:**
- Under `seniority_final`: panel entry rate **falls within-company** by 3.18 pp, partially offset by a +1.90 pp between-company composition gain.
- Under YOE ≤ 2: panel entry rate **rises within-company** by 1.52 pp, with a much larger +5.36 pp between-company composition gain.

**The two operationalizations disagree directionally on the within-company component** (−0.032 vs +0.015), confirming T06's earlier finding on the 115-company panel now on the full 240-company panel. This is the spec-dependence finding. Under EITHER operationalization, the aggregate rise in entry share is dominated by the between-company (new entrant composition) component — a company that's in BOTH 2024 and 2026 is NOT running more entry roles on average. The "entry share rose" story requires the new 2026 entrants to do the work.

**AI prevalence is overwhelmingly within-company** (+0.2103 of +0.2291 total change, 92%). The same 240 companies are listing AI vocabulary at 6.7× their 2024 rate. This is the cleanest within-company version of the RQ3 AI headline on the overlap panel.

**Description length and scope are mostly within-company** (desc 51% within, scope 82% within). Companies in both periods are writing longer, scope-heavier descriptions.

## 6. Archetype pivots

For each overlap-panel company we compute the distribution over T09 archetype labels in 2024 and 2026 (excluding the "No text / raw-only" gap bucket), then measure whether the **dominant archetype changed** and compute total variation distance (TVD) between the two period distributions.

- **179 of 240 companies (74.6%)** changed dominant archetype between 2024 and 2026.
- Restricted to companies with >=5 archetype-labeled rows per period: **60 of 82 (73.2%)** still pivoted.
- **Median per-company TVD = 0.629.** Half the companies have more than 63% of their archetype mass redistributed between periods.

This is unexpectedly high. Even with a minimum-5 filter the pivot rate holds, so it's not a small-sample artifact. The most likely mechanism is that companies are posting *different kinds* of SWE roles across the two snapshots — consistent with Cluster 3's "tool-stack adopter" pattern, but it applies broadly beyond cluster 3.

Caveat: T09 archetype labels cover 53.5% of rows (arshkon 99.9%, scraped 30.7% because text_source='llm' gate). For overlap companies, the arshkon side is well-labeled and the scraped side is text-coverage-capped. A company's 2026 labeled sample may be a non-random subset of its 2026 postings (Stage 9 router may favor certain titles). So the pivot rate may overstate true reshuffling if Stage 9 favored AI/LLM-looking titles.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **Tool-stack adopters are NOT tech-native.** The 46-company cluster 3 is dominated by consulting firms, system integrators, and enterprise software, not FAANG / AI-labs. The companies with the biggest ΔAI are the ones rewriting their templates to match industry vocabulary, not the ones actually building the AI systems.
2. **Archetype pivot rate is 73% at >=5 per period.** Companies are reconfiguring what SWE roles they post, not just growing their post count. This is the cleanest within-company evidence for "employer-side restructuring" — but the restructuring is *in archetype*, not in seniority.
3. **Within-company entry-final trend is NOT uniform across archetypes.** LLM/GenAI and Data engineering rise within-company (+0.148, +0.144); Java enterprise, Defense, Agile-generalist fall within-company (−0.054 to −0.185). T09's aggregate uniform rise was between-company composition, not within-company.
4. **The YOE ≤ 2 within-component collapses to +0.007 under aggregator exclusion.** The modest YOE-based within-company entry rise is entirely an aggregator phenomenon on the overlap panel. Real direct-employer companies in the overlap panel have roughly flat YOE ≤ 2 rates.
5. **New-in-2026 entrants look LESS junior under YOE ≤ 2 (0.152 vs 0.196 returning), but MORE junior under seniority_final (0.072 vs 0.061).** Specification dependence re-emerges at the new-entrant vs returning cut.

## Data quality caveats

- **`seniority_final` denominator drift.** 53% of scraped rows are `seniority_final = 'unknown'` (Stage 10 LLM budget exhausted). The 240-company panel is small enough that denominator drift at the company level produces unstable entry_final rates for companies with few known-seniority rows. Cluster 1 (the −0.49 entry dropper cluster) is dominated by companies whose 2024 "entry rate" is 50-100% off a sample of 3-12 rows. It is a small-n denominator artifact, not a real flip. The decomposition's 197-company denominator (rather than 240) reflects exclusion of companies where one period had zero known-seniority rows.
- **Archetype labels have 30.7% coverage on the scraped side** (vs 99.9% on arshkon). Per-company archetype distributions on 2026 may be biased by which titles Stage 9 routed to LLM extraction. The 73% pivot rate should be treated as an upper bound until the router's title-selection bias can be characterized.
- **Desc_len uses raw description char count.** Gate 1's length growth finding includes this metric. Characters are not boilerplate-sensitive at the count level, but the interpretation of "scope from desc length" IS — desc grew +1,767 chars on average in panel companies, of which T13 shows ~37% is responsibilities/role_summary and 0% is requirements.
- **Scope uses V1-validated patterns only** (end-to-end + cross-functional). Expected precision ~96-100%. Bare `ownership` is excluded.

| Dimension | Applied | Direction-changing? |
|---|---|---|
| (a) Aggregator exclusion | ✓ | No direction flip for entry (sen_fin), YOE ≤ 2, AI, scope. YOE ≤ 2 within shrinks to near-zero (materially sensitive on magnitude). |
| (b) Company capping (50/co) | Not applied as primary (240 companies × small avg n per period). Impact would be on cluster-3 Adobe/Deloitte-type companies whose 2026 n reaches ~60-130 — capped to 50 would dampen their Δ values but not change cluster membership. |
| (c) Seniority operationalization | ✓ (both `seniority_final` and YOE ≤ 2 throughout) | **Direction-flip on within-company entry component** — this IS the headline. |

**Material sensitivities:**
- YOE ≤ 2 within-company entry change under aggregator exclusion (+0.015 → +0.007) — magnitude sensitive, direction stable.
- `seniority_final` within-company entry change direction differs from YOE ≤ 2 direction — structural spec dependence (RQ5 evidence).
