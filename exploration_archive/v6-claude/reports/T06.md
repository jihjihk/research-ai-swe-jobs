## Headline (read this section before Wave 2)

1. **Company concentration is moderate-to-high in every source** (scraped Gini ≈ 0.69, top-20 share 18%). It is actively high enough to drive corpus-level term frequencies, topic models, and anything that aggregates mentions-per-posting.
2. **Entry-level posting is *highly* concentrated on a small subset of companies. 79% of arshkon companies, 89% of asaniczka companies, and 79% of scraped companies with ≥5 SWE postings post ZERO entry-labeled roles** (under `seniority_final`). The aggregate "entry share" is driven by a small group of entry-active companies, not a market-wide behavior.
3. **The entry-share change on the arshkon ↔ scraped overlap panel flips sign depending on operationalization.** Under `seniority_final`: arshkon 6.65% → scraped 5.74%, a **-0.9 pp DECLINE** with within-company component = **-3.2 pp**. Under YOE ≤ 2: arshkon 12.3% → scraped 19.7%, a **+7.4 pp INCREASE** with within-company component = **+1.1 pp** (the rest is composition). **These are the same companies. The operationalization is what changes the sign.** Downstream agents MUST report both.
4. **Stage 4 fixed the duplicate-template problem.** No within-source company has a dup_ratio that violates the ≈1.0 target after multi-location collapse. The prior "6 companies with 4-25 identical descriptions" issue is gone. Residual dup_ratios in scraped (Turing 3.0, DataAnnotation 2.7, Mercor 1.8) are small and are real repostings over the scrape window, not group artifacts.
5. **Cross-company hash collisions are small** — top collision is 6 companies sharing one description (likely an aggregator reposting under alternate company labels not caught by the canonicalizer). Worth noting but not structural.
6. **Top-6 scraped entry-final companies account for 18.8% of the entry pool (vs the prior run's 23%).** TikTok + ByteDance alone are 10.4% of the entry pool. The prior run's finding was substantially driven by description duplication; the current finding is driven by genuine entry-poster concentration plus one ByteDance-group spike. Treat the scraped entry pool as a small-number problem even after the fix.

### Reading the decomposition

**Entry share — sign flips.**
- Under `seniority_final`: **within-company entry share fell 3.2 pp**, but the mix of companies in the panel shifted toward higher-entry companies, bringing the aggregate only -0.9 pp. **If you held composition constant, every company on average hired *fewer* entry-labeled roles.**
- Under YOE ≤ 2: within-company rose only 1.1 pp; the overwhelming driver is composition (+6.3 pp). **If you held composition constant, the YOE ≤ 2 share barely moved.**
- **The two operationalizations disagree on the direction of within-company change at the same company-year panel.** This is the most important disagreement in the entire Wave 1 exploration.

Most likely mechanisms (to be investigated in Wave 2):
1. **Classifier shift.** Stage 5 title keywords changed between pipeline versions, or Stage 10's LLM is stricter than the arshkon native classifier. A row that arshkon labeled `entry` at YOE ≤ 2 now gets `mid-senior` under `seniority_final`. This would simultaneously decrease `seniority_final` entry share and leave YOE ≤ 2 unchanged. **This is consistent with the pattern.**
2. **Employer relabeling.** Companies post the same YOE range under a mid-level title now that they used to post under an entry title. This would also decrease `seniority_final` entry share and increase or leave flat YOE ≤ 2, which is what we see.
3. **Real scope inflation.** Companies ask for 2 YOE but call the role "Software Engineer II" now that they used to call "New Grad." This is RQ1 in its pure form and also fits the pattern.

**Nothing in the data distinguishes these three.** All three predict the observed disagreement. Wave 2 needs a title×YOE contingency for the panel companies to pick between them.

**Description length.** Within-company (+963) is larger than between-company (+803) and accounts for 55% of the total change. **This is good news** — it means length inflation is a within-company phenomenon, not a composition effect. Same Anduril, same JPMorgan, same Wells Fargo write longer descriptions now than they did in 2024.

**AI mention rate.** Within-company (+14.8 pp) is 95% of total change. **AI mentions are overwhelmingly within-company**; composition contributes essentially nothing. The "AI mentions are exploding" story is robust to composition.

### Implication

Any Wave 2 finding that depends on entry share direction must cite both operationalizations and both decomposition components. The agent recommends: **report `seniority_final` as the primary result, report YOE ≤ 2 as the proxy, and report the within-company decomposition on the overlap panel as the cleanest single estimate of the effect.** Headline: under `seniority_final`, within-company entry share dropped 3.2 pp at the same 115 companies that appear in both panels; under YOE ≤ 2, it rose 1.1 pp at 121 companies. The direction flip is itself the finding.

**Top-3 findings most likely to be concentration-driven and flip direction under a reasonable re-weighting, plus recommended handling:**

1. **Entry share cross-period change.** Sign depends on operationalization and on whether you use the overlap panel vs. aggregate. **Recommendation:** present `seniority_final` and YOE ≤ 2 side by side on the 125-company overlap panel, with within-company decomposition as the primary estimate. 2. **Corpus term frequencies for "junior/entry/scope inflation" language.** A single well-known company with a boilerplate template can move a term frequency by several percentage points. **Recommendation:** cap at 50 postings per `company_name_canonical` as primary; run once without capping as sensitivity; use company-name stoplist from preamble.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **The entry-share sign flip under `seniority_final` vs YOE proxy survives the overlap-panel decomposition.** This is not a sampling artifact; it is a classifier-vs-proxy disagreement on the same 115 companies. Wave 2 needs to figure out which side is right (it probably cannot, without hand-labeling).
2. **Walmart contributes 73.8% of its own YOE ≤ 2 rate, accounting for ~3.6% of the entire scraped YOE ≤ 2 pool.** I did not expect one retailer to be this dominant in a "junior share" metric.
3. **79-89% of substantive-presence companies post zero entry-labeled roles in every source.** This is the single most important framing for RQ1: "entry share" is a metric about a small subset of the employer population, not the median company.
4. **Stage 4 fix worked cleanly.** Dup_ratio = 1.0 for all scraped entry-posting companies. The prior-run's "6 companies with identical descriptions" is no longer visible, which means the concentration pattern we see now (top-6 = 18.8% of the entry pool) is the genuine concentration pattern.
5. **TikTok + ByteDance alone = 10.4% of the scraped entry pool.** This is a single corporate family. If ByteDance corporate policy changes, the scraped entry pool moves several percentage points.
6. **AI mention rate is the one finding that concentration does not threaten.** Within-component is 95% of the total change, and the effect is 50×-400× larger than any other metric. This is the most concentration-robust finding in the dataset.
7. **Cross-company hash collisions are small (max 6 companies) but real.** Probably aggregator relabeling under alternate brand names. A follow-up canonicalizer pass should fold them.

## Data quality caveats

- `company_name_canonical` is imperfect — some of the "new 2026" 5916 companies are almost certainly returning companies under a different brand label. This would inflate the between-company composition component.
- `seniority_final` is `unknown` for a substantial share of rows, and the unknown share differs by source. All entry-share numbers exclude unknowns; the unknown share itself is diagnostic and should be tracked separately.
- Aggregator detection depends on Stage 4 rules; false negatives are possible (e.g., small aggregators not in the rule list).
- YOE extraction via regex covers only ~60-70% of postings; the yoe_le2 share is always computed over the YOE-present subset and is sensitive to which companies write explicit YOE floors.
