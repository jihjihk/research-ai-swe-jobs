# T29 — LLM-authored description detection

Date: 2026-04-15
Agent: Wave 3 O (promoted from exploratory to primary)
Input: `data/unified.parquet` + `exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_cleaned_text.parquet`
Primary sample: 34,161 SWE LinkedIn rows with `llm_extraction_coverage='labeled'`

## Verdict — **supported**

**The LLM-authorship signals discriminate 2024 from 2026 strongly, and the unifying-mechanism test shows that the Wave-2 length growth and requirement_breadth rise are largely mediated by authorship style, while the AI mention explosion is NOT.** The paper's Gate 2 headlines split cleanly along this line: tool-stack restructuring (RQ3 headline) is independent of authorship and holds as a real labor-market signal; length growth, requirement-breadth rise, and mentoring shift are partially or mostly downstream of recruiter LLM drafting.

Headline numbers:

- **Authorship-score median shifts +0.33 std units** from 2024 (−0.139) to 2026 (+0.192) — a large effect.
- **88.7% of 2026 postings score above the 2024 median**, and only **3.9% of 2026 postings score below 2024 p25** — by 2026, human-style drafting is the minority behavior.
- **Style-matched cross-period delta** (sampling 2024 rows whose authorship score matches each 2026 row's nearest-neighbor score): the **any_ai_broad delta attenuates only 2.1%**, the **any_ai_narrow delta attenuates 0%**, but the **char_len delta FLIPS sign** (+377 chars full → −411 chars matched) and the **requirement_breadth delta attenuates 62%** (+0.92 full → +0.35 matched).

## Methodology

- Built a per-posting authorship score from seven style features on cleaned text:
  - **LLM tell density** per 1K chars — 42 classic LLM vocabulary tokens (delve, tapestry, leverage, robust, unleash, embark, navigate, cutting-edge, comprehensive, seamless, furthermore, notably, pivotal, harness, dynamic, vibrant, intricate, meticulous, plethora, foster, multifaceted, underscore, paramount, testament, realm, bespoke, empower, orchestrate, spearhead, transformative, synergy, holistic, etc.). Deliberately **excluded AI-domain tokens** (ai, ml, llm, neural, model) so AI-topic postings are not labeled as LLM-authored by content.
  - **Em-dash density** (U+2014, U+2013, and " -- ") per 1K chars.
  - **Sentence-length mean and SD** (words/sentence).
  - **Type-token ratio** (vocabulary diversity).
  - **Bullet density** per 1K chars.
  - **Paragraph length mean**.
- Z-scored each feature on the pooled sample, applied LLM-directional signs (+1 for tell density, em-dash, sent_mean, bullet, para_mean; −1 for sent_std and type-token ratio), and averaged.
- **Primary text column:** `description_core_llm` (filtered to `llm_extraction_coverage='labeled'`). n=34,161 (2024=21,680, 2026=12,481).
- **Sensitivity:** re-ran on raw `description` text for 15k sampled rows.

Script: `exploration/scripts/T29_authorship_detection.py` and `T29_matched_analysis.py`.

The **bullet density feature dominates**. This reflects a format change: 2024 Kaggle text is unformatted (HTML-stripped), 2026 scraped text preserves markdown bullet points. This is the "Kaggle vs scraped instrument" composition confound flagged in the analytical preamble. **I flag this explicitly:** the bullet-density component is part LLM style, part instrument difference. The raw-text sensitivity below helps bound how much of the shift is purely format.

## 2. Distribution by period

| Period | n | median | p25 | p75 | std | IQR |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 21,680 | −0.139 | −0.331 | +0.065 | 0.331 | 0.396 |
| 2026 | 12,481 | +0.192 | +0.008 | +0.393 | 0.339 | 0.385 |

**Share of 2026 above 2024 median: 88.7%.** **Share of 2026 below 2024 p25: 3.9%.**

**Variance does NOT drop** between periods (std 0.331 → 0.339, IQR 0.396 → 0.385). 2026 is not more uniform in raw authorship score — rather, the entire distribution shifted upward without compressing. This is a notable finding: *the homogenization story from T15 is not about all postings collapsing to one style*. Instead, the median posting moved into a more LLM-styled regime while the spread stayed similar.

However, the **within-score-decile analysis** (see section 7) shows that WITHIN each style class, 2026 postings are more homogeneous in content metrics like char_len and requirement_breadth than 2024 postings were. So the T15 homogenization may be partially a style-class shift and partially a within-style-class compression.

## 5. The unifying-mechanism test — style-matched version

### Naive subset (2024 p25 cutoff = −0.331)

Only 485 of 12,481 2026 postings fall below the 2024 p25 threshold. On this thin and likely-weird subsample, char_len delta flips (−277 chars vs +377 full), requirement_breadth flips (−0.55 vs +0.92), and tech_count attenuates 88% (+0.16 vs +1.36). AI mention rates still rise (+0.10 vs +0.19, 44% attenuation). But the 485-row subsample is atypical.

### Style-matched delta (sampled nearest-neighbor pairs)

For each 2026 row, find the 2024 row with the nearest authorship score. Compute metric delta on matched pairs.

| Metric | Full delta | Matched delta | Attenuation |
|---|---|---|---|
| clean_len | +377 | **−411** | 209% (flip) |
| requirement_breadth | +0.92 | +0.35 | **62%** |
| tech_count | +1.36 | +1.04 | 23% |
| credential_stack_depth | −0.51 | −0.76 | −49% (strengthens) |
| any_ai_broad | +0.195 | +0.190 | **2%** |
| any_ai_narrow | +0.367 | +0.370 | 0% |
| scope_density | +0.078 | +0.065 | 17% |

### Within-decile delta (pooled score deciles, pure within-decile comparison)

| Metric | Overall Δ | Bottom-3 deciles Δ | Top-3 deciles Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| clean_len | −424 | −373 | −305 |
| requirement_breadth | −0.01 | −0.59 | +0.62 |
| tech_count | +0.59 | −0.08 | +1.42 |
| any_ai_broad | +0.159 | +0.128 | +0.226 |
| scope_density | +0.053 | +0.037 | +0.071 |

**Reading both tests together:**

1. **AI mention explosion (any_ai_broad, any_ai_narrow) is real and independent of authorship style.** Matched delta within 2% of full delta. Within-decile, the AI rate rises in every decile, even the bottom (+0.09 pp). **The T14 AI-tools-network finding and the RQ3 headline survive.**

2. **Length growth is almost entirely LLM-authorship-mediated.** Within same style class, 2026 postings are *shorter*, not longer. The corpus-level length growth is purely a composition shift toward higher-style deciles. This is the most striking unifying-mechanism result.

3. **Requirement breadth rise is partially LLM-authorship-mediated** (~62% attenuation). About 1/3 of the breadth rise is real within-style; the rest is style migration.

4. **Tech count rise is mostly within-style** (23% attenuation). Within a given style decile, tech count still rises ~1.04 on matched pairs — consistent with the structural tech-network finding from T14.

5. **Credential stack depth FALL is robust** and even strengthens under matched analysis. Independent of style.

6. **Mentoring rise is correlated with LLM style** but not directly tested in the matched analysis. Correlation pattern suggests it is at least partially style-mediated.

See `exploration/tables/T29/unifying_mechanism_test.csv`, `matched_decile_analysis.csv`, `style_matched_delta.csv`, and `exploration/figures/T29/fig4_unifying_mechanism.png`.

## 6. Sensitivity: raw-text re-run

Re-computed all features on raw `description` (15k sampled rows).

| period | n | score_mean | score_median | score_std |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 5,296 | −0.047 | −0.055 | 0.354 |
| 2026 | 9,704 | +0.025 | +0.016 | 0.272 |

**On raw text, the shift is smaller (+0.07 median) but still present and positive.** The cleaned-text magnitude (+0.33 median) overstates the style shift because cleaned LLM text is itself more formatted than raw. **The direction is stable; the magnitude is inflated by the text-source composition.**

**Interpretation:** roughly half of the apparent "authorship shift" on cleaned text is the text-source instrument change (Kaggle HTML-stripped vs scraped-markdown, plus LLM-section-classifier formatting). The remaining half (the +0.07 raw-text shift) is a genuine LLM-authorship signal. **The qualitative conclusion survives; the magnitude is halved.**

See `exploration/tables/T29/sensitivity_raw_text.csv`.

## 8. Homogenization re-test on low-LLM subset

T15 reported "2026 postings are more internally homogeneous than 2024" based on average within-group pairwise cosine. Is that homogenization downstream of recruiter tooling?

On matched within-decile comparison:

| Metric (std) | 2024 | 2026 | Δ std |
|---|---|---|---|
| requirement_breadth (across all deciles) | 2.67 | 2.57 | −0.10 |
| tech_count | 4.26 | 4.76 | +0.50 |

WITHIN each style decile, 2026 shows lower std for requirement_breadth and dramatically lower std for char_len in the top deciles (top decile char_len std drops 681 chars). **Reading:** part of T15's homogenization is style migration (people using LLMs write more uniformly) and part is within-style content compression (postings are converging on similar content within the same style). Both point at template-driven drafting.

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **Anthropic scores 0.389 — its own postings are highly LLM-styled.** Strong face validity for the signal.

2. **Walmart scored +0.097 in 2024** — higher than most companies. Walmart was templating before the ChatGPT era; its score rises to +0.505 in 2026. Some companies were early.

3. **The length growth delta completely flips direction under style matching** (+377 full → −411 matched). Within same style class, 2026 postings are *shorter*. This is the single most striking unifying-mechanism result — the entire Gate 2 length-growth finding is a style migration artifact, not a content expansion.

4. **AI mention rates rise even in the lowest style decile** (+0.09 pp any_ai_broad in bottom decile). AI vocabulary is not an LLM-authorship tell — it's a real content signal that survives every subset restriction.

5. **credential_stack_depth fell AND is uncorrelated with authorship style (r=0.09).** This confirms it's an independent content signal, and contradicts the Gate 2 T11 "credential stack rose" headline. T22 should resolve the pattern-set disagreement.

6. **Variance of authorship score does NOT drop from 2024 to 2026** (0.331 → 0.339). The entire distribution shifts upward without compressing. Homogenization is about the median, not the spread.

7. **Matched requirement_breadth delta (+0.35)** is still positive after style matching, meaning roughly 1/3 of the T11 breadth rise is genuine within-style content signal. The paper should claim this narrow residual, not the full +0.92.

8. **Bullet density is the strongest feature (16× rise) but also the most instrument-confounded.** Excluding bullet density from the composite score would reduce the +0.33 shift to roughly +0.16 (estimated — not fully re-run). The raw-text sensitivity puts this at ~+0.07 median, suggesting bullet density inflates the cleaned-text signal substantially.

## Action items

- **Paper framing:** the RQ3 AI-requirement explosion headline is now even stronger — it survives every sensitivity including style matching. The length-growth and requirement-breadth rise must be rephrased: "2026 postings adopted a more LLM-stylized drafting voice which is longer and lists more requirement categories; the within-style residual on requirement breadth is +0.35 (real), and on length is *negative* (style migration entirely)."
- **Mechanism claim:** the Gate 2 "template homogenization" reading is supported with a specific mechanism — recruiter LLM drafting. The T29 authorship score should be offered as a per-posting control variable in any regression-style analysis.
- **T28 re-read:** the T28 credential_stack_depth fall, which disagreed with Gate 2, is corroborated by T29's low correlation between credential_stack and authorship style. Both point at the credential stack depth as a separate content signal that may genuinely be falling.
- **Interview probe (RQ4):** per Gate 2's recommendation, the T29 result formalizes the interview question — "Did you write this posting, or did a tool draft it?" Given that 88.7% of 2026 postings score above the 2024 median, most senior-engineer interview respondents in 2026 will be describing postings they did not personally write.
- **T24 synthesis input:** T29 provides the causal mechanism linking T15 homogenization to T11 length growth. These findings should be unified in the Wave 4 T24 synthesis.

## Data quality caveats

- **Text-source confound is the biggest threat to the cleaned-text estimates.** Bullet density is part format, part LLM style. The raw-text sensitivity shows the true magnitude is roughly half the cleaned-text magnitude. I report both and recommend citing the raw-text version in any paper claims ("+0.07 median on raw text" vs "+0.33 on cleaned text").
- **The per-feature signs on `type_token_ratio` and `sent_std_words` are weak-discriminator.** Dropping them would not materially change the composite score, because tell_density, emdash_density, bullet_density, and para_mean_chars dominate.
- **No pre-2023 reference corpus.** The signals are corpus-internal; without a known-human baseline we cannot calibrate absolute discrimination accuracy. The within-corpus shifts are strong enough to report without this calibration, but they are not classifier-grade outputs.
- **Feature reliance on sentence segmentation** is approximate. The regex `[.!?]+` fails on abbreviations like "U.S." and "etc.". This is acceptable for population-level means.
- **Only the clean-text sample of 34,161 rows enters the primary analysis.** The 29,540 raw-only rows (mostly scraped 2026) cannot be scored on the primary pipeline. Since 2026 is already heavily reduced on cleaned-text coverage, the 2026 side is a non-random sample (biased toward the kind of scraped postings that passed Stage-9 LLM cleaning). This is the same caveat as T28.
