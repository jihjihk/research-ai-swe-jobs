# T02: Seniority Comparability & Label Quality

## Headline Finding

**Asaniczka `associate` is NOT a usable junior proxy.** On every available signal — YOE distribution, title overlap, seniority_final conditional distribution, and rule-based imputation — asaniczka `associate` behaves like arshkon `associate`, not arshkon `entry`. Specifically: asaniczka associate has median YOE of 4.0 (vs 3.0 for arshkon entry), 72% are classified as `associate` by seniority_final (vs 98.4% of arshkon entry being classified as `entry`), and its rule-based imputation profile matches arshkon associate almost exactly. Treating asaniczka `associate` as entry-level would inflate the 2024 entry-level baseline and bias trend estimates.

## 3. Comparability Verdict

**Asaniczka `associate` behaves like arshkon `associate`, NOT like arshkon `entry`.** Evidence:

| Signal | Closer to entry? | Closer to associate? | Closer to mid-senior? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Median YOE | No (4.0 vs 3.0 for entry) | **Yes** (3.0 for assoc) | Partially (5.0 for mid) |
| Mean YOE | No (4.53 vs 4.12) | **Closest** (3.85) | Getting close (5.56) |
| Title overlap (row-weighted) | No (38.1%) | Partial (32.4%) | **Highest** (43.5%) |
| seniority_final profile | No (2.0% entry) | **Yes** (72.0% assoc) | Partial (25.9% mid) |
| seniority_imputed profile | No (3.3% entry) | **Yes** (6.9% assoc) | Closest to assoc |
| Junior title cues | Inconclusive | — | — |

**Decision:** Asaniczka `associate` is classified as **"lower-mid"** — a mix of associate and lower mid-senior, NOT a junior proxy. It should NOT be pooled with entry-level for any analysis.

**Practical consequence for entry-level trend analysis:**
- The only 2024 entry-level baseline is arshkon: **769 SWE rows** (seniority_native=entry) or **848** (seniority_final=entry).
- Asaniczka provides zero usable entry-level rows for the primary comparison.
- seniority_llm would resolve this in principle (by applying uniform labels to asaniczka descriptions), but at 2.6% known coverage for asaniczka SWE it currently yields only **39 entry-level asaniczka rows** — too few to materially expand the baseline.

## Surprises & Unexpected Patterns

1. **Asaniczka associate YOE is HIGHER than arshkon entry, not lower.** Mean 4.53 vs 4.12, median 4.0 vs 3.0. If anything, asaniczka associate skews more experienced than arshkon entry. This is the opposite of what a "junior proxy" assumption would predict.

2. **Arshkon entry has surprisingly high YOE.** Median 3.0 years for postings labeled "entry" on LinkedIn. This suggests either (a) LinkedIn's entry label is generous, or (b) YOE requirements in entry-level postings were already inflated in 2024. Compare with scraped entry at median 2.0 — this could be a genuine temporal shift or a source artifact.

3. **seniority_llm disagrees with seniority_native for arshkon entry.** Among the 85 arshkon entry rows where seniority_llm is non-null and non-unknown, 53 are classified as mid-senior (62.4%). The LLM's explicit-signal-only approach apparently detects senior signals in many postings that LinkedIn labeled as entry. This means either LinkedIn's entry label is noisy, or the LLM is miscalibrated. This warrants investigation before relying on seniority_llm.

4. **The 129 asaniczka "entry" rows in seniority_final are imputation artifacts.** Since asaniczka has zero native entry labels, these 129 entry rows come from rule-based title/description pattern matching. They have 0% industry coverage (asaniczka-wide gap) and only 4.7% ghost_llm coverage.

5. **seniority_final upclassifies ~26% of asaniczka associate to mid-senior.** This is consistent with the YOE evidence that asaniczka associate includes a substantial chunk of what would be mid-senior elsewhere.

## Data Quality Caveats

- YOE extraction is available for only 56-67% of rows in each seniority group, so YOE-based comparisons represent the subset with extractable mentions.
- Title cue analysis relies on regex patterns and may miss unconventional phrasing.
- seniority_llm comparisons use very small sample sizes (24-118 labeled rows per group) and should be treated as directional only.
- The comparability audit uses SWE rows with default filters only. Results may differ for SWE-adjacent or control groups.

## Recommended Usage Rules

1. **Entry-level 2024 baseline:** Use arshkon seniority_native=entry (n=769) as primary. Do not pool asaniczka.
2. **Seniority primary variable:** Use seniority_final (best coverage, 80-100% known). Report seniority_native and seniority_llm (where available) as ablation.
3. **Asaniczka associate:** Treat as "associate/lower-mid" in seniority analyses. Do not reclassify as junior.
4. **Cross-period entry share:** Report arshkon-only 2024 baseline alongside any pooled estimate. Flag directional discrepancy.
5. **seniority_llm caveat:** Investigate the 62.4% mid-senior rate for arshkon native-entry rows before using seniority_llm for any entry-level analysis.
