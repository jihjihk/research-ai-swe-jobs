# T03: Seniority Label Comparison

## Headline Finding

The choice of seniority operationalization **flips the direction of the RQ1 entry-level trend**. Using arshkon-only as the 2024 baseline, all rule-based variants (seniority_final, seniority_native, seniority_final_highconf) show a **decrease** in entry-level share from 2024 to 2026 (~21% to ~14%). Pooling asaniczka into 2024 (where it has near-zero entry labels) reverses this to an **increase** (3.6% to 14.2%). The seniority_llm column -- which can label asaniczka rows -- shows entry share increasing from 6.4% (asaniczka) / 16.7% (arshkon) to 27.7% (scraped), but on very small sample sizes (504-649 labeled non-unknown rows per source). This directional instability is the single most important finding for RQ1 credibility.

## Methodology

- **Population:** LinkedIn, English, date_flag=ok, is_swe=true rows (~63K total; arshkon 5,019; asaniczka 23,213; scraped 35,062).
- **Cross-tabulations:** seniority_native vs seniority_final, seniority_imputed vs seniority_final, seniority_native vs seniority_imputed (where both non-null/non-unknown). Also seniority_llm vs seniority_final and seniority_llm vs seniority_native where labeled.
- **Agreement metrics:** Simple agreement rate and Cohen's kappa for each pair.
- **Per-class accuracy:** Rule-based imputed vs native as pseudo-ground truth, separately for arshkon and scraped.
- **Junior share:** Computed under 6 operationalizations: seniority_final, seniority_native, seniority_final_highconf, seniority_final_POOLED_2024, seniority_llm (arshkon 2024), seniority_llm (asaniczka 2024).

## Results

### 1. Cross-tabulation agreement

| Pair | Agreement | Kappa | N |
|---|---|---|---|
| native vs final | 0.956 | 0.841 | 55,734 |
| imputed vs final | 0.951 | 0.715 | 37,726 |
| native vs imputed | 0.876 | 0.354 | 33,584 |
| llm vs final | 0.744 | 0.468 | 1,766 |
| llm vs native | 0.794 | 0.507 | 1,503 |

**Interpretation:** Native and final agree very well (kappa 0.84, near-perfect), as expected since final backfills from native. Imputed vs final is also high because imputed feeds final. The real diagnostic is native vs imputed: kappa only 0.354 (fair agreement), indicating that title-keyword rules have substantial disagreement with platform labels. LLM vs native/final kappa is moderate (0.47-0.51) -- driven by the LLM's explicit-signal-only design classifying many rows as unknown that native labels as mid-senior.

### 2. Rule-based classifier accuracy (imputed vs native)

**Arshkon SWE (1,706 rows with both):**

| Class | Precision | Recall | F1 | Support |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| entry | 0.49 | 0.34 | 0.40 | 143 |
| associate | 0.12 | 0.15 | 0.13 | 89 |
| mid-senior | 0.92 | 0.96 | 0.94 | 1,428 |
| director | 0.40 | 0.21 | 0.28 | 19 |
| Overall accuracy | | | 0.84 | 1,706 |

**Scraped SWE (15,727 rows with both):**

| Class | Precision | Recall | F1 | Support |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| entry | 0.46 | 0.54 | 0.50 | 972 |
| associate | 0.07 | 0.17 | 0.09 | 490 |
| mid-senior | 0.93 | 0.92 | 0.93 | 13,382 |
| director | 0.40 | 0.22 | 0.28 | 410 |
| Overall accuracy | | | 0.82 | 15,727 |

**Key finding: No temporal instability.** Arshkon and scraped show nearly identical overall accuracy (84% vs 82%) and very similar per-class patterns. Both periods have the same weaknesses: poor associate classification (F1 0.09-0.13, many misclassified as mid-senior), entry recall around 34-54%, and reasonable mid-senior performance. The classifier is temporally stable but uniformly poor at distinguishing associate from mid-senior.

### 3. Asaniczka seniority profile

| Column | Entry | Associate | Mid-senior | Unknown |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| seniority_native | 0 | 2,014 | 21,199 | 0 |
| seniority_final | 129 | 1,451 | 21,542 | 0 |
| seniority_llm (labeled) | 39 | 69 | 498 | 2,164 |

Asaniczka native has literally zero entry-level labels -- only associate and mid-senior. seniority_final recovers 129 entry-level labels via title keywords, but this is an extremely thin 0.56% rate. seniority_llm labels 39 asaniczka rows as entry out of 613 non-unknown, yielding a 6.4% entry rate -- substantially higher than rule-based but still below arshkon's rate. The LLM finds genuine entry-level signals in asaniczka that native labels miss entirely.

### 4. RQ1 junior share by operationalization

**Primary comparison: arshkon 2024 vs scraped 2026 (combined Mar + Apr):**

| Operationalization | 2024 Entry Share | 2026 Entry Share | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| seniority_final (arshkon only) | 21.0% | 14.2% | DECREASE |
| seniority_native (arshkon only) | 22.3% | 13.7% | DECREASE |
| seniority_final_highconf (arshkon only) | 21.4% | 14.4% | DECREASE |
| seniority_final POOLED 2024 | 3.6% | 14.2% | INCREASE |
| seniority_llm (arshkon) | 16.7% | 27.7% | INCREASE |
| seniority_llm (asaniczka) | 6.4% | 27.7% | INCREASE |

**Critical observations:**
1. All three rule-based operationalizations agree when using arshkon-only 2024 baseline: entry share **decreased** from ~21% to ~14%, a drop of ~7 percentage points.
2. Pooling asaniczka into 2024 **reverses the direction** to an apparent increase. This confirms the documented artifact.
3. seniority_llm shows the **opposite direction** from rule-based: entry share increases from 16.7% to 27.7%. However, this is based on very small samples (504 non-unknown arshkon, 649 non-unknown scraped). The LLM's high unknown rate (~82%) means only explicit-signal rows survive, which may not be representative.

### 5. seniority_final_source composition by source

| Source | title_keyword | native_backfill | unknown | Other |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| arshkon | 37.7% | 39.7% | 19.6% | 3.1% |
| asaniczka | 62.5% | 37.5% | 0% | 0% |
| scraped | 45.7% | 45.3% | 6.4% | 2.6% |

Asaniczka resolution is entirely split between title_keyword and native_backfill, with no unknown rows (since native is always available). Scraped has a much lower unknown rate (6.4%) than arshkon (19.6%), likely because scraped SWE titles are more keyword-rich.

## Surprises and Unexpected Patterns

1. **The LLM seniority column disagrees with all rule-based variants on the RQ1 direction.** This is not a data artifact -- it reflects a genuine methodological difference. The LLM's explicit-signal-only design produces high unknown rates and small labeled samples, making its entry-share estimates unstable. But it also finds entry-level signals that native labels miss (especially in asaniczka).

2. **Associate classification is catastrophically bad.** Across both periods, the rule-based classifier has F1 of 0.09-0.13 for associate-level roles. Most associates are misclassified as mid-senior. This means any analysis that distinguishes associate from mid-senior using rule-based labels is unreliable.

3. **Scraped data has a much higher entry-level share in seniority_final (13.3%) than arshkon (16.9%).** But when measured by seniority_native, both have similar magnitudes (22.3% vs 14.0%). The 2026 native entry-level count is actually substantial: 3,972 scraped entry-level postings vs 769 arshkon entry-level postings. Entry-level SWE postings are NOT vanishing in absolute terms.

4. **Intern labels are present in native data but collapse into other categories in seniority_final.** Native shows 20 interns (arshkon) and 585 interns (scraped), but seniority_final maps these to entry. The scraped data has ~14x more intern-labeled postings, suggesting either a genuine growth in intern postings or different LinkedIn labeling behavior in 2026.

5. **seniority_llm's unknown rate is ~82% even for labeled rows.** This means the LLM applies an extremely conservative explicit-signal threshold. Only ~18% of SWE postings have unambiguous seniority signals in their text/title. The unlabeled rows may systematically differ from labeled ones, creating selection bias.

## Data Quality Caveats

1. **Sample sizes for seniority_llm are dangerously small.** Only 504 arshkon and 649 scraped SWE rows have non-unknown LLM labels. These are not large enough for reliable share estimates, especially for minority classes like director.

2. **Arshkon's entry-level baseline is the binding constraint for rule-based RQ1.** All rule-based variants derive their 2024 entry rate primarily from arshkon's ~769-848 entry-level SWE postings. Arshkon is a single Kaggle snapshot from April 2024 with unknown selection methodology.

3. **Period 2026-04 has appeared in the scraped data** (8,565 mid-senior, 1,350 entry, etc.), indicating the scraper has been running into April 2026. The 2026 estimates should clarify whether they pool March and April scraped data.

4. **Native seniority labels in scraped data include executive (44 rows) and intern (585 rows)** that have no mapping path in rule-based columns. These collapse into other categories or unknown in seniority_final.

## Recommendation: Which seniority operationalization to use

| Analysis Type | Recommended | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **RQ1 entry-level share trend** | **seniority_native, arshkon-only 2024** as primary; seniority_final as sensitivity | Avoids the asaniczka contamination problem. seniority_native is highest quality where available. |
| **Cross-sectional comparison (single period)** | seniority_final | Best coverage within a period. Unknown rate is manageable for scraped (6.4%). |
| **Entry-level text analysis** | seniority_native = entry OR seniority_final = entry | Maximizes the entry-level sample while maintaining label quality. |
| **Senior archetype analysis** | seniority_final (mid-senior) | Abundant and reliable; mid-senior classification F1 ~0.94. |
| **Ablation / robustness** | Report all 4 variants | Agreement strengthens findings; disagreement is a critical flag. |
| **seniority_llm** | Cross-check only until larger budget run | Sample too small for primary estimates. Valuable for validating rule-based trends on the subset where both exist. |

**Bottom line for RQ1:** The entry-level share trend is sensitive to operationalization. Using rule-based labels with arshkon-only 2024 baseline: entry share decreased from ~21% to ~14%. Using seniority_llm: entry share increased from ~17% to ~28%. These directional disagreements must be prominently reported. The most defensible claim is that entry-level share is **in the 10-20% range across periods**, with the direction of change uncertain pending a larger LLM classification budget.

## Action Items

1. **Run a larger LLM classification budget** to increase seniority_llm coverage beyond 9,659 labeled SWE rows. The current labeled count provides only ~1,766 non-unknown rows, insufficient for reliable trend estimation.
2. **Report all 4 seniority variants** for any entry-level metric in downstream analyses. Flag directional disagreements prominently.
3. **Do not pool asaniczka with arshkon** for entry-level metrics using rule-based labels. This is confirmed to flip the trend direction.
4. **Investigate the intern/executive native labels** in scraped data -- are these a genuine LinkedIn labeling change or a scraper artifact?
5. **Consider a hybrid approach**: use seniority_llm where labeled, fall back to seniority_native for remaining rows. This maximizes both quality and coverage.
