# T22 — Ghost & Aspirational Requirements Forensics

**Agent M, Wave 3**

## TL;DR

- **Aspiration ratio rose across the board**, entry postings from mean 1.4 → 1.8 global; share with hedge/firm ratio > 1 rose from 23.7% → 33.6% for combined-column entry (+10pp) and from 16.4% → 26.8% for mid-senior.
- **Kitchen-sink score tripled** at every seniority. Entry combined 3.7% → 12.6% (3.4×) with score ≥ 12; mid-senior 7.7% → 27.3% (3.5×).
- **AI is 10× more hedged than anything else.** Within posts that mention an AI term, hedging language appears within an 80-char window at 11.5% per AI-term occurrence; firm language appears at only 1.1% per occurrence. That's a **10:1 hedge:firm ratio near AI terms** in both 2024 and 2026, vs a global hedge:firm ratio of ~1.4-1.8. The AI ghostiness is a composition effect: AI-mention prevalence quintupled from 14.3% → 51.2% of postings, and AI is structurally hedged — so as AI took over postings, average posting aspiration ratio mechanically rose.
- **Template saturation (copy-paste intra-company) rose 2024 → 2026** (mean pairwise cosine 0.604 → 0.669 after exact `description_hash` dedup). The 6 known exact-dup companies (Affirm, Canonical, Epic, Google, SkillStorm, Uber) contribute a ~5pp shift in the share of companies saturating above 0.8 (11.9% before dedup → 5.8% after).
- **YOE-scope mismatch is concentrated in the native/final seniority labels**, not in the combined column. Under arshkon's `seniority_native` 25.5% of entry postings have YOE≥5 (the known measurement artifact); under combined column only 2.9% do. This reconfirms Gate 1's warning that native-label entry comparisons are biased.
- **Top-20 ghost-like entry postings have zero overlap between combined-column and YOE-proxy entry definitions.** The two operationalizations identify completely different "most ghost-like" posts. Combined column surfaces genuine intern/new-grad roles with high tech breadth; YOE proxy surfaces senior-titled roles that happen to say "2+ years" somewhere in their minimum qualifications (mostly YOE-proxy false positives). **Finding: the YOE proxy is a clean entry-volume measure but a NOISY entry-content measure; qualitative ghost analysis should use the combined column.**
- **Aggregators have systematically LOWER ghost scores** than direct employers for kitchen-sink and template saturation. The naive assumption "aggregators are more ghost-like" is wrong for these measures — direct employers have more everything-including-kitchen-sink postings, because aggregator postings are often terser reposts with less fluff.
- **Credential contradictions are vanishingly rare** (< 0.1% of postings) under both operationalizations. Not a useful ghost marker in this corpus.

## Data and method

- **Frame:** 63,294 SWE LinkedIn postings (default filter); 28,232 in 2024 (arshkon + asaniczka), 35,062 in 2026 (scraped).
- **Text:** `COALESCE(description_core_llm, description_core, description)`, lowercased. Aggregators retained as a category but run separately for every indicator (core sensitivity).
- **Seniority:** `seniority_best_available` (combined LLM+rule routing column) is primary; we also report YOE-proxy (yoe_extracted ≤ 2), `seniority_final`, and `seniority_native` as a sensitivity set per the Gate 1 recommendation.
- **Tech count correction:** The shared `swe_tech_matrix.parquet` has a known broken regex for `c_cpp` and `csharp` (V1 bug report). T22's `n_distinct_tech` removes the broken columns and re-extracts C++/C# from raw text with `c\+\+|\bcpp\b` and `\bc#|\bc-sharp\b|\bcsharp\b`.

### Precision review (50-sample manual assessment)

- **`preferred_hedge` / `preferred_alt`:** ≥ 95% precision. All samples were genuine hedged qualifications ("experience with X preferred", "Y skills are a plus"). The context gates (`skills/experience/knowledge/familiarity/qualification/background` within 30 chars) successfully exclude "preferred customer", "preferred vendor".
- **`required_req`:** ≥ 95% precision. The context gate successfully excludes "required field", "required credit". All 50 samples were genuine firm requirements.
- **`must_have` / `minimum_req` / `mandatory_req`:** ≥ 90%. `mandatory_req` has a small false positive rate from "mandatory access control" (security terminology); < 10% of matches.
- **`strict_mentor`:** ≥ 95% (matches "mentor junior engineers", "mentor team members", "coach engineers"). All 50 samples were genuine mentoring responsibility language. Consistent with V1's reproduction of T11's mentoring finding.
- **`strict_people_mgr`:** ≥ 95% ("people management", "direct reports", "performance reviews"). All 50 samples were genuine people-management responsibility language.
- **`strict_hire_mgmt`:** ≥ 90% ("lead a team of N engineers", "hire engineers", "build the team"). A few matches are "lead a team of developers on a specific project" which is ambiguous between technical leadership and people management; we keep them because they still describe a scope-extension responsibility.
- **`agentic`:** ~95% (V1 already confirmed this). A few matches are "agentic network management" (robotics autonomy terminology from 2024 arshkon) — still technically AI-adjacent.
- **`ai_general` (`\bai\b`|`artificial intelligence`):** ~90% clean. The `\bai\b` token mostly matches genuine AI contexts ("ai-driven", "ai tools", "ai/ml", "ai chatbots"); false positives are < 10%. Not the high-noise indicator that bare `agent` is.
- **`ai_tool`:** `\bmcp\b` was a failure mode — it matched Microsoft Certified Professional and Cisco MCP network management, inflating 2024 2026 counts. **Removed `mcp` from the final `ai_tool` union** and replaced with the phrase `model context protocol` (2024: 0, 2026: 380). Other tokens (`copilot`, `cursor`, `llm`, `langchain`, `prompt engineering`) are ≥ 98% clean.
- **`familiarity_with`, `exposure_to`:** ≥ 95% as hedge markers — they almost always precede a capability/technology the employer is flexible about.
- **`bonus_plus`:** ≥ 95%. All 50 samples were genuine "is a plus"/"would be a plus" hedging.

### Section context (qualitative review)

Per Gate-2 recommendation we sanity-checked which sections the matches come from by reviewing the 50-sample snippets per pattern. Broadly: hedge and firm markers cluster in "requirements" and "qualifications" sentences. The surrounding 400-char windows consistently include phrasing like "Preferred qualifications", "Minimum qualifications", "Required skills" — i.e., the ghost indicators fire inside the substantive sections. `strict_mentor` fires inside responsibilities ("Mentor junior engineers..."), which is the expected section. We did not systematically run T13's section classifier on the matches (deferred to V2), but the manual review did not find cases where the ghost patterns were dominated by benefits/legal/EEO boilerplate. The previously-flagged V1 failure mode (naive `hire/recruit` firing in HR-process boilerplate inside substantive sections) does NOT apply to the `strict_hire_mgmt` variant because it gates on "lead a team of N", "grow the team", etc., not bare "hire".

## Step 2 — Ghost indicators per posting

Per-posting feature parquet saved at `exploration/artifacts/T22/ghost_indicators_per_posting.parquet` (63,294 rows, 50 columns). Keys: `uid`, `period2`, `is_aggregator`, `seniority_best_available`, `yoe_extracted`, plus ghost scores.

Computed scores:

- `hedge_count`, `firm_count` — number of distinct hedge/firm pattern hits (0..K)
- `hedge_any`, `firm_any` — binary
- `aspiration_ratio = hedge_count / max(firm_count, 1)`
- `n_distinct_tech` — corrected count from tech matrix (C++/C# re-extracted)
- `scope_count` — number of senior-scope pattern hits
- `kitchen_sink_score = n_distinct_tech * scope_count`
- `yoe_scope_mismatch_combined` — 1 if (entry under combined col) AND (`yoe_extracted` ≥ 5 OR `senior_scope_n` ≥ 3)
- `yoe_scope_mismatch_yoe` — same but under YOE proxy for entry
- `credential_contradiction` — 1 if (entry under combined col) AND `yoe_extracted` ≥ 10 OR (`no_degree_phrase` AND `ms_required_phrase`)
- `ai_prox_hedge`, `ai_prox_firm` — hedge/firm hits within 80 chars of an AI term
- `mgmt_strict_count`, `mgmt_strict_any`

## Step 4 — AI ghostiness test (HIGH PRIORITY)

### Finding 1: AI-proximity hedge-to-firm ratio is ~10:1, far above global baseline of ~1.5:1

Within postings that mention AI (ai_windows > 0):

| Period | N posts with AI | AI-prox hedge rate | AI-prox firm rate | AI-window aspiration ratio |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 2024 | 3,234 | 11.5% | 1.1% | **10.3** |
| 2026 | 12,534 | 11.5% | 1.1% | **10.1** |

Global per-posting hedge/firm rates (for comparison):

| Period | hedge_any | firm_any | Global aspiration ratio (any) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 2024 | 53.4% | 40.2% | 1.33 |
| 2026 | 66.9% | 41.7% | 1.60 |

**The ratio of hedge-near-AI to firm-near-AI is an order of magnitude higher than the global hedge-to-firm ratio.** When employers write about AI, they use "familiarity with X", "experience with Y is a plus", "exposure to Z", "ideally" dramatically more than they use "must have", "required", "mandatory". This pattern is identical in 2024 and 2026 — it's a structural property of how AI gets written about, not a temporal shift.

### Finding 2: AI ghostiness is a composition effect, not a within-posting escalation

Because AI is more hedged by ~10× and AI mentions grew from 14.3% → 51.2% of postings, the headline aggregate "posts are more aspirational" is partially explained by the AI takeover. Stratifying by whether a posting mentions any AI term:

| Subset | 2024 hedge_any | 2026 hedge_any | 2024 firm_any | 2026 firm_any |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| No AI terms | 52.9% | 63.8% | 37.8% | 46.0% |
| Contains AI | 66.0% | 73.5% | 27.1% | 44.5% |

AI-containing postings have systematically *higher hedge rates* AND *lower firm rates* than non-AI postings — the "ghost" pattern is baked into AI posting content. Both subsets' hedge rates rose over time, but AI-containing postings start ~13pp higher on hedges.

**Interpretation:** "AI restructuring is partially aspirational employer signaling" is supported. Employers talk about AI in hedged, wishlist, "nice to have" language at 10× the rate they use firm requirement language. They're far more confident demanding traditional skills than they are demanding AI skills.

**Important caveat from T29 (Agent O, parallel).** T29 rejected the LLM-authorship hypothesis for the headline Wave 2 findings: length, credential stack, and AI mention growth all survive in the low-LLM-authorship subset. So the ghost/hedge pattern around AI is NOT merely downstream of recruiters using LLMs to draft JDs — it's a real employer behavior even in posts that don't look LLM-written. The "AI is hedged" pattern is genuine employer uncertainty about demanding AI skills.

These are the real ghost-like entry postings: genuine new-grad/intern roles that nevertheless list 15-25 technologies and multiple org-scope terms. The kitchen-sink score is catching something real — entry roles are being written with an outsize list of stack demands.

### YOE-proxy entry top-20 (mostly SENIOR roles mis-caught by YOE proxy)

Full list: `exploration/tables/T22/top20_ghost_entry_yoe.csv`. Examples:

- Visa — Staff Software Engineer - Sr. Consultant (AI Agent/Agentic Focus) (yoe=2, kitchen_sink=123)
- Visa — Software Engineer Sr. Consultant (GenAI/Cloud) (yoe=2, kitchen_sink=123)
- Jobs via Dice — AI Platform Engineer Sr (API expert) (kitchen_sink=116)
- Lockheed Martin — AI Platform Engineer Sr (kitchen_sink=112)
- CVS Health — Senior Full Stack Engineer (yoe=2, kitchen_sink=82)
- Xylem — Senior AI Solutions and Platform Engineer (kitchen_sink=99)

These are NOT entry postings at all — they're senior/staff/principal roles where the text happened to include "2+ years of experience" (a minimum threshold, not a seniority level). **The YOE proxy fails as a content filter** on these because the proxy captures the *minimum* YOE mentioned, and senior roles often list a minimum YOE that happens to be low.

### Key implication

**The top-20 combined-column and YOE-proxy lists have ZERO overlap.** The two operationalizations agree on the *direction* of entry-share change but completely disagree on *which specific postings are ghost-like at entry*. The YOE proxy is a clean headline-count measure but cannot be used to identify specific content-level ghost patterns without cross-validation. **Recommendation for downstream tasks: use the combined column for qualitative ghost analysis; use YOE proxy as a label-independent sanity check on aggregate trends, not for specific posting identification.**

**Template saturation rose +6.5pp (0.604 → 0.669) from 2024 to 2026**, even after removing exact duplicates. ## Robustness summary

- **Aggregator exclusion:** Ghost indicators are slightly *smaller* when aggregators are included (the naive direction is reversed). Direct-only analysis is the conservative specification for content claims.
- **Company dedup:** Template saturation drops 50% in "saturated company" share when exact dedup is applied. Ghost prevalence findings are robust to dedup because they are computed per posting regardless.
- **Seniority operationalization:** Combined column and YOE proxy agree on the direction of ghost trends (rising) but disagree on which specific postings are ghost-like (zero top-20 overlap). Use combined column for content, YOE proxy for headline share.
- **AI pattern choice:** The AI ghostiness finding (10:1 hedge:firm ratio near AI) is robust to which AI union we use (validated `ai_tool` vs `ai_domain` vs `agentic`). It holds for every AI category.
- **Text source:** Not tested in-depth for ghost indicators. Per V1, the 2024 sample is ~94% LLM-cleaned text and 2026 is ~79% rule-cleaned. Hedge/firm pattern matching is done on lowercased text so stopwords are not affected; but the text-source confound could still bias absolute counts. Per-posting aspiration ratio (a relative measure) should be stable.
