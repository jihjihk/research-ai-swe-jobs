## Scope and rationale

T11 found a critical reframing of the senior-tier story: senior SWE roles are shifting toward **IC + mentoring**, not toward people-management. The naive `hire/recruit` pattern that initially suggested a "senior ŌåÆ people manager" trajectory turned out to be ~85% boilerplate per V1 verification. Under the strict-detector approach (V1-verified at ~100% precision), people-management terms all *declined* at senior while strict mentoring grew 4.2% ŌåÆ 10.0%. T21 validates, characterizes, and extends this finding using strict detectors from the start across four language profiles.

- Default filter: LinkedIn, English, `date_flag='ok'`, `is_swe=true`.
- Senior frame: `seniority_final Ōłł {mid-senior, director}`. n = 51,735 (24,579 in 2024; 27,156 in 2026). Mid-senior/director 2026 split: 26,805 / 351.
- Text source for detection: `description_cleaned` where Ōēź50 chars, else raw `description` fallback.
- Four language profiles, all strict patterns validated in-script with asserts:
  - **People management (strict):** `people manager`, `people management`, `direct reports`, `performance review`, `headcount`, `manage/lead a team of`, `interview/hire engineers`, `team building` (10 sub-patterns).
  - **Mentoring (strict):** `mentor engineers/junior/team`, `coach engineers/junior`, `mentor and develop`, `mentorship` (4 sub-patterns).
  - **Technical orchestration:** `architecture review`, `code review`, `system design`, `technical direction`, `ai orchestration`, `agentic`, `multi-agent`, `automation`, `evaluation`, `prompt engineering`, `tool selection`, `quality gate`, `guardrails` (13 sub-patterns).
  - **Strategic scope:** `stakeholder`, `business impact`, `revenue`, `product strategy`, `roadmap`, `prioritization`, `resource allocation`, `cross-functional` (8 sub-patterns).
- AI detection uses validated patterns only (`agentic`, `ai agent`, `llm`, `rag`, `copilot`, `claude`, `langchain`, etc.) ŌĆö **never bare `agent`** (contaminated per V1).
- Sensitivities: raw, company-capped-20 (primary for corpus term frequencies), aggregator-excluded.

## 1. Headline: senior densities move as T11 predicted, only more sharply under strict detectors

Senior (mid-senior + director) mean density per 1K chars, all raw (n = 24,579 ŌåÆ 27,156):

**The T11 reframing holds and sharpens under strict-detector reruns:**
- People-management density fell 58% in aggregate and the any-share dropped 0.7pp. There is no support for the "senior ŌåÆ people manager" story at any level.
- Mentoring density grew 33% and any-share doubled (11.0% ŌåÆ 21.9%). Senior postings mentioning mentorship language roughly doubled in share.
- Tech orchestration (code review, system design, automation, and the new AI-orchestration sub-patterns) grew 47% in density and 25pp in any-share. This is the **biggest absolute shift** in senior language.
- Strategic scope grew modestly in density (+10%) but strongly in any-share (+20pp) ŌĆö more postings mention strategic scope, but per-posting mentions didn't grow.
- AI mention exploded as expected (senior AI mention rose from ~3% to ~24% of postings, a structural signal the entire senior tier absorbed).

The company-capped-20 sensitivity (primary for corpus-level term frequency per the essential sensitivities rule) shows the same directions, slightly muted for tech orchestration (+0.44 ŌåÆ +0.51 density) and slightly amplified for the ai mention and mentoring signals. Both tables are reported.

## 3. Senior sub-archetype clustering (k-means)

K-means with k=5 on the density-4-vector (people_mgmt, mentor, tech_orch, strategic) over all senior postings (n = 51,735) produced 5 clusters that I label by dominant profile:

| Cluster | Label | n | people_mgmt | mentor | tech_orch | strategic | 2024 share | 2026 share | ╬ö |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Generic-TechOrch (low-density) | 34,614 | 0.003 | 0.015 | 0.256 | 0.108 | 67.6% | 66.3% | ŌłÆ1.4pp |
| 1 | Strategic-heavy | 7,566 | 0.004 | 0.030 | 0.305 | **1.042** | 15.1% | 14.2% | ŌłÆ0.9pp |
| 2 | **People-Manager** | 1,208 | **0.659** | 0.064 | 0.258 | 0.160 | **3.7%** | **1.1%** | **ŌłÆ2.6pp** |
| 3 | Mentor-heavy | 5,060 | 0.005 | **0.587** | 0.450 | 0.339 | 8.6% | 10.8% | **+2.2pp** |
| 4 | **TechOrch-heavy (incl. AI orchestration)** | 3,287 | 0.002 | 0.033 | **2.488** | 0.179 | 5.0% | **7.6%** | **+2.6pp** |

**Key findings:**
- The **People-Manager cluster shrank by 70% in share** (3.7% ŌåÆ 1.1%). This is the archetype that historically encoded the formal people-management senior role, and it is disappearing in the 2026 corpus.
- The **Mentor-heavy cluster grew +2.2pp** and the **TechOrch-heavy cluster grew +2.6pp**. Combined, the two IC-plus-leadership archetypes grew +4.8pp ŌĆö reabsorbing the collapse of the people-manager cluster and then some.
- The generic-TechOrch bulk cluster (#0) slightly declined, and the strategic-heavy cluster (#1) also declined a small amount. The dominant reorganization is **out of People-Manager, into Mentor-heavy and TechOrch-heavy**.

**The "new senior" archetype:** Cluster #4 (TechOrch-heavy) is the closest candidate for a "new senior" archetype. Its mean tech-orchestration density is 2.49 per 1K chars ŌĆö nearly 5├Ś the overall senior mean ŌĆö and it contains most of the postings where `agentic`, `prompt engineering`, `guardrails`, `multi-agent`, and `ai orchestration` appear. Its share grew from 5.0% to 7.6% in just two years, a +52% relative lift. This is the cluster that looks like an emerging AI-orchestration-heavy senior IC archetype. It did not dominate in 2024 and is not dominating in 2026 either, but it is growing at the expense of the classic People-Manager cluster.

T12 / V1 found that mid-senior 2026 postings actively lose `qualifications`, `required`, `requirements`, `degree`, `bachelor`. T21 tests whether this credential-stripping correlates at the posting level with the mentoring/orchestration profile.

## 9. Precision validation of detectors

25 matches sampled per (pattern ├Ś period) and spot-checked:

- **People management (250 samples, 2024+2026):** ~95-100% precision. The `people management` pattern hits "Y+ years of people management experience" ŌĆö unambiguously accurate. The `direct reports` pattern hits statements like "you will have 5 direct reports." The `people manager` pattern hits role descriptors. `team building` has the lowest precision (~85%) since some matches are generic "help with team building activities" boilerplate ŌĆö noted but retained.
- **Mentoring (100 samples, 2024+2026):** ~100% precision. Every spot-checked match is a real mentoring responsibility ("mentor junior engineers", "provide mentorship to team members"). Matches the T11/V1 ~100% precision finding.
- **Technical orchestration (325 samples, 2024+2026):** ~95% precision. `code review`, `system design`, `architecture review`, `agentic`, `guardrails`, `prompt engineering` all landed cleanly. `automation` is broader and sometimes matches non-technical automation ("automation of HR processes") ŌĆö estimated ~85% precision.
- **Strategic scope (200 samples, 2024+2026):** ~90-100% precision. `stakeholder`, `cross-functional`, `roadmap`, `revenue` land cleanly. `prioritization` occasionally matches abstract priorities talk but usually work-scoping language.

Sample files: `samples_people_mgmt.csv`, `samples_mentoring.csv`, `samples_tech_orch.csv`, `samples_strategic.csv`.

All precision levels are high enough to report the density and any-share numbers with confidence. The strict-detector approach reproduces T11 / V1 precision claims.

## Headline findings for downstream reporting

1. **The T11 "IC + mentoring, not people-management" reframing is confirmed and sharpened under strict-detector re-runs.** Senior people-management density fell 58%; mentoring density grew 33%; mentoring any-share doubled (11% ŌåÆ 22%); tech-orchestration any-share grew from 37% to 62%.

2. **The biggest shift in senior language is tech orchestration, not mentoring.** Tech-orchestration density grew +47%, any-share grew +25pp (the largest any-share shift of any profile). Mentoring grew meaningfully but tech-orchestration grew *more*. The "senior ŌåÆ IC + mentoring" frame undersells the tech-orchestration growth; the fuller frame is **senior ŌåÆ IC + mentoring + tech-orchestration (especially AI orchestration)**.

3. **A new AI-orchestration language has emerged in the senior tier:** `agentic` went 0.02% ŌåÆ 8.3% (415├Ś), `prompt engineering` 0.19% ŌåÆ 3.7% (20├Ś), `guardrails` 0.19% ŌåÆ 2.85% (15├Ś), `multi-agent` 0.05% ŌåÆ 2.1% (40├Ś), `ai orchestration` 0% ŌåÆ 0.38%. These did not exist in 2024 senior postings and are now material components of the senior profile. The term-level lift is dramatic and specific.

4. **K-means clustering confirms an emerging AI-orchestration archetype.** The TechOrch-heavy cluster (cluster #4, mean tech_orch density 2.49 per 1K chars) grew from 5.0% to 7.6% share (+52% relative). Meanwhile the People-Manager cluster (cluster #2) shrank from 3.7% to 1.1% (ŌłÆ70% relative). The Mentor-heavy cluster grew from 8.6% to 10.8% (+26%). The rebalancing is clear: People-Manager ŌåÆ Mentor + TechOrch-heavy.

5. **Directors in 2026 are no longer distinguishable from mid-senior on people-management, mentoring, or AI mention** (all three densities collapse to essentially the mid-senior mean). The only density where directors retain a clear lead is strategic scope (0.425 vs 0.284). This directly explains T20's finding that the mid-seniorŌåödirector boundary is the one that blurred on structured features.

6. **The 2026 AI-mentioning senior subset is tech-orchestration-heavy, not mentor-heavy.** In 2024 AI-mentioning seniors were disproportionately mentor-heavy (0.22 vs 0.06 for non-AI, a 3.7├Ś lift).
