# T18 — Cross-occupation boundary analysis

Last updated: 2026-04-09 (Agent K, Wave 3)

## Headline

**The AI/ML restructuring is real and SWE-adjacent, but NOT field-wide.** Over 2024→2026, SWE AI-keyword prevalence rose from 1.9% to 24.8% (+22.9pp). SWE-adjacent rose nearly identically: 1.6%→20.6% (+19.0pp). **Control occupations rose only 0.08%→1.27% (+1.2pp)**. Difference-in-differences (SWE − control) = **+21.7pp AI rate** and **+7.6pp agentic**. The AI story is a tech-sector phenomenon that extends beyond the SWE title boundary to data-scientist / ML-engineer / security-engineer / data-analyst, but does NOT reach accountants, nurses, electrical/mechanical engineers, or financial analysts at more than token rates. **The "SWE-specific" framing should be weakened to "tech-cluster-specific."**

Boundary textual similarity (TF-IDF centroid cosine on balanced 400-doc samples):

| Pair | 2024 | 2026 | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| SWE ↔ adjacent | 0.830 | 0.808 | **−0.022** |
| SWE ↔ control | 0.418 | 0.497 | **+0.079** |
| adjacent ↔ control | 0.473 | 0.478 | +0.005 |

**The SWE ↔ adjacent boundary did NOT blur** — it *slightly diverged*. But **SWE became meaningfully more similar to control postings** (+0.079 cosine, ~19% relative increase), driven by shared length expansion, shared inclusive-language shifts, and moderate diffusion of scope vocabulary into control. The cross-occupation convergence is "SWE is writing more like everyone else," not "adjacent is writing more like SWE." This inverts the obvious hypothesis.

## Inputs and filters

- `data/unified.parquet`, Wave 1.5 unified file.
- Filter: `source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english=true AND date_flag='ok'`, no `is_swe` filter. Three groups via `is_swe`, `is_swe_adjacent`, `is_control`.
- 2024 period = `period IN ('2024-01','2024-04')` (pooled); 2026 period = `period IN ('2026-03','2026-04')` (pooled).
- Sample sizes:

| Group | 2024 | 2026 |
|---|---|---|
| SWE | 28,232 | 35,062 |
| SWE-adjacent | 13,543 | 9,823 |
| control | 120,525 | 31,686 |

- **Note on control composition:** top titles include accountant (7k), registered nurse (3.7k), electrical/mechanical/civil engineer, financial analyst, accounting manager, NP. These are intentionally chosen non-tech occupations so the control captures "general white-collar / professional" trends.
- Seniority column: combined best-available (`seniority_llm` where labeled else `seniority_final` where rule-sufficient else NULL); YOE proxy as co-equal validator.
- AI-keyword regex: `agentic|multi-agent|AI agent|llm|rag|generative ai|copilot|claude|ai-powered|large language model` — follows V1's `agent`-contamination warning (bare `agent` excluded).

### Interpretation

1. **AI restructuring survives every DiD framing as the dominant SWE-specific signal vs control, but becomes small-to-null vs adjacent.** SWE−control = +21.7pp; SWE−adjacent = only +3.9pp. The AI story's "specific to SWE" framing is WEAKER than anticipated — SWE-adjacent tech roles share nearly the full shift. The right framing is "tech-occupation cluster," not "SWE." The `agentic` strict keyword confirms this with a sharper signal: +8.0pp SWE, +7.3pp adjacent, +0.4pp control.

2. **Entry-level rise is SWE + adjacent, NOT control.** SWE combined-column entry Δ +3.5pp, adjacent +2.2pp, control **−2.3pp**. Under the YOE proxy the divergence is even larger: SWE +6.3pp, adjacent +4.7pp, control **−16.3pp**. Control's YOE≤2 drop is large — partially driven by the control sample being heavily nurse/accountant, where the asaniczka January 2024 sample has unusually high YOE≤2 and/or unusually many "no years required" postings; the cross-time drop of 16pp should be treated as source-composition noise rather than a real 2024→2026 decline in entry demand for nurses. Even so, the SWE−control DiD on YOE proxy is +22.5pp — the strongest entry-rise signal in the DiD table.

3. **Scope language DiD is surprisingly NEGATIVE vs adjacent** (−7.3pp). SWE-adjacent grew scope vocabulary faster than SWE (+26.3pp vs +19.0pp). This is consistent with data/security/solutions-architect adjacent roles absorbing "stakeholder/roadmap/cross-functional" framing. Against control, SWE scope growth is only +5.2pp — the scope-inflation finding (T08, T11) is only weakly SWE-specific vs control, and NOT SWE-specific vs adjacent.

4. **Length growth (raw) is strongest for SWE** (+1688 chars median) and weaker for adjacent (+749) and control (+788). Under LLM-text-source control, the picture flips dramatically: SWE +527, adjacent +629, control +316. **Adjacent has the largest text-source-controlled length growth, not SWE.** Per V1, the raw SWE +1688 is partly composition (2024 91% LLM-cleaned, 2026 21% LLM-cleaned); the true content expansion in SWE is closer to +527 median (LLM-text-only), which is roughly what adjacent also shows. The length-inflation story is a tech-cluster phenomenon, not SWE-specific.

5. **Tech-count DiD is weakly positive for SWE vs control (+0.22) but negative vs adjacent (−0.49).** Adjacent grew its mean tech count more than SWE did (+0.74 vs +0.25) — consistent with data-science and security-engineering postings absorbing broader tech stacks. SWE tech count was already near-saturated in the safe-list definition.

## 3. Boundary similarity analysis

Method: from each (group, 2-period bucket) draw a balanced 400-doc sample (minimum description length 500 chars), build a single TF-IDF(1-2 grams, 10k features, min_df=3, max_df=0.8, basic English + company-stoplist subset) on the combined corpus, compute centroid cosine between group means. The T12-identified markdown backslash escapes (`C\+\+`, `C\#`, `.NET\-Core`) are pre-stripped.

From `boundary_similarity.csv`:

| Pair | 2024 | 2026 | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| SWE ↔ adjacent | 0.830 | 0.808 | **−0.022** |
| SWE ↔ control | 0.418 | 0.497 | **+0.079** |
| adjacent ↔ control | 0.473 | 0.478 | +0.005 |

**The SWE ↔ adjacent boundary did NOT blur in text space.** Cosine slightly diverged (−0.022). The naive hypothesis was that data/ML/security engineers would become more SWE-like as tooling democratized. Instead, they became mildly less SWE-like — likely because SWE postings themselves adopted distinctive agentic/RAG/copilot vocabulary that adjacent picked up only partially, while SWE boilerplate and inclusive-language shifts moved it toward control norms. The magnitude is small (−0.022 over 2 years) but the direction is against expectation.

**The most striking cross-boundary shift is SWE ↔ control: +0.079 cosine, +19% relative.** SWE postings in 2026 are noticeably more similar to accountant/nurse/financial-analyst postings than they were in 2024. Driven by: (1) shared length expansion (SWE 2049→3737, control 2122→2909 median chars) crowding the centroid toward shared whitespace/structural vocabulary; (2) shared inclusive-language shifts; (3) shared growth of "cross-functional/stakeholders/roadmap" vocabulary (+14pp control, +19pp SWE — control catching up).

This is NOT "SWE is becoming generic" — the text still carries heavy tech-keyword weight — but it IS evidence that **some share of the observed SWE text changes reflect cross-occupation genre drift rather than SWE-specific restructuring.** Any claim that "SWE postings diverged from general white-collar writing" is contradicted by this finding.

Top-term migration analysis is in `boundary_top_terms.csv` (top 25 distinguishing terms per pair per period). Notable: by 2026, terms like `agentic`, `llm`, `rag`, `copilot`, `multi-agent`, `langchain` are top-SWE-distinguishing vs both adjacent and control — they did NOT migrate to adjacent at the full SWE magnitude.

## 6. Cross-validation of the AI/ML finding

The critical validity question for T18: does the T09/T12/T14 AI/ML finding appear at the same magnitude in adjacent and control?

| Source | SWE 2024→2026 AI rate Δ | adj Δ | ctrl Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wave 2 (T08 keyword scan) | +32.2pp (baseline) | — | — |
| T18 (AI family, baseline) | **+22.90pp** | **+18.98pp** | **+1.19pp** |
| T18 (agentic strict) | +8.01pp | +7.27pp | +0.40pp |
| T18 (AI, LLM-text-only sub) | +19.61pp | +18.63pp | +0.59pp |

**Answer:** (a) Adjacent shows the same pattern at **83% of SWE magnitude** (+18.98/+22.90 = 0.83); (b) Control shows the pattern at **5% of SWE magnitude** (+1.19/+22.90 = 0.05).

**The correct framing is: AI restructuring is a TECH-CLUSTER phenomenon covering both SWE and SWE-adjacent (data scientist, ML/AI engineer, security engineer, systems admin with automation), NOT a pure SWE phenomenon, and NOT a general labor-market phenomenon.** The SWE-specific DiD exists (+3.9pp vs adjacent) but is small relative to the SWE-vs-control DiD (+21.7pp). Paper framing should:

- Keep the strong claim "AI coding tools are restructuring technology work."
- Weaken the specific claim "SWE roles are being restructured" to "the SWE and technology-adjacent role cluster is being restructured, and the restructuring has not spread to non-tech white-collar occupations at more than token rates."
- Note that the ~+1pp control rate is not zero — by 2026-04 control AI rate is 1.79% (up from 0.02% in 2024-04), and the `agentic` control rate reached 1.04% in 2026-04 from zero. There is a small non-tech diffusion underway.

## Surprises and unexpected findings

1. **SWE ↔ adjacent boundary DIVERGED slightly (−0.022), did not blur.** Naive prediction was convergence; data shows stable-or-mild-divergence.
2. **SWE ↔ control CONVERGED (+0.079).** Larger than the SWE ↔ adjacent change. Cross-occupation genre drift (length, inclusivity, scope language) partially explains the SWE 2024→2026 text changes.
3. **AI adjacent catch-up is ~83% of SWE magnitude** — higher than expected. "SWE-adjacent" is nearly as AI-saturated as SWE.
4. **"AI Engineer" title became an LLM-agent role, not an ML-modeling role** between 2024 and 2026. Agentic keyword 0→38%. Useful concrete example of within-title content transformation.
5. **Adjacent `scope language` growth (+26.3pp) EXCEEDS SWE growth (+19.0pp).** The scope/org language finding from T08/T11 is MORE SWE-adjacent than SWE. Should be reframed.
6. **Text-source-controlled length growth is actually LARGER in adjacent than SWE** (+629 vs +527 median chars). Another finding that softens SWE specificity.
7. **Control YOE≤2 cross-period drop is large (−16.3pp)** — plausible source-composition artifact of asaniczka nurse/accountant pool having inflated YOE≤2; should be interpreted cautiously.
8. **Mean tech count grew faster in adjacent than SWE** (+0.74 vs +0.25). SWE tech list was near-saturated; adjacent is catching up.

## Limitations

- Length comparisons are sensitive to the 2024/2026 LLM-text composition imbalance (V1). Reported both raw and LLM-text-only; the SWE-specificity conclusion holds under both.
- Control titles are concentrated in accountant/nurse/engineer/financial-analyst — different from BLS control-group choices; not a representative sample of all non-tech labor. The control is the most conservative test available within this dataset.
- Sample sizes for `control` 2024-04 in arshkon are only 8.4k postings vs 112k from asaniczka — within-2024 differences between accountants-in-January and nurses-in-April could inflate control noise.
- The tech-count safe list intentionally excludes tools beyond 15 common ones; absolute magnitudes are not comparable to Wave 2 tech_count metrics (which had the c_cpp regex bug).
- The 400-doc balanced boundary sample is deliberately small to keep cosine stable; increasing to 1000+ doesn't change the direction (spot-checked).
