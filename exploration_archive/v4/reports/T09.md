# T09: Posting Archetype Discovery — Methods Laboratory

## Headline Finding

**Unsupervised topic discovery surfaces a clean tech-domain partition of the SWE posting market, and the single largest 2024→2026 shift is the rise of an AI/ML archetype (4.1% → ~15%, +11 pp), with simultaneous double-digit-percent declines in .NET, frontend/web, and Spring/Java stacks.** BERTopic (min_topic_size=30, primary) yields 23 interpretable topics with 27.8% noise and strong core-only seed stability (mean ARI = 0.93). NMF (k=12, comparison) agrees on the main tech-domain axis (BERTopic↔NMF NMI = 0.33; 5 of 23 BERTopic topics have a ≥0.15 top-term-Jaccard match to an NMF component), but NMF's low-k components are heavily contaminated by company-specific boilerplate signatures. **Clusters do not align with seniority** (NMI vs combined seniority = 0.0155) or with the YOE-based junior proxy (NMI = 0.0078) — the dominant structure of the SWE market in this embedding is **tech domain**, not career level.

Surprise highlights (expanded below):
- Two topics exist **only in 2026** because unsupervised topic modeling recovered **scraper/preprocessing boilerplate artifacts** the cleaning pipeline missed (Dice reposts, Northrop Grumman footers).
- BERTopic and NMF agree where BERTopic has high-coherence technical topics (AI/ML, cloud/devops, data eng, web/react, android/mobile), but NMF spends 4 of its 12 components on boilerplate signatures that BERTopic either merges into noise or isolates into small, explicitly identifiable artifact topics — a useful diagnostic by itself.
- The combined best-available seniority column and the YOE-based junior proxy **disagree sharply at the archetype level** (pooled: entry share 7.7% vs YOE-junior share 18.2%), and the disagreement is not uniform across clusters.

**Target:** up to 8,000 SWE LinkedIn postings, balanced across three period buckets (2024, 2026-03, 2026-04) with within-period stratification by the combined best-available seniority column. Prefer `text_source='llm'` and prefer arshkon over asaniczka for 2024.

## 3. Method A — BERTopic (Primary)

### 3.1 min_topic_size sweep

| min_topic_size | n topics | noise | pct noise |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20 | 58 | 2,925 | 36.6% |
| **30 (primary)** | **23** | **2,223** | **27.8%** |
| 50 | 5 | 445 | 5.6% |

At mts=20 many near-duplicate niche topics appear (e.g., multiple Java/Spring variants). At mts=50 HDBSCAN collapses everything into 5 coarse clusters that lose the domain granularity that is the core finding. mts=30 is the interpretability optimum.

### 3.2 Stability (primary mts=30, 3 seeds)

| seeds | ARI all docs | ARI core-only (exclude noise) | n core |
|---|---|---|---|
| 42 vs 7 | 0.640 | 0.893 | 5,000 |
| 42 vs 2026 | 0.592 | 0.897 | 4,805 |
| 7 vs 2026 | 0.735 | 0.990 | 4,709 |
| **mean** | **0.656** | **0.927** |  |

Clusters assigned to the non-noise core are highly stable across seeds. The all-docs ARI is substantially lower because HDBSCAN's noise boundary is seed-sensitive — i.e., the same documents sometimes flip in/out of noise, but when they are clustered they land in the same topic. **For downstream analyses, core-only assignments can be trusted; noise should be treated as an "unclassified" stratum, not as a 24th cluster.**

### 3.3 Topic coherence

Mean UMass coherence across the 23 primary topics = **-1.342**. On our vocabulary this is in the normal-to-good range for short, dense technical documents; we use it as a comparative rather than absolute reference.

NMF ran on a TF-IDF matrix (1-2grams, min_df=10, max_df=0.6, sublinear_tf, L2-normalized). Top-8 terms per component at each k in `exploration/tables/T09/nmf_topic_terms.csv`; summary of k=12 (the best-interpreted k):

| 6 | infrastructure, cloud, devops, ci, cd, ci cd, terraform, automation | Cloud/DevOps — matches BERTopic T2 |
| 7 | open source, open, tech trends, external technology, experimenting learning, communities mentoring | **Boilerplate artifact**: a growth-culture boilerplate |
| 8 | data, data engineer, analytics, data engineering, etl, data pipelines, sql, pipelines | Data eng — matches BERTopic T3 |
| 9 | test, requirements, support, degree, hardware, required, testing, analysis | QA/Test/Requirements — matches BERTopic T8 loosely |
| 10 | qualifications capabilities, capabilities skills, chase, software applications, operational stability | **Boilerplate artifact**: JPMorgan Chase template |
| 11 | responses, model responses, responses coding, coding software, evaluation, strengths, reasoning, clarity | **Boilerplate artifact**: LLM-evaluation contract postings |

**Key methodological finding:** 6 of 12 NMF components (1, 2, 5, 7, 10, 11) are boilerplate/company signatures that encode the residual company-specific text that the shared cleaning did not strip. NMF's raw TF-IDF objective is more susceptible to these than BERTopic's embedding+HDBSCAN path, because sentence-transformer embeddings place similar boilerplate patterns close enough to merge into HDBSCAN's noise stratum or to be absorbed by larger on-domain topics.

k=5 and k=8 look worse (the top components are dominated by boilerplate). k=15 adds a clean clearance/embedded component and an "agentic/govcloud" micro-topic but also adds two more boilerplate components.

## 5. Methods Comparison

### 5.1 Alignment by top-term Jaccard (BERTopic ↔ NMF k=12)

Best NMF match per BERTopic topic (full table at `method_best_nmf_per_bertopic.csv`):

| BERTopic | best NMF | Jaccard | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| T3 data | C8 | **0.50** | strong match |
| T2 cloud/devops | C6 | **0.43** | strong match |
| T1 AI/ML | C3 | **0.36** | strong match |
| T5 Spring/Java | C4 | 0.20 | partial — NMF merges T4 web with T5 Java |
| T4 frontend/web | C4 | 0.15 | partial — same as above |
| all others | — | < 0.15 | either BERTopic-specific or NMF-specific |

**Method-robust topics (Jaccard ≥ 0.15):** 5 of 23. These are the five biggest mainstream technical axes: AI/ML, cloud/devops, data engineering, and web+Java. Cross-method NMI(BERTopic, NMF-k12) = **0.326**; ARI = 0.143.

**Why only five?** Not because BERTopic and NMF disagree on the "what" — they largely agree that AI/ML, cloud, data, and web are the main structures — but because:
1. BERTopic splits finer than NMF in the middle of the size distribution (mobile, embedded, SRE, .NET, servicenow, systems eng, python/django are distinct BERTopic topics but get folded into NMF's generic "applications" or "infrastructure" components).
2. NMF spends 6/12 components on boilerplate that BERTopic absorbs into noise or isolates in small dedicated topics (T14 Dice, T20 salary/shift boilerplate).
3. BERTopic's noise stratum (28% of docs) has no NMF analog — NMF assigns every doc to some component.

### 5.3 Noise characterization

The 2,223 BERTopic noise documents (27.8%) are **not** concentrated in any period (762/740/721 across 2024/2026-03/2026-04) or source. Manual inspection of 10 samples shows:
- Genuine niche roles that don't cluster (radar, embedded-RTOS, robotics perception).
- Fragmentary descriptions that survived cleaning with odd token sequences.
- Descriptions heavy on company-team boilerplate (PwC, Scribd team descriptions) where the technical content is a short tail.

The seniority distribution of noise is **similar to the overall known distribution** (20% mid-senior, 2% entry, 0.3% director). Noise is not a junior/senior-specific bucket — it is semantic-diversity-specific.

### 6.1 Entry-level share disagreements

`exploration/tables/T09/entry_share_by_archetype_period.csv` shows that, for every major archetype, the combined best-available entry share and the YOE-based junior share disagree in magnitude and sometimes in direction across periods:

- **T00 software/development, 2024→2026-03:** combined entry drops 13.1% → 10.5% (n_known 213→57), while YOE-junior share *almost doubles* 23.4% → 43.0% (n_known 252→223). These moves are in opposite directions.
- **T03 data eng, 2024→2026-03:** combined entry 2.9% → 24.2%, YOE-junior 11.7% → 23.9%. The combined jump is driven by a tiny known-label denominator (33).
- **T05 Spring/Java:** combined entry ~4% in 2024, 0% in 2026 (n_known 5–7); YOE-junior 11% → 14% → 8%. Small n_known makes combined unreliable.

**Interpretation:** the combined column's known-label denominator collapses under archetype stratification (often <30), and under those conditions its entry-share estimate is dominated by sampling noise. **For archetype-level seniority claims, the YOE proxy is more defensible** — but even there, T07's caveat about differential YOE extraction quality across snapshots applies (see `seniority_measurement_investigation.md`). Downstream tasks should **report both** and avoid drawing strong archetype-level seniority conclusions from the combined column alone.

### 7.1 Archetypes that exist in only one period

- **T14 Dice boilerplate (73 docs):** 0 in 2024, 12 in 2026-03, 61 in 2026-04. This is a **preprocessing artifact**: Dice cross-posts carry an "Apply via Dice today" header whose company-name stripping leaves the boilerplate intact. The ratcheting concentration in 2026-04 suggests this template was introduced to Dice's feed after 2026-03 or a scraper change began retaining more Dice rows. Recommended action: add "dice leading career destination" / "apply via dice" to the boilerplate-removal patterns for Stage 3.
- **T20 salary/shift boilerplate (35 docs):** 0 in 2024, 6 in 2026-03, 29 in 2026-04. Manual inspection: these are Northrop Grumman / Firefly Aerospace defense-contractor postings with a consistent "CLEARANCE TYPE / TRAVEL / Description / employees incredible opportunities" preface. The 2024 snapshot did not sample Northrop postings densely; the 2026 snapshot does.
- **T21 expedia/travel/cash (31 docs):** 7 in 2024, 24 in 2026 — mostly a company-signature cluster (Expedia).

These three small topics together account for ~140 documents. Their existence is a **process finding** about residual boilerplate, not a labor-market signal. Downstream analyses should flag them (dropping the noise topic *plus* the three artifact topics reduces the clean archetype set to 20).

**Implications for RQ1 (employer-side restructuring):**
- Clustering will **not** give us a "junior cluster vs senior cluster" decomposition.
- It will give us a **tech-domain partition** within which we can ask *how the junior share moved inside each domain*. The AI/ML growth (+11 pp) plus flat YOE-junior share *within* the AI/ML cluster (17.2%) plus its lower-than-average combined entry share (6.0%) means the AI/ML growth is **not** arriving as a flood of junior AI postings — it arrives in the same seniority mix as the rest of the market, which in level terms means AI/ML is making the overall job market *less* junior-heavy via composition (even if each domain held its junior share constant).

## 10. Sensitivities

### 10.1 Aggregator exclusion (sensitivity (a))

Removing `is_aggregator=true` rows changes the maximum-single-archetype share by ≤2.5 pp (max shift: noise at 2.5 pp). The AI/ML archetype moves by less than 1 pp. **Not materially sensitive.**

### 10.2 Text source: llm-only (sensitivity (d))

Restricting to `text_source='llm'` (drops ~1,618 rule-text rows, nearly all from 2026-04) changes archetype shares by at most 2.3 pp (T14 Dice boilerplate). The AI/ML, cloud, data, and web archetypes move by less than 1.3 pp each. **Not materially sensitive at the archetype-composition level.** However, 2026-04's within-period-share estimates are noisier because the llm-only subset of 2026-04 is only n=1,287.

Full sensitivity table: `exploration/tables/T09/archetype_sensitivities.csv`; summary at `archetype_sensitivity_summary.csv`.

## 11. Surprises & Unexpected Patterns

1. **AI/ML is the *only* large grower.** Every other archetype that gains share grows by <1.5 pp. The AI/ML jump of +11 pp absorbs essentially all the share that .NET (-3.5), web/react (-2.3), Spring/Java (-1.8), data eng (-1.1), equipment/sales (-0.9), python-django (-0.9), and misc typescript/sql (-0.9) give up. The story is a **stack-migration story**, not a diffusion story.

2. **Primary language is the single strongest latent dimension of the embedding** — 5× more explanatory than period or seniority. If you want to predict what cluster a job posting lands in, the *most* informative feature is which programming language it mentions.

3. **Two clusters (T14 Dice, T20 Northrop) exist only in 2026 — not because the labor market changed, but because scraper boilerplate changed.** Unsupervised topic modeling is acting as a **preprocessing QA tool** here: it surfaces residual boilerplate that the Stage 3 patterns missed. Recommended follow-up: add "dice leading career destination" / "apply via dice today" / "clearance type: [polygraph|top secret|secret]" to boilerplate removal.

4. **NMF dedicates half its components to company-specific boilerplate** at k=12, even though those postings are numerically a small minority. This means NMF is a poor primary choice for this corpus, but it is an excellent **boilerplate auditor**: the components it surfaces are literally a ranked list of residual boilerplate signatures in the corpus.

5. **Clusters do not align with seniority.** This is the most important negative result. The "archetype" structure of the SWE market is fundamentally a tech-stack structure, and the junior/senior distinction cuts across all stacks without producing its own cluster. This constrains how we can use the archetype labels in RQ1: they are a **control-for-composition** variable, not an outcome variable.

6. **Combined-seniority and YOE-junior disagree strongly at the archetype level.** In the T00 "generic software" archetype, combined entry share drops 2024→2026-03 while YOE-junior share nearly doubles. At archetype granularity, the combined column has too few known labels to be reliable (n_known often <30 per archetype×period cell), and the two proxies should be reported together, never collapsed.

7. **BERTopic's noise stratum is 28% — not small, not growing, not seniority-biased.** This is healthy: a large noise bucket is the method's honest admission that the SWE market has a semantic long tail. It is *not* a failure mode — forcing mts=50 drops noise to 5.6% but destroys the topic granularity that makes the AI/ML finding visible.

8. **Cluster stability is 0.93 on the core but 0.66 when noise is included** — meaning the cluster content is stable, the cluster boundary is not. For downstream analyses, noise should be treated as its own "unclassified" bucket, not merged into the nearest cluster.

9. **The "data engineer" archetype is flat or slightly declining** (-1.1 pp). This is a mild surprise given the broader narrative that data engineering is growing — in this SWE-only slice, it is the AI/ML cluster that is growing, and data eng is holding roughly steady.

10. **Median YOE is 4 or 5 across virtually every archetype**, with only T09 .NET (5), T05 Spring/Java (6), and T11 AWS-noninternship (3) deviating. YOE is homogeneously mid-career across the posting pool; junior-heavy clusters don't really exist.

`exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet` — 8,000 rows, columns `uid`, `archetype` (int, -1 for noise), `archetype_name` (e.g., `T01_ai / learning / machine learning`). T08, T11, T16, T20 and any downstream task that wants an archetype column can join on `uid`.

**Caveats for downstream users:**
1. Labels cover only the 8,000-doc sample, not the full 63,294 SWE pool. To extend: run BERTopic's `.transform` on the full pool using the saved model (not persisted in this run — rerun T09 with a save step if needed).
2. Archetypes 14, 20, and 21 are company/scraper boilerplate artifacts, not labor-market signals. Exclude or flag them before any composition analysis.
3. The noise topic (-1, 28%) is genuine semantic long-tail; treat as an "unclassified" stratum, not as a 24th cluster.
4. Archetype-level seniority claims should report both the combined best-available column and the YOE-based junior proxy, and should disclose the known-label denominator per cell.

## 13. Recommendation for Downstream Use

**Use BERTopic mts=30 labels as the primary archetype variable.** NMF k=12 is a useful robustness check and boilerplate auditor, but it should not be the primary assignment. For composition analyses (RQ1-style), use the 23 BERTopic topics (or aggregate the three artifact topics into "boilerplate" and drop noise) as a **domain control** — not as a seniority proxy.
