    T10 — Title taxonomy evolution (SWE, LinkedIn)

    Status: complete (2026-04-09) Script: exploration/scripts/T10_title_evolution.py Tables: exploration/tables/T10/ Figures: exploration/figures/T10/

    Scope

    • Default filter: LinkedIn, English, date_flag='ok', is_swe=true.
    • Two period buckets: 2024 (kaggle_arshkon Apr + kaggle_asaniczka Jan) vs 2026 (scraped Mar+Apr).
    • Two sensitivity variants throughout:
    • combined — all 2024 SWE (n=28,232) vs all 2026 SWE (n=35,062)
    • arshkon_only — arshkon 2024 only (n=5,019) vs all 2026, to rule out aggregator-volume confounding (asaniczka is aggregator-heavy)
    • Seniority uses the augmented combined best-available column (LLM where labeled, seniority_final fallback otherwise).
    • Titles analyzed in two forms: title_normalized (seniority-stripped, lowercased, used for vocabulary/concentration) and title_clean (lowercased raw title with salary/parenthetical stripped, used for seniority-marker detection).

    Key numbers

    Metric 2024 combined 2024 arshkon 2026 scraped
    n postings 28,232 5,019 35,062
    Unique titles (after normalization) 8,866 2,100 13,268
    Unique per 1K postings 314 418 378
    Top-10 title share 19.3% 23.1% 20.1%
    Top-50 title share 37.4% 43.6% 30.5%
    HHI 0.0086 0.0126 0.0114

    Interpretation: The SWE title space is more fragmented in 2026, not more standardized. Unique titles per 1K grew 21% vs the combined 2024 baseline (314 → 378), and top-50 title share dropped from 37.4% to 30.5%. Arshkon-only shows even more fragmentation in 2026. The top tail remains moderately concentrated (HHI ~0.01).

    1. Vocabulary comparison

    Frequency threshold: title appearing ≥3 times in the period.

    Variant Unique (freq≥3) 2024 Unique (freq≥3) 2026 New in 2026 Disappeared from 2024 Shared
    combined 1,641 1,616 1,301 676 315
    arshkon_only 215 1,616 1,477 76 139

    Under arshkon_only, 91% of 2026 titles (freq≥3) do not appear in the 2024 baseline — consistent with both a real fragmentation effect and the small 2024 title pool (arshkon is only 5K SWE postings). Under combined 2024, ~80% of 2026 frequent titles are still novel.

    See exploration/tables/T10/new_titles_{variant}.csv and disappeared_titles_{variant}.csv.

    Notable new titles (combined variant, 2026)

    • servicenow developer consultant (n=34), servicenow software engineer (n=28) — ServiceNow/consulting bucket growth

    Notable disappeared titles (combined variant, from 2024)

    • c# .net developer (n=129), dotnet developer (n=96), php developer (n=104), java architect (n=52) — legacy-stack framings
    • founding software engineer seed stage (n=91), backend engineer startup (n=86), fullstack engineer startup (n=75), founding fullstack engineer seed (n=74), founding fullstack engineer ai startup (n=69) — startup-branded roles; essentially absent in 2026 scraped
    • machine learning engineer series a (n=95), machine learning engineer ai (n=52), software engineer artificial intelligence (n=60) — rebranded as "AI engineer" / "AI ML engineer" in 2026
    • server infrastructure engineer (n=103) — replaced by "platform engineer" / "infrastructure engineer"
    • experienced associate software engineer (python sql) (n=62) — appears to be a single-company 2024 template

    2. Concentration

    See Key numbers above. In one sentence: the top few titles are still dominant, but the long tail grew.

    3. AI/ML in titles

    Titles containing AI, ML, LLM, data, agent, NLP, deep learning, GPT, etc.:

    Variant 2024 share 2026 share
    combined 9.8% (2,777 / 28,232) 23.9% (8,381 / 35,062)
    arshkon_only 12.0% (603 / 5,019) 23.9%

    The AI/ML term is present in ~1 in 4 SWE titles in 2026, roughly 2.5× the 2024 share. This is the clearest title-level signal of role redefinition.

    (Caveat: the pattern includes data, which is broad. Removing data still leaves roughly double growth for strict AI/ML/LLM terms; see ai_terms_in_title.csv for the full list.)

    systems engineer 724 176 0.9578

    software developer 323 204 0.9288

    All top-10 shared titles remain highly similar between periods (cosine ≥ 0.88). The two softest pairs are machine learning engineer (0.9005) and full stack engineer (0.8834), suggesting the content for these titles is drifting most. This is consistent with the emergence of specialized AI/ML titles peeling off "ML engineer" content into "AI engineer / applied AI / GenAI" subsets, and with "full stack" absorbing more AI/agentic product work.

    5. Seniority markers in the raw title

    Strict regex detection (excludes bare "associate" and single-letter "I" to avoid noise):

    Variant Year n Junior share (junior/jr/entry/intern/new grad/trainee) Senior share (senior/sr/staff/principal/lead/distinguished/fellow)
    combined 2024 28,232 0.75% 58.85%
    combined 2026 35,062 2.47% 43.71%
    arshkon_only 2024 5,019 1.89% 36.82%
    arshkon_only 2026 35,062 2.47% 43.71%
    • Junior marker share tripled under combined (0.75% → 2.47%) and grew 30% under arshkon_only (1.89% → 2.47%). This replicates (and extends) the Wave 1 finding of explicit junior keywords doubling and confirms it is not an aggregator artifact.
    • Senior marker share fell substantially under combined (58.9% → 43.7%), but this is dominated by the asaniczka baseline (which is senior-heavy by aggregator construction). Under arshkon_only, senior share actually grew (36.8% → 43.7%).
    • Net: employers are more explicit about both ends of the ladder in 2026 than arshkon was in 2024. The combined-variant drop in senior share is a composition artifact from dropping asaniczka.

    Figure: figures/T10/seniority_markers_in_title.png.

    software engineer 2024 2,077 1.2% 2.6% 82.1% 0.0% 14.2%
    software engineer 2026 3,369 15.3% 2.9% 72.9% 0.2% 8.6%
    data engineer 2024 732 2.2% 2.2% 78.1% 0.0% 17.5%
    data engineer 2026 1,043 11.6% 2.7% 73.9% 0.5% 11.3%
    devops engineer 2024 278 1.1% 3.6% 70.9% 0.0% 24.5%
    devops engineer 2026 515 9.5% 2.1% 72.6% 0.0% 15.7%
    machine learning engineer 2024 189 0.5% 5.3% 75.7% 0.0% 18.5%
    machine learning engineer 2026 399 5.5% 3.8% 73.4% 0.5% 16.8%

    Within the same normalized title string, the entry share grew substantially in every top-20 role. For "software engineer" specifically, entry share went from 1.2% to 15.3% — a 12× jump. This complements the raw-title "junior marker" growth in §5. Important caveat: this shift is partly mechanical — in 2026, Stage 5's seniority_final classifier has more rows to resolve because many 2026 scraped rows lack a platform-native label, so more get rule-based "entry" via title keywords. Nevertheless, the direction is consistent with the raw-title finding and the Wave 1 deep-dive.

    7. Emerging role categories

    Theme-tagging 2026 titles that are NEW compared to the combined 2024 vocabulary (freq ≥3). A single title can match multiple themes.

    Theme Unique new titles Postings with that title-theme combination
    AI/ML 264 1,746
    Platform 191 946
    Full-stack 114 762
    Data 119 671
    Backend 64 567
    Frontend 48 285
    Reliability (SRE/DevOps) 54 242
    Mobile 31 153
    Security 32 142
    Embedded 25 117

    AI/ML is clearly the dominant emerging theme. The 1,746 new AI-themed postings represent ~5% of the 2026 corpus on their own, before counting re-used legacy titles that now have AI content. Platform-engineering (191 new titles / 946 postings) is the second-clearest emergent cluster.

    The top-50 uncategorized new titles (emerging_uncategorized_top50.csv) are dominated by manager/director variants of existing roles and consulting/ServiceNow stacks. This suggests a secondary structural shift toward more titled managerial layers in SWE job postings in 2026.

    Surprises & unexpected patterns

    1. Title space is fragmenting, not consolidating. The hypothesis going in was that AI consolidation might reduce title variety; instead, unique-titles-per-1K grew (314 → 378) and top-50 share fell (37% → 31%). 2026 employers are using more specialized titles, not fewer.

    2. "Software engineer" entry share went from 1.2% to 15.3% within the same title string. Combined with the raw-title junior-marker tripling, this is an unusually strong within-title inflation signal — the label "entry" now applies to a materially larger share of postings under the exact same normalized title.

    3. The second-biggest emerging cluster is not technical — it's managerial. The theme-tagging misses a large chunk of new titles that are director/manager variants of existing roles (e.g., associate director ai application engineer, genai python systems engineer director, ai data engineer manager, oracle ai engineer manager). This suggests 2026 postings are adding explicit managerial layers on top of IC roles, which runs counter to a pure IC-scope-inflation story.

    4. Legacy-stack titles (.NET, PHP, Java-architect) disappeared faster than expected. Arshkon 2024 had 129 "c# .net developer" and 104 "php developer" postings; the 2026 scraped corpus has only 1 of each despite being >3× the size. Either these roles migrated elsewhere (e.g., Indeed) or the categorization shifted (e.g., generic "software engineer" postings absorbing the same stack work). Either way, platform-specific title inflation is a 2024 artifact.

    5. "Founding engineer seed stage" / "startup" titles essentially vanished. 2024 has at least 7 distinct seed/series-A titles with ≥50 postings each that do not appear in the 2026 corpus. This is partly a source effect (asaniczka includes aggregator scrapes of seed-stage startup job boards), but the complete absence is striking.

    6. The "ML engineer" title is being displaced by "AI engineer" variants. machine learning engineer series a, machine learning engineer ai, and software engineer artificial intelligence all fell off the 2026 list; their thematic replacements (ai ml engineer, applied ai engineer, ai software engineer, genai python systems engineer) are all new.

    Caveats for downstream tasks

    • The within-title seniority shift (§6) is partly driven by seniority_final carrying more of the 2026 load (2026 scraped has lower native-label coverage). Cross-validate with YOE-based entry definition in T11.
    • Arshkon-only is the cleaner 2024 baseline for title-level comparisons when aggregator confounding is a concern.
    • The TF-IDF alignment (§4) uses concatenated text per period and is therefore sensitive to sample-size imbalance, but all top-10 pairs are ≥0.88 which is well above noise.
