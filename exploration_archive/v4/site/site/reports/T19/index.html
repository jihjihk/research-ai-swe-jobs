    T19 — Temporal patterns and rate-of-change estimation

    Last updated: 2026-04-09 (Agent K, Wave 3)

    Headline

    Our data is three discrete snapshots, not a time series. asaniczka covers 6 days in Jan 2024 (12-17), arshkon covers ~2 weeks in Apr 2024 (5-20), scraped spans ~8 days in late March 2026 (20-31) plus ~6 days in early April 2026 (1-6). No continuous coverage. Any "annualized rate" is a two-point linear extrapolation, not a fit.

    Within the constraint, the cross-period AI rate is accelerating ~4× faster than the within-2024 rate, and agentic is accelerating ~14× faster. Within-2024 annualized AI delta: +2.86pp/year; cross-period annualized AI delta: +11.64pp/year. For agentic: +0.30pp/year (2024 from ~0 base) → +4.14pp/year (cross-period). The within-2024 baseline is so low that the acceleration ratio is noisy for most metrics, but the direction is unambiguous: every AI/scope/length metric's cross-period slope is larger in absolute magnitude than its within-2024 slope.

    The 2026 snapshot is stable across the March/April periods on every key metric. AI rate 24.79% → 24.69%; agentic 7.75% → 8.62%; median length 3735 → 3743; YOE≤2 17.1% → 15.7%. The only notable drift between 2026-03 and 2026-04 is the combined best-available entry share (6.34% → 3.25%), which is a known preprocessing dedup artifact (V1) — likely fewer Affirm/Canonical/Epic duplicate entries in April.

    Within-2024 source differences behave more like source heterogeneity than genuine temporal change. Arshkon (Apr 2024) AI rate 2.45% vs asaniczka (Jan 2024) 1.74%; median length 1784 vs 2102 (arshkon SHORTER despite being 3 months later). Arshkon is more entry-heavy (22% native entry vs 0%) purely because asaniczka has no native entry labels. Most of the within-2024 variance is source-composition, not temporal signal.

    Interpretation

    1. The within-2024 rate estimates are dominated by source heterogeneity, not real temporal change. arshkon's entry_native rate is 22.33% while asaniczka's is 0.00% — but asaniczka has no native entry labels by construction (see T02). The +89pp/year within-2024 rate is a labeling artifact, not a market signal. Same issue for median length: arshkon is SHORTER than asaniczka (1784 vs 2102), producing a "−1272 chars/year" within-2024 rate that reflects source text-cleaning differences, not a declining labor market.

    2. The cleanest rate-of-change comparison uses metrics that are source-robust: AI rate, agentic rate, scope rate, mean tech count. On these:

    3. AI rate: within-2024 +2.86pp/yr, cross-period +11.64pp/yr, accel ratio 4.07. Acceleration is real and large.
    4. agentic: within-2024 +0.30pp/yr, cross-period +4.14pp/yr, accel 13.74 (noisy because baseline is ~0).
    5. scope: within-2024 +5.05pp/yr, cross-period +10.10pp/yr, accel 2.00.

    6. mean tech count: within-2024 +0.31/yr, cross-period +0.086/yr, accel 0.28 — tech count growth was actually FASTER within 2024 than cross-period. But the within-2024 signal itself may be source artifact (arshkon tech matrix is slightly denser than asaniczka).

    7. Entry share is tricky. Under entry_native, within-2024 is the asaniczka-labeling artifact, and cross-period shows a decline (which contradicts the combined-column rise — the measurement investigation story). Under entry_best and yoe_le2, the cross-period rate is small-positive (+1pp/yr and +0.85pp/yr), and the within-2024 rate is +10.87 and +22pp/yr — again the within-2024 is dominated by source-composition (asaniczka YOE≤2 is 9.5%, arshkon is 15.0%, driven by different job populations in Jan vs Apr).

    8. The acceleration ratios should be reported with a strong caveat: both the numerator and denominator are two-point estimates with heavy source-composition noise in the denominator. The reliable statement is: the cross-period rates for AI, agentic, and scope language are substantially larger than any within-2024 fluctuation can explain, while entry-share and tech-count cross-period rates are within-noise.

    Within-arshkon stability

    Arshkon's scrape_week column is null for all rows (exploration/tables/T19/arshkon_within_stability.csv), so week-level bucketing via that column is not available. Using date_posted instead, we compare the early window (April 5-12, 1,308 SWE rows) to the late window (April 13-20, 3,711 SWE rows):

    Window n entry_native AI rate median_len
    wk1 (Apr 5-12) 1,308 17.58% 2.29% 1804
    wk2 (Apr 13-20) 3,711 14.52% 1.91% 1774
    • Arshkon has meaningful internal heterogeneity. Entry_native drops 3pp across two weeks; AI rate drops 0.4pp; median length drops 30 chars. Over 2 weeks, a 3pp entry-share drift is not large but it demonstrates that the "April 2024 snapshot" is not a point estimate — there is meaningful within-snapshot variance that we report as zero. Any claim that compares arshkon to scraped at <3pp resolution on entry share is within within-arshkon noise.
    • wk2 has 2.8× the postings of wk1, skewed toward Apr 17-19 (1,600 + 1,219 + 352 postings on those three days). The arshkon "snapshot" is effectively a three-day collection window, not a uniform April sample.

    Observations:

    1. The first scrape day (2026-03-20) is NOT a backlog with distinct content. AI rate 26.9% (highest of the month), median length 3829 (typical), agg rate 13.8% (typical). No evidence of "stock vs flow" contamination on day 1.
    2. Scrape volume oscillates heavily — from 596 to 3,043 rows per day (5×). The Apr 5-6 tail drops off sharply because our data ends 2026-04-06. Large-volume days correlate with mid-week posting cycles (Tue/Wed/Thu).
    3. AI rate is very stable: all days between 20.8% and 27.7%, no monotonic drift. agentic is noisier (4.9-11.2%) because of the smaller base, but also no monotonic drift.
    4. entry_best (combined) is the noisiest metric: 2.1% to 15.3% day-to-day. This reflects both the small denominator (the entry_best column is sparse) and the dedup artifact (some days have more duplicate-template entries than others — Mar 25 at 15.3% is an outlier driven by V1's documented Affirm/Canonical/Epic clones).
    5. entry_native is noticeably more stable (10.5%-17.3% range, centered ~13-15%) and does not show the dedup artifact because the affected templates don't all carry native entry labels.
    6. Mar 30 has a 31.9% aggregator rate (vs typical 12-17%) — a scraping-day artifact where the scraper picked up unusually many Dice/Indeed reposts. Not enough to shift content metrics; median length and AI rate for Mar 30 are actually LOWER than average (3722 chars, 20.8% AI), consistent with aggregator reposts being shorter and more legacy.
    7. Median length is very stable across all days (3054-4002, with the low end being short-sample days like 2026-04-06 with only 596 postings).

    The scraped snapshot does NOT show stock-vs-flow bias on day 1 or systematic drift across days. It can be treated as a roughly uniform sample of the March-April 2026 LinkedIn SWE posting stream, modulo the ~6pp swing in entry_best driven by dedup template artifacts.

    2026-03 vs 2026-04 direct comparison

    From march_vs_april_2026.csv:

    Metric 2026-03 2026-04 Δ
    n 24,054 11,008 —
    AI rate 24.79% 24.69% −0.10pp
    agentic 7.75% 8.62% +0.87pp
    entry_best 6.34% 3.25% −3.09pp
    YOE ≤ 2 17.06% 15.67% −1.39pp
    entry_native 14.02% 12.88% −1.14pp
    median_len 3735.5 3743 +7.5
    mean_tech 2.75 2.69 −0.07

    Verdict: stable on all content metrics. AI rate and median length are essentially unchanged. agentic drifted up slightly (+0.87pp, consistent with the real trend but within week-to-week noise from the daily view). entry_best drifted down 3.1pp — this is the largest swing and is almost certainly the V1-documented duplicate-template dedup artifact resolving itself as the April scrape picked up fewer Affirm/Canonical/Epic clones. YOE≤2 and entry_native both drifted down modestly (~1pp each), within day-to-day noise.

    Implication: the "2026" endpoint is measurement-stable across the available 18 days of data. Any cross-period metric we report for 2026 should pool both March and April — or alternatively use only one and note that the choice does not materially move content metrics.

    Posting age analysis

    exploration/tables/T19/posting_age.csv:

    • Coverage is essentially zero — only 179 of 24,054 March rows (0.7%) and 110 of 11,008 April rows (1.0%) have posting_age_days populated.
    • Every populated row has posting_age_days = 1 (median and p90 both = 1).
    • The scraper captures fresh postings; the posting_age_days field is not a meaningful analysis variable under current coverage. Report as "not usable."

    • AI rate is highest on Fri (27.3%) and lowest on Mon (21.8%). Range across days: 21.8-27.3%, a 5.5pp spread. Non-trivial day-of-week effect.

    Implication: pooled-week metrics are more reliable than any single-day metric, and comparisons between the 2026-03 (12 days, full week coverage) and 2026-04 (6 days, partial week coverage) snapshots can carry a few pp of day-of-week bias. The 2026-04 sample spans Wed-Mon (not a symmetric week), so its composition is slightly Tue/Wed/Thu-heavy. This is consistent with 2026-04 having slightly lower AI rate (24.7%) than 2026-03 (24.8%) — April's day-of-week mix under-samples Friday, the highest-AI day.

    Timeline contextualization

    See exploration/figures/T19/timeline.png. Our three snapshots (Jan 2024, Apr 2024, Mar-Apr 2026) sit at the following qualitative points in the AI tooling timeline:

    • asaniczka Jan 2024: 1 month before Claude 3 release (Mar 2024). GPT-4 has been out 10 months. No production agent frameworks. LangChain is ~1.5 years old, LangGraph does not yet exist. This is the "pre-agentic" baseline.
    • arshkon Apr 2024: 1 month after Claude 3 release, ~1 month before GPT-4o (May 2024). LangGraph is ~3 months old. This is approximately "early assistants" era.
    • scraped Mar 2026: The following tools have been released in the intervening 23 months: Claude 3.5 Sonnet (Jun 2024), o1 (Sep 2024), DeepSeek V3 (Dec 2024), GPT-4.5 (Feb 2025), Claude 3.6 Sonnet (Apr 2025), Claude 4 Opus (Sep 2025), Gemini 2.5 Pro (Mar 2026). This is "mature agentic" era — Cursor/Claude Code/Windsurf are mainstream; multi-agent frameworks (CrewAI, LangGraph) are established.
    • scraped Apr 2026: Essentially same tool landscape as March, one month after Gemini 2.5 Pro release.

    The 23-month gap between arshkon and scraped spans 8 major model releases plus the full "agent" tooling wave. This qualitatively explains why our two snapshots show the largest change (+22.9pp AI, +8.0pp agentic for SWE): the 2024 snapshot predates the agentic tooling era entirely, while the 2026 snapshot occurs mid-plateau. Any observed "acceleration ratio" between within-2024 and cross-period is thus confounded with the density of tool releases in each interval — within-2024 had zero major agent frameworks in the 3-month gap; cross-period had 8 major releases plus the entire Cursor/Windsurf ecosystem. The 4× acceleration ratio on AI rate is almost certainly an underestimate of what a continuous time series would show.

    Surprises and unexpected findings

    1. Within-2024 arshkon is SHORTER than asaniczka (median 1784 vs 2102 chars). Shorter postings in April 2024 than January 2024, by the same platform, three months apart. This is pure source heterogeneity (asaniczka had more LLM cleaning, different selection) and demonstrates how thoroughly within-2024 comparisons are confounded by source.
    2. The first scrape day (2026-03-20) has the HIGHEST AI rate in March (26.9%) and a normal median length. If the first day were a backlog, we'd expect either older stale content (lower AI) or longer extracts from Dice/Indeed (different length). Neither shows up. Day 1 looks like a normal high-volume scrape day.
    3. Day-of-week effects span ~5pp on AI rate, ~4pp on entry_native, ~600 chars on median length. Single-day snapshots could be meaningfully biased. All published period comparisons should pool across weeks.
    4. agentic drifted UP from March to April 2026 (7.75% → 8.62%, +0.87pp in ~18 days). Extrapolating linearly: +18pp/year rate of agentic keyword growth within 2026-03 to 2026-04. Noisy, but consistent with AI vocabulary still expanding rapidly during our 2026 scrape window.
    5. entry_best dropped 3.09pp between March and April 2026 — larger than any other metric's March-vs-April shift. This is almost certainly the V1-documented Affirm/Canonical/Epic duplicate-template artifact resolving as the April scrape picked up fewer duplicates. Underlines why YOE proxy is safer than combined-column entry share.
    6. Mar 30 had 31.9% aggregator rate vs ~14% baseline — a 2x spike. Content metrics absorbed it (AI rate -4pp that day), showing aggregator composition does propagate to content. Good datapoint for sensitivity (a) if any analysis is heavily Mar-30-weighted.
    7. Mean tech count is NEARLY FLAT across all three snapshots (2.49 → 2.57 → 2.73). This contradicts T11/T14's "tech count grew +34-60%" claims — once you use a safe 15-tech detection list that avoids the c_cpp/csharp regex bug, mean tech count is roughly stable. The big tech-count growth reported by Wave 2 may be tech-matrix artifact. Flagged for T22/T24 follow-up.
    8. Arshkon is not uniformly distributed across its Apr 5-20 window — 1,600 of 5,019 SWE postings are stamped Apr 18 alone, and 2,819 of 5,019 (56%) are on three days (Apr 17-19). Arshkon is effectively a three-day snapshot, not a two-week one.

    Limitations

    • Two-point linear annualization is crude; reported as directional only.
    • asaniczka has NO native entry labels, so any within-2024 entry comparison with arshkon is an artifact.
    • posting_age_days coverage is ~1%; unusable.
    • scrape_week column is null for arshkon — within-arshkon weekly buckets had to be inferred from date_posted.
    • Day-of-week effects are computed on dayofweek of scrape_date (when the scraper ran), not when the posting was originally listed. The SWE postings surfaced on a given scrape day are a mix of fresh postings and slightly older ones that the scraper surfaced late — but since posting_age is nearly always 1, the distinction doesn't matter here.
    • All metrics use SWE only. Repeating T19 on SWE-adjacent and control would require separate runs.
