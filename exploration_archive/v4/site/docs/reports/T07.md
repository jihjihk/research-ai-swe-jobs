# T07: External Benchmarks & Power Analysis

## Headline Finding

**The sample is geographically representative (r = 0.985 vs BLS OES) and all main aggregate comparisons are well-powered, but entry-level analyses under `seniority_llm` are severely underpowered (n = 84 arshkon entry, n = 180 scraped entry).** The binding constraint for the entire project is entry-level sample size under the LLM seniority variable. Under `seniority_final`, entry comparisons are adequate (n = 848 vs 4,656), and the observed 2024-to-2026 entry share decline (16.9% -> 13.3%) is already detectable. Metro-level analyses require pooling asaniczka into 2024, which is valid only for non-entry-level metrics unless `seniority_llm` resolves asaniczka's missing entry labels.

### 8. JOLTS Information Sector Context

JOLTS data (seasonally adjusted, series JTS510000000000000JOL) provides hiring-cycle context:

| Period | JOLTS Info Openings (000s) | Our Data |
|---|---|---|
| 2022 peak average | 224K | (pre-data) |
| Jan 2024 | 160K | Asaniczka period |
| Apr 2024 | 87K | Arshkon period |
| 2024 full-year average | 123K | -- |
| Late 2025 (Sep-Dec avg) | 109K | Closest to scraped |
| Mar 2026 | (not yet released) | Scraped period |

**Key observation:** Our two 2024 data points straddle a **significant JOLTS swing**. Asaniczka (Jan 2024) was collected during a relative uptick (160K openings), while arshkon (Apr 2024) was collected near a **trough** (87K, the lowest single month in 2024). This means:

1. Comparing asaniczka to scraped captures a different part of the hiring cycle than arshkon to scraped.
2. The late-2025 JOLTS level (~109K avg) is **below** Jan 2024 (160K) but **above** Apr 2024 (87K), so the scraped period is roughly in the middle of the 2024 range.
3. From the 2022 peak (~224K avg), information-sector openings fell **45%** by 2024 -- our data captures the post-peak period, not the peak itself.

### 10. Sample Representativeness Framing

**What our sample represents:**
- LinkedIn-posted SWE job openings across 26 major US metros
- Three time snapshots: Jan 2024 (asaniczka, large volume), Apr 2024 (arshkon, smaller but richer labels), Mar 2026+ (scraped, daily collection)
- Geographic coverage spans all 50 states + DC, with 77.7% of scraped SWE assigned to one of the 26 study metros

**What our sample does NOT represent:**
- All US SWE positions (LinkedIn captures a subset; Indeed, company websites, and internal postings are excluded)
- The full SWE labor market (postings != actual employment or hiring)
- Non-US labor markets
- The universe of tech job titles (our SWE classifier selects a specific occupation slice)
- Ghost/phantom postings are included in the count (our ghost assessment flags these but does not remove them)

**Population inference framework:** Our sample should be described as "a convenience sample of LinkedIn SWE postings across major US metros, highly correlated with BLS state-level SWE employment (r = 0.985)" rather than as a representative sample of the SWE labor market. The geographic representativeness claim is strong; the platform-coverage claim is necessarily limited.

## Surprises & Unexpected Patterns

1. **The seniority_llm entry-level crisis is worse than anticipated.** Only 84 arshkon entry and 180 scraped entry in the labeled LLM subset. This is not just "thin" -- it is below the threshold for any reasonable effect detection. The LLM classifier's explicit-signal-only approach produces ~2.7% entry rate vs seniority_final's ~16.9%. Most entry-level postings apparently lack explicit entry signals in the title/description and get classified as "unknown." This is a fundamental tension: the most methodologically rigorous seniority variable is also the least powerful for the project's central question.

2. **Arshkon was collected during a JOLTS trough.** The Apr 2024 JOLTS reading (87K) is the lowest single month in 2024, while Jan 2024 (asaniczka) shows 160K. Any comparison between arshkon and scraped is confounded by this hiring-cycle position. Pooling the two 2024 sources would partially average this out, but the asymmetric timing should be noted in any cross-period comparison.

3. **Industry distribution shifted between arshkon and scraped.** IT Services was the dominant industry in arshkon (24.4%) but fell to second place in scraped (11.1%), while Software Development rose from 15.8% to 25.7%. This could represent real market shifts, LinkedIn platform changes, or differences in how employers self-categorize. This is a potential confound for any analysis that assumes stable industry composition across periods.

4. **New York City metro is dramatically underrepresented in arshkon.** Only 11 SWE postings vs 1,862 in scraped. This is likely a search-query artifact in the original Kaggle dataset. NYC metro-level comparisons should use asaniczka (55 SWE) or pooled 2024 (66 SWE), but even pooled, this is thin.

5. **Scraped data now spans two periods (2026-03 and 2026-04).** The scraped source contains 24,054 SWE from 2026-03 and 11,008 from 2026-04, indicating the scraper has been running for over a month. This provides some within-2026 temporal variation for the scraped period.

## Data Quality Caveats

- **BLS OES numbers are hand-entered** from published May 2023 tables because the BLS CDN blocks automated downloads and the BLS public API does not serve OES cross-sectional data as time series. Values should be verified against [BLS OES 15-1252](https://www.bls.gov/oes/2023/may/oes151252.htm) if results are used in publication.
- **JOLTS data is for the Information sector (NAICS 51)**, not specifically software development. Information sector includes publishing, broadcasting, telecommunications, and data processing -- a broader category than our SWE sample.
- **Power calculations assume independent observations.** If postings cluster by company (i.e., one company posts 50 similar positions), the effective sample size is smaller than the nominal count, and MDEs are underestimates. This is especially relevant for the company panel analysis.
- **MDE calculations use standard normal approximations** (z-test for proportions, t-test for means). For very small cells (associate seniority_llm = 15, director = 1), these approximations are unreliable and exact tests should be used.

## Methodology

- **Power calculations:** Standard two-sample z-test for proportions and t-test for means, at 80% power, alpha = 0.05 (two-sided). MDE_binary computed at p_baseline = 0.30 for the feasibility table, and at actual observed baselines for the entry-share-specific table.
- **Geographic correlation:** Pearson r, Pearson r (log-log), and Spearman rho between our state-level SWE posting counts (all sources pooled) and BLS OES May 2023 employment for SOC 15-1252.
- **JOLTS data:** Seasonally adjusted series JTS510000000000000JOL from BLS API. Values are in thousands.
- **BLS OES state data:** Published May 2023 employment for SOC 15-1252 from bls.gov/oes/2023/may/oes151252.htm.
