| period | seniority | llm text | rule text |

**2024 is ~94% LLM-cleaned text; 2026 is ~79% rule-cleaned.** This is a huge text_source confound — the LLM extractor produces crisper, shorter, more prose-like text, while the rule-based extractor retains more boilerplate. Any "2024 vs 2026" similarity comparison is simultaneously comparing **the text-cleaning pipeline used**. This confound was noted in T05 for description length (+57% median). Here it may affect **every centroid distance**.

## 3. Convergence / divergence of seniority levels

Between-seniority cosine *within* each period, trimmed centroids.

| Pair | Representation | 2024 | 2026 | delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| junior vs mid | embeddings | 0.9938 | 0.9937 | -0.00008 |
| junior vs senior | embeddings | 0.9935 | 0.9922 | **-0.00131** |
| mid vs senior | embeddings | 0.9913 | 0.9941 | +0.00287 |
| junior vs mid | tfidf | 0.9825 | 0.9644 | **-0.01813** |
| junior vs senior | tfidf | 0.9853 | 0.9629 | **-0.02247** |
| mid vs senior | tfidf | 0.9772 | 0.9770 | -0.00026 |

**Directional split by representation:**

- **Embeddings:** Seniority contrasts are essentially flat 2024->2026. Junior vs senior drops microscopically (-0.001). Mid vs senior actually rises (+0.003). No convergence signal at all.
- **TF-IDF:** Junior DIVERGES from mid and senior. Junior-vs-senior cosine falls from 0.985 to 0.963 (Delta -0.022) — more than the within-2024 source variability (see Step 3b). Mid-senior similarity is unchanged.

**The two representations disagree on whether there is any seniority convergence, and on which direction it runs.** Neither shows the "junior jobs are becoming like senior jobs" convergence that a strong RQ1 story would want. If anything, TF-IDF says junior postings are becoming *more linguistically distinct* from senior postings by 2026 — the opposite direction. Embeddings say nothing is changing between seniority levels.

This is the single most important T15 finding for the re-analysis: **there is no embedding-level convergence of juniors toward seniors in descriptive text.**

### 3b. Within-2024 calibration (mandatory)

Trimmed-centroid cosines between source pairs:

| Representation | arshkon vs asaniczka | arshkon vs scraped | asaniczka vs scraped |
|---|---|---|---|
| embeddings | **0.9945** | 0.9673 | 0.9630 |
| tfidf | **0.9890** | 0.9022 | 0.8845 |

**Calibration check for convergence:** The within-2024 arshkon<->asaniczka centroid gap is 0.0055 (embeddings) or 0.0110 (TF-IDF). The junior-senior convergence we'd need to detect is at most 0.00131 (embeddings, wrong sign) or 0.02247 (TF-IDF, wrong sign).

- Under embeddings: **the seniority convergence signal is smaller than source-variation noise by 4x.** Not interpretable.
- Under TF-IDF: The seniority-divergence signal (0.022) is ~2x the within-2024 source noise (0.011). It may survive, but barely, and in the "junior more distinct" direction.

**The Agent I preamble instruction was:** "if the asaniczka->arshkon similarity shift exceeds the arshkon->scraped shift, the convergence signal doesn't survive calibration." In embeddings the arshkon<->asaniczka gap (0.0055) is smaller than arshkon<->scraped (0.0327), but the seniority pair Delta is a THIRD-order effect and doesn't exceed either. **Conclusion: no convergence claim is warranted from these data.**

Table: `tables/T15/within2024_calibration_centroids.csv`.

## 5. UMAP / PCA / t-SNE visualization

`figures/T15/umap_period_seniority.png` shows the full 9,234-point embedding UMAP colored by period x seniority. The dominant visible split is **period**, with the 2024 and 2026 clouds occupying visibly different regions. Within each period, seniority levels overlap heavily.

`figures/T15/umap_density_contours.png` shows three side-by-side panels (junior, mid, senior), each with 2024 vs 2026 density contours and a black arrow connecting the 2024 centroid to the 2026 centroid. All three arrows point in the same direction and have similar magnitudes — i.e., all seniority levels shift in parallel from 2024 to 2026. This is a clean visual confirmation that the period effect is large, seniority-constant.

`figures/T15/pca_period_seniority.png` and `figures/T15/tsne_period_seniority.png` tell the same story.

**Caveat:** The period shift visible in all three reductions overlaps strongly with the text_source shift (2024 mostly LLM-cleaned, 2026 mostly rule-cleaned). We cannot distinguish "description text really changed" from "the cleaning pipeline applied to it is different" from these plots alone.

## 6. Nearest-neighbor analysis (2026 junior -> 2024)

For each 2026 junior posting, find its 5 nearest neighbors among all 2024 sample points. Count seniority labels.

| Representation | NN seniority | count | observed rate | base rate (2024 sample) | excess |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| embeddings | junior | 2,148 | 21.5% | 17.7% | **+3.8pp** |
| embeddings | mid | 2,825 | 28.3% | 36.5% | -8.2pp |
| embeddings | senior | 5,027 | 50.3% | 45.8% | +4.4pp |
| tfidf | junior | 2,424 | 24.2% | 17.7% | **+6.5pp** |
| tfidf | mid | 2,529 | 25.3% | 36.5% | -11.2pp |
| tfidf | senior | 5,047 | 50.5% | 45.8% | +4.6pp |

**Read:** Under both representations, 2026 junior postings are slightly *more* likely to retrieve 2024 junior neighbors (+3.8 to +6.5pp over base rate) than you'd expect from base rates. The "2026 junior looks like 2024 senior" story would predict a large positive excess for `senior` neighbors — what we see is a small positive excess (+4.4 to +4.6pp) that's similar to the junior excess. The real redistribution is **AWAY from mid** (-8 to -11pp), toward both ends.

**Interpretation:** 2026 junior postings don't look like 2024 mid-career postings. They look like 2024 postings at the junior OR senior ends — a mild bimodality. This is subtle and inconsistent with a clean "scope inflation" story.

By source: most 2026 junior NN hit asaniczka (which had very few labeled juniors), not arshkon. The asaniczka-heavy retrieval is a function of asaniczka's 4:1 representation in 2024.

## 8. Outlier identification

Per-group bottom-15 postings by cosine to trimmed centroid. Selected examples:

**2024 junior outliers** — "Sales Application Engineer", "Brand Marketing Web Developer Intern", "Volunteer: Nature Counter React Native Full Stack Developer". The lowest-cosine 2024 juniors are genuine niche/adjacent roles (sales-eng, volunteer postings).

**2024 mid outliers** — volunteer postings (Nature Counter, Nobel Prize Library) dominate the bottom. This is a clear data-quality signal: asaniczka has volunteer job postings in its SWE set. These should probably be filtered upstream.

**2024 senior outliers** — contract/visa-specific postings ("GCP Data Engineer- Need on W2 or 1099"), embedded ("Senior Firmware Engineer"), and platform-specific ("Salesforce Developer"). These are real-but-distinctive engineering roles.

**2026 junior outliers** — "Backend AI-Forward Data Engineer", "Lab Software Engineer I", "Web Application Developer". More genuine SWE variety; no volunteer contamination.

**2026 senior outliers** — Foreign-language ("Senior Software Engineer- Electromechanical Systems (H/F)"), embedded hardware ("Senior Firmware..."). The lowest is an oddly short "Software Engineer" posting with cosine 0.146 — probably boilerplate-stripped to a stub.

**Surprise:** Volunteer postings contaminate asaniczka SWE. Should be flagged for the preprocessing pipeline (filter titles beginning with "Volunteer:" or containing unpaid/volunteer language).

## Surprises & unexpected patterns

1. **Period dominates variation; seniority barely registers.** Within-period seniority cosine is 0.99+; between-period is 0.88-0.97. This isn't surprising but it's strong: if you drop all seniority labels and just look at the text, 2024 vs 2026 is a night/day visual split in UMAP; junior vs senior within a period is indistinguishable.
2. **No convergence. Possibly junior-divergence.** The TF-IDF side shows junior-vs-senior cosine DROPPING from 0.985 to 0.963 (-0.022). That's not convergence — that's *divergence*, and it only appears in the lexical representation, not the semantic embedding. Interpretation candidate: the *specific vocabulary* used in junior postings became more distinct (perhaps explicit "entry-level" / "new grad" / "0-2 years" / "Copilot welcome" markers), while the overall thematic semantics barely moved.
3. **Representations disagree on convergence direction.** Embeddings say "no change"; TF-IDF says "mild divergence". Downstream analyses should NOT cite a convergence finding from T15.
4. **Text_source confound is the biggest threat to validity.** 2024 sample is 94% LLM-cleaned; 2026 sample is 79% rule-cleaned. All period-level differences are partially a pipeline-stage difference. The scraped data has low LLM-extraction coverage (21% per the README), and Stage 9/10 routing is why. Any "the postings changed" claim needs to be repeated within the LLM-text-only subset for 2026 (n ~7k — borderline feasible).
5. **NN retrieval bimodality.** 2026 junior postings don't look like 2024 mid — they look either like 2024 junior OR like 2024 senior, both at +4-6pp excess over base rate. If anything, there's a mild *polarization* pattern rather than inflation.
6. **Volunteer job contamination in asaniczka.** The bottom-cosine 2024 mid outliers are almost all "Volunteer: ..." postings. The preprocessing SWE filter lets these through. Suggest adding a "Volunteer:" title-prefix filter upstream.
7. **Within-group dispersion drops for TF-IDF junior 2024->2026 (-20%)** — the lexical diversity of 2026 junior postings is *lower* than 2024 junior postings. Combined with the divergence from mid/senior, this suggests a small but consistent vocabulary shift to a more homogeneous but different lexicon.
8. **Embedding dispersion barely moves.** The semantic centroid of each group is stable, but the lexical surface changed. This is a signature of "same meaning, different words" — exactly what a cleaning-pipeline change would produce.

## Sensitivities applied

- **(a) Aggregator exclusion:** applied.
- **(c) Seniority operationalization:** tested both label-based `seniority_3level` and YOE-based proxy; findings agree.
- **(d) Description text source:** documented; not sensitivity-tested further. This is the biggest open confound. Recommend a follow-up run on LLM-text-only subsamples for 2026 postings.
- **(e) Source restriction:** LinkedIn-only throughout.
- **(f) Within-2024 calibration:** applied; seniority convergence does not survive calibration in embeddings; TF-IDF divergence survives at ~2x noise floor.
- **(g) SWE classification tier robustness:** not split in this pass.
