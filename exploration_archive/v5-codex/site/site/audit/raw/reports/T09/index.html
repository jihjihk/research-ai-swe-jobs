    T09 Posting Archetype Discovery¶

    Headline finding¶

    The SWE posting space is organized much more by tech/domain bundles than by seniority or period, and the first representation that makes that visible is NMF k=15, not BERTopic. The selected archetypes split the market into a domain-first map: requirements/compliance, backend/API and backend/data-platform, frontend/web and frontend/angular, DevOps/infra/tooling, embedded/firmware/systems, data engineering/ETL, mobile/iOS, AI/LLM workflows, and a generic workflow/admin layer. The clearest 2026 expansion is the AI/LLM archetype, which is 91.5% 2026 in the full-corpus labels.

    Methodology¶

    I built a balanced primary sample of 5,400 SWE LinkedIn rows, 2,700 from 2024 and 2,700 from 2026. The 2024 side uses arshkon only, because it has the best text quality and entry coverage; the 2026 side uses scraped LinkedIn only. The primary sample is all text_source = 'llm'. Company caps and exact company/text deduplication were applied before sampling. The shared embeddings cover 26,219 llm rows overall, but the primary sample uses only the llm subset.

    I ran BERTopic at min_topic_size = 20, 30, 50 and NMF at k = 5, 8, 12, 15. Raw-text sensitivity used a separate raw-fallback sample of 4,592 rows. The downstream label artifact, swe_archetype_labels.parquet, is built from NMF k = 15.

    What we learned¶

    • BERTopic is not the best downstream representation here. It returns only 3-6 topics, mostly a boilerplate requirements cluster, an AI/LLM cluster, and a mobile cluster. Noise is low, but the semantic resolution is too coarse for the rest of the exploration.
    • NMF is the useful representation, but not at the smallest k. k = 5 and k = 8 maximize coherence/stability by collapsing the market into too few buckets. k = 15 is the first setting that cleanly separates backend, data, infra, embedded, AI, frontend, mobile, and requirement-related work.
    • The strongest structural signal is tech domain. On the full corpus, tech_domain has the highest NMI with the selected archetypes (0.123), while seniority (0.003), period (0.028), source (0.030), text source (0.013), and aggregator status (0.007) are all much weaker.
    • The largest full-corpus archetypes are requirements/compliance (22.7%), generic workflow/admin (9.4%), embedded/firmware (8.4%), cross-functional/delivery (7.5%), DevOps/infra (6.8%), AI/LLM workflows (6.6%), backend/API (5.6%), DevOps/tooling (5.5%), frontend/web (5.2%), and backend/data-platform (5.6%).

    What surprised us¶

    • The market map is not seniority-first. seniority_3level barely moves the archetypes, and the production seniority column is even less aligned.
    • The AI/LLM archetype is real but not the whole story. It is one of several domain families, not a universal overlay on the entire sample.
    • Raw fallback does not destroy the domain map, but it makes boilerplate more visible. The raw sensitivity mostly preserves the big families while pushing more rows into compliance/legal and generic boilerplate topics.

    BERTopic min_topic_size=30 6 0.467 0.491 1.6% coarse macro-topics

    The key tradeoff is obvious in the table: coherence/stability alone would select k = 5, but that is not a credible archetype map. k = 15 is the best compromise between stability and publishable granularity.

    Methods comparison¶

    BERTopic and NMF agree on a small set of robust themes. The strong overlaps are AI/LLM workflows and Mobile/iOS; requirements/compliance also overlaps, but mostly as a generic boilerplate bucket. Everything else that matters downstream is NMF-specific because BERTopic collapses backend, data, infra, and embedded into much broader blobs.

    The practical recommendation is to use BERTopic only as a coarse discovery check and to use the NMF k = 15 labels for the rest of the exploration. That is what the full-corpus label artifact now does.

    Sensitivity checks¶

    The raw-fallback sensitivity preserves the same broad families, but it is noisier and more boilerplate-heavy. The strongest raw alignment is still requirements/compliance, AI/LLM workflows, and frontend/web. Raw text makes the compliance/legal bucket more visible and weakens some backend/infra boundaries, which is exactly why the primary analysis stays on text_source = 'llm'.

    Data quality caveats¶

    • The primary sample is intentionally not proportional: it over-represents junior and mid rows relative to the corpus so those strata are visible in an unsupervised model.
    • company_size is only meaningfully available on arshkon, so company-size alignment is a weak, arshkon-heavy diagnostic.
    • The archetypes are posting-form bundles as much as substantive role families. Boilerplate, compliance, and legal language remain a real part of the market’s observable structure.

    Current paper position¶

    T09 shifts the framing away from a pure seniority story and toward domain recomposition. The most credible headline is that SWE postings are now organized around tech/domain archetypes, with AI/LLM as the clearest 2026 growth bundle, while seniority and source instrument explain very little of the latent structure.

    Downstream action items¶

    1. Use swe_archetype_labels.parquet as the domain stratifier for T11, T16, T20, and T28.
    2. Treat NMF k = 15 as the downstream archetype representation.
    3. Use BERTopic only as a coarse comparator and noise/outlier check.
    4. When a later result looks like a market-wide change, check whether it is really domain recomposition, especially into AI/LLM.
