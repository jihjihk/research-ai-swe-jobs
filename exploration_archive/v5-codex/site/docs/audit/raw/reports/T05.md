# T05 Cross-Dataset Comparability

## Headline finding
The three LinkedIn source datasets are not interchangeable, but the mismatch is uneven. Description length clearly shifts upward from the 2024 Kaggle sources to scraped 2026, and that shift is larger than the within-2024 calibration gap. By contrast, company overlap, geographic composition, and title vocabulary are already highly source-dependent in 2024, so they do not provide a clean temporal signal. Seniority is the most fragile axis: `seniority_final` is much more conservative than the YOE proxy in every source, and the 2024 source baseline is noisy enough that junior-share claims need the label-independent check.

## What we learned
- Current SWE counts on the default filtered frame are `kaggle_arshkon` 4,691, `kaggle_asaniczka` 18,129, and `scraped` 37,152 on LinkedIn; the scraped Indeed sensitivity sample adds 10,464 SWE rows.
- Description length is higher in scraped 2026 than in either 2024 Kaggle source. Mean length is 3,306 chars for arshkon, 3,881 for asaniczka, and 4,936 for scraped LinkedIn. The cross-period length effect is about 2.6x the within-2024 effect.
- Company, state, and title overlap are all low across sources, but the 2024 baseline is already low. For example, title Jaccard similarity is only 0.048 between arshkon and asaniczka and 0.027 between arshkon and scraped.
- Seniority distributions are not stable enough to treat `seniority_final` as the only junior proxy. Overall entry share under `seniority_final` is 3.7% in arshkon, 0.9% in asaniczka, and 2.8% in scraped LinkedIn, while the YOE proxy (`yoe_extracted <= 2`) is 9.3%, 7.3%, and 11.6% respectively.
- Indeed is a useful sanity check, not a contradiction. On scraped Indeed, entry share is 3.1% under `seniority_final` and 12.7% under the YOE proxy, which is close to the LinkedIn pattern rather than a platform-specific anomaly.

- The seniority story is not ŌĆ£same direction, different magnitude.ŌĆØ In `seniority_final`, the cross-period seniority distribution change from arshkon to scraped is *smaller* than the arshkon-vs-asaniczka baseline, which means the 2024 instrument noise floor is already larger than the apparent 2024-to-2026 shift.
- The title-level native label mix is unstable for generic titles. `developer` has the largest native-distribution gap in the shared-title sample, with the dominant native label shifting from entry in arshkon to mid-senior in scraped, even though the titleŌĆÖs YOE profile shift is much smaller than the label shift.
- Industry labels are semantically uneven across sources. ArshkonŌĆÖs top categories are broad labels like IT Services and IT Consulting and Software Development; scraped contains more compound or concatenated labels and a wider label inventory, so industry comparisons are descriptive only.

## Evidence assessment
- Description length: strong evidence. Large sample, large effect, survives aggregator exclusion. The cross-period CohenŌĆÖs d is -0.70, versus -0.28 within 2024.
- Company overlap: moderate evidence for source mismatch, but not a temporal story. Jaccard distances are roughly 0.86-0.89 in all pairings, and the 2024 baseline is essentially the same size as the 2024-to-2026 gap.
- Geography: moderate evidence of source composition differences, but not a useful temporal marker. CramerŌĆÖs V is 0.32-0.34 across all pairs, with almost no movement between within-2024 and cross-period comparisons.
- Seniority: weak-to-moderate evidence for temporal change because the within-2024 baseline is much larger than the 2024-to-2026 comparison. `seniority_final` is also conservative relative to the YOE proxy, which makes it risky to lead with junior-share claims alone.
- Title vocabulary: strong evidence that the three datasets are lexically different. Title Jaccard similarity is under 0.05 in every pair, and aggregator exclusion barely changes that fact.
- Industry: weak-to-moderate evidence because asaniczka has no industry data and the raw industry strings are not semantically aligned across the other two sources.

## Calibration and comparability
The calibration table says that only description length is clearly above the 2024 instrument baseline. The ratio of cross-period to within-2024 effect size is about 2.56 for description length, but is about 1.00 for company overlap and state composition, 1.02 for title vocabulary, and only 0.29 for seniority. That means:

1. Some 2026 changes are real on the text side.
2. Many categorical differences are already present in 2024.
3. Seniority needs the YOE proxy before it can support a junior-rung narrative.

## Platform labeling stability
The title-stability tables show that shared titles do not keep the same native seniority mix across periods. The clearest unstable titles are `developer`, `systems engineer`, `quality assurance engineer`, `java developer`, `full stack developer`, and `test engineer`. In the summary table, `developer` has the largest native seniority distance, and the dominant native label flips from `entry` in arshkon to `mid-senior` in scraped.

The YOE cell tables are more discriminating than the raw native-label tables. For several shared title/seniority cells, the YOE proxy moves less than the native label mix, which suggests a combination of relabeling and genuine composition shift. The largest cell shifts are in shared title cells like `system engineer`-`entry`, `embedded software engineer`-`entry`, `software developer`-`entry`, and `site reliability engineer`-`entry`.

## Sensitivity checks
Aggregator exclusion barely changes the qualitative story:

- Description length still has the same direction and a similar large effect.
- Company/title/state overlaps remain essentially unchanged.
- Seniority differences remain smaller than the 2024 baseline either way.

That is useful because it means the core comparability warning is not an aggregator artifact.

## Data quality caveats
- `description_core_llm` coverage is excellent in arshkon, still usable in asaniczka, and thin in scraped LinkedIn. For SWE rows, labeled coverage is 99.7% in arshkon, 85.9% in asaniczka, and 16.1% in scraped LinkedIn; Indeed has no LLM text coverage.
- `company_industry` is unavailable in asaniczka and semantically uneven in the other sources, so it should not be treated as a strict categorical baseline.
- `seniority_final` is conservative relative to the YOE proxy in every source, so it is useful as the primary seniority column only if it is always paired with the YOE floor check.
- Multi-location postings are excluded from state rollups, and unresolved states are carried as an explicit missing category.

## Action items for downstream tasks
- Use `seniority_final` as the primary seniority column only alongside the YOE proxy, not instead of it.
- Treat text analyses on scraped 2026 as coverage-restricted unless they are based on the raw description and clearly framed as binary presence checks.
- Treat industry as descriptive context, not a precise cross-source control.
- Read the title-stability tables before assuming that a title means the same thing in 2024 and 2026.
- When a later finding is small, compare it to the within-2024 calibration first. If it is not larger than the baseline instrument noise, do not over-interpret it.
