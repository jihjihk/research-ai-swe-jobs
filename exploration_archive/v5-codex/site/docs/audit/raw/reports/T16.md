# T16 Company Hiring Strategy Typology

## Headline finding
Returning companies do not split into a single “AI adopters versus everyone else” axis. On the primary overlap panel, 237 companies appear in both arshkon and scraped LinkedIn with at least 3 SWE postings on each side, and 122 clear the `>=5` robustness cut. K-means on the company-level change vectors picks four useful trajectories: `stack expansion` (100 companies), `template inflation / text-heavy` (52), `entry-heavy compact` (49), and `AI-forward recomposition` (36). The strongest aggregate signal is within-company growth in AI/LLM language, scope language, and cleaned-text length; the junior story is the most instrument-sensitive piece.

## What we learned
The company panel is large enough to support a real typology, but only if we treat text carefully. In the primary overlap panel, 2024 company-year rows are almost entirely `llm`-cleaned (`llm_text_share_2024 = 0.995`), while the 2026 side is mostly raw fallback (`llm_text_share_2026 = 0.140`). The llm-only overlap shrinks to 113 companies, so the strict text subset is a sensitivity check, not the main frame.

The robust company-level changes are the same ones Wave 2 already pointed to: AI/LLM mention share rises by 14.0 pp, scope share by 18.8 pp, cleaned-text length by 802 chars, and requirement breadth by 1.39 categories on the primary panel. Most of that movement is within-company. In the overlap decomposition, AI share is mostly within-company (+0.136 of +0.140), scope is mostly within-company (+0.173 of +0.188), and cleaned-text length is also mostly within-company (+593 of +802 chars). Tech count moves much less (+0.82), which is why this is not simply a “more tech” story.

The company clusters are interpretable. `AI-forward recomposition` companies look like ServiceNow, Procore, Macquarie Group, Infojini, and KPG99: AI/LLM share and domain share rise sharply, and requirement breadth expands. `stack expansion` companies such as Charter Global, ICE, Photon, and Visa look more like classic credential-stack inflation. `template inflation / text-heavy` companies such as Pinterest, Workday, Netflix, AT&T, and ByteDance add a lot of text without the same AI/domain jump. `entry-heavy compact` companies such as Anduril, OSI Engineering, TekWissen, Themesoft, and TikTok remain shorter and lower-tech but tilt more junior.

The entry story splits by instrument. On the primary arshkon-only overlap panel, explicit `seniority_final = entry` falls by 0.8 pp, while the YOE proxy rises by 5.7 pp. Under the cap-25 sensitivity, explicit entry falls by 1.0 pp and the YOE proxy rises by 0.9 pp. Under pooled 2024, explicit entry flips sign and rises by 1.4 pp, while the YOE proxy rises by 7.6 pp. That is not a cosmetic difference. It is the main measurement warning in this task.

## What surprised us
The pooled-2024 baseline matters more than expected. Using arshkon alone, the explicit-entry trend is mildly negative. Using pooled 2024, it becomes positive. The within-company AI/scope/length story survives all of those choices, but the junior claim does not. That is exactly the kind of instrument dependence the exploration was supposed to surface.

The domain story is also more nuanced than a clean “AI replaced everything else” narrative. At the family level, AI / LLM share rises, but so do Embedded and DevOps shares, while Frontend / Mobile, Data / Platform, and Requirements / Workflow all lose share. That reads more like recomposition toward AI plus infra/embedded than a one-dimensional collapse into AI jobs.

The domain decomposition is informative for explicit entry, but it is not clean for the YOE proxy. For `entry_final`, the sequential domain split is close, with only a small residual. For `entry_yoe`, the residual is large and nearly as large as the total change. I would not pretend that the domain table fully explains the YOE junior rise.

## Evidence assessment
`AI-forward recomposition` and `stack expansion` are the strongest company-strategy classes. They sit on large company counts, survive the no-aggregator and cap-25 cuts, and line up with the verified Wave 2 signals.

`template inflation / text-heavy` is also credible, but it should be framed as a posting-format trajectory as much as a hiring strategy. It is a reminder that longer descriptions do not always mean more substantive requirements.

`entry-heavy compact` is the most measurement-sensitive class. It is real, but it leans hardest on the explicit-vs-YOE split and therefore should be described as a junior-leaning compact strategy, not as a clean junior-hiring boom.

The no-aggregator and cap-25 sensitivities are reassuring. The sign of the key company-level changes does not move, and the magnitudes stay in the same ballpark. The llm-only overlap is much thinner, so it should remain a check rather than a headline.

## Narrative evaluation
The initial RQ1 framing is only partly right here. The data do not support a simple junior-collapse story at the company level. They support a more mixed story: returning firms are widening requirement stacks and AI language within their own postings, while the junior signal depends heavily on how we measure it.

The strongest alternative framing is company strategy recomposition under template drift. I prefer that over “junior roles disappeared” because it survives the verified AI/scope/length signals and does not require the unstable entry label to do all the work.

## Emerging narrative
The company-level story is not that every employer became AI-heavy. It is that firms diverged into a few strategies. Some companies added AI/LLM language and changed the domain mix; others mainly thickened the stack; others inflated text without changing the underlying technical profile much; and a smaller group stayed compact but skewed more junior-like.

## Research question evolution
RQ1 should be narrowed here to something like: `How did returning SWE employers change their posting strategy between 2024 and 2026, and how much of that change was within-company versus company composition?`

That phrasing keeps the measurement issue visible and avoids over-committing to a single junior-share narrative. The pooled-2024 sensitivity makes that revision necessary.

## Gaps and weaknesses
The llm-only overlap panel is thin, so text-heavy claims should always acknowledge the raw-fallback majority in 2026. The YOE domain decomposition is still too residual-heavy to be treated as a solved explanation. And the company clusters are heuristic, not latent truth.

## Direction for downstream analysis
Use the company typology as a filter for the next wave: follow the `AI-forward recomposition` and `stack expansion` firms into the cross-occupation and ghost-check tasks, and treat the `entry-heavy compact` firms as the best place to probe the junior measurement split.

## Current paper positioning
If we stopped here, the paper would still be an empirical restructuring paper, but the lead would be company strategy recomposition, not junior collapse. The methods angle is now central because the pooled-2024 sensitivity changes the entry conclusion while leaving the AI/scope story intact.
