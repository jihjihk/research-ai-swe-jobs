# T11: Temporal Drift

## Finding

SWE posting content has shifted substantially between 2024 and 2026, driven primarily by AI/LLM terminology and entry-level role restructuring. Overall unigram JSD (0.0376) matches within-2024 cross-source variation (0.0384), but entry-level JSD (0.0585) is 52% higher -- entry-level postings changed far more than senior ones. AI/ML keyword prevalence surged from 19.2% to 54.9% (broad) and from 3.6% to 24.2% (narrow LLM-specific), with the increase roughly uniform across seniority levels. Entry-level YOE requirements dropped sharply (mean 3.7 to 2.0 years, p < 0.001), suggesting reduced credential inflation rather than increased scope. Among the 74 newly emerging terms (>1% doc prevalence in 2026, <0.1% in 2024), the dominant pattern is AI tooling: agentic, claude, gemini, embeddings, mcp, cursor, codex, langgraph, anthropic.

## 1. Jensen-Shannon Divergence

JSD measures how different two probability distributions are (0 = identical, 1 = maximally different). Higher values indicate greater vocabulary divergence.

| Level | n_2024 | n_2026 | JSD |
|-------|--------|--------|-----|
| overall | 4438 | 4219 | 0.0376 |
| entry | 745 | 549 | 0.0585 |
| mid-senior | 2584 | 3262 | 0.0387 |
| within-2024 ref (arshkon vs asaniczka mid-senior) | 2584 | 3000 | 0.0384 |

The within-2024 reference JSD (0.0384) between arshkon and asaniczka (both early-2024 LinkedIn data) provides a baseline for how much variation exists within the same era due to source differences. The cross-era overall JSD (0.0376) is comparable, suggesting overall SWE vocabulary drift is similar to within-era source variation. However, entry-level JSD (0.0585) is 52% higher than both the overall and reference values, indicating that entry-level postings underwent disproportionately large vocabulary change. This is consistent with AI-driven role restructuring hitting junior roles harder.

## 2. Emerging and Disappearing Terms

Emerging: >1% document frequency in 2026, <0.1% in 2024. Disappearing: reverse. Document frequency = % of postings containing the term at least once.

**Note on artifacts:** Many "disappearing" terms are HTML concatenation artifacts from arshkon source formatting (e.g., "skillsa", "positionyou", "chatbotwrite"). These reflect formatting differences between sources, not real vocabulary changes. True substantive disappearances (e.g., "waterfall", "sustainment") reflect real methodology shifts.

## Implication for analysis

- **RQ1 (restructuring):** The JSD values and emerging-term analysis confirm that SWE job postings underwent substantial linguistic change from 2024 to 2026. This is not a stable baseline -- the language of hiring has shifted meaningfully.
- **RQ1 (scope inflation):** YOE data for entry-level roles provides direct evidence for or against credential inflation at the junior level.
- **RQ2 (task migration):** AI term prevalence by seniority shows whether AI requirements are being added uniformly or concentrated at particular seniority levels. Differential adoption rates across levels suggest task migration between seniority tiers.
- **RQ3 (divergence):** The broad vs. narrow AI prevalence split helps distinguish genuine technical AI requirements from generic buzzword insertion. The narrow (LLM-specific) measure is more likely to reflect actual tool requirements.

## Data quality note

- JSD computed on arshkon (2024) vs scraped (2026) only. Asaniczka excluded from JSD because it lacks entry-level labels, making seniority-stratified comparisons non-parallel.
- Description length grew ~50% from 2024 to 2026 (T05 finding). JSD uses normalized frequencies (rates, not counts), providing some but not complete robustness to length differences.
- The within-2024 reference JSD (arshkon vs asaniczka mid-senior) controls for cross-source variation.
- YOE extraction is regex-based and may miss non-standard phrasings. Coverage varies by source.
- AI keyword matching uses regex patterns, which may overcount (e.g., 'AI' in company names) or undercount (novel terminology). The narrow LLM-specific pattern is more conservative.
- Seniority uses the patched workaround (seniority_patched). Entry-level: arshkon n=745, scraped n=549.
