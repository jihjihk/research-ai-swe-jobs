# T13: Company-Level Patterns

## Finding

Of the 2,105 unique arshkon SWE companies and 1,563 scraped SWE companies, **379 appear in both periods** (18% of arshkon, 24% of scraped by company count; 36% and 40% by posting volume). The within-company vs between-company decomposition reveals that the **entry-level share decline is driven by within-company changes** (within-company delta: -9.3pp, partially offset by +5.6pp composition effect), while the **AI keyword surge is almost entirely within-company** (within: +33.0pp vs composition: +1.5pp). Description length growth is also primarily within-company (1.43x for overlap companies vs 1.64x overall), with new-to-2026 companies contributing shorter descriptions on average.

## Implication for analysis

**RQ1 (restructuring):** The entry-level share dropped from 20.6% to 11.3% within the same 379 companies -- a -9.3pp within-company decline. This is stronger than the overall -3.8pp decline because new 2026 companies (scraped-only) have a somewhat higher entry share (14.2%) than the 2026 overlap cohort (11.3%), partially masking the within-company shift. This is robust evidence that the entry-level reduction is not an artifact of sampling different companies; it is happening within continuing employers.

**RQ2 (AI adoption):** AI keyword prevalence jumped from 18.2% to 51.2% within overlapping companies (+33.0pp). The composition effect is near zero (+1.5pp), meaning new companies entering the 2026 sample do not substantially differ from continuing ones in AI adoption. This supports interpreting the AI keyword surge as a genuine field-wide shift, not a compositional artifact.

**Size-band pattern:** Large companies (10K+ employees) show the steepest entry-level decline (21% to 8%, -13pp) and the largest AI keyword jump (22% to 58%, +36pp). Small companies (<100) show less entry-level change but equally dramatic AI adoption (9% to 57%). This suggests large-firm restructuring is the primary driver of the entry-level squeeze.

**RQ3 context:** Firms like Deloitte, Oracle, and PwC went from 0% to 100% AI keyword rates. These are not AI-native companies -- they represent traditional enterprises adding AI language to every SWE posting. This may represent employer signaling rather than genuine task transformation, which is exactly the divergence RQ3 investigates.

## Data quality note

1. **Overlap rate:** 379 companies overlap out of 2,105 (arshkon) and 1,563 (scraped). The 18-24% overlap rate is expected given the two-year gap and different sampling frames (arshkon = Kaggle snapshot, scraped = active daily scrape). The overlap set is biased toward larger/more prominent companies.
2. **Aggregator contamination:** Aggregators like Apex Systems, TEKsystems, and Actalent appear in the top-20 overlap list. Their "entry-level" classifications are questionable (TEKsystems: 89% entry in arshkon vs 15% in scraped). Sensitivity analysis should exclude `is_aggregator = true` rows.
3. **Size-band data limited to arshkon:** Only arshkon has `company_size` from companion data. The size-band analysis assigns sizes based on arshkon data and applies them to the corresponding scraped postings from the same company. Companies that grew or shrank between 2024 and 2026 may be misclassified.
4. **Scraped-only companies include "Jobs via Dice" (130 rows):** This is likely an aggregator that escaped detection. The INDEX.md Gate 1 notes already flagged this. "Turing" (53 rows) is a remote staffing platform and should also be considered an aggregator.
5. **DataAnnotation** is arshkon's largest arshkon-only company (163 rows) and was correctly flagged as an aggregator. Its absence from scraped is likely due to it not appearing in 2026 LinkedIn search results rather than the company ceasing to exist.

## Action items

1. **For analysis:** Use the within-company panel (379 overlapping companies) as the primary identification strategy for RQ1. The -9.3pp within-company entry decline is more credible than the overall -3.8pp because it controls for company composition.
2. **Aggregator sensitivity:** Re-run the decomposition excluding `is_aggregator = true` rows. Several top overlap companies (Apex Systems, TEKsystems, Tata Consultancy) are staffing firms with erratic seniority labels.
3. **Company fixed effects:** The 71 companies with 3+ postings in both periods provide a micro-panel suitable for company fixed-effects analysis. This is a stronger identification strategy than cross-sectional comparison.
4. **Scraped-only flag "Jobs via Dice":** Add to aggregator list in next pipeline run, or exclude in sensitivity analysis.
