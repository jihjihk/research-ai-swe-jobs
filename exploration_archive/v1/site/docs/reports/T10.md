# T10: Fightin' Words Corpus Comparison

## Finding

Fightin' Words analysis across 6 corpus pairs reveals three key patterns. (1) AI/ML terminology is the single strongest temporal distinguisher: "ai" is the #1 term in all temporal comparisons (z=-26.8 for junior, z=-43.8 for senior), with 14 AI-related terms significantly distinguishing 2026 junior postings from 2024 and 19 for senior. Specific tooling terms (llm, agents, rag, copilot, langchain) dominate 2026 postings. (2) Ownership/autonomy language shifted toward 2026 (ownership z=-11.9 for senior, autonomous z=-8.8), while traditional management language ("management" z=+14.7) favors 2024. (3) Comparison 5 (Junior 2026 vs Senior 2024) shows 2026 entry-level postings are more AI-heavy than 2024 senior postings (ai z=+38.8), but still trail in experience/years/management language, partially supporting the scope-inflation hypothesis -- junior roles absorbed new AI requirements but not traditional senior scope. C6 confirms AI terms are SWE-specific, not labor-market-wide (ai z=+65.5 SWE vs control).

- RQ1: The Fightin' Words analysis provides direct evidence of how job posting language has shifted between 2024 and 2026. AI/ML terms emerging as strong distinguishers in temporal comparisons (C1, C2) supports the hypothesis of AI-driven role restructuring.

## Data quality note

- All corpora use `description` (full text, not `description_core`) to avoid boilerplate-removal artifacts.
- Seniority uses the patched workaround (seniority_patched) to incorporate native backfill labels.
- Minimum frequency thresholds applied: 5 total occurrences for unigrams, 3 for bigrams.
- Description length grew ~50% from 2024 to 2026 (per T05). Fightin' Words uses log-odds ratios which are somewhat robust to document length differences, but very long 2026 descriptions may contain more diverse vocabulary.
- All comparisons have n >= 50 in both corpora.
