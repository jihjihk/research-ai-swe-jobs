# T09: Posting Archetype Discovery -- Methods Laboratory

Generated: 2026-04-05

## Executive Summary

Unsupervised clustering of 8,002 SWE LinkedIn job postings reveals that the market's dominant
structure is organized by **technical specialization**, not by seniority level.
BERTopic identified 92 fine-grained topics (reduced to 14 interpretable archetypes),
while NMF produced consistent macro-level groupings. Key findings:

1. **Technology domain drives posting structure.** Clusters align most strongly with tech domain
   (NMI=0.175), followed by period (NMI=0.057), while seniority shows the weakest alignment
   (NMI=0.018). A senior data engineer posting is more similar to a junior data engineer posting
   than to a senior frontend posting. Seniority operates as a *within-domain* modifier, not a
   cross-cutting structural dimension.

2. **Four macro-archetypes dominate the market.** Frontend/Web (28%), Embedded/Systems (21%),
   Data/Analytics (19%), and ML/AI Engineering (13%) account for 81% of non-noise postings.
   The remaining 10 micro-clusters represent company-specific, aggregator, or niche patterns.

3. **ML/AI Engineering underwent massive growth.** It rose from 4% of SWE postings in 2024 to
   27% in 2026 (+22pp). Frontend/Web shrank from 41% to 24% (-17pp). This is the largest
   temporal shift in the data.

4. **Both methods agree on core structure.** 7 of 14 BERTopic archetypes have clear NMF
   counterparts (Jaccard > 0.1). Clustering is highly stable (ARI = 1.00 for HDBSCAN, 0.996 for NMF).

## 7. Key Discovery: What is the Market's Dominant Structure?

### Cluster alignment with external dimensions

|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **tech_domain** | **0.1752** | **0.1752** |
| 2 | period | 0.0570 | 0.0570 |
| 3 | is_aggregator | 0.0475 | 0.0475 |
| 4 | swe_tier | 0.0315 | 0.0315 |
| 5 | seniority_3level | 0.0180 | 0.0180 |

### Interpretation

The 14-archetype clustering reveals a clear hierarchy of what structures the SWE labor market:

1. **Technology domain is the strongest signal** (NMI=0.175). Even using a crude 5-category
   tech domain classification (AI, cloud, data, mobile, security, general), tech domain
   explains the clusters far better than any other dimension. The BERTopic archetypes themselves
   are tech-domain clusters: data engineering, embedded/systems, frontend/web, ML/AI, mobile.
   This is the central structural finding.

2. **Period ranks second** (NMI=0.057). Posting language changed substantially between 2024
   and 2026. The ML/AI Engineering archetype grew from 4% to 27%, while Frontend/Web shrank
   from 41% to 24%. Some of this reflects genuine market shifts; some may reflect source-specific
   text patterns.

3. **Aggregator status ranks third** (NMI=0.047). Several small archetypes are aggregator-dominated
   (Aggregator/AI: 100%, Streaming/Distributed: 73%). This is mostly a data quality signal --
   aggregator boilerplate creates distinctive text patterns that form their own clusters.

4. **Seniority is the weakest signal** (NMI=0.018). This is the most important negative finding:
   **job postings do NOT cluster by seniority level.** Every major archetype spans all seniority
   levels with roughly similar proportions (78-84% senior, 8-10% junior), reflecting the population
   seniority distribution rather than content differentiation. Seniority operates as a
   within-domain modifier, not a cross-cutting structural dimension.

This pattern is consistent across granularity levels:
- Fine-grained (92 topics): tech_domain NMI=0.152, period=0.073, seniority=0.029
- Reduced (14 archetypes): tech_domain NMI=0.175, period=0.057, seniority=0.018

### Implications for Research Questions

- **RQ1 (employer-side restructuring):** Seniority effects should be analyzed *within* technical
  domain archetypes. A pooled junior-vs-senior comparison masks domain-specific patterns.
  Junior data engineering roles may be affected very differently from junior frontend roles.
  The massive growth of ML/AI Engineering archetype (+22pp) is itself a restructuring signal.

- **RQ2 (task migration):** When studying which requirements migrate between seniority levels,
  the analysis should be stratified by archetype. A requirement that moves from senior to junior
  in one domain may not move at all in another.

- **Temporal dynamics are substantial.** ML/AI Engineering went from 4% to 27% of SWE postings.
  Frontend/Web shrank from 41% to 24%. Embedded/Systems shrank from 26% to 19%. These are not
  incremental shifts -- they represent a large-scale recomposition of what "SWE" means in the
  labor market between 2024 and 2026.
