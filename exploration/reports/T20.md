# T20: Seniority Boundary Clarity

**Task:** Measure how sharp the boundaries between seniority levels are and whether they blurred between 2024 and 2026.

**Sample:** SWE, LinkedIn-only, seniority_final != unknown. 2024: arshkon + asaniczka combined (n=27,279). 2026: scraped (n=22,469). Total: 49,748 rows.

---

## Headline Finding

**Seniority boundaries are ASYMMETRICALLY EVOLVING.** Lower boundaries (entry/associate, associate/mid-senior) SHARPENED from 2024 to 2026, while the top boundary (mid-senior/director) BLURRED dramatically. The features that separate levels also shifted: in 2024, YOE and tech count dominated separation; in 2026, scope density, management language, and AI mentions became the primary discriminators at lower boundaries. Meanwhile, associate roles moved CLOSER to entry in feature space -- not disappearing, but collapsing downward.

---

## Boundary Discriminability (AUC)

| Boundary | AUC 2024 | AUC 2026 | Change | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Entry vs Associate | 0.561 | 0.592 | +0.031 | SHARPENED |
| Associate vs Mid-Senior | 0.675 | 0.703 | +0.028 | SHARPENED |
| Mid-Senior vs Director | 0.750 | 0.639 | -0.111 | BLURRED |

Method: L2 logistic regression, 5-fold stratified CV, 7 features (YOE, tech count, AI mention, scope density, management density, description length log, education ordinal).

### Interpretation

1. **Entry/Associate boundary sharpened slightly** (0.561 to 0.592). This is still a very low AUC -- barely above random (0.5) -- meaning entry and associate roles remain nearly indistinguishable by structured features. The small improvement suggests 2026 entry-level postings have become marginally MORE distinctive from associate.

2. **Associate/Mid-Senior boundary sharpened** (0.675 to 0.703). The moderate discriminability reflects a genuine step function in requirements at this boundary, and it became slightly more pronounced.

3. **Mid-Senior/Director boundary BLURRED substantially** (-0.111 AUC). This is the most striking finding. In 2024, director roles were clearly distinguishable from mid-senior (AUC=0.75) by their higher YOE, longer descriptions, more scope language, and more management language. In 2026, this distinction weakened considerably (AUC=0.639). This suggests mid-senior roles absorbed characteristics previously distinctive to directors -- OR that director postings became less distinctive in their language.

## What Drives Separation? Feature Importance Shifts

### Entry vs Associate

| Rank | 2024 | 2026 |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Tech Count | **Scope Density** |
| 2 | Description Length | **Management Density** |
| 3 | Management Density | **AI Mention** |
| 4 | AI Mention | Tech Count |
| 5 | Education | Description Length |

In 2024, the entry/associate boundary was primarily about technical breadth (tech count, description length). In 2026, scope language, management expectations, and AI mentions became the top discriminators. This aligns with T11's finding that entry-level roles absorbed scope/management language disproportionately.

### Associate vs Mid-Senior

| Rank | 2024 | 2026 |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **YOE** | **YOE** |
| 2 | Tech Count | Description Length |
| 3 | Scope Density | AI Mention |
| 4 | Education | Education |
| 5 | Management Density | Tech Count |

YOE remains the strongest separator at this boundary in both periods. However, AI mention rose significantly as a discriminator in 2026, consistent with mid-senior roles absorbing more AI requirements than associate roles.

### Mid-Senior vs Director

| Rank | 2024 | 2026 |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **YOE** | Tech Count |
| 2 | Description Length | Scope Density |
| 3 | Scope Density | Description Length |
| 4 | Management Density | AI Mention |
| 5 | Education | YOE |

The most dramatic shift. In 2024, YOE was the primary director discriminator; in 2026, it dropped to rank 5. Tech count and scope density rose to the top. This suggests the "director" designation in 2026 is less about accumulated experience and more about technical breadth and organizational scope -- but these features are increasingly shared with mid-senior roles, explaining the blurring.

## The "Missing Middle": Associate Role Positioning

Associate roles are collapsing TOWARD entry, not mid-senior.

| Metric | Period | Associate-to-Entry Distance | Associate-to-MidSenior Distance | Relative Position (0=entry, 1=midsenior) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Feature space (Euclidean) | 2024 | 0.913 | 2.153 | 0.298 |
| Feature space (Euclidean) | 2026 | 0.335 | 1.822 | 0.155 |

In 2024, associate sat at position 0.30 between entry and mid-senior (closer to entry). In 2026, it moved to 0.16 -- significantly closer to entry. The associate-to-entry distance collapsed from 0.913 to 0.335, while the associate-to-mid-senior distance only decreased from 2.153 to 1.822.

### Feature-by-feature comparison

| Feature | Entry 2024 | Assoc 2024 | Entry 2026 | Assoc 2026 | Entry-Assoc gap 2024 | Entry-Assoc gap 2026 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| YOE (imputed) | 4.39 | 4.39 | 4.07 | 4.09 | 0.00 | 0.02 |
| Tech Count | 5.43 | 4.53 | 6.91 | 7.17 | -0.90 | 0.26 |
| AI Mention | 2.9% | 2.5% | 19.9% | 17.7% | -0.4pp | -2.2pp |
| Scope Density | 0.37 | 0.39 | 0.32 | 0.45 | 0.02 | 0.13 |
| Mgmt Density | 0.94 | 1.05 | 0.90 | 1.05 | 0.11 | 0.15 |
| Desc Length (log) | 7.25 | 7.32 | 7.86 | 7.84 | 0.07 | -0.02 |
| Education | 0.77 | 0.81 | 0.82 | 0.80 | 0.04 | -0.02 |

**The critical observation:** In 2024, entry and associate had near-identical YOE, similar AI rates, and the gap was mostly in tech count (associate had LESS tech breadth than entry -- counterintuitive but reflects asaniczka associate labeling). In 2026, both levels converged further: nearly identical YOE, description length, education, and AI rates. The remaining gap is in scope density and management density, where associate maintains a slight premium.

**Interpretation:** Associate is not "disappearing" in the sense of being eliminated, but its feature profile is becoming a near-duplicate of entry. If entry-level roles absorbed scope/management/AI requirements that previously distinguished associate, then the associate category loses its structural meaning. This is consistent with a "flattening" of the bottom two tiers.

## Sensitivity Checks

### (a) Aggregator Exclusion

| Boundary | Period | AUC (all) | AUC (no aggregator) | Diff |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Entry vs Associate | 2024 | 0.561 | 0.556 | -0.005 |
| Entry vs Associate | 2026 | 0.592 | 0.600 | +0.008 |
| Associate vs Mid-Senior | 2024 | 0.675 | 0.661 | -0.014 |
| Associate vs Mid-Senior | 2026 | 0.703 | 0.700 | -0.003 |
| Mid-Senior vs Director | 2024 | 0.750 | 0.689 | -0.061 |
| Mid-Senior vs Director | 2026 | 0.639 | 0.641 | +0.002 |

The blurring/sharpening pattern is robust to aggregator exclusion. One notable detail: the mid-senior/director boundary in 2024 drops from 0.750 to 0.689 without aggregators, suggesting aggregators contributed some spurious director discriminability in 2024. The 2026 director blurring is essentially unchanged.

### (c) Seniority Operationalization (3-level)

| Boundary (3-level) | AUC 2024 | AUC 2026 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Junior vs Mid | 0.561 | 0.592 | +0.031 |
| Mid vs Senior | 0.675 | 0.703 | +0.028 |

Using 3-level seniority (junior/mid/senior) produces identical AUCs for the lower boundaries (because the 3-level mapping is entry+associate -> junior/mid, mid-senior+director -> senior). This confirms the findings are not an artifact of 5-level classification granularity.

## Implications

1. **The seniority system is restructuring from the top down.** Director roles are becoming less distinguishable from mid-senior roles, while the entry/associate boundary sharpened slightly. This is consistent with a model where AI tools flatten the top of the hierarchy (orchestration and scope language diffusing to mid-senior) while scope inflation at the bottom makes entry roles more distinctive from a "new normal" associate.

2. **Associate is collapsing toward entry.** The feature-space distance between associate and entry halved from 2024 to 2026, while the gap to mid-senior barely changed. This suggests a de facto 3-tier system: entry/associate (junior), mid-senior (fully independent), director (barely distinct from mid-senior). The traditional 5-level hierarchy is compressing to ~3 meaningful levels.

3. **The features that define boundaries changed.** YOE and tech count mattered most in 2024. In 2026, scope density, management language, and AI mentions became the primary boundary markers. This means seniority is increasingly defined by WHAT you do (orchestrate, manage, scope) rather than HOW LONG you have done it.

4. **Director YOE expectations fell substantially** (7.72 to 5.78 mean). Combined with the boundary blurring, this suggests a redefinition of director away from "senior person with long tenure" toward "technically broad person with scope."
