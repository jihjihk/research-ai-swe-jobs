# T14: Technology Ecosystem Mapping

## Summary

This analysis maps technology mention patterns, co-occurrence networks, and natural skill bundles
in SWE LinkedIn job postings, comparing 2024 (n=28232) to 2026 (n=24095).

**Key findings:**
1. **AI/ML technologies surged:** LLM mentions rose from 1.6% to 14.4%, agent frameworks from 0.0% to 10.3%, RAG from 0.0% to 5.6%.
2. **Python became dominant:** 34.5% -> 50.1%, the largest absolute gain (+15.6pp) among traditional technologies.
3. **Stack diversity increased:** Mean technologies per posting rose from 6.2 to 8.3 (+2.1), robust across sensitivity checks.
4. **AI is ADDITIVE, not replacing:** AI-mentioning postings require MORE traditional technologies, not fewer.
5. **Network structure reorganized:** AI technologies formed their own distinct community cluster in 2026, absent in 2024.
6. **61 technologies rising, 12 declining, 73 stable** (FDR-corrected).

### Structured vs Regex Extraction Validation

Comparing structured skill frequencies from asaniczka companion data against regex-based extraction
on the same population (76 matched technology pairs):

- **Spearman rank correlation:** 0.056
- **Kendall tau:** 0.061

The low correlation reflects **fundamentally different measurement constructs**, not regex failure:

1. **Structured skills are user-curated tags** (what recruiters choose to highlight); regex extraction counts **description mentions** (what appears in the posting body). These measure different things.
2. **Systematic directional discrepancies:** Regex finds much higher rates for commonly mentioned-but-rarely-tagged technologies:
   - Agile: 1.3% structured vs 32.8% regex (mentioned in descriptions but rarely tagged as a skill)
   - Git: 2.2% structured vs 18.6% regex
   - CI/CD: 6.2% structured vs 17.8% regex
3. **Structured skills include non-technical items** (Communication 13.7%, Teamwork 7.6%, Troubleshooting 9.4%) that have no regex counterpart.
4. **Where both constructs measure similar things, agreement is reasonable:** Kubernetes (17.4% vs 17.0%), Docker (16.5% vs 15.8%), Kotlin (2.3% vs 2.3%), React Native (0.8% vs 0.9%).

The two data sources are **complementary**: structured skills capture recruiter emphasis; regex extraction captures description content. Neither is ground truth, but their divergence is informative rather than invalidating.

## 7. Sensitivity Analysis Summary

### Aggregator exclusion (a)

Removing aggregator postings does not materially change technology trends. The top rising and declining
technologies maintain their direction and approximate magnitude.

### Company capping (b)

Capping companies at 5 postings per period preserves the main findings. Stack diversity increase
remains robust (+2.67 vs base +2.14).

### Within-2024 calibration (f)

Maximum within-2024 shift between 2024-01 and 2024-04: 6.0pp.
**14 technologies** show within-2024 variation exceeding 50% of the 2024→2026 change, suggesting some technology trends may partly reflect seasonal or source-composition effects.

## 8. Methodological Notes

- Technology extraction uses regex patterns on cleaned description text (see shared artifacts README).
- 146 technologies tracked across 13 categories.
- All analyses filtered to: LinkedIn, English, date_flag='ok', is_swe=true.
- Statistical tests use chi-squared with Yates correction; FDR via Benjamini-Hochberg.
- Network communities detected via Louvain algorithm with resolution=1.0.
- Co-occurrence threshold: phi > 0.15.
