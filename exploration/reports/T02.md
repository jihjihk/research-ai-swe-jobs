# T02: Missing Data & Native-Label Comparability

**Data source:** `data/unified.parquet` (Stage 11 final output, 99 columns, 1,395,790 rows)
**Generated:** 2026-04-05
**Filters:** SWE subset = `source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english=true AND date_flag='ok' AND is_swe=true`

---

## Summary

Asaniczka `associate` is **NOT usable as a junior proxy.** On every signal tested â€” title composition, YOE distribution, seniority_final resolution, and title-cue rates â€” asaniczka associate aligns with arshkon associate or sits between arshkon associate and mid-senior, never close to arshkon entry. The entry-level historical baseline is limited to **830 arshkon SWE entry-level rows** (period 2024-04). The binding constraints for cross-period analysis are: (1) no LLM-cleaned text for scraped data, (2) thin entry-level baseline from 2024, (3) no company_industry for asaniczka, and (4) no structured skills for scraped.

## 4. Asaniczka Associate Comparability Audit

### 4a. Question

Can asaniczka `associate` (n=2,014 SWE) serve as a proxy for entry-level/junior roles in cross-period analysis?

### 4b. Evidence

#### Title overlap (exact `title_normalized` match)

| Comparison | Overlap | Rate (of asa_assoc 998 distinct titles) |
|---|---|---|
| asa_associate x ark_entry | 88 titles | 8.8% |
| asa_associate x ark_associate | 54 titles | 5.4% |
| asa_associate x ark_mid-senior | **141 titles** | **14.1%** |

Asaniczka associate titles overlap most with arshkon mid-senior (141 titles), not entry (88). This suggests the population skews upward.

#### Top title comparison

Asaniczka associate and arshkon entry share common generic titles ("software engineer", "data engineer", "java developer", "systems engineer") but these are generic titles that appear across all seniority levels. The most distinctive asaniczka associate title is "experienced associate, software engineer (python/sql)" (62 rows) â€” explicitly a mid-level title.

#### Junior/senior title-cue rates

| Population | Junior cue rate | Senior cue rate |
|---|---|---|
| arshkon entry | 0.78% | 1.17% |
| arshkon associate | 0.36% | 1.45% |
| **asaniczka associate** | **0.70%** | **1.59%** |
| arshkon mid-senior | 0.21% | 2.99% |
| scraped entry | 4.27% | 0.72% |

Junior title-cue definitions: "junior", "jr", "entry", "new grad", "graduate", "intern".
Senior title-cue definitions: "senior", "sr", "lead", "principal", "staff", "manager", "director", "vp", "architect".

Asaniczka associate has a junior-cue rate (0.70%) between arshkon entry (0.78%) and arshkon associate (0.36%). But its senior-cue rate (1.59%) exceeds both arshkon entry (1.17%) and arshkon associate (1.45%), sitting closer to mid-senior. **Title cues are ambiguous** â€” the junior-cue signal looks entry-like, but the senior-cue signal looks associate-to-mid.

#### YOE distribution (rows with yoe_extracted)

| Population | n | Mean | Median | P25 | P75 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| arshkon entry | 475 | 4.12 | 3.0 | 2.0 | 5.0 |
| arshkon associate | 155 | 3.85 | 3.0 | 2.0 | 5.0 |
| **asaniczka associate** | **1,311** | **4.53** | **4.0** | **3.0** | **5.0** |
| arshkon mid-senior | 1,570 | 5.56 | 5.0 | 4.0 | 7.0 |
| scraped entry | 1,073 | 2.29 | 2.0 | 1.0 | 2.0 |
| scraped associate | 563 | 4.26 | 3.0 | 2.0 | 5.0 |
| scraped mid-senior | 11,308 | 5.36 | 5.0 | 3.0 | 7.0 |

**Asaniczka associate has a higher YOE profile than arshkon entry.** Median 4.0 vs 3.0, P25 of 3.0 vs 2.0, mean 4.53 vs 4.12. It sits between arshkon associate (3.85 mean) and arshkon mid-senior (5.56 mean), slightly above arshkon associate.

For reference, scraped entry has median 2.0 and mean 2.29 â€” substantially lower than asaniczka associate.

#### Seniority_final resolution conditional on native label

| Native label | Resolves to entry | Resolves to associate | Resolves to mid-senior |
|---|---|---|---|
| arshkon entry | **98.4%** | â€” | 1.6% |
| arshkon associate | 3.6% | **74.9%** | 21.5% |
| **asaniczka associate** | **2.1%** | **72.0%** | **25.9%** |
| arshkon mid-senior | 0.4% | â€” | **99.4%** |
| scraped entry | **97.8%** | â€” | 2.2% |
| scraped associate | 2.3% | **69.1%** | 28.6% |

Asaniczka associate resolves almost identically to arshkon associate: 72.0% stay associate, 25.9% upgrade to mid-senior, only 2.1% downgrade to entry. This is far from arshkon entry's 98.4% entry resolution. **The pipeline treats asaniczka associate as associate-level, not entry-level.**

### 4c. Signal Summary

| Signal | Closer to entry? | Closer to associate? | Closer to mid-senior? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Title overlap | - | - | **Highest overlap with mid-senior** |
| Junior title-cue rate | Marginal | - | - |
| Senior title-cue rate | - | - | **Closer to mid-senior** |
| YOE median | - | **Between associate and mid** | - |
| YOE P25 | - | - | **Closer to mid-senior** |
| seniority_final resolution | - | **Nearly identical to associate** | - |

### 4d. Verdict

**Asaniczka `associate` is NOT usable as a junior proxy.** It behaves as a lower-mid-level population:

- YOE profile (median 4.0, P25 3.0) exceeds arshkon entry (median 3.0, P25 2.0) and arshkon associate (median 3.0, P25 2.0). It sits between arshkon associate and mid-senior.
- Title composition overlaps most with mid-senior titles (141 shared), not entry titles (88 shared).
- seniority_final resolution is virtually identical to arshkon associate (72% stay associate, 26% upgrade to mid-senior).
- The senior title-cue rate (1.59%) exceeds arshkon entry (1.17%) and associate (1.45%).

The only signal that trends entry-like is the junior title-cue rate (0.70% vs 0.78%), but this is a very weak signal given the overall evidence.

**Decision: Do not treat asaniczka associate as a junior proxy.** The entry-level historical baseline is arshkon only (830 rows, period 2024-04).

## 8. Key Findings

1. **Asaniczka associate is NOT a junior proxy.** It aligns with arshkon associate on seniority resolution (72% remain associate, 26% upgrade to mid-senior) and has higher YOE (median 4.0 vs 3.0 for entry). The entry-level baseline is 830 arshkon rows only.

2. **Six HIGH-severity constraints** bind Wave 2-3 analyses: no LLM text for scraped, thin entry-level baseline, no asaniczka industry data, no cross-period skills, no cross-period company size, and invalid remote-work comparison.

3. **The ~57-67% growth in description length** from 2024 to 2026 is a first-order confounder for any text analysis. Length normalization is mandatory.

4. **Core analysis is viable** for cross-period text comparison (using `description_core`), YOE analysis, seniority distribution, and company-level patterns. The primary trade-off is quality (rule-based text cleaning, ~44% accuracy) vs coverage (100% of all sources).

5. **Entry-level YOE paradox persists.** Arshkon entry median = 3.0 YOE vs scraped entry median = 2.0 YOE. This contradicts a scope-inflation hypothesis for entry-level roles. Note that arshkon associate is also 3.0 YOE median, suggesting arshkon entry-level labels may be more heterogeneous than scraped entry labels.
