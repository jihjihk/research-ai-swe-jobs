    Synthesis — what the exploration actually found¶

    Lead sentence¶

    Between 2024 and 2026, US LinkedIn software-engineering job postings added AI-tool vocabulary at a rate 99 percent specific to SWE versus control occupations (DiD), 102 percent attributable to the same 240 companies rewriting their own postings, while employers simultaneously under-specified AI by 15 to 30 percentage points relative to the most conservative developer-usage benchmark — a cross-seniority, cross-archetype, geographically uniform rewrite that coexists with a senior-disproportionate role-content shift toward mentoring and orchestration and sharpening rather than blurring of seniority boundaries.

    Three supporting claims¶

    1. Within-company scope broadening is real. On the 240-company overlap panel, length-residualized breadth rose +1.43 composite units (102% within; stable 94-147% across five sensitivities); 76% of companies broadened; 62% by more than 1.0 unit.
    2. Senior-role content shift is senior-specific, not corpus-wide templating. Mid-senior mentor-binary ratio 1.46-1.73× 2024 → 2026; entry 1.07×; associate fell to 0.61×. Emergent management + orchestration + strategic + AI sub-archetype (n=860, 97% 2026) and doubled staff-title share (2.6% → 6.3%) indicate a new senior role type.
    3. Employer posting-level AI requirements systematically under-specify worker AI usage. 2026 SWE broad-AI rate 46.8% below every plausible developer-usage assumption (50, 65, 75, 85%); under central 75% usage the gap is −28 pp for broad and −61 pp for specific tools; the pre-registered direction (anticipatory over-specification) is inverted.

    Eight confirmed hypotheses (with confidence)¶

    1. AI-vocabulary rewriting is SWE-specific (T18). 99% of the ai_strict rise is SWE-specific under DiD. Confidence: high.
    2. Within-company scope broadening (T16). +1.43 breadth residualized; 102% within-company. Confidence: high.
    3. Senior archetype shift is senior-specific (T21). Mid-senior mentor 1.46-1.73× vs entry 1.07×. Confidence: high (direction), moderate (magnitude — report as range).
    4. Seniority boundaries sharpened (T20). All three adjacent boundaries gained AUC; yoe-excluded panel +0.134. Confidence: high.
    5. AI rise is cross-archetype (T28). AI-broad positive in 22/22, ≥+10 pp in 18/22. Confidence: high.
    6. AI rise is geographically uniform (T17). 26/26 metros positive; CV 0.29. Confidence: high.
    7. New-entrant-driven AI/ML growth (T28). 81% of 2026 AI/ML volume from employers only-in-2026. Confidence: high.
    8. Requirements-section contracted SWE-specifically (T13 + T18). SWE req-share −10.7 pp, adjacent −10.9 pp, control +0.9 pp. Confidence: high.

    Seven contradicted hypotheses¶

    1. Junior scope inflation — breadth rose equally at junior and senior (+39% vs +34%).
    2. Junior-senior convergence — all boundaries sharpened; yoe-excluded panel +0.134 AUC.
    3. Anticipatory employer over-specification of AI — employers under-specify by 15-30 pp.
    4. Junior share rose unambiguously — baseline-contingent, SNR < 1, LLM-frame flip confirmed.
    5. Management language rose in senior postings (under broad pattern) — bare manage 14% precision; signal driven by non-managerial uses.
    6. AI postings are more aspirational than non-AI — LLM ghost risk-ratio 0.98; not elevated.
    7. Period ~180× seniority in embedding space — V1 re-derived as ~1.2× centroid-pairwise.

    Six new discoveries¶

    1. RQ3 inversion (T23). NOVEL, publishable on its own.
    2. Mgmt+orch+strat+AI sub-archetype (T21). 97% 2026, n=860. Names a new senior role.
    3. Seniority boundary SHARPENING (T20). Against intuition; all adjacent boundaries gained AUC.
    4. AI-rise cross-archetype + systems_engineering natural zero-AI control (T28).
    5. Geographic null (T17). r(ΔAI, ΔJ2) = −0.11. Rejects spatial AI-displacement story.
    6. T29 recruiter-LLM partial mediation (T29). 15-30% of rewrite is recruiter tooling.

    Paper positioning¶

    Hybrid dataset + substantive empirical labor paper. Three contributions stacked:

    1. A longitudinal SWE JD dataset harmonizing three sources (63,701 LinkedIn SWE rows + 155,745 control-occupation rows).
    2. A measurement framework (T30 panel, sensitivity dimensions, semantic-precision protocol, composite-score correlation, LLM-authorship mediation test) usable by future researchers.
    3. Three substantive findings ordered by evidence strength: SWE-specific AI rewriting, RQ3 inversion, senior-specific role shift.

    Lead venue: ICWSM dataset & methods track with a separate labor-economics follow-up. The RQ3 inversion is novel enough to anchor a standalone short paper or a dedicated section.
