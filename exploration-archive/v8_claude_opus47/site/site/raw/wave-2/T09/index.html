    T09 — Posting archetype discovery (methods laboratory)¶

    Agent: F (Wave 2) Run date: 2026-04-17 Sample: 8,000 SWE LinkedIn postings, text_source='llm' only, period-balanced. Primary junior slice: J2 (seniority_final ∈ {entry, associate}). Primary senior slice: S1 (seniority_final ∈ {mid-senior, director}).

    1. Headline (the single most important result)¶

    The SWE posting corpus clusters by TECH DOMAIN first, and period / seniority second. NMI between BERTopic clusters and a derived domain label is 0.26, 6.7× larger than NMI(cluster, period)=0.039 and 7.9× larger than NMI(cluster, seniority)=0.033. NMF on the same sample shows the same ordering: domain NMI 0.23, period 0.07, seniority 0.02. The domain- dominant ordering survives aggregator exclusion (domain NMI 0.26), swe-tier strict filtering (0.26), and raw-text sensitivity (0.22).

    Implication: the market organizes by tech domain, and the 2024→2026 changes are best understood as a composition shift and within-domain content shift, not as a period-defined macro regime change. This supports Gate 1 framing (1) ("Employers rewriting JDs toward AI skills is the real restructuring") and partial framing (3) ("domain composition explains apparent seniority shifts"), and weakens framing (2) ("pure platform/ boilerplate evolution") at the cluster level.

    However, the within-vs-between decomposition of the J2 entry share shows that within-archetype entry share rise is the larger driver (84.7% of the 2024→2026 sample-level J2 change) — so the paper cannot claim that aggregate junior-share shifts are pure composition. The composition story explains the AI-domain volume shift; it does not explain the fact that entry share rose WITHIN nearly every archetype. The first of these is domain restructuring; the second is platform/content drift. Both are real in this sample.

    4.3 Where the methods disagree¶

    • GenAI / LLM agents. NMF isolates a clean topic (topic 3: ai, generative, generative ai, workflows, ai systems, llms, llm, agent), while BERTopic merges GenAI with classical ML in topic 2 (ai, ml, data, engineering). NMF (Jaccard 0.20 vs BT topic 2) and the derived domain both suggest that GenAI is a distinct sub-archetype that the BERTopic run at mts=30 under-segments. Rerunning BERTopic at mts=20 splits a sub-topic that looks more clearly "LLM/RAG/agent" but adds noise elsewhere.

    • Government / security-clearance. NMF topics 14 and 18 isolate citizenship, clearance, u.s citizenship, secret clearance as their own archetype. BERTopic absorbs these into topic 3 (cloud_devops) and topic -1 (outliers). This is a real structural topic in gov-contractor postings that NMF captures more cleanly.

    • Cloud stack (AWS vs Azure). BERTopic creates three cloud-flavored clusters (3 cloud_devops, 11 aws_cloud_services, 16 azure_dotnet_web). NMF merges AWS usages across topics 4/5/15 and keeps Azure with .NET (topics 4, 8). Neither is wrong — they're different cuts of the same space. Any downstream domain analysis should treat cloud as one axis.

    • "Generic SWE" bucket is large and present in both methods. NMF topics 0, 7, 21 (software/systems/engineering with no strong flavor); BERTopic topic 0. These ~21-25% of postings look like mission statements without strong tech-domain content. This is real in JD corpora — many companies write generic "we need software engineers" copy.

    Interpretability verdict: NMF is slightly more interpretable at this sample size because its topics are better separated in TF-IDF space (the GenAI split and the gov-clearance split are both meaningful). However, BERTopic's c-TF-IDF terms are cleaner once the outlier bucket is reassigned (reduce_outliers). The two methods complement each other.

    4.4 Stability¶

    Raw per-pair ARI on three originally-planned seeds: 0.84, 0.01, 0.01 — one seed (1234) collapsed to 4 topics. Extended to 8 seeds: 5 produced 22-26 topics with pairwise ARI 0.62-0.87 (mean 0.74 reduced, 0.69 raw); 3 collapsed to 3-4 topics with outlier <1%. This is itself a finding: the UMAP (n_neighbors=15, n_components=5) manifold for this data has two basins — a fine-grained solution (~22 topics) and a near-degenerate solution (≤5 topics). We confirmed the fine-grained basin is the informative one (it produces recognizable domain clusters). Wave 3 agents using this artifact should be aware that re-running the BERTopic step without seed-retry logic has a ~38% probability of a collapsed run. The artifact we save uses the fine-grained basin.

    Reading: the within-archetype effect (entry share rising inside every meaningful archetype) is ~10× larger than the between-archetype (composition) effect. The ai_ml_engineering and recommendation_ecommerce archetypes DO contribute via between+interaction (the composition shift does move mass toward clusters with rising entry share), but that's a secondary story. generic_software_engineer alone contributes +4.87pp of the +19.5pp rise — largely from within-archetype entry share rising from 16% to 36%.

    Caveat: this decomposition is on our stratified sample, not the full corpus. Because our sample upweighted J2 and director strata by construction and because the stratum proportions are identical across periods, the within-archetype movement here reflects the underlying composition of which docs land in which archetype, NOT a pure unified- corpus count. A faithful corpus-level decomposition should redo this with unified-corpus period weights. The within >> between pattern qualitatively agrees with Gate 1's T06 finding that AI-mention within-company dominates — but the exact magnitudes are sample-specific.

    10. Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. UMAP has two basins of attraction for this data. 3/8 seeds collapsed to ≤5 topics with ~0% outliers; 5/8 produced 22-26 topics with 28-35% outliers. The collapse is not random noise — it's a structural feature of the (n_neighbors=15, n_components=5) UMAP on this embedding manifold. Downstream agents must retry on seed failure. This has implications for reproducibility of ANY BERTopic-based result on JD corpora of this scale and semantic homogeneity — the method is less deterministic than its success stories suggest.

    2. GenAI is an archetype NMF cleanly isolates but BERTopic merges into classical ML. Method choice matters for identifying the AI-specific vs ML-classical distinction that the paper cares about. This is the strongest method-disagreement finding, and it implies a Wave 3 follow-up: refit BERTopic with a seeded topic list (BERTopic(seed_topic_list=[["llm","rag","agent"], ["ml","model","training"]])) to force the split, or use NMF's topic 3 as the GenAI proxy.

    3. generic_software_engineer is the single largest driver of the sample J2 rise. Not AI/ML. The within-archetype entry share in generic-SWE jumped from 16% (2024) to 36% (2026) — a +20pp rise on the largest archetype. This is NOT what the AI-restructuring hypothesis predicts (H1 predicted the movement should be in AI-flavored archetypes). Generic-SWE is the 21% of postings that have no strong domain flavor — it's the "most interchangeable" job type. Possibly: 2026 entry-level SWE postings are more likely to have generic-boilerplate descriptions with heavy entry signaling (new grad, junior, intern) than domain-specific language. This is a content-drift hypothesis worth testing in Wave 3.

    4. systems_engineering fell from 14% of 2024-01 to 2.7% of 2026 and is the ONE archetype where AI-mention did NOT rise. A −6pp composition shift AND structural content immunity to AI. The combination suggests this archetype is being replaced, not rewritten. The top terms of this archetype (systems, engineering, systems engineering, design, systems engineer) match hardware/aerospace/defense roles; the 2026 scraped window may under-cover those roles. Wave 3 should check industry and geography distribution of this archetype.

    5. recommendation_ecommerce archetype is 94.7% J2 entry in 2026. The archetype grew from 0.1% to 2.3% of postings and is essentially a new-grad channel at large e-commerce/ads employers (the top terms are recommendation, ecommerce, algorithms, large-scale, search). This is Gate 1's tech-giant intern-pipeline story showing up as a distinct archetype.

    6. Within-archetype entry share rose in 18 of 22 archetypes — the J2 rise is broadly distributed across the domain taxonomy, not concentrated in AI/ML. This weakens the "AI jobs are the new entry lane" narrative. If the aggregate J2 rise is real (which Gate 1 already questioned via baseline-contingency), it's a corpus-wide shift, not a domain-specific one.

    7. NMF detected a clean gov-contractor archetype (citizenship, clearance, secret) that BERTopic absorbed into cloud_devops and outliers. This is a small but clean ~3% of postings that might matter for a geo/industry cross-cut analysis.

    8. The scraped 2026 internship_new_grad archetype (51 docs; 1.19% share) is detectable as its own topic. Entry+associate share is 100% by construction; the content is distinct from other archetypes. This is a useful holdout cluster for Wave 3's entry-pipeline analysis: archetype 20 tells us where LinkedIn's title-based "internship" signal is landing.

    9. Cloud/DevOps archetype AI-mention rose +19pp while its share fell −1.1pp. Both moves are small. This is the most "unchanged" archetype in the corpus: it's rewriting to mention AI tools (cursor, copilot in DevOps CI/CD contexts) but not changing in composition much. A candidate for the "tooling refresh, not paradigm shift" interpretation.

    11. Prevalence citation transparency¶

    Every number in §3-§6 comes from one of these frames:

    • Primary cluster labels: BERTopic min_topic_size=30, seed=20260417, with reduce_outliers(..., strategy="c-tf-idf") applied. The unreduced 27.9%-outlier version is shown only in §3 and §7 for method disclosure.
    • Sample frame: 8,000 LLM-cleaned SWE LinkedIn rows, text_source='llm' only, period-balanced per §2.
    • Archetype-level J2 entry share: denominator is known-seniority only (seniority_final != 'unknown') within the archetype × period cell. Numerator is entry+associate count. Cells with n_known < 10 are flagged as thin in §5.3.
    • Archetype-level AI-mention share: denominator is the full archetype × period cell count. Numerator is the broad-pattern regex hit count defined in T09_08_characterize.py. The same broad pattern used in Gate 1's calibration table.
    • NMI: sklearn's normalized_mutual_info_score (arithmetic average normalization) between cluster ID and each categorical axis. For the period axis, both 2-bucket (2024 pooled vs 2026) and 4-bucket (per period code) are reported; 2-bucket is the primary citation.
    • Derived domain: the 15-class regex taxonomy in T09_07_nmi.py::DOMAIN_PATTERNS (priority order: genai → ml_research → data_eng → devops → cloud → frontend → mobile → backend → .NET/Java → embedded → security → test → game → network). Domain-vs-cluster NMI would be slightly higher if we refined this taxonomy; we stayed coarse to keep it manifestly independent of the BERTopic clustering.

    13. Gaps / caveats for downstream readers¶

    • Sample is stratified, not random. Aggregate J2 shares in §6 do not match the corpus. Archetype-level WITHIN-cluster entry shares and the ORDERING of archetype-share deltas are robust, but absolute magnitudes require corpus-level reweighting before a paper-level claim.

    • GenAI is under-split in the primary BERTopic model. If Wave 3 needs a clean GenAI vs classical-ML cut, use NMF topic 3 labels, or refit BERTopic with a seeded topic list.

    • Raw-text scraped sensitivity covered only 2,000 rows. For full raw-text coverage (40,000+ scraped raw rows) a separate agent would need to compute a second embedding matrix; the shared artifact covers LLM rows only.

    • Cluster-to-domain NMI reflects our 15-class domain taxonomy. A finer domain taxonomy (e.g. including "ML platform engineer" as distinct from "data scientist") would raise both NMI values; the ORDERING (domain >> period >> seniority) would be unchanged.

    • systems_engineering contraction needs Wave 3 validation. The archetype falling from 14% of 2024-01 to 2.7% of 2026 may be a real content change OR a source/industry composition artifact. The archetype's raw 2024-01 (asaniczka) makeup looks different from arshkon; the 6pp drop 2024-01 → 2024-04 suggests a within-2024 source artifact, not a 2024→2026 trend.
