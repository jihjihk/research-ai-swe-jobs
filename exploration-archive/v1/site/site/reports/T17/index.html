    T17: Interview Elicitation Artifacts

    Finding

    Five interview elicitation artifacts were produced for RQ4 data-prompted interviews. Each artifact translates a quantitative finding from the exploration phase into a concrete, discussable object: (1) five real scope-inflated entry-level JDs from March 2026, (2) five same-company posting pairs showing 2024-vs-2026 evolution at Uber, Amazon, Deloitte, CACI, and Oracle, (3) an annotated junior-share decline chart with AI model release timeline, (4) a senior archetype shift chart contrasting management language with AI orchestration language, and (5) the employer-worker AI divergence chart from T14 showing the gap closing from ~40pp to ~0pp. All artifacts use real posting data and seniority_patched labels.

    Implication for analysis

    The artifacts ground RQ4's reflexive thematic analysis interviews in observed empirical patterns rather than abstract hypotheses. Key prompts enabled:

    • Artifact 1 (inflated JDs): Elicits interviewee reactions to real-world scope inflation -- entry-level postings demanding cross-functional collaboration, ownership, and AI proficiency simultaneously. Tests whether practitioners perceive these requirements as genuine or performative.
    • Artifact 2 (paired JDs): Enables within-company temporal comparison during interviews. The Deloitte pair (.NET Developer -> AI Data Engineer) and Amazon pair (SDE I 2024 -> SWE I 2026 with 3x longer descriptions) are particularly striking.
    • Artifact 3 (junior share + AI timeline): Provides temporal context connecting AI model releases to observed posting changes. Useful for probing causality vs. correlation in interviewees' minds.
    • Artifact 4 (senior archetype): Distinguishes two hypotheses about how senior roles are evolving -- "traditional management amplified" vs. "AI orchestration replacing management." The data shows both: management language increased AND AI language surged.
    • Artifact 5 (divergence): Tests whether interviewees experienced the rapid institutionalization of AI requirements, and whether they perceive employer requirements as reflecting genuine task changes or signaling behavior.

    Data quality note

    • Entry-level postings use seniority_patched, which reduces unknown rate but still depends on native platform labels (the Stage 5 backfill bug workaround). Artifact 1 postings are all genuinely labeled entry-level by LinkedIn's seniority field.
    • Paired JDs (Artifact 2) are not exact role replacements -- they are the best available same-company SWE postings from different periods. Title matching is approximate.
    • The junior-share trend chart (Artifact 3) connects only two data points (arshkon Apr 2024 and scraped Mar 2026). The intervening AI model timeline is suggestive but not causal evidence.
    • The senior archetype data (Artifact 4) combines prevalence rates from T12 (organizational language) and T11 (AI terms by seniority). Some rates were computed on slightly different subsamples (e.g., T12 combined 2024 sources for mid-senior).
    • The divergence chart (Artifact 5) uses StackOverflow Survey data as the worker-side benchmark, which measures self-reported behavior and may not be directly commensurable with posting-side keyword presence.

    Action items

    1. Artifacts are stored in exploration/artifacts/ with a README describing each.
    2. Interview protocol should present artifacts without leading interpretation and probe for mechanisms.
    3. The paired JDs (Artifact 2) should be refreshed if the pipeline is re-run with additional scraped data, as more overlap companies may emerge.
    4. After seniority_llm runs, verify that the 5 entry-level postings in Artifact 1 are still classified as entry-level.
    5. Consider adding a 6th artifact showing the cross-occupation contrast (T16: SWE junior down while control junior up) if the interview protocol needs a "macro context" prompt.
