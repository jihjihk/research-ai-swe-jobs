    T06: Company concentration

    Finding

    Company concentration is moderate and varies substantially across datasets, but no single employer dominates enough to bias overall findings. Asaniczka is the most concentrated (HHI=0.015, Gini=0.71, top-20 share=33%) due to Capital One (7.8%) and Recruiting from Scratch (7.5%) together accounting for 15% of SWE postings. Arshkon and scraped are less concentrated (HHI ~0.003-0.004, Gini 0.46-0.55, top-20 share 17-23%). The critical robustness result: capping at 10 postings per company barely moves seniority shares -- junior share shifts by at most 1 percentage point in any dataset, confirming that seniority findings are not driven by a few high-volume employers. Within-company seniority distributions for the 43 companies present in both arshkon and scraped are virtually identical (entry: 3.7% vs 3.7%; mid-senior: 93.9% vs 94.0%), suggesting no detectable within-company seniority shift between 2024 and 2026 among these employers.

    Implication for analysis

    RQ1 (junior share): The company-capped sensitivity test provides strong evidence that junior-share estimates are robust to employer concentration. The maximum shift from capping is +1.0pp for scraped (5.7% -> 6.7%) and +0.4pp for arshkon (4.4% -> 4.7%). This means any cross-period junior-share comparison is not an artifact of a few companies posting disproportionately at one seniority level.

    RQ1 (within-company trends): The 43 overlap companies show no within-company seniority shift. This null result is important: if the aggregate junior share changes across periods, it would be driven by compositional change (different companies entering/exiting the market) rather than within-company restructuring. However, the overlap sample (n=379 arshkon, n=519 scraped with known seniority) is small and dominated by mid-senior labels, so this null result has limited power to detect a real effect.

    RQ2/RQ3: The five dominant companies (Capital One, ClearanceJobs, DataAnnotation, Jobs via Dice, Recruiting from Scratch) are concentrated in specific datasets and should be examined individually in sensitivity analysis. Capital One alone is 7.8% of asaniczka SWE postings; any requirement pattern common across Capital One postings will appear as a population-level pattern in asaniczka.

    Data quality note

    1. Dominant companies differ by dataset. DataAnnotation tops arshkon (3.7%), Capital One + Recruiting from Scratch dominate asaniczka (15.3% combined), and Jobs via Dice tops scraped (3.1%). The fact that dominant employers are dataset-specific (and include aggregators like Dice and Recruiting from Scratch) means aggregator handling in Stage 2 is a consequential preprocessing choice.

    2. "Jobs via Dice" in scraped suggests residual aggregator leakage. This entity being the top SWE employer in scraped data (130 postings, 3.1%) indicates Stage 2 aggregator detection may not fully resolve Dice-aggregated postings. These should be examined to see if real_employer was extracted.

    3. Asaniczka concentration is anomalously high. Its Gini (0.71) is well above arshkon (0.46) and scraped (0.55). This may reflect asaniczka's scraping methodology (search-based collection that over-represents certain companies) rather than the actual labor market. Analysis should report asaniczka results with and without Capital One / Recruiting from Scratch.

    4. Overlap company sample is biased toward large, stable employers. The 43 companies appearing in both arshkon and scraped (with >=5 postings each) are survivorship-biased toward firms that were hiring in both 2024 and 2026. This is appropriate for within-company comparison but should not be generalized as representative of all SWE employers.

    Action items

    1. Include company-capped results as a standard robustness check in all seniority analyses. The shift is small (<1.5pp) but should be reported.
    2. Investigate "Jobs via Dice" aggregator residual. Check whether Stage 2 extracted real_employer for these 130 scraped postings. If not, consider adding Dice-specific extraction rules.
    3. Run asaniczka analyses with and without Capital One + Recruiting from Scratch (15.3% of SWE postings). Their removal tests whether requirement patterns are population-level or company-specific.
    4. Flag DataAnnotation in arshkon. DataAnnotation (3.7% of arshkon SWE) is a data-labeling company that may post systematically different SWE roles. Verify these are genuine SWE postings and not data-annotation tasks miscategorized.
    5. Within-company null result needs caveats. The identical seniority distributions (3.7% entry in both periods) come from just 43 companies with only 14 and 19 entry-level postings respectively. This is consistent with no change but lacks statistical power to rule out meaningful shifts.
