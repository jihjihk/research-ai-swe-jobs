    T15: Ghost Job & Anomaly Profiling

    Finding

    The rule-based ghost_job_risk flag is extremely conservative: only 354 of 1.16M LinkedIn postings (0.03%) score above "low," all of which are entry-level by construction. YOE-seniority contradictions affect 912 total rows (22 SWE), concentrated in entry-level postings requiring 3+ years experience. Description quality is excellent across all sources (>99.8% flagged "ok"), with length outliers (>15K or <100 chars) totaling <0.4% of any source. None of these anomalies threaten analytical validity, but ghost_job_risk will need the LLM-based ghost_assessment_llm to become analytically useful.

    Implication for analysis

    • Ghost job risk: The current rule-based flag (ghost_job_risk) is too narrow to serve as an analytical variable for RQ1/RQ3. It keys entirely off entry-level seniority_final + high YOE, so it is a special case of YOE-seniority contradiction rather than a broad ghost-job detector. The LLM-based ghost_assessment_llm (Stage 11) will be needed for meaningful ghost-job analysis.
    • YOE contradictions: The 22 SWE contradictions (entry-level postings requiring 3-18 years experience) are genuine scope-inflation signals relevant to RQ1 but too few to support quantitative claims. They serve as qualitative examples.
    • Description quality: Not a concern. Filtering to description_quality_flag = 'ok' loses <0.2% of rows from any source.

    Key results

    1. Ghost job risk profiling

    ghost_job_risk n Condition
    low 1,159,139 All non-entry or entry with YOE < 3
    medium 227 entry-level + YOE 3-4
    high 127 entry-level + YOE >= 5

    All 354 non-low rows have seniority_final = 'entry' by design (the flag only fires for entry-level postings). This is a definitional constraint, not an empirical finding.

    By source: arshkon has the most non-low rows (161, 44% of flagged rows, from only 10% of total data), suggesting arshkon entry-level postings disproportionately carry high YOE requirements. Asaniczka has 172 (48%), scraped has 21 (6%).

    By period: 2024-01: 172 (49%), 2024-04: 161 (45%), 2026-03: 21 (6%). The lower count in 2026-03 partly reflects fewer total entry-level rows in scraped data and partly the smaller dataset.

    Top companies among flagged rows: Medical Diagnostic Laboratories (26), Genesis Biotechnology Group (25), BCG Attorney Search (15), ClearanceJobs (10), Actalent (7). These are primarily staffing agencies and niche employers, not major tech companies. 15% of flagged rows are from aggregators.

    SWE specifically: Only 20 SWE postings are flagged (6 high, 14 medium), out of 30,283 SWE rows (0.07%). Ghost job risk is not a meaningful SWE-specific phenomenon in this dataset at current detection sensitivity.

    2. YOE-seniority contradictions

    912 total rows have yoe_seniority_contradiction = true (0.08% of all rows). 22 are SWE.

    SWE seniority_patched total SWE contradiction_n contradiction_pct
    entry 1,415 19 1.3%
    associate 1,577 1 0.1%
    mid-senior 26,007 2 0.0%

    The 19 entry-level SWE contradictions include genuine scope inflation examples: "Junior Software Developer" requiring 6 years experience (Amentum), "Entry Level Cloud Engineer" requiring 4 years (Dantech Corporation), and "Software Engineer 1 / Early Career" requiring 18 years (Nutanix -- likely a parsing error in the YOE extractor). Some are title-grade mismatches where the posting title says "Junior" but the description specifies experienced requirements.

    Contradiction rate is stable across periods: 0.1% for each source, suggesting no temporal trend in overt scope inflation detectable by the YOE heuristic.

    3. Description quality flags

    Source Total ok too_short empty ok %
    kaggle_arshkon 118,165 117,966 199 0 99.8%
    kaggle_asaniczka 1,014,752 1,014,554 198 0 100.0%
    scraped 26,576 26,575 1 0 100.0%

    Description quality is uniformly high. The too_short flag fires on description_core (post-boilerplate-removal), so some of the 398 too_short rows may have adequate raw descriptions that were over-stripped by the 44%-accuracy boilerplate remover.

    4. Description length outliers

    Source >15K chars <100 chars Total >15K % <100 % Median length
    arshkon 111 112 118,165 0.09% 0.09% 3,443
    asaniczka 3,103 91 1,014,752 0.31% 0.01% 3,470
    scraped 79 1 26,576 0.30% 0.00% 5,106

    Very long (>15K): 3,293 total. These are predominantly government/public-sector postings (County of Los Angeles, IRS, Hawaii DOE) with extensive compliance and benefits boilerplate. Zero are SWE except 11 (0.03% of SWE). Not a data quality concern; the boilerplate remover and description_core_llm are designed to handle these.

    Very short (<100): 204 total (5 SWE). Includes genuine junk ("CFAO", "On site", "TEST ROLE", "Thank You") concentrated in arshkon (112 of 204). These are effectively empty postings that somehow passed through the source. They are excluded by description_quality_flag != 'ok' if analysis needs clean text.

    Description length trend: Scraped data (2026) has median length 5,106 vs 3,443-3,470 for 2024 sources, consistent with the T05 finding of +50% description length growth. This affects keyword counting but not binary AI-mention flags.

    Data quality note

    • ghost_job_risk is definitionally limited to entry-level postings; it cannot detect ghost jobs at other seniority levels by design.
    • YOE extraction is regex-based and subject to parsing errors (e.g., the Nutanix 18-year entry-level case is likely a false positive).
    • The lack of temporal trend in contradiction rates (0.1% all periods) does not mean scope inflation is absent -- it means the YOE-heuristic approach lacks power to detect it. Subtler forms of scope inflation (requiring 10 technologies at entry level, or listing "5+ years" in a mid-senior posting) would not trigger the current flag.
    • Description quality flags key off description_core length, not description length, so the 44% boilerplate accuracy issue may inflate the too_short count slightly.

    Action items

    • [ ] Do not use ghost_job_risk as a primary analytical variable. Wait for ghost_assessment_llm (Stage 11) to enable meaningful ghost-job analysis.
    • [ ] The 22 SWE YOE contradictions can be used as qualitative examples of scope inflation in the RQ1 narrative but should not be cited as quantitative evidence.
    • [ ] For robustness, consider filtering out description_length < 100 rows (204 total, 5 SWE) in text-analysis tasks. This has negligible impact on sample size.
    • [ ] The very long postings (>15K chars) are concentrated in government/public sector. For keyword-rate analyses, consider whether to cap description length or normalize. Binary indicators (any mention) are unaffected.
