    T10: Title Taxonomy Evolution

    Task: Map how the SWE title landscape evolved between 2024 and 2026.

    Sample: SWE, LinkedIn-only. arshkon (n=5,019, April 2024) vs scraped (n=24,095, March 2026). asaniczka (n=23,213, Jan 2024) used for supplementary concentration analysis.

    Headline Findings

    1. AI title explosion: SWE postings with AI/ML terms in the title quadrupled from 3.9% (2024-04) to 16.1% (2026-03). Aggregator-excluded: 4.1% to 16.7%. This is not an aggregator artifact.

    2. "Staff" is the fastest-growing seniority marker: Staff-titled postings rose from 2.4% to 6.6% (+4.2pp), nearly tripling. This is the single largest seniority marker shift and suggests upward title migration as organizations restructure around more senior individual contributors.

    3. Title diversity is INCREASING, not consolidating: Unique titles per 1,000 postings went from 431 (arshkon) to 411 (scraped), roughly flat. But the fragmentation has shifted: top-10 title concentration decreased from 22.9% to 19.2%, meaning titles are becoming more specialized rather than consolidating.

    4. "Junior" marker in titles is declining, but weakly: Junior-titled postings fell from 0.78% to 0.66% (-0.12pp), not statistically meaningful. The real junior decline is happening via disappearing entry-level seniority labels, not via title changes.

    5. Titles still mean the same thing: Cosine similarity between 2024 and 2026 description centroids for the same title is high (median 0.95). Title-to-content alignment remains strong. Exception: "web developer" (0.77) and "back end developer" (0.80) show moderate content drift.

    Detailed Results

    1. New and Disappeared Titles

    New titles in 2026 (not in any 2024 source): 9,090 unique titles. Most are granular variations (team-specific suffixes, location modifiers). 258 had 5+ postings.

    The genuinely new high-frequency title patterns are: - AI-compound roles (1,168 postings across 751 new titles with 3+ postings): "AI data engineer", "genai python systems engineer", "AI native engineer", "agentic software engineer" - Granular team titles (many 20-30 posting titles): "software engineer, backend (merchant interfaces)", "software engineer, checkout (pba core)" -- companies posting more granular team-specific titles - "Agent" as a role category (24 postings): "agentic software engineer", "autonomous vehicle software engineer" -- very early emergence

    Disappeared titles (in arshkon with >=3, not in 2026): 65 titles. Mostly: - Legacy tech: "java architect" (24), "drupal developer" (9), "database developer" (14) - Company-specific: "software engineer, cash commerce" (9) - Renamed variants: "front-end engineer (remote)" likely became "frontend engineer"

    2. Title Concentration

    Metric 2024-01 2024-04 2026-03
    Unique titles per 1K postings 325.6 430.6 410.6
    Top-10 share 18.4% 22.9% 19.2%
    Top-50 share 36.4% 43.0% 29.3%
    HHI 0.0075 0.0122 0.0103

    Aggregator-excluded: arshkon 455 -> scraped 421 per 1K (mild decline). Top-10 share: 22.2% -> 18.9%.

    Interpretation: The title landscape is fragmenting slightly. Companies are using more specific, team-level titles rather than generic "Software Engineer" titles. This likely reflects organizational transparency or recruiting sophistication rather than fundamental role changes.

    3. AI/ML Compound Titles

    Period All postings Aggregator-excluded
    2024-01 3.6% (825) 2.4% (411)
    2024-04 3.9% (195) 4.1% (182)
    2026-03 16.1% (3,871) 16.7% (3,442)

    Within-2024 calibration: arshkon (4.1%) vs asaniczka (2.4%) shows ~1.5pp cross-source variation. The 2024-to-2026 jump of +12.6pp far exceeds this noise floor.

    Top emerging AI title categories: - "AI engineer" (227) -- standalone AI engineer role - "AI/ML engineer" (82) -- hybrid designation - "AI software engineer" (35) -- AI prefix on SWE - "Applied AI engineer" (29) -- applied emphasis - GenAI-specific titles: "genai python systems engineer" (28), "consultant - genai full stack developer" (25)

    4. Title-to-Content Alignment

    For 15 high-frequency titles appearing in both periods, cosine similarity between 2024 and 2026 mean description embeddings:

    Title Sim. Content drift
    devops engineer 0.973 Negligible
    data engineer 0.972 Negligible
    java developer 0.966 Negligible
    machine learning engineer 0.956 Low
    software engineer 0.942 Low
    full stack engineer 0.942 Low
    software developer 0.855 Moderate
    frontend developer 0.826 Moderate
    back end developer 0.799 High
    web developer 0.766 High

    Pattern: Infrastructure and ML roles have stable content. Front-end and web development roles show moderate-to-high content drift, possibly reflecting the shift toward AI-augmented development changing what "frontend" or "web" means.

    Percentage of SWE postings containing each marker in the raw title (aggregator-excluded):

    ii/iii/iv 3.6% 6.4% +2.8
    senior 23.2% 25.6% +2.4
    founding 0.09% 0.70% +0.6
    principal 2.9% 3.5% +0.6
    associate 0.77% 1.3% +0.5
    intern 0.93% 1.2% +0.3
    entry-level 0.09% 0.25% +0.2
    lead 6.7% 7.0% +0.3
    junior 0.81% 0.56% -0.2
    architect 1.8% 0.25% -1.6

    Key findings: - Staff engineer titles nearly tripled -- the dominant title-level shift, consistent with the IC ladder elevation thesis - Level numbering (II, III, IV) increased, suggesting more structured leveling in job postings - Architect titles collapsed from 1.8% to 0.25% -- possibly absorbed into "staff engineer" or "principal engineer" - Junior declined weakly; intern actually increased

    6. Emerging Role Categories

    Among 751 genuinely new titles (>=3 postings, not in any 2024 source):

    Theme Postings Examples
    AI/ML 1,168 AI data engineer, genai python systems engineer, AI native engineer
    Platform/Infra 725 Cloud engineer, web platform engineer, growth platform engineer
    Data 333 Data engineer - manager, data engineer - associate
    Backend 273 Backend (merchant interfaces), backend (streaming)
    Fullstack 238 PXE leadership full stack engineer
    Frontend 124 Frontend engineer (discovery), frontend engineer growth
    Security 66 AWS proactive security, endpoint security
    Embedded 43 BIOS firmware, connectivity/wireless
    Agent 24 Agentic software engineer, agentic intelligence
    Founding 18 Founding engineer ($3M pre-seed)

    The "agent" theme is embryonic but notable -- "agentic" as a job title qualifier has emerged from zero.

    Surprises & Unexpected Patterns

    1. Architect title collapse (-1.6pp): Not expected. "Architect" as a title is disappearing while "staff" is surging. This may indicate that the "architect" role is being absorbed into staff/principal IC tracks or becoming a responsibility rather than a distinct title.

    2. "Founding engineer" surge: From near-zero (0.09%) to 0.70% of postings -- 8x growth. This reflects the startup ecosystem's evolving hiring language.

    3. Web developer content drift: The highest content drift (0.77 cosine similarity) -- "web developer" in 2026 describes a substantially different role than in 2024. Worth investigating what specifically changed.

    4. Intern titles INCREASED: Despite junior share declining, intern postings grew from 0.93% to 1.21% of SWE titles. Companies may be restructuring the pipeline: fewer entry-level full-time roles but maintaining internship programs.

    Sensitivity Checks

    (a) Aggregator exclusion: All findings hold and are slightly amplified with aggregator exclusion. AI title share is 16.7% vs 16.1% (aggregators slightly dilute AI titles). Staff surge is larger at +4.9pp vs +4.2pp aggregator-excluded.

    Data Quality Caveats

    1. title_normalized strips seniority markers (senior, junior, staff, etc.). All seniority marker analysis used the raw title column.
    2. 2024 baseline comes from arshkon (n=5,019) which is small relative to scraped (n=24,095). Low-frequency title patterns may appear "new" simply because arshkon didn't sample them.
    3. Content alignment uses sentence-transformer embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2, 384-dim). These capture semantic similarity but may miss fine-grained requirement changes.
    4. Cross-source title normalization may differ between Kaggle scraped format and the current scraper format (e.g., suffix handling, Unicode).

    Action Items

    • T11 should check whether the staff-level complexity profile is distinct from senior (are staff postings more complex, suggesting a new super-senior IC tier?)
    • Wave 3 text analysis should investigate what changed in "web developer" and "frontend developer" job descriptions
    • The architect-to-staff title migration hypothesis could be tested by checking if companies that posted "architect" in 2024 now post "staff" in 2026
