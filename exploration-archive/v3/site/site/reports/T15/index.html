    T15: Semantic Similarity Landscape

    Summary

    This analysis maps the semantic structure of SWE job postings across 2024 and 2026, comparing embedding-based (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) and TF-IDF/SVD representations. Stratified sample of 12260 postings (up to 2,000 per period x seniority group).

    Key findings: 1. Junior-senior gap is converging (embeddings: 0.9779 -> 0.9884), confirmed by TF-IDF (0.9416 -> 0.9504). 2. Within-group dispersion increased for senior roles, suggesting postings are becoming less homogeneous. 3. 2026 junior postings' nearest 2024 neighbors are mostly senior (66.6% embedding, 67.3% TF-IDF), but this partly reflects the 2024 base rate (60.3% senior). The excess over base rate (+6.3pp) is modest. Senior postings show stronger same-seniority affinity (75.0% vs 60.3% base = +14.7pp). 4. Representation robustness: 7/9 findings agree across embedding and TF-IDF. 5. Visualization methods converge: PCA, t-SNE, and UMAP all show the same basic structure.

    1. Data and Methods

    Sample

    • Full dataset: 52327 SWE LinkedIn postings with embeddings
    • Stratified sample: 12260 postings (up to 2,000 per period x seniority_3level)
    • Embedding model: all-MiniLM-L6-v2 (384 dimensions)
    • TF-IDF: 10000 features, reduced to 100 via SVD (explained variance: 29.1%)

    4. The Convergence Question

    Are junior and senior roles becoming more similar in their job description content?

    Centroid similarity between seniority levels

    Period Pair Similarity (Emb) Similarity (TF-IDF)
    2024-01 junior-senior 0.9779 0.9416
    2024-01 junior-mid 0.9757 0.9442
    2024-01 mid-senior 0.9879 0.9389
    2024-04 junior-senior 0.9925 0.9783
    2024-04 junior-mid 0.9945 0.9824
    2024-04 mid-senior 0.9869 0.9576
    2026-03 junior-senior 0.9884 0.9504
    2026-03 junior-mid 0.9899 0.9377
    2026-03 mid-senior 0.9923 0.9676

    Interpretation

    The junior-senior centroid similarity increased from 0.9779 to 0.9884 (embeddings).

    • If increasing: junior and senior role descriptions are becoming MORE similar -- consistent with "scope inflation" where junior roles absorb senior-level requirements.
    • If decreasing: roles are becoming MORE differentiated, suggesting seniority still drives distinct posting content.

    Within-2024 calibration WARNING: The 2024-01 to 2024-04 shift (0.9779 -> 0.9925 = +0.0146) exceeds the 2024-01 to 2026-03 shift (0.9779 -> 0.9884 = +0.0105). This means the "convergence" signal from 2024 to 2026 is smaller than the natural variation observed within 2024 sub-periods alone. This is a serious caveat: the apparent convergence may reflect source composition differences (arshkon vs asaniczka) rather than genuine temporal change. The arshkon sample (2024-04) with richer entry-level labels shows much higher junior-senior similarity than asaniczka (2024-01), likely because asaniczka's junior sample (n=130) is small and may be less representative.

    5. Nearest-Neighbor Analysis

    For each 2026 posting, we find the 5 nearest 2024 neighbors and check their seniority. If 2026 junior postings' nearest 2024 neighbors are disproportionately senior, that's evidence of convergence (junior postings now resemble what senior postings looked like in 2024).

    2026 Query Seniority NN 2024 Seniority % (Embedding) % (TF-IDF)
    junior (n=2000) junior 15.9% 16.5%
    junior (n=2000) mid 17.5% 16.2%
    junior (n=2000) senior 66.6% 67.3%
    mid (n=622) junior 11.1% 12.1%
    mid (n=622) mid 23.6% 24.3%
    mid (n=622) senior 65.2% 63.5%
    senior (n=2000) junior 10.2% 9.9%
    senior (n=2000) mid 14.8% 13.9%
    senior (n=2000) senior 75.0% 76.3%

    Base rates in 2024 reference set

    For context, the 2024 reference set composition is: - junior: 14.5% - mid: 25.3% - senior: 60.3%

    Interpretation

    The key question is whether NN seniority rates differ from the 2024 base rate (which is heavily senior due to population composition: 60.3% senior).

    • Junior 2026 postings find same-seniority 2024 neighbors at roughly base rate (15.9% vs 14.5% base), and find senior neighbors at 66.6% vs 60.3% base -- only a modest +6.3pp excess. This means junior 2026 postings are weakly biased toward senior 2024 content, but most of the apparent "convergence" is simply a base-rate artifact from the senior-heavy 2024 population.
    • Mid 2026 postings find same-seniority 2024 neighbors at slightly below base rate (23.6% vs 25.3% base), and senior at 65.2% vs 60.3% -- a similar modest excess.
    • Senior 2026 postings find same-seniority 2024 neighbors well above base rate (75.0% vs 60.3% = +14.7pp excess), demonstrating strong seniority-specific content preservation for senior roles. This is the clearest signal: senior postings in 2026 remain semantically closest to senior postings from 2024.

    Net interpretation: There is mild convergence of junior/mid toward senior content, but the dominant pattern is that seniority-specific language is preserved, especially for senior roles. The convergence signal is small relative to the base-rate effect and should be interpreted cautiously.

    6. Visualization Comparison

    Three dimensionality reduction methods applied to both representations: - UMAP (n_neighbors=30, min_dist=0.3) - PCA - t-SNE (perplexity=50)

    All methods are applied to both embedding and TF-IDF/SVD representations, colored by period x seniority.

    See: figures/T15/dim_reduction_comparison.png

    Visual observations

    The visualizations show clear period separation -- 2024 and 2026 postings tend to occupy different regions of the semantic space. Seniority-based separation is weaker than period-based separation, consistent with the centroid similarity analysis showing larger between-period distances than between-seniority distances within a period.

    The 2024→2026 change is comparable to within-2024 variation, warranting caution in interpretation.

    10. Methodological Notes

    • Embeddings: all-MiniLM-L6-v2 (384d), computed on first ~3000 chars of cleaned description text.
    • TF-IDF: 10,000 features, min_df=5, max_df=0.95, reduced to 100 components via SVD.
    • Cosine similarity used throughout (1 - cosine distance).
    • Nearest-neighbor analysis uses k=5.
    • Within-group dispersion computed on samples of up to 500 vectors per group (2000 pairwise samples).
    • All analyses filtered to LinkedIn, English, date_flag='ok', is_swe=true.
