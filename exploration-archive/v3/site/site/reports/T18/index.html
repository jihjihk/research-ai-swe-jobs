    T18: Cross-Occupation Boundary Analysis

    Task: Determine which observed changes in SWE postings (2024 to 2026) are SWE-specific vs field-wide labor market trends.

    Method: Compare parallel trends across three occupation groups -- SWE (n=52K), SWE-adjacent (n=20K), and Control (accountants, nurses, engineers -- n=142K) -- using difference-in-differences. TF-IDF boundary analysis for occupational convergence. Keyword searches use description (full text) to avoid boilerplate-removal recall loss.

    Bottom line: AI adoption and tech stack expansion are overwhelmingly SWE-specific. Scope inflation (collaboration, mentoring, strategy language) is partly field-wide but has a meaningful SWE-excess component. Description length growth is largely field-wide.

    1. Parallel Trends

    1.1 AI/ML Keyword Prevalence

    Period SWE SWE-adjacent Control
    Jan 2024 14.8% 10.9% 0.4%
    Apr 2024 12.3% 14.3% 0.4%
    Mar 2026 39.1% 38.3% 2.8%

    SWE AI prevalence nearly tripled from ~12-15% to 39%. Control occupations went from 0.4% to 2.8% -- a 7x relative increase but still negligible in absolute terms. The SWE-Control gap widened from ~12pp to ~36pp.

    GenAI-specific terms (LLM, GPT, generative AI, copilot, langchain, agentic, etc.): - SWE: 2.7% -> 3.1% -> 23.3% (8x increase in absolute terms) - SWE-adjacent: 1.5% -> 3.6% -> 20.4% - Control: 0.0% -> 0.0% -> 0.8%

    AI tool usage language (ai-powered, ai-assisted, leverage AI, ai-driven): - SWE: 1.8% -> 2.5% -> 21.3% (8.5x) - Control: 0.0% -> 0.1% -> 1.5%

    AI adoption in job postings is overwhelmingly concentrated in SWE and SWE-adjacent roles, with minimal spillover to control occupations.

    1.2 Description Length

    Period SWE (median core) SWE-adj Control
    Jan 2024 2,419 2,624 2,280
    Apr 2024 2,504 2,764 1,928
    Mar 2026 4,171 4,471 3,160

    All three groups show substantial description length growth. SWE grew +1,667 chars (Apr 2024 to Mar 2026), Control grew +1,232 chars. The DiD is +435 chars -- meaningful but modest relative to the overall growth. Description length expansion is predominantly a field-wide phenomenon, with a mild SWE excess.

    1.3 Scope / Organizational Language

    Metric SWE change (04->26) Control change (04->26) DiD (SWE - Control)
    Cross-functional/stakeholder +16.0pp +10.7pp +5.3pp
    Mentoring +11.6pp +10.6pp +1.0pp
    Leadership +9.7pp +9.9pp -0.2pp
    Soft skills -0.6pp +4.5pp -5.1pp
    Strategy/roadmap +9.4pp +2.4pp +7.0pp

    Mentoring and leadership language growth is entirely field-wide -- the DiD is near zero. Cross-functional/stakeholder language has a modest SWE-specific excess (+5.3pp). Strategy/roadmap language growth is partly SWE-specific (+7.0pp). Soft skills actually grew less in SWE than in control occupations.

    1.4 Tech Mention Count (30 tracked technologies)

    Period SWE SWE-adjacent Control
    Jan 2024 4.74 2.18 0.31
    Apr 2024 4.79 2.61 0.33
    Mar 2026 5.67 3.64 0.43

    SWE change: +0.88. Control change: +0.10. DiD: +0.78. Tech stack expansion is strongly SWE-specific. Control occupations barely mention these technologies at all.

    1.5 Seniority Distribution

    Period SWE junior Control junior SWE senior Control senior
    Jan 2024 0.6%* 0.4%* 93.2% 94.7%
    Apr 2024 16.5% 19.0% 59.9% 58.8%
    Mar 2026 13.5% 40.9% 77.2% 50.9%

    *Jan 2024 (asaniczka) has no native entry-level labels -- values represent imputed entries only.

    Critical finding: Control occupations have a higher junior share (40.9%) than SWE (13.5%) in March 2026. From 2024-04 to 2026-03, SWE junior share declined 3.0pp while control junior share increased 21.9pp. The DiD is -24.9pp. This is strongly SWE-specific -- the junior role compression is not a general labor market trend; it is the opposite of what happened in control occupations.

    2. Difference-in-Differences Summary

    Metric SWE change (04->26) Control change DiD (SWE-Ctrl) SWE-specific?
    AI prevalence (broad) +26.7pp +2.4pp +24.4pp YES, strongly
    GenAI prevalence +20.2pp +0.8pp +19.4pp YES, strongly
    AI tool language +18.8pp +1.5pp +17.4pp YES, strongly
    Tech mention count +0.88 +0.10 +0.78 YES
    Strategy/roadmap +9.4pp +2.4pp +7.0pp YES, moderate
    Cross-functional +16.0pp +10.7pp +5.3pp Partly
    Description length +1,667 chars +1,232 chars +435 chars Partly
    Mentoring +11.6pp +10.6pp +1.0pp NO, field-wide
    Leadership +9.7pp +9.9pp -0.2pp NO, field-wide
    Soft skills -0.6pp +4.5pp -5.1pp NO, inverse

    SWE vs SWE-adjacent DiD: The DiD between SWE and SWE-adjacent is near zero for most metrics (AI: +2.7pp, tech: -0.15, collab: -4.9pp). SWE and SWE-adjacent move in lockstep. The distinguishing boundary is between technical roles (SWE + adjacent) vs non-technical control occupations.

    3. Boundary Shift Analysis

    3.1 TF-IDF Cosine Similarity (500 samples per group)

    Period SWE <-> SWE-adj SWE <-> Control Adj <-> Control
    Jan 2024 0.853 0.409 0.449
    Apr 2024 0.871 0.434 0.470
    Mar 2026 0.875 0.507 0.522

    SWE and SWE-adjacent remain highly similar (0.85-0.88) and this is stable or slightly increasing over time. SWE-Control and Adj-Control similarity increased modestly (0.41->0.51 and 0.45->0.52), suggesting all job descriptions are becoming more generically similar -- likely an artifact of increasing description length adding shared boilerplate language.

    3.2 Term Migration

    Shared terms gaining importance (2024 -> 2026): "ai" became a top shared term between SWE and SWE-adjacent by 2026 (SWE=0.055, Adj=0.043) that was not prominent in 2024.

    Persistent SWE-distinctive terms: "software", "code", "development", "java", "backend", "software engineer", "stack", "developer" -- core SWE vocabulary remains stable.

    Persistent SWE-adj distinctive terms: "security", "network", "data", "analytics", "data science", "database", "business" -- domain-specific vocabulary is preserved.

    The boundary between SWE and SWE-adjacent is stable, not blurring. The groups share more AI vocabulary in 2026 but retain their distinct domain identities.

    4. Specific Adjacent Roles

    4.1 Data Scientist

    • AI prevalence: 76% -> 82% -> 84% (already high, modest further increase)
    • GenAI: 12% -> 19% -> 34% (meaningful GenAI-specific growth)
    • Cross-functional: 49% -> 59% -> 72% (strong scope expansion)
    • Description length (med core): 2,854 -> 3,352 -> 4,712 (+1,360)

    4.2 Network Engineer

    • AI prevalence: 2.0% -> 4.0% -> 10.4% (low base, meaningful 5x growth)
    • GenAI: 0% -> 0% -> 3.2% (emerging GenAI presence)
    • Cross-functional: 10% -> 21% -> 36% (strong scope expansion)
    • Description length: 2,308 -> 2,814 -> 4,076 (+1,262)

    4.3 Security Engineer

    • AI prevalence: 4.6% -> 6.4% -> 17.1% (tripled)
    • GenAI: 0.8% -> 1.3% -> 11.3% (major GenAI surge)
    • Cross-functional: 23% -> 28% -> 47% (strong scope expansion)
    • Description length: 3,049 -> 3,518 -> 4,968 (+1,450)

    4.4 Quality Engineer

    • AI prevalence: 0.8% -> 0.7% -> 5.4% (low but growing)
    • GenAI: 0% -> 0% -> 0.9% (minimal)
    • Cross-functional: 33% -> 37% -> 48% (scope expansion)

    Pattern: Security and data science roles show meaningful AI adoption, while network and quality engineering show much less. Scope expansion (cross-functional language) is universal across all adjacent roles.

    Caveat on data engineers: Most "data engineer" titles are classified as SWE (n=1,260 in Jan 2024), not SWE-adjacent (n=110). The small SWE-adjacent data engineer sample has elevated AI prevalence (69%) because the few postings that failed the SWE classifier tend to be ML-heavy outliers. Data engineer trends should be interpreted from the SWE sample, not the adjacent sample.

    5. AI Adoption Gradient

    The SWE-Control AI gap is widening:

    Period SWE-Ctrl gap SWE-Adj gap
    Jan 2024 14.4pp 3.8pp
    Apr 2024 11.9pp -2.0pp
    Mar 2026 36.3pp 0.7pp

    The SWE-to-SWE-adjacent gap is near zero -- these groups track each other closely. The meaningful divide is between technical roles (SWE + adjacent, ~39% AI prevalence) and non-technical control roles (~3% AI prevalence). The gradient is widening dramatically.

    6. Sensitivity Analyses

    6a. Aggregator exclusion

    After removing aggregator postings: - SWE AI prevalence: 13.1% -> 40.9% (slightly higher without aggregators) - Pattern is robust. Aggregator-filtered SWE AI prevalence is actually slightly higher, suggesting aggregator-sourced postings slightly dilute AI content.

    6g. SWE classification tier (regex-only)

    Restricting to regex-tier SWE (highest confidence classification): - AI prevalence: 12.7% -> 39.9% (essentially identical to full sample) - All trends fully replicate at highest-confidence classification tier.

    7. Summary and Implications

    What is SWE-specific (strong DiD)

    1. AI/ML adoption -- 24pp excess over control. This is the defining SWE-specific change.
    2. GenAI adoption -- 19pp excess. GenAI terms barely exist in control occupations.
    3. AI tool language -- 17pp excess. "AI-powered", "AI-assisted" etc. are SWE phenomena.
    4. Tech stack expansion -- +0.78 more technologies listed than control's increase.
    5. Junior role compression -- SWE junior share declined while control's increased.
    6. Strategy/roadmap language -- 7pp SWE excess.

    What is partly SWE-specific (moderate DiD)

    1. Cross-functional/stakeholder language -- 5pp excess. Some SWE-specific component.
    2. Description length growth -- Both grew, SWE grew slightly more.

    What is field-wide (near-zero DiD)

    1. Mentoring language -- identical growth across occupations.
    2. Leadership language -- identical growth.
    3. Soft skills -- actually grew less in SWE than control.

    Implications for the paper

    The "AI-driven SWE restructuring" story is strongly supported. The core findings -- AI adoption, tech stack growth, junior share decline -- are overwhelmingly SWE-specific, not general labor market trends. However, some aspects of "scope inflation" (mentoring, leadership language) are field-wide phenomena that should not be attributed solely to AI. The paper should distinguish between: - SWE-specific restructuring: AI adoption, tech stack complexity, junior compression, strategic scope - Field-wide posting evolution: Description length growth, mentoring/leadership language, general scope expansion
