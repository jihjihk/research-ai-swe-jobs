    T25: Interview Elicitation Artifacts

    Status: Complete Agent: N (Wave 4) Date: 2026-04-05

    Summary

    Produced six interview artifacts for RQ4 semi-structured interviews, all using corrected findings from the full exploration pipeline (T01-T23). The artifacts are designed to elicit practitioner perspectives on three core puzzles: (1) the AI-entry orthogonality finding, (2) whether AI requirements are real hiring bars or signaling, and (3) how practitioners experience the domain recomposition from frontend/web toward ML/AI engineering.

    Artifacts Produced

    1. Entry-Level JD Examples (Tables)

    Files: - exploration/tables/T25/entry_2026_scope_jds.csv (20 rows) - exploration/tables/T25/entry_2024_contrast_jds.csv (20 rows)

    2026 entry-level postings selected for validated scope expansion: mentor, coach, project management, or stakeholder terms present; YOE <= 3; non-aggregator. 2024 postings from arshkon for temporal contrast. Each row includes company name, title, YOE, and binary scope indicator flags.

    Companies represented in 2026 scope examples: Accenture (AI Native Software Engineer, YOE 1), Bosch (Rotational Development Program, YOE 2), Transamerica (Intermediate Software Engineer, YOE 1), CGI (Data Engineer, YOE 2), Medtronic (Reliability Engineer II, YOE 2), among others.

    2. Company Panel for Paired JD Comparisons (Table)

    File: exploration/tables/T25/company_ai_entry_orthogonality.csv (463 companies)

    Each row shows a company with >= 3 SWE postings in both 2024 and 2026, with AI prevalence change and entry-share change. Enables selection of paired JDs illustrating: - AI-forward: Oracle (0% to 100% AI), Deloitte (0% to 94%), KPMG (0% to 97%) - Traditional hold: Google (96% to 96%, already saturated), Boeing (13% to 11%), Northrop Grumman (1% to 2%) - Entry expanders: Epic (0% to 99% AI AND 0% to 100% entry -- dramatic dual shift), Cisco (40% to 100% AI, 0% to 61% entry)

    3. Junior-Share Trend Visualization

    File: exploration/figures/T25/junior_share_trend.png

    Timeline from April 2024 to March 2026 showing entry-level share declining from 22.3% to 14.0% (seniority_native). Annotated with 6 major AI model releases. Includes prominent caveat box noting the seniority operationalization dependence and that LLM seniority labels will resolve the discrepancy.

    Data points: Arshkon (n=769 entry / 3,444 known) at April 2024; Scraped (n=2,789 entry / 19,896 known) at March 2026.

    4. Senior Archetype Chart

    File: exploration/figures/T25/senior_archetype_chart.png

    Two-panel figure: - Left: Management density by seniority level (2024 vs 2026). Shows universal expansion at entry/associate/mid-senior and -23% decrease at director. Highlights the corrected finding that management language expansion is field-wide, not SWE-specific. - Right: Senior sub-archetype shares from T21 clustering. Technical Orchestrator grew +29% relative (4.5% to 5.8%), Strategic Leader declined -20% relative (16.8% to 13.5%). People Manager grew modestly (+2.5pp). Low-touch Generalist stable at ~58%.

    5. AI Divergence Chart

    File: exploration/figures/T25/ai_divergence_chart.png

    Grouped bar chart comparing posting AI requirement rates vs estimated developer usage rates, by category (AI-any, AI-tool, AI-domain) in 2024 and 2026. Key visual message: posting bars are consistently below usage bars. AI-as-domain (22.7% posting vs ~20% usage) is the one exception where posting may slightly overshoot.

    Gap annotations: AI-any narrowed from -45pp to -34pp; AI-tool narrowed from -33pp to -32pp; AI-domain went from -4pp to +3pp (possible overshoot).

    6. AI-Entry Orthogonality Scatter

    File: exploration/figures/T25/ai_entry_orthogonality.png

    Company-level scatter of AI prevalence change vs entry-level share change for 463 overlap companies. Shows a flat regression line with r = -0.019 (p = 0.683). Includes explanation box noting the metro-level null result (r = -0.04) and framing the key message: "Companies that adopted AI did NOT cut junior roles."

    Interview Protocol Recommendations

    Priority Elicitation Order

    1. Start with the orthogonality scatter (Artifact 6). This is the most counterintuitive finding and generates the richest discussion. Frame: "We expected companies adopting AI would hire fewer juniors. They don't. What explains this?"

    2. Follow with junior-share trend (Artifact 3). Establish the macro context: junior SWE postings declined while AI models proliferated. Use the model release annotations as temporal anchors.

    3. Show the AI divergence chart (Artifact 5). Probe: "Posting requirements lag actual usage. Is this your experience? Are AI skills assumed? Or are they genuinely not required for some roles?"

    4. Present the senior archetype chart (Artifact 4). Probe director role evolution: "Directors are using less people-management language but more technical orchestration language. Is this a real change in the role, or a posting-language artifact?"

    5. Close with paired JDs (Artifacts 1-2). Ground the discussion in specific examples. Select pairs relevant to the interviewee's company/domain.

    Probes for Each Artifact

    Detailed probes are in exploration/artifacts/README.md.

    Caveats for Interview Use

    1. Management indicator is corrected. Use +4-10pp (strict validated patterns), not +31pp from T11's uncorrected broad patterns. The broad patterns captured "a leading company" and "cross-functional collaboration" as management indicators, which they are not.

    2. Entry-level direction is operationalization-dependent. Present the decline (seniority_native: 22.3% to 14.0%) as the primary estimate but note the caveat. Do not present as settled fact until LLM seniority labels are available.

    3. Usage benchmarks are estimates. The 75% developer AI usage is extrapolated from 2024 surveys. Present as approximate, not precise.

    4. 57% of aggregate change is compositional. When discussing company-level findings, note that the aggregate trends reflect different companies posting, not just the same companies changing behavior.
