    T26: Exploration Synthesis

    Status: Complete Agent: N (Wave 4) Date: 2026-04-05

    Summary

    The exploration synthesis consolidates findings from 23 task reports (T01-T25), 4 gate memos, and the INDEX into a single handoff document for the analysis phase.

    The full synthesis is at: exploration/reports/SYNTHESIS.md

    This report briefly summarizes what the synthesis covers and its key structural decisions.

    What the Synthesis Covers

    1. Data quality verdict per RQ -- RQ1 through RQ4, with explicit quality ratings and caveats for each analytical dimension.

    2. Recommended analytical samples -- SQL templates for primary SWE, entry-level, cross-occupation DiD, and company overlap panel samples.

    3. Seniority column recommendation -- Full ablation framework: seniority_llm (when available) > seniority_native > seniority_final. Explicit documentation of the operationalization discrepancy and why it matters.

    4. Known confounders -- 4 HIGH, 4 MODERATE, 3 LOW severity confounders with affected analyses and mitigation strategies.

    5. Discovery findings -- Organized as Confirmed (6 findings), Corrected (2 findings), Contradicted (3 hypotheses), and New (5 discoveries).

    6. Domain summaries from T09 (posting archetypes), T14 (technology evolution), T17 (geographic structure), T21 (senior archetypes), T22 (ghost/aspirational assessment).

    7. New hypotheses from T24, with H1 (domain recomposition drives junior decline) flagged as most testable with existing data.

    8. Method recommendations -- Primary and secondary methods for each RQ; recommended sensitivity framework (5 dimensions).

    9. Sensitivity requirements -- Which findings need formal robustness checks, and which specifications to run.

    10. Pending data improvements -- seniority_llm (highest priority) and description_core_llm for scraped data.

    11. Interview priorities -- Three themes: orthogonality puzzle, AI requirements as hiring bars vs signaling, domain recomposition experience.

    Key Structural Decisions in the Synthesis

    1. Honest about corrections

    The synthesis prominently documents that the management indicator was corrected from +31pp to +4-10pp (T22), that soft skills expansion is NOT SWE-specific (T18), and that semantic convergence fails calibration (T15). These corrections are not buried -- they appear in Section 5 (Discovery Findings) with explicit "Corrected" and "Contradicted" labels.

    2. Orthogonality finding is core, not caveat

    The AI-entry orthogonality (r ~ 0 at firm and metro level) is presented as a core finding, not a footnote. The synthesis explicitly states what the paper CANNOT claim (AI caused junior elimination within firms) alongside what it CAN claim (parallel market trends, SWE-specific via DiD).

    3. Pending data improvements are promises, not excuses

    The synthesis documents that seniority_llm and description_core_llm for scraped data will change specific findings when available, with concrete predictions about direction of change.

    4. The 57% compositional finding shapes framing

    The synthesis recommends the analysis phase frame its contribution as documenting BOTH within-firm behavioral changes (~43%) AND market-level compositional shifts (~57%), not just one or the other.
