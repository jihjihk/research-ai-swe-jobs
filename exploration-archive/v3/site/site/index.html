    SWE Labor Market Exploration

    Study overview

    This site presents the exploration findings from a study of how AI coding tools are restructuring software engineering (SWE) labor demand. Using ~52,000 SWE job postings and ~142,000 control-occupation postings from LinkedIn spanning 2024-2026, we conducted 26 exploration tasks across four waves to characterize what changed, validate which changes are SWE-specific, and identify what the data can and cannot support as claims.

    The presentation: MARP slide deck -- a ~25-slide summary of the core findings, suitable for a research advisor meeting.

    Core findings

    1. AI competency requirements surged (+24pp DiD), validated as genuine

    SWE AI mention rates went from 8% to 33%, while control occupations moved from 1% to 3%. The requirements are less hedged than traditional skills -- employers mean it.

    Full analysis: AI Requirements Surge

    2. Entry-level SWE share declined while control junior share increased (DiD = -25pp)

    The junior decline is SWE-specific and holds within companies. But the trend direction depends on seniority operationalization -- a key caveat awaiting resolution.

    Full analysis: Junior Hiring Decline

    3. The SWE domain landscape recomposed: ML/AI 4% to 27%, Frontend 41% to 24%

    Technology domain -- not seniority -- is the market's dominant structural axis (10x more important by NMI). The shift from frontend to ML/AI engineering is the largest compositional change.

    Full analysis: Domain Recomposition

    4. AI is additive to technology stacks (11.4 vs 7.3 techs per posting)

    AI requirements layer on top of existing stacks rather than replacing them. A new 25-technology AI/ML ecosystem emerged from near-zero.

    Full analysis: Technology Ecosystem

    5. AI adoption and junior decline are orthogonal (r ~ 0 at firm and metro level)

    The paper cannot claim AI caused junior elimination within firms. These are parallel market trends. 57% of aggregate change is compositional.

    Full analysis: The Orthogonality Puzzle

    6. The methodology self-corrected: +31pp became +4-10pp

    Wave 3 overturned Wave 2's management inflation headline. The correction demonstrates iterative validation and strengthens surviving findings.

    Full analysis: Corrections & Revisions

    Project details

    • Data: ~52K SWE + ~142K control postings from LinkedIn (2024-2026)
    • Method: Cross-occupation DiD, within-firm panel, shift-share decomposition
    • Exploration: 26 tasks, 4 waves, 4 gate memos, 95 figures
    • Date: April 2026
