    T17 — Geographic market structure¶

    Agent: J (Wave 3) · Date: 2026-04-15 Inputs: data/unified.parquet, exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_tech_matrix.parquet, exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet Script: exploration/scripts/T17_geographic_structure.py Sample: 18 metros qualifying with n >= 50 SWE postings in BOTH kaggle_arshkon 2024 and scraped 2026. Total metro-assigned rows: 1,962 (2024) + 24,220 (2026) = 26,182. Multi-location / remote rows excluded (see Section 1).

    Headline¶

    Metro-level AI surge vs entry-share change: Pearson r = −0.28, p = 0.255 (n=18). Spearman ρ = −0.441, p = 0.067. Under aggregator exclusion, Pearson r strengthens to −0.38 but remains non-significant (p=0.13). Under 50/company capping, r = −0.30.

    Verdict: The correlation is weakly negative but does not reject the null of "no metro-level relationship between the AI surge and entry-share change." This is consistent with Alt C's prediction that the AI surge is metro-uniform and orthogonal to entry-share movement. The point estimate leans toward "metros with larger AI surges had SLIGHTLY larger entry-share DECLINES" — a direction that is NOT what the simple junior-scope-inflation story predicts — but it is below the SNR threshold for a Wave 3 claim.

    AI surge is broadly uniform. 18 of 18 metros saw ΔAI any between +0.07 and +0.30 (mean +0.19, std 0.06). Every qualifying metro's AI rate at least tripled. The smallest surge (Detroit +0.07) is 2× the 2024 baseline; the largest (Seattle +0.30) is 4×. Geography is NOT a hiding place for AI prevalence change.

    Entry-share movement is NOT concentrated in tech hubs. The three biggest AI-surge metros (Seattle +0.30, SF Bay +0.28, Austin +0.24) had minimal entry-share movement under seniority_final (Seattle −0.028, SF Bay −0.026, Austin +0.001). The metros with the biggest entry-share drops are Phoenix (−0.13) and Houston (−0.12) — both modest-AI-surge markets.

    Archetype geography is real and shifting. SF Bay's LLM/GenAI share grew from 15.7% to 35.6% (+19.9 pp, relative index 1.78 vs global). Austin's JS frontend share collapsed from 25.3% to 9.1% while its LLM/GenAI share rose from 3.2% to 15.9%. Washington DC's SCI-cleared-SWE share nearly quadrupled (3.2% → 11.8%). Defense archetype is concentrated in LA (index 1.87), Denver (1.75), Detroit (1.70) — not what most people would guess. These are first-class geographic findings and they complicate the national-level narrative.

    3. Metro-level correlation test (primary Alt C test)¶

    Pearson and Spearman correlations between metro-level Δ metrics (n=18):

    x y Pearson r p Spearman ρ p
    ΔAI any Δ entry (sen_fin) −0.283 0.255 −0.441 0.067
    ΔAI any Δ entry (YOE≤2) +0.236 0.345 +0.164 0.515
    ΔAI any Δ desc len −0.158 0.531 −0.092 0.717
    ΔAI any Δ scope +0.117 0.643 +0.135 0.593
    ΔAI tools Δ entry (sen_fin) −0.117 0.644 −0.226 0.367
    Δ desc len Δ entry (sen_fin) +0.047 0.853 +0.154 0.542
    Δ scope Δ entry (sen_fin) −0.258 0.301 −0.273 0.272

    Alt C prediction test: Under Alt C, metro-level AI surge should be uniform (confirmed: std 0.06 around mean 0.19) and uncorrelated with metro-level entry-share movement (confirmed at conventional thresholds — neither Pearson nor Spearman is significant at p<0.05).

    Note: the Spearman ρ of −0.441 (p=0.067) for ΔAI any vs Δentry(sen_fin) is close to significance. If taken at face value, it would indicate that metros with larger AI surges had larger entry-share DECLINES under seniority_final — the direction expected under the "AI restructuring pushes juniors out" hypothesis. But under YOE ≤ 2, the rank correlation flips to +0.164 (positive). The spec dependence of the entry metric makes the metro-level correlation uninformative about the "AI restructuring pushes juniors out" claim: you can get either direction depending on which entry operationalization you use.

    Interpretation: the correlation result does NOT overturn Alt C. It is consistent with "uniform AI surge, instrument-driven entry-share noise." It is also technically consistent with a weak direction-unstable AI → entry relationship, but the spec dependence dissolves that interpretation.

    5. Remote work dimension¶

    In this schema, is_remote = TRUE rows carry metro_area = NULL (they are not assigned to a metro). So "remote share by metro" mechanically returns 0 for every metro. The valid test is to compare the remote-only pool vs the metro-assigned pool on 2026 SWE LinkedIn rows.

    Bucket n entry (sen_fin) entry (YOE≤2) AI any AI tools Scope Desc len (med) Tech count (med)
    Metro-assigned (2026) 30,560 0.065 0.173 0.274 0.093 0.375 4,611 6
    Remote-only (2026) 8,128 0.074 0.159 0.273 0.105 0.387 5,458 6

    Findings: - AI any is identical (0.273 vs 0.274) between remote and metro-assigned pools. Remote hiring is not more AI-forward than in-metro hiring. - AI tools-specific is slightly higher in remote (+0.012). Small. - Entry share under seniority_final is slightly HIGHER in remote (0.074 vs 0.065). Entry under YOE ≤ 2 is slightly LOWER in remote (0.159 vs 0.173). Opposite directions again — spec dependence at the remote/in-metro cut. - Remote descriptions are ~850 chars longer on median than metro-assigned. Scope rate slightly higher.

    Interpretation: The remote pool and the metro-assigned pool are structurally similar on the primary metrics. Remote hiring is not a distinct labor market segment for these metrics.

    Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. Austin's JS-frontend share collapsed from 25.3% to 9.1% while LLM/GenAI share quadrupled. This is the sharpest single metro × archetype shift in the data. Did not expect it.
    2. LLM/GenAI archetype is visibly geographically concentrated (SF 1.78×, Seattle 1.55×, everywhere else ~1×), even though the broad AI keyword surge is metro-uniform. There are TWO AI signals: the broad AI vocabulary that rose everywhere, and the LLM/GenAI job category that only rose sharply in SF and Seattle.
    3. Defense-related work is LA/San Diego/Detroit/Denver, not DC. DC is below-index on Defense-broad but above-index on SCI-cleared. The standard "DC = defense tech" intuition is wrong at the metro level and gets corrected when you split Defense into general-cleared vs SCI-cleared.
    4. Phoenix and Houston had the biggest entry-share drops (seniority_final) despite modest AI surges (+0.17, +0.17). The metros with the biggest entry drops are NOT the ones with the biggest AI surges.
    5. Raleigh-Durham had the biggest entry-share INCREASE (+0.10 under seniority_final) — but entry under YOE ≤ 2 barely moved (+0.013). This is another example of the spec dependence being live at the metro level. Something about Raleigh-Durham's 2026 sample relabeled toward seniority_final = entry.
    6. Remote vs metro-assigned pools are nearly identical on AI any (0.273 vs 0.274). Rhetoric about "remote engineering is the AI-native labor market" is not supported by the data — AI prevalence is orthogonal to remote/in-metro status.

    Data quality caveats¶

    • Small 2024 per-metro n. The 18 qualifying metros have arshkon counts from 51 (Raleigh-Durham) to 359 (SF Bay), averaging 129. With 10% entry rates, that's ~5-36 entry rows per metro in 2024. Per-metro rate estimates are noisy.
    • Archetype coverage asymmetry. Arshkon archetype labels cover 99.9%; scraped 30.7%. Per-metro 2026 archetype shares reflect the Stage 9 LLM router's selection, which may favor certain title patterns. The Austin JS-frontend collapse and SF LLM/GenAI surge should be verified against title-level distributions (not in T17 scope).
    • Entry-share metro rates have denominator drift — scraped seniority_final = unknown averages 53%, varying by metro. Per-metro entry-share comparisons carry residual denominator risk; the YOE ≤ 2 proxy is label-independent and carries no such risk.
    • Remote pool detected via is_remote. This is an explicit scraper-side flag (not the 0% 2024 data-artifact flag that Gate 1 warned about). Remote pool n=8,128 for scraped 2026.

    Sensitivity checks summary¶

    Dimension Applied Result
    (a) Aggregator exclusion ✓ (metro_metrics_no_aggregator.csv) Correlation direction preserved, magnitude slightly larger. No metros drop out (17 of 18 retained).
    (b) Company capping (50/company) ✓ (metro_metrics_cap50.csv) Direction preserved across all metrics. SF Bay ΔAI any shrinks from 0.283 to ~0.27 under cap (small companies like Adobe get capped).

    Not materially sensitive on any headline. The metro rankings by ΔAI are stable; the correlation is robust to direction but non-significant under every spec.

    Action items for downstream¶

    • For T28 (domain-stratified scope): The metro-level LLM/GenAI concentration in SF+Seattle (1.55-1.78×) is informative for domain stratification. T28 should verify that the within-archetype scope/requirement change it finds is not just an SF+Seattle effect.
    • For Gate 3 (V2 verification): Re-derive the metro-level Pearson r for ΔAI × Δentry under an independent script — verify the −0.28 and its Spearman analog.
    • For the paper: The metro-uniform AI surge is publishable as a geographic robustness claim. "AI adoption in SWE postings rose at least 2× in every one of the 18 metros qualifying at n≥50 per period" is a clean sentence. The entry-share geographic heterogeneity argues against a simple "juniors getting pushed out" reading that would predict entry declines proportional to AI surge.
    • For preprocessing owner: Remote rows are being flagged on scraped but carry metro_area = NULL. This is fine structurally but means per-metro analyses cannot measure remote share. Consider a metro_source = 'company_headquarters' fill for remote rows as a diagnostic option (not as a primary metro label).
