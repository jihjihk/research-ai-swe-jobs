    T11. Requirements complexity & credential stacking¶

    Agent G (Wave 2). Run: 2026-04-15. Data: shared swe_cleaned_text.parquet + swe_tech_matrix.parquet. Text-based features computed on text_source='llm' subset only (n=34,258). Binary per-row counts use the shared tech matrix across all 63,701 rows. Script: exploration/scripts/T11_requirements_complexity.py.

    Headline finding¶

    Requirements are thickening — more tech, more scope, more simultaneous categories — but the effect is modest once length-normalized, and the "management language rose" story fails validation.

    Across the LLM-cleaned subset:

    metric 2024 mean 2026 mean SNR (vs within-2024) verdict
    tech_count 4.74 6.09 5.4 robust
    requirement_breadth 6.88 9.27 10.2 robust
    credential_stack_depth_strict 2.77 3.31 10.5 robust
    credential_stack_depth_broad 3.07 3.63 6.0 robust
    scope_density (per 1K chars) 0.52 0.96 61.7 robust on SNR but fragile
    ai_term_count 0.09 0.80 10.1 robust
    tech_density (per 1K chars) 9.75 9.60 0.7 below noise
    mgmt_strict_count 0.090 0.100 0.35 below noise
    mgmt_broad_count 0.516 0.545 2.2 marginal
    soft_skill_count 0.37 0.40 0.22 below noise

    Key interpretations:

    1. Credential stacking is real and genuine. Entry-level postings in 2026 ask for more distinct requirement categories simultaneously, not just more tech within the same category. Under seniority_final, entry credential_stack_depth_strict rose 2.48 → 2.94. Under YOE ≤ 2, 3.17 → 3.78. This is the cleanest RQ2-adjacent finding in this task.

    2. Most of the breadth rise is NOT length-driven. Mean cleaned-description length in the LLM subset rose 627 → 735 chars (+17%). Mean requirement_breadth rose 6.88 → 9.27 (+35%). Breadth per 1K chars (length-normalized) still rose 13.6 → 14.4 (+6%). So ~½ of the breadth rise is length-expansion, ½ is genuine thickening. Excluding AI term mentions entirely, breadth still rose 6.78 → 8.47 (+25%).

    3. Tech density is flat. tech_density (techs per 1K chars) is essentially unchanged (9.75 → 9.60, SNR 0.7). Postings are mentioning more technologies, but only because they got longer. There is no evidence that individual sentences are getting tech-denser.

    4. Scope density looks huge (SNR 61.7) but is fragile. The within-2024 Cohen's d for scope_density is near zero (0.006), so the SNR ratio is numerically inflated. The absolute 2024 → 2026 lift (0.52 → 0.96, +85%) is real, but the SNR number should be read as "strong cross-period, weak instrument noise" rather than as a precision metric. Compare with tech_density which has a similar within-2024 stability but shows no cross-period lift — the contrast itself is the evidence that scope_density's rise is not a pure artifact.

    5. Management language rose, but does not survive validation. See next section — management broad inflates 4.37-4.56× over strict, and the broad pattern has 74% precision (below the 80% required threshold). Strict management rate is 8.5% → 9.5%, SNR 0.35, below instrument noise.

    Methodology¶

    Feature extraction¶

    Per-row regex extraction on description_cleaned from the LLM subset (text_source='llm', n=34,258). Tech counts come from the shared tech matrix (123 pre-validated regex patterns, covering all 63,701 rows — I used the LLM subset's tech counts here for consistency with the density metrics, but the matrix itself is available corpus-wide).

    Categories and representative patterns:

    • Soft skill (10 terms): communication, collaborat*, problem-solving, teamwork, leadership, interpersonal, adaptab*|flexib*, time management, analytical, detail-oriented.
    • Org scope (10 terms): ownership|own(s|ed|ing), end-to-end, cross-functional, stakeholder, autonom*, initiative, strateg*, vision*, roadmap, high-impact|drive impact|business impact.
    • Management strict (9 terms): manag(e|es|ed|ing) (excluding the bare noun "management"), mentor*, coach*, hir(e|es|ed|ing), direct reports, performance review, headcount, people management, 1:1|one-on-one.
    • Management broad extra (5 additional terms): lead|leads|leading, team(s), stakeholder(s), coordinat*, leadership. Broad = strict ∪ broad_extra.
    • Education tier: highest-matching of phd|doctorate (tier 3), m.s.|master* (tier 2), b.s.|bachelor* (tier 1).
    • AI terms (11 terms): ai|a.i., ml|machine learning, llm(s), agent*, copilot, cursor, claude, gpt, rag, generative, prompt engineering.
    • YOE indicator: yoe_extracted.notna().

    Complexity metrics¶

    • tech_count: sum of shared tech matrix boolean columns.
    • requirement_breadth: tech_count + soft_skill_count + org_scope_count + mgmt_strict_count + clip(education_tier, 0, 1) + ai_term_count + has_yoe. Max per row ≈ unbounded (driven by tech).
    • credential_stack_depth_strict: number of distinct requirement CATEGORIES (out of 7) with at least one mention. Max = 7.
    • credential_stack_depth_broad: same but uses mgmt_broad instead of mgmt_strict.
    • tech_density: tech_count / (desc_len_chars / 1000), floored at 0.1 to avoid div-by-zero.
    • scope_density: org_scope_count / (desc_len_chars / 1000).

    Calibration (within-2024 SNR)¶

    For each metric, compute: - within_2024_d = Cohen's d between kaggle_arshkon (2024-04) and kaggle_asaniczka (2024-01). - cross_period_d = Cohen's d between pooled 2024 and 2026. - SNR = |cross_period_d| / |within_2024_d|.

    Threshold: SNR ≥ 2 is "above instrument noise."

    Sensitivities applied¶

    • (a) aggregator exclusion: rerun primary means with is_aggregator = false. Result: no material change (requirement_breadth 6.88 → 6.72 2024; 9.27 → 9.29 2026). Aggregators are not driving the finding.
    • (b) company cap 50/company: rerun primary means with per-company random sample of ≤50 rows. Result: requirement_breadth 6.88 → 6.76 in 2024 and 9.27 → 9.38 in 2026 — slightly stronger after capping, meaning the top-poster concentration on the 2024 side is if anything suppressing the effect, not inflating it.
    • (c) seniority operationalization: entry-level complexity reported under both seniority_final = 'entry' and yoe_extracted <= 2. See "Entry-level" section below.
    • (f) within-2024 calibration: applied to every metric; column snr in complexity_snr_calibration.csv.

    Sensitivities NOT applied¶

    • (d) description text source (raw vs LLM): the features here are boilerplate-sensitive density metrics. Running on raw description would conflate boilerplate growth with requirement growth. The primary uses LLM subset; a raw-text cross-check is deferred to T13's section-anatomy work, which will better isolate boilerplate growth.
    • (g) SWE classification tier and (h) LLM text coverage: these are shared-artifact-level filters already applied by Agent Prep.

    Key results by question¶

    Q1 — Is credential stacking real?¶

    Yes. The distribution of credential_stack_depth_strict shifted right:

    depth 2024 share 2026 share
    0 2.4% 1.1%
    1 13.0% 8.1%
    2 26.7% 18.0%
    3 30.9% 29.1%
    4 18.6% 24.8%
    5 7.0% 13.7%
    6 1.4% 4.6%
    7 0.06% 0.6%

    Mean: 2.77 → 3.31 (+0.54, SNR 10.5). The share of postings asking for ≥4 categories rose from 27% → 44%. This is independent of the tech_count rise (tech is one category, and 94% of postings already had ≥1 tech in 2024).

    Q2 — Is the rise in requirements just length growth?¶

    Partially. Cleaned description length in the LLM subset rose 627 → 735 chars (+17%). Raw breadth rose +35%. Breadth per 1K chars rose only +6%. So roughly half of the raw breadth rise is length expansion. The other half is genuine requirement addition.

    tech_density is essentially flat (9.75 → 9.60), which directly tells you that per-character tech mentions did not increase — the extra techs come from longer text.

    scope_density, by contrast, rose 85% per 1K chars. So scope language is the thing actually getting packed in more tightly, while tech is diluting.

    Q3 — Entry-level: are they asking for more simultaneous categories?¶

    Yes, under both definitions. 2024 samples are thin and MDE is 8.2 pp per Gate 1; all entry-level numbers should be read with that in mind.

    Under seniority_final = 'entry' (n=376 in 2024, n=610 in 2026):

    metric 2024 2026 Δ
    tech_count 4.32 5.27 +0.95
    requirement_breadth 6.12 7.97 +1.85
    credential_stack_depth_strict 2.48 2.94 +0.46
    ai_term_count 0.11 0.74 +0.64
    desc_len_chars 562 644 +82 (+15%)
    breadth per 1K chars 12.2 13.3 +9%
    breadth excluding AI 6.02 7.23 +1.21 (+20%)

    Under yoe_extracted <= 2 (n=1,688 in 2024, n=1,458 in 2026):

    metric 2024 2026 Δ
    tech_count 4.59 5.62 +1.03
    requirement_breadth 7.15 9.24 +2.09
    credential_stack_depth_strict 3.17 3.78 +0.61
    ai_term_count 0.10 0.81 +0.70
    desc_len_chars 677 772 +95 (+14%)
    breadth excluding AI 7.05 8.44 +1.39 (+20%)

    Both definitions agree in direction and rough magnitude. Entry postings are asking for more distinct requirement CATEGORIES (credential_stack_depth +0.46 and +0.61 respectively), not just more tech within the same category. This supports RQ1b (within-posting scope thickening). The AI term contribution accounts for ~⅓ of the breadth rise but not all of it.

    Q4 — Management indicator validation (the most important step)¶

    Broad pattern FAILS. Strict pattern PASSES but effect is below noise.

    Rates by tier:

    tier 2024 share 2026 share inflation ratio vs strict
    strict 8.5% 9.5% 1.0 (baseline)
    broad 39.0% 41.5% 4.56× in 2024, 4.37× in 2026

    The 3-5× inflation risk flagged in Gate 1 is confirmed. Broad management inflates by ~4.5× over strict in both periods.

    Precision check on 50-row sample:

    • Strict sample (50 rows, 25 per period): by construction 50/50 contain a strict pattern. Precision: 100%.
    • Broad sample (50 rows, 25 per period): 13/50 (26%) also contain a strict pattern. The remaining 37 (74%) are broad-only — they matched only lead, team, stakeholder, coordinate, or leadership without any strict verb like manage, mentor, or direct reports. Broad-only fraction: 74%, meaning broad's "management" signal is 26% strict-backed and 74% generic collaboration language. This fails the ≥80% precision threshold from the analytical preamble.

    Broad-only trigger term distribution (n=37):

    trigger combination n
    team alone 19
    coordinate alone 7
    lead alone 4
    leadership alone 2
    team + stakeholder + coordinate 1
    team + coordinate 1
    stakeholder alone 1
    team + stakeholder 1
    lead + team 1

    Half of broad-only hits are the word team appearing once, typically in phrases like "work closely with the team." This is exactly the inflation pattern Gate 1 warned about.

    Strict-only cross-period comparison (the defensible version):

    • 8.5% → 9.5% in aggregate. Cohen's d = 0.030. SNR vs within-2024 = 0.35. Below threshold.
    • Top strict trigger terms in both periods are mentor (0.038 → 0.059) and manage (0.038 → 0.020). manage actually fell — this is because "management" as a standalone noun was deliberately excluded from the strict pattern, so the remaining verb form fell.

    Verdict: Neither the broad (pattern-fails-validation) nor the strict (below SNR threshold) management signal supports a "management language rose between 2024 and 2026" headline. The closest defensible claim is that mentor mentions rose (0.038 → 0.059, a real +55% relative) — which is a narrower mentoring-language claim, not a general management claim.

    Q5 — Outlier analysis (top 1% by requirement_breadth)¶

    Cutoff: requirement_breadth >= 23 (n=400 postings).

    Top 10 companies among outliers:

    company n outlier postings
    Jobs via Dice (aggregator) 12
    Walmart 8
    Citigroup 7
    Booz Allen Hamilton 6
    JPMorgan Chase & Co. 6
    Expedia Group 5
    Capital One 5
    BlackRock 5
    GEICO 4
    Allegiant 4

    Top outlier concentration is enterprise IT and financial services. One aggregator (Jobs via Dice) is the #1 outlier generator, which supports treating aggregator-derived extreme postings as potentially template-bloated. The rest (Walmart, Citigroup, BAH, JPMorgan, Capital One, BlackRock, GEICO) are large enterprise employers with long structured requirement lists — these are likely real postings that genuinely list 20+ technologies, multiple soft skills, and formal education requirements. The 50-sample inspection (outlier_top1pct_sample.csv) shows the outlier descriptions are long, legitimate, and tech-dense — they're the "full stack + cloud + database + messaging + testing + DevOps + security + data + ML" kind of posting that lists everything and asks for 10+ years of experience.

    Feed to T22 (Wave 3): the Jobs via Dice (aggregator) outliers and any other aggregator in the top 1% should be ghost-flagged. Real enterprise outliers should not.

    Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. tech_density is flat. I expected tech density to rise in 2026 along with the tech count. Instead, the tech count rise is entirely length-explained. Density is a better measure than count for "how much the individual posting sentences are thickening," and on that metric, the answer is not at all.

    2. scope_density rose 85% per 1K chars — scope language (ownership, end-to-end, cross-functional, stakeholder, autonomous, impact, etc.) is getting packed in more tightly, not just mentioned more often. This is the opposite pattern from tech. It's consistent with a soft-scope inflation story: employers are taking the same posting length and filling more of it with scope language.

    3. manage as a verb fell (3.8% → 2.0%) while mentor rose (3.8% → 5.9%). The two top strict management terms moved in opposite directions. This nuance is invisible under a combined management indicator but is potentially meaningful: the 2026 posting may ask engineers to mentor junior team members without "managing" them in the HR sense.

    4. Broad-only management hits are 51% driven by the single word team. Not lead, not stakeholder — just team. This is a stronger indictment of the broad pattern than Gate 1 anticipated; it's essentially counting any posting that uses the word "team" as a management posting, which is nearly all of them.

    5. Credential stack depth 7 (all categories present) rose from 0.06% to 0.6% — a 10× increase. These are postings asking for tech + soft skills + scope + management + education + YOE + AI all in one. Still rare but not negligible.

    6. Entry postings in 2026 mention AI terms ~7× more than entry postings in 2024. 0.11 → 0.74 AI term mentions per entry posting (under seniority_final). This is a large effect at the entry level specifically and should be contrasted with the all-SWE rate (0.09 → 0.80) — entry postings are mentioning AI terms at roughly the same rate as all SWE postings, meaning the AI requirement explosion is not seniority-segregated.

    Data quality caveats¶

    1. 2026-side sample is capped at the LLM subset (~12,534 scraped SWE rows). The primary density metrics are all computed on this subset. The Gate 1 binding constraint on text-based analyses applies.
    2. Entry-level n is thin (376 sen-entry in 2024, 610 in 2026; 1,688 yoe≤2 in 2024, 1,458 in 2026). Gate 1's 8.2 pp MDE constraint still applies to any entry-specific claim.
    3. Management pattern validation was automated, not human-coded. The "precision" figures are based on whether a strict pattern also fires in a broad sample row. A human would refine this further — some broad-only hits might be genuine management language that doesn't happen to include the strict verb list (e.g., "you will be responsible for a team of 5 engineers" doesn't match any strict verb). This is a lower bound on true broad precision, not the true number. Human coding of the 50-sample is a Wave 3 V1/V2 verification task.
    4. The manage strict pattern excludes the standalone noun "management" to avoid matching "project management" etc. That is why 2026 manage share fell despite other management indicators being stable — it is a pattern artifact, not a real decline. The pattern choice is defensible but worth naming.
    5. tech_density is driven by a clamped denominator (chars/1000 floored at 0.1). Very short descriptions (below 100 chars) get their density inflated. These are a small tail but inflate the 2024 mean relative to 2026 slightly.
    6. scope_density SNR of 61.7 is numerically inflated by a near-zero within-2024 denominator. Interpret as "very strong cross-period signal with essentially no within-2024 noise on this metric." The absolute effect (0.52 → 0.96, +85%) is the right number to cite; the SNR ratio is not a confidence measure.

    Sensitivity summary¶

    Dimension Applied Result for headline (requirement_breadth)
    (a) aggregator exclusion yes 6.88 → 6.72 (2024) / 9.27 → 9.29 (2026). Not material.
    (b) company cap 50 yes 6.88 → 6.76 (2024) / 9.27 → 9.38 (2026). Slightly strengthens the cross-period effect.
    (c) seniority operationalization yes Both seniority_final='entry' and yoe_extracted<=2 agree in direction; yoe version gives larger Δ due to bigger sample and slightly different population.
    (d) description text source n/a LLM subset is required for density metrics; raw-text version deferred to T13.
    (e) source restriction partial Primary uses pooled-2024 vs scraped; arshkon-only is the under-powered fallback.
    (f) within-2024 calibration yes SNR reported per metric. Headline metrics (tech_count, breadth, credential_stack, scope_density, ai_term_count) all survive SNR ≥ 2. Management strict (0.35), soft_skill_count (0.22), tech_density (0.70), education_tier (0.32) fail SNR.

    Materiality verdict: requirement_breadth and credential_stack_depth are robust to all applied sensitivities — within 2% of the primary under every alternative. management_broad fails validation. management_strict fails SNR. tech_density and soft_skill_count fail SNR.
