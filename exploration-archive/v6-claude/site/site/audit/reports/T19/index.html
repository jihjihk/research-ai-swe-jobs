    T19 — Temporal patterns & rate-of-change estimation¶

    Agent K, Wave 3. Run date: 2026-04-15. Input: data/unified.parquet.

    One-sentence verdict: The 2024→2026 AI vocabulary shift is genuinely faster than any within-2024 drift by a wide margin (acceleration ratio 3.1-3.2× on AI broad/narrow, 6.7× on copilot, 90× on claude_tool), the scraped window is internally stable enough that the JOLTS macro cooling cannot be driving the cross-period AI findings (cross-period / within-scraped-window ratio is 15-25× for AI metrics), and the one metric where the JOLTS confound IS present — entry share — has a cross-period effect size smaller than within-scraped-window noise (ratio 0.86), confirming Gate 2's "entry share rise is instrument-driven" reading.

    Headline findings¶

    1. Observed source date ranges (not hardcoded): asaniczka 2024-01-12 to 2024-01-17 (5 days), arshkon 2024-04-05 to 2024-04-20 (15 days), scraped 2026-03-19 to 2026-04-13 (date_posted where present) / 2026-03-20 to 2026-04-14 (scrape_date, 26 days). Source midpoints: asaniczka 2024-01-14.5, arshkon 2024-04-12.5, scraped 2026-04-01.0. Elapsed: asa→ars = 0.244 yr; ars→scr = 1.969 yr; asa→scr = 2.212 yr.

    2. Rate-of-change acceleration ratios (cross-period / within-2024):

    Metric Within-2024 rate (per yr) Cross-period rate (per yr) Acceleration
    AI broad (24-term union) +5.5 pp +17.7 pp 3.24×
    AI narrow (\bai\b) +5.7 pp +17.6 pp 3.09×
    Copilot +0.3 pp +2.0 pp 6.67×
    Claude tool +0.02 pp +1.8 pp 90.9×
    Scope (end-to-end / cross-func) +11.7 pp +7.7 pp 0.66× (decelerating)
    Description length (chars) −2363/yr +816/yr −0.35× (sign flip)
    Entry share of all (seniority_final) +10.7 pp −0.3 pp −0.03× (sign flip)
    YOE ≤ 2 share +8.0 pp +1.2 pp 0.15×

    The AI-specific metrics are the only ones where the 2024→2026 rate is meaningfully faster than the within-2024 rate. Every other metric either has a similar rate or flips sign. This is direct evidence that the AI restructuring specifically accelerated during 2024–2026, not that the whole market is uniformly in motion.

    1. JOLTS confound check — within-scraped-window vs cross-period effect sizes. If macro cooling were inflating the cross-period AI estimate, we'd see within-scraped-window effects drifting in the same direction at a similar rate. Instead:
    Metric Within-scraped Δ (early half vs late half) Cross-period Δ Ratio (cross/within-scraped)
    AI broad +0.014 +0.349 24.68×
    AI narrow +0.020 +0.346 17.71×
    Copilot +0.005 +0.039 8.10×
    Description length −117 chars +1606 chars 13.68×
    Entry share −0.007 −0.006 0.86× (confounded!)
    YOE ≤ 2 −0.005 +0.023 4.49×

    AI prevalence is 17–25× larger than anything the macro window can produce over the 26-day scraped period. This is strong evidence that the AI DiD in T18 and the broader RQ3 headline are NOT confounded by JOLTS cooling. Conversely, entry share is within 0.86× of the within-scraped-window noise — the cross-period entry-share effect is literally smaller than the within-window variation, confirming Gate 2's "instrument-driven" reading of the entry-share trend. This is the cleanest statistical statement of the junior-narrowing null result obtained so far.

    1. Within-arshkon stability is good. Splitting arshkon SWE into early and late halves by date_posted: AI broad 0.181 (early) vs 0.163 (late, n=3456) — a 1.7 pp gap, smaller than the pooled 2024 cross-snapshot gap of 3 pp between asaniczka and arshkon. Description length is essentially flat within arshkon (3321 vs 3300). Entry share is 3.7% in both halves. The arshkon snapshot behaves as a coherent window — not a heterogeneous stock/flow mix.

    2. Scraped daily volume has a clear weekly pattern and shows two-backlog days (Mar 20 and Apr 14 with 2448 and 1600 rows — the first and last days). Sundays (Mar 22, Mar 29, Apr 5, Apr 12) have the highest AI-broad rates (0.59-0.64 vs weekday median 0.51) and shorter runs (410-945 rows). Our interpretation: Sundays have fewer new postings, with a selection toward tech-companies that post around the clock; weekend postings overrepresent AI-forward employers. This is a sub-7 pp spread on AI broad, well within the 34.9 pp cross-period effect, so it does not bias the headline.

    3. Day-of-week effects (AI broad, pooled across weeks):

    Day n AI broad AI narrow Desc length
    Sunday 3,068 0.592 0.517 5,403
    Monday 2,179 0.528 0.443 5,416
    Thursday 5,882 0.531 0.456 4,940
    Wednesday 6,850 0.513 0.442 4,795
    Saturday 6,565 0.517 0.448 4,975
    Friday 8,844 0.512 0.443 4,900
    Tuesday 7,493 0.487 0.412 4,538

    Spread: 10.5 pp. Day-of-week is a real but small effect. A future scraped panel analysis should use day-of-week fixed effects.

    1. posting_age_days is essentially unusable. Coverage in scraped SWE is 329 / 40,881 = 0.8%, matching T01's flagged 1.4% for general LinkedIn. Every populated row has posting_age_days = 1. The field cannot support a stock-vs-flow analysis.

    2. Levene's test on description_length across scraped dates (26 days as groups): statistic 5.73, p < 0.0001. Daily description-length variance IS significantly different across dates. But the magnitude of daily-level variance is dwarfed by the cross-period effect (see ratio table above), so it is not a confounding concern for the headline.

    3. The first scrape day (March 20) does NOT behave like an obvious stock backlog: its AI broad (0.493) is below the scraped-window median (0.515), its description length (4872) is close to the median, and its entry share (0.034) is roughly average. We tentatively conclude the scraped window is flow-like throughout, not a stock-then-flow transition. The scraper ran with consistent yield across most days except the expected weekend dip.

    Methodology¶

    Rate-of-change estimation¶

    For each key metric, computed: - rate_within_2024_per_year = (arshkon_value − asaniczka_value) / 0.244 years - rate_cross_ars_to_scr_per_year = (scraped_value − arshkon_value) / 1.969 years - acceleration_cross_over_within = rate_cross / rate_within_2024

    Both rates use observed midpoint date differences, not hardcoded month gaps.

    Key caveat: The within-2024 rate is estimated on a 0.244-year window, so small differences become large per-year rates. An acceleration ratio > 1 does NOT automatically mean the cross-period rate is "large" in absolute terms — it means it is large relative to the noisy within-2024 baseline. We report both the raw rates and the ratio so the reader can judge.

    Within-source stability¶

    arshkon has 12 distinct date_posted values across its 15-day window; we split them into two halves of 6 days each and computed the headline metric set on each half.

    scraped has 26 distinct scrape_date values across the 26-day window; we computed headline metrics per-day then also in a first-half / second-half split for the JOLTS confound comparison.

    JOLTS confound ratio¶

    The comparison is: - Within-scraped delta = (last half mean) − (first half mean) — this estimates the maximum time-within-window effect the scraped snapshot can produce. - Cross-period delta = scraped overall mean − arshkon overall mean. - Ratio = |cross-period delta| / |within-scraped delta|.

    Interpretation: Ratio ≥ 10 means the cross-period effect is an order of magnitude larger than the largest within-window drift — a very conservative threshold for "not confounded". Ratios 1–3 would mean "confounded, findings should be treated as upper bounds."

    Levene's test¶

    scipy.stats.levene(*per_date_groups, center='median') on description_length, n=26 daily groups, one per scrape_date.

    Rate-of-change table (full)¶

    Metric asaniczka arshkon scraped Within-2024 per year Cross-period per year Acceleration
    AI broad 0.154 0.168 0.517 +0.055 +0.177 3.24×
    AI narrow 0.084 0.098 0.444 +0.057 +0.176 3.09×
    Copilot 0.0008 0.0015 0.0403 +0.003 +0.020 6.67×
    Claude tool 0.0002 0.0002 0.0352 +0.0002 +0.0178 90.87×
    Scope (end-to-end / cross-func) 0.210 0.239 0.390 +0.117 +0.077 0.66×
    Desc length mean 3881 3306 4912 −2363/yr +816/yr −0.35×
    Desc length median 3733 2974 4862 −3115/yr +959/yr −0.31×
    Entry share of all 0.011 0.037 0.031 +0.107 −0.003 −0.03×
    YOE ≤ 2 share 0.073 0.093 0.116 +0.080 +0.012 0.15×

    Why description length has a negative within-2024 rate: asaniczka mean length is higher than arshkon mean length (3881 vs 3306). This is a sampling / preprocessing artifact between the two kaggle sources, not a real 2024 trend. The sign flip between within-2024 and cross-period is exactly the signature of a cross-source instrument effect, not a temporal one. We report it honestly rather than pretend length moved monotonically.

    Why scope density has a below-1 acceleration ratio: within-2024 spread is relatively large (0.210 → 0.239 between asaniczka and arshkon over 0.244 yr), producing a within-2024 rate of +0.117 pp/year. The cross-period rate (+0.077/year) is actually smaller in per-year terms, even though the absolute cross-period rise is +15.1 pp. This is a measurement-window effect: short within-window baselines inflate within-2024 per-year rates.

    Why entry share has a sign-flipped acceleration ratio: within-2024, asaniczka→arshkon entry share rose strongly (0.011 → 0.037, +2.6 pp in 0.244 yr = +10.7 pp/year), but this is almost certainly a differential-labeling artifact — asaniczka has zero native entry labels, so its seniority_final entry share is only populated by Stage 10 LLM output. Cross-period entry share then FELL slightly (0.037 → 0.031). The "within-2024 rate is larger than the cross-period rate" signature is exactly the instrument-dominance pattern Gate 2 identified.

    JOLTS confound check¶

    Metric Arshkon early Arshkon late Scraped early Scraped late Within-arshkon Δ Within-scraped Δ Cross-period Δ Cross / within-arshkon Cross / within-scraped
    AI broad 0.181 0.163 0.510 0.524 −0.017 +0.014 +0.349 20.1× 24.7×
    AI narrow 0.114 0.092 0.435 0.455 −0.022 +0.020 +0.346 15.8× 17.7×
    Copilot 0.002 0.001 0.038 0.043 −0.0002 +0.005 +0.039 225× 8.1×
    Desc length 3321 3300 4966 4848 −20 −117 +1606 79.4× 13.7×
    Entry share 0.037 0.037 0.034 0.027 +0.00008 −0.007 −0.006 77.0× 0.86×
    YOE ≤ 2 0.093 0.093 0.118 0.113 −0.0002 −0.005 +0.023 97.9× 4.5×

    Two distinct verdicts:

    • AI metrics pass the JOLTS confound check cleanly. Cross-period rates are 8-25× within-window drift — an order of magnitude above noise.
    • Entry-share fails the JOLTS confound check. The cross-period entry-share delta (−0.006) is smaller in absolute value than the within-scraped-window drift (−0.007). We cannot distinguish the cross-period entry-share effect from noise within a single window. This is the cleanest statistical articulation of the Wave 2 / Gate 2 finding that the entry-share rise is instrument-driven rather than a real labor-market change.

    The second verdict is the single most important T19 finding for the paper: it gives a concrete "ratio below 1" confound-check failure that can be cited against any entry-level narrowing claim.

    Within-arshkon stability¶

    12 distinct date_posted values span 2024-04-05 to 2024-04-20. Two halves:

    Half Dates covered n AI broad AI narrow Desc length Entry share
    Early 2024-04-05 to 2024-04-11 1,235 0.181 0.114 3,321 0.037
    Late 2024-04-12 to 2024-04-20 3,456 0.163 0.092 3,300 0.037

    Within-arshkon is quite stable. The 1.7 pp AI broad drift is noise-level. Description length is within 21 chars (0.6%). Entry share is flat to 4 decimal places.

    Within-scraped-window per-day (26 days)¶

    Full table in exploration/tables/T19/within_scraped_stability.csv. Highlights:

    • Daily posting counts range from 310 (Apr 13, Monday) to 2465 (Apr 1, Wednesday).
    • Six "low days" (<1000 postings): Mar 22, Mar 23, Mar 29, Mar 30, Apr 5, Apr 6, Apr 12, Apr 13 — all weekends + tail Sunday/Monday.
    • AI broad range: 0.476 to 0.641 (16.6 pp spread).
    • Description length range: 4399 to 6065 (1666 char spread, 38%).
    • Entry share range: 0.012 to 0.109 (9.7 pp spread).
    • Unknown-seniority share: 0.363 to 0.625.

    The spread is large enough to detect within-window heterogeneity but small compared to the 35 pp AI broad cross-period effect. The weekend days have systematically higher AI broad prevalence and longer descriptions, pointing to an employer-mix effect (tech employers are more active on weekends than control-style employers). For RQ3 claims based on aggregate averages across the 26-day window, this weekend effect averages out.

    Posting-age analysis¶

    • posting_age_days is populated for 329 of 40,881 scraped SWE rows (0.80%).
    • All 329 populated rows have posting_age_days = 1.
    • The field cannot support a stock-vs-flow analysis as specified in T19 step 4.
    • Coverage gap flag for preprocessing owner: The scraper's LinkedIn posting-age field is being preserved for only ~1% of rows. If this is recoverable in the scraper's raw JSON but being dropped in ingestion, the preprocessing owner should investigate.

    Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. Entry-share cross-period effect is smaller than within-scraped-window drift. This is the cleanest single confound-check failure in the entire exploration so far. Any entry-level claim must cite this — not just the Gate 2 qualitative "instrument-driven" framing, but the quantitative 0.86× ratio.

    2. Scope-metric acceleration ratio is below 1 (0.66×). The within-2024 asaniczka→arshkon scope-density jump (+3 pp over 0.24 yr) is large per-year. The cross-period rise (+15 pp over 2 yr) is large absolute but smaller per-year. Interpretation: scope language may have been on a steady upward trend throughout 2024 that did not accelerate in 2026 — or more likely, this is an instrument difference between asaniczka and arshkon. We cannot distinguish with 3 snapshots. Scope density is therefore a weaker headline than AI prevalence: its rise is real in absolute magnitude, but the rate-of-change test does not cleanly rule out that it was already accelerating in 2024.

    3. Description length has negative within-2024 per-year rate, positive cross-period rate. This sign flip is a dead giveaway that the within-2024 comparison is an instrument artifact (asaniczka has higher mean length than arshkon by ~575 chars with no natural temporal interpretation). Any length-growth claim should use the cross-period rate (+816 chars/year or +1606 absolute) and explicitly disclose that the within-2024 rate is instrument-contaminated.

    4. Sundays have 0.59 AI broad (vs Tuesday's 0.49). Weekend employer mix is meaningfully different — likely tech employers with always-on scheduling vs traditional employers posting on weekdays. Any daily or weekly time-series modeling of scraped data needs day-of-week fixed effects.

    5. posting_age_days coverage is 0.8%, not the 1.4% T01 flagged. Even worse than previously thought. The field is effectively unusable for stock-vs-flow analysis.

    6. arshkon's within-window AI broad trend is slightly NEGATIVE (0.181 → 0.163 from early to late halves, a −1.7 pp drift over 8 days). This is consistent with day-of-week / employer-mix effects dominating a 15-day window, not a real declining AI trend in April 2024.

    7. Levene's test rejects variance homogeneity on description length (p < 0.0001) but the variance differences are completely dwarfed by the cross-period effect, so it is a statistically-detectable but practically-negligible source of noise.

    8. Copilot acceleration 6.7× vs Claude tool acceleration 90.9× — both clearly above 1, but the relative magnitudes reflect base rates. Claude tool had essentially zero in 2024 (0.02%), so its within-2024 denominator is tiny and any cross-period growth produces a giant ratio. Copilot had 0.15% in 2024 and its ratio is more conservative. Both are real effects; the magnitudes of the ratios are not directly comparable.

    Data quality caveats¶

    1. Source date-range asymmetry. asaniczka spans 5 days, arshkon 15 days, scraped 26 days. Within-source analyses are most informative on scraped (most days) and least informative on asaniczka (very short window).

    2. Instrument vs temporal confounding. The within-2024 rate estimates compare asaniczka (2024-01-15 midpoint) to arshkon (2024-04-12.5 midpoint) — different samplers, different platforms' internal pipelines, different search frames, different HTML-stripping behavior. We cannot disentangle instrument difference from real 0.24-year drift. The rate-of-change framework therefore treats within-2024 as an upper bound on instrument noise, and reports acceleration ratios as a conservative signal-to-noise statistic rather than a clean rate comparison.

    3. posting_age_days unusable (0.8% coverage, all = 1). Cannot characterize the posting lifecycle.

    4. Scraped scrape_date is fully populated (100%) but date_posted in scraped is only 0.8% — which is why posting_age_days is so thin. Any "when was this posting created" analysis in scraped must use scrape_date as a proxy.

    5. Controls for day-of-week are not applied in the main rate-of-change computation. If the scraped window had a different day-of-week distribution than arshkon (arshkon is uniform across its 15 days; scraped over-represents weekends relative to weekdays when counting rows), this could produce a small bias. The bias cannot exceed ~5 pp on AI broad given the 10 pp day-of-week spread; our cross-period effect is 35 pp; the day-of-week effect is therefore negligible for the headline but might matter for ±1 pp claims.

    Action items for downstream tasks¶

    1. T26 SYNTHESIS / paper draft: Use the T19 JOLTS confound check (AI broad cross/within-scraped = 24.7×) as the headline "not confounded by macro cooling" statement. Pair it with the entry-share 0.86× ratio as the clean null-result for junior narrowing. Together these are the cleanest macro-robustness statements the exploration produces.

    2. T28/T29: Any domain-stratified or archetype-stratified analyses can assume the cross-period AI effect is not contaminated by JOLTS cooling at a first-order level. Per-archetype JOLTS confound checks would strengthen specific claims but are not blockers.

    3. T27 presentation: The acceleration-ratio table makes a clean slide — AI metrics 3-90×, scope/length/entry all below 1×. Highlights the specificity of the AI finding.

    4. Preprocessing owner: (a) Investigate why posting_age_days coverage is only 0.8% — is this a scraping-field gap or an ingestion-pipeline gap? The field is recoverable for only 329 rows; the remaining ~40k rows may or may not have raw posting-age data in the scraper output. (b) Day-of-week posting volume differences are real and worth tracking — if the scraper runs weekly, employer mix will drift with weekday/weekend mix. A "day-of-week SWE share" panel in the quality report would surface any selection drift.

    5. Future scrapes: Extending the scraped window past 4+ weeks would strengthen the within-scraped-window stability check. At 26 days we have enough for the JOLTS confound check but not enough for a clean trend-vs-noise decomposition within the scraped window.
