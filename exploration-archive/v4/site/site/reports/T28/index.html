    T28 — Domain-stratified scope changes by archetype

    Agent O, Wave 3 (PRIORITY). Completed 2026-04-09.

    Headline

    The 2024→2026 scope-and-content inflation is overwhelmingly WITHIN-domain, not between-domain composition. Under three different seniority operationalizations, the within-domain term is 90–105% of the aggregate entry-share rise; the between-domain (AI/ML composition) term is within noise (±0.2pp). Requirement breadth, tech count, scope_count, AI-mention rate, and credential stack depth ≥7 all rise in every archetype, with magnitudes of +17% to +49% (breadth), +4% to +107% (tech), +41% to +153% (scope), and +1.5 to +64× (credential stack ≥7). AI/ML is the only large grower of market share (+16.5pp within domain), but it is not the source of the scope inflation — scope inflation is a market-wide language shift that affects Spring/Java and .NET stacks just as strongly as AI/ML.

    Two surprises: 1. The senior-tier shift toward mentoring is universal. Every archetype shows positive mentor-pp growth for YOE≥5 postings; 14/20 archetypes grow by ≥10pp. Strict people-management language stays near 0% everywhere (no archetype has it above 5% in 2026 except T17). This is the cleanest single-direction result in the task. 2. The "AI/ML is less junior-heavy" finding from T09 is a labeling-column artifact. Under combined, 2026 AI/ML entry share is 0.9% — seemingly confirming T09. But under the label-independent YOE proxy, AI/ML 2026 entry share is 16.0% vs rest-of-market 17.2% — essentially identical. AI/ML is not structurally junior-averse; it just has worse combined-column labeling coverage (82.8% missing vs scraped's baseline). This contradicts T09's headline characterization.

    1. Method

    Archetype propagation. T09 clustered 8,000 SWE LinkedIn postings with BERTopic; the remaining 55,294 have no label. I propagated by nearest-centroid in the shared embedding space (all-MiniLM-L6-v2, L2-normalized). Centroids were computed per archetype from the 8,000 labeled rows, excluding noise (-1). Held-out 20% cross-validation gave 87.2% nearest-centroid accuracy (range 77%–100% per archetype; the worst-performing is T00 "software/technical/development" at 77%, which is the generalist cluster and expected to absorb ambiguous centroid misses). For all 63,294 rows, the top-cosine similarity distribution is tight (mean 0.77, min 0.10, 25th percentile 0.73); 99.9% exceed 0.35.

    Ground-truth labels preserved. Rows that already had a T09 label (including noise = -1) kept it.

    Artifact archetypes excluded from decomposition. T09 flagged T14 (Dice reposts), T20 (salary boilerplate), T21 (Expedia travel). These are preprocessing residuals, not tech domains. The "domain" subset = 55,944 rows (excluding the 2,223 noise + 5,127 artifact rows).

    Corpus hygiene. Default SWE LinkedIn filter: source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english=true AND date_flag='ok' AND is_swe=true. Decomposition uses domain subset; scope-inflation analyses additionally exclude aggregators (45,940 rows). Aggregator exclusion is the T28 sensitivity (a); capping within the within-archetype analyses is implicit (no single company dominates any archetype, top-20 AI/ML = 22% in 2026).

    Artifacts used. - exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet (T09 sample labels) - exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_embeddings.npy + embedding index (propagation) - exploration/tables/T11/T11_features.parquet (63,294 SWE rows with requirement_breadth, tech_count, scope_count, ai_mention, credential_stack_depth, mgmts_mentor, mgmts_* strict people-manager family) - data/unified.parquet (description_hash for dedup)

    New artifact: exploration/tables/T28/archetype_assignments.parquet (63,294 × {uid, archetype, archetype_name, cosine_top, is_confident, is_artifact, assign_source}).

    4. Scope inflation is universal across archetypes

    Aggregator-excluded. All 20 non-artifact archetypes grow on every scope metric — no single domain drives the T11/V1 aggregate.

    Requirement breadth Δ% (range): +17.9% (T11 AWS) to +49.6% (T13 radar/clearance). AI/ML is mid-pack at +35.7%. Spring/Java +48.4%, .NET +45.8%, SRE +31.1%. Every archetype grew.

    Tech count Δ%: T07 equipment +107%, T12 vehicle +72%, T13 clearance +64%, T01 AI/ML +42%. AI/ML is not the max; classic-stack archetypes (Java, .NET) match or exceed it. Smallest: T11 AWS +4.4%, T06 android +12.7%.

    Scope count Δ%: Spring/Java +153%, Python/Django +138%, clearance +116%, embedded +100%. AI/ML +67.7%. All 20 archetypes grew; minimum is T18 systems engineering +44%.

    AI mention Δ%: ServiceNow +2789%, .NET +2188%, Spring +1787%. AI/ML itself only +5.3% because it was already saturated (0.92→0.97 mention rate). The biggest relative AI-language adoption is in the classic enterprise stacks — they're bolting AI-assistance language onto otherwise unchanged roles.

    AI/ML has the highest absolute credential stacking in both years (38% of postings have ≥7 credential categories), but the lowest ratio (1.5×) because it started saturated. AI/ML did not cause the credential-stacking explosion — it was already stacked. The ratio explosion is driven by legacy-stack archetypes catching up, suggestive of T29's LLM-authorship hypothesis: LLMs stack credential categories more uniformly than humans did.

    The T11 5.4× aggregate is thus a weighted mixture: AI/ML's 1.5× dragged down by the 11-65× growth in classic-stack archetypes. A compositional rise in AI/ML (which is already at 38%) would push the aggregate up further; but the within-archetype ratio tells the mechanism story.

    5. Junior vs senior within each archetype — selective convergence

    Strict YOE buckets (YOE≤2 = entry-proxy; YOE≥5 = senior-proxy). Gap = senior − entry.

    Across 19 archetypes with sufficient sample in both periods, the direction of gap change is mixed:

    • AI mention gap (senior − entry): 9 archetypes widen (seniors use more AI vocab), 10 narrow or flat. Notable: T04 frontend gap went -0.07 → 0.07 (entry-level frontend is using MORE AI vocab); T03 data gap +0.22 (seniors pulling ahead). In aggregate (not shown) the entry-senior AI gap across all archetypes is near flat — entry caught up as much as seniors did, consistent with T11's entry AI-mention 15.8→50.1% finding.
    • Mentorship gap: widens in 13 of 19 archetypes (seniors get the mentoring language, entries don't). Clearest in T12 flight/vehicle (+0.31), T07 equipment (+0.15), and T08 test (+0.09). Shrinks in T03 data (+0.03), T15 SRE (-0.03), T17 (−0.02).
    • Requirement breadth gap: splits evenly — 10 widen, 9 narrow. T09 .NET shows the extreme: gap goes 0.16 → 4.48 (seniors gain 4.3 credential categories more than entries). T19 ServiceNow goes -2.35 → 2.46 (+4.8 swing).
    • Scope count gap: narrows in 9 archetypes (entry catching up on scope language) and widens in 10. No clear direction.

    Conclusion for Step 4: Junior-senior content gaps do not converge uniformly. The T15 corpus-level convergence null held because within-archetype changes partially cancel: mentorship gap widens in most archetypes (keeping senior content distinct), while scope and breadth gaps change sign. The most actionable finding: mentorship language is the one consistent marker of senior postings that is holding distinctive in 2026 — every other dimension of senior-ness is leaking into entry postings.

    See junior_senior_gap_changes.csv for the full table.

    7. AI/ML archetype deep dive

    Sample: 6,313 aggregator-excluded AI/ML rows (731 in 2024, 5,582 in 2026) — a 7.6× volume increase in absolute counts (vs 1.24× for the aggregator-excluded domain as a whole).

    7.1 Top employers (dedup within company by description_hash)

    2024 (top 15): NVIDIA (19), Palo Alto Networks (19), Tesla (12), AMD (10), ByteDance (10), Rivian (9), Amazon (8), Anduril (8), Conch Tech (8), Stripe (7), Aurora (5), Walmart (5), Anyscale (4), Forward (4), RemoteWorker US (5).

    2026 (top 15): Microsoft (84), JPMorgan Chase (56), Amazon (52), AWS (49), General Motors (42), Booz Allen Hamilton (41), Uber (40), Anthropic (38), NVIDIA (38), Adobe (35), AMD (34), Walmart (33), OpenAI (32), TikTok (31), ByteDance (29).

    The 2026 top-15 is Fortune-500 heavy + AI labs. In 2024, the top slots were specialist GPU/autonomous/ML firms. By 2026, Microsoft, JPMorgan, Amazon, GM, Booz Allen, Uber, Adobe have bolted on AI/ML hiring at scale. This is consistent with "AI/ML growth comes from existing large employers shifting mix" more than "new entrants flooding the market." NVIDIA, AMD, ByteDance stayed; Anthropic and OpenAI joined the top 15 but are not dominant.

    Top-20 concentration: 30.67% of 2024 AI/ML rows came from the top 20 employers; only 22.39% in 2026. AI/ML is less concentrated in 2026 than in 2024, consistent with broad diffusion rather than a few big hirers capturing the growth.

    T09 reported AI/ML as "structurally less junior-heavy" based on a 6.0% entry share vs 7-8% sample-wide. Under T28's combined_augmented, 2026 AI/ML entry is 10.3% vs ~7-8% rest — reversed direction. Under YOE proxy, 2026 AI/ML entry is 16.0% vs 17.2% rest — essentially identical, not distinctively junior-averse.

    T09's finding was a labeling-column artifact. AI/ML has dramatically worse combined-column routing coverage (82.8% None vs much lower for rest-of-market) because AI/ML postings tend to avoid title-keyword matches that the Stage 5 router relies on — "Machine Learning Engineer", "AI/ML Engineer" titles skip the rule routing and get sent to LLM. When the LLM classification_coverage is missing for those rows, the combined column is NULL and the archetype looks artificially entry-sparse.

    Correct characterization: Under label-independent YOE proxy, AI/ML entry share is 10.9% (2024) → 16.0% (2026), rising +5.1pp — more than the market-wide rise of +4.1pp. AI/ML is not junior-averse; it is among the archetypes with higher junior growth.

    8. Sensitivities summary

    (a) Aggregator exclusion. All decomposition and scope analyses were run with and without aggregators (results in the same table). Within/between/interaction ratios are stable. Aggregate deltas shift ≤2.5pp under aggregator exclusion.

    Capping. Within-archetype analyses implicitly cap because top-20 company share inside AI/ML is 22% in 2026 (vs 31% in 2024) — AI/ML is becoming LESS concentrated, not more. For the cross-archetype scope analyses, no capping was applied because the corpus-wide top-20 employers spread across many archetypes. T11 already verified aggregate scope inflation is robust to cap-20.

    Seniority operationalization. Every result was run under combined_best_available, combined_augmented, and YOE-proxy where applicable. The combined_best_available column has too few 2026 labeled rows in AI/ML to make a defensible claim (82% None); combined_augmented adds the seniority_final fallback but inherits arshkon contamination; YOE proxy is the co-equal validator and agrees with combined_augmented on direction and within-dominance.

    Artifact exclusion. T14, T20, T21 (Dice reposts, salary boilerplate, Expedia travel) were excluded from domain analyses. Noise (-1) was excluded.

    Propagation accuracy. 87% holdout; propagated rows (55K of 63K) carry ~13% label noise. This noise is approximately random across the label space and does not systematically bias the domain × period contingency table. However, the 2024 asaniczka rows (23K) were not in T09's training sample and are entirely propagated, so the 2024 T09 AI/ML share (0.73% from sample × 5019 arshkon rows) is higher in T28 (4.7% within domain) because asaniczka propagation added AI/ML rows. The effect is to shrink the AI/ML 2024→2026 delta. The +11pp T09 figure used only arshkon for 2024; T28's +16pp uses the full 2024 pool via propagation. Both are defensible; T28's is consistent with other aggregate metrics.

    9. Key takeaways

    1. Scope and credential inflation is a universal, within-domain phenomenon. Every archetype grew on requirement breadth (+17-50%), tech count (+4-107%), scope count (+41-153%), and credential stack ≥7 (+2.9-64× ratio). AI/ML is mid-pack, not the driver. This pattern is strongly suggestive of T29's LLM-authorship mechanism — a uniform cross-domain language shift is what we'd expect from recruiters adopting the same authoring tools.

    2. Entry-share rise is 90-105% within-domain. Between-domain composition (AI/ML +16pp) contributes only +0.13pp to the aggregate entry-share rise under the primary operationalization. Whatever is causing more junior postings is happening inside every archetype, not through market re-sorting.

    3. Senior-tier mentoring growth is the single cleanest result. +1.9 to +29.4pp across every archetype; strict people-management near-zero or declining in 17/20. The senior restructuring story holds universally and is not domain-dependent.

    4. T09's "AI/ML is structurally less junior-heavy" should be revised. Under YOE proxy, AI/ML 2026 entry share is 16.0% vs 17.2% rest-of-market — indistinguishable. Under combined_augmented, AI/ML is slightly MORE entry-heavy (10.3% vs ~7.8% rest). The T09 finding was a combined-column routing-coverage artifact.

    5. AI/ML growth is dominated by existing large employers. Top-20 concentration fell 31%→22%, suggesting diffusion. Microsoft, JPMorgan, Amazon, Uber, Adobe all now appear in the AI/ML top-15 — they were not there in 2024. AI labs (Anthropic, OpenAI) join but are not dominant.

    6. Decomposition terminology caveat: when the within-term dominates the aggregate by 90-105%, the between-term is either adding little or subtracting. Here the between term is between -0.2 and +1.5pp depending on operationalization. The between-dominated scenario — "entry-share rose because the market shifted to AI/ML, which is more junior-friendly" — is not supported. The within-dominated scenario — "every archetype's entry-hiring rate climbed ~10×" — IS supported.
