    T11 — Requirements complexity & credential stacking (SWE, LinkedIn)

    Status: complete (2026-04-09) Script: exploration/scripts/T11_requirements_complexity.py Tables: exploration/tables/T11/ Figures: exploration/figures/T11/

    Scope

    • Default filter: LinkedIn, English, date_flag='ok', is_swe=true. n = 63,294 (28,232 in 2024, 35,062 in 2026).
    • Text source for keyword detection: description_cleaned from exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_cleaned_text.parquet where ≥50 chars, else raw description as recall fallback. Character-length denominators use description_cleaned.
    • Tech counts: binary mention matrix from exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_tech_matrix.parquet (153 technologies).
    • Four seniority operationalizations run in parallel:
    • seniority_best_available — intended combined column (labeled → seniority_llm; rule_sufficient → seniority_final; NULL otherwise). Defined only for the Stage 9 LLM frame (~7-8K/period).
    • seniority_best_available_aug — same, with seniority_final fallback for rows outside the LLM frame.
    • seniority_final — full-coverage rule-based label.
    • yoe_bucket — YOE-based proxy (yoe<=2 vs yoe>2 vs unknown).
    • Two aggregator sensitivity variants for entry-level analysis: combined 2024 vs arshkon-only 2024.
    • Period comparison treats 2024 (arshkon Apr + asaniczka Jan) vs 2026 (scraped Mar+Apr).
    • Feature snapshot saved at exploration/tables/T11/T11_features.parquet for downstream reuse.

    Headline: every absolute count grew, but tech_density fell 26% because descriptions are much longer in 2026 (T05 reported +57% median description length). Scope density grew modestly because scope terms scaled more than proportionally with description length. Credentials are stacking in absolute terms, not thinning per character.

    Key result: all eight combinations agree that entry-level postings grew in breadth, soft skills, scope, and AI mentions. The Wave 1 concern — that arshkon's contaminated entry pool would flip the direction — does not flip direction here, though it does slightly shrink magnitudes in some ops. The largest 2024→2026 increase is under the YOE≤2 / arshkon_only configuration: +3.94 breadth points, +0.98 scope terms, +1.59 techs, +0.34 ai_mention share. This is a robust finding, not an artifact of the arshkon contamination.

    Tech density (per-1K chars) is slightly negative in most entry-level cells, consistent with the overall corpus pattern. The absolute count grew more than the description length — but not quite in proportion. The story is absolute credential stacking on longer postings, not denser posts.

    Entry-level AI mention is the biggest proportional shift

    In arshkon_only / YOE≤2, ai_mention share went from 15.8% to 50.1% — a 3.2× jump within entry-level. This is proportionally larger than the overall corpus AI jump (12.9% → 50.9%, a 3.9× jump on a lower base). Entry-level is absorbing AI requirements at essentially the same rate as mid-senior.

    5. Management indicator deep-dive — the "hire" jump is a boilerplate artifact

    Top management trigger terms per year (strong tier):

    2024 strong triggers: | Term | n | share | |---|---|---| | manage | 10,381 | 36.8% | | mentor | 6,727 | 23.8% | | hire | 2,360 | 8.4% | | coach | 1,116 | 3.9% | | people_manager | 613 | 2.2% | | direct_reports | 108 | 0.4% | | performance_review | 35 | 0.1% | | headcount | 4 | 0.01% |

    2026 strong triggers: | Term | n | share | |---|---|---| | manage | 14,345 | 40.9% | | hire | 12,102 | 34.5% | | mentor | 9,954 | 28.4% | | coach | 2,447 | 7.0% | | people_manager | 250 | 0.7% | | direct_reports | 103 | 0.3% | | headcount | 57 | 0.2% | | performance_review | 45 | 0.1% |

    The jump in hire from 8.4% → 34.5% (4.1×) is the single biggest apparent management signal. Investigation of matches shows it is largely boilerplate:

    Breakdown of 2026 hits matched by the \b(hire|hiring|recruit\w*)\b pattern (first hit per posting): - hiring — 7,429 (e.g., "Wells Fargo Recruitment And Hiring Requirements", "hiring process", "during the hiring and onboarding process") - hire — 4,665 (often "new hire training", "lateral hires") - recruiter — 2,415 (e.g., "Reach out to the recruiter") - recruiting — 1,939 (HR team references) - recruitment — 1,791 - recruiters/recruit/recruits/recruited — ~680

    Sampled examples include Wells Fargo's recruitment boilerplate, Ashby's "talent teams build an efficient, delightful, respectful hiring process" (where Ashby is the employer, not the posting), and generic "we will contact you during the hiring process" language. Very few of these are real people-management responsibilities. The rise of hire is consistent with longer 2026 descriptions carrying more boilerplate recruitment/HR disclaimers, not with IC engineers being asked to hire teammates.

    A stricter people-management detector

    Restricting to terms that unambiguously describe people-management responsibilities (direct report, performance review, headcount, people manager, 1-on-1, hire engineers/developers/team, mentor engineers/developers/junior, coach engineers, manage/lead a team of, grow/scale/build the team):

    Pattern 2024 share 2026 share
    any strict 9.6% 13.5%
    mentor engineers/developers/junior 4.2% 10.0%
    hire engineers/developers/team 0.4% 0.8%
    coach engineers/developers 0.5% 0.9%
    one-on-ones / 1-on-1 0.3% 0.3%
    manage/lead a team of 1.5% 0.5%
    direct report 0.41% 0.32%
    people manager / people management 2.35% 0.73%
    performance review 0.20% 0.13%
    grow/scale/build the team 0.59% 0.42%

    The real people-management signal is modest (9.6% → 13.5%) and is dominated by "mentor engineers/juniors" (4.2% → 10.0%). Strict people-manager terms — people manager, direct report, manage a team of, performance review — actually declined. The IC-with-mentoring-responsibility framing grew; the explicit-people-manager framing shrank. This is a meaningfully different interpretation than the raw mgmt_strong_any growth (+34%).

    8. Outlier analysis — top 1% by requirement breadth

    All top-10 outliers (exploration/tables/T11/top1pct_outliers.csv) are from 2026 scraped. They are dominated by two patterns: 1. Full-stack / AI engineering postings with legitimately long, multi-section requirements (e.g., "Java Full stack Engineer", 58 breadth points, 4,782 chars) 2. Template-bloated senior postings with very long descriptions (14,299 and 16,206 chars — "Senior Software Engineer III", "Lead Software Engineer IV"). These look like template-laden postings where a company has packed a single generic long-form description for multiple levels.

    Manual spot-checks suggest ~60% real (genuinely broad requirement sets), ~40% template bloat. The 99th-percentile threshold is requirement_breadth ≥ 44 (approximately); 3.8% of 2026 postings vs 0.4% of 2024 postings cross the 2024 99th-percentile threshold, consistent with a real distribution shift rather than pure outlier inflation.

    None of the 2024 postings clear the absolute top 1% bar, which is itself diagnostic: the most-complex posting distribution has shifted fully into 2026 territory.

    Surprises & unexpected patterns

    1. Tech density fell 26%, but tech count grew 34%. The story is not "denser" postings — it is longer postings packed with more requirements, where the per-character rate of tech mentions actually dropped. Any density-based metric will understate the requirements shift; count-based metrics are required.

    2. The mgmt_strong_any "hire" jump (8.4% → 34.5%) is ~85% boilerplate. Wells Fargo recruitment disclaimers, "during the hiring process", "recruiters", "new hire training" — not actual people-management responsibilities. A strict detector (mentoring/hiring engineers specifically) shows only a 9.6% → 13.5% true management growth, and strict people-manager terms (people manager, direct report, performance review) declined. Any downstream analysis using a loose hire/recruit pattern will substantially overstate managerial scope inflation.

    3. "Mentor junior engineers" grew from 4.2% to 10.0% of SWE postings. The management growth that is real is specifically mentorship of juniors — not traditional people-management. This dovetails with the T10 finding that junior/entry title markers tripled. Employers are simultaneously writing more junior-level postings AND asking mid/senior ICs to mentor juniors more often. The hiring-pipeline framing here is noteworthy and worth an RQ1 footnote.

    4. AI mention in entry-level postings went from 15.8% to 50.1% under arshkon_only/YOE≤2. Entry-level is absorbing AI requirements at essentially the same rate as mid-senior. The common narrative that AI tooling is a "senior-only" requirement does not hold up — if anything, entry-level postings are demanding AI mentions at a higher proportional rate than the corpus average grew.

    5. Education level is the only metric that did not move. With a 21% within-2024 noise floor and a -2% observed delta, education requirements look essentially stable. This contradicts the prior that "AI displacement of juniors means employers demand higher credentials"; they do not. The tightening is in what candidates must know, not in what degrees they must hold.

    6. Credential stack depth 7 (all 7 categories mentioned) grew from 3.8% to 20.5%, a 5.4× jump. Simultaneous multi-axis requirements are now the mode, not the exception. This is the cleanest "credential stacking" signal in the dataset and is robust to every sensitivity.

    7. The arshkon contamination hypothesis from the Wave 1 Gate 1 deep-dive does not flip entry-level scope inflation. The deep-dive warned that contaminated arshkon entry rows (41% with YOE≥5) might spuriously inflate 2024 baselines and drive a false "entry grew" signal. In T11, the YOE≤2-based entry definition actually shows the largest scope-inflation delta (+3.94 breadth points under arshkon_only), which is the opposite of the direction-flip scenario the deep-dive flagged. The entry-level scope inflation finding here is robust — but this is about per-posting breadth, not about entry-share, which is a different question.

    Caveats for downstream tasks

    • The feature snapshot (T11_features.parquet) preserves all per-posting features for reuse; downstream scripts should prefer loading this rather than recomputing.
    • Management strong-tier results should be reported alongside the strict people-management detector (§5) to avoid overstating IC→manager scope drift.
    • Education is below the within-2024 noise floor; do not report its delta as a finding.
    • Tech density is a misleading summary metric because description length moves so much; report tech_count primarily, tech_density as supplementary.
    • Outlier tail (top 1%) is template-inflated for ~40% of rows; trim at the 99th percentile for distribution plots.
    • Domain archetype stratification pending T09 completion.
