    T08 — Distribution profiling & anomaly detection

    Agent E, Wave 2 (2026-04-10) Script: exploration/scripts/T08_distribution_profiling.py Tables: exploration/tables/T08/ (20 CSVs) Figures: exploration/figures/T08/ (4 PNGs)

    TL;DR

    1. The Gate-1 native-label-quality finding is replicated cleanly (41.3% of arshkon seniority_native = 'entry' rows with extracted YOE have YOE>=5 vs 9.5% in scraped). Label-independent measures (YOE proxy, seniority_best_available of known) agree that entry share is rising, not declining. The "entry decline" under seniority_native/seniority_final with arshkon-only 2024 baseline is a measurement artifact.
    2. The combined seniority_best_available column is not fully comparable across periods because its coverage is structurally driven by LLM frame selection: 99.9% coverage in arshkon (2024), only 34% in asaniczka (2024-01), 21% in scraped (2026). Raw population-level ratios using it are misleading; the "share of known" denominator is the only defensible reading.
    3. The largest single-metric change is core_length (+59%, Cohen d = 0.85) and description_length (+33%, d = 0.57). These effects dwarf the within-2024 calibration noise (d ~0.2-0.3), so they are plausibly real — but the Wave-1 noise floor is non-trivial and cross-source formatting differences contribute substantially.
    4. AI / agentic vocabulary has exploded from near-zero to substantial share: desc_contains_ai 8.7% -> 40.9% (abs +32pp, rel +372%), desc_contains_llm 1.0% -> 10.7% (+931%), desc_contains_agent 0.6% -> 12.8% (+2180%), desc_contains_rag 0.07% -> 4.2% (+5600%), desc_contains_copilot 0.08% -> 4.2% (+5050%). The AI inflection is by far the cleanest cross-period signal.
    5. Surprises: (a) is_remote_inferred is 0.0% everywhere (column appears broken/unused); (b) NYC Metro SWE count 66 (2024 pooled) -> 1862 (2026) — a 28x jump against 1.2x overall growth, almost certainly a scraper geographic-coverage artifact; (c) is_aggregator drops 24.6% -> 14.3%, mostly driven by asaniczka's very high aggregator rate (27%) diluting 2024; (d) industry labelling format changed across sources (2026 uses compound "X, Y, and Z" strings).

    Arshkon leader: IT Services and IT Consulting (24% of labeled rows). Scraped 2026-03 leader: Software Development (26%). This is a real compositional shift in industry coverage across sources, not a market change. Format anomaly: 2026 sources contain compound industry strings like "Information Services and Technology, Information and Internet" and "Accounting, IT Services and IT Consulting, and Business Consulting and Services" — these appear to be LinkedIn's multi-industry tags and are absent from arshkon's label format. Any industry-level cross-period analysis must harmonize these labels.

    Anomaly 1 — is_remote_inferred is 0.0% everywhere. Confirmed by raw inspection: 0 true values out of 63,294. Meanwhile is_remote (source-provided) has 22.5% true in 2026-03, 21.6% in 2026-04, and 0 in 2024. The rule-based ghost heuristic keys on seniority_native = 'entry' + YOE contradictions, so arshkon (the only source with meaningful native entry labels plus inflated-YOE-for-entry rows) is the only source where it fires. Use ghost_assessment_llm for any cross-period comparison; LLM coverage is 62% on arshkon but only 11% on the others, which means ghost-job analyses are underpowered outside the arshkon subset.

    4. Native-label quality diagnostic — replication and extension (table 11_native_label_yoe_profile.csv, figure fig3_native_entry_yoe_profile.png)

    Replicating the Wave-1 finding with clean "share of rows with extracted YOE" denominator (not "share of all rows"):

    Source Level n n_with_yoe share YOE<=2 (of yoe) share YOE>=5 (of yoe)
    arshkon 2024-04 entry 769 475 29.7% 41.3%
    scraped 2026-03 entry 2,785 1,070 81.2% 9.5%
    scraped 2026-04 entry 1,187 482 ~76% ~10%

    Confirmed. Arshkon's "entry" label is structurally contaminated — 41% of its native-entry rows with extracted YOE demand 5+ years. Scraped entry labels look like actual entry (81% have YOE<=2). Any "entry decline 2024 -> 2026" computed with seniority_native or seniority_final (using arshkon-only 2024 baseline) is driven largely by the 2024 baseline including a large contingent of senior roles mislabeled as "entry" on the LinkedIn platform side.

    Extending to other levels:

    Source Level n yoe_mean yoe_median share YOE>=5 (of yoe)
    arshkon associate 275 3.85 3 17.5% (of yoe)
    arshkon mid-senior 2,344 5.56 5 45.2% (of yoe)
    arshkon director 25 7.79 8 68.0% (of yoe)
    scraped associate 795 4.26 3 26.4% (of yoe)
    scraped mid-senior 15,574 5.37 5 46.4% (of yoe)
    scraped director 241 7.83 8 73.0% (of yoe)

    Reassuring: mid-senior and director YOE profiles are nearly identical across sources (5.4 vs 5.6 mean, 46 vs 45% YOE>=5 for mid-senior). The label drift is concentrated in entry — arshkon's native-entry label is corrupt; mid-senior and up are well-behaved. Label-based analyses of mid-senior and above should be safer than label-based entry analyses.

    associate is also noisy: arshkon 17.5% YOE>=5 vs scraped 26.4%. The associate label is unreliable across both sources but the drift direction is opposite to entry.

    6.4 Verdict

    • seniority_native and seniority_final, arshkon-only 2024 baseline: entry share declines 22% -> 14% (of known). This is the Gate-1 result. The deep-dive showed it is mostly a label-quality artifact: 41% of the 2024 baseline is mislabeled senior.
    • seniority_best_available of known: entry share rises 3.0% -> 9.3% (+6.3pp). But the 2024 pooled denominator is heavily biased by asaniczka's tiny LLM-frame subset; the arshkon-only 2024 reading is 7.4% -> 11.2% (scraped 2026-03), which is a ~4pp rise.
    • seniority_imputed of known: entry share rises 2.0% -> 6.7% (+4.6pp).
    • YOE proxy: entry (YOE<=2) rises 10.3% -> 16.6% (+6.3pp) of YOE-known rows. YOE<=3 rises 24% -> 34% (+10pp).

    Three of four operationalizations agree: entry share is rising, and the one dissenter (native/final with arshkon-only 2024) is the one we already know has a contaminated baseline.

    The scraped 2026-04 snapshot has a slightly lower entry share than 2026-03 (11.2% -> 5.5% under best-available). Without more months we cannot tell if this is monthly volatility or a systematic drift; worth monitoring.

    Best defensible claim: entry share has risen modestly in 2026 relative to 2024, by roughly 4-6 percentage points (of known-seniority rows), with the exact magnitude depending on operationalization. This directly contradicts the naive "AI eats juniors" narrative and aligns with the Gate-1 revised reading.

    12. Surprises & unexpected patterns

    Ordered by how much they change the default reading:

    1. Entry share is rising, not falling (§6). Three of four operationalizations plus the YOE-based proxy agree. The one dissenting operationalization is the one we already know has a contaminated 2024 baseline.
    2. Description length growth (+59% core, +33% full) is the largest single-metric change and dwarfs within-2024 calibration noise for the core-length variant. Cohen d = 0.85 is "large" by Cohen's rubric — this is a real, sizeable change in how postings are written.
    3. AI vocabulary explosion — desc_contains_agent at 12.8%, desc_contains_llm at 10.7%, and desc_contains_rag at 4.2% from near-zero. The biggest relative changes in the dataset, and the cleanest cross-period signals (near-zero within-2024 noise).
    4. Largest companies post the most entry roles in arshkon. Q4-by-size has 1.6x the entry share of Q1. Runs against the folk wisdom of startups hiring juniors.
    5. Company set is stable (6,503 -> 6,450 companies) while posting volume per company rises 25%. The expansion is intensive, not extensive. Useful for H1 analysis: within-company dynamics dominate between-company composition.
    6. Mid-senior and director native labels are clean across sources (45-46% YOE>=5 for mid-senior, 68-73% for director). Only entry and associate have the label-drift problem. Label-based analyses of mid-senior+ roles can probably be trusted more than previously assumed.
    7. is_remote_inferred is broken (0/63,294 true) — column never populated. Downstream analyses cannot rely on it; use source-provided is_remote (available for 2026 only, so no cross-period remote analysis is possible without re-running the text inference).
    8. NYC Metro 28x growth versus the overall 1.2x — scraper geographic-coverage artifact, not a market change. Same-metro cross-period comparisons should exclude or downweight NYC.
    9. Industry label format changes across sources (compound multi-industry strings in 2026). Any cross-period industry analysis needs harmonization.
    10. Asaniczka's high aggregator share (27%) drives most of the apparent "aggregator decline" between periods. The real between-period delta is much smaller (11.8% arshkon vs 14.3% scraped — essentially flat).
    11. Scraped 2026-04 entry share (5.5% under best-available) is much lower than 2026-03 (11.2%) — worth monitoring with more months of scraped data.
