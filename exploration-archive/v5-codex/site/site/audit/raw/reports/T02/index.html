    T02 Seniority Comparability & Label Quality¶

    Headline finding¶

    kaggle_asaniczka associate is not a usable junior proxy. It does not line up cleanly with arshkon entry on title overlap, title cues, YOE, or seniority_final. The best description is mixed, leaning lower-mid: its exact titles overlap more with arshkon mid-senior than with arshkon entry, while its YOE profile sits between entry and associate rather than matching either one cleanly.

    Methodology¶

    I compared the SWE-only native-label subsets that matter for the question:

    • kaggle_asaniczka rows with seniority_native = 'associate'
    • kaggle_arshkon rows with seniority_native IN ('entry', 'associate', 'mid-senior')

    I used exact title_normalized overlap, title-cue rates from a small regex audit, yoe_extracted distributions, and seniority_final conditional on native label. I treated associate as its own diagnostic token, not as a junior cue, so the junior-cue rate is not tautologically inflated.

    The exact-title signal points away from a junior proxy. On unique normalized titles alone, asaniczka associate is closest to arshkon mid-senior, not arshkon entry.

    Entry rows are mostly title-keyword driven, not LLM-driven:

    source title_keyword llm
    arshkon 92 83
    asaniczka 123 35
    scraped 923 119

    Among entry rows with YOE available, every one is yoe_extracted <= 2. That is a sign that seniority_final = 'entry' is high precision, but it also means the bucket is conservative and recall-limited.

    Surprises and unexpected patterns¶

    • asaniczka associate has very weak explicit junior signaling in the title text. The junior-cue rate is under 1%, essentially the same order as the arshkon comparators.
    • Exact title overlap is more supportive of a lower-mid or mid-senior interpretation than a junior one.
    • The final entry bucket is tiny but very clean on YOE when YOE is present. That is useful for precision, but it means entry-level trend work will be recall-sensitive.
    • arshkon native entry is not a perfect gold standard either: most of those rows still resolve to seniority_final = 'unknown'.

    Data quality caveats¶

    • seniority_native is not comparable across sources for entry-level work because asaniczka has zero native entry labels by construction.
    • The title-cue patterns are only a diagnostic. They are helpful for triangulation, not a standalone label.
    • YOE coverage is thin enough that sample-size language matters. The final verdict should rest on the pattern across title overlap, YOE, and seniority_final, not on any single statistic.

    Verdict¶

    kaggle_asaniczka associate should not be used as a junior proxy. If forced into a coarser interpretation, it is best treated as a mixed lower-mid bucket: it carries some junior-like YOE mass, but its exact titles and seniority_final placement are too shifted toward mid-senior / unknown to stand in for entry.

    Downstream action items¶

    1. Use arshkon entry as the junior baseline for any seniority-stratified work.
    2. Do not pool asaniczka associate into a junior-only bucket.
    3. When an analysis needs more power, consider a separate lower-mid / ambiguous bucket instead of relabeling asaniczka associate as junior.
    4. For any entry-level result, report the YOE proxy and the arshkon-only native-label diagnostic alongside seniority_final.
