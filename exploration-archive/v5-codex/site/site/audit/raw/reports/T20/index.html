    T20 Seniority Boundary Clarity¶

    Headline finding¶

    The seniority ladder is measurable, but it is not uniformly sharp. The clearest boundary is associate -> mid-senior; the weakest is entry -> associate, especially in 2024 raw text. The explicit junior label remains conservative relative to the YOE proxy, so the junior-like pool is larger than seniority_final = entry.

    What we learned¶

    • On the LinkedIn SWE frame with known seniority, explicit entry is 4.5% of rows, while yoe_extracted <= 2 is 6.1%.
    • The raw-text boundary model is strongest for associate -> mid-senior in both periods, with mid-senior -> director next and entry -> associate weakest in 2024.
    • The cleaned-text sensitivity keeps the same ordering, but it makes the entry boundary look less random in 2024 and slightly softens the top boundary in 2026.

    Boundary discriminability¶

    • Raw entry -> associate: AUC 0.505 in 2024 and 0.718 in 2026.
    • Raw associate -> mid-senior: AUC 0.764 in 2024 and 0.773 in 2026.
    • Raw mid-senior -> director: AUC 0.707 in 2024 and 0.665 in 2026.
    • Cleaned sensitivity: entry -> associate 0.658 -> 0.712, associate -> mid-senior 0.724 -> 0.761, mid-senior -> director 0.703 -> 0.599.

    Missing middle¶

    associate is thin and mixed. In mean feature space it sits closer to entry than to mid-senior in both periods, but the classifier boundary to mid-senior is much more stable than the boundary to entry. That makes associate a transition label, not a clean junior proxy and not a clean mid-level bridge.

    Feature drivers¶

    The strongest separators are yoe_extracted, log_description_length, scope_density, tech_count, ai_any, and education_level. Management density is weaker than the other features and should not be treated as the main boundary signal.

    Domain-stratified boundary analysis¶

    The domain slice is only partially identifiable. In 2026, mid-senior -> director is modelable for AI/LLM, Frontend, Data, and Infra; Embedded is too thin. Data is the sharpest of the modelable domains (AUC 0.720), then Infra (0.688), AI/LLM (0.674), and Frontend (0.628). In 2024, almost all domain cells are too sparse except Embedded and one weak Data estimate, so domain-level blur/sharpening should stay cautious.

    Sensitivity checks¶

    • Aggregator exclusion does not change the boundary ordering.
    • The cleaned-text subset is smaller, but it does not reverse the main result.
    • The junior comparison must travel with the YOE proxy; seniority_final is the strict explicit subset, not the full junior-like pool.

    Narrative evaluation¶

    The initial junior-collapse framing is still not the best story. The more defensible reading is that the explicit seniority ladder remains usable, but the junior side is conservative and the middle boundary is the stable one. If the paper needs a seniority claim, it should lead with measurement asymmetry rather than disappearance.

    Current positioning¶

    T20 is a validity and boundary-structure result. It supports using seniority_final as the explicit label backbone, but it also says junior analyses must keep the YOE proxy beside it. The main downstream implication is that associate should be treated as a thin transition class, not as a substitute for entry.
