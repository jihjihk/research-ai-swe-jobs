    Data sources and samples¶

    Data corpus facts¶

    • 68,137 SWE LinkedIn rows under the default filter: is_swe = TRUE AND source_platform = 'linkedin' AND is_english = TRUE AND date_flag = 'ok'.
    • Split: arshkon 4,691 + asaniczka 18,129 + scraped 45,317.
    • Three temporal snapshots:
    • asaniczka — 2024-01-12 to 2024-01-17 (6 days).
    • arshkon — 2024-04-05 to 2024-04-20 (15 days).
    • scraped — 2026-03-20 to 2026-04-18+ (30+ days, growing).
    • Cross-source span: arshkon to scraped midpoint ≈ 791 days (2.17 years), encompassing ~8 major frontier-model releases (GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, o1, DeepSeek-V3, GPT-4.5, Claude 3.6 Sonnet, Claude 4 Opus, Gemini 2.5 Pro).
    • LLM-labeled coverage: 2024 sources 99.9%; scraped 56.9% labeled (not_selected 43.0%, deferred 0.02%).

    Three sources¶

    Source Platform Temporal role Strength Key gap
    Kaggle arshkon LinkedIn 2024 snapshot Has native entry labels Small SWE count
    Kaggle asaniczka LinkedIn 2024 snapshot Large volume No native entry labels
    Scraped (ongoing) LinkedIn + Indeed 2026 current window Fresh; search metadata Query-stratified

    LinkedIn is the primary analysis platform. Indeed is used only for sensitivity. Both Kaggle sources are LinkedIn-only, making LinkedIn the cleanest cross-period surface.

    Seven analytical samples¶

    Per SYNTHESIS.md §4.

    Sample 1 — Full SWE LinkedIn frame¶

    • Rows: 68,137 under default filter.
    • Use: descriptive + prevalence claims, within-2024 SNR calibration, cross-period raw pp deltas.
    • Key caveat: scraped n=45K is query-stratified by scraper design. Within-SWE share claims are safe; raw SWE-share comparisons cross-period are not valid.

    Sample 2 — Returning-companies cohort¶

    • Rows: 13,437 (2024) + 24,927 (2026) = 38,364 rows. 2,109 firms present in 2024 and 2026. 55% of 2026 postings; 25% of 2026 unique firms.
    • Artifact: exploration/artifacts/shared/returning_companies_cohort.csv.
    • Use: sampling-frame sensitivity (T37), between-vs-within decomposition. 13 of 15 headlines retain ratio >= 0.80 on this cohort.

    Sample 3 — T16 overlap panels¶

    Three panels for within-company longitudinal analysis:

    Panel name Threshold N firms
    arshkon_min3 arshkon n >= 3 AND scraped n >= 3 243
    arshkon_min5 arshkon n >= 5 AND scraped n >= 5 125
    pooled_min5 pooled (arshkon+asaniczka) n >= 5 AND scraped n >= 5 356

    Within-company AI-strict rewriting: arshkon_min5 +8.34 pp; pooled_min5 +7.65 pp; T37 returning-cohort +7.91 pp. Consolidated range +7.7 to +8.3 pp.

    Caveat: the pooled panel is asaniczka-dominated (asaniczka 4x arshkon). Asaniczka has an LLM-frame selection artifact; report both arshkon-only and pooled magnitudes for senior claims.

    Sample 4 — T31 same-company-same-title pair panel¶

    • Rows: 23 pairs (arshkon_min3, n >= 3 per pair-period); V2 replicates n=37 (relaxed filter +9.98 pp) or n=12 (strict arshkon-only +13.3 pp).
    • Pattern: ai_strict_v1_rebuilt.
    • Use: within-firm same-title AI drift.
    • Verdict: range-report +10 to +13 pp; direction (pair > company) robust.

    Sample 5 — T30 LLM-frame labeled-only subset¶

    • Rows: 48,277 under llm_classification_coverage = 'labeled'.
    • Use: YOE-based J3/S4 primaries require this sample.
    • Caveat: scraped 2026 is 56.9% labeled; 43% not_selected. Text-sensitive analyses must report labeled-vs-not split.

    Sample 6 — T09 archetype panel¶

    • Rows: 8,000 T09-labeled + 40,223 projected + 19,914 unassigned.
    • Artifacts: exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet and exploration/tables/T28/T28_corpus_with_archetype.parquet.
    • Use: archetype-stratified analyses.
    • Caveat: 17% direct label + 83% projected labels; noise-outlier cluster (46.8% of BERTopic output) is "stack-agnostic senior SWE."

    Sample 7 — T22 + T34 interview exemplar sample¶

    • Rows: T22 top-20 ghost entry + T34 cluster-0 20 exemplars = 40 postings.
    • Use: RQ4 interview artifacts (T25).

    Default filter¶

    WHERE is_swe = TRUE
  AND source_platform = 'linkedin'
  AND is_english = TRUE
  AND date_flag = 'ok'

    Key seniority codes¶

    Code Definition
    J1 seniority_final = 'entry' (label-based)
    J2 seniority_final IN ('entry','associate') (label-based)
    J3 yoe_min_years_llm <= 2 (YOE-based, primary junior)
    J4 yoe_extracted <= 2 (rule-based YOE)
    J5 title keyword: intern/junior/jr/new grad
    J3_rule J3 OR rule-based title keyword
    S1 seniority_final = 'mid-senior' (label-based)
    S2 seniority_final = 'director' (label-based; thin; use diagnostically only)
    S3 seniority_final IN ('mid-senior','director') (label-based)
    S4 yoe_min_years_llm >= 5 (YOE-based, primary senior)
    S5 yoe_extracted >= 5 (rule-based YOE)
    S4_rule S4 OR rule-based title keyword (senior/staff/principal/lead/architect)

    T30 panel cross-tabs 13 of these definitions against 6 analysis groups; 12 of 13 direction-consistent. See exploration/artifacts/shared/seniority_definition_panel.csv (78 rows).
