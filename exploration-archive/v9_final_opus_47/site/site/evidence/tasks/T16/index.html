    T16 — Company Hiring Strategy Typology¶

    Agent J · Wave 3 · 2026-04-20

    Scope: SWE, LinkedIn-only, default filter (source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english=true AND date_flag='ok' AND is_swe=true). Denominator n=68,092 SWE LinkedIn posting-level rows across 12,872 canonical companies.

    All patterns V1-validated (mgmt_strict_v1_rebuilt, ai_strict, V1 scope).

    TL;DR¶

    1. Panel sizes match V1 references exactly. arshkon∩scraped ≥3 = 243 companies; arshkon∩scraped ≥5 = 125; pooled∩scraped ≥5 = 356. Gate 2 Tension A is reproduced by construction, not papered over.
    2. J3 within-vs-between decomposition is panel-specific, exactly as Gate 2 required. arshkon_min5 within-co ≈ 0 (−0.12 pp), between-co +5.14 pp. pooled_min5 within-co +4.47 pp, between-co +3.43 pp. Both numbers are correct; they measure different things. Both are reported with n, volume, and denominators per task.
    3. Same-co senior-vs-junior scope inflation (Gate 2 preflight 6). S4 within-co breadth_resid +2.18 (arshkon_min5) / +2.32 (pooled_min5) dominates J3 +1.94 / +1.43. Scope inflation is universal, senior magnitudes larger — verified on same-co restriction. Scope term count same-co: S4 +0.55 > J3 +0.42 (arshkon_min5).
    4. Within-firm AI rewriting is the cleanest single signal. AI-strict within-co: arshkon_min5 +8.34 pp, pooled_min5 +7.65 pp. Between-co component is negative on both panels (−0.57, −0.37) — the firms that entered 2026 without 2024 presence actually have slightly lower AI mention rates than the returning cohort. The +7.3 pp aggregate AI rise is entirely within-firm, not composition.
    5. New market entrants (n=6,368 companies, 45% of 2026 postings) have LOWER J3 entry share than returning (12.8% vs 15.3%) but HIGHER AI-strict (12.5% vs 9.3%). This contradicts Gate 1's prior that new entrants drive the junior rise — new entrants are AI-forward more than junior-forward.
    6. Four defensible strategy clusters on pooled_min5 (k=5). traditional_hold (49% of overlap, two sub-clusters), scope_inflator (23%), ai_forward_scope_inflator (5%, n=16; 38% aggregators), mixed_change (12%).
    7. Aggregator-exclusion moves numbers <20% on all metrics. Within-co J3 pooled_min5: +4.47pp → +4.83pp without aggregators. Within-co scope +0.47 → +0.45. AI-strict within-co +7.65 → +6.92. Robust.
    8. Management (V1 rebuilt) is near-flat within-firm on both junior and senior same-co — under 0.02 pp. Consistent with Gate 2's "provisional until T22" framing.

    3. Within-company vs between-company decomposition — Gate 2 priority¶

    Symmetric-weight (Oaxaca-like) decomposition on the full SWE frame, restricted to companies in each panel. within_delta sums company-level metric changes weighted by the symmetric volume share; between_delta captures share re-weighting. total_delta is the aggregate (arshkon-panel deltas are computed against arshkon-2024 only; pooled-panel deltas use pooled-2024).

    Panel Aggregate Δ Within-co Between-co n overlap 2024 labeled denom 2026 labeled denom
    arshkon_min3 +4.82 pp −0.24 pp +5.06 pp 225 1,361 5,863
    arshkon_min5 +5.02 pp −0.12 pp +5.14 pp 121 1,045 4,724

    Reading: both numbers are correct. On arshkon-only companies, the J3 rise is entirely market recomposition (new firms entering the 2026 frame with higher entry posting rates). On the pooled-min-5 frame, within-firm rewriting is ~56% of the aggregate. V1's Gate 2 resolution is reproduced.

    Sensitivities: J1 (seniority_final='entry') and J2 ('associate') on pooled_min5 show small positive within-co (+1.1 pp J1, +0.15 pp J2). The within-co J3 direction is consistent with J1/J2 direction on pooled, and inconsistent with J1 on arshkon (J1 arshkon_min5 within −0.38 pp) — likely because arshkon has the strongest LLM-frame selection artifact at the label level.

Here, using V1-validated ai_strict on the cleaned-text artifact, the within-co effect is +8.3 pp on arshkon_min5. The direction and "AI is within-firm" conclusion are robust, but the magnitude in Gate 1 was inflated by the broader (less precise) pattern. The ratio of within to aggregate is ≥1 on all three panels — AI rewriting is robustly within-firm, but report the V1-validated magnitude.

    Companies in 2026 with no 2024 match (new entrants) = 6,368; returning = 2,109; 2024-only = 4,395.

    Surprise. Gate 1 posited new entrants drive the junior-share rise. This is false on 2026 snapshot: new entrants have lower J3 share (12.8% vs 15.3%) and higher AI-strict (12.5% vs 9.3%). Between-co components report is correct; the interpretation changes from "new entrants bring juniors" to "firms that exited the 2024 panel were lower-J3 than firms that stayed."

    10. Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. New entrants are less junior-heavy than the returning cohort (J3 12.8% vs 15.3%), reversing the Gate 1 narrative. The +5 pp between-firm J3 movement on arshkon panels is driven by exit, not entry. This reframes the RQ1-revised composition story: it's not "new firms arriving as entry specialists," it's "firms with lower-than-typical J3 share dropped out of the 2026 LinkedIn panel." Whether this is real exit (firms stopped hiring SWEs) or scraping-coverage change is Wave 3.5 T37's job.
    2. AI-forward-scope-inflator cluster (n=16) is 38% aggregators (VDart, ApTask, Talener, Intellectt, SSG, Sev1Tech). The extreme JD-broadening cluster is partly a staffing-firm writing-style artifact, not pure demand-side restructuring. Three of the 16 are defense/health-care enterprises (Bristol Myers Squibb, Honeywell, CAE) — NOT big tech, NOT consumer internet.
    3. Within-firm AI magnitude (+7.7 to +8.3 pp) is 4× smaller than T06's +31 pp. V1-validated ai_strict gives cleaner but more conservative numbers. The direction ("AI is within-firm") holds on every panel, but the absolute magnitude claim of "+31 pp within-firm" from Gate 1 was an artifact of the pre-V1 broader pattern. Gate 3 synthesis should cite the +7.7–8.3 pp range.
    4. Management language (V1 rebuilt) is flat within-firm. Even on the sample where T11 reported a −0.02 to −0.04 decline, the rebuilt pattern shows essentially zero within-firm movement on both J3 and S4. T22 validation is the gate, but the "senior management → orchestration" narrative has no within-firm trace on the rebuilt pattern.
    5. Scope inflator cluster has slight J3 DECLINE (−2.9 pp). The firms broadening their JDs are simultaneously narrowing junior posting rates. The "scope inflation = junior bar raised" reading collapses: scope is inflating at the SENIOR level, and the firms doing it are actually posting FEWER J3 roles. Cluster evidence is another nail in the "junior-specific scope inflation" framing.
    6. Mixed_change cluster (n=41) has +40.8 pp J3 jump. Tech-giant intern/new-grad cycle companies. Amazon, AWS, TikTok, JPMorgan Chase, Walmart, and the returning Google all return to 2026 with heavy new-grad hiring. This is not universal market reshaping — it's large-employer campus cycle timing.
    7. Direct employers' 2024 breadth resid was −1.41 (deeply below average) → +0.03 in 2026. The direct-employer 2024 baseline was much narrower than mean; the rise is a "catch-up to aggregator verbosity" phenomenon. Interpretive caveat: length growth in direct employers is partially a convergence on a staffing-firm writing style.

    11. Handoff notes¶

    To Wave 3.5 T31 (same-co × same-title drift, H_M)¶

    Primary panel: pooled_min5 (n=356). company_change_vectors.csv has per-company deltas needed for pair-level drift analyses. same_co_senior_vs_junior.csv is the seniority-slice baseline. Directional expectation: within-co title-controlled entry share Δ should remain non-zero on pooled (Gate 2 Tension A), decay toward zero on arshkon_min5 (V1 finding).

    To Wave 3.5 T37 (sampling-frame returning-cohort sensitivity, H_H)¶

    Overlap panel + new-entrants analysis is the primary input. Key question: does restricting to arshkon_min5 vs pooled_min5 flip any Wave 2 headlines? T16 shows it flips J3 within-co sign (0 → +4.5 pp), leaves AI within-co direction intact (+8.3 → +7.7), and preserves scope/breadth within-co magnitudes. T37 must decide which is the analysis-plan-registered primary.

    To Wave 3.5 T38 (hiring-selectivity × scope correlation, H_N)¶

    company_change_vectors.csv provides the scope delta (breadth_resid_delta, org_scope_delta) and the volume proxy (2026/2024 post-count ratio derivable from overlap_panel). The scope_inflator cluster (n=81) is the "elevated selectivity candidates"; mixed_change (n=41) is the "volume-up with entry focus" candidates.

    To Gate 3 synthesis¶

    • Gate 2 Tension A reproduced cleanly. Use pooled_min5 as primary with arshkon_min5 as co-primary (V1 recommendation).
    • Senior-scope-inflation-greater-than-junior claim verified on same-co restriction.
    • Scope inflator cluster composition is not junior-specialist-dominated.
    • Within-firm AI magnitude: report +7.7 to +8.3 pp not +31 pp.
    • New-entrants interpretation is exit-driven, not entry-driven — reframe composition story.
