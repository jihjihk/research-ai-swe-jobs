    T08 — Distribution profiling & anomaly detection¶

    Agent: E Run date: 2026-04-20 Inputs: data/unified.parquet (SWE LinkedIn universe; 68,137 rows), exploration/artifacts/shared/* Default filter: is_swe AND source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english AND date_flag='ok' Scripts: exploration/scripts/T08_distribution_profiling.py, T08_supplemental.py, T08_within_between.py, T08_within_between_strict.py Tables: exploration/tables/T08/*.csv (28 CSVs) Figures: exploration/figures/T08/fig1_desc_length.png, fig2_yoe_llm.png, fig3_seniority_final.png, fig4_effect_ranking.png

    Headline finding¶

    The junior-share rise 2024→2026 is not uniformly between-company as Gate 1 claimed — the "within-company ≈ 0" claim is specific to the arshkon-only ≥5 panel (n=125) and does not replicate under pooled, asaniczka-only, or looser size cuts, where within-company J3 rise is +3.5 to +6.4 pp (weighted) and accounts for most of the aggregate delta. The Gate 1 decomposition is materially sensitive to overlap-panel construction. Separately: the pattern inverts within senior-titled postings (sr/senior SWE titles show J3 drop from 4.3% → 2.5% while generic SWE rises 8.6% → 15.9%), suggesting role redefinition, not pure composition. AI-mention rise (+16.7 pp aggregate, +14.2 pp within-company on returning cohort) is robust to length, labeled-coverage, and aggregator sensitivities.

    1. Methodology¶

    • Universe: 68,137 SWE LinkedIn rows under default filter. arshkon 4,691 (period 2024-04); asaniczka 18,129 (2024-01); scraped 45,317 (2026-03/04).
    • LLM labeled coverage: 2024 sources 99.9%+; scraped 56.9% (not_selected 43.0%, deferred 0.02%). The scraped labeled subset is the only place yoe_min_years_llm exists (table labeled_split_by_source.csv).
    • Primary seniority operationalizations: J3 yoe_min_years_llm ≤ 2 (labeled), S4 yoe_min_years_llm ≥ 5 (labeled). J1 / J2 use seniority_final. All four junior definitions and all four senior definitions reported as T30 panels.
    • Baseline calibration SNR: loaded from calibration_table.csv and joined to every cross-period finding.
    • Company-cap sensitivity: 20 rows per company_name_canonical per period (junior_senior_cap20.csv). Aggregator exclusion, entry-specialist exclusion, strict SWE-tier subset are additional sensitivities.

    7. Data quality caveats¶

    1. Scraped LLM labeled coverage is 56.9% (25,816 of 45,317). Any analysis using yoe_min_years_llm, description_core_llm, or ghost_assessment_llm on scraped is computed on a subset. The supplemental check (A12) shows AI-strict rate is nearly identical labeled vs not_selected — so the labeled subset is not obviously biased for AI signals. But J3 shares are defined only on labeled.
    2. Arshkon seniority_native='entry' is not clean (A1). The seniority_native column should be retired from sanity-check duty entirely.
    3. Industry labels drifted (A11). Do not stratify cross-period by raw company_industry.
    4. Remote flags are uniformly 0 (A9). Use is_remote_inferred only.
    5. Asaniczka has no native entry labels (A14, already known from T01).
    6. Within-company decomposition depends strongly on overlap-panel construction (A5). Any "within vs between" claim must specify min_n filter and source subset.
    7. Company cap sensitivity is material (A2). Any aggregate share claim should report cap-20 alongside.

    9. Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. Gate 1 "J3 within-co ≈ 0" does not replicate outside the arshkon-only n=125 panel. On pooled min1/min3/min5/min10 and asaniczka-only min5, within-company J3 rise is +3.5 to +6.4 pp weighted — the majority of the aggregate delta. The Gate 1 framing of "junior rise is a composition story, AI rise is within-firm" needs to be softened. Junior rise has both components, and the within-company share is material once you move off the arshkon-only high-volume panel.

    2. Arshkon baseline J3 is HIGHER than asaniczka (13.0% vs 8.2%) — reverse of what's in the orchestrator mental model. In calibration, the within-2024 asymmetry on J3 (4.75 pp) is comparable to the cross-period effect (5.04 pp). Under arshkon-only baseline, J3 rise collapses to +1.2 pp — well below noise. This sharpens the Gate 1 "magnitude fragility" warning from senior-side to junior-side as well.

    3. Senior-SWE-titled postings (sr/senior) J3 share DROPS from 4.3% → 2.5% while generic-SWE J3 share RISES from 8.6% → 15.9%. This is not visible in the T30 panel because S4 and J3 definitions collapse title variation. Title-family stratification reveals the market is bifurcating: senior-labeled roles are tightening experience bar, generic SWE roles are absorbing juniors. Combined with the description_length growth in senior-titled postings (+1,400 chars), this looks like "scope inflation within senior bucket, junior absorption into generic."

    4. Arshkon seniority_native='entry' has 45% of rows at ≥5 YOE and median YOE of 4. This was assumed to be a usable cross-sectional baseline; it is not. The native classifier equates "entry" with "early-career" broadly, not with ≤2 YOE. T01's "arshkon entry = 15.7%" and "seniority_final entry = 3.8%" gap is explained: the native-entry pool is 5-year-median people labeled as "entry" at the source. Recommendation: seniority_native should not be used as a sanity check in Wave 2+ at all, not even on arshkon.

    5. AI-strict prevalence is 18.1% on labeled and 18.2% on not_selected scraped rows. The LLM-routing decision is essentially orthogonal to AI content presence. This is strong reassurance that the labeled-only AI findings are not subset-biased. But it is a sobering note for interpretation: the LLM cleanup is NOT filtering AI-dense vs AI-sparse rows, so comparisons of AI rates between labeled and not_selected subsets should not expect to find coverage artifacts.

    6. Length-matched counterfactual: only 7.7% of the AI-strict rise is length-driven. A bulk of the AI signal survives even if 2026 posts were shortened to 2024 length distribution. This is powerful evidence that the AI rise is content, not boilerplate bloat.

    7. ML engineer volume grew 8.4× (381 → 3,193). This is far more than the broader SWE growth (68,137 → 45,317 total is ~2×). ML engineer is a major composition shift in 2026; paper MUST source-stratify ML/AI-specific claims by the arshkon-vs-asaniczka-vs-scraped split and note the 19.6 pp cross-source ML-SWE classification gap (T04).

    8. Company_size quartile 1 vs Q4 on arshkon: J3 share 10.7% vs 16.6% — large employers post MORE entry roles. This is the opposite of the intuition that startups hire more juniors (arshkon only captures company_size, so this is 2024-only). AI-strict share also scales with size (3.2% Q1 → 5.0% Q4). If the 2026 composition shifts toward larger employers (returning-cohort dominated by big tech), these size effects explain some of the aggregate J3 and AI rises.

    9. The "unknown" seniority_final share is 50–53% in arshkon and scraped, but 34% in asaniczka. Asaniczka LLM routing found more explicit seniority signals. Possible mechanism: asaniczka postings are more template-driven. This is an LLM-routing artifact noise floor that every seniority share analysis inherits.

    10. NYC metro share jumps 0.4% → 6.5% between 2024 and 2026. This is too large for organic market change. It is almost certainly scraper query design (NYC was barely queried in 2024 data sources). Any NYC-specific claim must be excluded from cross-period trend narratives. CA (+6.6 pp), TX (+3.8 pp), WA (+3.0 pp) are more defensible trends.
