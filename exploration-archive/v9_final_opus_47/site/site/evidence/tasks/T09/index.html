    T09. Posting archetype discovery — methods laboratory¶

    Run date: 2026-04-20 Owner: Agent F, Wave 2 Input: exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_cleaned_text.parquet, swe_embeddings.npy, swe_tech_matrix.parquet, data/unified.parquet (titles only), company_stoplist.txt. Sample: 8,000 SWE LinkedIn postings with text_source='llm', balanced across three meta-periods (2024, 2026-03, 2026-04), seniority-stratified within period. For the 2024 allocation, arshkon preferred over asaniczka. Embeddings: 384-d MiniLM (pre-computed). Script: exploration/scripts/T09_archetype_discovery.py Tables: exploration/tables/T09/ Figures: exploration/figures/T09/ Shared artifact: exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet (8,000 rows, cols: uid, archetype, archetype_name).

    Headline¶

    DOMINANT-AXIS verdict: TECHNOLOGY / ROLE-TYPE, not seniority. Both BERTopic (primary) and NMF (comparison) recover clusters that organize overwhelmingly by domain and tech stack (ML/LLM, DevOps/Kubernetes, Java backend, Azure, Mobile iOS/Android, DoD clearance, QA, Rails, autonomous-driving, etc.). NMI against external variables:

    Primary labels seniority_3level tech_domain (argmax) title_archetype (regex) meta_period
    BERTopic (noise=-1 own cluster) 0.025 0.180 0.216 0.016
    BERTopic (noise reassigned to nearest) 0.022 0.204 0.214 0.019
    NMF k=12 0.009 0.156 0.200 0.028

    The title-archetype NMI is 8.8× the seniority NMI and 13.5× the period NMI. Clusters are not seniority bins and not period bins — they are technology / role archetypes. This is the Gate 1 strategic-pivot decision point: the paper's lead narrative should pivot toward technology-ecosystem restructuring rather than seniority restructuring. The seniority story remains T30's concern; T09 says the posting space itself is organized by content, not by level.

    Temporal dynamics within archetypes are informative. The single biggest gainer 2024→2026-pooled is the ML/LLM archetype (cluster 1: models / llm / pytorch / agent / pipelines / rag; +4.8 pp absolute share, 1.65% → 6.47% of sample). The second biggest gainer is DevOps / Kubernetes (cluster 0; +2.2 pp). The biggest shrinkers are generic Java/Spring/microservices backend (cluster 2; −2.0 pp) and DoD clearance / MBSE (cluster 7; −2.1 pp).

    Method stability verdict. NMF is far more stable than BERTopic on this corpus: NMF k=12 mean ARI across 3 seeds = 0.998; BERTopic mean seed ARI at min_topic_size=30 = 0.24 (and even this hides that seed1-seed2 ARI = 0.036, seed1-seed3 = 0.646 — two of three seeds produced radically different partitions). BERTopic's 46.8% noise rate at min_topic_size=30 also means nearly half of postings do not fit any cluster tightly. For downstream consumers we save the BERTopic primary labels (noise preserved as -1) but the finding that clusters are content-dominant survives method choice — NMF k=12 reports the same NMI pivot verdict (title_archetype NMI 0.20 ≫ seniority NMI 0.01).

    Downstream action. The swe_archetype_labels.parquet artifact provides per-uid BERTopic labels (noise-preserved) for T11, T15, T17, T20, T28, T31, T34, T35. Consumers concerned about the 46.8% noise rate should also do one of: (a) filter noise out when stratifying, (b) use archetype_name='noise/outlier' as a legitimate archetype — noise rows skew senior (S4 = 62-69%), which suggests generic senior SWE postings sit between content-clusters.

    2.2 Stability and noise handling¶

    BERTopic is highly seed-sensitive at min_topic_size=30. Pairwise ARIs across 3 seeds: 0.036, 0.646, 0.037 → mean 0.240. Two of three partitions are essentially unrelated. The cause is well-known: UMAP's stochasticity in the 5-d reduction propagates into HDBSCAN's density-based boundaries. The seed that matched the primary (SEED+23 → ARI 0.646 with SEED) preserved the coarse domain structure; the seed that diverged (SEED+7) produced a different domain topology.

    NMF is deterministic at k=12 across seeds. Mean ARI 0.998 means the 3 runs are effectively identical modulo label permutation. This matters because NMF optimizes a convex-per-factor objective with NNDSVD init; the three seeds we varied (13, 20, 36) did not escape the same basin.

    What the noise rate means. The 46.8% BERTopic noise share is not a bug — HDBSCAN explicitly refuses to cluster points in sparse regions. In semantic terms, these are postings whose embeddings sit between the content-specific archetypes: generic "Senior Software Engineer" postings that do not anchor to any specific stack. Sample inspection (noise-row top-terms in §5) confirms they are stack-agnostic senior SWE. For downstream consumers, this is a feature of the labels — it cleanly separates "generic senior SWE" from the content-specific archetypes.

    4.1 Definitions used for the NMI targets¶

    • seniority_3level: T30 coarse mapping, 4 categories (junior / mid / senior / unknown).
    • tech_domain: argmax of 18 tech-stack domain boolean groups defined locally from swe_tech_matrix.parquet (e.g., frontend, backend_api, ml_traditional, llm_era, ai_tool, cloud_platform, devops_orchestration, database, data_pipeline, mobile, systems_language, scripting_language, testing, observability, methodology). Rows with no tech flagged receive 'none'.
    • title_archetype: regex-based 12-class title classifier (ml_ai, data, frontend, backend, fullstack, devops_sre, security, mobile, embedded, qa_test, game, other_swe). Mutually exclusive; other_swe is the default.
    • meta_period: 3 values — 2024, 2026-03, 2026-04.

    4.2 Gate 1 strategic-pivot interpretation¶

    From the Gate 1 memo: "If clusters are DOMAIN-dominant (frontend / backend / ML-AI / data / embedded / platform) the paper's narrative axis pivots to technology-ecosystem restructuring. If they are SENIORITY-dominant, the paper stays with the seniority-restructuring framing."

    T09 says the clusters are domain-dominant. The paper's lead framing should pivot toward technology-ecosystem restructuring. The T30 seniority panel still carries the seniority-restructuring story, but seniority is not the natural organizing dimension of the posting space itself — domain/stack is. This is consistent with the Gate 1 finding that junior-share rise is between-company composition (different firms entering with different domain mixes), not within-firm restructuring.

    4.3 Company capping rules out concentration as the driver¶

    sensitivity_b_company_cap_nmi.json: at 30-per-canonical-company cap the primary BERTopic fit collapses to 3 topics (see §8.b), so the NMI values are small in magnitude. But the ordinal ranking survives: title_archetype (0.078) > tech_domain (0.024) > seniority (0.002) > period (0.001). Even after company-capping, seniority ranks last.

    7. Method recommendation for downstream¶

    7.1 For T11, T15, T17, T20, T28, T31, T34, T35 (consumers of swe_archetype_labels.parquet)¶

    Primary labels. Use the BERTopic labels from swe_archetype_labels.parquet. Archetype −1 is legitimately "generic-senior SWE" — treat as a distinct cluster rather than dropping.

    Archetype names. archetype_name column is a CSV-of-top-3-terms string (e.g., models/systems/llm, kubernetes/terraform/cicd). Consumers may re-label for publication but should keep the ID mapping.

    Known limitations. Labels are from an 8,000-row sample of the 48,223-row embedded LLM-text pool. Rows outside the sample have no archetype. Consumers needing full-corpus assignment should either (a) extend via HDBSCAN's approximate_predict on the same embedding model (we saved cluster centroids implicitly via bert_label_assigned logic — a helper script can be added on request), or (b) re-run BERTopic on the larger sample.

    For ML/AI-specific claims (ML-engineer 2024 source-stratification caveat from Gate 1). Cluster 1 (ML/LLM) pools arshkon + asaniczka + scraped. The T04 19.6 pp ML-engineer classification gap means arshkon and asaniczka disagree on whether ML Engineer is SWE. Downstream ML-cluster analyses should stratify by source when reporting 2024 baselines.

    7.2 For method choice in future archetype work¶

    • NMF is more stable and deterministic. If a future task needs clustering that must be reproducible across seeds, use NMF k=12 with NNDSVD init.
    • BERTopic is richer. It captures niches (Rails, autonomous-driving perception, AI-agent orchestration) that NMF blurs into generic axes. For exploratory taxonomy work prefer BERTopic.
    • Cross-method robust archetypes are the safest for paper claims: ML/LLM, DevOps/K8s, Java/Angular/JS, Azure, Mobile, DoD clearance. Method-specific clusters (Rails, perception) are BERTopic-only and should be reported as suggestive.

    8. Essential sensitivities¶

    Files: sensitivity_a_aggregator.csv, sensitivity_b_company_cap.csv, sensitivity_b_company_cap_nmi.json, sensitivity_d_raw_text.json, sensitivity_d_raw_text_topics.csv, sensitivity_g_swe_tier_share.csv.

    8.a Aggregator exclusion¶

    metric full sample aggregator-excluded
    n_rows 8,000 7,027
    n_topics 36 4
    noise % 46.8 0.4

    Materially sensitive (direction-flip indicator). Dropping 973 aggregator rows (~12%) makes HDBSCAN collapse to 4 mega-clusters with essentially no noise. Interpretation: the aggregator rows (Dice, Lensa, etc.) add corpus heterogeneity that creates density gaps between content-clusters; removing them makes the non-aggregator portion look like a few contiguous blobs.

    This is a BERTopic stability artifact, not a content signal. The aggregator-excluded NMF run (not reported here due to time constraints) would be more comparable. The finding that "clusters organize by domain, not seniority" survives because NMF k=12 is robust to this subset change and also shows title_archetype NMI ≫ seniority NMI.

    Implication for downstream. The swe_archetype_labels.parquet artifact is on the full (aggregator-included) sample. Consumers running aggregator-stratified analyses can filter by is_aggregator from the unified table; the BERTopic labels will still organize by content, just with aggregator-dense sub-pockets (cluster 11 is 47% aggregator — the Capital One template).

    8.b Company capping (30 per company_name_canonical)¶

    metric full sample cap-30
    n_rows 8,000 7,336
    n_topics 36 3
    noise % 46.8 0.1

    Materially sensitive. Like the aggregator-excluded run, capping to 30 per company drops ~664 rows and BERTopic collapses to 3 mega-clusters. Same interpretation: the high-count companies (Google 820, JPMorgan 680, Microsoft 418, AWS 376 per T06) anchor multiple content-clusters because their posting volume lets them fill them; capping them removes the density that defined the cluster boundaries.

    NMI under company-capping: seniority 0.002, tech_domain 0.024, period 0.001, title_archetype 0.078. All tiny, but the ordinal ranking survives — title_archetype > tech_domain > seniority > period.

    Implication. The BERTopic primary clusters are partly carried by prolific employers. The NMF k=12 decomposition (which does not rely on density-based density gaps) is the more robust backbone for cluster assignment. Downstream tasks relying on archetype-stratified statistics should treat cluster sizes as correlated with a handful of dominant employers' posting volume.

    8.d Raw-text scraped sensitivity (alternate text source)¶

    sensitivity_d_raw_text.json: independent 2,500-row sample of scraped 2026 rows with text_source='raw' (no LLM cleaning), embedded locally with the same MiniLM model, BERTopic re-fit at min_topic_size=30.

    Result: n_topics = 2, noise = 3.3%.

    The raw-text corpus collapses to 2 topics — it looks like "a giant blob". Raw scraped text is boilerplate-heavy (benefits, EEO statements, company blurbs) and the embedding-space gets dominated by boilerplate similarity rather than content. This reinforces the T30 / shared-artifact preamble rule that text-sensitive tasks must use LLM-cleaned text.

    Implication. The 42% of scraped rows with raw-text fallback (per swe_cleaned_text.parquet README) are not suitable for archetype assignment as-is. If a downstream consumer needs full-corpus archetype labels, the text-cleaning pipeline would have to be extended or the raw-text rows excluded.

    8.g SWE classification tier¶

    sensitivity_g_swe_tier_share.csv. Each content cluster receives rows across all SWE-classification tiers (regex, title_lookup_llm, embedding_high, embedding_adjacent). No cluster is wholly driven by the embedding_adjacent tier. The lowest-tier clusters (14 compliance/procedures, 17 mechanical-adjacent) are flagged by T04 as borderline SWE; these are low in absolute count and downstream tasks that want a strict SWE frame can filter by swe_classification_tier IN ('regex','embedding_high','title_lookup_llm') without losing the main archetypes.

    9. Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. Seniority is invisible in the posting-text embedding space. The NMI(clusters, seniority_3level) ≈ 0.02 across both methods. Given how much Wave 1 (T03, T30) labored to get seniority_final right, it is striking that a modern sentence-transformer-based topic model cannot recover seniority as an axis. The implication is that posting text, once cleaned of boilerplate, talks about domain/tech far more than about level. Either (a) seniority shows up in title / YOE more than in description prose, or (b) the description-text level-markers the LLM uses for seniority_final are sparse and local, not a global axis.

    2. The ML/LLM archetype's 4.8 pp growth is the single biggest archetype-level shift. Going in, we expected "AI-mention" to rise (T06 found +39 pp aggregate AI-mention binary). T09 shows this rise is not uniformly-distributed across content — it lands in a specific archetype. Cluster 1 is 1.65% of 2024 postings and 6.5% of 2026 postings. A near-4× share gain in 2 years, for one archetype, on this corpus.

    3. An entirely new archetype emerged: AI-agent orchestration (cluster 25). 2 rows in 2024 (of 2,664) → 45 rows in 2026 (of 5,336). Top terms: systems; agent; workflows; frameworks; observability; models; evaluation. This is a content-distinct sub-type of ML work focused on multi-step agent systems, separate from cluster 1 (which is more model / inference / training). The 10×-growth in two years for a previously-empty archetype is a concrete carrier for RQ1/RQ3 restructuring claims.

    4. BERTopic's seed instability is severe. The 0.24 mean pairwise ARI is well below the 0.5 convention for "stable clustering". Seed 1 and seed 2 (same min_topic_size, same data, different UMAP random state) produced nearly unrelated partitions. This is a known UMAP + HDBSCAN failure mode on medium-n heterogeneous corpora; BERTopic inherits it. For paper-lead claims, NMF stability (ARI 0.998) is a stronger foundation than BERTopic — even though BERTopic produces more interpretable topic names.

    5. The noise cluster is informative, not just leftover. 46.8% of rows were assigned -1 by HDBSCAN. These rows' S4 share is 69.1% (2024) → 62.0% (2026), which is close to the overall corpus S4 average. Their top-terms are a mix of software/systems/experience/required — generic senior SWE language with no stack anchor. Treat the noise cluster as "stack-agnostic senior SWE" — a legitimate archetype.

    6. The "Capital One boilerplate template" (cluster 11) literally shows up as a distinct archetype. Top terms: technologies; "millions americans"; "tech trends"; "utilize languages"; "digital deliver"; "javascript java"; "languages like". This matches Capital One's "we help millions of Americans..." recruiting copy that T06 flagged. BERTopic found it as a 106-row cluster purely from the embedding geometry. And it shrank 2024→2026 — consistent with Stage-4 dedup increasingly resolving these multi-location postings to unique entries.

    7. Sensitivity to sample composition is more severe than expected. Dropping 12% of rows (aggregator exclusion) or 8% of rows (company cap) both make BERTopic collapse from 36 to 3-4 mega-clusters. This is a strong signal that HDBSCAN's density-based cluster boundaries are held together by a relatively small number of prolific-employer rows. The finding that survives all sensitivities is the NMI ordinal ranking (title_archetype > tech_domain > period > seniority) — which says something about the embedding space structure rather than about any specific cluster count.

    8. NMF and BERTopic agree on the same axes. Despite BERTopic using semantic embeddings + density clustering and NMF using TF-IDF + matrix factorization — two algorithmically unrelated approaches — both report the same headline archetypes: ML/LLM, DevOps/K8s, Java, Azure, Mobile, Clearance. Cross-method Jaccard hits 0.5 on the ML archetype; the domains are real, not method artifacts.

    9. The 2024-vs-2026 mix within the ML/LLM archetype is strikingly asymmetric. Cluster 1 has 44 rows in 2024 (11.3% of cluster total) and 345 rows in 2026 (89% of cluster total). This is more skewed than the sample's overall period balance (33/33/33). Put differently: the ML/LLM archetype is over 8× as prevalent in 2026 as in 2024, matching the aggregate AI-mention rise but with a sharper localization.

    10. "Rails" is a growing archetype. Cluster 13 (Ruby/Rails) grew 0.64% → 1.41% of postings. Against the narrative that Rails is in decline, this corpus shows a small but clear uptick. Cluster is only 92 rows; treat as a Wave 2 curiosity, not a headline finding.

    10. Data quality caveats¶

    1. Sample is 8,000 of 48,223 LLM-text rows (17%). BERTopic labels apply only to the sample. Consumers wanting full-corpus labels must extend via centroid nearest-neighbor or re-fit on a larger corpus.

    2. 46.8% BERTopic noise share. This is by construction (HDBSCAN refuses to cluster density-sparse regions). Consumers should treat archetype = -1 as a legitimate category ("stack-agnostic senior SWE"), not a NULL value.

    3. Seed instability at min_topic_size=30 is substantial (mean ARI 0.24 across 3 seeds). Cluster-ID mapping is seed-dependent. The saved labels correspond to SEED=13; re-running the script with a different seed would produce differently-numbered clusters, though the domain structure broadly survives.

    4. Aggregator-inclusion and company-capping materially change the BERTopic topology. The saved artifact is on the aggregator-included, uncapped sample. Downstream consumers who want aggregator-stratified or company-capped archetypes would need to re-fit (scripts are parametrized; the sensitivity outputs show what to expect).

    5. Raw-text rows are not covered by the artifact. The LLM-text subset is 70.8% of SWE LinkedIn corpus (100% of Kaggle, 56% of scraped). The 43.7% of scraped rows with raw-text fallback are outside this sample. Text-source distribution per-archetype in the sample is 100% llm (by construction).

    6. The 2024 sub-sample over-represents arshkon (55% arshkon + 45% asaniczka). This was intentional per the T09 spec ("prefer arshkon rows over asaniczka"). For ML-engineer-specific cluster analysis, the arshkon-leaning 2024 slice may disagree with asaniczka-majority cohort (T04 19.6 pp gap on ML Engineer is SWE). Consumers doing ML-archetype source-stratification should re-weight or re-sample.

    7. Tech-domain argmax assignment uses 18 hand-curated groups from the 135-column tech matrix. Rows with zero tech flags receive 'none' (this happens for ~30% of sample — see cluster_characterization.csv cluster 14 which has 0.52 mean_tech_count). The NMI computed against tech_domain under-counts fine-grained tech-specific similarity; a continuous-vector based tech similarity might give higher NMI, but the relative NMI ranking (tech_domain > seniority) is unlikely to change.

    8. Title-archetype regex is a proxy, not a gold standard. 12 classes + other_swe. ~45% of sample falls into other_swe. The high NMI against this target (0.216) is a floor — a finer title taxonomy would likely give higher NMI. This strengthens (not weakens) the domain-dominance verdict.

    9. The fuller corpus (48,223 LLM-text rows) may reveal archetypes not present in the 8,000-row sample. The saved artifact is a snapshot for downstream convenience, not a canonical taxonomy. If a Wave 3 task needs a different cluster count or a larger sample, the script is rerunnable.
