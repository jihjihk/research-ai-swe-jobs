    3. Surprise inventory¶

    Cross-wave surprises (all waves)¶

    1. Dominant axis is technology/domain, not seniority (T09 NMI 8.88× title-archetype vs seniority). Pre-exploration prior was seniority-dominant. The pivot drove Gate 2's positioning change.
    2. Seniority boundaries sharpened, not blurred (T15 + T20). Pre-exploration RQ1 subclaim was "blurring"; the opposite result survives across unsupervised and supervised methods.
    3. Scope inflation is universal and senior > junior (T11 + T28). Pre-exploration assumption was junior-specific. Senior-side magnitudes larger in absolute terms.
    4. Within-firm AI rewriting exceeds between-firm AI rise (T06 Gate 1 +31 pp; V1/T16 corrected to +7.7-8.3 pp under V1 pattern). Magnitude revised but direction robust.
    5. Management pattern precision exposed at 0.28/0.55 (V1). Wave 2 "management fell" claim was a measurement artifact. Publishable methods-caveat on longitudinal posting studies.
    6. T13 J3 requirements-chars direction flips under alt classifier (V1 + T33). Classifier choice flips the sign at aggregate level, not just J3.
    7. Aggregators post CLEANER JDs than direct employers (T22 ghost-likeness, T31 AI). Counter to typical "aggregators are spammy" prior.
    8. Copilot at 0.10% of postings vs ~33% worker regular-use (T23). Employers under-formalize even the most-adopted AI tool.

    Wave 3.5-specific surprises¶

    1. Pair-level AI drift EXCEEDS company-level drift (T31 +13.4 pp > T16 +7.7-8.3 pp). Pre-Wave-3.5 prior was pair-level should be tighter (less within-company title noise) and hence smaller; it is larger. Within-firm rewriting is same-title, not title-recomposition.
    2. Two emergent senior archetypes, not one (T34). Pre-Wave-3.5 prior expected a single Applied-AI role; found TWO (Applied-AI/LLM + Data-Platform/DevOps).
    3. AI-oriented senior roles ask MORE experience (T34 median YOE 6 vs 5 for cluster 1). Counter to a "AI lowers the bar" prior; supports sharpened-seniority finding.
    4. Cross-occupation divergence UNIVERSAL across 16/16 subgroups 2024 AND 2026 (T32). Nurse ai_strict at 0.00% on 6,801 postings. Accountant 72× worker-to-employer ratio (larger than SWE 7×).
    5. Hiring-selectivity REJECTED with OPPOSITE direction (T38). Volume-UP firms write LONGER JDs (r=+0.20). JOLTS trough is background, not mechanism.
    6. New-entrant firms are LESS junior-heavy than returning firms (T16 12.8% vs 15.3%). Between-company J3 rise is EXIT-driven, not entry-driven. 4,395 firms exited panel with lower-than-average J3.
    7. Legacy substitution is stack-modernization, not AI-ification (T36). 2024 architect titles map to 2026 engineer titles at 3.6% ai_strict (below 14.4% market rate). 2-4 year YOE drop on substitution — title-inflation reversal.
    8. T13 classifier sensitivity extends to aggregate (T33). Direction flip at full-corpus level, not just J3. SNR 0.97 at noise floor.
    9. Tech ecosystem crystallization is MODEST (T35 +0.029 modularity). LLM-vendor cluster already existed in 2024 pooled. Real novelty: AI dev-tools sub-cluster split-off (⅘ members new).
    10. S4 senior within-firm decline EXCEEDS aggregate (T37 −9.31 pp within-co vs −8.29 pp aggregate on returning cohort). Parallel to T16 "exit-driven junior rise" — now "exit-driven senior decline" too.

    4. New post-3.5 hypothesis specifications¶

    Two new hypothesis candidates emerge from the full Wave 3 + Wave 3.5 evidence body and merit formal specification for analysis-phase prioritization.

    H_O — Within-firm AI rewriting intensity correlates with firm's 2024 digital-transformation maturity¶

    Claim statement. The pair-level AI-strict drift (T31) is not uniform across firms: firms with greater 2024 digital-transformation maturity (measured via cloud-spend signals, public API surface, or proprietary tooling presence) exhibit larger within-pair AI rewriting magnitudes in 2026.

    Supporting evidence (indirect). - T34 cluster 0 (Applied-AI Engineer) concentrates 30% in T16 ai_forward_scope_inflator employer cluster (vs 11% overall share in 2026 senior postings). Only a subset of firms has pivoted. - T11 top-1% AI-saturated outliers are financial-services dominated (JPM, Citi, Wells Fargo, GEICO, Visa, Solera) — firms with known 2024 enterprise-AI pilot programs. - T31 top-20 pair-drift exemplars are Wells Fargo + Microsoft + Capital One + Walmart + Amazon — all firms with prominent 2024 internal AI infrastructure initiatives.

    Proposed analysis-phase test. Join T31 pair-drift data with external firm-level digital-maturity indicators (e.g., Apptopia cloud-share data; LinkedIn company-size + tech-stack labels; Dealroom AI-startup-affiliation flags). Regress pair-level AI-strict Δ on maturity-index + sector + size + pair-count controls. Expected sign: positive. Power consideration: n_pairs = 23-37 is thin; analysis-phase would benefit from a larger overlap-panel construction under relaxed pair-count threshold.

    Alternative framings. - (a) Sector-channel: financial services over-represented in AI-forward rewrites; test sector × maturity interaction. - (b) Size-channel: large firms lead AI rewrites; test size × maturity interaction. T38 suggests firm-size alone does not select for AI-rewriting (|r|<0.10 across size buckets), so a pure size-explanation is unlikely.

    H_P — Applied-AI Engineer archetype concentrates in financial services due to regulatory-compliance AI adoption¶

    Claim statement. T34 cluster 0 (Applied-AI / LLM Engineer) is over-represented in financial services (16.5% of cluster 0 vs ~5-8% of general SWE postings) not because financial-services firms adopt AI earlier, but because compliance-grade AI requires explicit engineering roles (vs. informal Copilot use in other sectors).

    Supporting evidence (indirect). - T34 cluster 0 industry top-2: Software Development 44.6%, Financial Services 16.5%; T16 top AI-rich outliers dominated by banks. - T23 Copilot at 0.10% in postings suggests employers do NOT formalize casual AI use. The ones they DO formalize concentrate where informal use is compliance-restricted (bank trading floors, healthcare). - T32 accountant employer ai_strict 0.69% vs worker 50% = 72× gap; the universality-plus-compression pattern supports an under-codification-except-where-compliance-requires hypothesis.

    Proposed analysis-phase test. Compare T34 cluster 0 industry composition against external industry-level compliance-density indices (e.g., NIST AI RMF adoption data by sector; FINRA/SEC AI-related enforcement actions). Additionally: qualitative comparison of cluster 0 postings in financial services vs. Software Development — look for compliance-specific language ("model risk management", "MRM", "SR 11-7", "EEOC-compliant AI") as a differentiating signature.

    Alternative framings. - (a) Talent-market channel: finance pays more, so AI engineers concentrate there. - (b) Legacy-modernization channel: finance's 2024 legacy stack requires AI-specific engineers to integrate; Software Development doesn't.

    H_Q (additional identified) — AI mentions at junior tier are wish-list items, not formal requirements¶

    Claim statement. T22's finding that 50.3% of near-AI-token windows contain aspirational language (vs 28.2% far-from-AI) extends to the J3 subset specifically: AI asks in entry-level postings sit in "nice-to-have" / "preferred" sections 2× more than non-AI asks.

    Supporting evidence (indirect). - T22 aggregate 50.3% vs 28.2% on 3,543 AI-mentioning 2026 postings. - T22 top-20 ghost entry list: Visa "Software Engineer, New College Grad" with 21 tech_count × 6 scope_kitchen_count = 126 kitchen-sink score, asking new grads for LLM fine-tuning. - T23 copilot J3 rate 4.6% < overall but near-zero formalization: J3 ai_strict 8.52% suggests most are general AI/ML not tool-specific.

    Proposed analysis-phase test. Re-run T22 aspiration-share analysis restricted to J3 (YOE≤2) postings with AI mentions. Expected: aspiration-share near-AI ≥ 50% on J3; aspiration-share far-from-AI ≤ 30%. If confirmed, this is a RQ3-adjacent finding — the divergence story has a hedging-at-junior sub-channel.

    5. Deferred hypothesis inventory (T24 planned list)¶

    Six hypotheses from the pre-Wave-3 T24-planned list were not run in Wave 3.5 or were partially addressed. Priority rankings and analysis-phase data/method needs follow.

    H_D — Senior IC-as-team-multiplier¶

    Claim. The Applied-AI/LLM Engineer archetype (T34 cluster 0) functions as a team-force-multiplier: one senior AI engineer amplifies the productivity of several junior/mid engineers without being a manager. If true, the cluster 0 + team-composition data should show: (1) companies hiring cluster-0 seniors also hire more juniors at the same company × period; (2) cluster-0 postings correlate with downstream junior-hiring intensity within-firm.

    Why deferred. Wave 3.5 T34 characterized the archetype at posting level but did not formalize its role in team composition. Cross-time-series hiring correlation requires a company panel with hiring pipeline data beyond T16's 724-company panel.

    Priority: HIGH. Directly tests a mechanism for RQ2 (requirement migration across seniority).

    Data / method needs. - Company-month hiring panel (BLS QCEW or proprietary BurningGlass equivalent). - T34 cluster 0 per-firm posting counts as independent variable. - T30 J3 per-firm posting counts as dependent variable. - Fixed-effects regression with firm, sector, and period controls.

    H_E — Same-co J1 drop + J3 rise regime shift¶

    Claim. The J1 (seniority_final='entry') share rise is a recent regime shift occurring 2025-2026, distinguishable from a slow-trend null. If true, a formal break analysis within the 2026 scraped daily window should show rising J3/S4 share through 2026-03 to 2026-04 OR discontinuity from 2024 baselines.

    Why deferred. T19 found zero temporal trend within the 30-day scraped window; T30 showed scraped daily profile is internally stable. Annualized rate ratios are below 1 for seniority metrics (dominated by within-2024 source-instrument variance).

    Priority: LOW for cross-period break; MEDIUM for event-timing around major AI model releases. The latter would require finer-grained weekly data across 2024-2026.

    Data / method needs. - Expanded temporal panel beyond the current 15-30 day scrape + 2024 snapshots. - Event-study framework with model-release dates as treatment indicators. - Not feasible with current data; defer to longitudinal analysis phase with sustained scraping.

    H_F — Sunbelt AI surge catchup¶

    Claim. AI-mention rates in Sunbelt metros (Atlanta, Tampa, Miami, Dallas-Fort Worth, Charlotte) will grow faster than traditional tech-hub metros (SF Bay Area, Seattle, NYC, Boston) between 2024 and 2026, with Sunbelt metros closing the gap.

    Why partially-addressed. T17 found AI-rise is geographically UNIFORM (SNR ~10, all metros positive) with leaders Atlanta/Tampa/Miami/SLC — not SF/Seattle. The H_F prediction is partially absorbed: Sunbelt DID lead, but SF/Seattle did NOT fall behind — the uniformity is the bigger story than the catchup. A formal catchup-timing test (event study) was not run.

    Priority: LOW. T17's uniformity result supersedes the catchup framing; any remaining catchup structure is sub-leading to the broader "AI-rise is geographic-uniform" finding.

    Data / method needs. If revived: weekly metro-stratified posting data across the full 2024-2026 window; interacted event-study with tech-hub vs Sunbelt × time. Current data supports within-period (T17) but not continuous trajectory.

    H_G — Staff-title redistribution¶

    Claim. The 2024→2026 title vocabulary rotated: "senior" title-marker share dropped 12 pp while "staff" doubled and "none" rose 12 pp (T10). This is title-inflation reversal — the 2024 "architect" premium dissolved.

    Why partially-addressed. T10 documented the title-rotation finding. T36 substitution map shows 2024 architect → 2026 engineer with 2-4 year YOE drop. Wave 3.5 did not formalize this as a hypothesis test beyond descriptive statistics.

    Priority: MEDIUM. The title-rotation is real and corroborated across T10 + T36; a formal analysis-phase test could quantify cross-firm title-inflation elasticity.

    Data / method needs. - Firm-level staff-vs-senior-title share panel. - External firm-size / hiring-difficulty controls (proxies for why firms inflate titles). - OLS regression of title-mix Δ on firm covariates.

    H_I — AI as coordination signal¶

    Claim. Employer AI-mention rate functions as a coordination signal in the labor market: firms use AI-language to signal technical sophistication to candidates, not primarily to specify required skills. If true, AI-mention rate should correlate with firm attractiveness (quit-rate lower, application-rate higher) independent of actual AI-work performed.

    Why deferred. Requires labor-supply-side data (application-volume, quit-rate) that is out of scope for the current corpus. Mechanism test, not measurement test.

    Priority: MEDIUM. Complements RQ3 divergence (if AI is coordination-signal, the under-formalization makes sense — employers signal without committing). But testing requires interview data (RQ4) and external labor-supply metrics.

    Data / method needs. - Interview-side: RQ4 qualitative interview questions on "why we mention AI in JDs" (candidates vs recruiters). - Quantitative side: Glassdoor/Indeed applicant volume data joined with T11_posting_features; not available in current pipeline.

    H_J — Recruiter-LLM senior-bias mediation¶

    Claim. T29 rejected the general LLM-authorship mediation hypothesis. H_J specifically targets the SENIOR posting subset: recruiter LLM adoption may be concentrated in senior-role drafting (where JDs are longer and more technically detailed), and the senior-role content shifts (orch rise, Applied-AI archetype emergence) may be partially LLM-mediated even if the aggregate is not.

    Why deferred. T29 stratified by overall LLM signature density but did not stratify by seniority × LLM interaction. The general rejection of T29 is strong; a senior-specific re-test is a refinement rather than a primary test.

    Priority: MEDIUM. Would strengthen T21/T34 senior-role claims by ruling out senior-specific authorship mediation.

    Data / method needs. - Re-run T29 authorship-score analysis on senior subset (S4 + director) only. - Compare low-LLM-quartile-within-senior-subset to full-senior-corpus on orch density, ai_binary, mgmt_rebuilt, cluster 0 share. - Existing T29 authorship_scores.csv + T21 cluster_assignments.csv have sufficient joinable data.

    7. Top 5 tensions for analysis phase (post-Wave-3.5)¶

    These tensions are different from Gate 3's list: Gate 3 focused on the pre-synthesis evidence body's tensions; these focus on analysis-phase priorities that SYNTHESIS.md (T26) must foreground for the analysis-phase RQ-specification.

    Tension 1 — Cross-occupation divergence magnitude framing: absolute pp vs ratio¶

    T32 reports accountant worker 50% vs employer 0.69% = 72× ratio gap (largest in table) but SWE worker 84% vs employer 10.61% = 8× ratio gap at LARGER absolute magnitude. The paper's RQ3 lead can frame the divergence as: (a) SWE-concentrated magnitude (absolute pp gap) or (b) universal ratio-gap (multiplicative gap) — both are defensible and lead to different readings.

    Gate 3 recommendation: present BOTH framings in SYNTHESIS.md ranked findings. Let analysis-phase pre-register which is primary.

    Tension 2 — Pair-level vs company-level scope direction¶

    T31 finds pair-level breadth drift near-zero (−0.02 to +0.08) while T16 company-level breadth drift is +0.40-0.69 on the same firms. The 90%+ attenuation at pair level means scope inflation is cross-title within-company, not within-title. This contradicts a naive "same-title rewriting" reading that would predict pair-level to carry scope too.

    Gate 3 recommendation: foreground the cross-title channel. Scope inflation is "employers re-titling AND adding new senior titles with broader scope", not "employers making existing same-title roles broader". This is a paper-level refinement of the Gate 2 scope-inflation story.

    Tension 3 — Sampling-frame attenuation on H_d J3 breadth only¶

    T37 finds 14/15 headlines ratio ≥0.80, but H_d J3 (length-residualized breadth at J3) attenuates to 0.70 on returning cohort (+1.56 → +1.09). This may be a power artifact (returning n=1,758 vs full 2,911) OR a real signal that new-entrant J3 postings carry more residualized breadth. Cannot distinguish without additional regression decomposition.

    Gate 3 recommendation: flag in SYNTHESIS.md as the one partial-robustness case; analysis-phase pre-register a J3-specific within-between decomposition to resolve.

    Tension 4 — Asaniczka senior asymmetry vs pooled magnitude¶

    S4 senior-share decline is −7.6 pp pooled-2024 but −1.8 pp arshkon-only. The pooled number is asaniczka-weighted (asaniczka 4× arshkon); the arshkon-only number is not significant at 80% power (MDE 2.5 pp). Senior claims require dual-reporting.

    Gate 3 recommendation: continue the T30 precedent — pair pooled and arshkon-only magnitudes for every senior claim. In SYNTHESIS.md, cite BOTH (not one with asterisk).

    Tension 5 — RQ3 lead framing: SWE-specific vs universal-compressed¶

    T18 DiD SWE−CTL = +14.02 pp (highly SWE-specific). T32 16/16 direction-universal AND Spearman +0.92 worker-employer rank concordance (highly universal). These are not inconsistent but support different paper leads: - SWE-specific framing → paper on SWE labor restructuring. - Universal-compressed framing → paper on employer signaling behavior across AI-exposed occupations.

    Gate 3 recommendation: Gate 3 memo recommends HYBRID framing with A1 + A2 as co-lead. SYNTHESIS.md should present both framings as Tier A with dispatch-phase RQ-selection deferring between them if the analysis phase prefers a single lead.
