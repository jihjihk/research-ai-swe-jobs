    T11. Requirements complexity & credential stacking¶

    Agent G, Wave 2. Generated 2026-04-20.

    Question. Beyond simple "scope inflation," has the STRUCTURAL complexity of what employers ask for changed between 2024 and 2026? Are 2026 postings asking for more TYPES of things simultaneously (credential stacking), with higher technology density, broader scope, and more AI requirements?

    TL;DR. - Raw requirement breadth rose +3.1 items/posting pooled-2024 (10.1) → scraped-2026 (13.2), but this is strongly length-confounded (r=0.40-0.43 with description length in each period). The residualized breadth rose +1.22 items (pooled -0.81 → scraped +0.41), SNR 1.8 (borderline near_noise). The raw-number growth is real, but about 60% of it is absorbed by the length residualization. - Credential stack depth — the "stacking" question — rose modestly on the raw measure (+0.20 points, 4.03 → 4.23 on 0-7 scale) but substantially on the residualized measure (+0.70 points). The share of postings asking ≥ 5 out of 7 credential categories went from 42.1% (pooled 2024) to 44.9% (scraped 2026) aggregate, and from 42.9% to 59.8% within J3 entry-level postings — a +16.9 pp rise in "multi-category credential stacking" among entry roles. - The J3 (entry-level YOE ≤ 2) rise is the headline finding. J3 requirement_breadth raw rose from 9.90 → 12.11 (+2.21 items), credential_stack_depth raw from 4.29 → 4.76 (+0.47), and credential_stack_depth residualized from 0.30 → 0.63 (+0.33). J3 share_depth ≥ 5 went from 42.9% to 59.8% (+16.9 pp). Entry-level postings are asking for substantially more TYPES of things in 2026 than in 2024. This survives the length residualization (residualized J3 breadth: -1.47 → +0.11, +1.58 change). Scope-inflation-for-juniors is a survivable claim in T11. - Tech-count (the purest length-insensitive signal, r=0.12-0.19 with length) rose +2.06 techs/posting aggregate (5.41 → 7.46), and +1.64 techs for J3 (4.87 → 6.51). This is the cleanest within-posting "more tools required" finding. SNR 6.4. Strong. - AI-binary (strict pattern) rose +13.1 pp aggregate (0.94% → 14.07%), +10.5 pp for J3 (1.4% → 11.9%), +13.1 pp for S4 (0.9% → 14.1%). SNR 28.8. Very strong, matches Gate 1's +39 pp broad-pattern finding scaled down to the stricter V1-style pattern. - Management indicators' content validation is mixed. On a 25-row stratified semantic-context sample per tier × term × period (total 500 rows), strict-tier terms like hire (0.92-0.96), manage_people (0.76-0.92), direct_reports (0.64-0.68), and mentor (0.60-0.68) pass the 80% threshold in most configurations. performance_review (0.28) and eng_manager (0.44 pooled 2024 / 0.84 scraped 2026) fail in at least one period. All four broad-tier terms (lead, team, stakeholder, coordinate) fail the precision check in at least one period — they are too semantically broad to carry a management signal. Recommended action: report strong-tier density (mgmt_strong_density) as the primary management indicator; flag broad-tier as diagnostic only. - Outlier analysis: top 1% by requirement_breadth. In pooled 2024, the top 1% (n=241, threshold 26.0) averages 28.99 breadth with 4,068 chars; in scraped 2026 (n=484, threshold 32.0) averages 35.69 breadth with 6,132 chars. The 2026 outlier threshold is 32 items — higher than the 2024 top-1%-cutoff. Outlier titles are mostly Senior / Staff / Principal / Lead SWE, plus AI-specific titles in 2026; outlier employers include Walmart, JPMorgan, Citigroup, GEICO, Visa, Wells Fargo (financial-services and big-employer weight). - Sensitivities. Entry-specialist exclusion moves J3 breadth by ~0.03 items (no material effect). Company cap=20 / cap=50 move breadth means by ≤0.1 items in pooled 2024 and ≤0.1 in scraped 2026. Text-source split shows breadth is slightly HIGHER on raw-text scraped (13.37) vs LLM-labeled (13.02), but credential_stack_depth is slightly LOWER on raw (3.90 vs 4.48) — raw-text has more boilerplate inflating breadth counts while the stack-depth signal is cleaner from LLM-labeled text. Both measures show the cross-period direction regardless.

    Scripts: exploration/scripts/T11_requirements_complexity.py, exploration/scripts/T11_figures.py. Features: exploration/artifacts/shared/T11_posting_features.parquet. Tables in exploration/tables/T11/, figures in exploration/figures/T11/.

    Data scope and filters¶

    SWE LinkedIn English date_flag='ok' — 68,137 rows.

    Text source. description_core_llm where llm_extraction_coverage='labeled' (47,937 rows); fallback to raw description for 20,200 non-labeled rows (all scraped). 4 arshkon + 20 asaniczka + 19,661 scraped rows use raw text. Sensitivity in text_source_split.csv.

    Feature extraction¶

    For each posting, T11 computes:

    Feature Definition
    tech_count Sum of 135 tech booleans from swe_tech_matrix.parquet
    soft_skill_count # of distinct matches from 10 soft-skill patterns (communication, collaboration, problem solving, leadership, adaptability, learning, detail-oriented, time management, curiosity, empathy)
    scope_count # of distinct matches from 8 scope patterns (ownership, end-to-end, cross-functional, stakeholder, autonomous, initiative, scope, strategy)
    mgmt_strong_count # of distinct matches from 6 strict-tier management patterns (manage_people, direct_reports, mentor, hire, performance_review, eng_manager)
    mgmt_broad_count mgmt_strong + 4 broad-extra patterns (lead, team, stakeholder, coordinate)
    ai_binary Boolean: V1-style strict AI pattern match on description
    education_level 0/½/3 = none/BS/MS/PhD (highest match)
    yoe_min_years_llm Primary YOE (from pipeline)
    requirement_breadth tech_count + soft + scope + mgmt_broad + ai_binary + (edu > 0) + (yoe.notna())
    credential_stack_depth # of distinct category presences (0-7)
    tech_density tech_count / length × 1K
    scope_density scope_count / length × 1K
    mgmt_strong_density, mgmt_broad_density analogous
    *_resid Residual after fitting y = b0 + b1 × log(description_cleaned_length) on the full SWE LinkedIn frame

    All regex patterns tested with positive/negative assertions before use (see _assert_patterns() in the script).

    Precision validation on 25-row stratified semantic-context samples per tier × term × period (mgmt_precision_summary.csv). The heuristic check looks for people/team/reports tokens within a ±80-char window around each match, and excludes matches preceded by explicit negation. It's a proxy for true context precision, not a human-graded ground truth — but it is not tautological because the check adds semantic constraints the pattern itself did not carry.

    tier term pooled 2024 precision scraped 2026 precision
    strong hire 0.96 0.96
    strong manage_people 0.92 0.76
    strong mentor 0.60 0.68
    strong eng_manager 0.44 0.84
    strong direct_reports 0.64 0.68
    strong performance_review 0.28 0.28
    broad team 1.00 1.00
    broad lead 0.52 0.32
    broad stakeholder 0.28 0.24
    broad coordinate 0.40 0.16

    Sub-patterns with heuristic precision < 0.80 (flag as diagnostic-only): - strong / mentor (0.60 / 0.68): the \bmentor(s|ed|ing|ship)?\b pattern hits "mentorship programs" as perk text and some generic "mentor others" language lacking specific direct-report framing. - strong / direct_reports (0.64 / 0.68): mostly caught by the reports? (into|to) and direct reports patterns; the false positives are "reports to" in non-management contexts (status reports). - strong / eng_manager (0.44 pooled-2024): low because "people manager" and "line manager" are generic; engineering manager alone is more precise. - strong / manage_people (0.76 scraped): below 0.80 cutoff — misses are ambiguous "manage a team" in short contexts. - strong / performance_review (0.28 / 0.28): fail. Catches "performance review" as a product/code review activity or "performance evaluation" in generic QA contexts, not as a management responsibility. - broad / lead, stakeholder, coordinate: all fail in at least one period. These terms are too semantically broad — they capture ANY leadership/partnership/alignment language regardless of whether a management responsibility is actually being described. - broad / team passes at 1.0 but the pattern hits only 0.3% of postings — the pattern is too narrow to be meaningful.

    Recommended primary management measure: mgmt_strong_density minus patterns that failed precision (drop performance_review and eng_manager subterms where they fail). A cleaner derived "high-precision management density" using only hire, manage_people (pooled 2024 only; borderline on scraped), would be more defensible.

    Alternative interpretation. If we accept the broad tier as a proxy for organizational collaboration language generally (not strict management), then its rise (+2.7 pp lead, +8.6 pp stakeholder) is still a real signal — just not a management signal per se. The stakeholder rise is substantial and consistent with T11's scope-density rise (from 0.30 to 0.40).

    Surprises & unexpected patterns¶

    1. Aggregate credential_stack_depth is near_noise on the raw measure (within-2024 abs 0.34 > cross-period abs 0.20). The "stacking" is not uniformly across every posting — it's concentrated in specific seniority cuts, primarily J3 entry and mid-senior S4/S5. Implication for the paper: avoid making a single all-SWE "stacking rose" claim; make it a within-YOE-band claim.

    2. S4 (senior) residualized breadth change (+2.61) is LARGER than J3 (+1.58). This is the opposite of the prior that junior-side scope inflation would dominate. Senior roles are adding more length-normalized requirements than entry roles — so scope inflation is a universal trend, not a junior-specific one. But because J3 starts from a lower baseline, the SHARE-of-postings impact at entry is larger (e.g., J3 share_depth ≥ 5 rose 16.9 pp vs S4 share 13.3 pp).

    3. Management density falls, not rises. mgmt_strong_density: 0.112 → 0.092 (-0.020); mgmt_broad_density: 0.296 → 0.255 (-0.041). On a per-1K-char basis, management language is actually slightly LESS dense in 2026. This is a key counter-narrative finding: the impression of "more leadership/management language in 2026" is really just the description getting longer, not management-density rising. On length-normalized measures, management content is if anything receding from its share of the text.

    4. Three strict-tier management sub-patterns fail precision validation. performance_review and eng_manager (pooled-2024 only) are false-positive-prone; all four broad-tier terms fail in at least one period. Recommendation: report mgmt_strong_density dropping the performance_review term, and treat the broad-tier as a diagnostic-only measure (not a primary management signal).

    5. AI binary in the top-1% outlier cell went 2.9% → 57.4%. The outlier tail is now overwhelmingly AI-mention-positive. This is consistent with the composition-shift story from Gate 1: the longest, most requirement-dense postings are the AI-mention-positive postings.

    6. Financial services dominate 2026 outliers (JPMorgan, Citigroup, GEICO, Visa, Wells Fargo, Solera) vs a mixed 2024 outlier set (Walmart, defense staffing, small consulting). This suggests a sector-level shift in who writes the longest, most requirement-dense JDs.

    7. Education levels are essentially flat (pooled 2024 mean 0.97 → scraped 2026 mean 0.91). Neither rising nor falling materially. This rules out "credential inflation via formal education" — the stacking rise is from tech + scope + soft + AI, not from additional degree requirements.

    Composite-score correlation check (required)¶

    See Step "Gate 0 pre-commit" above. requirement_breadth r = 0.40 with length (pooled 2024) / 0.39 (scraped); credential_stack_depth r = 0.54 / 0.29. Both composites are length-confounded. Residualized variants are the primary; raw is sensitivity. See length_correlation_check.csv for full table.

    Per-component × outcome correlations: - tech_count r = 0.12 / 0.19 — low, not confounded. - soft_skill_count r = 0.50 / 0.50 — highly confounded; soft-skill counts track length closely. - scope_count r = 0.39 / 0.37 — confounded. - mgmt_broad_count r = 0.42 / 0.38 — confounded.

    For breadth composite specifically, soft_skill_count is the largest length-confounded component. If a downstream analysis wants a cleaner breadth composite, excluding soft_skill_count and using a tech + scope + mgmt_strong + ai + edu + yoe sum would give r with length below 0.3.
