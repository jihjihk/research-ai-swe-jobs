    T34 — Emergent senior-role archetype profiling (H_C)¶

    Wave 3.5, Agent R. Run: 2026-04-20. Inputs: exploration/tables/T21/cluster_assignments.csv (33,693 senior rows × 11 cols), data/unified.parquet, exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet (8,000 T09 labels) + projected via MiniLM embeddings for the remaining senior rows, exploration/tables/T16/overlap_panel.csv (T16 per-company strategy clusters), exploration/artifacts/shared/T11_posting_features.parquet. Script: exploration/scripts/T34_run.py.

    Orientation: DISCOVERY. Goal: test whether T21 candidate emergent clusters are genuine coherent senior roles or clustering artifacts, and let the content determine role names.

    Headline verdict — cluster 0 is a genuine, coherent Applied-AI / LLM Engineer archetype; cluster 1 is the Data-Platform / DevOps archetype¶

    Cluster 0 (T21 "AI-oriented, low-orch"): genuine emergent senior role. All diagnostic tests pass: 15.6× growth (144 → 2,251 postings), 94% of rows are 2026, title distribution 28% AI Engineer + 18% senior engineer + 8% staff + 4% principal (aggregator-diverse), ai_binary=1.00 and orch_density=1.86/1K chars, dominant T09 archetypes models/systems/llm (over-represented 6.7×) and systems/agent/workflows (5.7×). Exemplar content cleanly maps to Applied-AI / LLM Engineer work. Distinguishing n-grams (vs other senior clusters): rag pipelines, claude code, rag architectures, github copilot claude, langchain llamaindex, fine-tuning, similarity search, augmented generation rag. Proposed role name: Senior Applied-AI / LLM Engineer (alternates: Senior AI Systems Engineer, AI Platform Engineer).

    Cluster 1 (T21 "Tech-orchestration, non-AI"): less sharp but still coherent — Data-Platform / DevOps senior role. n=7,292 (2.6× growth, 72% of 2026), ai_binary=0 by construction, orch_density=2.27/1K chars (highest across clusters). Titles: 20% senior engineer + 13% data engineer + 11% DevOps/SRE + 5% AI. Dominant T09 archetypes kubernetes/terraform/cicd (2.1×) and pipelines/sql/etl (2.6×). Exemplars emphasize ETL, Snowflake/Databricks, Airflow, PySpark, CI/CD, platform work — with data-engineering slightly ahead of SRE. Precondition check formally flagged "1 elevated signal" (orch only — ai=0 and mgmt=0 by construction drag the signal count down), but the content is clearly coherent and 2026-weighted. Proposed role name: Senior Data-Platform / DevOps Engineer (alternates: Senior Data Engineer — Pipelines & Workflows; Senior Platform / SRE Engineer).

    Aggregator-exclusion moves cluster feature means by <3% on all metrics — both archetypes are robust to aggregator removal. The two clusters jointly account for ~28% of 2026 senior postings (9,700 of 33,693 senior rows, 48% of 2026 senior rows alone). These are the two dominant emergent senior roles in 2026 SWE posting language.

    Final role-name proposals¶

    Cluster 0 (AI-oriented, low-orch in T21's label): - Primary proposal: Senior Applied-AI / LLM Engineer. - Alternate 1: Senior AI Systems Engineer (production-focused framing). - Alternate 2: Staff AI / Agent Engineer (if targeting the 8% staff-titled subset). - Novel finding: this role name did not exist at meaningful scale in 2024. Content and title distinguish it from traditional ML Engineer (which is more research-adjacent / modeling-focused). The 2026 instance emphasizes production LLM integration, RAG, agentic workflows, AI-tool use (Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, Cursor), and prompt/evaluation engineering — this is a Senior Applied-AI Engineer, a distinct role from ML Engineer or AI Research Engineer.

    Cluster 1 (Tech-orch, non-AI): - Primary proposal: Senior Data-Platform / DevOps Engineer. - Alternate 1: Senior Data Engineer (Pipelines & Workflows) — for the DE-dominant subset. - Alternate 2: Senior Platform / SRE Engineer (CI/CD & Orchestration) — for the SRE-dominant subset. - Note: cluster 1 is less sharp than cluster 0 because it bundles two related-but-distinct archetypes (DE + DevOps/SRE). A k=6 or k=7 clustering would likely split these. The role-name should acknowledge the dual character; "Data-Platform / DevOps" captures both. This archetype ALSO existed in 2024 but grew +11.4 pp as a share — it is "expanding", not "emergent" in the 15.6× sense of cluster 0.

    10. Surprises and unexpected patterns¶

    1. Cluster 0's title distribution has 28% explicit "AI Engineer" at face value; true share with qualified variants is likely >45%. The pattern is clear, and "AI Engineer" is emerging as a distinct senior title category — not just "ML Engineer" dressed up. The 15.6× growth (144 → 2,251) is the largest archetype growth in the senior-SWE posting space 2024→2026.

    2. Financial services is the #2 industry in cluster 0 (16.5%), after Software Development. This is not a consumer-tech or startup-dominated archetype. Banks and fintech firms (Capital One, JPMorgan, Wells Fargo) are heavily represented. AI-oriented SWE senior roles are an ENTERPRISE phenomenon, not a tech-bubble phenomenon — consistent with Gate 2 finding #7.

    3. Cluster 0 is CONCENTRATED in T16's ai_forward_scope_inflator employer cluster at 30% — much higher than its 11% overall share of 2026 senior postings. Cluster 0 is organic to a subset of employers that have pivoted toward AI. It's not universally distributed.

    4. Cluster 1 is MOST concentrated in traditional_hold_2 (28.5%) and traditional_hold_1 (24.6%) employer clusters — the companies that haven't pivoted toward AI or scope inflation. Data/DevOps work is the quiet backbone at employers whose posting language is otherwise stable. This is the "modern SWE infrastructure default."

    5. Median YOE is 6 years for cluster 0 vs 5 for cluster 1. AI-oriented senior roles ask for ONE MORE year of experience than traditional senior SWE. This is counter to a possible hypothesis that AI tooling would lower the senior bar (because AI lets seniors do more). Instead AI roles are the HIGHEST-YOE variant of the senior archetypes — supporting the "sharpened seniority" finding (T15/T20).

    6. Cluster 0 n-gram hero tokens include specific AI tools: Claude, Cursor, Copilot, RAG, LangChain, LlamaIndex, Hugging Face. The cluster is characterized by specific toolchain mentions, not generic "ML" language. This is the "working with these specific technologies" signature — consistent with H_K (ecosystem crystallization) as tested by T35.

    7. Cluster 1's n-gram set surfaces the Alignerr / Scale / RLHF / annotation-contract market. Trigrams like "data annotation validation", "evaluation workflows develop", "teams support model", "native fluent english speaker", "commit hours per" (week) are classic annotation-contract language. A meaningful sub-segment of cluster 1 is data-contract work supporting AI model training — indirect AI-market activity that does NOT show up as ai_binary=1 because the ad itself doesn't require AI skill, it requires data-engineering skill AT an AI lab.

    8. Directors are 2× more common in cluster 0 than cluster 1 (1.9% vs 1.0%) — AI-oriented senior roles disproportionately include director titles. T21 found the director AI-strict rose 1.1% → 14.8% (13.4× growth). T34's 1.9% director share in cluster 0 corresponds to 45 director postings — small absolute but 3× the share in cluster 1.

    9. Cluster 0 has HHI 38.6 (Gini 0.44), relatively LOW for a senior archetype. It is broadly distributed across 1,163 distinct companies. AI-oriented senior work is NOT a concentrated "3 companies do 80% of it" market; it's reasonably democratized across ~1,000 firms that have at least one such posting.

    10. The T21 label "AI-oriented (low-orch)" is misleading. Cluster 0 has orch_density 1.86 — only slightly below the tech-orch-non-AI cluster's 2.27, and far above every other cluster. It is NOT "low orch"; its orch density is high, just composed of AI-flavored orch terms rather than CI/CD-flavored terms. The T21 label emphasized its relative rank vs cluster 1; the absolute picture is that cluster 0 is orch-heavy AND AI-heavy.

    Caveats¶

    1. Title categorization regex is conservative — 26–38% of titles land in "other_unmatched". Manual spot-check confirms these are compound / qualified variants of the same categories. The share estimates are lower bounds.
    2. T09 archetype projection via nearest-centroid is noisy for rows far from any existing cluster centroid. Projection covers 62% of cluster 0 and 76% of cluster 1; 24–38% of the cross-tab rows are "archetype unknown". The over-representation ratios are computed within the covered subset.
    3. Cluster 1 ("Tech-orch non-AI") is visibly a mixture of two related archetypes (data-engineering + DevOps/SRE) plus the data-contract work at AI labs. A finer (k=6-7) clustering would probably split it cleanly. The dual-name proposal reflects this.
