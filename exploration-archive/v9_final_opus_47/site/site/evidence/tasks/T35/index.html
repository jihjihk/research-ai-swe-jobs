    T35 — Technology ecosystem crystallization (H_K)¶

    Agent S · Wave 3.5 · 2026-04-20 Input: swe_tech_matrix.parquet (68,137 × 135) + data/unified.parquet (company/period metadata) + T28_corpus_with_archetype.parquet (archetype cross-tab) + T14 as reference. Frame: default filter (is_swe AND source_platform='linkedin' AND is_english AND date_flag='ok'). Period split: 2024 = arshkon+asaniczka pooled, 2026 = scraped. Company cap 50 per company_name_canonical within each period. phi threshold 0.15 (matches T14). Script: exploration/scripts/T35_tech_ecosystem_crystallization.py (runtime 2.7 s). Primary Louvain seed 42; stability reported across 10 seeds. Tables in exploration/tables/T35/; figure in exploration/figures/T35/network_sidebyside.png.

    Headline¶

    Tech co-occurrence networks crystallized 2024 → 2026: modularity +0.029 (0.632 → 0.662), edges +24% (317 → 393), communities 9 → 10, all on the same 135-tech taxonomy. The shift is not dramatic — 5 of 10 2026 communities are "stable_partial" with Jaccard ≥ 0.57 against their 2024 counterparts — but it is directionally real, seed-robust (modularity SD < 0.002 both periods), and survives aggregator exclusion (+0.022 Δ) and cap-20 (+0.029 Δ).

    The biggest structural change is on the data side, not the AI side. T14's headline phi-pair crystallizations (pinecone×weaviate 0→0.71, rag×llm 0.20→0.51) produced the coalescence of an LLM/AI cluster in 2024 already (pooled arshkon+asaniczka had enough adjacency at phi > 0.15 to form a 16-tech cluster). What crystallized in 2026 was:

    1. AI coding-tools split off from the LLM-engineering cluster into their own tight 5-tech community (copilot, cursor_tool, chatgpt, claude_tool, codex) — genuine new-in-2026 clustering that didn't exist in 2024 (4 of 5 members had no above-threshold co-occurrence in 2024).
    2. Legacy data-eng fragmented into a modern data-eng stack (Snowflake, dbt, Airflow, Databricks, BigQuery, Spark, Flink) and a web-backend/PHP cluster (PHP, Laravel, Django, MongoDB, Redis) — a clean two-way split of 2024's composite comm 2.
    3. Python ML stack separated from Hugging Face into a traditional-ML cluster (pandas, numpy, sklearn, pytorch, tensorflow, keras, jupyter, mlflow, xgboost) — hugging_face migrated to the LLM cluster.

    Step 5 — LLM-vendor cluster verification¶

    Key correction vs T14 narrative. T14 showed specific phi pairs crystallizing (pinecone×weaviate 0→0.71). At the cluster level, the pooled 2024 baseline (arshkon+asaniczka) already had a 16-tech LLM/AI cluster with phi > 0.15 connectivity — it did not "form" in 2026. What happened in 2026:

    • Same core cluster persisted (Jaccard 0.65 with the 2024 counterpart): langchain, llamaindex, rag, ai_agent, llm, pinecone, weaviate, vector_database, prompt_engineering, hugging_face, fine_tuning, anthropic, gpt_model.
    • 4 new arrivals that weren't above threshold in 2024: mcp, gemini, chroma, claude_api. These are the cluster's 2026 crystallization.
    • Hugging Face migrated into the LLM cluster (was in 2024 traditional-ML dl cluster).
    • AI coding tools (copilot, cursor_tool, chatgpt, claude_tool, codex) split OFF into their own distinct 2026 community. In 2024 they co-occurred with langchain/rag/llm; in 2026 their within-cluster phi exceeds their phi with the engineering stack, so Louvain separates them.

    Classification of the 2026 LLM-vendor cluster: stable_partial (not "coalesced" or "new" — T14's headline overstated the newness). But comm 7 (AI dev tools) is effectively a quasi-new cluster under the 50% new-arrival threshold (40% new = "weakly_mapped" by the rule, but 4 of its 5 members either didn't exist in 2024 or weren't above threshold).

    Dispatch question: which techs were NOT above phi > 0.15 in 2024 but ARE in the 2026 LLM-vendor community? Answer (from llm_vendor_cluster.csv): claude_api, gemini, mcp, chroma (4 techs; the 5th "expected member" openai_api is still sub-threshold in 2026 too — real adoption is low). The coalescence happened in 2024 already per the pooled panel; 2026 added branded-model-and-vector-store members.

    Table: llm_vendor_cluster.csv.

    Step 6 — other crystallizations (named communities)¶

    Coalesced / new clusters (beyond the LLM-vendor stack):

    1. AI dev-tools cluster (comm 7, n=5: copilot + cursor_tool + chatgpt + claude_tool + codex). 4 of 5 members (all except chatgpt) had no 2024 phi > 0.15 edges. This is the cleanest genuine-new 2026 clustering in the corpus. Modularity contribution is small (tight internal but narrow span). In T09 archetypes this aligns most with ML/LLM archetype (AI/ML domain share 0.06 vs 0.03 overall) but it is not a dedicated domain — rather, a cross-domain tool-use signal.

    2. CI/CD narrow cluster (comm 8, n=2: circleci + travis_ci). Both legacy-but-still-used tools. 2024 had no circleci↔travis_ci edge above threshold because circleci was embedded in the devops_platform_stack. This is "newly isolated" rather than "newly emerging" — CI/CD as a practice is inside the devops_platform_stack (which contains github_actions, gitlab_ci, jenkins, ci_cd itself). Low interpretive weight (n=2).

    3. Observability fully merged into devops_platform (comm 1, n=24). In 2024 observability was a separate cluster (comm 7, n=7: datadog, new_relic, pagerduty, grafana, prometheus, splunk, elasticsearch). In 2026 all 7 members sit in the 2026 devops_platform_stack. This is an observability→devops coalescence, visible as cross-community merger rather than a new community. The 2026 devops_platform_stack gained 3 nodes net vs 2024.

    4. Modern data-eng stack (comm 4, n=13: airflow, beam, bigquery, databricks, dbt, hadoop, snowflake, spark, flink, scala, sql, sql_server, c_sharp, dot_net). Fragmented out of 2024's mixed comm 2. Core members (Snowflake, dbt, Airflow, Databricks) co-occur at higher phi than they do with web-backend DBs (Postgres/MongoDB), which migrated to the php-stack cluster. (c_sharp/dot_net/sql_server grouped here because they co-occur with SQL/SSIS-style warehouse stacks in legacy-enterprise postings.)

    Expected candidates from dispatch — verified presence: - Observability stack (Datadog + New Relic + PagerDuty + Grafana) → subsumed into devops_platform_stack. - Data-engineering stack (Snowflake + dbt + Airflow + Kafka) → Kafka lives in php_laravel_stack but Snowflake+dbt+Airflow+Databricks cleanly cluster in data_engineering_stack. - DevOps/platform (Terraform + Kubernetes + Helm + ArgoCD + CircleCI) → Terraform/K8s/Helm/ArgoCD in devops_platform_stack; CircleCI split into its own tiny n=2 cluster.

    Surprises¶

    1. LLM cluster already existed in 2024 pooled (comm 3, n=16). The narrative that "AI ecosystem formed between 2024 and 2026" is overstated at the cluster level — what formed was the edge-weighted adjacency (phi rose from near-zero to 0.5-0.7 on specific pairs), but the tech set was largely already co-occurring above the 0.15 threshold in 2024 because arshkon+asaniczka pooled has ~19K rows. The 2026 change is an absorption of 4 new members (mcp, gemini, chroma, claude_api) plus a split into LLM-engineering vs AI dev-tools sub-clusters.

    2. AI dev-tools split off into their own community in 2026. This is Louvain's finding that copilot+cursor+chatgpt+claude_tool+codex co-occur more tightly with each other than with langchain/rag/pinecone in 2026. Interpretation: "AI tools at the desk" (editors, chatbots) now live in postings separately from "LLM engineering" (vector DBs, retrieval, prompt engineering, fine-tuning). The developer-productivity use case is semantically distinct from the LLM-systems-engineering use case.

    3. Observability disappeared as a separate cluster in 2026 — Datadog/New Relic/PagerDuty/Grafana/Prometheus/Splunk all joined the devops_platform_stack. Interpretation: observability tools are now mentioned in the same postings as K8s/Terraform/Docker/AWS — platform work has consolidated. This is consistent with T14's CI/CD Δ +20.6pp as the largest S4 riser.

    4. Data-eng cluster fragmented (Snowflake/dbt/Airflow/Databricks separated from Kafka/MongoDB/PostgreSQL). 2026 postings distinguish "warehouse + analytics stack" from "event + web-backend DB stack" more sharply. 2024 postings mentioned both more interchangeably.

    5. Python ML stack is "stable_partial" not growing. The pandas/numpy/sklearn/pytorch/tensorflow constellation already existed in 2024 as a clean cluster; it maintained its coherence (Jaccard 0.64) but did NOT coalesce further. The real AI growth lives in the LLM cluster.

    6. Louvain extreme stability (SD < 0.002) — expected this to be noisier. The underlying phi matrix is dense enough that Louvain's randomness doesn't matter.

    7. Only 2 true "new" or "weakly_mapped" communities — the fragmentation and merger events (devops+observability, data-eng→2 pieces, ruby separation) dominate over genuine new cluster formation. Most 2026 communities descend from 2024 communities with modification.

    Limitations¶

    1. Company cap is applied within period_era — not within-source within-period. A high-posting employer active in both 2024 arshkon and 2026 scraped can contribute up to 50 postings per period. This is the design: we want per-period structure, not per-source.

    2. Phi threshold 0.15 is arbitrary but T14-consistent. Lowering to 0.10 would add more edges and reduce modularity (more blurred); raising to 0.20 would produce smaller clusters and higher modularity. Direction of Δ (rising modularity 2024→2026) should survive threshold variation because both periods use the same threshold, but magnitude would shift.

    3. AI dev-tools n=5 cluster is tight but small. Its "40% new-arrivals" classification is sensitive to the threshold rules. Functionally it is a new-in-2026 cluster; formally under the dispatch rules it is "weakly_mapped." Report as "quasi-new."

    4. Modularity Δ of +0.03 is small in absolute terms. Louvain modularity on realistic networks commonly sits in 0.5-0.8; a 0.03 absolute rise is ~4.6% relative. Directionally clear; substantively moderate. Do not over-state "dramatic crystallization."

    5. T14's specific-pair phi crystallizations (pinecone×weaviate 0→0.71) are real and V1-verified. The cluster-level finding here adds that these pairs sit inside an already-coherent cluster that has been stable-plus-accretionary rather than newly-formed.

    Headline claims for SYNTHESIS¶

    • Tech co-occurrence networks crystallized modestly 2024 → 2026: modularity +0.029 under primary spec (0.632 → 0.662); n_communities 9 → 10; n_edges +24% — evidence: per_period_network_summary.csv, louvain_stability_10seeds.csv, sensitivity verdict: robust (SD < 0.002 across 10 seeds; Δ survives aggregator-excluded and cap-20).

    • The LLM/AI community existed in 2024 already (pooled arshkon+asaniczka, n=16 techs at phi > 0.15) and is classified "stable_partial" (Jaccard 0.65) in 2026, with 4 new arrivals: mcp, gemini, chroma, claude_api. T14's phi-pair crystallizations occurred inside an already-coherent cluster. — evidence: llm_vendor_cluster.csv, community_classification.csv, sensitivity verdict: robust (Jaccard and membership stable across seeds and specs).

    • In 2026 an "AI dev-tools" cluster separated from the LLM-engineering stack: copilot + cursor_tool + chatgpt + claude_tool + codex form their own tight community (4 of 5 members had no 2024 phi > 0.15 edges). — evidence: communities_2026.csv (comm 7), llm_vendor_cluster.csv, sensitivity verdict: partial (n=5 members; weakly_mapped by numerical threshold but semantically coherent new cluster — report as "quasi-new").

    • Observability tools merged into the devops/platform cluster in 2026; the 2024 observability-only cluster no longer exists separately. — evidence: communities_2024.csv (comm 7) vs communities_2026.csv (comm 1), sensitivity verdict: robust (merger holds across seeds and specs).

    • The 2024 composite data-eng cluster fragmented in 2026 into a modern warehouse/analytics stack (Snowflake + dbt + Airflow + Databricks + BigQuery + Spark + Flink) and a web-backend/PHP stack (PHP + Laravel + Django + Kafka + MongoDB + PostgreSQL + Redis). — evidence: fragmented_2024_communities.csv, community_classification.csv (rows 3 and 4), sensitivity verdict: robust (fragmentation present under all 3 specs).

    • Each 2026 community is archetype/domain-concentrated: LLM-vendor 0.47 in ai_ml domain (9× baseline), data-eng stack 0.69 in data_eng domain (3.3× baseline), python_ml_stack 0.21 in ai_ml (4.2× baseline), ai_dev_tools FLAT across domains (0.02-0.06) — AI coding tools are spreading universally while LLM engineering remains ai_ml-concentrated. — evidence: archetype_domain_crosstab.csv, sensitivity verdict: robust (T09-projected archetypes, large samples).

    • Louvain stability was excellent (SD 0.0019 in 2024, SD 0.0005 in 2026; n_comm range 2 in 2026), meeting the dispatch-pre-committed robustness threshold (SD ≤ 0.05, range ≤ 2). — evidence: louvain_stability_10seeds.csv, sensitivity verdict: robust.
