    T12 — Open-Ended Text Evolution¶

    Wave 2, Agent H. Run: 2026-04-20. Depends on T13's section classifier (exploration/scripts/T13_section_classifier.py).

    Inputs: exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_cleaned_text.parquet (68,137 rows, 48,223 with LLM-cleaned text). Primary text source: description_cleaned (LLM-cleaned, stopword-stripped). Token counting: my custom tokenizer preserves compound tech tokens (c++, c#, dotnet) and breaks on ., /, , etc. Company stoplist applied, with AI-tool whitelist so names like copilot, langchain, anthropic are NOT stripped.

    Headline verdict — Period shift is AI + infra dominant¶

    The 2024 → 2026 text evolution is dominated by AI-concept terms (LLM, agent, RAG, prompts, embeddings, multimodal, multi-agent, MCP, LangChain family) and cloud/platform-infra terms (workflows, pipelines, environments, observability, distributed, large-scale, backend, CI/CD). Legacy stack terms (Scala, PHP, COBOL, Puppet, Drupal, Oracle-era DB tooling) decline. The pattern holds whether we compare full text, section-filtered text (requirements + responsibilities only), label-based (J3/S4 primary, J1/S1 sensitivity), or YOE-band (≤2 / ≥5).

    Critical relabeling diagnostic: 2026 entry postings do NOT resemble relabeled 2024 senior postings — they resemble 2026 senior postings (minus years/lead/mentor terms). Both the J3-label-based and the YOE-≤2-band-based diagnostics agree. Content shifted period-wide, within seniority bands; it did not move down the seniority ladder. This is consistent with T13's finding that junior REQUIREMENTS share actually dropped (−6.6 pp) — juniors are not getting reassigned senior scope; the entire market moved to AI+infra language.

    2. Section-filtered comparison (requirements + responsibilities only)¶

    Stripped all boilerplate (summary, preferred, benefits, about, legal) using the T13 classifier. Coverage: arshkon 3,506 of 4,653 rows (75%), scraped 23,252 of 25,509 (91%). The 2024 gap reflects that arshkon postings often lack a clear requirements header and go straight to inline text.

    Top 10 UP in 2026 reqresp: qualifications (z=27.2), workflows (22.5), pipelines (19.5), environments (19.4), systems (16.3), hands-on (15.5), observability (14.3), backend (14.2), apis (13.6), contribute (13.4). Top 10 remain AI/infra dominant even after stripping boilerplate.

    AI terms that survive section-filter: llm (z=11.4), agent (11.0), llms (10.5), rag (10.3), models (11.9). Section-filtering does not dampen the AI signal — the AI language is in requirements/responsibilities, not in benefits/about/legal.

    This is the key T13-T12 cross-check: T13 found requirements section grew only +11% in absolute chars and shrank in share. T12 finds that WITHIN those (smaller-share) requirements sections, the actual content is heavily AI+infra. The requirements are not fewer in number but different in kind.

    Relabeling diagnostic: Entry 2026 vs Mid-senior 2024 (label-based)¶

    If 2026 entry postings resemble 2024 senior postings (seniority-dominant relabeling), entry-2026-specific terms should be mostly senior-role vocabulary. If they resemble a period-wide shift applied to entry roles (period-dominant), the terms should be 2026-period vocabulary.

    Top UP in entry_J3_2026 vs midsenior_S4_2024: workflows (z=36.1), contribute (29.1), features (25.6), pipelines (25.2), backend (23.0), llm (22.3), feedback (21.3), familiarity (21.2), exposure (20.8), models (20.0), accessibility (19.9).

    Top UP in midsenior_S4_2024 (down in entry_J3_2026): develops (-6.7), description (-6.7), j2ee (-6.7), empowerment (-6.6), functions (-6.6), verification (-6.5), soap (-6.5), months (-6.6), like (-6.6).

    The DOWN side is pure 2024-era-senior vocabulary (J2EE, SOAP, "empowerment", "develops/describes" phrasing). The UP side is 2026 AI-period vocabulary. Verdict: period-dominant, not seniority-dominant relabeling. This is the cleanest within-paper evidence that 2026 junior roles look like 2026 roles, not like 2024 senior roles.

    6. Within-2024 calibration: arshkon S1 vs asaniczka S1¶

    n_arshkon=2,003, n_asaniczka=11,509 (both mid-senior label). Top UP in arshkon (same year, both midsenior): azure (z=17.7), backend (15.3), cd (14.1), pipelines (12.6), ensuring (11.1), problem-solving (10.7), scalability (10.6), application (10.1), cross-functional (9.5).

    Within-2024 (same year, same seniority) differences on these generic infra terms are substantial. pipelines, cd, backend, scalability all show within-2024 z > 10. Cross-period z-scores for the same terms (primary FW) run 12–55. Ratio of cross-period to within-2024 ranges from 0.7× (cd) to 3.5× (workflows).

    Implication: infra terms (pipelines/cd/backend/scalability) have LOW SNR for cross-period claims because they drift between 2024 sources at similar magnitude. AI terms (llm, agent, rag) do NOT appear in the within-2024 list at all — they are 2026-specific. So the AI signal is clean; the infra-term signal is noisy and should be read cautiously.

    7. BERTopic cross-validation¶

    Lightweight BERTopic fit on 5,000 balanced sample (2,500 per period) using the prep MiniLM embeddings. min_cluster_size=30, no class-conditioning during fit; period used as class after assignment.

    Result: only 3 topics found. - Topic 0 (generic SWE): 2,430 / 2,421 split (2024 / 2026), diff −9. Words: software, systems, solutions, requirements, including, ability, management, degree. - Topic 1 (mobile): 56 / 67, diff +11. Words: android, ios, kotlin, software, practices, apis, features, teams. - Topic -1 (noise): 14 / 12.

    BERTopic at this parameterization did not resolve the AI-shift signal — one mega-cluster absorbs nearly everything. The Fightin' Words result is stronger; BERTopic here serves only as a null — the period difference is too fine-grained for min_cluster_size=30 on 5k docs. A higher-resolution topic model (HDBSCAN with smaller min_cluster_size, or multiple BERTopic runs with different UMAP seeds) could pick it up, but given FW's strong findings and the 26-second runtime cap, this is a satisfactory methods-methods-cross-check null result: the FW signal is not contradicted.

    The cleaner archetype work by T09 (domain-axis, LLM-era AI-concept archetype +4.8 pp period shift) is the better complement to T12. T12's FW and T09's archetype results agree: 2026 shifted toward AI-content domains.

    Surprises and unexpected patterns¶

    1. Section-filtered Fightin' Words top terms are essentially identical to full-text top terms. The AI/infra shift is not boilerplate — it survives stripping benefits/about/legal sections (T13 classifier). In fact, AI terms are CLEANER in reqresp-only (z=10–11 for llm/agent/rag without the noise from boilerplate-rich 2026 postings).
    2. Relabeling diagnostic agreement. Label-based (J3 vs S4) and YOE-based (YOE ≤ 2 vs YOE ≥ 5) relabeling diagnostics give IDENTICAL top-10 terms. No label drift is inflating the entry-2026-vs-senior-2024 signal. Period-dominance is robust.
    3. RAG accelerated 75× 2024 → 2026. "rag" moved from 22 total mentions to 2,128. That's the largest single-term acceleration in the corpus.
    4. MCP (Model Context Protocol) emerged from 20 → 740 mentions (29×). MCP was released in late 2024 by Anthropic; the 2026 scraped window shows it already integrated into SWE postings at nontrivial rates. New protocol adoption in 12-18 months.
    5. Multi-agent 24× and agentic/agent 17× — agentic architecture is a named skill in 2026 postings. In 2024 only 20-120 postings mentioned it; in 2026 over 2,500.
    6. Legacy stack decline is REAL but MUCH smaller in magnitude than AI growth. The top negative terms (scala, powershell, jdbc, cobol, puppet, drupal) have |z| = 5-8 while the top positive terms have |z| = 15-28. The 2024→2026 period is additive on AI, not subtractive on legacy. Employers are not replacing legacy stacks; they are adding AI work on top.
    7. Within-2024 arshkon vs asaniczka differences on "backend", "pipelines", "cd", "scalability", "azure" are as large as the cross-period differences. These are the infra-terms; their SNR for cross-period claims is low. The AI terms (llm/agent/rag) do not appear in the within-2024 list — they are cleanly 2026-specific.
    8. c++ disappearing = tokenization bug. The prep step used for swe_cleaned_text.parquet stripped the + from scraped C\+\+ (escaped markdown). This does NOT reflect a real C++ decline; the tech_matrix (built from raw description with the escape-fix) shows C++ at 18.6% of scraped rows. T12's disappearing list is unreliable for compound tech tokens.
    9. "familiarity" and "exposure" rose in entry postings (familiarity z=11.3 for J3 2024→2026, exposure z=20.8 for YOE≤2 2026 vs YOE≥5 2024). Employers writing entry JDs shifted to "familiarity with" / "exposure to" language — softer qualification wording. T13's +14% inclusive-density rise is consistent.

    Sensitivities¶

    • (a) Aggregator: not rerun separately; T13 aggregator sensitivity shows the section and tone patterns survive aggregator exclusion. T12 term-level patterns are not expected to flip.
    • (b) Company capping: not applied here; the entry-J3 sample is small enough (1,661 / 2,879) that dominant-firm concentration is a concern. Future iteration: cap at 20-50 per firm, rerun FW on J3.
    • (d) Text source: primary uses LLM cleaned text (description_cleaned in swe_cleaned_text.parquet). Raw-description sensitivity not run (scraped raw has too much boilerplate and different formatting to compare directly). T13's text-source sensitivity confirms readability/tone directions are invariant to text source.
    • (e) Source restriction: section-filter uses arshkon + scraped only (asaniczka excluded from primary). Within-2024 uses arshkon vs asaniczka. Both work.
    • (f) Within-2024 calibration: reported in §6. Infra terms have low SNR, AI terms have high SNR.
    • © T30 panel: J3/S4 primary, J1/S1 sensitivity. Direction consistent across all four.
    • (g) SWE tier: not stratified by swe_classification_tier.

    What this means for downstream¶

    • T12 strongly supports the period-dominant reframe of RQ1. The "junior scope inflation" concern is partially addressed: entry postings in 2026 are not relabeled seniors; they are entry postings in the 2026 AI-period content universe. T13 showed the requirements section of juniors actually shrank in absolute chars. T12 shows the language INSIDE that (smaller) requirements section is dominated by AI/infra terms, the same as senior JDs.
    • For RQ2 (task and requirement migration across seniority): T12 does NOT find that 2026 entry language is a subset of 2024 senior language. Both 2026 entry and 2026 senior language are dominated by the same AI+infra terms. Migration ISN'T "scope flowing down the ladder"; it's "the whole market's language shifted."
    • For RQ3 (employer-requirement vs worker-usage divergence): the AI-term growth is so dramatic (RAG 75×, agent 17×, llm 15×) that it is almost guaranteed to outpace worker-usage benchmark movement. T32 will test this cross-occupation.
    • T29 (LLM-authored description detection, Wave 3): the recruiter-LLM drafting hypothesis predicts that 2026 postings are drafted by LLMs that know about these AI terms even when the role doesn't strictly need them. T29 should look for correlation between LLM-detection score and AI-term density.

    Caveats¶

    1. c++ tokenization artifact. The prep-step cleaning in swe_cleaned_text.parquet stripped + from scraped C\+\+ (escaped markdown) but preserved it in Kaggle sources. T12 shows C++ as the only "disappearing" term and as −6.1 z in primary FW. This is NOT a real C++ decline; the tech_matrix (escape-fixed raw description) shows C++ at 18.6% of scraped rows. Do not cite T12 for C++ trends.
    2. Company stoplist whitelist. The original prep stoplist included copilot and langchain as company names. For T12 I whitelisted these plus other AI-tool/product names (anthropic, openai, claude, cursor, codex, etc.) so the AI-tool signal is preserved. A purely stoplist-filtered version would miss these terms entirely.
    3. BERTopic at min_cluster_size=30 on 5k docs collapsed to 1 generic cluster. This is a null result for BERTopic cross-validation, not a contradiction of FW. A finer-grained topic model could validate the AI shift independently; at this parameterization it did not.
    4. Emerging threshold too strict. r26 > 0.001 requires count_2026 > 3,151; even agent (2,589) doesn't cross. Use the accelerating list for new-term evidence.
    5. Entry n sizes modest (J3: 1,661 / 2,879; J1: 414 / 1,182). Top-100 stability sensitive to sampling. Direction and top-20 robust; tail of the list (ranks 50-100) is noisier.
    6. Within-2024 calibration applies differently to different term types. AI terms have clean cross-period signal; generic infra terms (backend/pipelines/cd/scalability) have near-noise cross-period signal per the calibration. Use per-term SNR judgment when citing specific magnitudes.
