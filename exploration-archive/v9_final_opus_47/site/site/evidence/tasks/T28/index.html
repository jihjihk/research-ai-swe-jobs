    T28 — Domain-stratified scope changes by archetype¶

    Agent O (Wave 3). Date: 2026-04-20. Inputs: data/unified.parquet, exploration/artifacts/shared/swe_archetype_labels.parquet, swe_embeddings.npy + index, T11_posting_features.parquet, swe_tech_matrix.parquet. Script: exploration/scripts/T28_archetype_analysis.py. Outputs: exploration/tables/T28/.

    Headline¶

    Scope inflation is predominantly WITHIN-DOMAIN, not between-domain recomposition. Under the pooled-2024 → 2026 scope change:

    Metric Aggregate Δ Within-domain Between-domain Interaction
    requirement_breadth_resid +2.07 +1.27 (61%) +0.56 (27%) +0.24 (12%)
    tech_count +1.96 +1.21 (62%) +0.49 (25%) +0.26 (13%)
    scope_density +0.162 +0.125 (77%) +0.031 (19%) +0.006 (4%)
    credential_stack_depth +0.450 +0.385 (85%) +0.009 (2%) +0.056 (13%)
    ai_binary +0.127 +0.082 (64%) +0.016 (12%) +0.030 (24%)

    The 2024→2026 change in SWE posting scope is 60-85% within-domain. The Gate 2 "scope inflation universal, senior > junior" finding now has a clean decomposition: it is not a domain-mix shift; it is the same work being written as more expansive across domains.

    J3 entry share (+5.07 pp pooled-2024 → 2026) is also within-domain: +6.84 pp within, -0.02 pp between, -1.75 pp interaction. Domain recomposition does NOT explain the entry-share rise. (Between-firm composition — per T06 — still explains most of it at the company level; the archetype-level decomposition here is a cleaner cut.)

    AI/ML archetype is the single largest domain shift: 601 → 3,262 rows, AI-binary rate 15.1% → 45.7% (+30.6 pp), tech_count 6.4 → 8.9 (+2.55), description length 2,581 → 3,158 chars (+577). 50% of 2026 AI/ML volume is from employers not present in 2024 corpus.

    Senior > Junior scope inflation holds in most domains, not universally. Of 15 domains, S4 breadth delta exceeds J3 in 11 (including AI/ML, backend, cloud_devops, qa_test, data_eng). In 4 (intern_early, mobile, dotnet_legacy, customer_apps) J3 inflated more or both declined. In ai_ml specifically, J3 and S4 both inflated +~2.5, i.e. the AI/ML senior premium is narrower than in other domains.

    Steps and findings¶

    Step 1 — Archetype coverage and domain bucketing¶

    • T09 archetype labels: 8,000 of 48,223 labeled rows (17%), with "noise/outlier" dominant (n=3,741).
    • Nearest-centroid projection on embeddings: +40,223 rows projected to 37 non-noise archetypes (and noise-labeled rows got a "primary" nearest non-noise archetype).
    • Unassigned (no embedding): 19,914 rows, all raw-text scraped. T28 scope-related analyses are computed on the 48,223-row LLM-labeled subset; entry-share decomposition uses only rows with YOE, which is ~ the same set.
    • 37 non-noise archetype names bucketed into 15 domains (ai_ml, backend, cloud_devops, clearance_defense, data_eng, dotnet_legacy, embedded_firmware, customer_apps, frontend_full, generic_swe, intern_early, mobile, process_scrum, qa_test, research_academic + other).

    Step 2 — Entry-share decomposition¶

    J3 (YOE ≤ 2, primary):

    Component pp
    aggregate Δ +5.07
    within-domain +6.84
    between-domain -0.02
    interaction -1.75

    Interpretation. The domain mix essentially did not move the J3 entry share; the J3 rise is within-domain recomposition. Combined with Gate 1 T06 finding (J3 rise is between-company at arshkon-only n=125), we have a layered picture: the J3 share increase is NEW entrant FIRMS posting at existing DOMAINS, not existing firms shifting which domain they hire in.

    J1 / J2 (seniority_final=entry, label-based, pooled-2024):

    • Aggregate Δ: +2.81 pp (near-identical to one another because J1 and J2 coincide at the entry level in the pooled 2024 frame).
    • Within-domain: +3.40 pp; between: +0.01 pp; interaction: −0.60 pp.

    S4 (YOE ≥ 5):

    • Aggregate Δ: -7.58 pp.
    • Within-domain: -9.10 pp; between: -0.58 pp; interaction: +2.10 pp.

    The S4 share decline is almost entirely within-domain. A small positive interaction (+2.10 pp) suggests some of the domain mix changes favored domains with higher 2026 S4 rates, partially offsetting the within-domain senior-share collapse.

    Step 6 — AI/ML archetype deep dive¶

    Surprises and unexpected patterns¶

    1. Entry-share rise is within-domain, not between-domain. Going in, the expectation was that J3 rise might be heavily driven by the emergence of new AI/ML postings which tend to be senior. Actual: J3 within-domain +6.84 pp, between-domain -0.02 pp. The domain mix shift does NOT drive J3 share. Combined with T06's between-firm result, the J3 rise is: new firms post at existing domains and those new firms post more entry roles.

    2. AI/ML is a senior-dominated domain. J3 rate in AI/ML is 10% in both 2024 and 2026 — same as the corpus baseline. S4 rate is 63-66% (well above corpus). The narrative that "AI is replacing juniors" via AI/ML postings specifically is not supported: AI/ML hiring is senior. But the junior AI/ML postings exist and show comparable breadth inflation to senior (junior +2.39 breadth vs senior +2.88), so juniors who DO enter AI/ML face almost as expansive a requirement set as seniors.

    3. Clearance/defense is shrinking 5.8 pp of the 2026 SWE LinkedIn corpus, the biggest single-domain decline. This isn't about AI; this is a real posting-volume drop. The clearance domain still inflates breadth (+1.69 resid) — within-domain content growth even as the domain shrinks.

    4. dotnet_legacy domain has the SECOND-largest breadth inflation (+1.54 resid) despite shrinking 2.6 pp share. The remaining .NET/legacy postings in 2026 ask for MORE, not less, even as the domain recedes. Possible explanation: as generalist companies move off the legacy stack, the remaining .NET/Visual-Studio postings are from firms with specialized legacy needs that demand more depth.

    5. Senior-gap widening is universal but magnitude varies by domain. The simple Gate 2 "senior > junior" scope story holds in 11 of 15 domains. Where it doesn't (dotnet_legacy, process_scrum, intern_early, customer_apps), these are small or declining domains. AI/ML is an edge case where senior gap widening is small (+0.49 vs +1.0+ elsewhere).

    6. Researcher/PhD-archetype ("research_academic") scope actually DECREASED slightly in 2026. In a period where AI-research attention is high, the research-academic postings are relatively stable in scope. This is counter-narrative and deserves a look in Wave 3.5 — perhaps research postings in 2024 were already high-scope and did not need to inflate, or they moved out of "SWE" classification.

    7. qa_test domain inflated +1.46 breadth / +1.63 tech_count despite stable volume. The Gate 1 T10 finding of qa_testing -1.6 pp share is real, and now we see that the remaining QA postings are packing more tech/breadth. Classic "fewer but more demanding" pattern.

    8. 50/50 new/returning employer split in AI/ML 2026. This is a materially different mix than the overall returning-cohort share (55% per T06). New employers are over-represented in AI/ML specifically.

    Limitations¶

    1. Archetype coverage is 71% via nearest-centroid projection, with 29% unassigned (raw-text scraped). The decomposition numbers apply to the LLM-labeled-text subset.
    2. Domain bucketing is by the assistant, grouping 37 archetypes into 15 domains by substring rules on archetype names. A different bucketing could change individual-domain numbers, but the aggregate decomposition should be stable.
    3. The "other" domain catches residual archetypes not matched to any category; it shows a sharp scope DECREASE (-1.17 breadth). This is partly a bucketing-artifact and its interpretation is limited.
    4. Senior magnitudes are fragile on arshkon-only (S4 Δ -1.71 pp vs pooled -7.58 pp). Cite both consistently.

    Paper implications¶

    • The Gate 2 "scope inflation universal, senior > junior" finding is now strengthened with within-vs-between-domain decomposition. Scope change is not an artifact of domain-mix shift (e.g., "everyone just posts more AI/ML now, those postings are long, so scope rose"). Real within-domain content inflation across 12 of 15 domains.
    • The entry-share composition-shift story has a new layer. Gate 1 T06 showed J3 rise is between-COMPANY not within-company (at the arshkon-only n=125 panel). T28 shows it is also within-DOMAIN not between-domain. The consistent picture: firms new to posting SWE arrive, post at EXISTING domains, at HIGHER entry rates.
    • AI/ML is a senior-dominated adjacency emergence. The +10.2 pp AI/ML domain share rise is senior-biased (S4 rate 63-66%), and J3/S4 content gap is NARROW inside AI/ML. If there is a "junior exposure to AI work" subnarrative, it is specifically within AI/ML where juniors are few but face near-senior-scope requirements.
    • The remaining paper questions for RQ1-revised: (a) Does the within-domain content change reflect a real labor-market requirements shift, or is it text-tool mediated (T29 answer)? (b) Does the same within-domain pattern hold in SWE-adjacent and control occupations (T18 answer)? © Is senior role content reorganizing or just expanding (T21 answer, separately)?
