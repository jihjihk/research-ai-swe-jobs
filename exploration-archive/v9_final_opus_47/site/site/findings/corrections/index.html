    Measurement corrections¶

    Two Wave 2 findings were corrected during V1 adversarial verification. Publishing these corrections is itself a methods contribution — longitudinal posting-content studies need semantic pattern validation, or they will confabulate instrument artifacts as content shifts.

    Correction 1 — Management language is flat, not falling¶

    What T11 originally reported¶

    Senior management density fell between 2024 and 2026. The report attributed this to a "senior shift away from management."

    What V1 found¶

    The management pattern V1 audited had a semantic precision of 0.28 on a stratified 50-row sample. All four broad-tier tokens fail the 0.80 threshold (lead 0.12, team 0.08, stakeholder 0.18, coordinate 0.28, manage 0.22). The strict version was 0.55 precision, with sub-pattern hire at 0.07 ("contract-to-hire", "how-we-hire/accommodations" — HR metadata, not management responsibility) and performance_review at 0.25 ("code review", "peer review" in QA contexts).

    The corrected measurement (T21)¶

    V1 published a rebuilt pattern mgmt_strict_v1_rebuilt:

    \b(?:mentor(?:s|ed|ing)? (?:junior|engineers?|team(?:s)?|others|the team|engineering|peers|sd(?:e|es))|coach(?:es|ed|ing)? (?:team|engineers?|junior|peers)|direct reports?|headcount|hiring manager|hiring decisions?)\b

    T22 validated this rebuilt pattern at 0.98 to 1.00 precision on a fresh stratified 50-row sample.

    Result under the validated pattern (T21)¶

    • Mid-senior mgmt_rebuilt density: 0.039 to 0.038 — flat. SNR 0.1.
    • Director mgmt_rebuilt density: 0.031 to 0.026 — essentially flat.
    • Junior-side density: 0.000 to 0.004 — near-zero at both periods.
    • V2 independently replicated this: 0.034 to 0.034 on a fresh calculation.

    The corrected narrative¶

    The senior shift is toward orchestration, not away from management. T21 orch_density at mid-senior rose +0.67 per 1K chars (SNR 5.6). The "management exodus" claim collapses; the orchestration rise remains.

    This correction is the basis for B2 — Management flat, not falling — a measurement correction (Tier B).

    Correction 2 — T13 junior-requirements shrank is classifier-sensitive (demoted)¶

    What T13 originally reported¶

    Junior requirements-section characters shrank by about 5%, while responsibilities and benefits sections grew. The narrative was "length growth is boilerplate + responsibilities, not requirements."

    What V1 found¶

    Under T13's classifier, J3 requirements went 1,057 to 1,000 chars (-5.4%) — exactly reproducible. But a simpler-regex classifier (V1's independent implementation) gave +88% on the same J3 subset. The direction flips under the alternative classifier.

    What T29 added¶

    The T13 requirements-share decline near-disappears on the low-LLM-authorship subset: -0.025 to -0.002. The shrink is partly an authorship artifact.

    What T33 added¶

    Classifier-sensitivity extends from J3 to the aggregate level. T13 classifier: aggregate period coef -0.019; simple-regex: +0.030. Both HC3-robust p < 1e-13 but opposite sign.

    The corrected claim¶

    "Junior descriptions densified on AI/tech tokens; net requirements-section direction is classifier-uncertain."

    What survives direction-robust:

    • Length growth is boilerplate-led (benefits +89%, legal +80%, responsibilities +49%).
    • Requirements content changed (AI tokens, tech stack, orchestration) even if the section's share of the full description is classifier-dependent.

    This correction is the basis for D1 — Junior requirements shrank (demoted to flagged claim) (Tier D).

    What these corrections illustrate methodologically¶

    1. Semantic-precision validation is not optional. A 0.28-precision pattern will confabulate a content shift in the direction of whatever semantic neighbors dominate cross-period. Longitudinal posting studies need pattern-level precision audits.

    2. Classifier sensitivity is a first-class reported quantity. Dual-classifier reporting (or better, LLM-adjudicated spot-checks) is the minimum standard for section-based claims.

    3. Pattern validation artifacts persist. See exploration/artifacts/shared/validated_mgmt_patterns.json for the 7 primary patterns at >= 0.92 precision, each with semantic_precision_measured: true.
