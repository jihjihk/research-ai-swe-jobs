    What we rejected¶

    Five alternative explanations for the 2024-to-2026 SWE content shifts were tested at the quantitative level and rejected. These negative findings are a paper strength: they rule out leading prior hypotheses rather than leaving them as competing explanations.

    Five rejections¶

    1. Hidden hiring-bar lowering — REJECTED¶

    Hypothesis (H_B): requirement-section contraction means employers are lowering the bar.

    Evidence against (T33):

    • Classifier-sensitive magnitude: T13 adjusted period coefficient is -0.019 (shrink); a simpler-regex classifier gives +0.030 (growth). Both HC3-robust at p < 1e-13.
    • Hiring-bar-proxy correlations uniformly |ρ| <= 0.28, sign-flipping across classifiers.
    • Within-company Δ(req_share) × Δ(J3 share) ≈ 0 under both classifiers (n=356 overlap panel).
    • Narrative 50-sample: 0 of 50 contain explicit loosening language.
    • Δ(req_chars) × Δ(desc_length) r ≈ +0.35 supports "narrative expansion" over "hiring bar lowering."

    Report: T33.

    2. Legacy stack to AI substitution — REJECTED¶

    Hypothesis (H_L): 2024 legacy titles (Java/Drupal/PHP/.NET architect) disappeared into AI-enabled 2026 roles.

    Evidence against (T36):

    • 6 of 11 disappearing 2024 titles match 2026 neighbors via TF-IDF cosine in [0.30, 0.59].
    • 2026 neighbors' ai_strict rate is 3.6% — below the 14.4% market rate.
    • Content drift: SSIS/Unix-scripts/Drupal/PHP/Scala leave; Postgres/pgvector/CI-CD/microservices/Terraform/ArgoCD arrive.
    • 2-to-4-year YOE drop on architect-to-engineer substitutions.

    Substitution is legacy-stack to modern-stack, not legacy-to-AI.

    Report: T36.

    3. Hiring-market selectivity during the JOLTS trough — REJECTED¶

    Hypothesis (H_N): JOLTS Information openings at 0.71x 2023 avg (T07) means selective firms are writing richer JDs.

    Evidence against (T38):

    Metric Pearson r p-value Direction
    breadth_resid Δ -0.032 0.617 null
    ai_strict Δ -0.089 0.169 null
    mentor_on_S1 Δ -0.072 0.287 null
    desc_len_median Δ +0.203 0.0015 opposite
    yoe_llm_median Δ -0.008 0.899 null
    scope_v1 Δ -0.033 0.605 null

    Only desc_length is significant — positive direction. Volume-up firms write longer JDs, not shorter. Robust across three panels.

    Report: T38.

    4. LLM-authored JDs explain the content rise — REJECTED as dominant mediator¶

    Hypothesis: recruiters using LLMs to write JDs inflated AI-mention rates.

    Evidence against (T29):

    Metric Full corpus delta Low-LLM-quartile delta Percent preserved
    AI-binary Δ +0.131 +0.115 88%
    Tech count Δ +2.06 +1.99 97%
    Credential stack (J3) Δ +16.9 pp +16.6 pp 97%
    Scope density Δ +0.096 +0.123 128% (stronger)
    Requirement breadth_resid Δ +1.22 +0.98 80%
    Length growth +1,130 chars +583 chars 52% (half LLM-mediated)

    Content effects persist at 80 to 130% on the low-LLM-style subset. Length growth is half-LLM-mediated (boilerplate verbosity), but AI-mention, credential stacking, scope inflation, and CI/CD rise are real.

    Report: T29.

    5. Sampling-frame artifact — REJECTED¶

    Hypothesis (H_H): the 2026 scraped panel over-weights different kinds of firms than 2024 Kaggle sources, so headlines are composition-driven.

    Evidence against (T37):

    Metric Full Δ Returning-cohort Δ Ratio Verdict
    AI-strict prevalence +9.72 pp +8.36 pp 0.86 robust
    J3 entry share (pooled) +5.05 pp +6.17 pp 1.22 amplified
    S4 senior share (pooled) -7.62 pp -8.29 pp 1.09 amplified
    Breadth residualized (S4) +2.60 +2.55 0.98 robust
    Credential stack >= 5 (J3) +17.1 pp +16.5 pp 0.97 robust
    Scope kitchen-sink +22.82 pp +23.21 pp 1.02 robust
    CI/CD at S4 +20.62 pp +20.27 pp 0.98 robust
    AI-oriented senior cluster +10.19 pp +9.18 pp 0.90 robust

    13 of 15 headlines robust on the 2,109-firm returning cohort (55% of 2026 postings, 25% of 2026 unique firms). J3 and S4 directions intensify on returning-only. Only H_d J3 breadth_resid drops to 0.70 (partially robust).

    Report: T37.

    What these rejections mean¶

    With these five alternatives ruled out, the paper can claim the SWE content shifts are:

    • Same-firm (not a composition artifact).
    • Demand-side content change (not selectivity).
    • Real content, not boilerplate (not LLM-authorship drift).
    • Additive AI, not legacy-substituted AI (no disappearing roles replaced by AI-enabled ones).
    • Not a relaxed-bar compensation (not hiring-bar lowering hidden in narrative).

    This leaves genuine within-firm content rewriting plus new-archetype emergence (A2, A5) as the live causal stories for RQ4 interviews.
