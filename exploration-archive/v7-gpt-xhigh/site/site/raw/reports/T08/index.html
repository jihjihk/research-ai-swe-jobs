    T08. Distribution Profiling & Anomaly Detection¶

    Headline¶

    The strongest distributional facts are not a simple junior decline. In the default LinkedIn SWE frame, pooled-2024 to scraped-2026 junior shares rise under all J1-J4 definitions, but label-based J1/J2 are source-sensitive: arshkon-only to scraped moves slightly down while YOE-based J3/J4 move up. The more stable findings are: scraped 2026 has a large unknown-seniority pool, raw descriptions are much longer, AI/LLM/CI-CD/API/observability indicators rise sharply, and broad senior shares fall while director-only labels rise.

    The main anomaly is measurement/composition structure: low-YOE postings are usually not labeled entry. In scraped 2026, 72.4% of yoe_extracted <= 2 rows are seniority_final = 'unknown' and 21.5% are mid-senior; only 5.5% are J1 entry. That is a boundary-labeling problem, not a clean junior-volume story.

    Methodology¶

    Script: exploration/scripts/T08_distribution_profile.py.

    Frame: data/unified.parquet, source_platform = 'linkedin', is_english = true, date_flag = 'ok', is_swe = true. Current default frame has 63,701 LinkedIn SWE rows: arshkon 4,691, asaniczka 18,129, scraped March 2026 19,777, scraped April 2026 21,104.

    Memory posture: DuckDB PRAGMA memory_limit='4GB' and PRAGMA threads=1. The script materializes only grouped tables and small plotting frames. It loads shared artifacts rather than rebuilding them: T30 seniority panel, T09 archetype labels, cleaned text, and the shared tech matrix.

    Keyword definitions and denominators are documented in exploration/tables/T08/metric_definitions_and_denominators.csv. AI prevalence uses selected shared tech-matrix indicators for AI tools/domains, excluding generic numpy/pandas/scipy/sklearn. Org/scope language uses LLM-cleaned text only. Manual context audit: AI keyword screen 49/50 true positive precision; org/scope screen 50/50, saved in keyword_precision_audit.csv.

    Main Distribution Findings¶

    1. Description length grew materially beyond within-2024 instrument variation. Mean raw description length rises from 3,763 chars pooled 2024 to 4,912 chars scraped 2026 (+1,149); median rises from 3,594 to 4,862 (+1,269). In mid-senior rows, arshkon-to-scraped growth is 3.62x the arshkon-vs-asaniczka gap.

    2. AI/tool/domain prevalence is a large calibrated change. Selected AI tech-matrix prevalence rises from 3.75% pooled 2024 to 23.70% scraped 2026 (+19.94 pp). In mid-senior rows: arshkon 5.43%, asaniczka 3.55%, scraped 25.77%; calibration ratio 10.86. The audited AI context sample had 98% precision, with one false positive from MCP as a legacy certification.

    3. The low-YOE/entry-label split is the central junior anomaly. Among scraped 2026 J1 entry rows, 75.1% have missing YOE and only 20.4% have yoe_extracted <= 2. Among scraped 2026 low-YOE rows, only 5.5% are labeled entry.

    4. Arshkon native-entry is not ground truth. Arshkon seniority_native = 'entry' has 738 rows; among 458 YOE-known rows, mean YOE is 4.18, median is 3, 42.6% require 5+ years, and only 28.6% require <=2 years. Native entry can be a diagnostic, not a clean baseline.

    5. Tech stack intensity rises. Mean count of true shared tech indicators rises from 5.20 pooled 2024 to 7.26 scraped 2026 (+2.06). Mid-senior tech count calibration ratio is 5.10. The largest stack movers include Python (+17.0 pp pooled-to-scraped), CI/CD (+18.2 pp), API design (+14.4 pp), observability (+12.0 pp), LLM (+12.0 pp), Kubernetes (+9.0 pp), and AWS (+8.7 pp). CI/CD is not cleanly above within-2024 noise in the mid-senior calibration ratio (1.54), so keep it as a candidate rather than a settled temporal claim.

    Domain Decomposition¶

    T09 archetype coverage is high in historical rows and limited in scraped: arshkon 97.5%, asaniczka 92.6%, scraped March 31.6%, scraped April 29.1%. Domain results are therefore labeled-subset evidence.

    In the labeled subset, archetype composition shifts strongly: AI LLM Platforms rise from 3.1% to 17.4%, Product Backend from 6.9% to 15.2%, and Cloud DevOps from 10.0% to 15.9%; Enterprise Application Support falls from 33.9% to 18.3%.

    The junior-share decomposition does not support domain recomposition as the main explanation for pooled junior increases:

    Definition Total change Within-domain component Between-domain component Interpretation
    J1 +3.16 pp +3.30 pp -0.15 pp Within-domain rise, not domain mix
    J2 +3.38 pp +3.61 pp -0.23 pp Same
    J3 +5.97 pp +6.77 pp -0.80 pp Same, between-domain slightly offsets
    J4 +8.64 pp +8.40 pp +0.24 pp Mostly within-domain

    Subpopulation anomaly: Cloud DevOps Infrastructure is consistently least junior-heavy in scraped 2026 (J1 2.1%, J3 9.3%, J4 24.7%), while Large Employer Data Platforms is unusually low-YOE-heavy (J1 12.0%, J3 37.1%, J4 60.8%). T09 already flagged this archetype as partly employer-template-driven, so do not overinterpret it as a clean domain effect.

    Sensitivity Checks¶

    Aggregator exclusion: J1/J2/J3/J4 directions are stable. S1/S4 stay down and S2 stays up. AI prevalence and tech count are stable. Aggregator exclusion attenuates broad-senior declines, especially raw-title S3, but does not flip the senior verdict.

    Company cap: capping at 50 postings per company leaves J1/J2 growth almost unchanged but materially reduces low-YOE growth: J3 pooled-to-scraped falls from +5.98 pp to +3.04 pp; J4 from +8.12 pp to +4.80 pp. Company concentration is a first-order mechanism for YOE-based junior movement.

    SWE tier exclusion: excluding swe_classification_tier = 'title_lookup_llm' preserves the main directions: J1 +1.45 pp all, J3 +6.00 pp known, S1 -16.08 pp all, S4 -7.39 pp known.

    Source restriction: label-based J1/J2 are the sensitive case. Pooled 2024 to scraped is up, but arshkon-only to scraped is slightly down. YOE-based J3/J4 are up under both source choices.

    Within-2024 calibration: description length, AI prevalence, org/scope language, tech count, Python, Kubernetes, AWS, LLM, observability, and API design are above source noise. Junior/senior share effects are mostly not above source noise except sparse S2 and artifact-prone S3.

    Indeed cross-platform: scraped Indeed and scraped LinkedIn broadly agree in 2026. Indeed has longer descriptions (median 5,229-5,391 vs LinkedIn 4,803-4,920), similar raw-regex AI rates (35.6-36.8% vs 35.2-35.7%), similar J1 entry shares (~3.0% vs 2.6-3.6%), and similar J3 low-YOE shares (~17.9-18.4% vs 17.0-17.4%). Seniority unknown remains high on both platforms.

    Company size/posting volume: within arshkon, the largest company-size quartile has higher J1 (4.64%) and J3 (19.90%) than the smallest quartile (2.92%, 11.95%). In scraped 2026, the highest posting-volume quartile has lower J1 (2.89%) than low-volume quartiles (4.08-4.37%), but comparable/higher J3 (17.74%) and higher aggregator share (18.38%).

    Anomaly List¶

    Severity Area Finding Gate 2 action
    High Text coverage Scraped LinkedIn cleaned-text coverage averages 30.7%. Treat text/domain findings as labeled-subset evidence.
    High Seniority Scraped seniority unknown share averages 53.4%. Keep all/known denominators and YOE validators.
    High Native labels Arshkon native-entry rows have 42.6% YOE>=5 among YOE-known. Do not treat native entry as ground truth.
    Medium Description length Length growth exceeds within-2024 variation. Normalize text metrics by posting and length.
    Medium AI keywords AI prevalence is a large calibrated increase. Validate downstream AI requirement semantics by subpattern.
    Medium Domain labels T09 coverage in scraped is ~30%. Domain claims need coverage caveats.
    Medium Metric changes Top changes mix real content and pipeline coverage. Separate market movement from source/coverage artifacts.
    Low Company size company_size is heavy-tailed in arshkon. Use quartiles/log transforms, not raw means.

    Surprises & Unexpected Patterns¶

    • The most important junior result is disagreement, not direction: employer entry labels and low-YOE proxies capture mostly different rows.
    • Domain recomposition does not explain the labeled-subset junior increase; within-domain changes dominate J1-J4.
    • AI LLM Platforms is not the least junior-heavy archetype; Cloud DevOps is.
    • Arshkon native entry is much noisier than expected and often not low-YOE.
    • Company capping materially reduces YOE-based junior growth but barely changes label-based J1/J2, implying company mix matters more for YOE floors than explicit entry labels.

    Data Caveats¶

    • Asaniczka has no native entry labels; any native-label diagnostic is arshkon-only.
    • seniority_final is conservative and unknown-heavy. Dropping unknowns is not representative.
    • T09 archetypes cover only LLM-cleaned rows with sufficient text; scraped 2026 domain evidence is subset evidence.
    • Remote flags remain a 2024 artifact and are not interpreted here.
    • Company industry is missing for asaniczka and should not be pooled as a full historical control.
    • Posting age is nearly unavailable: >99% missing in scraped LinkedIn and 100% missing in historical sources.

    Downstream Action Items¶

    1. Treat junior-share direction as unresolved/source-sensitive. Use J1 for employer-labeled junior, J3 for powered low-YOE validation, and J4 as broad sensitivity.
    2. Investigate the low-YOE unknown pool directly: title families, companies, domains, and whether descriptions use senior-scope language despite low YOE.
    3. For AI/LLM claims, split AI-tool requirements from AI/ML domain roles. The broad AI screen is strong, but downstream RQ claims need subpattern-level validation.
    4. Company-cap or model company/posting-volume effects in every YOE-based junior analysis.
    5. Use T09 domains only with explicit coverage reporting; consider expanding LLM-cleaned text coverage before making full-corpus domain claims.
