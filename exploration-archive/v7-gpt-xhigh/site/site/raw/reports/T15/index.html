    T15. Semantic Similarity Landscape & Convergence Analysis¶

    Headline¶

    The corpus-level semantic convergence claim does not survive calibration. Under embeddings, junior-senior similarity rises only trivially from arshkon 2024 to scraped 2026 (+0.005), while the within-2024 arshkon-vs-asaniczka gap is larger (+0.022). Under TF-IDF/SVD, junior and senior postings become less similar from arshkon 2024 to scraped 2026 (-0.042). Raw-description TF-IDF also rejects convergence.

    The dominant structure is domain/archetype, not seniority. In the bounded 9,054-row LLM-text sample, T09 archetype labels explain much more representation variance than seniority: eta-squared is 0.111 for archetype versus 0.007 for seniority in embeddings, and 0.132 versus 0.007 in TF-IDF/SVD.

    Methodology¶

    Script: exploration/scripts/T15_semantic_similarity_landscape.py.

    Inputs: swe_embeddings.npy, swe_embedding_index.parquet, swe_cleaned_text.parquet, swe_archetype_labels.parquet, the T30 seniority panel, and narrow metadata columns from data/unified.parquet. The script loaded shared embeddings only; it did not recompute embeddings, rerun BERTopic, rerun preprocessing, or make LLM calls.

    Primary text frame: text_source = 'llm', length(description_cleaned) >= 100, linked to shared embedding rows. This gives 33,741 eligible LLM-text rows.

    Bounded modeling sample: up to 2,000 rows per year x seniority_3level, deterministic by UID hash, including unknown seniority for the structural map. Final sample size is 9,054 rows.

    Sample coverage:

    Source-period Junior Mid Senior Unknown
    arshkon 2024 166 15 337 594
    asaniczka 2024 197 24 1,663 1,406
    scraped 2026-03 360 26 970 945
    scraped 2026-04 241 25 1,030 1,055

    Text-source coverage in the full shared artifact remains the main limitation: arshkon has 4,687 LLM rows and 4 raw-fallback rows; asaniczka has 17,037 LLM and 1,092 raw; scraped has 12,534 LLM and 28,347 raw. Therefore semantic findings describe the LLM-cleaned subset, especially for scraped 2026.

    Representations:

    • Embeddings: shared all-MiniLM-L6-v2 embeddings, L2-normalized.
    • TF-IDF/SVD: TF-IDF over description_cleaned, unigram/bigram, 20,000 max features, reduced to 100 SVD components and L2-normalized.
    • Raw-text sensitivity: TF-IDF/SVD over sampled raw description from data/unified.parquet.

    Centroid method: trimmed centroids remove the 10% most distant rows from each group before computing cosine similarity. Within-group dispersion uses an exact bounded formula from normalized vector sums, avoiding O(N^2) all-pairs matrices.

    Figures:

    • exploration/figures/T15/umap_structural_map.png
    • exploration/figures/T15/pca_structural_map.png
    • exploration/figures/T15/centroid_similarity_heatmaps.png
    • exploration/figures/T15/nearest_neighbor_excess.png

    t-SNE was skipped because UMAP/PCA were sufficient and the prior OOM made another heavy reducer an unnecessary risk.

    Main Findings¶

    Domain Dominates Seniority¶

    Variance explained by factor:

    Representation Archetype Source Year Seniority
    Embedding 0.111 0.010 0.008 0.007
    TF-IDF/SVD 0.132 0.014 0.013 0.007

    This reproduces and strengthens T09's finding: natural posting structure is mostly technology/domain structure. Seniority is visible in some local neighborhoods, but it is not the main axis of semantic variation.

    Corpus-Level Convergence Is Rejected¶

    Primary seniority_3level junior-senior centroid similarity:

    Representation Arshkon 2024 Asaniczka 2024 Pooled 2024 Scraped 2026 Arshkon -> scraped Within-2024 calibration Verdict
    Embedding 0.954 0.976 0.974 0.959 +0.005 +0.022 reject
    TF-IDF/SVD 0.884 0.939 0.934 0.842 -0.042 +0.055 reject

    The embedding result is a tiny positive shift only if arshkon is the 2024 comparator. It disappears against pooled 2024: scraped 2026 is lower than pooled 2024 by -0.015. The TF-IDF result points the other way, with scraped junior and senior postings farther apart than arshkon junior and senior postings.

    Raw-description TF-IDF also rejects convergence: arshkon junior-senior similarity is 0.948, scraped 2026 is 0.913, for a -0.035 shift. That matters because a boilerplate-heavy raw representation does not rescue the convergence story.

    T30 Panel¶

    The T30 seniority panel gives the same practical verdict.

    • J1/S1 embedding similarity rises by +0.005, but within-2024 calibration is +0.022, so it rejects.
    • J1/S1 TF-IDF falls by -0.042, so it rejects directly.
    • J1/S4 embedding is essentially flat (-0.002), and TF-IDF falls (-0.055).
    • J2 behaves like J1.
    • J3/J4 low-YOE definitions show small positive embedding shifts against S1/S4, but those shifts are below within-2024 calibration. TF-IDF is flat or negative.
    • The only apparent embedding passes are J3/S3 and J4/S3, but S3 is the artifact-prone title-normalized senior proxy and is thin in the bounded sample: arshkon S3 n = 18 and scraped S3 n = 15. I do not treat those as substantive evidence.

    Conclusion: no seniority operationalization supports a robust corpus-level junior-senior convergence claim.

    Sensitivities¶

    The primary rejection survives the required sensitivity checks.

    Spec Embedding shift TF-IDF/SVD shift Verdict
    Primary all rows +0.005 -0.042 reject
    Exclude aggregators +0.003 -0.054 reject
    Company cap 50 +0.003 -0.042 reject
    Exclude title_lookup_llm tier +0.005 -0.048 reject
    Raw-description TF-IDF n/a -0.035 reject

    Source restriction is decisive. Arshkon-only gives the most favorable embedding comparison, but its +0.005 shift is smaller than the +0.022 arshkon-vs-asaniczka calibration gap. Pooled 2024 to scraped 2026 is negative under both representations.

    Within-Group Dispersion¶

    Groups become more internally similar in 2026, especially in embeddings:

    Group Embedding avg pairwise cosine 2024 2026
    Junior 0.492 0.520
    Senior 0.494 0.543
    Unknown 0.451 0.503

    TF-IDF/SVD shows the same direction but smaller magnitudes. This is not junior-senior convergence; it is within-group consolidation. A plausible interpretation is that 2026 postings are more templated/domain-concentrated, or that the LLM-cleaned scraped subset is narrower than the full scraped corpus.

    Nearest Neighbors¶

    For each 2026 junior-definition query, the script finds 5 nearest 2024 neighbors in a company-capped 8,000-row candidate pool and reports excess over the candidate base rate.

    Senior-neighbor excess:

    Query definition Embedding TF-IDF
    J1 entry label +3.2 pp -8.6 pp
    J2 entry/associate +4.2 pp -7.2 pp
    J3 YOE <= 2 +12.4 pp +4.6 pp
    J4 YOE <= 3 +12.6 pp +0.4 pp

    This is the most nuanced semantic finding. Label-based 2026 entry rows are enriched for 2024 junior neighbors under both representations: J1 junior-neighbor excess is +8.2 pp in embeddings and +7.0 pp in TF-IDF. Low-YOE rows, however, are more senior-neighbor-heavy, especially under embeddings. That supports the broader Wave 2 puzzle: low-YOE postings and explicit entry labels are not the same construct.

    Archetype-Stratified Convergence¶

    Within-domain convergence is mostly thin or representation-sensitive.

    • Enterprise Application Support passes the embedding calibration check by a small margin (+0.016 arshkon-to-scraped versus +0.015 within-2024), but TF-IDF rejects it (+0.012 versus +0.047 calibration).
    • AI LLM Platforms has a large positive embedding shift (+0.052), but arshkon junior n = 14 and within-2024 calibration is unavailable, so it is descriptive only.
    • Several other archetypes have arshkon junior cells below 10, making calibration impossible.

    The right next step is not to claim within-domain convergence from T15; it is to test domain-specific requirement migration with more targeted, validated features.

    Outliers¶

    Outliers are mostly domain pockets, not seniority mistakes. Examples include embedded/C++ junior roles, defense-clearance junior roles, Android unknown roles, mainframe unknown roles, AI-heavy senior roles, and Google/large-employer platform postings. This aligns with the variance finding: unusual technology/domain content moves a posting farther from its seniority centroid than the seniority label itself.

    Surprises & Unexpected Patterns¶

    • The embedding representation is so high-similarity at the centroid level that the favorable convergence estimate is only +0.005 and is swamped by source calibration.
    • TF-IDF/SVD is more discriminating and points toward divergence, not convergence.
    • 2026 rows are more internally homogeneous within seniority groups, even though junior and senior groups are not converging.
    • J1 entry-labeled rows have junior-neighbor enrichment, while J3/J4 low-YOE rows have senior-neighbor enrichment. This is a stronger semantic version of the label-vs-YOE split found in T08/T30.
    • Domain/archetype explains about 15-18x as much variance as seniority in embeddings and TF-IDF.

    Caveats¶

    • Scraped 2026 LLM-cleaned coverage is only 12,534 rows out of 40,881 default LinkedIn SWE rows. Semantic claims are LLM-subset evidence.
    • The bounded sample keeps memory stable but makes tiny groups thinner. S2 and S3 should be treated as descriptive only.
    • T30 S3 inherits the known title-normalization caveat from Wave 1; apparent S3 convergence is not a substantive senior-role finding.
    • UMAP/PCA are communication aids, not estimators. The tables drive the conclusions.
    • Nearest-neighbor results are representation-sensitive and should be manually spot-checked before being used as paper-grade evidence.

    Downstream Action Items¶

    1. Gate 2 should reject a generic "junior postings became semantically like senior postings" claim.
    2. Keep the lower-YOE/entry-label split as a central mechanism: J1 behaves differently from J3/J4 in nearest-neighbor structure.
    3. T20/T28 should test convergence within specific archetypes using validated requirement families, not broad corpus centroids.
    4. Expand scraped LLM-cleaned coverage before making final semantic-landscape claims in the paper.
    5. Use T15 figures to communicate that the posting space is organized by domain and technology first, with seniority as a weaker secondary layer.
