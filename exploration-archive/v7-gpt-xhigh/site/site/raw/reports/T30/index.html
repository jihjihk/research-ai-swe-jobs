    T30. Seniority Definition Ablation Panel¶

    Headline¶

    Junior definitions J1-J4 all move up from pooled 2024 to scraped 2026. Senior definitions are directionally split: S1, S3, and S4 move down, while director-only S2 moves up. The robust reading is "junior share rises across definitions, broad senior/YOE senior share falls, and director labels rise as a distinct top-ladder phenomenon."

    The canonical panel exists at exploration/artifacts/shared/seniority_definition_panel.csv. I verified its schema with DuckDB: 44 rows, one row per definition x source-period for J1-J6 and S1-S5, with the required columns definition, side, period, source, n_of_all, n_of_known, share_of_all, share_of_known, mde_arshkon_vs_scraped, mde_pooled_vs_scraped, within_2024_effect, cross_period_effect, and direction.

    Evidence base: existing T30 tables/figures in exploration/tables/T30/, exploration/figures/T30/, and the shared panel artifact. No panel regeneration was performed.

    Senior verdict: S1-S4 do not all agree. S1, S3, and S4 decline; S2 rises. This is not a nuisance result. It suggests that director-only labels are behaving differently from broad senior labels and YOE-based seniority.

    Overlap Interpretation¶

    Figures:

    • exploration/figures/T30/junior_overlap_heatmap.png
    • exploration/figures/T30/senior_overlap_heatmap.png

    Junior overlap:

    • J1 is nested in J2 and J6 by construction.
    • J5 is high precision but narrow: 90.6% of J5 rows are also J1, while J5 captures only 42.4% of J1.
    • J3 is a subset of J4 by construction. The low J1/J3 row overlap is partly a missing-YOE artifact: the overlap matrix treats rows without extracted YOE as nonmatches.
    • J3/J4 are much broader than label-based junior definitions. They should be interpreted as "low stated YOE" rather than employer-posted junior labels.

    Senior overlap:

    • S2 and S3 are both subsets of S1 in the observed panel.
    • S3 has only 596 rows overall under title_normalized, so it is not a useful high-recall senior title measure as currently computed.
    • S4 is related to S1 but not equivalent: 54.7% of S1 rows have yoe_extracted >= 5, and 65.4% of S4 rows are S1.
    • S5 is a strict subset of S4 and remains directionally down.

    Title-Keyword Spot Checks¶

    J5-only-not-J1 has population n = 73. The existing 50-row sample is overwhelmingly real junior/new-grad title language: Graduate, New College Graduate, University Graduate, 2026 Start, and intern native labels dominate. I count 49 of 50 as semantically junior or new-grad; one row, Sr Systems Engineer (New Grad- Salt Lake City, UT), is contradictory and should be treated as ambiguous. Estimated J5-only precision from the existing sample: about 98%, with the caveat that many rows are concentrated in graduate hiring pipelines.

    S3-only-not-S1 has no sampled rows because the population is zero: every normalized-title S3 row is already in S1. Precision for "S3 only" is therefore not estimable from the existing artifact.

    Important artifact: title_keyword_raw_title_sensitivity.csv shows that raw-title senior matching is far larger than normalized-title S3. Raw-title S3 would be 56.7% of pooled 2024 SWE rows and 42.3% of scraped 2026 SWE rows, while normalized-title S3 is only 1.95% and 0.37%. The likely mechanism is that title_normalized strips seniority indicators. Do not use current S3 as a standalone senior-title prevalence measure.

    Aggregator Sensitivity¶

    Excluding aggregators does not change directions:

    Definition group Include aggregators Exclude aggregators
    J1-J4 all up all up
    S1 down down
    S2 up up
    S3 down down
    S4 down down

    Magnitude shifts are modest for label-based junior definitions and larger for YOE seniority. For example, J1's known-denominator cross-period effect moves from +3.99 pp to +3.43 pp without aggregators; S4 moves from -7.50 pp to -8.90 pp. Aggregators are not driving the direction result, but they affect some effect sizes enough that Wave 2/3 should keep aggregator exclusion as a required sensitivity.

    Primary Recommendation¶

    Primary junior definition: J1 (seniority_final = 'entry'). It is the cleanest employer-posted junior label, has strong YOE validity from T03, and agrees in direction with J2-J4. For subgroup/content analyses where J1 is thin, report J3 as the main label-independent robustness check rather than silently switching the estimand.

    Primary senior definition: S1 (seniority_final IN ('mid-senior','director')). It is the best label-based broad-senior measure and is well powered. Always pair it with S4 (yoe_extracted >= 5) because S2 shows that director-only roles are moving differently, and current S3 is not a reliable title-keyword proxy.

    Surprises & Unexpected Patterns¶

    • Junior direction is more consistent than expected: label-based and YOE-based definitions all rise.
    • The senior side is substantively split. Director-only rises while broad senior and YOE senior decline.
    • Normalized-title S3 is not measuring ordinary senior-title prevalence. The raw-title sensitivity implies a preprocessing/title-normalization artifact.
    • J2 adds little power over J1 in the current data, so combining entry and associate is not a major solution to thin junior cells.
    • Within-2024 calibration is large. Even when directions agree, historical-source variability is large enough to constrain Gate 1 claims.
