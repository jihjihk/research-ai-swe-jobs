    We can make posting-language claims, not hiring-bar or employment claims.¶

    The current evidence is strong enough for a posting-content restructuring paper. It is not strong enough for a causal employment story or a worker-usage divergence story.

    What we can claim now¶

    Claim type Claim
    Posting content SWE and software-adjacent technical postings expanded around AI/tool/platform/workflow language from 2024 to 2026.
    Company mechanism Returning companies contributed strongly to AI/tool expansion; the change is not only new-company entry.
    Occupation boundary The shift is SWE-amplified relative to controls but appears almost in parallel for adjacent technical roles.
    Geography Eligible metros show broad AI/tool increases, so the change is not only Bay Area/Seattle/NYC.
    Breadth Requirement and technology breadth rose across junior and senior definitions, with LLM-coverage caveats.
    Measurement Entry labels, low-YOE floors, and semantic role content do not identify the same junior population.
    Structure Domain and technology explain posting representation structure much more than seniority.

    What we cannot claim yet¶

    Overclaim Why not
    AI eliminated junior SWE roles. J1-J4 evidence is mixed by source and construct; several panels show junior definitions rising.
    Postings measure employment or hires. The dataset is job-ad content, not filled jobs, headcount, or employment stock.
    AI/tool mentions are screened requirements. T22 failed, and current measures mostly identify mentions, breadth, or text patterns.
    Ghost or aspirational posting language is prevalent. Plausible threat, but no completed T22 evidence quantifies it.
    Employer requirements outpace worker usage. T23 failed; there is no external worker-usage benchmark comparison.
    Temporal rates or annualized trends are stable. T19 failed; no scraped-window flow/stability analysis exists.
    Senior-role archetypes were independently redefined. T21 failed; T11 supports senior breadth and mentorship/coordination growth, not a full senior-role redefinition model.
    Wave 3 findings are independently verified like Wave 2. V2 failed; T16/T17/T18 are synthesized by Gate 3 but not independently verified.

    How to phrase the paper¶

    Use:

    Between 2024 and 2026, postings for SWE and software-adjacent technical work broadened around AI/tool/platform/workflow requirements, while seniority labels and YOE floors diverged as measurement signals.

    Avoid:

    AI caused employers to eliminate junior SWE jobs.

    Employers now require AI tools more than workers use them.

    The AI requirements are mostly ghost or aspirational.

    Those stronger claims are plausible research questions for the next phase, not completed findings.
