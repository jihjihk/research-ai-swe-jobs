As your academic research mentor, I have organized the primary source material for our project into a formal reference list. These sources represent the current frontier of labor economics, human-computer interaction, and sociology regarding the AI-driven transformation of work.

Following each reference, I have included a brief summary of how we will utilize that specific source to maintain the rigor of our proposed research directions.

### **Annotated Research Reference List**

**1. Chen, A., Kim, S. S., Dharmasiri, A., Russakovsky, O., & Fan, J. E. (2025). Portraying Large Language Models as Machines, Tools, or Companions Affects What Mental Capacities Humans Attribute to Them. *CHI EA '25: Extended Abstracts of the CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems*.**
*   **Research Utility:** This study provides the psychological foundation for our "AI Native" definition,. It demonstrates that portraying LLMs as "companions" (the intentional stance) significantly increases the mental capacities users attribute to them,. We will use their survey instruments to test if the "AI Native" generation has a higher baseline attribution of "mind" to AI than previous cohorts, which may explain the higher rates of "AI Delegation" and cognitive offloading we seek to measure,.

**2. Akanegbu, A. (2026). (404) Job Not Found: What Workforce Training Can’t Fix for Black Atlantans in the Age of AI. *Data & Society Research Institute*.**
*   **Research Utility:** This ethnographic work introduces the concept of "strategic abstraction"—the intentional vagueness used by power brokers to define "AI Literacy" without concrete standards,. This source is critical for our analysis of job descriptions; it provides the theoretical lens to argue that "AI Literacy" requirements often function as gatekeeping mechanisms or "linguistic code-switches" rather than functional job requirements,.

**3. Chandar, B. (2025). AI and Labor Markets: What We Know and Don’t Know. *Stanford Digital Economy Lab*.**
*   **Research Utility:** This source synthesizes the current state of economic knowledge, highlighting the mismatch between aggregate employment growth and the concentrated decline in hiring for young workers in AI-exposed roles,. It serves as a guide for our macro-economic inquiry, specifically regarding the "Expertise Upheaval" and the "Flipped Pyramid" staffing models emerging in the tech and finance sectors,.

**4. Brynjolfsson, E., & Richardson, N. (2026). AI and the Great Job Unbundling: A Collaboration to Measure Granular Changes in Job Responsibility. *Stanford Digital Economy Lab & ADP Research*.**
*   **Research Utility:** This source provides the technical blueprint for our "Rigorous Upgrade B" methodology,. It details how to use Natural Language Processing (NLP) to extract O*NET tasks from millions of job postings and apply "hedonic regression" to estimate the economic value of individual work activities,. This allows us to move beyond job titles to prove the financial devaluation of entry-level tasks,.

**5. Levanon, G., Sigelman, M., Mamertino, M., de Zeeuw, M., & Guilford, G. (2025). No Country for Young Grads: The Structural Forces That Are Reshaping Entry-Level Employment. *The Burning Glass Institute*.**
*   **Research Utility:** This report identifies the structural crisis facing recent graduates, noting that a bachelor’s degree is failing to deliver on its promise of professional entry,. It provides the "Expertise Upheaval" framework we will use to analyze how AI-powered automation targets "stepping stone" tasks, thereby raising the first rung of the career ladder out of reach for juniors,.

**6. Shen, J. H., & Tamkin, A. (2026). How AI Impacts Skill Formation. *arXiv preprint*.**
*   **Research Utility:** This randomized controlled trial is the cornerstone for our study of the "Supervision Paradox",. It proves that AI assistance significantly impairs conceptual understanding and debugging abilities, the very skills required to supervise AI,. We will utilize their six behavioral interaction patterns (e.g., "AI Delegation" vs. "Conceptual Inquiry") to cluster our own experimental subjects,.

**7. Brynjolfsson, E., Chandar, B., & Chen, R. (2025). Canaries in the Coal Mine? Six Facts about the Recent Employment Effects of Artificial Intelligence. *Stanford Digital Economy Lab*.**
*   **Research Utility:** This paper provides the econometric rigor needed for our "Flipped Pyramid" analysis,. By using "firm-time fixed effects," it demonstrates that early-career employment declines are not macro-economic accidents but are specifically tied to occupations where AI automates rather than augments labor,. We will adopt their classification of "automative" vs. "augmentative" AI usage to distinguish between roles that are shrinking and those that are evolving,.

**8. Challapally, A., Pease, C., Raskar, R., & Chari, P. (2025). The GenAI Divide: State of AI in Business 2025 Report. *MIT NANDA*.**
*   **Research Utility:** This report documents the organizational landscape, revealing a "divide" where 95% of GenAI pilots fail to move beyond individual productivity gains to P&L impact,. It offers the business context for our investigation into why "AI Literacy" is prioritized in hiring even when official deployments are failing, often due to a lack of "persistent memory" and learning in AI systems,.
